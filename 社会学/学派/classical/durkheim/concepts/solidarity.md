# 社会团结 · Social Solidarity (Solidarite Sociale)

## 概念界定 / Concept Definition

社会团结（Social Solidarity / Solidarite Sociale）是涂尔干社会学的核心问题和贯穿性概念。涂尔干毕生追问的是：社会是如何可能的？在传统社会的宗教和共同体纽带被现代化进程瓦解之后，什么力量能够将分散的个人凝聚为一个有秩序的社会整体？

Social Solidarity (Solidarite Sociale) is the central problematic and pervasive concept of Durkheim's sociology. Durkheim's lifelong question was: how is society possible? After traditional religious and communal bonds are eroded by modernization, what force can bind dispersed individuals into an ordered social whole?

## 两种团结类型 / Two Types of Solidarity

### 机械团结 / Mechanical Solidarity

**基础 / Basis:** 社会成员之间的相似性——共同的信仰、价值观和生活方式 / Similarity among social members—shared beliefs, values, and ways of life

**社会类型 / Social Type:** 传统社会、部落社会、前工业社会 / Traditional societies, tribal societies, pre-industrial societies

**集体意识 / Collective Consciousness:** 强大、具体、涵盖生活的几乎所有方面 / Strong, concrete, encompassing almost all aspects of life

**法律类型 / Legal Type:** 压制性法律（droit repressif）——对违反集体意识的行为施以惩罚 / Repressive law (droit repressif)—punishing violations of collective consciousness

**个人与社会的关系 / Individual-Society Relation:** 个人完全隶属于集体，几乎没有独立的空间 / Individual entirely subordinate to the collective, with almost no independent space

### 有机团结 / Organic Solidarity

**基础 / Basis:** 社会成员之间的功能互补和相互依赖——分工造成的差异化 / Functional complementarity and interdependence among members—differentiation caused by division of labour

**社会类型 / Social Type:** 现代社会、工业社会 / Modern societies, industrial societies

**集体意识 / Collective Consciousness:** 弱化、抽象，仅限于基本的道德原则（如人权、个人尊严）/ Weakened, abstract, limited to basic moral principles (e.g., human rights, individual dignity)

**法律类型 / Legal Type:** 恢复性法律（droit restitutif）——恢复被破坏的社会关系 / Restitutive law (droit restitutif)—restoring disrupted social relationships

**个人与社会的关系 / Individual-Society Relation:** 个人具有自主性和独立空间，但通过功能依赖与他人联结 / Individual has autonomy and independent space, but connected to others through functional dependence

## 团结的"物质密度" / "Moral Density" of Solidarity

涂尔干引入了"道德密度"（densite morale / moral density）概念来解释团结类型的转变。道德密度是指社会成员之间互动的频率和强度。人口增长、城市化和交通通讯技术的发展都增加了道德密度，导致社会成员之间的竞争加剧，从而推动了社会分工——分工是对竞争的一种"和平解决方案"。

Durkheim introduced the concept of "moral density" (densite morale) to explain the transformation of solidarity types. Moral density refers to the frequency and intensity of interactions among social members. Population growth, urbanization, and communication-technology development all increase moral density, intensifying competition among members and driving social division of labour—division of labour is a "peaceful solution" to competition.

## 团结的病理 / Pathologies of Solidarity

### 失范团结 / Anomic Solidarity
当社会变迁过于迅速，新的规范体系尚未建立时，社会陷入"失范"状态。经济危机和经济繁荣都可能导致失范——前者因为旧规范被暴力打破，后者因为繁荣带来的欲望失去了传统规范的约束。

When social change occurs too rapidly and new normative systems have not yet been established, society falls into "anomie." Both economic crises and economic booms can cause anomie—the former because old norms are violently disrupted, the latter because prosperity unleashes desires freed from traditional normative constraints.

### 强制的分工 / Forced Division of Labour
当分工不是基于个人能力和倾向的自然匹配，而是基于社会不平等（如阶级特权、种姓制度）的强制安排时，分工非但不能产生团结，反而加剧了社会冲突。涂尔干称之为分工的"反常形式"。

When the division of labour is based not on natural matching of individual abilities and inclinations but on forced arrangements reflecting social inequality (e.g., class privilege, caste systems), it fails to produce solidarity and instead intensifies social conflict. Durkheim called this a "pathological form" of the division of labour.

## 当代意义 / Contemporary Significance

涂尔干的团结理论在全球化时代具有新的相关性：跨国移民和多元文化主义挑战着民族国家的团结基础；数字平台创造了新型的"虚拟团结"；全球性问题（气候变化、疫情）呼唤着超越国界的"全球团结"。涂尔干关于"个人崇拜"作为现代社会道德基础的论述，为人权话语和社会包容政策提供了理论支持。

Durkheim's solidarity theory acquires new relevance in the era of globalization: transnational migration and multiculturalism challenge the solidarity foundations of nation-states; digital platforms create new forms of "virtual solidarity"; global problems (climate change, pandemics) call for "global solidarity" transcending national borders. Durkheim's argument about the "cult of the individual" as modern society's moral foundation provides theoretical support for human-rights discourse and social-inclusion policies.
