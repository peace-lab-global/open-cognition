# 无心 Wuxin — No-Mind

> "无心者，无一切心也。"
> "No-mind means no mind at all — no grasping mind, no rejecting mind, no mind of any kind."
> ——达摩血脉论（传） / *Bloodline Treatise* (attributed to Bodhidharma)

---

## 概述 / Overview

无心（Wuxin，no-mind）是达摩禅法中的核心概念之一，指一种不执著于任何念头、不攀缘任何境界的心理状态。它不是"没有心"——不是意识的消失或麻木——而是超越了有意识的分别心，达到一种自然、无造作、不执著的清醒状态。

No-mind (无心, Wuxin) is one of the core concepts in Bodhidharma's Chan, referring to a mental state of non-attachment to any thought, non-clinging to any experience. It is not "having no mind" — not the disappearance or numbing of consciousness — but a transcendence of conscious discriminating mind, reaching a natural, uncontrived, non-attached state of lucid wakefulness.

在日语中，无心对应"無心"（mushin），后来成为武士道、茶道、书法等日本传统文化的核心心理概念。在韩语中对应"무심"（musim），在越南语中对应"vô tâm"，均为东亚精神传统的重要概念。

In Japanese, no-mind corresponds to "無心" (mushin), which later became a core psychological concept in bushido, tea ceremony, calligraphy, and other Japanese traditional arts. In Korean it corresponds to "무심" (musim), in Vietnamese to "vô tâm" — all important concepts in East Asian spiritual traditions.

## 达摩的无心思想 / Bodhidharma's Thought on No-Mind

达摩对无心的阐述主要见于《达摩血脉论》（虽可能为后人托名，但思想与达摩禅一致）：

Bodhidharma's exposition of no-mind is primarily found in the *Bloodline Treatise* (possibly attributed by later generations, but consistent with Bodhidharma's Chan thought):

### 无心的本质 / The Nature of No-Mind

"无心"不是没有心，而是"心无所住"——心不驻留在任何对象上。达摩认为，凡夫之所以受苦，是因为心总是攀缘：攀缘好的、排斥坏的、追逐未来的、悔恨过去的。当心不再攀缘任何对象时，就进入了"无心"状态。

"No-mind" is not the absence of mind but "mind abiding nowhere" — the mind does not settle on any object. Bodhidharma held that ordinary beings suffer because the mind is always grasping: grasping the pleasant, rejecting the unpleasant, chasing the future, regretting the past. When the mind no longer grasps any object, it enters the state of "no-mind."

### 无心与佛性 / No-Mind and Buddha-Nature

达摩将无心与佛性直接等同：无心的状态就是佛性显现的状态。"即心是佛"的"心"，不是分别心、攀缘心，而是"无心"——超越了二元对立的本来心。在这个意义上，无心不是修行的手段，而是修行的目的——它就是觉悟本身。

Bodhidharma directly equates no-mind with Buddha-nature: the state of no-mind is the state in which Buddha-nature manifests. The "mind" in "this mind is Buddha" is not the discriminating, grasping mind but "no-mind" — the original mind that transcends binary opposition. In this sense, no-mind is not the means of practice but its goal — it is awakening itself.

### 安心与无心 / Pacifying the Mind and No-Mind

达摩与慧可的著名对话深刻揭示了安心与无心的关系：

The famous dialogue between Bodhidharma and Huike profoundly reveals the relationship between pacifying the mind and no-mind:

> 慧可："我心未宁，乞师与安。"
> 达摩："将心来，与汝安。"
> 慧可："觅心了不可得。"
> 达摩："我与汝安心竟。"

> Huike: "My mind is not at peace. Please, Master, pacify it for me."
> Bodhidharma: "Bring me your mind, and I will pacify it for you."
> Huike: "I search for my mind but cannot find it."
> Bodhidharma: "There — I have pacified your mind for you."

这一公案表明：当你寻找"心"而不可得时，就已经处于"无心"状态——而这就是真正的"安心"。安心不是给心加上某种安定，而是发现心本来就无可安定——因为那个需要被安定的"心"本身就是一个幻象。

This koan demonstrates: when you search for "mind" and cannot find it, you are already in the state of "no-mind" — and this is true "pacification of the mind." Pacifying the mind is not adding stability to the mind, but discovering that the mind was never in need of pacification — because the "mind" that needs pacifying is itself an illusion.

## 哲学内涵 / Philosophical Implications

无心的概念涉及几个重要的哲学维度：

The concept of no-mind involves several important philosophical dimensions:

**主体性的解构 Deconstruction of Subjectivity**：无心思想挑战了"有一个固定主体在思考"的假设。当达摩说"无心"时，他实际上是在消解一个独立、持续、固定的"思考者"概念。这与佛教"无我"（anatman）教义一脉相承，但以更为直接和实践性的方式表达。

The no-mind thought challenges the assumption that "there is a fixed subject doing the thinking." When Bodhidharma says "no-mind," he is effectively dissolving the concept of an independent, continuous, fixed "thinker." This is continuous with the Buddhist teaching of "no-self" (anatman), but expressed in a more direct and practical way.

**自由的本质 The Nature of Freedom**：无心状态中的自由不是"想做什么就做什么"的自由，而是"不被任何念头所束缚"的自由。这是一种消极自由——从念头的束缚中解放——而非积极自由——实现某种理想状态。

Freedom in the state of no-mind is not the freedom of "doing whatever one wants" but the freedom of "not being bound by any thought." This is a negative freedom — liberation from the bondage of thoughts — rather than positive freedom — achieving some ideal state.

**自然与无造作 Nature and Non-Contrivance**：无心与道家的"无为"（wuwei）有深层共鸣。二者都指向一种不刻意、不造作的自然状态。但达摩的"无心"更侧重于心理层面——心的无造作，而道家的"无为"更侧重于行为层面——行动的无造作。

No-mind deeply resonates with Daoism's "non-action" (wuwei). Both point to a natural state that is non-deliberate and non-contrived. However, Bodhidharma's "no-mind" focuses more on the psychological dimension — the non-contrivance of mind — while Daoist "non-action" focuses more on the behavioral dimension — the non-contrivance of action.

## 在各宗派中的发展 / Development Across Schools

无心概念在后世各禅宗流派中得到了不同的发展：

The concept of no-mind developed differently across subsequent Chan schools:

- **南宗慧能 Huineng's Southern School**：慧能的"无念"（无念为宗）直接继承并发展了达摩的无心思想。"无念"不是"没有念头"，而是"于念而无念"——念头来了又去，心不执著。
  Huineng's "no-thought" (wunian) directly inherited and developed Bodhidharma's no-mind thought. "No-thought" is not "absence of thoughts" but "no-thought within thought" — thoughts come and go, the mind does not cling.

- **临济宗 Linji School**：临济义玄提出"无心道人"的概念，将无心视为修行的最高境界。"无心"在临济宗中特别强调在日常行为中的自然流露。
  Linji Yixuan proposed the concept of the "no-mind person of the Way," regarding no-mind as the highest state of practice. In the Linji school, no-mind particularly emphasizes natural expression in everyday behavior.

- **曹洞宗 Soto School**：道元的"只管打坐"（shikantaza）可被理解为一种"无心的坐禅"——坐禅时不追求开悟，不排斥妄想，只是纯粹地坐着。
  Dogen's "just sitting" (shikantaza) can be understood as a form of "no-mind sitting meditation" — during sitting, one does not seek enlightenment, does not reject thoughts, but simply sits purely.

- **日本禅 Japanese Zen**：无心的概念（mushin）在日本文化中得到了极为广泛的运用——从剑道中的"无心之剑"到茶道中的"无心之茶"，从书法中的"无心之笔"到园林设计中的"无心之美"。
  The concept of no-mind (mushin) found extraordinarily wide application in Japanese culture — from "the sword of no-mind" in kendo to "the tea of no-mind" in tea ceremony, from "the brushstroke of no-mind" in calligraphy to "the beauty of no-mind" in garden design.

## 当代心理学视角 / Contemporary Psychological Perspectives

从当代心理学角度看，无心可以与多个概念对话：

From a contemporary psychology perspective, no-mind can dialogue with multiple concepts:

**流动状态 Flow State**：心理学家米哈里·契克森米哈赖（Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi）提出的"心流"（flow）概念——完全沉浸于活动中，自我意识消失——与无心有显著的结构相似性。区别在于：心流通常发生在高度投入的活动中，而无心可以在任何状态下发生，包括完全的静止。

Psychologist Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi's concept of "flow" — complete immersion in an activity with the disappearance of self-awareness — shows significant structural similarity to no-mind. The difference: flow typically occurs during highly engaged activities, while no-mind can occur in any state, including complete stillness.

**默认模式网络 Default Mode Network (DMN)**：神经科学研究表明，当 DMN 活动降低时，自我参照思维减少，这与"无心"的体验高度吻合。长期冥想者在静坐时表现出显著降低的 DMN 活动。

Neuroscience research shows that when DMN activity decreases, self-referential thinking diminishes, which closely matches the experience of "no-mind." Long-term meditators exhibit significantly reduced DMN activity during seated meditation.
