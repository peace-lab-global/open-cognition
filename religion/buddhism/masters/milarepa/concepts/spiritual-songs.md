# 道歌 Spiritual Songs (Dohā / mgur)

> "我不识文字不读书，心中自然涌道歌；
> 一切有情听我唱，皆入解脱安乐道。"
>
> "I cannot read nor write, yet songs of realization flow naturally from my heart;
> All beings who hear my singing enter the path of liberation and bliss."

## 概述 Overview

道歌（藏文：མགུར་，mgur 或 རྡོ་རྗེའི་གླུ་，rdo rje'i glu；梵文：Dohā），是密勒日巴传法的核心媒介。不同于传统的书面经典和寺院讲经，密勒日巴通过即兴的诗歌吟唱来回应弟子、施主和求法者的提问。他的道歌涵盖了从基础的因果教导到最深奥的大手印指引的全部修行道路。

Spiritual songs (Tibetan: མགུར་, mgur or རྡོ་རྗེའི་གླུ་, rdo rje'i glu; Sanskrit: Dohā) are the central medium of Milarepa's teaching. Unlike traditional written scriptures and monastic lectures, Milarepa responded to questions from disciples, patrons, and seekers through spontaneous poetic song. His songs cover the entire path of practice, from basic teachings on cause and effect to the most profound Mahamudra instructions.

"道歌"这一术语中，"道"指修行道路上的体验和证悟，"歌"则是表达这些体验的形式。密勒日巴的道歌不是文学创作——它们是修行体验的直接流露，是"从证悟心中自然涌出的金刚歌"。

In the term "spiritual song," "spiritual" refers to experiences and realizations on the path of practice, and "song" is the form of expression. Milarepa's songs are not literary creations — they are the direct expression of meditative experience, "vajra songs naturally arising from a realized mind."

## 历史源流 Historical Origins

### 印度道歌传统 Indian Dohā Tradition

道歌传统起源于印度的大成就者（Mahāsiddha）传统。八至十二世纪的印度大成就者们——如萨拉哈（Saraha）、帝洛巴（Tilopa）和那不洛巴（Naropa）——以通俗的阿帕布兰沙语（Apabhraṃśa）而非典雅的梵语创作道歌，将密法的深奥义理用日常语言表达。萨拉哈的道歌尤为著名：

The dohā tradition originated in India's Mahāsiddha tradition. The Indian Mahasiddhas of the 8th–12th centuries — such as Saraha, Tilopa, and Naropa — composed spiritual songs in colloquial Apabhraṃśa rather than elegant Sanskrit, expressing profound tantric meanings in everyday language. Saraha's songs are particularly celebrated:

> "箭射入了心，谁知道射者？
> 我已饮此水，谁能解我渴？"

> "The arrow has pierced the heart — who knows the archer?
> I have drunk this water — who can quench my thirst?"

玛尔巴将这一传统从印度带回西藏，而密勒日巴将其推向了前所未有的高峰。

Marpa brought this tradition from India to Tibet, and Milarepa elevated it to unprecedented heights.

### 藏文道歌的特征 Characteristics of Tibetan Dohā

密勒日巴的道歌在形式上采用了藏文民歌的结构，通常为六音节或八音节一行，四行或更多行组成一节。他的诗歌语言质朴有力，大量使用自然意象——雪山、湖泊、花朵、野兽——来比喻修行的各个层面。

Milarepa's songs formally adopted Tibetan folk-song structures, typically with six or eight syllables per line, organized in stanzas of four or more lines. His poetic language is direct and powerful, richly employing natural imagery — snow mountains, lakes, flowers, wild animals — as metaphors for various dimensions of practice.

## 《密勒日巴道歌集》 The Hundred Thousand Songs of Milarepa

《密勒日巴道歌集》（མི་ལའི་མགུར་འབུམ་，Mi la'i mgur 'bum）是密勒日巴道歌的总集，传统上说包含十万首道歌。现存版本由桑杰坚赞（Tsangnyön Heruka, 1452–1507）编纂，分为六十一章（或不同版本有不同分法），记录了密勒日巴在不同地点、面对不同听众时所唱的道歌。

*The Hundred Thousand Songs of Milarepa* (མི་ལའི་མགུར་འབུམ་, Mi la'i mgur 'bum) is the collected spiritual songs of Milarepa, traditionally said to contain one hundred thousand songs. The surviving version was compiled by Tsangnyön Heruka (1452–1507) and divided into sixty-one chapters (or varying divisions in different editions), recording songs Milarepa sang at different places and to different audiences.

### 道歌的主题分类 Thematic Classification

道歌集中包含多种类型的教导：

The collection contains multiple types of teaching:

**见地之歌 Songs of View**：直接指示心性的本来面目，属于大手印的直指教授。

**Songs of View**: Directly pointing to mind's innate nature; belonging to the Mahamudra pointing-out instructions.

**修行之歌 Songs of Meditation**：描述各种修行方法——拙火、止观、梦瑜伽等——及其修行体验。

**Songs of Meditation**: Describing various practice methods — tummo, shamatha-vipashyana, dream yoga — and meditative experiences.

**行为之歌 Songs of Conduct**：关于修行者的行为准则——舍弃世俗生活、简朴修行、慈悲利他。

**Songs of Conduct**: On practitioners' ethical conduct — renouncing worldly life, practicing simply, acting with compassion.

**果位之歌 Songs of Fruition**：描述证悟的各个阶段和最终成佛的体验。

**Songs of Fruition**: Describing stages of realization and the ultimate experience of Buddhahood.

**劝诫之歌 Songs of Admonition**：对弟子的过失和偏差进行温和或严厉的纠正。

**Songs of Admonition**: Gently or sternly correcting disciples' faults and deviations.

## 道歌作为教学方法 Songs as Pedagogy

### 应机施教 Adapting to the Student

密勒日巴道歌最显著的特征之一是其因材施教的特质。面对不同的听众，他用完全不同的方式唱法：

One of the most striking features of Milarepa's songs is their adaptation to the audience. To different listeners, he taught in entirely different ways:

- 对**猎人**，他以慈悲和因果来劝导他们放弃杀生。
- 对**学者僧侣**，他以尖锐的批评来破除他们对概念的执着。
- 对**普通村民**，他用简单的比喻来传达基本的修行道理。
- 对**具根弟子**，他直接指出心性的本来面目。

- To **hunters**, he advised renouncing killing through compassion and karma.
- To **scholarly monks**, he sharply criticized their attachment to concepts.
- To **villagers**, he used simple metaphors to convey basic practice principles.
- To **worthy disciples**, he directly pointed out mind's innate nature.

### 即兴性 Spontaneity

道歌不是预先写好的文本，而是对当下情境的即兴回应。这一特质使道歌具有独特的鲜活性和针对性——每首道歌都与特定的时间、地点、人物和事件紧密相连。

Songs were not pre-written texts but spontaneous responses to present situations. This quality gives dohās a unique vividness and relevance — each song is intimately connected to a specific time, place, person, and event.

这种即兴性也体现了密勒日巴对"当下"的重视——修行不在过去也不在未来，只在此时此刻。

This spontaneity also reflects Milarepa's emphasis on the present moment — practice is neither in the past nor the future, but only here and now.

## 道歌中的自然意象 Natural Imagery in the Songs

密勒日巴的道歌大量使用自然意象，将外在的自然世界转化为内在精神世界的映射：

Milarepa's songs make extensive use of natural imagery, transforming the outer natural world into a mirror of the inner spiritual landscape:

| 自然意象 Image | 修行寓意 Spiritual Meaning |
|---|---|
| 雪山 Snow mountain | 心性的纯净 / Purity of mind's nature |
| 太阳/月亮 Sun/Moon | 智慧与方便 / Wisdom and skillful means |
| 河流 River | 念头的流动 / Flow of thoughts |
| 虚空 Sky/Space | 心性的空性 / Emptiness of mind |
| 花朵 Flower | 善根与功德 / Virtuous roots and merit |
| 野兽 Wild animals | 未被驯服的心 / Untamed mind |
| 洞穴 Cave | 修行的庇护所 / Refuge for practice |
| 云雾 Clouds/Mist | 妄念的遮蔽 / Obscuration of discursive thoughts |

## 道歌与世界诗歌传统的比较 Comparison with World Poetry Traditions

密勒日巴的道歌可以与世界其他精神诗歌传统进行比较：

Milarepa's songs can be compared with other world spiritual poetry traditions:

**鲁米（Rumi）**：波斯苏菲派诗人鲁米的诗歌同样以自然意象表达精神体验，并强调超越二元对立的爱的体验。两者都将诗歌视为超越概念的直接表达。

**Rumi**: The Persian Sufi poet Rumi similarly used natural imagery to express spiritual experience and emphasized love that transcends duality. Both treated poetry as direct expression beyond concepts.

**寒山与拾得**：中国唐代隐士诗人寒山和拾得的诗歌，以质朴语言和山林意象表达禅悟体验，与密勒日巴的山居道歌有显著的共鸣。

**Hanshan and Shide**: The Tang dynasty hermit poets Hanshan and Shide used plain language and mountain imagery to express Chan/Zen realization, resonating notably with Milarepa's mountain songs.

**圣十字若望（St. John of the Cross）**：基督教神秘主义者的"灵魂的黑夜"等诗歌，以诗意的语言描述灵性旅程中的极端体验。

**St. John of the Cross**: The Christian mystic's poems, such as "Dark Night of the Soul," describe extreme experiences of the spiritual journey in poetic language.

## 道歌的现代意义 Contemporary Significance

### 口传传统的价值 Value of Oral Tradition

在一个日益依赖书面和数字媒介的时代，密勒日巴的道歌提醒我们口传传统的独特力量——直接的、人格化的、当下的精神传递。

In an age increasingly reliant on written and digital media, Milarepa's songs remind us of the unique power of oral tradition — direct, personal, present-moment spiritual transmission.

### 诗歌作为心灵表达 Poetry as Spiritual Expression

道歌展示了诗歌在表达不可言说的精神体验方面的独特能力。当概念语言到达其极限时，诗歌的隐喻、节奏和情感力量可以传达理性论述无法触及的真理。

Spiritual songs demonstrate poetry's unique capacity to express inexpressible spiritual experience. When conceptual language reaches its limits, poetry's metaphor, rhythm, and emotional power can convey truths that rational discourse cannot reach.
