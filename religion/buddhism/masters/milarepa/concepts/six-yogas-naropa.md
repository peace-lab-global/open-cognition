# 那洛六法 Six Yogas of Naropa

> "六法如梯级，一级引一级；
> 拙火为根本，光明为极果。"
>
> "The six yogas are like rungs of a ladder, each leading to the next;
> Tummo is the root, clear light the ultimate fruit."

## 概述 Overview

那洛六法（藏文：ནཱ་རོ་ཆོས་དྲུག་，nā ro chos drug）是印度大成就者那不洛巴（Naropa, 956–1040）所传、由玛尔巴译师引入西藏、经密勒日巴实修证悟并发扬光大的无上瑜伽密续修行体系。它构成了噶举派密法修行的核心框架，也是藏传佛教中最系统化的密续修行方法之一。

The Six Yogas of Naropa (Tibetan: ནཱ་རོ་ཆོས་དྲུག་, nā ro chos drug) is the unsurpassed yoga tantra practice system transmitted by the Indian Mahasiddha Naropa (956–1040), brought to Tibet by Marpa the Translator, and fully realized and propagated by Milarepa. It constitutes the core framework of Kagyu tantric practice and is one of the most systematic tantric practice methods in Tibetan Buddhism.

六法不是六个独立的修行技巧，而是一个有机的整体——每一法都为其他法提供基础，六法共同构成了一条从凡夫到成佛的完整修行道路。

The six yogas are not six independent techniques but an organic whole — each provides the foundation for the others, and together they form a complete path from ordinary being to Buddhahood.

## 传承谱系 Lineage

那洛六法的传承如下：

The lineage of the Six Yogas:

**金刚持 Vajradhara**（法身佛 / Dharmakaya Buddha）
↓
**帝洛巴 Tilopa** (988–1069)（印度大成就者 / Indian Mahasiddha）
↓
**那不洛巴 Naropa** (956–1040)（编纂六法 / Compiled the Six Yogas）
↓
**玛尔巴 Marpa** (1012–1097)（传入西藏 / Brought to Tibet）
↓
**密勒日巴 Milarepa** (1052–1135)（实修证悟 / Realized through practice）
↓
**冈波巴 Gampopa** (1079–1153)（制度化传播 / Institutionalized transmission）

密勒日巴在这一传承中的独特位置在于：他是最纯粹的实修者。玛尔巴是学者-翻译家，冈波巴是学者-医师，而密勒日巴则完全以苦行瑜伽士的身份将六法推向了实修的极致。

Milarepa's unique position in this lineage is that he was the purest practitioner. Marpa was a scholar-translator, Gampopa a scholar-physician; Milarepa alone, as a yogi of pure austerity, pushed the Six Yogas to their practical extreme.

## 六法详解 The Six Yogas in Detail

### 一、拙火 Tummo (Inner Heat)

**藏文**：གཏུམ་མོ་（gtum mo）
**核心**：通过观修气脉明点生起内在热能，引生大乐智慧。

**Tibetan**: གཏུམ་མོ་ (gtum mo)
**Core**: Generating inner heat through channel-wind-drop meditation, giving rise to bliss-wisdom.

拙火是六法的基础和根本。如密勒日巴所说："如果拙火不生，其余五法无从修起。"拙火的修法涉及观想脐下的短"阿"字生起火焰，沿中脉上升，融化顶轮白明点，引生四喜四空的体验。

Tummo is the foundation and root of the Six Yogas. As Milarepa said: "If tummo does not arise, the other five cannot be practiced." The practice involves visualizing the short "A" syllable at the navel igniting into flame, ascending through the central channel, melting the white drops at the crown, generating the experience of four joys and four emptinesses.

拙火的目的是将粗重的气（业气）转化为智慧气，使心安住于明空不二的状态中。这一转化在大手印修持中至关重要。

Tummo's purpose is to transform gross winds (karmic winds) into wisdom winds, enabling mind to rest in the state of clarity-emptiness non-duality. This transformation is crucial for Mahamudra practice.

（详见 [拙火 Tummo](tummo.md)）

### 二、幻身 Gyulu (Illusory Body)

**藏文**：སྒྱུ་ལུས་（sgyu lus）
**核心**：认识到一切现象——包括自己的身体——如幻如梦，从而超越对实有的执着。

**Tibetan**: སྒྱུ་ལུས་ (sgyu lus)
**Core**: Recognizing all phenomena — including one's own body — as illusory and dreamlike, thereby transcending grasping at solidity.

幻身修法包含两个层次：

Illusory body practice has two levels:

**不净幻身 Impure Illusory Body**：在定中观想自己为本尊身，认识到此本尊身虽有形相但无实体，如镜中映象。行者进而将此认知扩展到一切日常体验——看到的一切如幻化、听到的一切如回声。

**Impure Illusory Body**: In meditation, visualize oneself as the deity's form, recognizing that this form, though appearing, has no substance, like a reflection in a mirror. The practitioner extends this recognition to all daily experience — everything seen is like illusion, everything heard is like echo.

**清净幻身 Pure Illusory Body**：在气入中脉后，由最微细的气（持命气）所成的光明之身。这是成佛后利益众生的色身基础。

**Pure Illusory Body**: After the winds enter the central channel, a luminous body composed of the most subtle wind (life-sustaining wind). This is the basis for the form body that benefits beings after Buddhahood.

密勒日巴在道歌中以"水中月"、"镜中像"和"梦境"来比喻幻身的体验。

Milarepa used metaphors of "moon in water," "reflection in a mirror," and "dream" to describe the illusory body experience in his songs.

### 三、光明 Ösel (Clear Light)

**藏文**：འོད་གསལ་（'od gsal）
**核心**：体验心性最深层的光明——超越一切概念的明空不二。

**Tibetan**: འོད་གསལ་ ('od gsal)
**Core**: Experiencing the deepest luminosity of mind's nature — the unity of clarity and emptiness beyond all concepts.

光明分为两种：

Clear light is divided into two types:

**譬喻光明 Example Clear Light**：通过修行方法（如拙火）所引生的类似光明的体验，是真正光明的"预演"。

**Example Clear Light**: A semblance of clear light experience generated through practice methods (such as tummo), a "preview" of genuine clear light.

**实义光明 Actual Clear Light**：在死亡过程中或深度禅定中自然显现的心性光明。这是最究竟的实相体验——一切概念活动完全止息，心性的本来光明赤裸裸地显现。

**Actual Clear Light**: Mind's luminosity naturally manifesting during the death process or deep meditation. This is the most ultimate experience of reality — all conceptual activity completely ceases, and mind's innate luminosity appears nakedly.

密勒日巴被认为是少数能够在今生就完全证悟实义光明的修行者之一。他在道歌中将光明描述为"超越日月的光"、"不生不灭的明"。

Milarepa is considered one of the rare practitioners who fully realized actual clear light in this very life. In his songs, he describes clear light as "light surpassing sun and moon," "luminosity unborn and unceasing."

### 四、梦瑜伽 Milam (Dream Yoga)

**藏文**：རྨི་ལམ་（rmi lam）
**核心**：在梦中保持觉知，认识梦的幻化本质，进而将梦转化为修行的道场。

**Tibetan**: རྨི་ལམ་ (rmi lam)
**Core**: Maintaining awareness in dreams, recognizing the illusory nature of dreams, and transforming dreams into a field of practice.

梦瑜伽的修持分为几个阶段：

Dream yoga practice proceeds through several stages:

1. **认梦 Recognizing the dream**：在梦中意识到"我在做梦"（类似现代的清明梦/lucid dreaming）。
2. **转梦 Transforming the dream**：在梦中改变梦境——变大变小、变化形象、飞行等。
3. **悟梦 Realizing the dream**：认识到醒时体验与梦没有本质区别——两者都是心的投射。
4. **超越梦 Transcending the dream**：最终超越梦与非梦的二元对立，安住于无梦的光明睡眠中。

1. **Recognizing the dream**: Becoming aware "I am dreaming" while dreaming (similar to modern lucid dreaming).
2. **Transforming the dream**: Changing the dream content — growing large or small, changing forms, flying, etc.
3. **Realizing the dream**: Recognizing that waking experience is no different in essence from dreaming — both are projections of mind.
4. **Transcending the dream**: Ultimately transcending the duality of dream and non-dream, resting in dreamless clear-light sleep.

密勒日巴的道歌中经常提到梦瑜伽，他将整个轮回比作一场大梦：

Milarepa's songs frequently reference dream yoga, comparing all of samsara to a great dream:

> "醒时如梦，梦时如醒。
> 梦醒不二时，即是证悟。"

> "Waking is like dreaming, dreaming like waking.
> When dream and waking are non-dual — that is realization."

### 五、迁识 Phowa (Consciousness Transference)

**藏文**：འཕོ་བ་（'pho ba）
**核心**：在死亡时刻将意识转移到净土或更高的存在状态。

**Tibetan**: འཕོ་བ་ ('pho ba)
**Core**: Transferring consciousness to a pure land or higher state of existence at the moment of death.

迁识法是六法中唯一可以在没有完全证悟的情况下使用的"保险"法门。行者通过修行，在生前反复练习将意识从顶门射出、融入上师或本尊的心间。这样，在真正死亡时，行者能够自主地将意识导向解脱。

Phowa is the only one of the Six Yogas that serves as an "insurance" practice usable without complete realization. The practitioner repeatedly trains during life to eject consciousness through the crown aperture and merge it with the guru's or deity's heart. Thus, at actual death, the practitioner can autonomously direct consciousness toward liberation.

迁识法有多种层次：

Phowa has multiple levels:

- **法身迁识**：将意识直接融入法界空性（最高层次）。
- **报身迁识**：将意识转移到净土（如阿弥陀佛极乐世界）。
- **化身迁识**：选择有利的转世以继续修行。

- **Dharmakaya phowa**: Merging consciousness directly with the expanse of emptiness (highest level).
- **Sambhogakaya phowa**: Transferring consciousness to a pure land (such as Amitabha's Sukhavati).
- **Nirmanakaya phowa**: Choosing a favorable rebirth to continue practice.

密勒日巴传记中记载，他能够自如地运用迁识法，甚至帮助他人进行迁识。

Milarepa's biography records that he could employ phowa at will, even assisting others in consciousness transference.

### 六、中阴 Bardo (Intermediate State)

**藏文**：བར་དོ་（bar do）
**核心**：在死亡与转世之间的中阴阶段中保持觉知，实现解脱。

**Tibetan**: བར་དོ་ (bar do)
**Core**: Maintaining awareness during the intermediate state between death and rebirth, achieving liberation.

中阴教法描述了死亡后意识经历的三个阶段：

Bardo teachings describe three stages of post-death consciousness:

**法性中阴 Chönyid Bardo**：死后最初阶段，心性的光明（实义光明）自然显现。如果行者生前已通过光明修法证悟了这一光明，就能在此刻获得法身佛果。

**Chönyid Bardo**: The initial post-death stage, where mind's luminosity (actual clear light) naturally manifests. If the practitioner has realized this luminosity through clear light practice in life, dharmakaya Buddhahood can be attained at this moment.

**投生中阴 Sidpa Bardo**：如果未能在法性中阴中获得解脱，意识进入投生阶段，各种幻象和声音出现，驱使意识趋向新的投生。行者需要识别这些幻象的本质，避免被业力牵引。

**Sidpa Bardo**: If liberation was not achieved in the chönyid bardo, consciousness enters the rebirth stage, where various visions and sounds appear, driving consciousness toward new rebirth. The practitioner must recognize the nature of these visions and avoid being drawn by karmic forces.

**受生中阴**：最终投生于六道之一。

**Rebirth bardo**: Ultimate rebirth in one of the six realms.

密勒日巴的中阴教法强调：中阴的体验与生前禅修的体验本质相同——如果生前能够认识心性的光明，死后中阴中也能认识同样的光明。

Milarepa's bardo teachings emphasize: bardo experiences are essentially identical to meditative experiences in life — if one can recognize mind's luminosity in life, one can recognize the same luminosity in the death bardo.

## 六法的整体框架 Integrated Framework

六法构成了一个有机的修行系统：

The Six Yogas form an organic practice system:

```
拙火 (Tummo) ← 能量基础 / Energetic foundation
  ↓
幻身 (Illusory Body) ← 对治实有执着 / Counteracting grasping at solidity
  ↓
光明 (Clear Light) ← 证悟实相 / Realizing ultimate reality
  ↓
梦瑜伽 (Dream Yoga) ← 贯穿日夜的修行 / Practice permeating day and night
  ↓
迁识 (Phowa) ← 死亡的保险 / Insurance for death
  ↓
中阴 (Bardo) ← 中阴的解脱 / Liberation in the intermediate state
```

密勒日巴将这六法比喻为一棵大树：拙火是根，幻身是干，光明是果，梦瑜伽是枝，迁识是花，中阴是种子——每一法都孕育着下一法。

Milarepa compared the Six Yogas to a great tree: tummo is the root, illusory body the trunk, clear light the fruit, dream yoga the branches, phowa the flowers, bardo the seeds — each yoga giving rise to the next.

## 与密勒日巴其他概念的关系 Relation to Other Milarepa Concepts

- **大手印**：六法是达到大手印证悟的密续方法；大手印是六法的终极目标。
- **苦行转化**：六法为密勒日巴的极端苦行提供了理论基础和技术框架。
- **道歌**：密勒日巴在道歌中以诗歌形式传授六法的修行体验和指引。

- **Mahamudra**: The Six Yogas are tantric methods for achieving Mahamudra realization; Mahamudra is the ultimate goal of the Six Yogas.
- **Ascetic transformation**: The Six Yogas provided the theoretical basis and technical framework for Milarepa's extreme austerity.
- **Spiritual songs**: Milarepa transmitted Six Yogas practice experiences and instructions in poetic form through his songs.
