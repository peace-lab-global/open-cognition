# 五种正行 The Five Primary Practices

> "一心专读诵此《观经》《弥陀经》《无量寿经》等；一心专注思想、观察、忆念彼国二报庄严；一心专敬供养阿弥陀佛；一心专称彼佛名号；一心专赞叹供养彼佛。"——善导《观经四帖疏》
> "Single-mindedly and exclusively recite the Contemplation Sūtra, the Amitābha Sūtra, and the Larger Sukhāvatīvyūha Sūtra; single-mindedly focus on contemplating, observing, and recollecting the庄严 adornments of the two rewards of that land; single-mindedly reverently make offerings to Amitābha; single-mindedly recite that Buddha's Name; single-mindedly praise and make offerings to that Buddha." — Shandao, Commentary on the Contemplation Sūtra

---

## 定义 Definition

五种正行是善导在《观经四帖疏》中提出的净土修行分类体系。"正行"（zhèngxíng）指专门指向阿弥陀佛和极乐世界的修行，与之相对的是"杂行"（záxíng）——指向其他佛菩萨或其他目标的修行。善导将正行分为五种，并在其中进一步区分了"正定业"（正业、决定往生之业）和"助业"（辅助的修行），形成了一个层次分明的修行架构。

The Five Primary Practices is a classification system for Pure Land practice proposed by Shandao in his Commentary on the Contemplation Sūtra. "Primary practices" (zhèngxíng) are those specifically directed toward Amitābha Buddha and the Pure Land of Ultimate Bliss, as opposed to "miscellaneous practices" (záxíng) — those directed toward other Buddhas, bodhisattvas, or other goals. Shandao divided primary practices into five types, further distinguishing within them between the "rightly established practice" (zhèngdìng yè — the practice that assuredly leads to rebirth) and "auxiliary practices" (zhùyè — supporting practices), forming a clearly layered practice architecture.

## 五种正行详述 The Five in Detail

### 第一、读诵正行 Primary Practice of Recitation (Scriptural Reading)

专门读诵净土三部经：《无量寿经》（Larger Sukhāvatīvyūha Sūtra）、《观无量寿佛经》（Contemplation Sūtra）、《阿弥陀经》（Smaller Sukhāvatīvyūha Sūtra）。善导强调"一心专读诵"——不是广泛涉猎各种经典，而是专注于净土根本经典。这种专注的读诵有助于行者深入理解净土法门，增强信心和愿心。

This involves exclusively reciting the three fundamental Pure Land sūtras: the Larger Sukhāvatīvyūha Sūtra, the Contemplation Sūtra, and the Smaller Sukhāvatīvyūha Sūtra. Shandao emphasized "single-minded exclusive recitation" — not broadly studying various sūtras, but focusing on the foundational Pure Land texts. This focused recitation helps practitioners deeply understand the Pure Land Dharma gate and strengthen their faith and aspiration.

### 第二、观察正行 Primary Practice of Contemplation

专门观想极乐世界的种种庄严——包括阿弥陀佛的相好光明、极乐世界的七宝庄严、观音和大势至两位菩萨的殊胜形相，以及其他种种微妙景象。这一修行源自《观经》的十六种观想法门，但善导将其归入"助业"，而非"正定业"。

This involves specifically contemplating the various庄严 adornments of the Pure Land — including the auspicious marks and radiance of Amitābha Buddha, the seven-treasure adornments of Sukhāvatī, the magnificent forms of the bodhisattvas Avalokiteśvara and Mahāsthāmaprāpta, and other subtle and wondrous scenes. This practice derives from the sixteen contemplation methods in the Contemplation Sūtra, but Shandao classified it as an "auxiliary practice," not the "rightly established practice."

### 第三、礼拜正行 Primary Practice of Prostration

专门礼拜阿弥陀佛。善导认为，身业（身体的行为）应当与口业（言语的行为）和意业（心意的活动）统一，都指向阿弥陀佛。礼拜不仅是一种身体动作，更是恭敬心和归依心的外在表达。

This involves exclusively prostrating to Amitābha Buddha. Shandao held that the karma of body (physical action) should be unified with the karma of speech (verbal action) and the karma of mind (mental activity), all directed toward Amitābha. Prostration is not merely a physical gesture, but the outward expression of reverence and taking refuge.

### 第四、称名正行 Primary Practice of Verbal Recitation (Nianfo) ★正定业

专门称念"南无阿弥陀佛"。这是五种正行中唯一被善导标为"正定业"的修行。善导的理由是："望佛本愿"——阿弥陀佛的第十八愿明确指定了称名为往生的正因，因此称名是最直接、最确定地乘托佛力的修行方式。

This involves exclusively reciting "Namo Amituofo." This is the only practice among the five that Shandao designated as the "rightly established practice" (zhèngdìng yè). Shandao's reasoning is: "looking to the Buddha's Original Vow" — Amitābha's Eighteenth Vow explicitly designates recitation as the cause of rebirth, making it the most direct and assured way of relying on the Buddha's power.

善导对此有一段经典的论述：

Shandao offered a classic exposition on this:

"上来虽说定散两门之益，望佛本愿，意在众生，一向专称弥陀佛名。"
"Although the benefits of both the settled and scattered gates have been explained above, looking to the Buddha's Original Vow, the intent is for beings to single-mindedly and exclusively recite Amitābha Buddha's Name."

这句话被后世净土学者称为"废立之文"——是善导"废"观想等行、"立"称名念佛的关键性宣言。

This passage is known to later Pure Land scholars as the "text of setting aside and establishing" — Shandao's key declaration "setting aside" contemplative and other practices and "establishing" verbal nianfo as paramount.

### 第五、赞叹供养正行 Primary Practice of Praise and Offerings

专门赞叹阿弥陀佛的功德、供养阿弥陀佛。这包括唱赞佛偈、以香花灯烛等供养佛像，以及以恭敬心忆念佛的功德。

This involves exclusively praising Amitābha Buddha's virtues and making offerings to Amitābha. This includes singing hymns of praise, offering incense, flowers, lamps, and candles to Buddha images, and recollecting the Buddha's virtues with reverence.

## 正定业与助业 Rightly Established Practice and Auxiliary Practices

善导在五种正行中做了一个关键的区分：

Shandao made a crucial distinction within the five primary practices:

| 类别 Category | 修行 Practice | 地位 Status |
|---|---|---|
| **正定业** Rightly Established | 称名念佛 Verbal Nianfo | 决定往生的根本修行 / Assured cause of rebirth |
| **助业** Auxiliary | 读诵、观察、礼拜、赞叹供养 | 辅助称名的支持修行 / Supporting practices for nianfo |

助业的作用是帮助行者建立和增强信心，引导行者归入称名念佛的正定业。善导用了一个比喻：助业如渡河前的准备工作（如造船、备桨），正定业如实际渡河。一旦上了船（称名），准备工作（助业）自然包含其中，但真正渡河的是船（称名）本身。

The role of auxiliary practices is to help practitioners build and strengthen faith, guiding them toward the rightly established practice of verbal nianfo. Shandao used an analogy: auxiliary practices are like preparations before crossing a river (building a boat, preparing oars), while the rightly established practice is the actual crossing. Once aboard the boat (nianfo), the preparations (auxiliary practices) are naturally included, but what actually crosses the river is the boat (nianfo) itself.

## 杂行 Miscellaneous Practices

与正行相对，善导将不属于五种正行的一切修行都归为"杂行"。杂行包括：读诵非净土经典、观想非阿弥陀佛的佛土、礼拜非阿弥陀佛的其他佛菩萨、修习非净土导向的禅定和义学等。

In contrast to primary practices, Shandao classified all practices not belonging to the five primary practices as "miscellaneous practices" (záxíng). These include: reciting non-Pure Land sūtras, contemplating Buddha-lands other than Amitābha's, prostrating to Buddhas and bodhisattvas other than Amitābha, and practicing meditation and doctrinal study not oriented toward the Pure Land.

善导并没有完全否定杂行的价值——杂行本身也是善法，但与净土往生的目标关系较远。他建议行者"舍杂行归正行"——将有限的精力集中在最直接、最有效的修行上。

Shandao did not entirely negate the value of miscellaneous practices — they are in themselves wholesome dharmas — but their relationship to the goal of Pure Land rebirth is more distant. He advised practitioners to "set aside miscellaneous practices and return to primary practices" — concentrating limited energy on the most direct and effective practice.

## 五种正行的当代实践 Contemporary Practice of the Five Primary Practices

在现代汉传佛教的净土修行中，五种正行仍然被广泛遵循。典型的净土行者日常修行包括：

In modern Chinese Buddhist Pure Land practice, the five primary practices continue to be widely followed. A typical Pure Land practitioner's daily practice includes:

- **晨课**：读诵《阿弥陀经》（读诵正行）→ 念佛（称名正行）→ 回向发愿
- **Morning liturgy**: Recite the Amitābha Sūtra (primary recitation) → Nianfo (primary verbal recitation) → Dedicate and aspire
- **日间**：随时念佛（称名正行），见佛像即礼拜（礼拜正行）
- **Daytime**: Nianfo at any time (primary verbal recitation), prostrate upon seeing Buddha images (primary prostration)
- **晚课**：念佛、赞佛偈（赞叹供养正行）、回向
- **Evening liturgy**: Nianfo, hymns of praise (primary praise and offerings), dedication

这种将五种正行融入日常生活的修行模式，使得净土法门成为最适合忙碌现代人修行的佛教道路之一。

This model of integrating the five primary practices into daily life makes the Pure Land Dharma gate one of the most suitable Buddhist paths for busy modern practitioners.

## 与日本净土宗的关系 Relationship with Japanese Pure Land Schools

善导的五种正行分类对日本净土宗产生了决定性影响。法然在《选择本愿念佛集》中全面继承了善导的"正行杂行"判分和"正定业"概念，并进一步简化为"选择本愿念佛"——即阿弥陀佛在四十八愿中"选择"了称名念佛作为往生的正因。

Shandao's classification of five primary practices exerted a decisive influence on Japanese Pure Land schools. Hōnen fully inherited Shandao's "primary and miscellaneous" classification and "rightly established practice" concept in his Senchaku Hongan Nenbutsu Shū, further simplifying it to "the selected Original Vow nianfo" — that Amitābha "selected" verbal nianfo from among the forty-eight vows as the cause of rebirth.

亲鸾则在法然的基础上进一步，认为在末法时代，五种正行中只有称名念佛是可行的，其他四种助业（读诵、观察、礼拜、赞叹供养）也已经不再必要——因为信心一旦建立，称名便自然流出，无需其他辅助。

Shinran went further than Hōnen, holding that in the Dharma-Ending Age, only verbal nianfo among the five primary practices is viable, and the other four auxiliary practices (recitation, contemplation, prostration, praise and offerings) are no longer necessary — because once faith is established, nianfo naturally flows forth without need for other supports.
