# 荣西 Yōsai (1141-1215)

> "禅是护国之法宝，镇护国家、利益众生之无上法门。"——《兴禅护国论》
>
> "Zen is the supreme dharma for protecting the nation, the peerless method for guarding the state and benefiting all beings." — *Kōzen Gokoku-ron*

---

## 基本信息 / Basic Information

**荣西**（1141年—1215年），日本镰仓时代僧人，日本**临济宗**创立者。备中国（今冈山县）人，少年出家于比叡山延历寺，学习天台宗与密教。曾于1168年和1187年两度入宋求法，最终从临济宗黄龙派虚庵怀敞禅师处得法印可，将纯正的临济禅引入日本。他还把宋代的饮茶习俗与茶种带回日本，著有《吃茶养生记》，被誉为"日本茶祖"。其根本道场为京都建仁寺，是日本院最早兼修禅、天台、密教三宗的禅寺。

**Yōsai** (1141–1215) was a monk of Japan's Kamakura period and founder of the Japanese **Rinzai Zen** school. Born in Bitchū Province (present-day Okayama), he entered monastic life young at Enryaku-ji on Mount Hiei, studying Tendai doctrine and esoteric Buddhism (Mikkyō). He traveled to Song China twice — in 1168 and 1187 — and ultimately received dharma transmission from the Rinzai master Kian Ejō (虚庵怀敞) of the Ōryū (黄龙) lineage, bringing authentic Linji Chan to Japan. He also introduced Song-dynasty tea culture and tea seeds, authoring the *Kissa Yojoki* (Treatise on Drinking Tea for Health), and is honored as the "Father of Tea in Japan." His head temple, Kennin-ji in Kyoto, was the earliest Japanese Zen monastery to integrate Zen, Tendai, and esoteric practice.

## 核心命题 / Core Propositions

1. **兴禅护国 Kōzen Gokoku (Zen Protects the Nation)**：禅宗是护持国家的最高法门；推广禅修能带来社会安定与精神觉醒。
   Zen is the supreme method for protecting the nation; promoting meditation brings social stability and spiritual awakening.

2. **茶禅一味 Sa-Zen Ichimi (Tea and Zen Are One Taste)**：饮茶不仅是养生手段，更是禅修的辅助与延伸。
   Tea drinking is not merely a health practice but an aid and extension of Zen meditation.

3. **看话禅 Kanna Zen (Kōan-Introspection Zen)**：以公案参究为禅修核心方法，通过"大疑"达至"大悟"。
   Kōan introspection as the core method of Zen practice, reaching great awakening through great doubt.

4. **兼修立场 Kenshū Richiba (Combined Practice)**：禅、天台、密教三宗融合并修，各取所长。
   The integration of Zen, Tendai, and esoteric Buddhism, each complementing the others.

5. **吃茶养生 Kissa Yōjō (Tea Drinking for Health)**：茶是"末法时代"养生延命的妙药，也是修行者保持清醒的工具。
   Tea is the wondrous medicine for health and longevity in the Age of Dharma Decline, and a tool for practitioners to maintain alertness.

## 思想脉络 / Intellectual Trajectory

### 比叡山时期 / Mount Hiei Period (1154-1168)

荣西十四岁（1154年）登比叡山出家，受天台宗系统训练。比叡山作为日本佛教的学术中心，为他提供了广泛的知识基础——涵盖天台教观、密教仪轨、戒律学。然而，他也深感当时日本佛教的危机：贵族化、形式化、修行精神的衰退。这一时期，他听闻中国禅宗的兴盛，萌发了入宋求法的志愿。

Yōsai was ordained on Mount Hiei at age fourteen (1154), receiving systematic Tendai training. As the academic center of Japanese Buddhism, Hiei provided him a broad intellectual foundation spanning Tendai doctrine, esoteric ritual, and vinaya studies. Yet he keenly sensed a crisis in Japanese Buddhism: aristocratization, formalism, and the decline of genuine practice. Hearing reports of flourishing Chan Buddhism in China, he conceived the ambition to travel to Song dynasty China.

### 第一次入宋 / First Journey to China (1168)

1168年，荣西随商船渡海至明州（今宁波），在天台山和阿育王寺参学数月。此次行程虽短，但他带回了天台宗新译经典三十余部，对日本天台教学产生了重要影响。更重要的是，他初步接触了当时蓬勃发展的禅宗丛林制度，种下了日后再次入宋的种子。

In 1168, Yōsai crossed to Mingzhou (present-day Ningbo) on a merchant vessel, studying for several months at Mount Tiantai and Ayuwang Monastery. Though brief, this trip yielded over thirty newly translated Tiantai texts that significantly influenced Japanese Tendai scholarship. More importantly, he was first exposed to the thriving Chan monastic system, planting the seed for his later, deeper engagement.

### 第二次入宋与得法 / Second Journey and Dharma Transmission (1187-1191)

1187年，荣西再赴中国，本意是经中国前往印度朝圣，但因边境管制未能成行。他转而遍参禅林名刹，最终在天台山万年寺遇到临济宗黄龙派的虚庵怀敞禅师。虚庵是当时最具声望的禅师之一，以严格的看话禅风著称。荣西在虚庵座下参学四年（1187-1191），精参公案，终于获得"大悟"的印可，受传临济宗法脉及袈裟、宝瓶等法具。

虚庵对荣西的嘱托极具远见："此宗（禅宗）在东土（日本）未传，汝当归国弘通，利乐有情。"这一嘱托成为荣西后半生的使命。

In 1187, Yōsai returned to China, originally intending to travel onward to India for pilgrimage. Border restrictions thwarted this plan, and he instead visited renowned Chan monasteries. At Wannian-si on Mount Tiantai, he met the master Kian Ejō (虚庵怀敞) of the Ōryū lineage, one of the most respected Chan masters of the era, renowned for his rigorous kōan-introspection teaching. Yōsai studied under him for four years (1187–1191), intensively working on kōans until receiving certification of great awakening along with dharma transmission, robes, and ritual implements of the Linji lineage.

Kian's charge was prophetic: "This school has not yet been transmitted to your eastern land. Return home and propagate it for the benefit of all beings." This commission became Yōsai's life mission.

### 回国弘禅与建仁寺 / Return to Japan and Kennin-ji (1191-1215)

1191年归国后，荣西先在博多（今福冈）开创圣福寺，这是日本第一座纯粹的禅宗寺院。随后，他将活动重心移至京都。1202年，在幕府将军源赖家的支持下，荣西在京都创建建仁寺。建仁寺的独特之处在于其"三宗兼学"的体制——禅宗、天台宗、密教三宗并修，反映了荣西一贯的兼容并蓄立场。

此举也是政治智慧的表现。当时比叡山天台宗势力强大，对"纯禅"抱有强烈敌意。荣西通过兼修体制化解对立，同时确保禅宗在日本佛教格局中站稳脚跟。

荣西还积极推广饮茶习俗，将从中国带回的茶种种植于京都栂尾山的高山寺（后移植至宇治等地），并撰写《吃茶养生记》献给将军源实朝，以茶的养生功效说服上层社会接受饮茶。

Returning in 1191, Yōsai first established Shōfuku-ji in Hakata (present-day Fukuoka), Japan's first purely Zen monastery. He then shifted his base to Kyoto, where in 1202, with the patronage of Shogun Minamoto no Yoriie, he founded Kennin-ji. Kennin-ji was distinctive for its "three-school combined study" system — Zen, Tendai, and esoteric Buddhism coexisting — reflecting Yōsai's integrative stance.

This was also political wisdom. Mount Hiei's Tendai establishment was powerful and hostile to "pure Zen." Through the combined-practice system, Yōsai neutralized opposition while securing Zen's foothold in Japanese Buddhism.

Yōsai also vigorously promoted tea drinking, planting Chinese tea seeds at Kōzan-ji on Mount Togano'o near Kyoto (later transplanted to Uji and other regions), and presented the *Kissa Yojoki* to Shogun Minamoto no Sanetomo, convincing the elite of tea's health benefits.

### 晚年与《兴禅护国论》 / Final Years and the Kōzen Gokoku-ron (1211-1215)

面对来自天台宗和真言宗学僧的猛烈攻击——他们指责禅宗"不立文字"是邪说、"教外别传"非正统——荣西于1211年撰写了《兴禅护国论》三卷，以经论为据，系统论证禅宗的正当性与必要性。这部著作是日本禅宗史上最重要的护教文献，确立了禅宗在日本的理论基础。

1215年，荣西在建仁寺圆寂，享年七十五岁。后世尊称"千光祖师"。

Facing fierce criticism from Tendai and Shingon scholars — who attacked Zen's "no reliance on words" as heterodoxy and its "transmission outside the scriptures" as illegitimate — Yōsai composed the three-volume *Kōzen Gokoku-ron* (Treatise on Promoting Zen for the Protection of the Nation) in 1211. Drawing on scriptural authority, it systematically defended Zen's legitimacy and necessity. This work remains the most important apologetic text in Japanese Zen history, establishing Zen's theoretical foundation in Japan.

Yōsai passed away at Kennin-ji in 1215, aged seventy-five. He was posthumously honored as "Senkō Koso" (千光祖师, Patriarch of a Thousand Lights).

## 与镰仓幕府的关系 / Relationship with the Kamakura Shogunate

荣西的弘禅事业与镰仓幕府的政治需求形成了深刻的历史共振。理解这一关系，对于把握荣西思想的社会学维度至关重要。

Yōsai's Zen-promotion project resonated profoundly with the political needs of the Kamakura shogunate. Understanding this relationship is essential for grasping the sociological dimension of Yōsai's thought.

### 武士阶层的精神需求 / Spiritual Needs of the Warrior Class

镰仓幕府的建立（1185年）标志着日本政治权力从京都贵族转移到武士阶层。新兴的武士统治者面临一个迫切的问题：他们缺乏自己的精神传统。京都的天台宗和真言宗与旧贵族势力盘根错节，不能成为武士阶层的精神支柱。禅宗——尤其是其强调的自律、直截、面对死亡的勇气——恰好契合了武士的精神需要。

The establishment of the Kamakura shogunate (1185) marked the transfer of political power from Kyoto aristocrats to the warrior class. The new military rulers faced an urgent problem: they lacked their own spiritual tradition. The Tendai and Shingon schools of Kyoto were deeply intertwined with the old aristocratic power structure and could not serve as spiritual pillars for the warrior class. Zen — with its emphasis on discipline, directness, and courage in facing death — met the warriors' spiritual needs precisely.

### 荣西的政治智慧 / Yōsai's Political Acumen

荣西敏锐地意识到这一历史机遇。他的策略包含三个层面：

Yōsai keenly recognized this historical opportunity. His strategy operated on three levels:

1. **理论层面——"兴禅护国"**：通过论证禅宗能保护国家，他使禅宗从"外来宗教"转变为"有利于国家的法门"，获得了政治合法性。
   Theoretical level — "Promoting Zen Protects the Nation": by arguing that Zen safeguards the state, he transformed Zen from a "foreign religion" into a "dharma that benefits the nation," gaining political legitimacy.

2. **制度层面——"三宗兼学"**：建仁寺的兼修体制使禅宗不至于被传统佛教势力视为威胁，同时为禅宗在日本佛教格局中争取到一席之地。
   Institutional level — "Three-School Combined Study": Kennin-ji's combined-practice system prevented Zen from being perceived as a threat by established Buddhist institutions while securing its place in Japan's religious landscape.

3. **文化层面——茶道引入**：通过推广饮茶，荣西在武士贵族中建立了一种与禅宗相关联的精致生活方式，使禅宗获得了文化吸引力。
   Cultural level — Tea introduction: by promoting tea drinking, Yōsai established among warrior elites a refined lifestyle associated with Zen, giving the school cultural appeal.

### 三代将军的支持 / Patronage of Three Generations of Shoguns

- **源赖朝** (Minamoto no Yoritomo, 1147-1199): 幕府创始者，对荣西的禅宗持开放态度
  Founder of the shogunate; open to Yōsai's Zen

- **源赖家** (Minamoto no Yoriie, 1182-1204): 支持荣西创建建仁寺（1202年）
  Supported Yōsai's founding of Kennin-ji (1202)

- **源实朝** (Minamoto no Sanetomo, 1192-1219): 接受荣西献茶和《吃茶养生记》，推动饮茶风气
  Received Yōsai's tea presentation and the *Kissa Yojoki*, promoting tea culture

## 文化遗产 / Cultural Legacy

荣西的影响远超宗教领域，深刻塑造了日本文化的基本面貌：

Yōsai's influence extended far beyond religion, profoundly shaping the fundamental character of Japanese culture:

- **茶道**：从荣西引入茶种和饮茶习俗，到后世千利休确立"和敬清寂"茶道四规，日本茶道的精神脉络可追溯至荣西。茶道成为日本最具代表性的文化实践之一。
  Tea ceremony: from Yōsai's introduction of tea seeds and customs to Sen no Rikyū's establishment of the four principles (harmony, reverence, purity, tranquility), the spiritual lineage of Japanese tea ceremony traces back to Yōsai. *Chadō* became one of Japan's most representative cultural practices.

- **禅寺建筑**：建仁寺开创了日本禅寺的建筑范式——"七堂伽蓝"（山门、佛殿、法堂、僧堂、库院、东司、浴室），这一格局影响了此后所有的日本禅寺设计。
  Zen architecture: Kennin-ji pioneered the Japanese Zen monastery architectural paradigm — the "seven-hall compound" (gate, Buddha hall, Dharma hall, monks' hall, kitchen, lavatory, bathhouse) — influencing all subsequent Japanese Zen temple design.

- **禅与艺术**：荣西所开启的禅宗传统催生了枯山水庭园、水墨画、茶道花道等一系列独具特色的日本艺术形式。这些艺术形式的共同特征——极简、留白、当下性——根源于禅宗的精神。
  Zen and the arts: the Zen tradition inaugurated by Yōsai gave rise to dry-landscape gardens, ink painting, tea and flower arrangement, and other distinctly Japanese art forms. Their shared characteristics — minimalism, empty space, presentness — are rooted in Zen spirit.

- **日本饮食文化**：荣西引入的抹茶制备法成为日本茶道的核心技术。此外，禅寺的"精进料理"（素食）传统也通过荣西一系的禅寺传播至日本社会。
  Japanese food culture: the matcha preparation method Yōsai introduced became the core technique of Japanese tea ceremony. Additionally, the Zen monastic tradition of *shōjin ryōri* (vegetarian cuisine) spread to Japanese society through Yōsai-lineage temples.

- **武术与禅**：虽然这一关联在荣西时代尚未明确建立，但后来日本武道（剑道、弓道等）与禅修的深层联系，其精神源头之一可追溯至荣西所引入的看话禅传统。
  Martial arts and Zen: although this connection was not yet clearly established in Yōsai's era, the deep link between later Japanese martial arts (kendo, kyudo, etc.) and Zen practice traces one of its spiritual origins to the Kanna Zen tradition Yōsai introduced.

## 思想坐标 / Intellectual Coordinates

荣西占据一个独特的思想坐标位置：

Yōsai occupies a unique position in the intellectual landscape:

- **传播者 Transmitter**：将中国临济禅系统传入日本的第一人，是日本禅宗法脉的源头。
  The first to systematically transmit Chinese Linji Chan to Japan; the source of Japanese Zen dharma lineage.

- **综合者 Synthesizer**：不是简单移植中国禅，而是创造性地将禅与日本既有的天台、密教传统融合。
  Not a mere importer of Chinese Chan, but a creative synthesizer who integrated Zen with Japan's existing Tendai and esoteric traditions.

- **文化塑造者 Culture-Shaper**：通过引入茶道、建立禅寺制度、推动禅与艺术的结合，深刻塑造了日本文化的基本面貌。
  By introducing tea culture, establishing Zen monastic institutions, and promoting the union of Zen and the arts, he profoundly shaped the fundamental character of Japanese culture.

- **护教者 Apologist**：面对来自传统佛教势力的批判，以学术论证捍卫禅宗的合法性。
  Defending Zen's legitimacy through scholarly argumentation against attacks from established Buddhist institutions.

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

- **茶文化 Tea Culture**：荣西是日本茶道的奠基人，其《吃茶养生记》是茶文化的元典。当代日本茶道（里千家、表千家、武者小路千家三千家流派）的精神源头均可追溯至荣西。每年在京都建仁寺举行的"四头茶会"仍以宋代禅林茶礼为范本，延续着荣西引入的茶禅传统。在全球范围内，日本茶道已成为东方文化的象征之一，其根源在于荣西赋予饮茶的修行意义。
  Yōsai is the foundational figure of the Japanese Way of Tea; his *Kissa Yojoki* is the proto-text of tea culture. The spiritual origins of contemporary Japanese tea schools (the three Sen houses: Urasenke, Omotesenke, Mushakōjisenke) all trace back to Yōsai. The annual "Yotsugashira Chakai" (Four-Head Tea Gathering) held at Kennin-ji still follows the model of Song-dynasty Chan monastic tea etiquette, continuing the tea-zen tradition Yōsai introduced. Globally, Japanese tea ceremony has become one of the icons of Eastern culture, its roots lying in the contemplative significance Yōsai bestowed upon tea drinking.

- **心理学 Psychology**：看话禅的公案参究与当代正念 (mindfulness)、认知解构技术形成有趣对话。公案所要求的"大疑情"——一种对根本问题的全身心投入——与存在主义心理治疗中对"终极关怀"（死亡、自由、孤独、无意义）的直面有深层共鸣。同时，坐禅中的注意力训练已被神经科学研究证实能改变大脑的前额叶皮层活动模式。
  The kōan introspection of Kanna Zen creates interesting dialogue with contemporary mindfulness and cognitive deconstruction techniques. The "great doubt" demanded by kōans — a whole-body engagement with fundamental questions — resonates deeply with existential psychotherapy's direct confrontation with "ultimate concerns" (death, freedom, isolation, meaninglessness). Meanwhile, the attentional training in sitting meditation has been confirmed by neuroscience research to alter prefrontal cortex activity patterns.

- **管理学 Leadership**：禅修传统对日本企业管理（"禅的管理学"）影响深远。建仁寺至今提供企业禅修项目，将坐禅、公案参究和茶道引入商业培训。"看话禅"所培养的"突破固有思维框架"的能力，被视为创新领导力的重要素质。稻盛和夫等日本企业家公开承认禅修对其经营哲学的深刻影响。
  The Zen meditation tradition has profoundly influenced Japanese corporate management ("Zen management studies"). Kennin-ji continues to offer corporate Zen training programs, introducing sitting meditation, kōan work, and tea ceremony into business education. The ability to "break through conventional thinking frameworks" cultivated by Kanna Zen is regarded as a crucial quality for innovative leadership. Japanese entrepreneurs such as Kazuo Inamori have publicly acknowledged Zen meditation's deep influence on their management philosophy.

- **医学与养生 Health**：《吃茶养生记》中茶的药用论述与当代茶多酚研究形成科学呼应。现代研究证实绿茶中的EGCG（表没食子儿茶素没食子酸酯）具有强大的抗氧化活性，L-茶氨酸能促进α脑波产生——这些发现与荣西八百年前关于茶"清心安神""调和五脏"的论述高度吻合。荣西所开创的"茶医学"传统在当代日本仍然活跃。
  The medicinal claims in the *Kissa Yojoki* find scientific echoes in contemporary tea polyphenol research. Modern studies confirm that EGCG (epigallocatechin gallate) in green tea possesses powerful antioxidant activity, and L-theanine promotes alpha brain wave production — findings highly consistent with Yōsai's eight-hundred-year-old claims about tea "clearing the heart and calming the spirit" and "harmonizing the five organs." The "tea medicine" tradition Yōsai pioneered remains active in contemporary Japan.

- **艺术 Arts**：禅宗美学——枯山水庭园（以砂石表现山水）、水墨画（以留白表达空性）、茶道花道（以简素体现"侘寂"精神）、书法（以笔墨传达心性）——的精神源头之一可追溯至荣西所引入的临济禅。这些艺术形式共同构成了西方世界对日本文化最鲜明的认知图景。
  Zen aesthetics — dry-landscape gardens (representing mountains and water with sand and stone), ink painting (expressing emptiness through blank space), tea and flower arrangement (embodying wabi-sabi spirit through simplicity), calligraphy (conveying mind-nature through brush and ink) — trace one of their spiritual sources to the Rinzai Zen Yōsai introduced. These art forms collectively constitute the Western world's most vivid image of Japanese culture.

## 常见误读 / Common Misreadings

### 1. "荣西创茶道" / "Yōsai created the Way of Tea"
荣西并非"创造"了茶道，而是将宋代的饮茶习俗引入日本，并赋予其修行意义。后世千利休等人将其发展为成熟的"茶道"艺术。荣西是引入者和推广者，不是原创者。
Yōsai did not "create" the Way of Tea but rather introduced Song-dynasty tea customs to Japan and imbued them with contemplative significance. Later masters like Sen no Rikyū developed it into the mature art of *chadō*. Yōsai was the introducer and promoter, not the originator.

### 2. "荣西是纯禅者" / "Yōsai was a pure Zen practitioner"
与道元的"纯粹禅"立场不同，荣西始终是"兼修禅"——融合天台、密教与禅。将他视为"纯禅"代表是后世临济宗简化叙事的结果。
Unlike Dōgen's "pure Zen" stance, Yōsai always practiced "combined Zen" — integrating Tendai, esoteric Buddhism, and Zen. Representing him as a "pure Zen" figure is a later Rinzai simplification.

### 3. "兴禅护国 = 民族主义" / "Kōzen Gokoku equals nationalism"
"护国"在佛教语境中有特定的宇宙论意义——佛法的正确传播被认为能感得诸天护法的庇佑，从而保护国家。这是一种佛教普世主义的政治表达，不是近代民族主义。
"Protecting the nation" carries specific cosmological meaning in the Buddhist context — proper propagation of the Dharma was believed to invoke the protection of celestial beings, thereby safeguarding the state. This is a political expression of Buddhist universalism, not modern nationalism.

### 4. "看话禅只是猜谜" / "Kanna Zen is just riddle-solving"
公案不是普通谜语或逻辑难题。参究公案是一种全身心投入的修行——需要"大疑情"、"大愤志"，最终超越逻辑思维达到"大悟"。将其等同于智力游戏是对其修行本质的误解。
Kōans are not ordinary riddles or logical puzzles. Working with them requires total engagement — generating "great doubt" and "great determination" — to ultimately transcend discursive thought and attain "great awakening." Equating them with intellectual games misrepresents the nature of the practice.
