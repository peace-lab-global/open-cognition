# 曼荼罗 Mandala (曼荼罗 · Mandara)

> "曼荼罗者，法界之图也。"
> "The maṇḍala is the map of the Dharma Realm."

## 定义 · Definition

**曼荼罗**（梵文：maṇḍala；日文：まんだら，mandara）字面意为"圆形"或"中心"，在密教中特指以图像方式呈现的宇宙秩序和佛菩萨的坛城。曼荼罗不仅是宗教艺术品，更是密教修行的核心工具——修行者通过观想曼荼罗中的佛菩萨、种子字和象征物，将自己的心识与法界实相相融合。

**Maṇḍala** (Sanskrit: maṇḍala; Japanese: mandara) literally means "circle" or "center." In Esoteric Buddhism, it specifically refers to pictorial representations of cosmic order and the sacred assembly of Buddhas and Bodhisattvas. Maṇḍalas are not merely religious artworks but core instruments of esoteric practice — through visualizing the Buddhas, Bodhisattvas, seed syllables, and symbolic objects within the maṇḍala, practitioners fuse their consciousness with the reality of the Dharma Realm.

空海将曼荼罗视为大日如来"身密"的视觉显现。如果说真言是佛的"口密"之声学表达，那么曼荼罗就是佛的"身密"之视觉表达。两者共同构成了密教修行的感官基础。

Kūkai regarded maṇḍalas as the visual manifestation of Mahāvairocana's "Body Mystery." If mantras are the acoustic expression of the Buddha's "Speech Mystery," then maṇḍalas are the visual expression of the Buddha's "Body Mystery." Together, they constitute the sensory foundation of esoteric practice.

## 两界曼荼罗 · The Two Realm Maṇḍalas

空海密教的核心图示系统是**两界曼荼罗**（りょうかいまんだら，ryōkai mandara），由**金刚界曼荼罗**和**胎藏界曼荼罗**组成。这两部曼荼罗分别基于两部根本经典：《金刚顶经》和《大日经》，合称"金胎两部"。

The central representational system of Kūkai's Esoteric Buddhism is the **Two Realm Maṇḍalas** (ryōkai mandara), comprising the **Vajradhātu Maṇḍala** (Diamond Realm) and the **Garbhadhātu Maṇḍala** (Womb Realm). These two maṇḍalas are based respectively on two root scriptures — the *Vajraśekhara Sūtra* and the *Mahāvairocana Sūtra* — collectively known as the "Two Divisions of Vajra and Womb" (kintai ryōbu).

### 金刚界曼荼罗 · Vajradhātu Maṇḍala (Diamond Realm)

**金刚界**（こんごうかい，kongōkai）曼荼罗表现的是大日如来**智慧**的一面。"金刚"（vajra）意为坚固不坏，象征着觉悟的智慧能够摧毁一切烦恼。

The **Vajradhātu** (Diamond Realm) maṇḍala represents the **wisdom** aspect of Mahāvairocana. "Vajra" (diamond/thunderbolt) signifies indestructibility, symbolizing the wisdom of awakening that can destroy all afflictions.

金刚界曼荼罗的核心结构是**九会曼荼罗**，中央为"成身会"，大日如来居中，四方四佛围绕：

The Vajradhātu Maṇḍala's core structure is the **Nine Assemblies Maṇḍala**, with the "Perfected Body Assembly" at center, where Mahāvairocana is surrounded by four Buddhas in the four directions:

- **东方 · 阿閦佛**（Akṣobhya）：大圆镜智 / East: Akṣobhya — Mirror-like Wisdom
- **南方 · 宝生佛**（Ratnasaṃbhava）：平等性智 / South: Ratnasaṃbhava — Wisdom of Equality
- **西方 · 阿弥陀佛**（Amitābha）：妙观察智 / West: Amitābha — Discriminating Wisdom
- **北方 · 不空成就佛**（Amoghasiddhi）：成所作智 / North: Amoghasiddhi — All-Accomplishing Wisdom

大日如来本身代表**法界体性智**（dharmadhātu-jñāna），即了知一切法界本质无差别的智慧。五佛合称"五智如来"，象征佛智的五个面向。

Mahāvairocana himself represents **Dharma Realm Wisdom** (dharmadhātu-jñāna) — the wisdom that knows the fundamental non-differentiation of the entire Dharma Realm. The five Buddhas together are called the "Five Wisdom Tathāgatas," symbolizing five dimensions of Buddha-wisdom.

### 胎藏界曼荼罗 · Garbhadhātu Maṇḍala (Womb Realm)

**胎藏界**（たいぞうかい，taizōkai）曼荼罗表现的是大日如来**慈悲**的一面。"胎藏"（garbha）意为"子宫"或"胚胎"，象征着佛性如同种子蕴藏在大地的子宫中，等待因缘成熟而生长显现。

The **Garbhadhātu** (Womb Realm) maṇḍala represents the **compassion** aspect of Mahāvairocana. "Garbha" means "womb" or "embryo," symbolizing Buddha-nature as a seed stored within the earth's womb, awaiting the maturation of conditions to grow and manifest.

胎藏界曼荼罗的核心结构是**中台八叶院**，大日如来居于中央莲花，八方围绕四佛四菩萨，形如八瓣莲花。这一图像象征众生心中的佛性如同一朵含苞待放的莲花。

The Garbhadhātu Maṇḍala's core structure is the **Central Eight-Petal Court**, where Mahāvairocana resides on the central lotus, surrounded by four Buddhas and four Bodhisattvas in eight directions, forming an eight-petaled lotus. This image symbolizes the Buddha-nature within sentient beings' hearts as a lotus bud awaiting blooming.

胎藏界曼荼罗还包括**十二大院**，从中台的佛部到外院的天部、金刚部，展现了从觉悟核心到世俗世界的层层展开。

The Garbhadhātu Maṇḍala also includes **Twelve Great Courts**, extending from the central Buddha division to the outer courts of celestial beings and vajra divisions, depicting the layered unfolding from the core of awakening to the mundane world.

## 两界不二 · Non-Duality of the Two Realms

空海密教的深刻之处在于：金刚界（智慧）和胎藏界（慈悲）不是两个分离的领域，而是同一实相的两个面向。正如一枚硬币的两面，智慧和慈悲不可分离。这一原则被称为**金胎不二**（kintai funi）。

The profundity of Kūkai's Esoteric Buddhism lies in the principle that the Vajradhātu (wisdom) and Garbhadhātu (compassion) are not two separate realms but two aspects of the same reality. Like two sides of a single coin, wisdom and compassion are inseparable. This principle is known as **the Non-Duality of Vajra and Womb** (kintai funi).

在实际修行中，这意味着：

In practical terms, this means:

- **理智不二**：觉悟不是纯粹的理论认知，也不是纯粹的情感体验，而是智慧与慈悲的完整实现。
  **Wisdom-Compassion Non-Duality**: Awakening is neither purely intellectual cognition nor purely emotional experience, but the complete realization of both wisdom and compassion.
- **因果不二**：金刚界代表"果"（佛的觉悟状态），胎藏界代表"因"（众生本具的佛性）。两者不二，即因即果。
  **Cause-Effect Non-Duality**: The Vajradhātu represents the "fruit" (the Buddha's awakened state), the Garbhadhātu represents the "cause" (beings' inherent Buddha-nature). Their non-duality means cause and fruit are simultaneous.

## 曼荼罗在修行中的运用 · Maṇḍala in Practice

### 灌顶仪式 · Abhiṣeka Ceremony

在真言宗的灌顶仪式中，受法者被蒙眼引导至两界曼荼罗前，然后掷花于曼荼罗上。花落在哪尊佛菩萨身上，即表示受法者与哪尊本尊有缘。这一仪式被称为"投花得佛"（tōka tokubutsu）。

In Shingon abhiṣeka ceremonies, the initiate is blindfolded and guided before the Two Realm Maṇḍalas, then casts a flower onto the maṇḍala. Whichever Buddha or Bodhisattva the flower lands upon indicates the initiate's karmic affinity with that particular deity. This ceremony is known as "casting the flower to receive one's Buddha" (tōka tokubutsu).

### 观想修行 · Visualization Practice

高阶修行者会在冥想中逐一观想曼荼罗中的每一尊佛菩萨、每一个种子字和每一个象征物，最终将整个曼荼罗完整地呈现在心识中。这一过程不仅是视觉训练，更是对法界秩序的直接体验。

Advanced practitioners visualize each Buddha, Bodhisattva, seed syllable, and symbolic object within the maṇḍala one by one in meditation, until the entire maṇḍala is fully present in consciousness. This process is not merely visual training but a direct experience of the Dharma Realm's order.

### 护摩火供 · Homa Fire Ritual

**护摩**（goma）是真言宗最具视觉冲击力的仪式之一。修行者在坛前燃火，将供品投入火中，同时观想火焰即是大日如来的智慧之火，燃烧一切烦恼。护摩坛的布置严格按照曼荼罗的结构，是大日如来法界的微观呈现。

**Homa** (護摩) is one of Shingon Buddhism's most visually striking rituals. The practitioner lights a fire before the altar, casts offerings into the flames, while visualizing the fire as Mahāvairocana's wisdom fire consuming all afflictions. The homa altar is arranged strictly according to maṇḍala structure, serving as a microcosmic manifestation of Mahāvairocana's Dharma Realm.

## 曼荼罗与当代思想 · Maṇḍala and Contemporary Thought

曼荼罗的概念与若干当代领域形成了有意义的对话：

The maṇḍala concept engages meaningfully with several contemporary domains:

- **荣格心理学**：卡尔·荣格（Carl Jung）将曼荼罗视为"自性"（Self）的象征，认为画曼荼罗是个体化过程的重要工具。这一理解虽有简化之嫌，但揭示了曼荼罗作为心灵地图的深层功能。
  **Jungian Psychology**: Carl Jung viewed maṇḍalas as symbols of the Self, regarding maṇḍala-drawing as an important tool in the individuation process. Though somewhat reductive, this reveals the maṇḍala's deep function as a map of the psyche.
- **系统论**：曼荼罗的结构——中心与周边的层级关系、各部分之间的对应与呼应——与复杂系统理论中的涌现和自组织概念相通。
  **Systems Theory**: The maṇḍala's structure — the hierarchical relationship between center and periphery, the correspondence and resonance among parts — resonates with emergence and self-organization concepts in complex systems theory.
- **信息可视化**：曼荼罗可以被视为一种将复杂的多维信息系统进行视觉编码的古老尝试，与现代信息可视化技术有着异曲同工之妙。
  **Information Visualization**: The maṇḍala can be viewed as an ancient attempt to visually encode complex multidimensional information systems, paralleling modern information visualization techniques.

## 日本曼荼罗艺术 · Japanese Maṇḍala Art

空海从唐朝带回的两界曼荼罗原件（彩色绢本）至今保存在京都东寺，被指定为日本国宝。这些曼荼罗不仅是宗教圣物，也是东亚艺术的巅峰之作。

The original Two Realm Maṇḍalas (polychrome silk paintings) that Kūkai brought from Tang China are preserved at Tō-ji in Kyoto and designated as Japanese National Treasures. These maṇḍalas are not only sacred religious objects but also masterworks of East Asian art.

此后一千二百年间，日本画师不断摹写和重新诠释曼荼罗，形成了丰富的艺术传统。从精细的工笔彩绘到抽象的墨书种子字曼荼罗，日本曼荼罗艺术展现了密教思想与日本美学的深度融合。

Over the subsequent twelve centuries, Japanese artists have continuously copied and reinterpreted maṇḍalas, forming a rich artistic tradition. From meticulous polychrome paintings to abstract ink-brush seed-syllable maṇḍalas, Japanese maṇḍala art demonstrates the deep fusion of esoteric thought with Japanese aesthetics.
