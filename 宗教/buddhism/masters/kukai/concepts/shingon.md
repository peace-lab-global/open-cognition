# 真言宗 Shingon School (真言宗 · Shingon-shū)

> "真言者，真实之语也。大日如来之所宣说，非方便之教。"
> "Mantra is the speech of truth — proclaimed by Mahāvairocana himself, not expedient teaching."

## 定义 · Definition

**真言宗**（しんごんしゅう，Shingon-shū）是空海于九世纪初创立的日本佛教宗派，以"真言"（mantra，咒语）命名，强调通过身口意三密修行即身成佛。真言宗是日本最古老且至今仍有广泛影响的佛教宗派之一，拥有超过一万二千座寺院和数百万信众。

**Shingon-shū** is the Japanese Buddhist school founded by Kūkai in the early ninth century, named after "shingon" (mantra), emphasizing attainment of Buddhahood in this very body through the Three Mysteries of body, speech, and mind. Shingon is one of Japan's oldest and most widely influential Buddhist schools, with over twelve thousand temples and millions of adherents today.

"真言"一词的日文发音为"しんごん"（shingon），是梵文"mantra"的意译。宗派以此命名，表明其核心修行方法——真言（mantra）念诵——在密教体系中的中心地位。

The Japanese pronunciation "shingon" is the translation of Sanskrit "mantra." Naming the school after this term underscores the centrality of mantra recitation within the esoteric system.

## 传承体系 · Lineage System

### 付法八祖 · The Eight Patriarchs

真言宗的传承体系从法身佛大日如来开始，经过印度和中国的祖师，直到空海。这一传承被称为**付法八祖**（The Eight Dharma-Transmitting Patriarchs）：

Shingon's lineage system begins with Dharma Body Buddha Mahāvairocana and passes through Indian and Chinese patriarchs to Kūkai. This lineage is known as the **Eight Dharma-Transmitting Patriarchs**:

1. **大日如来**（Mahāvairocana）— 法身佛，密法的源头 / Dharma Body Buddha, source of the esoteric Dharma
2. **金刚萨埵**（Vajrasattva）— 大日如来的首座弟子 / Mahāvairocana's foremost disciple
3. **龙树**（Nāgārjuna）— 开启南天铁塔，取出密教经典 / Opened the Iron Stupa in South India, retrieving esoteric scriptures
4. **龙智**（Nāgabodhi）— 龙树的弟子 / Nāgārjuna's disciple
5. **善无畏**（Śubhakarasiṃha, 637–735）— 将《大日经》传入中国 / Brought the *Mahāvairocana Sūtra* to China
6. **金刚智**（Vajrabodhi, 671–741）— 将《金刚顶经》传入中国 / Brought the *Vajraśekhara Sūtra* to China
7. **不空**（Amoghavajra, 705–774）— 融合两部大法 / Synthesized both mandala systems
8. **惠果**（Huiguo, 746–805）— 第七代祖师，空海的灌顶师 / Seventh patriarch, Kūkai's initiation master

空海本人被尊为第九代祖师，也是日本真言宗的开祖。

Kūkai himself is honored as the ninth patriarch and the founding master of Japanese Shingon Buddhism.

### 传法灌顶 · Denpō Kanjō (Dharma Transmission Initiation)

真言宗的传承通过**传法灌顶**（denpō kanjō）仪式来实现。只有经过传法灌顶的阿阇梨（ācārya）才有资格传授密法、为弟子灌顶。这一制度确保了密法的纯正传承，从惠果到空海，再从空海的弟子代代相传至今。

Shingon transmission is realized through the **denpō kanjō** (dharma transmission initiation) ceremony. Only an ācārya who has received denpō kanjō is qualified to transmit esoteric teachings and perform initiations for disciples. This system has ensured the purity of the esoteric lineage, from Huiguo to Kūkai, and from Kūkai's disciples generation after generation to the present day.

## 教义体系 · Doctrinal System

### 根本经典 · Root Scriptures

真言宗的根本经典是"三部经"：

Shingon's root scriptures are the "Three Sutras":

1. **《大日经》**（Mahāvairocana Sūtra）：七卷本，阐述胎藏界曼荼罗和三密修行。
   *Mahāvairocana Sūtra* (Seven fascicles): Expounds the Garbhadhātu Maṇḍala and Three Mysteries practice.
2. **《金刚顶经》**（Vajraśekhara Sūtra）：阐述金刚界曼荼罗和五相成身观。
   *Vajraśekhara Sūtra*: Expounds the Vajradhātu Maṇḍala and the five-phase self-realization visualization.
3. **《苏悉地经》**（Susiddhikara Sūtra）：密教仪轨的综合手册。
   *Susiddhikara Sūtra*: A comprehensive manual of esoteric ritual procedures.

### 空海的论著 · Kūkai's Treatises

空海本人的论著构成了真言宗教义的系统化阐述：

Kūkai's own treatises constitute the systematized elaboration of Shingon doctrine:

- **《十住心论》**（Jūjūshin-ron）：十卷，判教体系，将一切思想纳入十个心识层次。
  *Treatise on the Ten Stages of the Mind* (10 fascicles): Hermeneutical system organizing all thought into ten stages of consciousness.
- **《即身成佛义》**（Sokushin Jōbutsu Gi）：一卷，论证即身成佛的可能性。
  *Meaning of Becoming a Buddha in This Very Body* (1 fascicle): Argues the possibility of sokushin jōbutsu.
- **《声字实相义》**（Shōji Jissō Gi）：一卷，语言哲学。
  *Meaning of Sound, Letter, and Reality* (1 fascicle): Philosophy of language.
- **《秘藏宝钥》**（Hizō Hōyaku）：三卷，密法精华总结。
  *Precious Key to the Secret Treasury* (3 fascicles): Summary of esoteric Dharma essentials.

### 判教 · Doctrinal Classification

真言宗的判教以《十住心论》为核心，将真言密教置于一切佛教和世间思想之上。但这不是一种排斥性的等级制度，而是一种包容性的发展观：每一个较低的层次都是通向最高真理的必经之路。

Shingon's doctrinal classification centers on the *Jūjūshin-ron*, placing esoteric Buddhism above all Buddhist and worldly thought systems. However, this is not an exclusionary hierarchy but an inclusive developmental vision: every lower stage is a necessary step toward the highest truth.

## 组织与道场 · Organization and Sacred Sites

### 高野山 · Mount Kōya (Kōya-san)

**高野山**（こうやさん，Kōya-san）位于和歌山县，海拔约八百米，是空海于 816 年建立的根本修行道场。高野山是真言宗的总本山，也是日本最重要的佛教朝圣地之一。1993 年被列入联合国教科文组织世界遗产。

**Mount Kōya** (Kōya-san), located in Wakayama Prefecture at approximately 800 meters elevation, was established by Kūkai in 816 as the foundational practice center. Kōya-san is the head temple of Shingon Buddhism and one of Japan's most important Buddhist pilgrimage sites. It was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1993.

高野山的核心建筑包括：

Key structures at Kōya-san include:

- **金刚峰寺**（Kongōbu-ji）：真言宗总本山 / Head temple of Shingon Buddhism
- **壇上伽蓝**（Danjō Garan）：根本大塔，安放两界曼荼罗 / Fundamental Great Stupa housing the Two Realm Maṇḍalas
- **奥之院**（Oku-no-in）：空海入定之所 / Site of Kūkai's entry into samādhi

### 东寺 · Tō-ji (Kyō-ō Gokoku-ji)

**东寺**（とうじ，Tō-ji）位于京都，823 年由嵯峨天皇赐予空海。东寺是真言宗在京都的布教中心，其五重塔（约 57 米）是京都的标志性景观。东寺的讲堂内安放有空海从唐朝带回的二十一尊佛像，构成"立体曼荼罗"。

**Tō-ji** (Kyō-ō Gokoku-ji), located in Kyoto, was bestowed upon Kūkai by Emperor Saga in 823. Tō-ji serves as Shingon's propagation center in the capital. Its five-storied pagoda (approximately 57 meters) is an iconic Kyoto landmark. The Lecture Hall houses twenty-one Buddhist images arranged by Kūkai as a "three-dimensional maṇḍala," brought from Tang China.

### 现代组织 · Modern Organization

真言宗在现代日本分为多个派系，最大的两个是：

Modern Shingon Buddhism in Japan is divided into several branches, the two largest being:

- **高野山真言宗**（Kōya-san Shingon-shū）：以高野山金刚峰寺为总本山。
  **Kōya-san Shingon-shū**: Headquartered at Kongōbu-ji on Mount Kōya.
- **真言宗智山派**（Shingon-shū Chisan-ha）和**丰山派**（Buzan-ha）：分别以长谷寺和智积院为中心。
  **Chisan-ha** and **Buzan-ha**: Centered at Hase-dera and Chishaku-in respectively.

## 修行体系 · Practice System

### 四度加行 · Shido Kegyo (Four Preliminary Practices)

真言宗的修行入门被称为**四度加行**，包括四种基础修行：

The entry into Shingon practice is known as the **Four Preliminary Practices** (shido kegyo), comprising four foundational disciplines:

1. **十八道**（Jūhachi-dō）：基础仪轨和手印 / Basic ritual procedures and mudrās
2. **金刚界法**（Kongō-kai hō）：金刚界曼荼罗的修法 / Practice methods of the Vajradhātu Maṇḍala
3. **胎藏界法**（Taizō-kai hō）：胎藏界曼荼罗的修法 / Practice methods of the Garbhadhātu Maṇḍala
4. **护摩法**（Goma hō）：火供仪式 / Homa fire ritual

完成四度加行后，修行者方可接受传法灌顶，正式成为真言宗的阿阇梨。

After completing the Four Preliminary Practices, the practitioner may receive denpō kanjō and formally become a Shingon ācārya.

### 日常修行 · Daily Practice

真言宗僧侣和在家信众的日常修行包括：

Daily practice for Shingon monks and lay adherents includes:

- **晨课**（朝勤行）：在佛坛前诵经、持咒和观想 / Morning service: sutra recitation, mantra chanting, and visualization before the home altar
- **光明真言**（Kōmyō Shingon）：最常用的真言之一，被认为能消除罪障 / Light Mantra: one of the most commonly recited mantras, believed to purify karmic obstacles
- **般若心经**：真言宗也诵念《般若心经》，视其为密教的基础 / Heart Sutra: Shingon also recites the Heart Sutra, viewing it as a foundation for esoteric practice

## 真言宗与其他宗派 · Shingon and Other Schools

### 与天台宗的关系 · Relationship with Tendai

空海与最澄的关系是日本佛教史上的一段佳话。两人同时入唐求法，回国后最初关系密切，最澄甚至向空海学习密法。但后来因教义分歧而分道扬镳。最澄认为天台圆教与密教平等，空海则坚持密教高于一切显教。

The relationship between Kūkai and Saichō is a notable episode in Japanese Buddhist history. Both traveled to Tang China simultaneously and initially maintained close relations after their return — Saichō even studied esoteric methods under Kūkai. However, they later parted ways over doctrinal differences. Saichō held that Tendai's perfect teaching was equal to Esoteric Buddhism, while Kūkai maintained that Esoteric Buddhism surpassed all exoteric teachings.

### 与禅宗的关系 · Relationship with Zen

禅宗（特别是临济宗和曹洞宗）后来传入日本，形成了与真言宗的对照。禅宗强调"不立文字"和自力修行，而真言宗重视"声字实相"和三密加持。两者代表了日本佛教中"自力"与"他力/加持力"的两种不同路径。

Zen Buddhism (particularly Rinzai and Sōtō) was later transmitted to Japan, forming a contrast with Shingon. Zen emphasizes "not establishing words" and self-power practice, while Shingon values "sound, letter, and reality" and Three Mysteries empowerment. These represent two different paths in Japanese Buddhism: "self-power" versus "other-power / empowerment-power."

## 当代真言宗 · Contemporary Shingon

当代真言宗面临的挑战包括：

Contemporary Shingon Buddhism faces challenges including:

- **世俗化**：日本社会的世俗化趋势对寺院经济和信众基础构成压力。
  **Secularization**: Secularizing trends in Japanese society pressure temple economies and lay membership bases.
- **国际传播**：真言宗在北美和欧洲建立了少量道场，但规模远小于禅宗和净土宗。
  **International Propagation**: Shingon has established some temples in North America and Europe, though on a far smaller scale than Zen or Pure Land Buddhism.
- **学术复兴**：近年来，对空海哲学（特别是语言哲学和十住心论）的学术研究日益活跃，为真言宗提供了新的思想活力。
  **Academic Revival**: In recent years, academic research on Kūkai's philosophy (especially his philosophy of language and the Ten Stages) has become increasingly active, providing new intellectual vitality for Shingon Buddhism.
