# 无常佛性 Mujō-Bushō (Impermanence-Buddha-Nature)

> "无常はすなわち佛性なり"——《正法眼藏·佛性》
>
> "Impermanence is itself Buddha-nature." — *Shōbōgenzō Bushō*

## 概念概述 / Overview

"无常佛性"是道元思想中最具颠覆性的主张之一。在传统佛教中，无常（mujō）通常被视为苦难的根源——万物迁流变化，因此不可依赖，因此是苦。修行的目标是超越无常，达到永恒的涅槃。道元彻底翻转了这一框架：无常不是需要超越的缺陷，而是佛性本身的表现形式。变化不是问题，变化就是答案。

*Impermanence-Buddha-Nature* (*mujō-bushō*) is one of Dōgen's most subversive claims. In traditional Buddhism, impermanence (*mujō*) is typically seen as the root of suffering — all things change, therefore cannot be relied upon, therefore are suffering. Practice aims to transcend impermanence and attain eternal nirvana. Dōgen overturns this entirely: impermanence is not a defect to transcend but the very manifestation of Buddha-nature. Change is not the problem; change is the answer.

## 核心论证 / Core Argument

### 无常即佛性 / Impermanence is Buddha-Nature

在《佛性》卷中，道元引用六祖慧能的话："无常者，即佛性也。"慧能的原意可能是指佛性超越常与无常的二元对立。但道元将此推向了更激进的方向：无常不是佛性的反面，无常本身就是佛性。

In the *Bushō* fascicle, Dōgen cites the Sixth Patriarch Huineng: "Impermanence is itself Buddha-nature." Huineng's original meaning may have been that Buddha-nature transcends the duality of permanence and impermanence. But Dōgen pushes this in a more radical direction: impermanence is not the opposite of Buddha-nature; impermanence itself is Buddha-nature.

这意味着：

This means:

- **花开花落是佛性**：花朵的绽放与凋零不是"遗憾"，而是佛性的完美展现。
  The blooming and falling of flowers is not "regret" but Buddha-nature's perfect expression.
- **生老病死是佛性**：生命的诞生、成长、衰老、死亡不是需要克服的苦难，而是佛性的完整过程。
  Birth, aging, illness, and death are not suffering to overcome but Buddha-nature's complete process.
- **四季流转是佛性**：春夏秋冬不是遮蔽真理的现象，而是真理本身的时间性表达。
  The turning of seasons is not phenomena obscuring truth but truth's own temporal expression.

### 常与无常的超越 / Beyond Permanence and Impermanence

道元的无常佛性不是简单地用"无常"取代"常"——那只是颠倒了二元对立，并没有超越它。道元的立场更深刻：佛性既不是"常"也不是"无常"，而是常与无常的统一——但这个统一不是抽象的综合，而是每一刻的具体现实。

Dōgen's impermanence-Buddha-nature does not simply substitute "impermanence" for "permanence" — that would merely invert the duality without transcending it. Dōgen's stance is deeper: Buddha-nature is neither "permanent" nor "impermanent," but the unity of both — a unity that is not abstract synthesis but concrete reality in each moment.

### 与"有时"的关系 / Relation to Uji

无常佛性与"有时"概念紧密相连。如果时间即存在（有时），那么存在的无常变化就不是发生在时间中的事件，而是时间-存在本身的展开方式。无常是"有时"的动态面。

Impermanence-Buddha-nature is tightly linked to *uji*. If time is being, then the impermanent changes of existence are not events that happen in time but the unfolding mode of being-time itself. Impermanence is the dynamic face of *uji*.

## 哲学意义 / Philosophical Significance

### 对传统佛教的颠覆 / Subversion of Traditional Buddhism

| 传统观点 / Traditional | 道元 / Dōgen |
|---|---|
| 无常是苦 / Impermanence is suffering | 无常是佛性 / Impermanence is Buddha-nature |
| 目标是超越变化 / Goal: transcend change | 目标是融入变化 / Goal: embody change |
| 永恒是价值 / Eternity is valued | 无常是价值 / Impermanence is valued |
| 涅槃 = 不变 / Nirvana = unchanging | 涅槃 = 无常的完全实现 / Nirvana = full realization of impermanence |

### 与过程哲学的对话 / Dialogue with Process Philosophy

道元的无常佛性与怀特海 (Whitehead) 的过程哲学形成深刻共鸣：

Dōgen's impermanence-Buddha-nature resonates deeply with Whitehead's process philosophy:

- **生成即实在**：怀特海主张"过程"是实在的根本特征，而非附加属性。道元同样主张变化是佛性的根本表现。
  Whitehead holds "process" as reality's fundamental character. Dōgen similarly holds change as Buddha-nature's fundamental expression.
- **事件本体论**：两者都以"事件"而非"实体"作为存在论的基础单位。
  Both take "events" rather than "substances" as the basic ontological unit.
- **内在关系**：事物不是独立的实体，而是关系性的过程——这与无常佛性的"一切存在即佛性"相通。
  Things are not independent substances but relational processes — echoing "all being is Buddha-nature."

### 生态哲学意涵 / Ecological Implications

无常佛性为生态哲学提供了深刻的思想资源：

Impermanence-Buddha-nature offers profound resources for ecological philosophy:

- **生态整体论**：如果一切变化都是佛性，那么生态系统的动态平衡——捕食、分解、演替——都是佛性的表现。
  If all change is Buddha-nature, then ecosystem dynamics — predation, decomposition, succession — are all Buddha-nature's expression.
- **可持续性**：接受无常意味着接受有限性——这对消费主义文化构成根本挑战。
  Accepting impermanence means accepting finitude — a fundamental challenge to consumerist culture.
- **深层生态学**：万物皆佛性的主张与深层生态学的"生态自我"概念深度呼应。
  The claim that all phenomena are Buddha-nature resonates with deep ecology's "ecological self."

## 与《现成公案》的关系 / Relation to Genjōkōan

"无常佛性"与《现成公案》中道元对自然现象的描述密切相关。在《现成公案》中，道元写道：

Impermanence-Buddha-nature is closely connected to Dōgen's descriptions of natural phenomena in *Genjōkōan*. There he writes:

> "诸法の佛法なる時節、すなはち迷悟あり。万法ともにわれにあらざる時節、まどひなくさとりなく、諸佛なく衆生なく、生なく滅なし。"
>
> "When all things are Buddhist phenomena, there is delusion and realization. When all things are not 'mine,' there is no delusion, no realization, no Buddhas, no sentient beings, no birth, no death."

这段文字展示了道元的核心洞见：无常不是发生在佛性之外的现象，而是佛性自身的动态表达。花谢不是一个"令人遗憾的事实"，而是佛性在此刻的完整展现。

This passage reveals Dōgen's core insight: impermanence is not a phenomenon occurring outside Buddha-nature but the dynamic expression of Buddha-nature itself. A flower's falling is not a "regrettable fact" but Buddha-nature's complete manifestation at this moment.

## 常见误读 / Common Misreadings

- **"无常佛性 = 享乐主义"**："无常即佛性"不是"及时行乐"的借口。道元的意思是：正因为一切无常，所以每一刻都不可重复——这要求更深的觉察和投入，而非更浅的放纵。
  Impermanence-Buddha-nature is not a justification for hedonism. Dōgen means: precisely because everything is impermanent, each moment is unrepeatable — demanding deeper awareness and engagement, not shallower indulgence.
- **"无常佛性否定了因果"**：道元不否定因果律。事实上，他在《深信因果》卷中强调因果的绝对性。"无常即佛性"不是取消因果，而是在因果的框架内赋予变化以终极的存在论价值。
  Dōgen does not deny causality. Indeed, in the *Shinjin Gaku* fascicle he emphasizes causality's absolute nature. Impermanence-Buddha-nature does not cancel causality but grants change its ultimate ontological value within the causal framework.
- **"无常佛性 = 消极宿命论"**：接受无常不是消极地"认命"，而是积极地拥抱存在的真实面貌。道元的立场是一种激进的存在肯定——包括痛苦、衰老和死亡在内的全部存在都被肯定为佛性。
  Accepting impermanence is not passive resignation but active embrace of existence's true face. Dōgen's stance is a radical affirmation of being — all existence, including pain, aging, and death, is affirmed as Buddha-nature.

## 实践意涵 / Practical Implications

无常佛性不是一个需要"理解"的理论，而是一个需要在坐禅中"体验"的现实：

Impermanence-Buddha-nature is not a theory to "understand" but a reality to "experience" in zazen:

- **接纳变化**：坐禅中，念头、感受、声音不断生灭——修行者学会不执着于任何状态，因为每一刻的无常本身就是完整的。
  In sitting, thoughts, feelings, sounds constantly arise and pass. The practitioner learns not to cling to any state, because each moment's impermanence is already complete.
- **当下的珍贵**：正因为一切无常，每一刻都不可替代、不可重复——这赋予了当下一种深刻的庄严感。
  Precisely because everything is impermanent, each moment is irreplaceable and unrepeatable — granting the present a profound dignity.
