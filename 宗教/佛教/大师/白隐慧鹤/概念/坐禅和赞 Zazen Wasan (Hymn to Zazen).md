# 坐禅和赞 Zazen Wasan (Hymn to Zazen)

> "众生本来佛，恰如水与冰。离水则无冰，众生外无佛。"——白隐慧鹤
>
> "All beings are from the outset Buddha — just like water and ice. Apart from water, no ice; apart from beings, no Buddha." — Hakuin Ekaku

---

## 概述 / Overview

《坐禅和赞》（日语：座禅和讃 *Zazen Wasan*）是白隐慧鹤晚年创作的一首禅修赞歌，用日语和歌体裁写成，是白隐所有著作中最广为流传、最常被诵读的一篇。它以极其简洁优美的语言，浓缩了白隐禅的核心教义——众生本具佛性、修行即发现、坐禅即是佛行。这首赞歌至今仍在日本临济宗寺院中被日常诵读，也被翻译成多种语言在世界各地的禅修中心流传。

白隐选择用日语而非传统的汉文来写作，是其弘法策略的重要体现——他要让普通的日本百姓、而非仅限于受过汉文教育的僧人和学者，都能理解禅修的真义。《坐禅和赞》的平易语言使它成为连接精英禅修与民间信仰的桥梁。

The *Zazen Wasan* (Hymn to Zazen) is a meditation hymn composed by Hakuin in his later years, written in Japanese *waka* verse form. It is the most widely circulated and frequently chanted of all Hakuin's works. In extremely concise and beautiful language, it distills the core teaching of Hakuin's Zen — all beings inherently possess Buddha-nature, practice is discovery, zazen itself is Buddha-activity. This hymn continues to be chanted daily in Japanese Rinzai temples and has been translated into many languages at Zen centers worldwide.

Hakuin's choice to write in Japanese rather than classical Chinese was a significant aspect of his teaching strategy — he wanted ordinary Japanese people, not only monks and scholars educated in Chinese, to understand the true meaning of meditation. The accessible language of the *Zazen Wasan* bridges elite Zen practice and popular 宗教.

## 核心段落解析 / Analysis of Core Passages

### 开篇：众生本来佛 / Opening: All Beings Are Originally Buddha

> **众生本来佛，恰如水与冰。离水则无冰，众生外无佛。**
>
> *All beings are from the outset Buddha, just like water and ice. Apart from water, no ice; apart from beings, no Buddha.*

这是整首赞歌的基石命题。白隐用"水与冰"的比喻来阐明佛性与众生的关系：冰看起来与水不同——它更硬、更冷、有固定形状——但冰的本质就是水。同样，众生看起来与佛不同——众生有烦恼、有执着、有生老病死——但众生的本质就是佛。

这个比喻的深刻之处在于：冰不需要"变成"水——它只需要"融化"就能恢复水的本性。同样，众生不需要"变成"佛——他们只需要"融化"（放下执着和妄想），本有的佛性就自然显现。修行不是创造佛性，而是发现本有的佛性。

This is the foundational proposition of the entire hymn. Hakuin uses the "water and ice" metaphor to illuminate the relationship between Buddha-nature and beings: ice appears different from water — harder, colder, fixed in shape — but ice's essence is water. Similarly, beings appear different from Buddha — having afflictions, attachments, birth and death — but beings' essence is Buddha.

The metaphor's profundity: ice doesn't need to "become" water — it only needs to "melt" to restore water's nature. Similarly, beings don't need to "become" Buddha — they only need to "melt" (release clinging and delusion) for inherent Buddha-nature to naturally manifest. Practice is not creating Buddha-nature but discovering inherent Buddha-nature.

### 迷失与寻找 / Lost and Seeking

> **不远求佛佛，恰如穷子走。不知近处有，到处去寻求。**
>
> *Not seeking Buddha far away — like the prodigal son wandering. Not knowing it is near, seeking it everywhere.*

白隐引用《法华经》中"穷子喻"的故事——一个富人的儿子离家出走，在贫困中流浪多年，不知道自己的父亲是富豪——来说明众生的根本困境：不是缺少佛性，而是不认识自己的佛性。佛性不在远处，就在"近处"——就在当下的每一个心念之中。

这一段落呼应了白隐"众生本来佛"的开篇——问题不在于"有没有佛性"，而在于"知不知道自己本有佛性"。坐禅的目的，就是让修行者直接体验到这个本有的佛性。

Hakuin references the Lotus Sutra's "Prodigal Son" parable — a rich man's son leaves home, wanders in poverty for years, unaware his father is wealthy — to illustrate beings' fundamental predicament: not lacking Buddha-nature, but failing to recognize it. Buddha-nature is not far away but "near" — in every present-moment thought.

This passage echoes the opening — the problem is not "whether Buddha-nature exists" but "whether one knows one already has it." The purpose of zazen is to allow practitioners to directly experience this inherent Buddha-nature.

### 坐禅的功德 / The Merit of Zazen

> **赞叹坐禅功德：自利利他，圆满具足。一切诸佛皆从此出。**
>
> *Praising zazen's merit: self-benefit and benefiting others, perfectly complete. All Buddhas arise from this.*

白隐在此将坐禅置于所有修行之上——不仅因为坐禅是开悟的手段，更因为坐禅本身就是佛行。这与道元的"只管打坐"有精神上的呼应，尽管白隐的禅风与道元截然不同。白隐的坐禅不是"无为"的默照，而是充满"大疑"的看话禅——在坐禅中参究公案，直到疑团爆破，本有佛性自然显现。

值得注意的是白隐特别强调"自利利他"——坐禅不仅是为了自己的解脱，也是为了能够利益他人。这与大乘佛教的菩萨精神一致：真正的觉醒必然导向利他行为。

Hakuin here places zazen above all practices — not only as a means to awakening, but because zazen itself is Buddha-activity. This resonates spiritually with Dōgen's "just sitting," though Hakuin's Zen style differs radically from Dōgen's. Hakuin's zazen is not "effortless" silent illumination but kōan-introspection Zen filled with "great doubt" — contemplating kōans in sitting until the doubt-mass shatters and inherent Buddha-nature naturally manifests.

Notably, Hakuin emphasizes "self-benefit and benefiting others" — zazen is not only for one's own liberation but for the capacity to benefit others. This aligns with Mahāyāna bodhisattva spirit: genuine awakening necessarily leads to altruistic action.

### 结尾：回归日常 / Closing: Return to the Ordinary

> **若人一回坐禅，一切恶业消灭。何处更求净土？当下即是莲台。**
>
> *If one sits in zazen even once, all evil karma is extinguished. Where else seek the Pure Land? This very place is the lotus throne.*

赞歌的结尾回归到日常——坐禅的功德不在来世、不在他方净土，而在当下此地。"当下即是莲台"——你此刻坐禅的地方，就是佛的莲座。这一表述既是对在家信众的极大鼓励（不需要出家、不需要远赴名山），也是对修行目标的重新定义——净土不在死后，而在此刻的觉醒中。

The hymn's closing returns to the everyday — zazen's merit lies not in the afterlife or distant Pure Land but in this very moment and place. "This very place is the lotus throne" — where you sit in zazen now is Buddha's lotus seat. This statement both powerfully encourages laypeople (no need to become monks or travel to famous mountains) and redefines the practice goal — Pure Land is not after death but in this moment's awakening.

## 文学与修辞特征 / Literary and Rhetorical Features

《坐禅和赞》在文学上具有以下特征：

The *Zazen Wasan* exhibits the following literary characteristics:

1. **和歌体裁 Waka Verse Form**：以日本传统的五七五七七音节的和歌形式写成，朗朗上口，易于诵读和记忆。
   Written in traditional Japanese *waka* form (5-7-5-7-7 syllables), melodious, easy to chant and memorize.

2. **平易日语 Vernacular Japanese**：刻意使用通俗日语而非学术性汉文，使普通百姓能够理解。
   Deliberately using colloquial Japanese rather than scholarly Chinese, accessible to ordinary people.

3. **比喻的丰富性 Rich Metaphors**：水与冰、穷子、莲台——每个比喻都将深奥的佛理转化为日常可感的意象。
   Water and ice, the prodigal son, the lotus throne — each metaphor transforms profound Buddhist principles into everyday, tangible images.

4. **结构上的递进 Progressive Structure**：从"众生本来佛"（本体论）→"迷失与寻找"（问题诊断）→"坐禅的功德"（方法）→"回归日常"（目标达成），构成完整的修行逻辑。
   From "all beings are originally Buddha" (ontology) → "lost and seeking" (problem diagnosis) → "merit of zazen" (method) → "return to ordinary" (goal achieved), forming a complete practice logic.

## 在修行传统中的地位 / Position in Practice Traditions

《坐禅和赞》在白隐的禅修体系中占据独特地位：

The *Zazen Wasan* occupies a unique position in Hakuin's practice system:

- **修行宣言 Practice Declaration**：它不是技术指导（如《槐安国语》），而是修行的精神宣言——回答"为什么要坐禅"的根本问题。
  It is not technical guidance (like *Kaian Kokugo*) but a spiritual declaration of practice — answering the fundamental question "why sit in zazen?"

- **日常诵读 Daily Chanting**：在日本临济宗寺院中，《坐禅和赞》被作为日常功课的一部分来诵读，起到不断强化修行动机的作用。
  In Japanese Rinzai temples, the *Zazen Wasan* is chanted as part of daily practice, continuously reinforcing practice motivation.

- **入门引导 Introductory Guide**：对于初次接触禅修的在家信众，《坐禅和赞》是最常被推荐的入门文本——它不需要任何佛学知识背景就能理解。
  For laypeople first encountering meditation, the *Zazen Wasan* is the most frequently recommended introductory text — it requires no Buddhist scholarly background to understand.

## 与道元坐禅观的比较 / Comparison with Dōgen's View of Zazen

| 维度 / Dimension | 白隐 Hakuin | 道元 Dōgen |
|---|---|---|
| 坐禅性质 / Nature of zazen | 佛行——发现本有佛性 / Buddha-activity — discovering inherent Buddha-nature | 佛行——本身就是证悟 / Buddha-activity — itself is realization |
| 方法 / Method | 看话禅——参究公案 / Kanna-zen — kōan introspection | 只管打坐——无特定对象 / Shikantaza — no specific object |
| 目标 / Goal | 见性——体验本有佛性 / Kenshō — experiencing inherent Buddha-nature | 修证一等——坐即证 / Practice-enlightenment identity — sitting is realization |
| 受众 / Audience | 出家+在家 / Monastics + laity | 主要为出家僧人 / Primarily monastics |
| 语言 / Language | 日语——面向大众 / Japanese —面向 masses | 汉文+日语——面向学者 / Chinese + Japanese —面向 scholars |

尽管方法论截然不同，白隐与道元在一个根本点上一致：坐禅不是成佛的"手段"，坐禅本身就是佛的行为。

Despite radically different methodologies, Hakuin and Dōgen agree on one fundamental point: zazen is not a "means" to becoming Buddha — zazen itself is Buddha's activity.
