# 只手之声 Sound of One Hand (Sekishu no Onjō)

> "两手相拍则有声音。只手的聲音如何？"——白隐慧鹤
>
> "You know the sound of two hands clapping. What is the sound of one hand?" — Hakuin Ekaku

---

## 概述 / Overview

"只手之声"是白隐慧鹤最具标志性的创设公案，也是他留给世界禅修传统的最持久遗产。其形式极其简洁——"两手相拍则有声音，只手之声音如何？"——然而其力量在于将修行者的意识逼入一个理性完全无法抵达的境地：一只手无法"拍"，因此不可能有物理声音；但如果回答说"没有声音"，那又落入了否定性的概念判断，而非直接体验。这个公案的设计目的，是让修行者在理性的尽头发现一种超越理性的认知方式。

"The Sound of One Hand" is Hakuin Ekaku's most iconic kōan creation and his most enduring legacy to the world's contemplative traditions. Its form is extremely simple — "Two hands clapping make a sound. What is the sound of one hand?" — yet its power lies in forcing the practitioner's consciousness to the edge where rationality cannot reach: one hand cannot "clap," so there can be no physical sound; yet answering "no sound" falls into negative conceptual judgment rather than direct experience. The kōan is designed to lead practitioners to discover, at rationality's limit, a mode of knowing that transcends rationality.

## 历史背景 / Historical Context

在白隐之前，临济宗标准的初始话头（第一个公案）是赵州从谂禅师的"狗子佛性"公案——即赵州回答"狗子还有佛性也无？"时说的"无"（Mu/Wu）。这个公案源自中国唐代，经由大慧宗杲的提倡而成为看话禅的核心话头。

然而，白隐在实践中发现，"无"字公案对于某些根器的修行者来说过于抽象——"无"作为一个否定词，容易被修行者当作哲学概念来理解（"一切皆空"之类），而非作为直接体验的对象。相比之下，"只手之声"涉及具体的身体经验——拍手、声音——更容易让修行者从日常生活出发去参究，同时又不可能被理性解答。

白隐大约在四十二岁时，在自身参究"只手之声"的过程中获得了又一次重大突破。此后，他将这个公案确立为临济宗的初始话头，取代了"无"字公案的优先地位。这一改变延续至今——当代日本临济宗寺院中，初学者通常首先参究的便是"只手之声"。

Before Hakuin, the standard initial *watō* (first kōan) in Rinzai Zen was Zhaozhou's "Does a dog have Buddha-nature?" — answered with *Mu* (Nothing). This kōan originated in Tang China and became the core practice topic through Dahui Zonggao's advocacy.

However, Hakuin found in practice that the *Mu* kōan was too abstract for certain practitioners' capacities — *Mu* as a negative term was easily grasped as a philosophical concept ("everything is empty") rather than as an object of direct experience. By contrast, "Sound of One Hand" involves concrete bodily experience — clapping, sound — making it easier for practitioners to engage from daily life while remaining impossible to resolve rationally.

At approximately forty-two, while working with "Sound of One Hand" himself, Hakuin experienced another major breakthrough. Thereafter he established it as the initial Rinzai *watō*, displacing *Mu*'s priority. This change persists today — in contemporary Japanese Rinzai temples, beginners typically begin with "Sound of One Hand."

## 公案的运作机制 / How the Kōan Works

"只手之声"的精妙之处在于它同时在多个层面运作：

The brilliance of "Sound of One Hand" lies in its simultaneous operation on multiple levels:

### 1. 理性悖论层 / Rational Paradox Layer
一只手拍不出声音——这是物理事实。修行者如果试图在理性层面回答（"空气振动的声音"、"沉默"、"心跳"等），都会被禅师否定。这迫使修行者承认理性的限度。
One hand cannot produce a clapping sound — this is a physical fact. Any rational answer ("air vibration," "silence," "heartbeat") is rejected by the teacher. This forces acknowledgment of rationality's limits.

### 2. 主体性转向层 / Subjective Turn Layer
当修行者穷尽一切外在答案后，注意力被迫转向内在——不是"只手的声音是什么？"而是"在问这个问题的我是谁？"。公案从外在问题变成了自我追问。
When the practitioner exhausts all external answers, attention is forced inward — not "what is the sound of one hand?" but "who am I, the one asking this question?" The kōan transforms from an external question into self-inquiry.

### 3. 直接体验层 / Direct Experience Layer
最终的突破不是"回答"了问题，而是问题本身的消融——当修行者进入一种前概念的直接体验状态时，"声音"与"非声音"、"一只手"与"两只手"的分别都不再生起。这不是理解了答案，而是超越了问题。
The ultimate breakthrough is not "answering" the question but the dissolution of the question itself — when the practitioner enters a pre-conceptual state of direct experience, the distinctions between "sound" and "no-sound," "one hand" and "two hands" simply do not arise. This is not understanding an answer but transcending the question.

## 修行方法 / Practice Method

白隐指导弟子参究"只手之声"的方法包括：

Hakuin's method for guiding disciples through "Sound of One Hand" included:

1. **提起话头 Raising the Topic**：行住坐卧中不断提起"只手之声如何？"的问题，使其成为意识的中心焦点。
   Continuously raising "What is the sound of one hand?" during walking, standing, sitting, and lying — making it the central focus of awareness.

2. **大疑团 Great Doubt Mass**：让疑问凝聚为一个巨大的"疑团"——白隐形容为"银山铁壁"——修行者的整个存在都变成了一个巨大的问号。
   Allowing the doubt to condense into a massive "doubt-mass" — Hakuin described it as "a silver mountain, an iron wall" — the practitioner's entire being becomes one enormous question mark.

3. **疑团爆破 Breakthrough**：当疑团凝聚到极致时，某种因缘触发突然的爆破——白隐称之为"桶底脱落"——所有的疑问、执着、分别在一瞬间瓦解。
   When the doubt-mass reaches its extreme, some condition triggers sudden breakthrough — Hakuin called it "the bottom falling out of the bucket" — all questioning, clinging, and discrimination collapse in a single instant.

4. **印证与深化 Confirmation and Deepening**：弟子向禅师报告体验（"独参"），禅师以进一步的问题检验其深浅。真正的开悟不是一次性事件，而是需要不断深化。
   The disciple reports the experience to the teacher in private interview (*dokusan*); the teacher tests its depth with further questions. Genuine awakening is not a one-time event but requires continual deepening.

## 与"无"字公案的比较 / Comparison with Mu

| 维度 / Dimension | 无 (Mu) | 只手之声 (One Hand) |
|---|---|---|
| 来源 / Origin | 赵州从谂（唐代中国）/ Zhaozhou (Tang China) | 白隐慧鹤（江户日本）/ Hakuin (Edo Japan) |
| 形式 / Form | 一个字——"无" / Single character — *Mu* | 一个问题 / A question |
| 抽象度 / Abstraction | 高度抽象 / Highly abstract | 具象——涉及身体经验 / Concrete — involves bodily experience |
| 入门难度 / Entry difficulty | 较高——容易被概念化 / Higher — easily conceptualized | 较低——从日常经验出发 / Lower — starts from daily experience |
| 适用根器 / Suitable capacities | 利根——善于抽象思维者 / Sharp — those good at abstract thought | 广泛——包括在家信众 / Broad — including laypeople |

## 当代回响 / Contemporary Resonance

"只手之声"在现代文化中产生了广泛回响。Arthur Koestler 在其著作 *The Act of Creation* 中将"只手之声"视为创造性思维的典范——通过制造"不可能的问题"来触发思维模式的跃迁。在心理学领域，Milton Erickson 的治疗性悖论与"只手之声"的逻辑结构惊人相似——都是通过制造不可解的问题来绕过意识的防御机制，直接触及更深层的认知。

"Sound of One Hand" has resonated widely in modern culture. Arthur Koestler, in *The Act of Creation*, treated it as a paradigm of creative thinking — triggering cognitive leaps by posing "impossible questions." In psychology, Milton Erickson's therapeutic paradoxes bear striking structural similarity — both create unsolvable problems to bypass conscious defenses and directly access deeper cognition.
