# 白隐慧鹤 Hakuin Ekaku (1686-1769)

> "众生本来佛，恰如水与冰。离水则无冰，众生外无佛。"——白隐《坐禅和赞》
>
> "All beings are from the outset Buddha — just like water and ice. Apart from water, no ice; apart from beings, no Buddha." — Hakuin, *Zazen Wakan* (Hymn to Zazen)

---

## 基本信息 / Basic Information

**白隐慧鹤**（1686年1月19日—1769年1月18日），日本江户时代中期禅僧，被誉为日本**临济宗的中兴祖师**。面对当时禅宗的衰颓——公案沦为文学游戏、禅修流于形式——白隐以深刻的悟道体验、公案系统的创造性重组、对在家信众的积极弘法、以及独创的身体调养方法，使临济宗重新焕发活力。今天日本临济宗的所有法脉，无一例外皆出自白隐一系。他不仅是宗教改革者，也是思想家、书法家、画家和教育家，其影响遍及日本文化的方方面面。

**Hakuin Ekaku** (January 19, 1686 – January 18, 1769) was a Zen monk of Japan's mid-Edo period, revered as the **reviver of the Japanese Rinzai Zen school**. Confronting a Zen tradition in decline — where kōans had devolved into literary games and meditation had become mere form — Hakuin revitalized Rinzai through profound awakening experiences, creative reorganization of the kōan system, vigorous lay teaching, and an original body-cultivation method. Every Rinzai lineage in Japan today traces back to Hakuin without exception. He was not only a religious reformer but also a thinker, calligrapher, painter, and educator whose influence permeates every facet of Japanese culture.

## 核心命题 / Core Propositions

1. **公案系统化 Kōan Systematization**：将散乱的公案整理为有层次的修行体系（约1700则），设定循序渐进的"过关"路径。
   Organizing scattered kōans into a hierarchical practice system (~1,700 cases) with a progressive "gate-passing" curriculum.

2. **悟后修行 Post-Awakening Cultivation**：开悟不是终点，悟后仍需"牧牛"——强调大悟多次、小悟无数次。
   Awakening is not the endpoint; post-satori "ox-herding" cultivation is essential — emphasizing great awakenings many times, small awakenings countless times.

3. **只手之声 Sound of One Hand**：自创公案，取代"无"字公案成为临济宗初始话头，以直觉突破理性樊笼。
   A self-created kōan replacing the *Mu* kōan as the initial Rinzai practice topic, designed to shatter the cage of rationality through direct intuition.

4. **禅与日常生活 Zen and Everyday Life**：白隐的禅强调在日用中参究，不脱离世间，使禅修真正融入在家生活。
   Hakuin's Zen emphasizes practice within daily life, never departing from the world, making meditation genuinely accessible to laypeople.

5. **身心调和 Body-Mind Harmonization**：独创"软酥法"等身体调养方法，对治禅修中出现的"禅病"，将身体纳入修行体系。
   Creating the "soft-butter method" and other body-cultivation techniques to remedy "Zen sickness" and integrate the body into the practice system.

6. **众生本来佛 All Beings Are Originally Buddha**：《坐禅和赞》的核心宣言——佛性不是修来的，是本有的；修行是发现而非获得。
   The core declaration of the *Zazen Wakan* — Buddha-nature is not acquired through practice but is original; practice is discovery, not attainment.

## 思想脉络 / Intellectual Trajectory

### 早年求道 / Early Seeking (1686-1707)

白隐生于骏河国原宿（今静冈县沼津市），俗姓长泽，父亲是驿站经营者。母亲是一位虔诚的日莲宗信徒，对白隐的宗教气质有深刻影响。据说他幼年即对生死问题深感恐惧，八岁时听闻地狱故事而夜不能寐。十五岁时，在母亲的支持下于当地松荫寺出家，法名慧鹤。

然而，年轻的慧鹤并不安于寺院的安逸生活。他广泛游历，参访各地禅匠，阅读大量经典，但始终未能获得真正的安心。他在美浓国（岐阜县）的禅堂中经历了第一次重要的修行体验——连续数日的精进坐禅，但仍未彻底。这段漂泊期的焦虑与求索，为他后来的深刻突破积蓄了能量。

Hakuin was born in Hara-shuku, Suruga Province (present-day Numazu, Shizuoka Prefecture), to the Nagasawa family; his father managed a post station. His mother, a devout Nichiren Buddhist, profoundly shaped his religious temperament. As a child he reportedly suffered intense fear of death, losing sleep after hearing stories of hell at age eight. At fifteen, with his mother's support, he was ordained at the local Shōin-ji, receiving the dharma name Ekaku.

Yet the young Ekaku could not rest in monastic routine. He traveled widely, visited Zen masters across Japan, and devoured scriptures, yet never found genuine peace. At a meditation hall in Mino Province (Gifu), he experienced his first significant breakthrough — days of intense zazen — but it was incomplete. The anxiety and seeking of this wandering period stored up the energy for his later radical breakthrough.

### 大悟与印可 / Great Awakening and Confirmation (1707-1717)

白隐二十三岁时，在越后国（新潟县）遇见了隐居的禅修者**正受老人**（Shōju Rōjin），这位严厉的老人成为他最重要的老师。在正受老人的激烈逼拶下，白隐经历了深刻的精神危机，最终在一次极度的身心耗竭后获得了第一次重大开悟——据其自述，天地万物与自身浑然一体，所有的疑惑当下消散。

正受老人印可了他的悟境，但警告他："这还只是初步，不可住著。"白隐此后又经历了多次深浅不同的悟道体验，他曾说自己"大悟十八次，小悟无数次"。这种对悟境的不断深入——而非一次性的终极体验——成为他后来教学的核心特征。

At twenty-three, in Echigo Province (Niigata), Hakuin encountered the reclusive hermit **Shōju Rōjin** (Old Man Shōju), who became his most important teacher. Under Shōju's fierce pressure, Hakuin underwent a profound spiritual crisis and finally attained his first major awakening — according to his own account, all beings and the universe merged into one, and every doubt dissolved in that instant.

Shōju confirmed his realization but warned: "This is only the beginning — do not cling to it." Hakuin subsequently experienced many awakenings of varying depth; he famously claimed "eighteen great awakenings and countless small ones." This continual deepening of insight — rather than a single terminal experience — became the hallmark of his later teaching.

### 只手之声与教学体系的建立 / Sound of One Hand and the Teaching System (1717-1740s)

白隐三十三岁时被任命为故乡松荫寺住持，此后逐渐建立起系统化的教学体系。大约在四十二岁时，他在参究"只手之声"（两手相拍有声，只手之声如何？）时获得了又一次重大突破。这个公案是白隐自己创设的，旨在取代传统的"无"字公案（赵州"狗子还有佛性也无？"），为初学者提供更直接有力的切入点。

白隐在这一时期开始系统地整理和组织公案。他将约1700则公案分为五个层次——理屈、机关、研锥、狮子啮人、透法身——形成了循序渐进的修行路径。同时，他开始广泛接纳在家弟子，用平易近人的方式讲解深奥的禅理。

At thirty-three Hakuin was appointed abbot of Shōin-ji in his hometown, and gradually built a systematic teaching framework. Around forty-two, while contemplating "the sound of one hand" (two hands clapping make a sound — what is the sound of one hand?), he experienced another major breakthrough. This kōan was Hakuin's own creation, designed to replace the traditional *Mu* kōan (Zhaozhou's "Does a dog have Buddha-nature or not?") and provide beginners with a more direct, powerful entry point.

During this period Hakuin began systematically organizing kōans. He classified roughly 1,700 cases into five levels — *rikutsu* (principle-breaking), *kikan* (mechanism-cutting), *kensui* (deep-probing), *shishi-gijin* (lion-biting), and *tō-hosshin* (dharma-body-penetrating) — forming a progressive practice path. Simultaneously he began accepting lay disciples widely, explaining profound Zen teachings in accessible language.

### 晚年弘法 / Later Teaching (1740s-1769)

白隐的晚年是最高产的时期。他在骏河松荫寺、龙泽寺等道场指导大量弟子，既有出家的僧人，也有武士、商人和农民等在家信众。他著述丰富——包括《夜船闲话》（自传性禅修体验记）、《槐安国语》（公案修行教科书）、《远罗天斧》（禅病诊断手册）等重要著作，以及那首脍炙人口的《坐禅和赞》。

白隐同时也是一位杰出的书法家和画家，他的禅画——尤其是一休和尚、达摩、布袋等题材——至今被视为日本禅画的经典。1769年（明和五年），白隐在松荫寺圆寂，享年八十四岁。大正年间被追赠"神机独妙禅师"之号。

Hakuin's later years were his most productive. He taught large numbers of disciples at Shōin-ji, Ryūtaku-ji, and other temples in Suruga — not only monks but also samurai, merchants, and farmers. He wrote extensively: *Yasen Kanna* (autobiographical meditation memoir), *Kaian Kokugo* (kōan practice textbook), *Enra-teppu* (Zen-sickness diagnostic manual), and the beloved *Zazen Wakan* (Hymn to Zazen).

Hakuin was also an outstanding calligrapher and painter. His Zen paintings — especially of Ikkyū, Bodhidharma, and the laughing Budai — are regarded as classics of Japanese Zen art to this day. He died at Shōin-ji in 1769 (Meiwa 5), aged eighty-four. Posthumously awarded the title *Shinki Dokumyō Zenji* during the Taishō era.

## 历史背景：白隐之前的临济宗 / Historical Context: Rinzai Before Hakuin

要理解白隐的贡献，必须了解他之前的日本临济宗状况。临济宗由荣西（1141-1215）从中国传入日本后，经历了数百年的发展。到了十六、十七世纪，日本临济宗面临严重的危机：

To appreciate Hakuin's contribution, one must understand the state of Japanese Rinzai before him. Rinzai Zen, transmitted to Japan by Yōsai (1141-1215) from China, had developed over several centuries. By the sixteenth and seventeenth centuries, Japanese Rinzai faced a serious crisis:

- **公案的文学化 Literary Degradation of Kōans**：公案——原本是禅师们活生生的机锋对答——被当作文学作品来欣赏。僧人们研究公案的文学价值，撰写优美的"颂古"（诗歌式评注），却不再将公案作为修行的工具。禅修变成了学术研究而非活生生的体验。
  Kōans — originally living exchanges between Chan masters — were appreciated as literary works. Monks studied kōans' literary value, composing elegant "verses of praise," but no longer used them as practice tools. Meditation became academic study rather than living experience.

- **寺院教育的僵化 Ossification of Temple Education**：禅堂（修行道场）虽然存在，但教学方法缺乏统一标准。不同禅师的教学方式差异巨大，修行者缺乏清晰的进步标志和检验方法。
  Meditation halls existed, but teaching methods lacked unified standards. Different masters' approaches varied enormously; practitioners lacked clear progress markers and verification methods.

- **在家信众的疏离 Lay Disconnection**：禅修被日益视为出家僧人的专利，普通百姓被排斥在外。这与大乘佛教"普度众生"的精神形成矛盾。
  Meditation was increasingly seen as a monastic monopoly, with ordinary people excluded. This contradicted the Mahāyāna spirit of "liberating all beings."

白隐正是在这一危机中崛起，以全面的改革——重新激活公案的修行功能、建立系统化教学、向在家信众开放、加入身体调养维度——使临济宗重新焕发生命力。

Hakuin rose precisely within this crisis, with comprehensive reform — reactivating the kōan's practice function, establishing systematic teaching, opening to laypeople, and adding a body-cultivation dimension — revitalizing Rinzai Zen.

## 文化影响 / Cultural Influence

白隐对日本文化的影响远超宗教领域：

Hakuin's influence on Japanese culture extended far beyond religion:

- **禅画 Zen Painting (Zenga)**：白隐是江户时代最重要的禅画家之一。他的画作以大胆、粗放、充满生命力的笔触著称——与早期禅画的精致、内敛风格形成对比。他的达摩像（大眼睛、粗眉毛、红色袈裟）成为日本文化中最广为人知的达摩形象。他的布袋和尚（笑口常开的胖和尚）画作则体现了禅的自在与欢喜精神。
  Hakuin was one of the most important Zen painters of the Edo period. His paintings are characterized by bold, rough, vitally energetic brushwork — contrasting with earlier Zen painting's refined, restrained style. His Daruma portraits (large eyes, thick eyebrows, red robe) became the most widely recognized Daruma image in Japanese culture. His Budai (laughing fat monk) paintings embody Zen's spirit of freedom and joy.

- **书法 Calligraphy**：白隐的书法刚劲有力，常以大字书写禅语和自作偈颂。他的书法风格影响了后世的日本书道，被视为"禅书"（禅意书法）的典范。
  Hakuin's calligraphy is bold and vigorous, often writing Zen phrases and self-composed verses in large characters. His style influenced subsequent Japanese calligraphy and is regarded as a model of "Zen sho" (Zen calligraphy).

- **民间教化 Popular Education**：白隐用通俗易懂的语言向农民、商人讲解禅理，使禅宗从精英圈子走向民间。他的一些教导——如"工作即修行""日常生活即是道场"——深深影响了日本的工作伦理和"匠人精神"。
  Hakuin taught Zen to farmers and merchants in accessible language, bringing Zen from elite circles into popular life. Some of his teachings — "work is practice," "daily life is the training ground" — profoundly influenced Japanese work ethics and the spirit of *shokunin* (craftsmanship).

- **近现代传承 Modern Lineage**：白隐的法脉经由今北洪川（1816-1892）、山本玄峰（1866-1961）等人传承到近现代。二十世纪将禅宗介绍到西方的重要人物——如铃木大拙（虽然不是严格的白隐法脉，但深受其影响）——其思想背景中可以追溯到白隐的革新传统。
  Hakuin's lineage was transmitted to modernity through figures such as Imakita Kōsen (1816-1892) and Yamamoto Gempō (1866-1961). Key figures who introduced Zen to the West in the twentieth century — such as D.T. Suzuki (not strictly in Hakuin's lineage but deeply influenced by it) — trace their intellectual background to Hakuin's reform tradition.

## 重要概念 / Key Concepts

- **公案系统化 Kōan Systematization** — 将散乱的公案组织为五级递进修行体系。详见 [公案系统化](concepts/koan-systematization.md)。
  Organizing scattered kōans into a five-level progressive practice system.

- **只手之声 Sound of One Hand** — 白隐自创的初始公案："两手拍则有声音，只手之声音如何？"详见 [只手之声](concepts/sound-one-hand.md)。
  Hakuin's self-created initial kōan: "Two hands clapping make a sound — what is the sound of one hand?"

- **悟后修行 Post-Awakening Cultivation** — 开悟不是终点，悟后仍需不断深入。详见 [悟后修行](concepts/post-awakening.md)。
  Awakening is not the endpoint; continuous deepening after satori is essential.

- **软酥法 Soft-Butter Method** — 白隐独创的身体调养法，以意象观想对治"禅病"。详见 [软酥法](concepts/body-cultivation.md)。
  Hakuin's original body-cultivation technique using visualization to remedy "Zen sickness."

- **坐禅和赞 Hymn to Zazen** — "众生本来佛"的赞歌，白隐禅的精髓浓缩。详见 [坐禅和赞](concepts/zazen-hymn.md)。
  The hymn declaring "all beings are originally Buddha" — the essence of Hakuin's Zen distilled.

## 思想坐标 / Intellectual Coordinates

白隐占据一个承前启后的关键思想位置：

Hakuin occupies a pivotal position bridging past and future:

- **临济宗中兴者 Rinzai Reviver**：在白隐之前，日本临济宗已趋于衰微——公案被当作文学作品而非修行工具，寺院教育形式化。白隐恢复了公案的修行功能，重新激活了看话禅的传统。
  Before Hakuin, Japanese Rinzai was in decline — kōans treated as literary exercises rather than practice tools, temple education ossified. Hakuin restored the kōan's function as practice and reactivated the tradition of *kanna-zen* (kōan-introspection Zen).

- **大慧宗杲的继承者 Heir of Dahui Zonggao**：白隐的看话禅直接继承了南宋大慧宗杲（1089-1163）的传统，但他在此基础上进行了创造性发展——系统分级、新增公案、身体调养方法的加入。
  Hakuin's kanna-zen directly inherits the tradition of the Southern Song master Dahui Zonggao (1089-1163), but creatively develops it — adding systematic classification, new kōans, and body-cultivation methods.

- **在家禅的推广者 Promoter of Lay Zen**：与道元的严格出家路线不同，白隐积极向在家信众弘法，使禅修真正走向民间。这使他的影响远超寺院围墙。
  Unlike Dōgen's strict monastic path, Hakuin actively taught laypeople, bringing meditation genuinely into popular life. His influence thus extended far beyond monastery walls.

- **身心一体的实践者 Body-Mind Integrationist**：白隐的"软酥法"使他成为少数认真对待身体维度的禅宗大师，与现代身心医学形成有意义的对话。
  Hakuin's "soft-butter method" makes him one of the few Zen masters to take the bodily dimension seriously, forming meaningful dialogue with modern psychosomatic medicine.

## 教学方法 / Teaching Methodology

白隐的教学方法体现了他作为教育家的天才：

Hakuin's teaching methods reveal his genius as an educator:

- **严厉与慈悲的结合 Severity and Compassion**：白隐以严厉著称——他经常对弟子大喝、甚至以杖击之——但他的严厉来自深切的慈悲。他的目标是帮助弟子突破，而非惩罚。在严厉逼拶之后，他常常以温和的语言开示，解释逼拶的用意。
  Hakuin was known for severity — frequently shouting at or even striking disciples — but his severity arose from deep compassion. His aim was to help disciples break through, not to punish. After fierce pressure, he often followed with gentle explanation of the purpose behind his methods.

- **因人而异的教学 Individualized Teaching**：白隐不拘泥于固定的教学模式——对于根器锐利的弟子，他使用激烈的逼拶方法；对于根器较钝或信心不足的弟子，他则温和引导，甚至推荐净土念佛作为辅助。
  Hakuin never adhered to a fixed teaching pattern — for sharp-capacity disciples, he used intense pressure; for those of lesser capacity or weaker confidence, he guided gently, even recommending Pure Land recitation as a supplement.

- **语言与艺术的多元教学 Multiple Modalities**：白隐不仅用语言教导，还用书法、绘画、甚至身体力行来传达禅的信息。他的禅画不仅是艺术品，也是教学工具——一幅达摩像可以传达言语无法表达的信息。
  Hakuin taught not only through language but also through calligraphy, painting, and embodied example. His Zen paintings were not merely art but teaching tools — a single Daruma portrait could communicate what words could not.

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

- **禅修指导 Meditation Teaching**：白隐的公案分级体系是现代"学习路径设计"和"渐进式教学法"的先驱。
  Hakuin's graded kōan system is a precursor to modern "learning path design" and "progressive pedagogy."

- **身心健康 Health and Wellness**："软酥法"与身心医学（somatic therapy）、生物反馈（biofeedback）的深度对话。
  The "soft-butter method" in deep dialogue with psychosomatic medicine and biofeedback.

- **领导力与持续精进 Leadership and Kaizen**：白隐的"悟后牧牛"概念与现代"持续精进"（kaizen）和终身学习理念。
  Hakuin's "post-awakening ox-herding" concept resonates with kaizen and lifelong learning.

- **心理治疗 Psychotherapy**：白隐对禅病（气脉紊乱、精神偏差）的诊断与现代心理治疗中的创伤后应激、冥想不良反应等议题相关。
  Hakuin's diagnosis of Zen sickness (energy-channel disturbance, psychological imbalance) relates to modern issues of post-traumatic stress and adverse meditation effects.

- **艺术教育 Arts Education**：白隐的禅画和书法实践为"以艺术为修行"提供了范例。
  Hakuin's Zen painting and calligraphy practice offers a model for "art as spiritual practice."

## 常见误读 / Common Misreadings

### 1. "白隐只是复兴了旧传统" / "Hakuin merely revived an old tradition"
白隐的公案体系是创造性重组——他发明了新公案（只手之声），重新分级，并加入了身体调治方法。他不是复古主义者，而是革新者。
Hakuin's kōan system was a creative reorganization — he invented new kōans (Sound of One Hand), reclassified them, and added body-cultivation methods. He was not a revivalist but an innovator.

### 2. "日本禅 = 中国禅" / "Japanese Zen equals Chinese Chan"
白隐之后的日本临济宗与宋元禅有显著差异：更系统、更注重在家修行、更贴近日常生活。白隐的贡献恰恰在于这种日本化的创造性转化。
Post-Hakuin Japanese Rinzai differs significantly from Song-Yuan Chan: more systematic, more oriented toward lay practice, closer to everyday life. Hakuin's contribution lies precisely in this creative Japanese transformation.

### 3. "白隐的禅是纯粹的看话禅" / "Hakuin's Zen is pure kanna-zen"
白隐虽然以看话禅为主，但他并不排斥默照禅（曹洞宗传统），也不排斥净土念佛。他的态度是实用主义的——什么方法对什么人有效就用什么。
Although Hakuin's primary method was kanna-zen, he did not reject silent-illumination (Sōtō tradition) or Pure Land recitation. His attitude was pragmatic — use whatever method works for whatever person.

### 4. "开悟是终点" / "Awakening is the endpoint"
白隐最反对的就是这种"一悟了事"的想法。他强调"大悟十八次"，悟后仍需不断修行——牧牛。这是他区别于许多禅宗大师的关键。
What Hakuin most opposed was the idea of "one awakening and you're done." He emphasized "eighteen great awakenings" and insisted on continuous post-satori practice — ox-herding. This distinguishes him from many Zen masters.

### 5. "白隐反对学问" / "Hakuin opposed scholarship"
虽然白隐批评将公案当作文学来研究的做法，但他本人是一位博览群书的学者。他精通《法华经》《楞严经》《华严经》等大乘经典，并经常在教导中引用经文。他反对的不是学问本身，而是将学问替代实修的倾向——"知解宗徒"（只有知识、没有体验的人）。
Although Hakuin criticized treating kōans as literary objects, he was himself a widely-read scholar. He mastered the *Lotus Sutra*, *Shurangama Sutra*, *Avatamsaka Sutra* and other Mahāyāna classics, frequently citing scriptures in his teachings. What he opposed was not scholarship itself but the tendency to substitute knowledge for practice — "followers of intellectual understanding" (people with knowledge but no experience).
