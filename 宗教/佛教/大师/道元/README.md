# 道元 Dōgen (1200-1253)

> "有时，高高峰顶立；有时，深深海底行。"——《正法眼藏·有时》
>
> "Sometimes standing on the highest peak, sometimes walking on the deepest ocean floor." — *Shōbōgenzō Uji*

---

## 基本信息 / Basic Information

**道元**（1200年1月19日—1253年9月22日），日本镰仓时代僧人、哲学家、宗教改革者，日本曹洞宗创立者。出身京都贵族久我家，父亲为内大臣源通亲，母亲为关白藤原基房之女。双亲早逝后由叔父良观法眼抚养。少年出家，曾于1223年入宋（中国南宋）求法，从天童如净禅师得法归日，后创越前永平寺。其巨著《正法眼藏》（95卷）是日本佛教哲学的巅峰文本，也是东亚思想史上最具原创性的哲学体系之一。道元不仅是一位宗教实践者，更是一位深刻的存在论思想家——他以独特的语言策略和现象学方法，重新定义了时间、存在、修行与证悟之间的关系。后世明治天皇追赠"承阳大师"号。

**Dōgen** (January 19, 1200 – September 22, 1253) was a monk, philosopher, and religious reformer of Japan's Kamakura period, founder of the Japanese Sōtō Zen school. Born into the aristocratic Kuga family of Kyoto — his father was Minister of the Interior Minamoto Michichika, his mother a daughter of Regent Fujiwara no Motofusa — he lost both parents early and was raised by his uncle Ryōkan Hōgen. Ordained young, he traveled to Song China in 1223, where he received dharma transmission from Master Rujing (如净) of Tiantong Monastery. Upon returning to Japan he established Eihei-ji in Echizen province. His magnum opus *Shōbōgenzō* (95 fascicles) stands as the apex of Japanese Buddhist 哲学 and one of the most original philosophical systems in East Asian intellectual history. Dōgen was not merely a religious practitioner but a profound ontological thinker who redefined the relationships among time, being, practice, and realization through unique linguistic strategies and phenomenological methods. He was posthumously awarded the title *Jōyō Daishi* (Master Who Carries the Light) by Emperor Meiji.

## 核心命题 / Core Propositions

1. **只管打坐 Shikantaza**：坐禅本身就是佛行，不是成佛手段。这是道元最具革命性的主张——彻底否定了修行的工具性逻辑。详见 [只管打坐](concepts/shikantaza.md)。
   Sitting itself is Buddha-activity, not a means to becoming Buddha. This is Dōgen's most revolutionary claim — a thorough rejection of instrumentalist logic in spiritual practice. See [Shikantaza](concepts/shikantaza.md).

2. **修证一等 Practice-Enlightenment Identity**：修行与证悟是一件事，不是两件事。不存在"先修后证"的时间结构，因为每一刻的修行本身就是证悟的完整表现。详见 [修证一等](concepts/practice-enlightenment.md)。
   Practice and realization are one thing, not two. There is no temporal structure of "first practice, then realization," because each moment of practice is itself the complete expression of realization. See [Practice-Enlightenment](concepts/practice-enlightenment.md).

3. **有时 Uji**：时间即存在，存在即时间——二者不可分离。道元以"有时"一词消解了时间与存在的本体论区分，主张每一存在都是时间的具体表现形式。详见 [有时](concepts/being-time-uji.md)。
   Time is being, being is time — the two are inseparable. Dōgen dissolves the ontological distinction between time and being with the compound *uji*, holding that every existence is a concrete manifestation of time. See [Uji](concepts/being-time-uji.md).

4. **身心脱落 Dropping Off Body-Mind (Shinjin Datsuraku)**：坐禅中，身心脱落、本来面目显现。这不是神秘的"出神"体验，而是身体与心智的二元对立在当下彻底消融的存在论事件。
   In sitting, body and mind drop away, revealing the original face. This is not a mystical "out-of-body" experience but an ontological event in which the body-mind duality dissolves thoroughly in the present.

5. **无常即佛性 Impermanence is Buddha-Nature**：无常不是负面，是佛性的表现。道元将变化从佛教的"苦难标志"转化为佛性的动态展示——花开花落、生老病死，都是佛性的完美表达。详见 [无常佛性](concepts/impermanence-buddha-nature.md)。
   Impermanence is not negative; it is the manifestation of Buddha-nature. Dōgen transforms change from Buddhism's "mark of suffering" into the dynamic display of Buddha-nature — blooming and falling, birth and death are all Buddha-nature's perfect expression. See [Impermanence-Buddha-Nature](concepts/impermanence-buddha-nature.md).

6. **本证妙修 Original Enlightenment, Wondrous Practice (Hongaku Myōshū)**：本有的证悟 + 妙的修行——从证悟出发的修行。这一概念解决了"本来成佛为何还需修行"的根本悖论：正因为本来成佛，修行才自然发生。
   Original awakening plus wondrous practice — practice arising from realization. This concept resolves the fundamental paradox of "if already Buddha, why practice?": precisely because one is originally Buddha, practice naturally occurs.

## 思想脉络 / Intellectual Trajectory

### 早年的大疑 / Early Great Doubt

道元出身京都贵族久我家，父亲内大臣源通亲早逝。他自幼聪慧过人，据传九岁时已读《汉书》，对天台宗"本来成佛"的教义产生深刻疑问："若众生本来是佛，何须修行？"这一问题——"本证"与"始修"之间的张力——成为他一生思想探索的起点。

Born into the aristocratic Kuga family, Dōgen lost his father early. Precociously brilliant — reportedly reading Chinese classics by age nine — he was struck by a profound doubt regarding Tendai doctrine: "If all beings are originally Buddha, why practice at all?" This tension between original enlightenment (*hongaku*) and acquired practice (*shikaku*) became the engine of his lifelong philosophical quest.

### 入宋求法 / Journey to Song China (1223)

1223年，道元随明全入宋，先参天童无际了派，遍访径山、天台诸刹，未能解惑。最终在天童寺遇到如净禅师，于"有时"的参究中获得"身心脱落"的彻底体验。如净印可其开悟，授以曹洞宗法脉。

In 1223 Dōgen sailed to China with Myōzen. He visited Tiantong, Jingshan, and Tiantai but found no resolution. Finally at Tiantong he met Master Rujing, under whom he experienced the radical "dropping off of body and mind" (*shinjin datsuraku*). Rujing confirmed his awakening and transmitted the Caodong (Sōtō) lineage.

### 回日弘法 / Return to Japan (1227-1243)

1227年归日后，道元先在京都建仁寺、后在深草兴圣寺弘法。他的严格作风——批判"兼修禅"、拒绝权贵供养——招致比睿山天台势力的强烈反对。1243年，在的的庇护下退居越前（今福井县），建立大佛寺（后改名永平寺），确立了纯粹坐禅的道风。

Returning in 1227, Dōgen taught first at Kennin-ji and then Kōshō-ji in Kyoto. His rigorous stance — criticizing syncretic Zen, refusing aristocratic patronage — provoked fierce opposition from Mount Hiei's Tendai establishment. In 1243, under the patronage of Hatano Yoshishige, he relocated to Echizen (present-day Fukui) and established Eihei-ji, establishing a pure sitting-meditation tradition.

### 晚期著述 / Later Writings (1243-1253)

在永平寺的十年是道元思想最成熟的时期。他完成了《正法眼藏》大部分卷帙，撰写了《永平清规》等寺院规范文本，建立了完整的曹洞宗修行体系。1253年因病赴京都医治途中圆寂，享年53岁。

The decade at Eihei-ji was Dōgen's most philosophically productive period. He composed most of the *Shōbōgenzō*, wrote monastic regulations (*Eihei Shingi*), and built a complete Sōtō praxis. He died en route to Kyoto for medical treatment in 1253, aged 53.

## 重要概念 / Key Concepts

- **只管打坐 Shikantaza** — 坐禅即佛行，不以求悟为目的。详见 [只管打坐](concepts/shikantaza.md)。
  Sitting itself is Buddha-activity, with no goal of attaining enlightenment.

- **修证一等 Practice-Enlightenment Identity** — 修行与证悟不可分割。详见 [修证一等](concepts/practice-enlightenment.md)。
  Practice and realization are inseparable.

- **有时 Uji (Being-Time)** — 时间与存在的非二元统一。详见 [有时](concepts/being-time-uji.md)。
  The non-dual unity of time and being.

- **身心脱落 Shinjin Datsuraku (Dropping Off Body-Mind)** — 坐禅中身心的二元对立消融。
  The dissolution of body-mind duality in sitting.

- **无常佛性 Impermanence-Buddha-Nature** — 无常即佛性，变化不是缺陷。详见 [无常佛性](concepts/impermanence-buddha-nature.md)。
  Impermanence is Buddha-nature; change is not a defect.

- **本证妙修 Hongaku Myōshū** — 从本有的证悟出发的妙修行。
  Wondrous practice arising from original enlightenment.

- **现成公案 Genjōkōan** — 万物皆是公案，日常经验本身即是启示。
  All phenomena are kōans; everyday experience is itself revelation.

- **办道 Bendō** — 致力于佛道，全身心投入修行。
  Wholehearted engagement with the Buddha-Way.

- **非思量 Hishiryō** — 坐禅中的"不思量"状态，超越思考与不思考。
  The state of "non-thinking" in zazen, beyond thought and non-thought.

- **面授 Menju** — 面对面的传授，强调活生生的传承。
  Face-to-face transmission, emphasizing living lineage.

## 思想坐标 / Intellectual Coordinates

道元占据一个独特的思想坐标位置：

Dōgen occupies a unique position in the intellectual landscape:

- **激进非二元 Radical Non-Dualism**：修行/证悟、时间/存在、自我/世界的彻底非二元。
  Thoroughgoing non-duality of practice/enlightenment, time/being, self/world.

- **现象学先驱 Proto-Phenomenologist**：以第一人称经验为出发点，比胡塞尔早七百年。
  Taking first-person experience as the point of departure, seven centuries before Husserl.

- **语言哲学家 Philosopher of Language**：对语言的创造性使用——翻转经典语句、创造新词——本身就是哲学方法。
  Creative use of language — reversing classical phrases, coining neologisms — as philosophical method.

- **存在-时间哲学家 Philosopher of Being-Time**：与海德格尔的"时间性"(Zeitlichkeit) 形成深刻平行。
  Deep parallel with Heidegger's *Zeitlichkeit* (temporality).

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

- **哲学 Philosophy**：与海德格尔 (Heidegger) 的时间-存在论对话是最活跃的研究领域之一。Joan Stambaugh 的比较研究开辟了道元-海德格尔平行研究的新路径。梅洛-庞蒂 (Merleau-Ponty) 的身体现象学与"身心脱落"和"只管打坐"形成深度共鸣——两者都以身体为认知的原点，而非心灵的附属品。怀特海 (Whitehead) 的过程哲学与"无常佛性"有天然的亲和力。
  Dialogue with Heidegger's being-time ontology is among the most active 研究 areas. Joan Stambaugh's comparative work opened a new path for Dōgen-Heidegger parallel studies. Merleau-Ponty's body phenomenology resonates deeply with *shinjin datsuraku* and *shikantaza* — both take the body as the origin of cognition, not the mind's appendage. Whitehead's process 哲学 has natural affinity with impermanence-Buddha-nature.

- **心理学 Psychology**：正念 (mindfulness) 运动的哲学根源可以追溯到道元的坐禅理论，尽管当代正念实践做了大量简化。"临在"(presence) 的概念——全然地活在当下——是"有时"思想在心理治疗领域的应用。具身认知 (embodied cognition) 的研究为"只管打坐"提供了科学验证的框架。
  The philosophical roots of the mindfulness movement can be traced to Dōgen's zazen theory, though contemporary mindfulness practice is heavily simplified. The concept of "presence" — fully inhabiting the present — is an application of *uji* thought in psychotherapy. Embodied cognition 研究 provides a scientific validation framework for *shikantaza*.

- **生态哲学 Eco-Philosophy**：无常佛性与生态整体论的共振是当代最引人注目的应用之一。"一切存在即佛性"的主张为深层生态学 (deep ecology) 提供了存在论基础。万物互即的存在论——草木瓦石皆为佛性——挑战了人类中心主义和主客二元的生态观。道元的"山水经"为当代自然哲学提供了"自然是主体"的思想资源。
  The resonance of impermanence-Buddha-nature with ecological holism is among the most compelling contemporary applications. The claim "all being is Buddha-nature" provides ontological grounding for deep ecology. The ontology of mutual interpenetration — grass, trees, tiles, stones as Buddha-nature — challenges anthropocentrism and subject-object duality in ecological thinking. Dōgen's *Sansuikyō* provides the resource of "nature as subject" for contemporary natural 哲学.

- **艺术 Arts**：禅意美学——极简主义、当下性、物我两忘的创作状态——深受道元影响。日本传统艺术（茶道、花道、庭园设计、书法）中的"当下"美学与"有时"思想直接相关。当代极简主义艺术和设计哲学中，"减到本质"的理念与"身心脱落"形成呼应。
  Zen 美学 — minimalism, presentness, the creative state of self-world dissolution — is deeply influenced by Dōgen. The 美学 of "the present" in Japanese traditional 艺术 (tea ceremony, flower arrangement, garden design, calligraphy) relates directly to *uji* thought. In contemporary minimalist art and design philosophy, the principle of "reducing to essence" echoes *shinjin datsuraku*.

- **教育学 Education**：以"办道"为核心的全身心学习理念——学习不是获取知识的工具性行为，而是存在的转化方式。"修证一等"在教育中的应用意味着：学习过程本身就是教育目标的实现，而非仅仅通向未来结果的手段。
  A whole-body learning approach centered on *bendō* — learning is not instrumental knowledge-acquisition but a mode of existential transformation. Applied to education, *shushō-ittō* means: the learning process itself realizes educational aims, not merely as a means to future outcomes.

## 常见误读 / Common Misreadings

### 1. "道元只是禅修者" / "Dōgen was just a meditator"
道元不仅是实践者，更是日本最重要的哲学家之一。《正法眼藏》的哲学深度可与任何西方哲学巨著比肩。
Dōgen was not merely a practitioner but one of Japan's most important philosophers. The *Shōbōgenzō*'s philosophical depth rivals any Western philosophical masterwork.

### 2. "只管打坐 = 不动" / "Shikantaza means sitting still"
只管打坐不是被动的静止，而是全身心投入的动态静默——一种彻底的临在。
Shikantaza is not passive stillness but dynamically engaged silence — a total presence.

### 3. "有时 = 爱因斯坦相对论" / "Uji equals Einstein's relativity"
有时是现象学的时间-存在论，描述的是主体性经验中时间与存在的不可分离，而非物理学的时间理论。
Uji is a phenomenological being-time ontology, describing the inseparability of time and being in lived experience, not a physical theory of time.

### 4. "道元反对文字" / "Dōgen opposed language"
恰恰相反，道元是佛教传统中最富语言创造性的思想家之一。他不是否定语言，而是以语言超越语言的局限。
Quite the opposite — Dōgen is one of the most linguistically creative thinkers in the Buddhist tradition. He did not reject language but used it to transcend language's limitations.

| 无常佛性 Mujō-Bushō | 怀特海 过程哲学 / Whitehead process 哲学 | 生成即实在 / Becoming as reality | 怀特海的"现实实有"(actual entities) 和道元的"有时"都以事件/过程而非实体为存在论基础。/ Whitehead's "actual entities" and Dōgen's *uji* both ground ontology in events/processes rather than substances. |
