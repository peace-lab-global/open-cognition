# 只管打坐 Shikantaza (Just Sitting)

> "学道者，先须肯信自己本来是佛。"——《办道法》
>
> "To study the Way is to study the self." — *Bendōhō*

## 概念概述 / Overview

"只管打坐"（Shikantaza）是道元禅修方法的核心——也是其哲学最具革命性的主张。字面意义为"只管打坐"：只是坐着，不做任何其他事。但这个"只是"并非贫乏的"仅仅"，而是一个极其丰富的"只是"——坐禅本身就是佛行，坐禅本身就是证悟，不需要任何额外的目标或成果。

*Shikantaza* — literally "just sitting" — is the core of Dōgen's meditation practice and his most revolutionary claim. But this "just" is not a thin, impoverished "merely." It is an enormously rich "just" — sitting itself is Buddha-activity, sitting itself is realization, requiring no additional goal or attainment.

## 历史渊源 / Historical Origins

"只管打坐"这一术语最早由道元的老师如净禅师使用，但道元将其发展为一个完整的哲学-实践体系。如净教导弟子："只管打坐，身心脱落。"道元在如净处获得这一体验后，将其带回日本并系统化为曹洞宗的核心修行法门。值得注意的是，道元的"只管打坐"与中国曹洞宗宏智正觉的"默照禅"有渊源关系，但道元将其推向了更彻底的方向——默照禅仍然保留了"照"（观照）的对象性，而道元的坐禅彻底取消了任何对象性。

The term *shikantaza* was first used by Dōgen's teacher Rujing, but Dōgen developed it into a complete philosophical-practical system. Rujing instructed his students: "Just sit, drop off body and mind." After experiencing this under Rujing, Dōgen brought it to Japan and systematized it as the core practice of Sōtō Zen. Notably, Dōgen's *shikantaza* has roots in the "silent illumination" (*mokushō*) of Chinese Caodong master Hongzhi Zhengjue, but Dōgen pushed it further — silent illumination still retained the object-oriented dimension of "illumination," while Dōgen's zazen thoroughly eliminated all object-orientation.

## 核心论证 / Core Argument

### 坐禅即佛行 / Zazen as Buddha-Activity

道元对坐禅的理解与传统佛教截然不同。在多数佛教传统中，坐禅是达到证悟的"手段"——一种工具性的修行方法。道元彻底颠覆了这一框架：坐禅不是成佛的手段，坐禅本身就是佛行。

Dōgen's understanding of zazen differs radically from most Buddhist traditions. In many traditions, sitting meditation is a "means" to enlightenment — an instrumental practice. Dōgen overturns this framework entirely: zazen is not a means to becoming Buddha; zazen itself is Buddha-activity.

这一主张的逻辑基础是"本证妙修"——众生本来具足佛性（本证），因此从佛性出发的修行（妙修）本身就是佛性的表达，而不是获取佛性的途径。

The logical foundation is *hongaku myōshū* (original enlightenment, wondrous practice): since all beings originally possess Buddha-nature (*hongaku*), practice arising from that nature (*myōshū*) is itself Buddha-nature's expression, not a path to acquiring it.

### 非思量 / Hishiryō (Non-Thinking)

道元在《普劝坐禅仪》中描述了坐禅的意识状态为"非思量"（hishiryō）。这个词来自一则著名的公案：

In *Fukan Zazengi*, Dōgen describes the state of consciousness in zazen as *hishiryō* (non-thinking). This term derives from a famous kōan:

> 药山禅师坐禅时，僧问："兀兀地思量什么？"师曰："思量个不思量底。"曰："不思量底如何思量？"师曰："非思量。"
>
> When Master Yaoshan was sitting, a monk asked: "What are you thinking about so fixedly?" The master said: "I'm thinking about not-thinking." "How do you think about not-thinking?" "Non-thinking (*hishiryō*)."

"非思量"不是"不思考"（那是一种压抑），也不是"胡思乱想"（那是散乱）。它是一种超越思考与不思考对立的意识状态——思维自然流动，但不被执着或排斥。

*Hishiryō* is not "not-thinking" (which would be suppression), nor is it random thought (which would be distraction). It is a state beyond the duality of thinking and non-thinking — thoughts flow naturally, neither grasped nor rejected.

### 身心脱落 / Dropping Off Body and Mind

坐禅的深层经验是"身心脱落"（shinjin datsuraku）——身体与心智的二元对立脱落。这不是身体消失或心智停止，而是"身体-心智"作为认知双极的分离感消融。修行者不再有"我在坐禅"的自我意识，而是坐禅自己在发生。

The deeper experience of zazen is *shinjin datsuraku* — the dropping off of body and mind. This is not the disappearance of body or the cessation of mind, but the dissolution of the separation between body and mind as cognitive poles. The practitioner no longer has the self-consciousness of "I am sitting"; sitting simply happens.

## 具体技法 / Concrete Technique

道元在《普劝坐禅仪》中详细描述了坐禅的具体方法：

In *Fukan Zazengi*, Dōgen describes the concrete method of zazen in detail:

- **坐姿 Posture**：结跏趺坐（全莲花坐）或半跏趺坐（半莲花坐）。脊柱自然挺直，不僵硬也不松懈。耳朵与肩齐，鼻与脐对。
  Full or half lotus posture. The spine naturally upright, neither rigid nor lax. Ears aligned with shoulders, nose aligned with navel.
- **手印 Hand position**：左手在上，右手在下，两手拇指轻触（法界定印）。
  Left hand on top, right hand below, thumbs lightly touching (*hokkai jōin*, cosmic mudra).
- **眼睛 Eyes**：眼睛半开半闭，目光自然落在前方约一米处。不完全闭眼（防止昏沉），也不完全睁开（防止散乱）。
  Eyes half-open, gaze falling naturally about one meter ahead. Neither fully closed (to prevent drowsiness) nor fully open (to prevent distraction).
- **呼吸 Breathing**：自然呼吸，不刻意控制。鼻吸鼻呼，呼吸细长。
  Natural breathing without deliberate control. Inhale and exhale through the nose, breath long and fine.
- **心态 Attitude**：不思善、不思恶，不取不舍。不追求开悟，不排斥杂念。只是坐着，让一切自然发生。
  Neither thinking good nor evil, neither grasping nor rejecting. Not pursuing enlightenment, not排斥 miscellaneous thoughts. Just sitting, letting everything happen naturally.

## 常见误读 / Common Misreadings

- **"只管打坐 = 什么都不想"**：只管打坐不是压抑思想，而是让思想自然流动而不执着。"非思量"不是"无念"，而是超越念与无念的对立。
  *Shikantaza* does not mean suppressing thoughts but letting them flow naturally without attachment. *Hishiryō* is not "no-thought" but transcendence of the duality between thought and no-thought.
- **"只管打坐不需要老师"**：虽然道元强调坐禅本身即足，但他同时强调"面授"（面对面传授）的重要性。坐禅需要在合格教师的指导下进行。
  While Dōgen emphasizes that zazen itself is sufficient, he simultaneously stresses the importance of *menju* (face-to-face transmission). Zazen requires guidance from a qualified teacher.
- **"只管打坐是一种放松术"**：坐禅可能带来放松的效果，但这不是其目的。"只管打坐"是存在的彻底实现，不是压力管理技术。
  Zazen may produce relaxation, but this is not its purpose. *Shikantaza* is the thorough realization of existence, not a stress-management technique.

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

- **正念运动 Mindfulness movement**：当代正念练习（如 MBSR）虽然简化了道元的框架，但"不评判的觉察"与"非思量"有深层共鸣。
  Contemporary mindfulness practices (e.g., MBSR), while simplified, share deep resonance with *hishiryō*'s non-judgmental awareness.
- **具身认知 Embodied cognition**：道元的坐禅是一种彻底的具身实践——认知不是"头脑中的活动"，而是整个身体的存在方式。
  Dōgen's zazen is radically embodied — cognition is not "activity in the head" but the whole body's mode of being.
- **心理学中的"流" Flow states**：契克森米哈赖的"心流"概念与"身心脱落"有有趣的平行——当自我意识消融时，最佳表现发生。
  Csikszentmihalyi's "flow" parallels *shinjin datsuraku* — optimal performance occurs when self-consciousness dissolves.
