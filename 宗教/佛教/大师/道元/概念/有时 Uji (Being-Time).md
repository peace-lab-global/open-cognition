# 有时 Uji (Being-Time)

> "有る時(有时)は高高峰頂に立ち、有る時は深深海底に行く。"
>
> "Sometimes standing on the highest peak, sometimes walking on the deepest ocean floor."

## 概念概述 / Overview

"有时"是道元哲学中最具原创性的概念之一，出自《正法眼藏》的《有时》卷。道元以"有时"一词颠覆了日常语言中"时间"与"存在"的二元分离：时间不是在存在之外流逝的容器，存在也不是在时间中持存的实体——时间即存在，存在即时间。

*Uji* (sometimes rendered "being-time") is one of Dōgen's most original philosophical contributions, articulated in the *Uji* fascicle of *Shōbōgenzō*. Dōgen uses the compound to subvert the everyday duality between "time" and "being": time is not a container that flows outside of existence, nor is being an entity that persists within time. Time is being; being is time.

## 核心论证 / Core Argument

### 有时之有时 / The Time of Being-Time

道元写道："有时之有时、尽己有时、尽他有时。"——每一个"时刻"都是一个完整的存在-时间。一座山是一座山，不仅仅是在某个时间点存在的山；这座山本身就是时间的一种表现形式。同理，一棵松树、一根竹竿、一个念头，都是"有时"的具体实例。

"Sometimes being-time is all times; sometimes it exhausts its own time; sometimes it exhausts another's time." Every "moment" is a complete being-time. A mountain is a mountain not merely existing at some point in time; the mountain itself is a manifestation of time. Likewise, a pine tree, a bamboo stalk, a single thought — all are concrete instances of *uji*.

### 经行 / Passage

道元强调"有时"的"经行"（kyōgyō）——时间的通过。但这种通过不是从一个时刻到下一个时刻的线性流动，而是每一刻自身的完整展开。冬天不是"变成"春天，冬天就是冬天的有时，春天就是春天的有时。"有时"的经行是每一刻自身的圆满实现。

Dōgen emphasizes the "passage" (*kyōgyō*) of being-time. But this passage is not a linear flow from one moment to the next. Rather, each moment is its own complete unfolding. Winter does not "become" spring; winter is winter's being-time, spring is spring's being-time. The passage of *uji* is the full actualization of each moment in itself.

### 有时与"现在" / Uji and "Now"

道元的"现在"（いま）不是一个无限分割的时间点，而是一个存在论的完整事件。"有时"的现在包含了过去与未来——不是作为记忆和预期，而是作为当下存在的完整维度。

Dōgen's "now" (*ima*) is not an infinitely divisible point on a timeline but an ontologically complete event. The "now" of *uji* contains past and future — not as memory and anticipation, but as integral dimensions of present existence.

## 语言哲学 / Linguistic Philosophy

道元在《有时》卷中展示了极具特色的语言哲学方法。他通过对"有时"一词的语法创造性使用，实现了哲学突破。日语中"有る時"（aru toki）通常是一个时间副词短语，意为"有时候"——一个普通的、不起眼的时间表达。道元将"有"（存在）和"時"（时间）这两个概念焊合为一个不可分割的复合词"有时"（uji），从而在语言层面实现了存在与时间的本体论统一。

Dōgen demonstrates a distinctive 哲学 of language in the *Uji* fascicle. He achieves philosophical breakthrough through grammatical creativity with the word *uji*. In Japanese, *aru toki* is ordinarily a temporal adverb phrase meaning "sometimes" — a common, unremarkable expression. Dōgen welds the two concepts of *u* (being/existence) and *ji* (time) into the inseparable compound *uji*, thereby enacting the ontological unity of being and time at the linguistic level.

这种方法——以语言的创造性使用来突破概念的局限——是道元哲学的标志性特征。他不是"描述"时间-存在的统一，而是通过造词来"实现"这种统一。语言在这里不是表达工具，而是哲学行动本身。这一点使道元与维特根斯坦的语言哲学形成有趣的对话：两者都认识到语言的界限即世界的界限，但道元的应对方式不是"沉默"，而是创造性地重塑语言。

This method — using creative language to break through conceptual limitations — is a hallmark of Dōgen's 哲学. He does not "describe" the unity of being-time but "enacts" it through word-creation. Language here is not an expressive tool but philosophical action itself. This makes Dōgen an interesting dialogue partner for Wittgenstein's 哲学 of language: both recognize that the limits of language are the limits of the world, but Dōgen's response is not silence but creative reshaping of language.

## 与海德格尔的对话 / Dialogue with Heidegger

道元的"有时"与海德格尔的"时间性"（Zeitlichkeit）形成深刻平行：

Dōgen's *uji* and Heidegger's *Zeitlichkeit* share profound parallels:

- **反实体论 Anti-substantialism**：两者都拒绝将时间视为独立于存在的"流"。
  Both reject time as an independent "stream" separate from being.
- **存在即时间 Being as temporality**：此在的存在方式就是时间性；有时即是存在本身。
  Dasein's mode of being is temporality; *uji* is being itself.
- **当下性 The present**：海德格尔的"瞬间"（Augenblick）与道元的"有时之现在"都强调当下的存在论完整性。
  Heidegger's *Augenblick* and Dōgen's "now of *uji*" both stress the ontological completeness of the present.

关键区别在于：海德格尔的时间性以此在为中心，而道元的"有时"将时间-存在推广到万物——松树、竹竿、瓦砾，都是"有时"。

A key difference: Heidegger's temporality centers on Dasein, while Dōgen's *uji* extends being-time to all phenomena — pine trees, bamboo, tiles and rubble are all *uji*.

## 实践意涵 / Practical Implications

"有时"不是抽象理论，而是坐禅经验的哲学表述。在坐禅中，修行者不是"在时间中"坐着——坐禅本身就是时间的完整表现。这意味着：

*Uji* is not abstract theory but a philosophical articulation of zazen experience. In sitting, the practitioner is not sitting "in time" — sitting itself is time's complete expression:

- **当下的完整性**：每一坐都是完整的佛行，不是通向未来的手段。修行者不需要等待"某一天"才能证悟——此刻的坐禅就是证悟的全部展现。
  Each sitting is complete Buddha-activity, not a means to a future end. The practitioner need not wait for "someday" to realize awakening — this very sitting is the full manifestation of realization.
- **全时间的投入**：修行者的全部存在——过去、现在、未来——在此刻展开。"有时"的现在不是时间线上的一个点，而是包含了所有时间维度的完整事件。
  The practitioner's entire existence — past, present, future — unfolds here. The "now" of *uji* is not a point on a timeline but a complete event containing all temporal dimensions.
- **万物皆时**：环境、身体、念头，都是"有时"的不同表现形式。坐禅时的风声、身体的酸痛、浮现的回忆，都不是"干扰"——它们都是"有时"的具体实例。
  Environment, body, thoughts — all are different manifestations of *uji*. The sound of wind during zazen, the ache in the body, the memories that surface — none of these are "disturbances." They are all concrete instances of *uji*.
- **生死即有时**：生与死不是"有时"的对立面，而是"有时"的两种表现形式。对死亡的恐惧来自将死亡视为"时间的终结"，而在"有时"的框架中，死亡本身就是时间的完整表现。
  Birth and death are not opposites to *uji* but two of its manifestations. Fear of death arises from viewing death as "time's end," but within the *uji* framework, death itself is time's complete expression.

## 常见误读 / Common Misreadings

- **"有时 = 爱因斯坦相对论"**：有时不是物理学的时间理论，而是现象学的时间-存在论。它描述的是主体性经验中时间与存在的不可分离，而非光速不变或时间膨胀。
  *Uji* is not a physics theory of time but a phenomenological being-time ontology. It describes the inseparability of time and being in lived experience, not the constancy of light speed or time dilation.
- **"有时 = 活在当下"**：有时不是通俗心理学中的"活在当下"口号。"当下"在"有时"中是一个极其丰富的存在论概念，包含了全部的时间维度和存在维度。
  *Uji* is not the pop-psychology slogan of "living in the moment." The "now" in *uji* is an enormously rich ontological concept, containing all dimensions of time and being.
