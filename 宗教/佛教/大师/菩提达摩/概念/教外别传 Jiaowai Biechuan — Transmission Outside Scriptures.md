# 教外别传 Jiaowai Biechuan — Transmission Outside Scriptures

> "教外别传，不立文字；直指人心，见性成佛。"
> "A special transmission outside the scriptures; not depending on words and letters; directly pointing to the human mind; seeing into one's nature and attaining Buddhahood."
> ——禅宗四句宗旨 / The Four-Line Motto of Chan

---

## 概述 / Overview

"教外别传"（Jiaowai Biechuan）是禅宗最具标志性的自我定义之一，意为"在经教体系之外的特别传承"。这一概念主张：真正的佛法传承不在文字经典之中，而在于师徒之间心心相传的觉悟体验。虽然这四句宗旨被归于达摩名下，但学界普遍认为它们是后世禅宗对自身精神的总结，而非达摩亲说。

"Transmission outside the scriptures" (教外别传, Jiaowai Biechuan) is one of Chan Buddhism's most iconic self-definitions, meaning "a special transmission outside the doctrinal system." This concept asserts that true Dharma transmission lies not in written scriptures but in the awakening experience transmitted mind-to-mind between master and disciple. Although this four-line motto is attributed to Bodhidharma, scholars generally agree it is a later Chan summary of its own spirit rather than Bodhidharma's own words.

然而，这一精神的源头确实可以追溯至达摩。达摩虽然"藉教悟宗"（借助《楞伽经》来契入宗旨），但其核心关注始终是超越文字的直接体证。他将袈裟和心法传给慧可的行为，确立了禅宗"以心传心"的传承模式。

However, the source of this spirit can indeed be traced to Bodhidharma. Although Bodhidharma "used teachings to awaken to the source" (relying on the *Lankavatara Sutra* to penetrate the principle), his core concern was always the direct experiential realization that transcends words. His act of transmitting the robe and mind-Dharma to Huike established the Chan mode of "mind-to-mind transmission."

---

## 概念解析 / Conceptual Analysis

"教外别传"这一概念包含四个相互关联的维度：

The concept of "transmission outside the scriptures" contains four interrelated dimensions:

### 1. 教外 Outside the Teachings

"教"（jiao）指经教体系——一切以文字形式记录的佛教教义。"教外"不是否定经典的价值，而是指出经典的局限：文字只能描述实相，不能等同于实相。如同菜单不等于饭菜本身，地图不等于实际的地域。

"教" (jiao) refers to the doctrinal system — all Buddhist teachings recorded in written form. "Outside the teachings" does not deny the value of scriptures but points out their limitation: words can only describe reality, they cannot be identical to reality. Just as a menu is not the meal itself, a map is not the actual territory.

达摩本人的态度是"藉教悟宗"——借助经典来契入超越经典的实相。这意味着经典是桥梁，不是终点。禅宗后来的"不立文字"是对这一立场的更激进表达。

Bodhidharma's own attitude was "using teachings to awaken to the source" — relying on scriptures to penetrate a reality that transcends scriptures. This means scriptures are bridges, not destinations. Chan's later "not establishing words and letters" is a more radical expression of this stance.

### 2. 别传 Special Transmission

"别传"意味着一种独特的、不同于常规经教传承的传递方式。在常规佛教传承中，师父通过讲授经典来传授佛法——知识从文字到文字，从语言到语言。而"别传"则是一种超越文字的传递——师父将自己亲证的觉悟体验直接传递给弟子。

"Special transmission" implies a unique mode of transmission distinct from conventional doctrinal transmission. In conventional Buddhist transmission, the master teaches Dharma through lecturing on scriptures — knowledge passes from text to text, from words to words. "Special transmission" is a transmission that transcends words — the master directly transmits their own realized awakening experience to the disciple.

### 3. 不立文字 Not Establishing Words

"不立文字"不是"不用文字"，而是"不以文字为终极依据"。禅宗大量使用文字（公案、偈颂、语录），但始终提醒修行者：文字只是工具，不可执著。这一立场与维特根斯坦的"梯子隐喻"有相似之处——语言是帮助攀爬的梯子，到达高处后就应该放下。

"Not establishing words" is not "not using words" but "not taking words as the ultimate authority." Chan makes extensive use of words (koans, verses, recorded sayings) but always reminds practitioners: words are merely tools, not to be grasped. This stance resembles Wittgenstein's "ladder metaphor" — language is a ladder to help one climb; upon reaching the height, one should let it go.

### 4. 直指人心 Directly Pointing to the Human Mind

"直指"（direct pointing）是禅宗教学方法的核心特征。与经教的间接方式（通过理论分析来引导理解）不同，禅宗的"直指"是一种直接的、当下即刻的指向——用言语、沉默、棒喝、动作等一切方式来直接触发弟子的觉悟。

"Direct pointing" is the core characteristic of Chan teaching methodology. Unlike the indirect approach of doctrinal teaching (guiding understanding through theoretical analysis), Chan's "direct pointing" is immediate and instantaneous — using speech, silence, shouts, blows, gestures, and every possible means to directly trigger the disciple's awakening.

## 达摩的传承实践 / Bodhidharma's Transmission Practice

达摩传法给慧可的过程，是"教外别传"精神的典范实践：

Bodhidharma's Dharma transmission to Huike is the exemplary practice of the "transmission outside scriptures" spirit:

### 传袈裟 Transmitting the Robe

达摩将袈裟传给慧可作为法脉凭证。这一物质信件的传递象征着法脉的连续性——从师父到弟子，一代接一代。袈裟本身不是佛法，但它是传承关系的外在标记。这一传统延续到六祖慧能，此后因袈裟引起的争端而被终止。

Bodhidharma transmitted the robe to Huike as a credential of the Dharma lineage. The transmission of this material symbol represents the continuity of the lineage — from master to disciple, generation after generation. The robe itself is not the Dharma, but it is an external marker of the transmission relationship. This tradition continued to the Sixth Patriarch Huineng, after which it was discontinued due to disputes over the robe.

### 传心法 Transmitting the Mind-Dharma

比袈裟更重要的是"心法"的传递。达摩在传法时考察了弟子们的理解层次：

More important than the robe was the transmission of "mind-Dharma." When transmitting the Dharma, Bodhidharma examined his disciples' levels of understanding:

> 道副说："如我所见，不执文字，不离文字，而为道用。"达摩说："汝得吾皮。"
> 尼总持说："我今所解，如庆喜见阿閦佛国，一见更不再见。"达摩说："汝得吾肉。"
> 道育说："四大本空，五阴非有，而我见处，无一法可得。"达摩说："汝得吾骨。"
> 慧可礼拜后默然依位而立。达摩说："汝得吾髓。"

> Daofu said: "As I see it, one should neither cling to nor abandon words and letters, but use them as the Way." Bodhidharma said: "You have attained my skin."
> Nun Zongchi said: "My understanding now is like Ananda seeing the Buddha-land of Akshobhya — once seen, never to be seen again." Bodhidharma said: "You have attained my flesh."
> Daoyu said: "The four elements are inherently empty, the five aggregates do not exist; in my seeing, there is not a single dharma to be grasped." Bodhidharma said: "You have attained my bones."
> Huike bowed and then stood silently in his place. Bodhidharma said: "You have attained my marrow."

慧可的沉默——超越了一切语言表达——被达摩认定为最深的理解。这一"沉默的传承"是"教外别传"精神的极致体现。

Huike's silence — transcending all linguistic expression — was recognized by Bodhidharma as the deepest understanding. This "transmission through silence" is the ultimate expression of the "transmission outside scriptures" spirit.

## 法脉传承体系 / The Dharma Lineage System

"教外别传"催生了禅宗独特的法脉传承体系：

"Transmission outside the scriptures" gave rise to Chan's unique Dharma lineage system:

**西天二十八祖 Twenty-Eight Indian Patriarchs**：从摩诃迦叶到达摩，代表了从佛陀到达摩的印度传承。
From Mahakasyapa to Bodhidharma, representing the Indian lineage from the Buddha to Bodhidharma.

**东土六祖 Six Chinese Patriarchs**：达摩 → 慧可 → 僧璨 → 道信 → 弘忍 → 慧能。这是中国禅宗的核心传承链。
Bodhidharma → Huike → Sengcan → Daoxin → Hongren → Huineng. This is the core transmission chain of Chinese Chan Buddhism.

**五家七宗 Five Houses and Seven Schools**：慧能之后，禅宗分化为多个流派——临济、曹洞、沩仰、云门、法眼五家，其中临济又分出黄龙和杨岐两派。每个流派都声称自己的法脉可追溯至达摩。
After Huineng, Chan split into multiple schools — the five houses of Linji, Caodong, Weiyang, Yunmen, and Fayan, with Linji further dividing into Huanglong and Yangqi branches. Each school claims its lineage traces back to Bodhidharma.

## 与西方知识传承的比较 / Comparison with Western Knowledge Transmission

"教外别传"与西方知识传统中的传承方式形成了有意义的对照：

"Transmission outside the scriptures" forms meaningful contrasts with transmission modes in Western intellectual traditions:

- **学术传承 Academic Transmission**：西方学术传统以论文、书籍、讲座为主要传承方式——高度依赖文字。禅宗的"教外别传"提供了一种替代性的知识传递模式，强调师徒间的直接体验传递。
  Western academic tradition relies primarily on papers, books, and lectures — highly text-dependent. Chan's "transmission outside scriptures" offers an alternative model of knowledge transmission, emphasizing direct experiential transmission between master and disciple.

- **师徒制 Apprenticeship**：西方的手艺师徒制（如中世纪行会）在某些方面与禅宗的"教外别传"相似——都强调通过共同生活和直接示范来传递不可言说的技能知识（tacit knowledge）。
  Western craft apprenticeship (such as medieval guilds) resembles Chan's "transmission outside scriptures" in some respects — both emphasize transmitting inexpressible skill knowledge (tacit knowledge) through shared living and direct demonstration.

- **波兰尼的默会知识 Polanyi's Tacit Knowledge**：迈克尔·波兰尼（Michael Polanyi）提出的"默会知识"（tacit knowledge）——"我们所知道的比我们能说出的多"——与"教外别传"精神高度契合。
  Michael Polanyi's "tacit knowledge" — "we know more than we can tell" — closely aligns with the spirit of "transmission outside scriptures."
