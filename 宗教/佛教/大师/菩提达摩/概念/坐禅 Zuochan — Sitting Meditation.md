# 坐禅 Zuochan — Sitting Meditation

> "面壁而坐，终日默然，人莫之测，谓之壁观。"
> "Sitting facing the wall in silence all day long, none could fathom him; this was called wall-gazing."
> ——道宣《续高僧传》 / Daoxuan, *Continued Biographies of Eminent Monks*

---

## 概述 / Overview

坐禅（Zuochan，sitting meditation；日语：zazen）是禅宗最基本的修行形式，其原型可追溯至达摩在嵩山少林寺面壁九年的实践。"坐禅"一词由"坐"（身体姿态）和"禅"（冥想，源自梵文 dhyana）组成，字面意思是"坐着进行禅修"。

Sitting meditation (坐禅, Zuochan; Japanese: zazen) is the most fundamental practice form in Chan Buddhism, its archetype traceable to Bodhidharma's practice of facing the wall at Shaolin Temple on Mount Song for nine years. The term "zuochan" combines "坐" (sitting, a physical posture) and "禅" (meditation, from Sanskrit dhyana), literally meaning "sitting to practice meditation."

达摩虽然以"壁观"为其禅法核心，但壁观的实施方式就是坐禅——面壁而坐。达摩在少林寺的实践确立了坐禅在中国禅宗中的核心地位，这一传统此后从未中断。

Although Bodhidharma centered his Chan method on "wall-gazing," the mode of implementing wall-gazing was sitting meditation — sitting facing the wall. Bodhidharma's practice at Shaolin Temple established sitting meditation's central position in Chinese Chan Buddhism, a tradition that has continued unbroken ever since.

## 达摩的坐禅实践 / Bodhidharma's Sitting Practice

### 面壁九年 / Nine Years Facing the Wall

达摩在嵩山少林寺的九年面壁，是禅宗史上最具象征意义的修行事件。这一实践包含几个重要层面：

Bodhidharma's nine years facing the wall at Shaolin Temple on Mount Song is the most symbolically powerful practice event in Chan history. This practice encompasses several important dimensions:

**时间维度 Temporal Dimension**：九年是一个极长的修行时间。这一时间长度本身就传达了一种信息：真正的觉悟需要极大的决心和持久的投入。然而，从顿悟的角度看，九年面壁也可以理解为：即使在顿悟之后，持续的坐禅修行仍然是必要的——悟后的修行（保任）与悟前的修行同样重要。

Nine years is an extraordinarily long practice duration. This length itself communicates a message: true awakening requires immense determination and sustained commitment. Yet from the perspective of sudden enlightenment, nine years of wall-gazing can also be understood as: even after sudden awakening, continued sitting practice remains necessary — post-awakening cultivation (preservation) is as important as pre-awakening cultivation.

**身体维度 Physical Dimension**：面壁而坐要求一种稳定的、不动摇的身体姿态。身体的安定为心灵的安定创造了条件。达摩的坐禅姿态虽然缺乏详细的早期文献描述，但可以推断包括：结跏趺坐（全莲花坐或半莲花坐）、面向墙壁、双目可能半开或闭合。

Sitting facing a wall requires a stable, unmoving physical posture. Physical stability creates conditions for mental stability. Although detailed early textual descriptions of Bodhidharma's sitting posture are lacking, it can be inferred to include: the lotus or half-lotus position, facing the wall, with eyes either half-open or closed.

**社会维度 Social Dimension**：达摩选择远离人群，在少林寺的僻静处独自修行。这种隐修方式与梁武帝的宫廷佛教形成鲜明对比——达摩拒绝了宗教政治化的道路，选择了纯粹的修行道路。

Bodhidharma chose to withdraw from society, practicing alone in a secluded area of Shaolin Temple. This eremitic approach starkly contrasts with Emperor Wu of Liang's court Buddhism — Bodhidharma rejected the path of religious politicization, choosing instead the path of pure cultivation.

## 坐禅的发展与演变 / Development and Evolution of Sitting Meditation

### 达摩至弘忍时期 Bodhidharma to Hongren (6th–7th Century)

在早期禅宗（从达摩到五祖弘忍），坐禅主要以"壁观"为特征——面向墙壁，减少外在刺激，专注于内心的安定。东山法门（弘忍的教学中心）时期，坐禅开始与其他修行方法结合，形成了更系统的禅修体系。

In early Chan (from Bodhidharma to the Fifth Patriarch Hongren), sitting meditation was primarily characterized by "wall-gazing" — facing the wall, reducing external stimuli, focusing on inner stabilization. During the East Mountain Dharma Gate period (Hongren's teaching center), sitting meditation began to integrate with other practice methods, forming a more systematic meditation system.

### 南北宗分化 Northern-Southern Split (8th Century)

北宗神秀的坐禅强调"渐修"——通过持续的坐禅来逐步净化心灵，如同"时时勤拂拭"。南宗慧能则对形式化的坐禅提出了挑战，主张"外离相为禅，内不乱为定"——真正的禅定不在于坐的姿态，而在于心是否不被外相所迷乱。

Northern School Shenxiu's sitting meditation emphasized "gradual cultivation" — through continuous sitting to gradually purify the mind, like "constantly wiping diligently." Southern School Huineng challenged formalized sitting meditation, asserting "external detachment from appearances is Chan, internal non-disturbance is samadhi" — true meditation does not depend on sitting posture but on whether the mind is undisturbed by external appearances.

### 道元的"只管打坐" Dogen's "Just Sitting" (13th Century)

日本曹洞宗创始人道元将坐禅推向了新的理论高度。他提出"只管打坐"（shikantaza）——坐禅不是为了获得开悟，坐禅本身就是开悟的完全表达。"修证一如"（practice and realization are one）——修行与觉悟不是两个分离的阶段，坐禅的每一刻都完整地包含了觉悟。

Japanese Soto school founder Dogen elevated sitting meditation to new theoretical heights. He proposed "just sitting" (shikantaza) — sitting meditation is not performed to attain enlightenment; sitting meditation itself is the complete expression of enlightenment. "Practice-realization are one" (修證一如) — cultivation and awakening are not two separate stages; every moment of sitting meditation completely contains awakening.

### 默照禅与看话禅 Silent Illumination and Koan Introspection (12th Century)

南宋时期，坐禅发展出两大流派：
- **默照禅 Silent Illumination**（宏智正觉 Hongzhi Zhengjue）：强调静坐中的纯粹觉照——在完全的静默中保持清醒的觉知
- **看话禅 Koan Introspection**（大慧宗杲 Dahui Zonggao）：在坐禅中参究公案话头——以"无"字公案等为对象进行深度参究

During the Southern Song period, sitting meditation developed into two major streams:
- **Silent Illumination** (Hongzhi Zhengjue): Emphasizes pure awareness within stillness — maintaining lucid awareness in complete silence
- **Koan Introspection** (Dahui Zonggao): Investigating koan phrases during sitting meditation — deeply contemplating cases like the "wu" (mu) koan

## 坐禅的身心效应 / Physical and Mental Effects of Sitting Meditation

### 生理层面 Physiological Level

现代科学研究发现，长期坐禅实践可以带来多种生理变化：

Modern scientific 研究 has found that long-term sitting meditation practice can produce various physiological changes:

- 降低皮质醇（压力激素）水平 / Reduced cortisol (stress hormone) levels
- 改善心率变异性（HRV），增强自律神经系统的平衡 / Improved heart rate variability (HRV), enhancing autonomic nervous system balance
- 增加大脑前额叶皮层的灰质密度 / Increased gray matter density in the prefrontal cortex
- 减少杏仁核（情绪反应中心）的活动 / Reduced amygdala (emotional response center) activity
- 改善免疫功能 / Improved immune function

### 心理层面 Psychological Level

- 增强注意力的稳定性和持续性 / Enhanced attentional stability and sustainability
- 减少反刍思维（rumination）和焦虑 / Reduced rumination and anxiety
- 提高情绪调节能力 / Improved emotion regulation capacity
- 增强自我觉察和元认知能力 / Enhanced self-awareness and metacognitive capacity
- 促进"去中心化"（decentering）——观察念头而不认同念头 / Promotes "decentering" — observing thoughts without identifying with them

## 坐禅的方法要点 / Key Methodological Points

虽然不同流派的坐禅方法各有特色，但以下要点是共通的：

Although sitting meditation methods vary across schools, the following key points are shared:

1. **调身 Regulating the Body**：采用稳定的坐姿（全莲花坐、半莲花坐或椅坐），脊柱自然挺直，双手结定印（或禅定印），双肩放松。
   Adopt a stable sitting posture (full lotus, half lotus, or chair sitting), spine naturally erect, hands forming the dhyana mudra (meditation seal), shoulders relaxed.

2. **调息 Regulating the Breath**：自然呼吸，不刻意控制。逐渐使呼吸变得深长、细微、均匀。数息（数呼吸）是常用的入门方法。
   Breathe naturally without deliberate control. Gradually allow breathing to become deep, subtle, and even. Breath counting is a common introductory method.

3. **调心 Regulating the Mind**：不追随念头，不压制念头，也不刻意造作任何心理状态。让心自然地安住于当下。当心散乱时，温和地将注意力带回。
   Neither follow thoughts, nor suppress thoughts, nor deliberately create any mental state. Allow the mind to naturally abide in the present. When the mind wanders, gently bring attention back.

4. **日常持续 Daily Continuity**：坐禅不是一次性的活动，而是日常的修行。早期禅宗丛林制度中，每天安排多个时段的坐禅，形成严格的修行节奏。
   Sitting meditation is not a one-time activity but a daily practice. In early Chan monastic regulations, multiple daily sitting periods were scheduled, forming a rigorous practice rhythm.
