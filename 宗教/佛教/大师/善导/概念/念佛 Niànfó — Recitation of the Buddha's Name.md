# 念佛 Niànfó

> "念念不舍。"——善导
> "Never ceasing even for a single thought." — Shandao

---

## 定义 Definition

念佛（niànfó，日语 nembutsu）是净土宗最核心的修行方法，字面意为"忆念佛"或"称念佛名"。在善导的净土思想体系中，念佛特指口称"南无阿弥陀佛"（Namo Amituofo / Namo Amitābhāya Buddhāya）六个字，即"称名念佛"。这不同于更广义的念佛——后者可以包括观想佛的形象（观想念佛）、忆念佛的功德（忆念念佛）、或实相念佛（证入佛的法身实相）。善导将这些形式做了清晰的区分，并认定称名念佛是阿弥陀佛本愿所指定的、一切众生都能修行的"正定业"。

Nianfo (Chinese niànfó, Japanese nembutsu) is the most essential practice of the Pure Land school, literally meaning "to recollect the Buddha" or "to recite the Buddha's Name." In Shandao's Pure Land framework, nianfo specifically refers to the vocal recitation of "Namo Amituofo" (Namo Amitābhāya Buddhāya) — the six-character formula known as "verbal nianfo" (chēngmíng niànfó). This differs from broader definitions of nianfo, which may include visualizing the Buddha's form (contemplative nianfo), recollecting the Buddha's virtues (remembrance nianfo), or realizing the Buddha's Dharmakāya reality (ultimate-reality nianfo). Shandao clearly distinguished these forms and identified verbal nianfo as the "rightly established practice" (zhèngdìng yè) designated by Amitābha's Original Vow — a practice accessible to all beings.

## 念佛的历史演变 Historical Evolution of Nianfo

### 印度起源 Indian Origins

念佛的概念可追溯至早期佛教的"佛随念"（Buddhānusmṛti），即忆念佛的十种功德名号。在大乘佛教兴起后，念佛发展为对特定佛陀（如阿弥陀佛、药师佛）的忆念和称名。《般舟三昧经》（Pratyutpanna Samādhi Sūtra，约公元1世纪）是最早提倡通过持续念佛来见佛的禅观经典之一。

The concept of nianfo traces back to early Buddhism's "recollection of the Buddha" (Buddhānusmṛti) — the practice of recalling the Buddha's ten epithets. With the rise of Mahāyāna Buddhism, nianfo evolved into the recollection and recitation of specific Buddhas such as Amitābha and Medicine Buddha. The Pratyutpanna Samādhi Sūtra (c. 1st century CE) is among the earliest sūtras to advocate sustained nianfo as a means of seeing the Buddha in meditation.

### 中国早期发展 Early Chinese Development

在中国，念佛实践经历了从观想到称名的转变。东晋慧远（334–416）在庐山结"白莲社"，提倡观想念佛，但这主要面向知识精英。昙鸾（476–542）受印度论师菩提流支的影响，开始强调称名念佛的殊胜性，认为口称佛号即是乘托阿弥陀佛的本愿力。道绰（562–645）进一步发展这一思想，提出"末法时期"众生根机低劣，称名念佛是唯一可行的修行道路。

In China, nianfo practice underwent a transformation from contemplation to recitation. Huiyuan of the Eastern Jin (334–416) established the "White Lotus Society" at Lushan, promoting contemplative nianfo, but this was oriented toward intellectual elites. Tanluan (476–542), influenced by the Indian master Bodhiruci, began emphasizing the superiority of verbal nianfo, arguing that vocal recitation means relying upon Amitābha's vow-power. Daochuo (562–645) developed this further, proposing that in the "Dharma-Ending Age" beings have inferior capacities, making verbal nianfo the only viable practice.

### 善导的革命性诠释 Shandao's Revolutionary Interpretation

善导在继承昙鸾、道绰的基础上，完成了念佛理论的最终确立。他在《观经四帖疏》中提出了一个关键性的论证：《观经》虽然教授了十六种观想法门，但佛陀讲述此经的真正意图是要引导众生归入称名念佛。善导以"望佛本愿"（着眼于佛的本愿）为诠释原则，论证阿弥陀佛的第十八愿明确指定了"乃至十念"的称名为往生正因。

Building upon the foundations of Tanluan and Daochuo, Shandao completed the definitive establishment of nianfo theory. In his Commentary on the Contemplation Sūtra, he advanced a crucial argument: although the Sūtra teaches sixteen contemplation methods, the Buddha's true intent was to guide beings toward verbal nianfo. Shandao employed the hermeneutical principle of "looking to the Buddha's Original Vow" (wàng fó běnyuàn), arguing that Amitābha's Eighteenth Vow explicitly designates "even ten recitations" as the cause of rebirth.

## 称名念佛的修行方法 Practice of Verbal Nianfo

### 基本方法 Basic Method

称名念佛的修行极其简洁：以信心口称"南无阿弥陀佛"六字名号。善导对此的要求是"一心专念"——专注而不散乱地称念，不论行走、站立、端坐还是躺卧（"行住坐卧"），不论时间长短（"不问时节久近"），持续不断地念诵（"念念不舍"）。

The practice of verbal nianfo is remarkably simple: recite the six-character Name "Namo Amituofo" with faith. Shandao's requirements are "single-minded exclusive recitation" — chanting with focus and without distraction, whether walking, standing, sitting, or lying down, regardless of the duration of time, and never ceasing.

### 数目与定时 Number and Timing

善导没有规定固定的念佛次数，但在《往生礼赞》中提到"日念三万、五万、十万声"等不同层次的精进标准。后世净土行者发展出"计数念佛"（用念珠计数）和"定时念佛"（固定时段专心念佛）等方法。善导的核心关切不在于数量，而在于"相续"——念佛成为生命的常态。

Shandao did not prescribe a fixed number of recitations, but in the Hymns of Rebirth Praise he mentions varying levels of diligence such as "30,000, 50,000, or 100,000 recitations per day." Later Pure Land practitioners developed methods such as "counted nianfo" (using prayer beads) and "scheduled nianfo" (dedicated time periods for recitation). Shandao's central concern was not quantity but continuity — making nianfo the constant backdrop of one's life.

### 信愿行的统一 Unity of Faith, Vow, and Practice

善导强调称名念佛必须与"三心"（至诚心、深心、回向发愿心）相应。单纯口念佛号而无真实信心，不能往生。因此，称名念佛不是机械的重复，而是信愿行的统一体——信心为根本，愿生净土为方向，称名为具体实践。

Shandao emphasized that verbal nianfo must accord with the "Three Minds" (sincere mind, deep mind, and mind of merit-transfer and vow). Mere vocal recitation without genuine faith cannot lead to rebirth. Therefore, nianfo is not mechanical repetition, but the unity of faith, vow, and practice — with faith as the root, the vow for rebirth as the direction, and recitation as the concrete practice.

## 念佛与其他修行 Nianfo and Other Practices

善导将念佛置于整个佛教修行体系的顶端。他认为在"末法时期"（佛法衰微的时代），众生的根机已无法胜任复杂的禅定和深奥的义学修行，而称名念佛是佛为末法众生特别开设的"易行道"（easy path），与"难行道"（difficult path）形成对比。这一判教思路源自龙树《十住毗婆沙论》中的"难易二道"说，经昙鸾、道绰传承，至善导而集大成。

Shandao placed nianfo at the apex of the entire Buddhist practice system. He held that in the "Dharma-Ending Age" (an era of declining Dharma), beings' capacities are no longer adequate for complex meditation or profound doctrinal study, and that verbal nianfo is the "Easy Path" (yìxíng dào) that the Buddha specially opened for beings of this age, in contrast to the "Difficult Path" (nánxíng dào). This taxonomy of teachings derives from Nāgārjuna's Treatise on the Ten Stages discourse on the "Easy and Difficult Paths," transmitted through Tanluan and Daochuo, and brought to completion by Shandao.

## 念佛在日本的发展 Nianfo in Japan

善导的念佛思想传入日本后产生了深远的影响。法然（1133–1212）在《选择本愿念佛集》中全面继承了善导的称名念佛理论，创立日本净土宗。亲鸾（1173–1263）则进一步发展，提出"信心为本"——念佛不是修行的手段，而是信心的自然流露，是佛恩的报谢。亲鸾甚至主张"恶人正机"——越是罪业深重的凡夫，越是阿弥陀佛救度的首要对象。这一思想将善导的念佛理论推向了更为激进的结论。

After Shandao's nianfo thought was transmitted to Japan, it exerted profound influence. Hōnen (1133–1212) fully inherited Shandao's verbal nianfo theory in his Senchaku Hongan Nenbutsu Shū, founding the Japanese Jōdo-shū. Shinran (1173–1263) went further, proposing "faith as the root" — nianfo is not a means of practice but the natural expression of faith, an act of gratitude for the Buddha's benevolence. Shinran even advanced the doctrine of "the evil person as the true object" — the more deeply sinful an ordinary being, the more they are the primary object of Amitābha's salvation. This pushed Shandao's nianfo theory to its most radical conclusion.

## 当代实践 Contemporary Practice

今天，念佛是东亚佛教最普遍的修行方式之一。中国净土宗寺院每日早晚课诵中都包含念佛环节，"打佛七"（连续七天专修念佛）是最常见的精进修法。日本的"念仏"（nembutsu）实践形式多样，从净土宗的称名念佛到净土真宗的信心念佛，各有侧重。在全球范围内，随着正念冥想（mindfulness）的流行，念佛作为一种专注修行方式也开始受到西方学者的关注。

Today, nianfo is one of the most widespread practices in East Asian Buddhism. Chinese Pure Land temples include nianfo in their daily morning and evening liturgies, and "Buddha-recitation retreats" (dǎ fó qī — seven days of intensive nianfo) are the most common intensive practice. Japanese nembutsu takes diverse forms, from Jōdo-shū's vocal recitation to Jōdo Shinshū's faith-centered nianfo, each with its own emphasis. Globally, as mindfulness meditation gains popularity, nianfo as a form of concentrative practice has begun to attract the attention of Western scholars.
