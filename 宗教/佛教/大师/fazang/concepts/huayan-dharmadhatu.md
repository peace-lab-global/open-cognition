# 华严法界 Huayan Dharmadhatu

## 中文

### 概念定位

"法界"（梵文 dharmadhatu）是华严宗最核心的概念，也是法藏整个哲学体系的基石。在原始佛教中，"法界"一词主要指一切法的范围或领域；在中观学派中，法界与空性（sunyata）同义；在唯识学中，法界指真如（tathata）。法藏对"法界"进行了创造性的重新诠释，使其成为华严哲学的总纲。

### 法藏的诠释

在法藏的体系中，"法界"不再是一个静态的范畴，而是一个动态的、全息的缘起场域。法界既是万法的总称（一切法即法界），又是万法的本体（法界即真如），更是万法之间缘起关系的总体结构。

法藏在《华严经探玄记》中提出，法界有三个面向：

1. **法界的"体"**——即真如、如来藏，是法界的本体论根基。
2. **法界的"相"**——即事法界中一切差别现象，是法界的显现。
3. **法界的"用"**——即法界缘起中万法之间的互摄互入，是法界的功能。

体、相、用三者不是分离的，而是圆融无碍的——这就是华严法界与早期佛教法界概念的根本区别。

### 法界与缘起的融合

法藏最具革命性的贡献在于将"法界"与"缘起"完全融合。传统佛教讲"缘起"（pratityasamutpada），强调的是条件性的因果关系——有此故有彼，此灭故彼灭。法藏则提出"法界缘起"——一切法同时、互相、无余地缘起。

这意味着：

- **同时性**：不是先有A后有B的线性因果，而是A与B同时互为因果。
- **互遍性**：每一法不仅与相邻的法有缘起关系，而是与法界中一切法都有缘起关系。
- **无尽性**：缘起关系层层叠叠，无穷无尽。一法中含一切法，一切法中各含一切法，形成"重重无尽"的结构。

### 法界的四重结构

法藏将法界展开为四重结构（详见 [[four-dharmadhatu|四法界]]）：

1. **事法界**——差别的现象界。
2. **理法界**——平等的本体界。
3. **理事无碍法界**——本体与现象的交融。
4. **事事无碍法界**——现象之间的直接交融。

这四重结构是法藏的独创，为华严宗提供了一个清晰的认知阶梯。值得注意的是，第四重"事事无碍法界"是法藏体系中最高的境界——它超越了"理"的中介，实现了现象之间的直接互渗。这使华严宗区别于其他强调"理体"的佛教传统。

### 法界与因陀罗网

法藏以因陀罗网（Indra's Net）作为法界的核心隐喻（详见 [[indra-net|因陀罗网]]）。因陀罗网是帝释天宫殿中悬挂的宝网，网上的每一颗宝珠都映照出所有其他宝珠，而被映照出的宝珠又各自映照一切宝珠，如此层层映照，无穷无尽。

这个隐喻精准地表达了法界的几个关键特征：

- **全息性**：每一个节点（宝珠）都包含整体的信息。
- **递归性**：映照是无限递归的——映照中的映照，无穷无尽。
- **非中心性**：没有哪一颗宝珠是"中心"，每一颗宝珠都同时是中心和非中心。

### 法界与华严修行

法界不仅是哲学概念，更是修行目标。华严宗的修行以"入法界"为核心——《华严经》中善财童子的五十三参，就是"入法界"的历程。法藏强调，入法界不是进入某个外在的空间，而是认知的根本转化——从分别识转为般若智，从事法界升华到事事无碍法界。

### 与相关概念的关系

- **四法界**（[[four-dharmadhatu|四法界]]）：法界的四重展开。
- **因陀罗网**（[[indra-net|因陀罗网]]）：法界的核心隐喻。
- **理事无碍**（[[li-shi-wu-ai|理事无碍]]）：法界的第三重结构。
- **性起**（[[nature-arising|性起]]）：法界的生成原理。

## English

### Conceptual Positioning

The term "dharmadhatu" (法界) stands as the most central concept in Huayan Buddhism and the foundation stone of Fazang's entire philosophical system. In early Buddhism, dharmadhatu referred primarily to the sphere or domain of all dharmas (phenomena). In Madhyamaka, it was synonymous with emptiness (sunyata). In Yogacara, it denoted suchness (tathata). Fazang creatively reinterpreted the term, transforming it into the master concept of Huayan philosophy.

### Fazang's Interpretation

In Fazang's system, the dharmadhatu is not a static category but a dynamic, holographic field of dependent origination. It is simultaneously the collective designation for all phenomena (all dharmas are the dharmadhatu), the ontological ground of all phenomena (the dharmadhatu is suchness), and the total relational structure through which all phenomena arise in mutual dependence.

In his *Investigations into the Profound Mysteries of the Avatamsaka Sutra* (华严经探玄记), Fazang identifies three dimensions of the dharmadhatu:

1. **Essence** (体) — suchness or tathagata-garbha, the ontological ground.
2. **Characteristics** (相) — the differentiated phenomena of the phenomenal world.
3. **Function** (用) — the mutual containment and interpenetration of all phenomena within dependent origination.

These three dimensions are not separate but perfectly fused — this is the fundamental distinction between the Huayan dharmadhatu and earlier Buddhist conceptions.

### The Fusion of Dharmadhatu and Dependent Origination

Fazang's most revolutionary contribution is the complete fusion of "dharmadhatu" with "dependent origination" (pratityasamutpada). Traditional Buddhism teaches dependent origination as conditional causation — "this being, that becomes; this ceasing, that ceases." Fazang proposes "dharmadhatu-dependent-origination" (法界缘起) — all dharmas arise simultaneously, mutually, and exhaustively.

This implies:

- **Simultaneity**: Not linear causation (A causes B) but simultaneous mutual causation.
- **Universality**: Each dharma is related not merely to adjacent dharmas but to all dharmas in the dharmadhatu.
- **Inexhaustibility**: The relational structure is infinitely layered — one dharma contains all dharmas, each of which contains all dharmas, producing an "inexhaustibly layered" structure.

### The Fourfold Structure

Fazang unfolds the dharmadhatu into four dimensions (see [[four-dharmadhatu|Four Dharmadhatu]]): the dharmadhatu of events, of principle, of non-obstruction between principle and events, and of non-obstruction among events. The fourth level — non-obstruction among events — is the supreme achievement of Fazang's system, transcending the mediation of principle to realize direct mutual interpenetration among phenomena.

### The Dharmadhatu and Indra's Net

Fazang employs Indra's Net (see [[indra-net|Indra's Net]]) as the central metaphor for the dharmadhatu. The net suspended in Indra's palace bears a jewel at every node, each reflecting all other jewels, and the reflected jewels themselves reflecting all jewels, ad infinitum. This metaphor precisely captures the holographic, recursive, and non-central character of the dharmadhatu.

### The Dharmadhatu and Huayan Practice

The dharmadhatu is not merely a philosophical concept but a soteriological goal. The central practice of Huayan Buddhism is "entering the dharmadhatu" (入法界) — the journey of Sudhana in the *Avatamsaka Sutra*, visiting fifty-three teachers, is precisely this process of entry. Fazang emphasizes that entering the dharmadhatu does not mean entering an external space but undergoing a fundamental cognitive transformation — from discriminative consciousness to prajna wisdom, from the dharmadhatu of events to the dharmadhatu of non-obstruction among events.
