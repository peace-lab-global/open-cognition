# 理事无碍 Non-obstruction of Principle and Events

## 中文

### 概念定位

"理事无碍"（理事无碍法界）是法藏华严哲学的枢纽性概念，处于四法界的第三层——从理法界的"空性认知"跃升到事事无碍法界的"全息认知"的关键转折就发生在这里。如果说四法界是一座桥梁，那么"理事无碍"就是桥拱的顶点——从此处可以望见两边的风景。

"理"（li）指本体、空性、真如、法性——一切法的共通本性。"事"（shi）指现象、差别、具体存在——一切法的个别显现。"无碍"（wu-ai）意为没有障碍、不相互妨碍、融通无二。

### 理与事的关系

法藏对"理"与"事"关系的分析是极其精密的。他提出理事之间有十层关系（"理事十门"），展现了从浅到深的理解：

1. **理遍于事**：空性遍在一切现象中。如水遍在于一切波中。
2. **事遍于理**：一切现象都在空性中。如一切波都在水中。
3. **依理成事**：现象依空性而成立。正因为空，一切法才能缘起。
4. **事能显理**：现象显现本体。通过具体的事物可以体悟空性。
5. **以理夺事**：从本体的角度看，一切差别消融于空性。
6. **事能隐理**：执着于差别现象会遮蔽对本体的认知。
7. **真理即事**：本体就是现象——"色即是空"的逆命题："空即是色"。
8. **事法即理**：现象就是本体——"色即是空"。
9. **真理非事**：本体不等同于现象——理是平等性，事是差别性。
10. **事法非理**：现象不等同于本体——事有界限，理无界限。

这十层关系看似矛盾（如第七层说"理即事"，第九层说"理非事"），但法藏的高明之处正在于此——他不是在给出一个固定的命题，而是在描述一种动态的、多层次的认知过程。理与事的关系不是"是"或"不是"的二元判断，而是"即且非"的中道。

### 核心论证

法藏对"理事无碍"的核心论证可以概括为：

**从理到事的论证**：如果理（空性）是真实的，那么它不能与事（现象）相矛盾。一个真正的空性不能"空掉"现象——否则空性本身就成了一个与现象对立的"东西"，就不再是真正的空性。因此，真正的空性必然融通于一切现象，不妨碍任何差别。

**从事到理的论证**：如果事（现象）是缘起的，那么它必然无自性——即必然是"空"的。一个有自性的事物不能变化、不能生灭、不能与其他事物发生关系。因此，一切现象的存在本身就证明了空性——事不碍理。

**互融的论证**：理与事不是两个东西——理不离事（空性不在现象之外），事不离理（现象不离空性）。如同水与波：没有无水之波，也没有无波之水（水是湿性，波是形态；湿性即是形态之性，形态即是湿性之显）。

### 《华严金师子章》中的阐释

在《华严金师子章》中，法藏以金狮子为例阐述理事无碍：

- **金**（理/本体）：金子的本性——柔软、可塑、无固定形态，比喻空性。
- **狮子**（事/现象）：狮子的形态——有头、有爪、有具体的形象，比喻差别现象。

金与狮子的关系正是理与事的关系：
- 金不妨碍成为狮子——本体不妨碍显现为差别现象。
- 狮子不妨碍是金——差别现象不妨碍其本性为空。
- 金即是狮子，狮子即是金——本体与现象不二。

法藏向武则天讲解时指出：看到狮子的时候，就同时看到了金——不需要消灭狮子才能看到金。同样，在差别现象中就能体悟空性——不需要离开现象界才能证入真如。

### 与中观、唯识的比较

**中观学派**：中观通过否定（"八不"：不生不灭、不常不断等）来显示空性。法藏继承了空性的洞见，但进一步追问：空性与现象之间的积极关系是什么？理事无碍回答了这个问题——空性不仅不否定现象，而且积极地融通现象。

**唯识学派**：唯识通过三性说（遍计所执性、依他起性、圆成实性）来分析理与事的关系。法藏的理事无碍与唯识的"圆成实性"有相通之处，但更为彻底——不仅理与事无碍，而且事与事也直接无碍。

**天台宗**：天台的"三谛圆融"（空、假、中三谛互融）与法藏的理事无碍高度相似。但天台更强调"一念三千"的当体即是，华严更强调法界的重重无尽。

### 哲学意义

"理事无碍"的深层哲学意义在于：它提供了一种超越本体论与现象学之间二元对立的方案。在西方哲学传统中，本体（noumenon）与现象（phenomenon）之间存在着难以逾越的鸿沟——康德的"物自体"不可知，柏拉图的"理念"与可感世界分离。法藏的"理事无碍"从根本上消解了这一对立：本体就在现象中，现象就是本体的显现。

这一思想对当代哲学也有深刻启发：它预示了海德格尔对"存在"与"存在者"关系的追问，以及梅洛-庞蒂的肉身本体论（ontology of flesh）——在世界之中、通过身体来揭示存在。

## English

### Conceptual Positioning

"Non-obstruction between Principle and Events" (理事无碍, li-shi wu-ai) is the pivotal concept in Fazang's Huayan philosophy, occupying the third level of the Four Dharmadhatu. The critical transition from the "emptiness cognition" of the Dharmadhatu of Principle to the "holographic cognition" of the Dharmadhatu of Non-obstruction among Events passes through this concept. If the four dharmadhatu form a bridge, then non-obstruction between principle and events is the apex of the arch — from here one can see the landscape on both sides.

"Principle" (理, li) denotes the ontological ground — emptiness, suchness, dharmata, the common nature of all phenomena. "Events" (事, shi) denotes phenomena — differentiated, concrete, particular existences. "Non-obstruction" (无碍, wu-ai) means absence of mutual hindrance, free interpenetration, non-dual harmony.

### The Relationship Between Principle and Events

Fazang's analysis of the li-shi relationship is extraordinarily precise. He identifies ten dimensions ("ten gates of li and shi"):

1. Principle pervades events: emptiness is present in all phenomena, as water pervades all waves.
2. Events pervade principle: all phenomena exist within emptiness, as all waves exist in water.
3. Events are established on the basis of principle: precisely because things are empty, they can arise dependently.
4. Events manifest principle: through concrete phenomena one can realize emptiness.
5. Principle absorbs events: from the perspective of the absolute, all differentiation dissolves.
6. Events can conceal principle: clinging to differentiated phenomena obscures awareness of the ground.
7. Principle is events: the absolute is the phenomenal — "emptiness is form."
8. Events are principle: the phenomenal is the absolute — "form is emptiness."
9. Principle is not events: the absolute is not identical with the phenomenal — principle is equality, events are difference.
10. Events are not principle: phenomena are not identical with the absolute — events have limits, principle has none.

These ten dimensions appear contradictory (dimension seven says "principle is events," dimension nine says "principle is not events"), but Fazang's genius lies precisely here: he describes not a fixed proposition but a dynamic, multi-layered cognitive process. The li-shi relationship is not a binary judgment but a middle way that is "both is and is-not."

### Core Arguments

**From principle to events**: If principle (emptiness) is genuine, it cannot contradict events (phenomena). A genuine emptiness cannot "empty out" phenomena — otherwise emptiness itself would become an "entity" opposed to phenomena and would no longer be genuine emptiness. Therefore, genuine emptiness must freely interpenetrate all phenomena without obstructing differentiation.

**From events to principle**: If events are dependently originated, they must lack self-nature — they must be "empty." A thing with self-nature could not change, arise, perish, or relate to other things. Therefore, the very existence of phenomena proves emptiness — events do not obstruct principle.

**Mutual interpenetration**: Principle and events are not two things — principle does not depart from events, events do not depart from principle. Like water and waves: there are no waves without water, and no water without waves.

### The Golden Lion Illustration

In the *Golden Lion Treatise*, Fazang uses the golden lion to illustrate li-shi non-obstruction: gold (principle/emptiness) is malleable and formless; the lion-shape (events/phenomena) is concrete and differentiated. Gold does not obstruct becoming a lion-shape; the lion-shape does not obstruct being gold. Gold is the lion, the lion is gold. When one sees the lion, one simultaneously sees the gold — one need not destroy the lion-form to perceive the gold. Similarly, within differentiated phenomena one can realize emptiness — one need not leave the phenomenal world to enter suchness.

### Comparison with Other Traditions

**Madhyamaka**: Uses negation (the "eight negations") to reveal emptiness. Fazang inherits this insight but asks further: what is the positive relationship between emptiness and phenomena? Non-obstruction answers this — emptiness does not merely negate phenomena but actively interpenetrates them.

**Yogacara**: Analyzes li-shi through the three natures (parikalpita, paratantra, parinispanna). Fazang's non-obstruction resonates with parinispanna but is more thoroughgoing — not only are principle and events non-obstructive, but events are directly non-obstructive with one another.

**Tiantai**: The "perfect fusion of three truths" (空假中) closely parallels Fazang's li-shi non-obstruction, though Tiantai emphasizes "three thousand realms in a single thought-moment" while Huayan emphasizes inexhaustible layering.

### Philosophical Significance

The deep philosophical significance of li-shi non-obstruction is that it provides a way beyond the dualism between noumenon and phenomenon that has plagued Western philosophy. Kant's thing-in-itself is unknowable; Plato's forms are separated from the sensible world. Fazang dissolves this opposition at its root: principle is in events, events are the manifestation of principle. This anticipates Heidegger's questioning of the Being/beings distinction and Merleau-Ponty's ontology of flesh — revealing Being within the world, through embodied existence.
