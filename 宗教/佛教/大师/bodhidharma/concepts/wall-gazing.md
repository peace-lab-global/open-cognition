# 壁观 Biguan — Wall-Gazing Meditation

> "外息诸缘，内心无喘，心如墙壁，可以入道。"
> "Externally cease all conditions; internally the mind does not waver. Mind like a wall — thus one may enter the Way."
> ——达摩《二入四行论》 / Bodhidharma, *Two Entries and Four Practices*

---

## 概述 / Overview

壁观（Biguan，wall-gazing）是达摩禅法的核心修行方法，也是中国禅宗最早、最具标志性的冥想技术之一。它要求修行者面壁而坐，通过截断一切外在感官刺激，使内心达到如墙壁般坚固不动的状态，从而契入真实。

Wall-gazing (壁观, Biguan) is the core practice method of Bodhidharma's Chan and one of the earliest and most iconic meditation techniques in Chinese Chan Buddhism. It requires the practitioner to sit facing a wall, cutting off all external sensory stimulation, so that the inner mind achieves an immovable state like a wall, thereby penetrating reality.

壁观不仅仅是一种身体姿态——面对墙壁而坐——更是一种深层的心灵修炼。达摩在嵩山少林寺面壁九年的传说，将壁观提升到了近乎神话的地位，使其成为禅宗精神追求的象征。

Wall-gazing is not merely a physical posture — sitting facing a wall — but a profound spiritual cultivation. The legend of Bodhidharma facing the wall at Shaolin Temple on Mount Song for nine years elevated wall-gazing to near-mythical status, making it a symbol of Chan spiritual aspiration.

## 词源与语义 / Etymology and Semantics

"壁观"由两个字组成："壁"（墙壁）和"观"（观察、观照）。字面意义是"观照墙壁"或"如壁之观"。这一术语蕴含着双重含义：

The term "壁观" combines two characters: "壁" (wall) and "观" (observation, contemplation). Literally "contemplating a wall" or "contemplation like a wall." This term carries a dual meaning:

1. **方法层面 Methodological**：面对物理的墙壁而坐，以墙壁为视觉焦点，减少外在干扰。
   Sitting facing a physical wall, using the wall as a visual focal point to reduce external distraction.

2. **境界层面 Soteriological**：使内心如墙壁般坚固不动，不被任何妄念所动摇。墙壁不分别、不执著、不追逐——心亦应如此。
   Making the inner mind immovable like a wall, undisturbed by any delusive thoughts. A wall does not discriminate, does not cling, does not chase — the mind should be the same.

在梵文传统中，壁观可能与印度的"壁喻"（wall metaphor）修行传统有关。印度早期冥想文献中也有以墙壁比喻心之安定的用法，达摩可能继承并发展了这一传统。

In the Sanskrit tradition, wall-gazing may relate to Indian wall-metaphor meditation practices. Early Indian meditation literature also uses the wall as a metaphor for mental stability, a tradition Bodhidharma may have inherited and developed.

## 修行方法 / Practice Method

达摩的壁观修行可分为几个层次：

Bodhidharma's wall-gazing practice can be divided into several layers:

### 第一层：外息诸缘 / First Layer: Externally Cease All Conditions

修行者选择一个安静的场所，面对墙壁而坐。物理的墙壁充当了感官屏障——视觉上没有变化的刺激，听觉上墙壁可以部分隔音。修行者有意识地放下一切外在牵挂：人际关系、工作压力、未来计划、过去回忆。

The practitioner selects a quiet place and sits facing a wall. The physical wall serves as a sensory barrier — visually there is no changing stimulation, and the wall partially dampens sound. The practitioner consciously releases all external concerns: relationships, work pressures, future plans, past memories.

### 第二层：内心无喘 / Second Layer: Internally the Mind Does Not Waver

"喘"在这里不是指呼吸的喘息，而是指内心的躁动不安——念头的翻涌、情绪的起伏、欲望的涌动。当外在干扰被减少后，修行者面对的是自己内心的纷乱。壁观的关键在于：不追随任何念头，也不压制任何念头，而是让心如墙壁般自然地安定下来。

"喘" (chuǎn) here does not refer to physical panting but to inner agitation — the churning of thoughts, the rise and fall of emotions, the surging of desires. Once external disturbance is reduced, the practitioner confronts the turbulence of their own mind. The key of wall-gazing lies in neither following any thought nor suppressing any thought, but allowing the mind to naturally settle like a wall.

### 第三层：心如墙壁 / Third Layer: Mind Like a Wall

这是壁观的深层境界。修行者的心不再是被外界牵动的被动反应者，而是成为一个坚固、稳定、不动摇的存在——如同墙壁本身。在这个状态中，主体与客体的二元对立逐渐消融，观察者与被观察者不再分离。

This is the deep state of wall-gazing. The practitioner's mind is no longer a passive reactor pulled by external conditions but becomes a firm, stable, unmoving presence — like the wall itself. In this state, the subject-object duality gradually dissolves; observer and observed are no longer separate.

### 第四层：可以入道 / Fourth Layer: Thus One May Enter the Way

当心如墙壁般安定时，真实本性（佛性）自然显现。达摩认为，众生之所以不能见性成佛，不是因为佛性缺失，而是因为心被妄念遮蔽。壁观通过去除遮蔽，让本有的佛性自行显现——这不是"获得"什么，而是"显露"本来就在那里的东西。

When the mind is stable like a wall, one's true nature (Buddha-nature) naturally manifests. Bodhidharma held that beings fail to see their nature and become Buddha not because Buddha-nature is absent, but because the mind is obscured by delusive thoughts. Wall-gazing removes the obscuration, allowing the innate Buddha-nature to reveal itself — this is not "gaining" something but "uncovering" what was always there.

## 历史发展 / Historical Development

壁观的历史发展可分为几个阶段：

The historical development of wall-gazing can be traced through several phases:

**达摩时期 Bodhidharma's Period (6th century)**：壁观作为达摩"理入"的核心方法被提出。据《续高僧传》记载，达摩在嵩山少林寺"面壁而坐，终日默然"，长达九年。
Wall-gazing was proposed as the core method of Bodhidharma's "entry through principle." According to the *Continued Biographies of Eminent Monks*, Bodhidharma "sat facing a wall in silence all day long" at Shaolin Temple on Mount Song for nine years.

**早期禅宗 Early Chan (6th–8th century)**：壁观被二祖慧可及后继者继承和发展。在这一时期，壁观不仅是个人修行方法，也是师徒间传法印心的媒介。
Wall-gazing was inherited and developed by the Second Patriarch Huike and his successors. During this period, wall-gazing served not only as a personal practice method but also as a medium for Dharma transmission between master and disciple.

**南北宗分裂 Period of Northern-Southern Split (8th century)**：神秀的北宗强调渐修，壁观逐渐与"时时勤拂拭"的渐修路线结合；慧能的南宗强调顿悟，对壁观的形式化修行有所批评。
Shenxiu's Northern School emphasized gradual cultivation, and wall-gazing became associated with the gradual approach of "constantly wiping the mirror." Huineng's Southern School emphasized sudden enlightenment and offered some critique of wall-gazing's formalized practice.

**日本禅宗 Japanese Zen (13th century onward)**：道元将曹洞宗的"只管打坐"（shikantaza）传入日本，壁观的精神以新的形式在日本禅中得到延续和发展。
Dogen transmitted the Soto school's "just sitting" (shikantaza) to Japan, and the spirit of wall-gazing continued and evolved in new forms within Japanese Zen.

## 与其他冥想传统的比较 / Comparison with Other Meditation Traditions

壁观与其他冥想传统既有共通之处，也有独特之处：

Wall-gazing shares commonalities with other meditation traditions while maintaining distinctive features:

- **与止观（Samatha-Vipassana）的比较**：止观强调先止后观，壁观则将止与观合一——在如墙壁般安定的同时，本觉自然显现。
  Compared with Samatha-Vipassana: Samatha-Vipassana emphasizes first calming then insight; wall-gazing unifies calming and insight — while the mind is stable like a wall, primordial awareness naturally manifests.

- **与正念（Mindfulness）的比较**：现代正念强调对当下经验的开放觉察，壁观则更强调减少外在刺激和内在扰动。正念是"开放地觉知"，壁观是"坚固地安定"。
  Compared with mindfulness: Modern mindfulness emphasizes open awareness of present experience; wall-gazing emphasizes more strongly reducing external stimulation and internal agitation. Mindfulness is "openly aware"; wall-gazing is "firmly stable."

- **与瑜伽冥想（Yoga Meditation）的比较**：印度瑜伽传统中有多种冥想方法，壁观最接近的是"pratyahara"（感官收摄）与"dharana"（专注），但壁观的目标不是瑜伽的"三摩地"（samadhi），而是禅宗的"见性"。
  Compared with yoga meditation: Indian yoga traditions include multiple meditation methods; wall-gazing most closely resembles pratyahara (sense withdrawal) and dharana (concentration), but its goal is not yoga's samadhi but Chan's "seeing one's nature."

## 当代意义 / Contemporary Significance

在当代语境中，壁观具有独特的价值：

In the contemporary context, wall-gazing holds unique value:

**科学研究 Scientific Research**：壁观作为一种减少感官输入的深度冥想形式，为神经科学研究提供了有趣的对象。fMRI 研究表明，长期冥想者在进行壁观类练习时，默认模式网络（DMN）的活动显著降低，这与"内心无喘"的体验描述高度吻合。

As a form of deep meditation that reduces sensory input, wall-gazing offers an interesting subject for neuroscience research. fMRI studies show that long-term meditators engaging in wall-gazing-type practices exhibit significantly reduced default mode network (DMN) activity, closely matching the experiential description of "the mind not wavering."

**数字时代应用 Digital Age Applications**：在信息过载和注意力碎片化的当代，壁观的"外息诸缘"原则尤其珍贵。数字断食（digital detox）和冥想静修（silent retreat）可被视为壁观精神在当代的延伸。

In an era of information overload and attention fragmentation, wall-gazing's principle of "externally ceasing all conditions" is especially precious. Digital detox and silent retreats can be seen as contemporary extensions of the wall-gazing spirit.
