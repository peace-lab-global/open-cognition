# 四句破 Catuskoti / Tetralemma

> "诸法不自生，亦不从他生，不共不无因，是故知无生。"——《中论》1.1
>
> "Neither from itself nor from another, nor from both, nor without a cause, does anything anywhere ever arise." — MMK 1.1 (Garfield trans.)

---

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

**四句破 (Catuskoti)**，又称"四句否定"或"四句全非"，是龙树哲学的核心逻辑方法。对任何形而上学命题，龙树穷尽其四种逻辑可能性——是、否、亦是亦否、非是非否——然后全部否定。这一方法不是反逻辑，而是以逻辑自身的严密性展示概念思维的极限。

**The Tetralemma (Catuskoti)**, also called "four-cornered negation," is the core logical method of Nagarjuna's 哲学. For any metaphysical proposition, Nagarjuna exhausts its four logical possibilities — affirmation, negation, both affirmation and negation, neither affirmation nor negation — and then negates all four. This method is not anti-logic but uses logic's own rigor to demonstrate the limits of conceptual thinking.

四句的逻辑形式 / Logical form of the tetralemma:
1. **P** — 肯定 / Affirmation
2. **¬P** — 否定 / Negation
3. **P ∧ ¬P** — 双是 / Both
4. **¬P ∧ ¬¬P** — 双非 / Neither

龙树论证：对于涉及自性 (svabhava) 的任何命题，这四个位置都是不可成立的。

Nagarjuna demonstrates: for any proposition involving svabhava, all four positions are untenable.

## 哲学分析 / Philosophical Analysis

### 《中论》第一章：因果四句破 / MMK Chapter 1: The Tetralemma of Causation

龙树在《中论》开篇即运用四句破分析因果关系：

Nagarjuna opens the MMK by applying the tetralemma to causation:

1. **不从自生 (Not from itself)**: 如果果已存在于因中，则"生"无意义——已经是的东西不需要再生 / If the effect already exists in the cause, "arising" is meaningless — what already exists need not arise again
2. **不从他生 (Not from another)**: 如果因与果完全不同，则任何事物都能生任何事物——因果之间没有关系 / If cause and effect are wholly different, anything could produce anything — there is no relationship between them
3. **不从共生 (Not from both)**: 自生和他生各自的缺陷不会因为结合而消失 / The defects of self-production and other-production do not disappear when combined
4. **不无因生 (Not without cause)**: 无因生意味着一切随机发生，否定了因果秩序 / Causeless arising means everything happens randomly, negating causal order

结论不是"因果不存在"，而是"因果关系不能被自性化地理解"。因果是缘起的 (pratityasamutpanna)，不是自性的 (sasvabhava)。

The conclusion is not that "causation does not exist" but that "causal relations cannot be understood in terms of svabhava." Causation is dependently originated, not inherently existent.

### 四句破的逻辑地位 / Logical Status of the Tetralemma

四句破引发了关于其逻辑地位的长期争论：

The tetralemma has provoked long-standing debate about its logical status:

**应成派解读 (Prasangika reading)**:
- 四句破是纯粹的归谬法 (prasanga)——龙树不持任何正面主张 / The tetralemma is pure reductio ad absurdum — Nagarjuna holds no positive thesis
- "若我有少宗，则我有过失；我无少宗故，唯我无过失" (Vigrahavyavartani 29) / "If I had any thesis, I would have the fault; since I have no thesis, I alone am without fault"

**自续派解读 (Svatantrika reading)**:
- 四句破暗含一个正面主张——"一切法无自性" / The tetralemma implicitly holds a positive thesis — "all dharmas lack svabhava"
- 允许使用自立量推理 (svatantra-anumana) 来论证空性 / Permits autonomous inference to establish emptiness

**现代逻辑学解读 (Modern logical reading)**:
- 四句破可被理解为对排中律 (Law of Excluded Middle) 的挑战 / Can be understood as a challenge to the Law of Excluded Middle
- 也可被理解为展示经典逻辑在形而上学问题上的不适切性 / Can also be understood as demonstrating classical logic's inadequacy for metaphysical questions
- 与直觉主义逻辑 (intuitionistic logic) 和多值逻辑 (many-valued logic) 形成对话 / Enters dialogue with intuitionistic logic and many-valued logic

### 四句破与辩证法 / Tetralemma and Dialectics

四句破不同于黑格尔辩证法 (Hegelian dialectics)。黑格尔的正-反-合 (thesis-antithesis-synthesis) 保留了前两个环节并达到更高综合。龙树的四句破不提供综合——它彻底消解所有概念位置，留下的是概念穷尽后的寂静 (upasama)。

The tetralemma differs from Hegelian dialectics. Hegel's thesis-antithesis-synthesis preserves the first two moments and achieves a higher synthesis. Nagarjuna's tetralemma offers no synthesis — it thoroughly dissolves all conceptual positions, leaving silence (upasama) after conceptual exhaustion.

## 历史发展 / Historical Development

### 印度传统中的源流 / Sources in Indian Tradition

四句破在龙树之前已有先例。《长部》(Digha Nikaya) 中佛陀对"十四无记"问题保持沉默——世界常还是无常、如来死后有还是无——这些问题的结构暗含四句模式。龙树将这种沉默转化为系统性的逻辑论证。

The tetralemma had precedents before Nagarjuna. In the Digha Nikaya, the Buddha maintained silence on the "fourteen unanswerable questions" — whether the world is eternal or not, whether the Tathagata exists after death — whose structure implies a tetralemma pattern. Nagarjuna transformed this silence into systematic logical argumentation.

### 后世争论 / Later Debates

- **月称 (Candrakirti)**: 坚持四句破的纯粹归谬解读 / Maintained the pure reductio reading of the tetralemma
- **清辨 (Bhaviveka)**: 主张在世俗层面允许自立量推理 / Advocated permitting autonomous inference at the conventional level
- **法称 (Dharmakirti)**: 从因明 (hetuvidya) 的角度重新审视四句破的逻辑有效性 / Re-examined the logical validity of the tetralemma from the perspective of Buddhist logic

### 当代研究 / Contemporary Research

- **Richard Robinson** (1967): 将四句破分析为逻辑命题形式 / Analyzed the tetralemma as logical propositional form
- **Jay Garfield** (1995): 从语义学角度解读四句破 / Interpreted the tetralemma from a semantic perspective
- **Graham Priest** (2009): 用次协调逻辑 (paraconsistent logic) 分析四句破 / Analyzed the tetralemma using paraconsistent logic

## 修行应用 / Practice Applications

- **概念解构 Conceptual deconstruction**: 四句破训练修行者识别并超越概念固化 / The tetralemma trains practitioners to recognize and transcend conceptual fixation
- **疑情禅法 Investigative meditation**: 在四句穷尽后的"疑"——不是困惑，而是概念穷尽后的开放性 / The "doubt" after exhausting all four positions — not confusion but openness after conceptual exhaustion
- **公案修行 Koan practice**: 许多禅宗公案的结构与四句破相似——逼迫心智超越概念框架 / Many Zen koans share a structure similar to the tetralemma — forcing the mind beyond conceptual frameworks

## 当代对话 / Contemporary Dialogue

- **哥德尔 (Godel)**: 不完备定理展示形式系统的内在限制——四句破展示概念思维的内在限制 / Incompleteness theorems reveal inherent limits of formal systems — the tetralemma reveals inherent limits of conceptual thinking
- **维特根斯坦 (Wittgenstein)**: 《逻辑哲学论》7——"对于不可说的，必须保持沉默"——与四句破后的寂静平行 / Tractatus 7 — "Whereof one cannot speak, thereof one must be silent" — parallel to the silence after the tetralemma
- **直觉主义逻辑 (Intuitionistic logic)**: 拒绝排中律——与四句破对经典逻辑的挑战形成对话 / Rejects the Law of Excluded Middle — enters dialogue with the tetralemma's challenge to classical logic
- **次协调逻辑 (Paraconsistent logic)**: 允许矛盾而不导致逻辑爆炸——与第三句 (P ∧ ¬P) 的可能联系 / Allows contradictions without logical explosion — possible connection to the third position (P and not-P)
