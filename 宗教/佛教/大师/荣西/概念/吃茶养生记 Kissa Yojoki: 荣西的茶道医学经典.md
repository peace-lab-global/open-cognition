# 吃茶养生记 Kissa Yojoki / Treatise on Drinking Tea for Health

## 定义 / Definition

**《吃茶养生记》**是荣西于1214年（晚年）撰写的两卷本著作，是日本最早的茶学专著。其核心论点为：茶是"末法时代"养生延命的妙药，也是修行者保持身心健康的工具。这部著作不仅是日本茶文化的元典，也是东亚茶医学传统的重要文献。

The ***Kissa Yojoki*** (Treatise on Drinking Tea for Health) is a two-volume work composed by Yōsai in 1214, near the end of his life. It is Japan's earliest treatise on tea. Its central thesis: tea is the wondrous medicine for health and longevity in the Age of Dharma Decline (*mappō*), and a tool for practitioners to maintain physical and mental well-being. This work is not only the foundational text of Japanese tea culture but also an important document in the East Asian tradition of tea medicine.

## 写作背景 / Context of Composition

### 末法思想的危机感 / The Crisis of Mappō Consciousness

荣西撰写《吃茶养生记》的时代背景是"末法"（mappō）思想的广泛流行。根据佛教末世论，佛陀入灭后，佛法经历三个阶段：

Yōsai wrote the *Kissa Yojoki* against the backdrop of the widespread belief in *mappō* (Age of Dharma Decline). According to Buddhist eschatology, after the Buddha's passing, the Dharma passes through three ages:

1. **正法时代** (Shōbō)：佛法正确流传，修行者能够证果。
   Age of True Dharma: the Dharma is correctly transmitted; practitioners can attain realization.

2. **像法时代** (Zōhō)：佛法形式存在，但实质衰退。
   Age of Semblance Dharma: the Dharma exists in form but declines in substance.

3. **末法时代** (Mappō)：佛法名存实亡，修行者难以证果。
   Age of Dharma Decline: the Dharma exists in name only; practitioners find realization nearly impossible.

在末法思想的影响下，荣西认为当时的日本人身心俱衰——不仅道德和精神堕落，身体也变得虚弱多病。在这样的背景下，茶作为一种"妙药"具有特殊的意义：它既能养生健体，又能辅助修行。

Under the influence of mappō thought, Yōsai believed that the Japanese people of his time were declining in both body and mind — not only morally and spiritually debased but physically weakened and disease-prone. In this context, tea as a "wondrous medicine" had special significance: it could both maintain health and support practice.

### 献给将军 / Presentation to the Shogun

《吃茶养生记》的直接写作契机是荣西将此书献给幕府将军源实朝（1192-1219）。据《吾妻镜》记载，源实朝因酗酒而身体虚弱，荣西献茶并附上《吃茶养生记》，将军饮后大为好转。这一事件不仅推广了饮茶习俗，也为荣西赢得了幕府的持续支持。

The direct occasion for the *Kissa Yojoki* was Yōsai's presentation of the text to Shogun Minamoto no Sanetomo (1192–1219). According to the *Azuma Kagami* chronicle, Sanetomo was weakened by excessive drinking; Yōsai presented tea along with the treatise, and the Shogun's health improved markedly. This event not only popularized tea drinking but also secured Yōsai's continued patronage from the shogunate.

## 内容结构 / Content Structure

### 上卷：茶的功效 / Upper Volume: Tea's Efficacy

上卷以"茶是养生之仙药"开篇，系统论述茶的医学价值：

The upper volume opens with "Tea is the elixir of health cultivation," systematically discussing tea's medical value:

**五脏调和论 / Five-Organ Harmony Theory**

荣西以中国传统医学的"五脏"（心、肝、脾、肺、肾）理论为基础，论证茶能调和五脏、平衡阴阳：

Based on traditional Chinese medicine's "five organs" (heart, liver, spleen, lungs, kidneys) theory, Yōsai argues that tea harmonizes the five organs and balances yin-yang:

- **心脏**：茶味苦，苦入心，故茶最能养心。茶能清心火、安神志。
  Heart: tea's bitter flavor enters the heart channel; thus tea best nourishes the heart, clearing heart-fire and calming the spirit.

- **肝脏**：茶能疏肝理气，解除肝郁。
  Liver: tea soothes the liver and regulates qi, relieving hepatic stagnation.

- **脾脏**：茶能健脾消食，帮助运化。
  Spleen: tea strengthens the spleen, aids digestion, and assists transformation.

- **肺脏**：茶能清肺化痰，通利气道。
  Lungs: tea clears the lungs, transforms phlegm, and opens the airways.

- **肾脏**：茶能滋肾利水，排除毒素。
  Kidneys: tea nourishes the kidneys, promotes urination, and eliminates toxins.

**茶的制备方法 / Tea Preparation Methods**

荣西详细描述了宋代"抹茶"（碾茶）的制备方法——这与后来日本茶道使用的抹茶一脉相承：

Yōsai describes in detail the Song-dynasty method of preparing "matcha" (powdered tea) — directly ancestral to the matcha used in later Japanese tea ceremony:

1. 采摘茶叶（以春季嫩芽为上）
   Harvesting tea leaves (spring buds preferred)
2. 蒸制杀青
   Steaming to halt oxidation
3. 干燥
   Drying
4. 用石磨碾成细粉
   Grinding to fine powder with a stone mill
5. 以沸水冲泡，用茶筅（竹制搅拌器）搅拌至起泡
   Whisking with hot water using a *chasen* (bamboo whisk) until frothy

### 下卷：桑的药用 / Lower Volume: Mulberry's Medicinal Uses

下卷出人意料地转向桑树（桑）的药用价值。荣西认为桑与茶一样具有广泛的养生功效：

The lower volume unexpectedly turns to the medicinal uses of the mulberry tree. Yōsai argues that mulberry, like tea, has broad health benefits:

- 桑叶可以煮汤饮用，治疗消渴症（糖尿病）
  Mulberry leaves, boiled as a drink, treat wasting-thirst disorder (diabetes)
- 桑枝可以通络活血
  Mulberry branches unblock channels and invigorate blood
- 桑根白皮（桑白皮）可以清肺降火
  Mulberry root bark clears lungs and reduces fire

这一部分内容反映了荣西深厚的中医药学知识，也为后世日本本草学的发展做出了贡献。

This section reflects Yōsai's deep knowledge of Chinese pharmacology and contributed to the later development of Japanese *honzōgaku* (materia medica studies).

## 茶的二重价值 / Tea's Dual Value

《吃茶养生记》最深刻的洞见在于揭示了茶的双重价值：

The *Kissa Yojoki*'s most profound insight lies in revealing tea's dual value:

### 作为养生手段 / As a Health Practice

- 预防疾病、延年益寿
  Preventing disease, extending longevity
- 调和身心、保持平衡
  Harmonizing body and mind, maintaining equilibrium
- 在末法时代保护修行者脆弱的身体
  Protecting practitioners' fragile bodies in the Age of Dharma Decline

### 作为修行辅助 / As a Practice Aid

- 遣除昏沉、保持禅修中的清醒
  Dispelling drowsiness, maintaining alertness in meditation
- 培养正念——饮茶过程中的每一个步骤都要求专注
  Cultivating mindfulness — every step of tea preparation demands focused attention
- 以茶为媒介，在日常生活中实践禅的精神
  Using tea as a medium to practice Zen's spirit in daily life

这两重价值不是分离的，而是统一的：身体的健康有利于修行，修行的深入也有利于身体健康。这正是"茶禅一味"的具体体现。

These two values are not separate but unified: bodily health supports practice, and deepening practice supports bodily health. This is precisely the concrete manifestation of "Tea and Zen Are One Taste."

## 科学回响 / Scientific Echoes

荣西在八百年前的论述与当代科学研究形成了有趣的呼应：

Yōsai's claims from eight centuries ago find interesting echoes in contemporary science:

- **茶多酚与抗氧化**：现代研究证实绿茶中的茶多酚（特别是EGCG——表没食子儿茶素没食子酸酯）具有强大的抗氧化活性，能保护细胞免受自由基损伤。
  Tea polyphenols and antioxidants: modern 研究 confirms that tea polyphenols in green tea (especially EGCG — epigallocatechin gallate) possess powerful antioxidant activity, protecting cells from free radical damage.

- **L-茶氨酸与注意力**：绿茶中的L-茶氨酸能促进α脑波产生，有助于放松而不昏沉——与荣西所说的"清心安神"高度吻合。
  L-theanine and attention: L-theanine in green tea promotes alpha brain wave production, facilitating relaxation without drowsiness — closely matching Yōsai's "clearing the heart and calming the spirit."

- **茶与代谢**：绿茶被证实能促进脂肪氧化和能量消耗，与荣西"消食健脾"的论述一致。
  Tea and metabolism: green tea has been shown to promote fat oxidation and energy expenditure, consistent with Yōsai's "aiding digestion and strengthening the spleen."

当然，荣西的论述基于传统医学理论，与现代科学的论证方式完全不同。但二者在经验层面的交汇是值得注意的。

Of course, Yōsai's arguments rest on traditional medical theory, entirely different from modern scientific methodology. But the convergence at the experiential level is noteworthy.

## 历史影响 / Historical Impact

《吃茶养生记》的历史影响远超茶学领域：

The *Kissa Yojoki*'s historical impact extends far beyond tea studies:

1. **推广饮茶习俗**：从贵族和僧侣阶层向更广泛的社会群体推广饮茶。
   Popularizing tea drinking: extending it from aristocratic and monastic circles to broader social groups.

2. **奠定茶道基础**：为后来村田珠光、武野绍鸥、千利休等人发展茶道提供了精神源头。
   Founding tea ceremony: providing the spiritual wellspring for later tea masters' development of *chadō*.

3. **宇治茶的起源**：荣西推广的茶种最终在宇治地区发展为日本最负盛名的茶产区。
   Origin of Uji tea: the tea seeds Yōsai promoted eventually developed in the Uji region into Japan's most celebrated tea-producing area.

4. **茶医学传统**：开创了日本"茶医学"的先河，影响了后世本草学的发展。
   Tea medicine tradition: pioneering Japan's "tea medicine" tradition, influencing later materia medica studies.

## 常见误读 / Common Misreadings

### 1. "《吃茶养生记》只是一本茶叶手册" / "The Kissa Yojoki is just a tea manual"
这部著作的哲学维度——将茶置于末法时代的修行框架中——使其超越了单纯的技术文本。茶不仅是饮品，更是救度众生的法门。
The work's philosophical dimension — situating tea within the mappō framework of practice — elevates it beyond a mere technical text. Tea is not just a beverage but a dharma gate for liberating beings.

### 2. "荣西的茶与现代绿茶相同" / "Yōsai's tea is identical to modern green tea"
荣西引入的是宋代"蒸青碾茶"（抹茶），与现代日本抹茶基本相同，但与中国后来的"炒青"绿茶（如龙井）制法不同。
Yōsai introduced Song-dynasty "steamed and ground tea" (matcha), basically identical to modern Japanese matcha, but different from the later Chinese "pan-fired" green teas (e.g., Longjing).
