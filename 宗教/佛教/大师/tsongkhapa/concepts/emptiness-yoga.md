# 空性瑜伽 / Emptiness Yoga: The View and Meditation of Śūnyatā

## 概念概述 / Overview

空性（śūnyatā，藏文 stong-pa-nyid）是宗喀巴思想体系的哲学核心。对宗喀巴而言，空性不是一种神秘体验或形而上学的信仰对象，而是一个可以通过严格推理证成、并通过系统禅修实证的哲学命题。他的空性瑜伽——即将空性的理论理解与深层禅修相结合——是整个菩提道次第中最精微、最具挑战性的修行环节。

Emptiness (śūnyatā; Tib. stong-pa-nyid) is the philosophical core of Tsongkhapa's thought system. For Tsongkhapa, emptiness is neither a mystical experience nor a metaphysical object of faith, but a philosophical proposition that can be established through rigorous reasoning and realized through systematic meditation. His emptiness yoga—the integration of theoretical understanding with deep meditative practice—is the most subtle and challenging dimension of the lamrim path.

## 宗喀巴的空性见 / Tsongkhapa's View of Emptiness

### "无自性"而非"无" / "Lack of Inherent Existence," Not "Nothingness"

宗喀巴严格区分了两种"空"：

Tsongkhapa strictly distinguished two kinds of "emptiness":

1. **自性空**（rang-bzhin gyis stong-pa）：一切法不具有独立、固有、不依赖他者的自性（svabhāva）。这是正确的空性见。
2. **断灭空**（med-pa）：认为一切都不存在，因果、善恶、修行都没有意义。这是危险的虚无主义。

1. **Emptiness of inherent existence** (rang-bzhin gyis stong-pa): All phenomena lack independent, inherent, self-sufficient existence (svabhāva). This is the correct view of emptiness.
2. **Nihilistic emptiness** (med-pa): The belief that nothing exists—that karma, ethics, and practice are meaningless. This is dangerous nihilism.

宗喀巴的著名论断是：**"正因为无自性，缘起才成为可能"**（stong-pa-nyid las rten-'byung）。如果事物有自性，它们就是固定不变的，因果与变化都不可能发生。空性不是否定现象世界的存在，而是否定现象世界具有独立自性的存在方式。

Tsongkhapa's famous thesis: **"It is precisely because things lack inherent existence that dependent arising is possible."** If things had inherent existence, they would be fixed and unchanging—neither causation nor transformation could occur. Emptiness does not deny the existence of the phenomenal world; it denies that phenomena exist in a self-sufficient, independent manner.

### 应成派的归谬法 / Prāsaṅgika Reductio

宗喀巴继承月称（Candrakīrti）的应成派方法，对一切"自性有"的命题进行归谬反驳：

Tsongkhapa inherited Candrakīrti's Prāsaṅgika method, applying reductio ad absurdum against all theses of "inherent existence":

- **对"自性有"的破斥**：如果事物有自性，它就不依赖条件而生；但一切事物都是依赖条件而生的（缘起），所以事物无自性。
- **Refuting inherent existence**: If things had inherent existence, they would not depend on conditions for arising; but all things arise dependently, therefore things lack inherent existence.

- **对"自性无"的破斥**：如果说"一切无自性"意味着"一切不存在"，那就否定了世俗经验（包括因果法则），落入断灭见。
- **Refuting nihilism**: If "all lack inherent existence" means "nothing exists," this negates conventional experience (including the law of karma) and falls into nihilism.

宗喀巴认为，中观哲学的"中道"正是走在"常见"（一切有自性）与"断见"（一切不存在）之间的细线上。他在《入中论善显密意疏》中写道："中观见如行于刀刃之上。"

Tsongkhapa held that the Madhyamaka "middle way" walks precisely the razor's edge between eternalism (everything has inherent existence) and nihilism (nothing exists). In his *Illumination of the Thought*, he wrote: "The Madhyamaka view is like walking on a blade."

## 止观双运 / Union of Śamatha and Vipaśyanā

### 止（śamatha）/ Calm Abiding

止是使心安住于单一所缘的能力。宗喀巴详细描述了修止的九个阶段（九住心），从最初的心识散乱到最终的"等持"（samādhi）：

Calm abiding is the ability to rest the mind on a single object. Tsongkhapa described nine stages of śamatha (nine mental abidings), from initial distraction to final equipoise (samādhi):

1. 内住（安置心于所缘）
2. 等住（持续安住）
3. 安住（散乱后返回）
4. 近住（紧密安住）
5. 调伏（体验禅悦）
6. 寂静（平息烦恼）
7. 最极寂静（彻底平息）
8. 专注一趣（无间安住）
9. 等持（无需努力的任运安住）

1. Placement (setting the mind on the object)
2. Continuous placement (sustaining attention)
3. Repeated placement (returning after distraction)
4. Close placement (tight focus)
5. Taming (experiencing meditative joy)
6. Pacifying (calming afflictions)
7. Thorough pacifying (complete calming)
8. One-pointedness (uninterrupted focus)
9. Equipoise (effortless sustained absorption)

### 观（vipaśyanā）/ Insight

观是以分析智慧观察所缘的真实本性——即空性。在止的基础上，修行者运用中观的推理方法（如"四句破"、"七相推求"等）对所缘（包括自我）进行深入分析，直至获得对空性的确定解。

Insight is the analytical wisdom that investigates the true nature of phenomena—namely, emptiness. On the basis of śamatha, the practitioner applies Madhyamaka reasoning (such as the tetralemma or the sevenfold analysis of the chariot) to deeply analyze the object of meditation (including the self) until gaining definitive understanding of emptiness.

### 双运 / Their Union

宗喀巴特别强调止与观不可偏废：

Tsongkhapa especially emphasized that śamatha and vipaśyanā must not be separated:

- **仅有止而无观**：如入黑暗，虽有定力但不能断烦恼根。外道也能修到甚深的禅定，但因缺乏空性见而不能解脱。
- **Concentration without insight**: Like entering darkness—one has stability but cannot uproot afflictions. Non-Buddhist practitioners can achieve deep absorption but, lacking the emptiness view, cannot attain liberation.

- **仅有观而无止**：如风中烛火，虽有智慧但不稳定，无法穿透烦恼的深层习气。
- **Insight without concentration**: Like a candle in a gale—one has wisdom but it is unstable, unable to penetrate deep-rooted habitual patterns.

只有止观双运，才能在深度禅定中运用分析智慧，直接证悟空性，从根本上断除无明。

Only through their union can analytical wisdom operate within deep meditative absorption, directly realizing emptiness and fundamentally uprooting ignorance.

## 空性禅修的具体方法 / Specific Methods of Emptiness Meditation

### 四句破 / Tetralemma Analysis (catuṣkoṭi)

对任何现象（如"自我"）进行四种可能性的分析：
Analyzing any phenomenon (such as "self") through four possibilities:

1. 它是自生吗？→ 否，因为自生意味着它已经存在，无需再生。
2. 它是他生吗？→ 否，因为完全异质的原因不可能产生结果。
3. 它是自他共生吗？→ 否，因为前两者都不成立。
4. 它是无因生吗？→ 否，因为无因则一切随机，因果法则不成立。

1. Does it arise from itself? → No, because self-arising means it already exists.
2. Does it arise from other? → No, because wholly heterogeneous causes cannot produce effects.
3. Does it arise from both? → No, since neither individually holds.
4. Does it arise without cause? → No, because causation would be random.

### 七相推求 / Sevenfold Analysis

以"车"为例，分析"我"与五蕴的关系：
Using the "chariot" example to analyze the relationship of "I" to the five aggregates:

1. "我"即是五蕴吗？→ 否
2. "我"异于五蕴吗？→ 否
3. "我"依于五蕴吗？→ 否
4. 五蕴依于"我"吗？→ 否
5. "我"具有五蕴吗？→ 否
6. 五蕴的集合是"我"吗？→ 否
7. 五蕴的形状是"我"吗？→ 否

1. Is the "I" identical to the aggregates? → No
2. Is the "I" different from the aggregates? → No
3. Does the "I" depend on the aggregates? → No
4. Do the aggregates depend on the "I"? → No
5. Does the "I" possess the aggregates? → No
6. Is the collection of aggregates the "I"? → No
7. Is the shape of the aggregates the "I"? → No

结论：在七种可能的关系中，都找不到一个独立自有的"我"。但这并不意味着"我"不存在——"我"只是作为名言假立（prajñapti）而存在，依赖五蕴而被概念性地施设。

Conclusion: Among seven possible relationships, no independently existing "I" can be found. Yet this does not mean the "I" does not exist—the "I" exists merely as a conceptual designation (prajñapti), dependently imputed upon the aggregates.

## 二谛 / The Two Truths

宗喀巴的二谛观是其空性理论的框架性支撑：

Tsongkhapa's two-truth framework provides the structural support for his emptiness theory:

| 世俗谛 Conventional Truth (kun-rdzob bden-pa) | 胜义谛 Ultimate Truth (don-dam bden-pa) |
|---|---|
| 现象在名言中的存在方式 How phenomena exist conventionally | 现象的究竟本性——空性 The ultimate nature—emptiness |
| 因果、善恶在此层面有效 Karma and ethics are valid at this level | 超越一切概念分别 Beyond all conceptual elaboration |
| 不可被胜义分析所否定 Cannot be negated by ultimate analysis | 是胜义分析所证悟的对象 The object realized by ultimate analysis |

宗喀巴强调二谛是**同一现象的两个面向**，不是两个分离的实在。世俗谛不是"假的"，胜义谛也不是"真的"——二者都是"真"的，只是从不同的认知层面而言。否定世俗谛的有效性是断灭见，否定胜义谛的有效性是常见。

Tsongkhapa emphasized that the two truths are **two aspects of the same phenomenon**, not two separate realities. Conventional truth is not "false," and ultimate truth is not "true"—both are "true" from different cognitive levels. Denying the validity of conventional truth is nihilism; denying the validity of ultimate truth is eternalism.

## 认知意义 / Cognitive Significance

空性瑜伽从认知科学的角度可以被理解为一种**本体论解构训练**——系统性地瓦解人类认知中根深蒂固的"本质主义"倾向（即认为事物具有独立、固有本能的认知偏见）。发展心理学研究表明，本质主义思维是人类认知的默认模式（Gelman, 2003）；宗喀巴的空性方法提供了一套系统的反本质主义认知训练。

Emptiness yoga can be understood from a cognitive science perspective as a form of **ontological deconstruction training**—systematically dismantling the deeply ingrained "essentialist" tendency in human cognition (the cognitive bias that things have independent, inherent natures). Developmental psychology research shows essentialist thinking is the default mode of human cognition (Gelman, 2003); Tsongkhapa's emptiness methods provide a systematic counter-essentialist cognitive training.
