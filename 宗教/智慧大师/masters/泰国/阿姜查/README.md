# 阿姜查 · Ajahn Chah (1918–1992)

## 概述 / Overview

阿姜查·苏跋陀（Ajahn Chah Subhaddo，1918年6月17日—1992年1月16日）是二十世纪最具影响力的南传佛教森林传统大师之一。他在泰国乌汶省创立了巴蓬寺（Wat Nong Pah Pong），并培养了众多杰出的僧俗弟子，其中包括阿姜苏美多（Ajahn Sumedho）、阿姜布拉姆（Ajahn Brahm）、杰克·康菲尔德（Jack Kornfield）等西方禅修先驱。阿姜查以其朴实无华的教学风格、对自然现象的深刻观察，以及将深奥佛法转化为日常生活智慧的能力，赢得了世界各地修行者的敬仰。

Ajahn Chah Subhaddo (June 17, 1918 – January 16, 1992) was one of the most influential Theravada Buddhist masters of the forest tradition in the twentieth century. He founded Wat Nong Pah Pong in Ubon Ratchathani province, Thailand, and nurtured many distinguished monastic and lay disciples, including Ajahn Sumedho, Ajahn Brahm, Jack Kornfield, and other pioneering Western meditation practitioners. Ajahn Chah earned the reverence of practitioners worldwide through his unadorned teaching style, his profound observation of natural phenomena, and his ability to transform deep Dhamma truths into wisdom for everyday life.

## 生平 / Biography

### 早年与出家 / Early Life and Ordination

阿姜查于1918年6月17日出生在泰国东北部乌汶省瓦林县（Warin Chamrap）的一个小村庄。他出生在一个普通的农民家庭，从小便展现出对精神生活的浓厚兴趣。年仅九岁时，他便离开家庭，到村中的寺院成为沙弥（samanera），开始学习巴利文和佛教基础经典。三年的沙弥生活为他奠定了坚实的佛学基础。

Ajahn Chah was born on June 17, 1918, in a small village in Warin Chamrap district, Ubon Ratchathani province, in northeastern Thailand. Born into an ordinary farming family, he displayed a strong interest in spiritual life from an early age. At just nine years old, he left home to become a samanera (novice monk) at the village monastery, where he began studying Pali and foundational Buddhist texts. His three years as a novice laid a solid foundation for his Buddhist education.

二十岁时，他受具足戒（upasampada），成为比丘，法名苏跋陀（Subhaddo）。受戒后的头几年，他依照传统在寺院中研习戒律和经典。然而，他内心始终渴望更深层的修行体验。1946年，他放弃了相对安逸的学院生活，开始了艰苦的头陀行（tudong），步行穿越泰国东北部的森林和荒野，托钵乞食，在树下露宿，寻找真正的修行之路。

At age twenty, he received upasampada (full ordination) as a bhikkhu with the Pali name Subhaddo. During the first years after ordination, he followed tradition by studying Vinaya (monastic discipline) and scriptures at the monastery. However, his heart always yearned for a deeper experiential understanding. In 1946, he abandoned the relatively comfortable academic life and embarked on the arduous tudong (wandering ascetic practice), walking through the forests and wilderness of northeastern Thailand, living on alms food, sleeping under trees, seeking the authentic path of practice.

### 师承阿姜曼 / Studying with Ajahn Mun

头陀行期间，阿姜查有幸遇到了泰国森林传统最伟大的大师——阿姜曼·布里达多（Ajahn Mun Bhuridatta）。阿姜曼以严格的禅修训练和深邃的证悟著称。虽然阿姜查跟随阿姜曼学习的时间并不长，但这次相遇彻底改变了他的修行方向。阿姜曼教导他观察心念的本质，而非仅仅依赖书本知识。阿姜查后来常说："阿姜曼教给我的最重要的一课，就是去看——用你自己的眼睛去看。"

During his tudong wanderings, Ajahn Chah had the good fortune to meet Ajahn Mun Bhuridatta, the greatest master of the Thai forest tradition. Ajahn Mun was renowned for his rigorous meditation training and profound realization. Although Ajahn Chah studied with Ajahn Mun for only a relatively brief period, this encounter completely transformed his practice direction. Ajahn Mun taught him to observe the nature of mind directly, rather than relying solely on book knowledge. Ajahn Chah later often said: "The most important lesson Ajahn Mun taught me was to look — to see with your own eyes."

### 创立巴蓬寺 / Founding Wat Nong Pah Pong

1954年，阿姜查回到家乡乌汶省，受邀在一片偏远的森林中建立修行道场。这片森林被当地人视为荒野之地，甚至有老虎出没，但阿姜查认为这正是理想的修行环境。巴蓬寺（Wat Nong Pah Pong）就此诞生。

In 1954, Ajahn Chah returned to his home province of Ubon and was invited to establish a practice center in a remote forest area. The local people considered this forest a wilderness — tigers were known to roam there — but Ajahn Chah regarded it as the ideal environment for practice. Thus Wat Nong Pah Pong was born.

巴蓬寺的生活极其简朴。僧人们住在简陋的高脚木屋（kuti）中，每天清晨托钵乞食，日中一食。寺院没有电力和自来水，修行者们必须亲自打水、劈柴、清扫。阿姜查刻意维持这种艰苦的生活条件，因为他相信物质上的匮乏恰恰是培养内心力量的最佳土壤。

Life at Wat Nong Pah Pong was extremely simple. Monks lived in rudimentary raised wooden huts (kuti), went on alms rounds each morning, and ate one meal a day. The monastery had no electricity or running water; practitioners had to fetch water, chop firewood, and sweep the grounds themselves. Ajahn Chah deliberately maintained these austere conditions, because he believed that material scarcity was the best soil for cultivating inner strength.

### 西方弟子的到来 / Arrival of Western Disciples

1960年代末，第一批西方人开始来到巴蓬寺。这些西方求法者大多是在越南战争期间或之后来到东南亚的美国人和欧洲人，他们对东方精神传统怀有真诚的兴趣。阿姜查对这些外国弟子展现出了非凡的开放性和慈悲心。尽管语言不通，他依然用泰语教导他们，并通过翻译和身教来传递佛法。

In the late 1960s, the first Westerners began arriving at Wat Nong Pah Pong. These Western seekers were mostly Americans and Europeans who had come to Southeast Asia during or after the Vietnam War era, harboring a sincere interest in Eastern spiritual traditions. Ajahn Chah demonstrated extraordinary openness and compassion toward these foreign disciples. Despite the language barrier, he taught them in Thai and transmitted the Dhamma through translators and through his own embodied example.

阿姜苏美多（原名罗伯特·雅各布斯，Robert Jacobs）是阿姜查最重要的西方弟子之一。他后来在英国建立了契特寺（Chithurst Monastery）和阿玛拉瓦蒂寺（Amaravati Monastery），成为将森林传统移植到西方世界的关键人物。杰克·康菲尔德（Jack Kornfield）则在回到美国后，与莎朗·萨尔茨伯格（Sharon Salzberg）和约瑟夫·戈德斯坦（Joseph Goldstein）共同创立了内观禅修社（Insight Meditation Society），极大地推动了正念运动在西方主流社会的发展。

Ajahn Sumedho (born Robert Jacobs) was one of Ajahn Chah's most important Western disciples. He later established Chithurst Monastery and Amaravati Monastery in England, becoming a key figure in transplanting the forest tradition to the Western world. Jack Kornfield, upon returning to the United States, co-founded the Insight Meditation Society with Sharon Salzberg and Joseph Goldstein, greatly advancing the mindfulness movement in mainstream Western society.

### 晚年与圆寂 / Final Years and Passing

1970年代末，阿姜查的健康开始恶化。他患有严重的糖尿病，并经历了多次手术。然而即使在病痛中，他依然坚持教导弟子。他的身体逐渐衰弱，最终几乎无法行走和说话。但他利用自身的病痛作为教导——向弟子们展示如何以平等心面对老、病、死。他曾说："我的身体在衰退，但心并没有跟随它一起衰退。"

In the late 1970s, Ajahn Chah's health began to deteriorate. He suffered from severe diabetes and underwent multiple surgeries. Yet even in illness, he persisted in teaching his disciples. His body gradually weakened until he could barely walk or speak. But he used his own illness as a teaching — showing his disciples how to face aging, sickness, and death with equanimity. He once said: "My body is declining, but the mind does not decline along with it."

1992年1月16日，阿姜查在巴蓬寺安详圆寂，世寿七十三岁。他的荼毗（火化）仪式吸引了数十万人参加，成为泰国佛教史上最盛大的葬礼之一。

On January 16, 1992, Ajahn Chah passed away peacefully at Wat Nong Pah Pong at the age of seventy-three. His cremation ceremony drew hundreds of thousands of attendees, becoming one of the most grandiose funerals in the history of Thai Buddhism.

## 核心思想 / Core Teachings

### 一、自然观察 / Natural Observation

阿姜查最独特的教学方法之一，是通过观察自然现象来阐释深奥的佛法真理。他常常用落叶、流水、季节更替等自然现象来比喻无常（anicca）、苦（dukkha）和无我（anatta）这三法印。

One of Ajahn Chah's most distinctive teaching methods was using observations of natural phenomena to illuminate profound Dhamma truths. He frequently used falling leaves, flowing water, the changing of seasons, and other natural events as metaphors for the three marks of existence: impermanence (anicca), suffering (dukkha), and non-self (anatta).

他教导弟子们"向自然学习"——不是从书本上理解无常，而是通过亲眼观察一片叶子从发芽、成长、枯黄到凋落的过程，来体验无常的真实性。这种教学方法与他的森林生活环境密不可分——森林本身就是一本活的教科书，处处展示着变化的法则。

He taught his disciples to "learn from nature" — not to understand impermanence from books, but to experience its reality by directly observing a leaf as it sprouts, grows, yellows, and falls. This teaching method was inseparable from his forest living environment — the forest itself was a living textbook, demonstrating the law of change everywhere.

### 二、森林禅修 / Forest Meditation

森林禅修是阿姜查整个修行体系的基础环境。他坚信，远离城市的喧嚣和物质的诱惑，在森林中独处静修，最有利于培养内心的宁静和洞察力。森林不仅提供了安静的外在环境，更重要的是，它提供了一个与自然节律同步的生活空间，让修行者能够更直接地体验生命的实相。

Forest meditation was the foundational environment of Ajahn Chah's entire practice system. He firmly believed that solitude and practice in the forest, away from urban noise and material temptations, was most conducive to cultivating inner tranquility and insight. The forest not only provided a quiet external environment, but more importantly, offered a living space synchronized with natural rhythms, enabling practitioners to experience the reality of life more directly.

### 三、当下觉察 / Present-Moment Awareness

阿姜查反复强调，修行不在过去，不在未来，而在此时此刻。他教导弟子们在每一个日常活动中保持觉察——吃饭时知道自己在吃饭，走路时知道自己在走路，呼吸时知道自己在呼吸。这种觉察不需要特殊的姿势或场所，只需要一颗清醒的心。

Ajahn Chah repeatedly emphasized that practice is neither in the past nor in the future, but in this very moment. He taught his disciples to maintain awareness in every daily activity — knowing that you are eating while eating, knowing that you are walking while walking, knowing that you are breathing while breathing. This awareness requires no special posture or location, only an awake mind.

### 四、简单生活 / Simple Living

阿姜查一生践行简朴。他教导说，真正的自由不在于拥有更多，而在于需要更少。在巴蓬寺，僧人们被要求将个人物品减至最少——一件袈裟、一个钵、几本经书。这种极简主义不是苦行主义的自我折磨，而是一种有意识的选择，旨在减少外物对内心的干扰。

Ajahn Chah lived a life of simplicity. He taught that true freedom lies not in having more, but in needing less. At Wat Nong Pah Pong, monks were required to minimize personal possessions — one robe, one alms bowl, a few books. This minimalism was not self-mortification but a conscious choice aimed at reducing external distractions to the inner life.

### 五、西方弘法 / Teaching Westerners

阿姜查是最早积极接纳西方弟子的南传佛教大师之一。他不仅允许西方人在巴蓬寺出家，还支持他们在各自国家建立禅修中心。这一决定对南传佛教在全球的传播产生了深远的影响。

Ajahn Chah was among the earliest Theravada masters to actively embrace Western disciples. He not only permitted Westerners to ordain at Wat Nong Pah Pong but also supported them in establishing meditation centers in their home countries. This decision had a profound impact on the global spread of Theravada Buddhism.

## 教学风格 / Teaching Style

阿姜查的教学风格可以用三个词概括：直接、幽默、务实。他不喜高谈阔论，不引用复杂的哲学论证，而是用最朴素的语言、最日常的例子来传达最深的真理。他经常开玩笑，用自嘲式的幽默来打破弟子们的执着和紧张。

Ajahn Chah's teaching style can be summarized in three words: direct, humorous, and pragmatic. He disliked lofty discourse and avoided complex philosophical arguments, instead conveying the deepest truths in the plainest language with the most everyday examples. He often joked, using self-deprecating humor to break through his disciples' attachments and tensions.

一个著名的例子是，当有人问他"什么是佛法"时，他拿起桌上的玻璃杯说："这个杯子，对我来说已经碎了。我使用它，珍惜它，但我知道它随时可能破碎。当它真的碎了，我不会难过——因为我已经看到了它的本质。一切事物都是如此。这就是佛法。"

In one famous example, when someone asked him "What is Dhamma?", he picked up a glass from the table and said: "This glass, to me, is already broken. I use it, I cherish it, but I know it can shatter at any moment. When it truly breaks, I will not be sad — because I have already seen its nature. Everything is like this. This is Dhamma."

## 思想坐标 / Intellectual Coordinates

### 承接 / Inheritance
- **佛陀原始教法**：阿姜查始终强调他所教的不是自己的发明，而是佛陀本人的教导。他将巴蓬寺的修行方式视为对佛陀时代僧团生活的忠实再现。
- **泰国森林传统**：他继承了由阿姜曼所代表的泰国森林禅修传统，强调头陀行、简朴生活和直接体验。
- **The Buddha's original teachings**: Ajahn Chah always emphasized that what he taught was not his own invention but the Buddha's own teachings. He regarded the practice at Wat Nong Pah Pong as a faithful reproduction of the Sangha life in the Buddha's time.
- **Thai Forest Tradition**: He inherited the Thai forest meditation tradition represented by Ajahn Mun, emphasizing tudong practice, simple living, and direct experience.

### 批判 / Critique
- **现代物质主义**：阿姜查批判现代社会对物质的过度追求，认为这是痛苦的主要来源。
- **过度依赖经典文字**：他警告不要将佛学知识等同于实际证悟——"就像一个人养牛却从不喝牛奶。"
- **Modern materialism**: Ajahn Chah critiqued modern society's excessive pursuit of material goods, considering it a primary source of suffering.
- **Over-reliance on scriptural text**: He warned against equating Buddhist scholarship with actual realization — "like a person who raises cattle but never drinks the milk."

### 影响 / Influence
- **西方内观运动**：通过他的西方弟子，阿姜查对西方的内观禅修运动产生了根本性的影响。
- **当代正念运动**：乔·卡巴金（Jon Kabat-Zinn）的正念减压疗法（MBSR）间接受益于阿姜查的教学传统。
- **Western Insight Movement**: Through his Western disciples, Ajahn Chah had a fundamental influence on the Western insight meditation movement.
- **Contemporary Mindfulness Movement**: Jon Kabat-Zinn's Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) benefited indirectly from Ajahn Chah's teaching tradition.

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

1. **心理治疗 / Psychotherapy**：阿姜查的自然观察法和当下觉察法被整合进多种现代心理治疗方法中，特别是正念认知疗法（MBCT）和接纳承诺疗法（ACT）。
2. **环保伦理 / Environmental Ethics**：他的简单生活理念和自然尊重态度为当代环保运动提供了精神资源。
3. **临终关怀 / End-of-Life Care**：他对死亡和无常的教导被应用于临终关怀和悲伤辅导。
4. **领导力培训 / Leadership Training**：他的觉知训练和简单决策方法被引入企业管理和领导力发展项目。

1. **Psychotherapy**: Ajahn Chah's natural observation and present-moment awareness methods have been integrated into various modern psychotherapeutic approaches, particularly Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT) and Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT).
2. **Environmental Ethics**: His simple living 哲学 and attitude of respect for nature provide spiritual resources for contemporary environmental movements.
3. **End-of-Life Care**: His teachings on death and impermanence have been applied to hospice care and grief counseling.
4. **Leadership Training**: His awareness training and simple decision-making approaches have been introduced into corporate management and leadership development programs.

## 戒定慧三学 / The Three Trainings

阿姜查的教学体系完整地涵盖了佛教传统的三学——戒（sila）、定（samadhi）、慧（panna）。他经常强调这三者是不可分割的整体，就像一个三角架的三条腿，缺少任何一条都无法站稳。

Ajahn Chah's teaching system comprehensively encompasses Buddhism's traditional three trainings — sila (ethics), samadhi (concentration), and panna (wisdom). He often emphasized that these three are an indivisible whole, like a tripod's three legs — without any one of them, it cannot stand.

**戒（Sila）**是基础。在巴蓬寺，僧人严格遵守二百二十七条比丘戒律，在家居士则遵守五戒或八戒。阿姜查将戒律视为"保护"而非"束缚"——它保护修行者免受有害行为的后果，为禅修提供稳固的心理基础。他说："一个犯戒的人，内心不可能真正平静。因为愧疚和恐惧会像虫子一样不断啃咬他的心。"

**Sila (Ethics)** is the foundation. At Wat Nong Pah Pong, monks strictly observed the 227 bhikkhu precepts, while laypeople observed the five or eight precepts. Ajahn Chah regarded the precepts as "protection" rather than "constraint" — they protect practitioners from the consequences of harmful behavior, providing a stable psychological foundation for meditation. He said: "A person who breaks precepts cannot have a truly calm mind. Because guilt and fear will gnaw at the heart like insects."

**定（Samadhi）**是核心。阿姜查教导多种禅修方法，包括安般念（出入息念）、慈心禅和不净观。但他强调，禅修的目的不是追求特殊体验或神秘境界，而是培养稳定的觉察力，使心能够如实地看到事物的本质。

**Samadhi (Concentration)** is the core. Ajahn Chah taught various meditation methods including anapanasati (mindfulness of breathing), metta bhavana (loving-kindness meditation), and asubha (contemplation of impurity). But he emphasized that the purpose of meditation is not to pursue special experiences or mystical states, but to cultivate stable awareness that enables the mind to see things as they truly are.

**慧（Panna）**是果实。当戒和定建立了稳固的基础，智慧就会自然地生起——不是从书本上学来的知识，而是从直接体验中涌现的洞见。这种智慧看到一切现象的无常、苦、无我本质，从而产生真正的出离和解脱。

**Panna (Wisdom)** is the fruit. When sila and samadhi establish a stable foundation, wisdom arises naturally — not knowledge learned from books, but insight emerging from direct experience. This wisdom sees the impermanent, unsatisfactory, and non-self nature of all phenomena, thereby generating genuine renunciation and liberation.

> "一切事物都在教导我们。如果你不知道如何听，那么一切都毫无意义。但如果你知道如何听，那么每件事都是佛法。"
> "Everything is teaching us. If you don't know how to listen, then everything is meaningless. But if you know how to listen, then everything is Dhamma."

> "不要执着于任何事物，包括'不执着'本身。"
> "Don't cling to anything, including 'non-clinging' itself."

> "当你生气时，看着那个愤怒。它从哪里来？它要到哪里去？不要试图消灭它——只是看着它。当你真正看到它时，它就消失了。"
> "When you're angry, look at that anger. Where does it come from? Where does it go? Don't try to eliminate it — just watch it. When you truly see it, it disappears."

## 常见误读 / Common Misreadings

| 误读 / Misreading | 澄清 / Clarification |
|---|---|
| 阿姜查反对学习经典 / Ajahn Chah opposed studying scriptures | 他反对的是以学习替代实修，而非学习本身。他鼓励在实修基础上理解经典。/ He opposed substituting study for practice, not study itself. He encouraged understanding scriptures on the basis of practice. |
| 森林禅修必须在森林中进行 / Forest meditation must be done in forests | 森林禅修的核心是心态而非地点。阿姜查说："真正的森林在你心中。"/ The core of forest meditation is mindset, not location. Ajahn Chah said: "The true forest is in your heart." |
| 阿姜查只教禅修 / Ajahn Chah only taught meditation | 他的教学涵盖戒、定、慧三学，包括伦理道德、禅修技巧和智慧开发。/ His teaching encompassed all three trainings — sila (ethics), samadhi (concentration), and panna (wisdom). |
| 简单生活就是贫穷 / Simple living means poverty | 简单生活是有意识地选择减少不必要的需求，而非被迫的匮乏。/ Simple living is a conscious choice to reduce unnecessary wants, not forced deprivation. |
