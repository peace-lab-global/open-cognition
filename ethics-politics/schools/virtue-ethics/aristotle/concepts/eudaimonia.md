# 幸福 · Eudaimonia

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

Eudaimonia（εὐδαιμονία）是亚里士多德伦理学的终极目标和核心概念，通常译为"幸福"或"蓬勃人生"（flourishing）。然而，这一翻译容易引起误导：eudaimonia 不是现代意义上的主观快乐或心理满足感，而是一种**客观的、关于人的整体生活的评价性概念**。在亚里士多德看来，eudaimonia 是"灵魂合乎美德的活动"（energeia tes psyches kat' areten），更准确地说，是灵魂在一生中持续地、完整地发挥其最卓越功能的状态。

Eudaimonia (εὐδαιμονία) is the ultimate goal and central concept of Aristotle's ethics, commonly translated as "happiness" or "flourishing." However, these translations can mislead: eudaimonia is not subjective pleasure or psychological satisfaction, but an **objective, evaluative concept concerning a person's whole life**. For Aristotle, eudaimonia is "an activity of the soul in accordance with virtue" (energeia tes psyches kat' areten) — more precisely, the state in which the soul continuously and completely exercises its most excellent function throughout a lifetime.

Eudaimonia 之所以是终极目标，在于它是**自足的**（autarkes）和**最终极的**（teleion）——我们追求财富、荣誉、健康都是为了幸福，但我们追求幸福不再是为了其他任何东西。这一概念贯穿了整个西方伦理学传统，并在20世纪被积极心理学（Positive Psychology）重新发现和重构。

Eudaimonia is the ultimate end because it is **self-sufficient** (autarkes) and **final** (teleion) — we pursue wealth, honor, and health for the sake of happiness, but we pursue happiness for nothing further. This concept pervades the entire Western ethical tradition and was rediscovered and reconstructed by Positive Psychology in the 20th century.

## 理论内涵 / Theoretical Content

### 功能论证 / The Ergon Argument

亚里士多德在《尼各马可伦理学》第一卷第七章提出了著名的"功能论证"（ergon argument），这是理解 eudaimonia 的关键：

Aristotle presents the famous "ergon argument" in *Nicomachean Ethics* I.7, which is key to understanding eudaimonia:

1. **前提一**：每种事物都有其特有的功能（ergon）。刀的功能是切割，眼的功能是观看。一物之善在于其功能的出色实现。/ **Premise 1**: Each thing has a distinctive function (ergon). A knife's function is cutting, an eye's is seeing. A thing's good consists in excellent performance of its function.

2. **前提二**：人类必定有其特有的功能。这个功能不能是营养生长（植物也有），不能是感觉感知（动物也有），只能是**理性活动**（logos）——包括理性思维和实践理性的运用。/ **Premise 2**: Humans must have a distinctive function. This cannot be nutrition/growth (shared with plants) or sense perception (shared with animals), but must be **rational activity** (logos) — including both theoretical reasoning and practical rationality.

3. **结论**：人类的善（eudaimonia）在于理性功能的出色实现，即"灵魂合乎美德的活动"。如果人有多种美德，那么 eudaimonia 在于合乎**最完善的美德**的活动。/ **Conclusion**: The human good (eudaimonia) consists in excellent realization of the rational function, i.e., "activity of soul in accordance with virtue." If there are multiple virtues, eudaimonia consists in activity according to the **most complete virtue**.

### 理智美德与品格美德 / Intellectual and Character Virtues

亚里士多德区分了两种美德：**理智美德**（dianoetike arete）——如智慧（sophia）、实践智慧（phronesis）——主要通过教导获得；**品格美德**（ethike arete）——如勇敢、节制、公正——主要通过习惯化获得。Eudaimonia 需要两种美德的共同作用，但在第十卷中，亚里士多德暗示沉思生活（theoria）可能是最完善的幸福形式，因为它运用了人类最高级的能力——理智（nous）。

Aristotle distinguishes two kinds of virtue: **intellectual virtues** (dianoetike arete) — such as wisdom (sophia) and practical wisdom (phronesis) — acquired mainly through teaching; and **character virtues** (ethike arete) — such as courage, temperance, justice — acquired mainly through habituation. Eudaimonia requires both kinds, but in Book X Aristotle suggests the contemplative life (theoria) may be the most complete form of happiness, as it exercises humanity's highest faculty — intellect (nous).

### 外在善的作用 / The Role of External Goods

Eudaimonia 不是纯粹内在的——它需要**外在善**的辅助：朋友、适度的财富、健康、好的出身、甚至好看的容貌。亚里士多德务实地承认，一个遭受极端不幸的人（如普里阿摩斯失去一切）很难说是幸福的。但这种对外在善的依赖并不否定 eudaimonia 的核心在于美德活动——外在善是使美德活动成为**可能的条件**，而非美德活动本身。

Eudaimonia is not purely internal — it requires **external goods**: friends, moderate wealth, health, good birth, even good looks. Aristotle pragmatically acknowledges that someone suffering extreme misfortune (like Priam losing everything) can hardly be called happy. But this dependence on external goods does not deny that eudaimonia's core lies in virtuous activity — external goods are the **conditions enabling** virtuous activity, not the activity itself.

## 思想史脉络 / Intellectual History

Eudaimonia 的概念深深扎根于古希腊文化。在荷马史诗中，eudaimonia 更多指外在的好运和繁荣；苏格拉底将其内在化，主张美德本身即足以使人幸福；柏拉图在《理想国》中论证了正义之人比不义之人更幸福。亚里士多德综合了这些传统，既坚持美德的核心地位（承继苏格拉底），又承认外在善的辅助作用（比苏格拉底更务实）。

The concept of eudaimonia is deeply rooted in ancient Greek culture. In Homeric epic, it referred more to external good fortune; Socrates internalized it, arguing virtue alone suffices for happiness; Plato argued in the *Republic* that the just person is happier than the unjust. Aristotle synthesized these traditions, maintaining virtue's centrality (inheriting Socrates) while acknowledging external goods' auxiliary role (more pragmatic than Socrates).

在中世纪，阿奎那将 eudaimonia 与基督教的"至福直观"（beatific vision）相结合——最终的幸福在于对上帝的直接认识。启蒙运动后，康德以义务论取代了幸福论，功利主义以快乐最大化取代了 eudaimonia。直到20世纪下半叶，安斯科姆（1958）和麦金太尔（1981）才重新将 eudaimonia 置于伦理学讨论的中心。

In the Middle Ages, Aquinas combined eudaimonia with the Christian "beatific vision" — ultimate happiness in direct knowledge of God. After the Enlightenment, Kant replaced eudaimonism with deontology; utilitarianism replaced eudaimonia with pleasure maximization. Not until the late 20th century did Anscombe (1958) and MacIntyre (1981) restore eudaimonia to the center of ethical discussion.

## 关键文本 / Key Passages

> "每一种技艺和每一种探究，同样地，每一种行动和追求，都被认为是以某种善为目的的；因此人们恰当地宣称，善是一切事物所追求的目的。"
> — 《尼各马可伦理学》I.1, 1094a1–2

> "Every art and every inquiry, and similarly every action and pursuit, is thought to aim at some good; and for this reason the good has rightly been declared to be that at which all things aim."
> — *Nicomachean Ethics* I.1, 1094a1–2

**分析**: 这一开篇确立了亚里士多德伦理学的目的论结构——所有人类活动都指向善，伦理学的任务是找到最高善。

**Analysis**: This opening establishes the teleological structure of Aristotle's ethics — all human activities aim at the good, and ethics' task is to find the highest good.

> "人的善是灵魂合乎美德的活动，如果美德有多种，则合乎最完善、最终极的美德的活动。"
> — 《尼各马可伦理学》I.7, 1098a16–18

> "The human good turns out to be activity of soul in accordance with virtue, and if there are more than one virtue, in accordance with the best and most complete."
> — *Nicomachean Ethics* I.7, 1098a16–18

> "因为一只燕子或一个好天气不能构成春天，同样，一天或短暂的时间不能使人幸福或蒙福。"
> — 《尼各马可伦理学》I.7, 1098a18–20

> "For one swallow does not make a summer, nor does one day; and so too one day, or a short time, does not make a man blessed and happy."
> — *Nicomachean Ethics* I.7, 1098a18–20

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

积极心理学（Positive Psychology）的创始人马丁·塞利格曼（Martin Seligman）明确借鉴了亚里士多德的 eudaimonia 概念，将"蓬勃人生"（flourishing）作为积极心理学的核心目标，区别于传统心理学对"主观幸福感"（subjective well-being）的过度关注。塞利格曼的 PERMA 模型（积极情绪、投入、关系、意义、成就）可视为 eudaimonia 的当代心理学重构。

Martin Seligman, founder of Positive Psychology, explicitly drew on Aristotle's eudaimonia, establishing "flourishing" as its central goal, distinct from traditional psychology's excessive focus on "subjective well-being." Seligman's PERMA model (Positive emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, Achievement) can be seen as a contemporary psychological reconstruction of eudaimonia.

在组织管理学中，"employee flourishing"已成为替代"员工满意度"的新范式——关注员工在工作中实现其潜能和美德，而非仅仅感到快乐。在发展经济学中，阿马蒂亚·森（Amartya Sen）和纳斯鲍姆（Martha Nussbaum）的"能力进路"（Capability Approach）将 eudaimonia 的功能论证转化为衡量发展质量的核心指标——发展不是GDP增长，而是人类核心能力的扩展。

In organizational management, "employee flourishing" has replaced "employee satisfaction" as a new paradigm — focusing on employees realizing their potential and virtues at work, not merely feeling happy. In development economics, Amartya Sen and Martha Nussbaum's Capability Approach transforms eudaimonia's ergon argument into a core metric for development quality — development is not GDP growth but the expansion of core human capabilities.

## 批评与回应 / Critiques and Responses

**批评1：功能论证的生物学缺陷** / **Critique 1: Biological flaws in the ergon argument**
现代进化生物学质疑"每种事物有特定功能"的目的论前提——人类的"功能"可能只是进化的偶然产物，不具有规范性。/ Modern evolutionary biology questions the teleological premise that each thing has a specific function — humanity's "function" may be an evolutionary accident without normative force.

**回应**: 当代亚里士多德主义者（如 Hursthouse）以"自然 goodness"论证回应——即使没有形而上学的目的论，我们仍可参照生物物种的典型生活方式来评估个体的功能完善。/ **Response**: Contemporary Aristotelians (like Hursthouse) respond with "natural goodness" arguments — even without metaphysical teleology, we can evaluate individual flourishing by reference to a species' typical life form.

**批评2：文化特殊主义** / **Critique 2: Cultural particularism**
亚里士多德对"人的功能"的界定过度依赖古希腊文化对理性的崇拜，忽视了情感、身体和关系性的维度。/ Aristotle's definition of "human function" over-relies on ancient Greek rationality-worship, neglecting emotional, bodily, and relational dimensions.

**回应**: 亚里士多德本人承认灵魂有非理性部分（orektikon），且强调美德涉及情感和欲望的正确引导，并非纯理性活动。当代诠释进一步扩展了"理性活动"的含义。/ **Response**: Aristotle himself acknowledges the soul's non-rational part (orektikon) and emphasizes that virtue involves correct guidance of emotions and desires, not pure rational activity. Contemporary interpretations further broaden "rational activity."

## 跨文化回响 / Cross-cultural Echoes

在佛教传统中，"涅槃"（nirvana）与 eudaimonia 有结构上的相似性——两者都指向一种客观的、不以主观感受定义的人类最高状态。然而，涅槃强调从苦难中解脱（苦灭），而 eudaimonia 强调在积极的理性活动中实现人的潜能。在儒家传统中，"至善"或"圣人"境界与 eudaimonia 最为接近——两者都将最高的人生状态定义为美德的充分实现而非享乐。庄子所追求的"逍遥游"则提供了一种替代性的"蓬勃人生"愿景——通过超越人为规范、回归自然本性来实现生命的完满。

In Buddhism, nirvana shares structural similarities with eudaimonia — both point to an objective, non-subjective highest human state. However, nirvana emphasizes liberation from suffering, while eudaimonia emphasizes realizing human potential through active rational activity. In Confucianism, "the highest good" or sagehood most closely approximates eudaimonia — both define life's highest state as full virtue-realization rather than pleasure. Zhuangzi's "free and easy wandering" offers an alternative flourishing vision — realizing life's fullness by transcending artificial norms and returning to natural spontaneity.
