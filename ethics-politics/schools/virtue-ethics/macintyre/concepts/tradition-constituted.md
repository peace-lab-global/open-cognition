# 传统构成的合理性 · Tradition-Constituted Rationality

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

"传统构成的合理性"是麦金泰尔在《谁之正义？何种合理性？》（1988）中系统发展的核心概念。它挑战了启蒙运动以来关于理性的主流观念——即理性是一种超越所有传统和历史处境的普遍能力。麦金泰尔论证说，所有推理都在某种传统之内进行：我们学习推理的方式是通过进入和参与某个特定的智识传统。合理性本身是传统构成的（tradition-constituted）——不存在脱离任何传统的"纯粹理性"。然而，这并不意味着相对主义：麦金泰尔发展了一套关于传统间理性争论的精细理论，论证了不同传统之间的比较和评判是可能的。

"Tradition-constituted rationality" is the central concept that MacIntyre systematically developed in *Whose Justice? Which Rationality?* (1988). It challenges the mainstream conception of reason since the Enlightenment — that reason is a universal faculty transcending all traditions and historical situations. MacIntyre argues that all reasoning occurs within some tradition: we learn to reason by entering into and participating in a particular intellectual tradition. Rationality itself is tradition-constituted — there is no "pure reason" detached from any tradition. However, this does not entail relativism: MacIntyre develops a sophisticated theory of rational dispute between traditions, arguing that comparison and evaluation between different traditions is possible.

## 理论内涵 / Theoretical Content

麦金泰尔将"传统"定义为"关于争论的活着的论证"（a living argument about the goods of that tradition）。这一定义有几个关键含义。首先，传统不是静态的教条集合，而是一个动态的、持续的争论过程。一个活着的传统始终包含着关于其自身的善、目的和方向的内部争论。其次，传统具有历史性——它是由一系列历史事件、文本、制度和实践所构成的延续性叙事。理解一个传统就是理解它的历史，包括它的成就、失败和转折点。

MacIntyre defines a "tradition" as "a living argument about the goods of that tradition." This definition carries several key implications. First, a tradition is not a static collection of dogmas but a dynamic, ongoing process of argument. A living tradition always contains internal debate about its own goods, purposes, and direction. Second, a tradition has historicity — it is constituted by a continuing narrative of historical events, texts, institutions, and practices. To understand a tradition is to understand its history, including its achievements, failures, and turning points.

传统构成的合理性的核心主张是：合理性标准本身是在传统内部发展出来的。不同的传统可能有不同的合理性标准——亚里士多德传统的合理性标准不同于康德传统，不同于佛教传统。然而，麦金泰尔拒绝因此陷入相对主义。他提出了"认识论危机"（epistemological crisis）的概念：当一个传统面对来自其他传统的挑战时，如果它无法在自身的概念框架内对这种挑战给出令人满意的回应，它就陷入了认识论危机。在这种情况下，一个传统可以认识到另一个传统在解决某些问题上更为优越——这就是传统间理性比较成为可能的机制。

The core claim of tradition-constituted rationality is that standards of rationality themselves are developed within traditions. Different traditions may have different standards of rationality — the Aristotelian tradition's standards differ from the Kantian tradition's, which differ from the Buddhist tradition's. However, MacIntyre refuses to collapse into relativism. He introduces the concept of "epistemological crisis": when a tradition confronts challenges from other traditions and cannot provide a satisfactory response within its own conceptual framework, it enters an epistemological crisis. In such situations, one tradition can recognize that another is superior in addressing certain problems — this is the mechanism by which rational comparison between traditions becomes possible.

## 思想史脉络 / Intellectual History

麦金泰尔的传统概念有其丰富的思想渊源。黑格尔的历史哲学——认为理性是在历史过程中展开的——对年轻的麦金泰尔产生了重要影响。伽达默尔的诠释学——强调理解总是受到传统（Wirkungsgeschichte）的中介——提供了另一个重要来源。库恩（Thomas Kuhn）的科学范式概念——认为科学推理总是在特定范式之内进行——也对麦金泰尔产生了影响。然而，麦金泰尔对这些影响进行了独特的综合，发展出了一套既承认传统的构成性作用、又避免相对主义的合理性理论。

MacIntyre's concept of tradition has rich intellectual origins. Hegel's philosophy of history — the idea that reason unfolds through historical processes — profoundly influenced the young MacIntyre. Gadamer's hermeneutics — emphasizing that understanding is always mediated by tradition (*Wirkungsgeschichte*) — provided another important source. Kuhn's concept of scientific paradigms — the idea that scientific reasoning always occurs within a particular paradigm — also influenced MacIntyre. However, MacIntyre uniquely synthesized these influences to develop a theory of rationality that acknowledges the constitutive role of tradition while avoiding relativism.

在《三种对立的道德探究》（1990）中，麦金泰尔进一步区分了三种对待传统的态度：百科全书派（Encyclopaedists，如启蒙思想家）将传统视为非理性的偏见；谱系学家（Genealogists，如尼采和后现代思想家）将传统解构为权力关系；而传统主义者（如亚里士多德和托马斯）将传统视为理性探究的活的框架。麦金泰尔论证第三种态度最为哲学上有说服力。

In *Three Rival Versions of Moral Enquiry* (1990), MacIntyre further distinguished three attitudes toward tradition: Encyclopaedists (like Enlightenment thinkers) treat tradition as irrational prejudice; Genealogists (like Nietzsche and postmodern thinkers) deconstruct tradition as power relations; while Traditionalists (like Aristotle and Aquinas) treat tradition as a living framework for rational inquiry. MacIntyre argues that the third attitude is the most philosophically compelling.

## 关键文本 / Key Passages

> "A tradition is an argument extended through time in which certain fundamental agreements are defined and redefined in terms of two kinds of conflict: those with critics and enemies external to the tradition... and those internal, interpretive debates through which the meaning and rationale of the fundamental agreements are defined and by being defined, redefined." (*Whose Justice? Which Rationality?*, Ch. 18)

这段定义揭示了麦金泰尔对传统的动态理解：传统不是固定教条，而是通过内部争论和外部挑战而不断重新定义的活的论证。This definition reveals MacIntyre's dynamic understanding of tradition: not fixed dogma but a living argument constantly redefined through internal debate and external challenge.

> "To understand the central concepts of a tradition is to understand the history through which those concepts have been developed." (*Whose Justice? Which Rationality?*, Ch. 18)

麦金泰尔在此强调传统的历史性：概念的意义只能通过理解它们在传统中的历史发展来把握。MacIntyre here emphasizes the historicity of tradition: the meaning of concepts can only be grasped by understanding their historical development within a tradition.

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

传统构成的合理性概念在当代跨文化哲学、宗教对话和比较政治学中得到了广泛应用。在跨文化哲学中，它为不同哲学传统之间的对话提供了框架——既避免了虚假的普遍主义，又避免了封闭的相对主义。在宗教对话中，它帮助不同宗教传统理解彼此的合理性标准，并在"认识论危机"的框架内理解信仰转变。在比较政治学中，它为分析不同政治传统（如西方自由主义与东亚儒家政治传统）之间的对话和冲突提供了理论工具。

The concept of tradition-constituted rationality has been widely applied in contemporary cross-cultural philosophy, religious dialogue, and comparative politics. In cross-cultural philosophy, it provides a framework for dialogue between philosophical traditions — avoiding both false universalism and closed relativism. In religious dialogue, it helps different religious traditions understand each other's standards of rationality and comprehend faith transitions within the framework of "epistemological crisis." In comparative politics, it offers theoretical tools for analyzing dialogue and conflict between different political traditions (such as Western liberalism and East Asian Confucian political traditions).

## 批评与回应 / Critiques and Responses

**批评 / Critique**: 哈贝马斯（Jürgen Habermas）等批评者认为，麦金泰尔的传统主义最终无法为跨传统的理性批判提供基础，因为合理性标准本身是传统构成的。Critics like Habermas argue that MacIntyre's traditionalism ultimately cannot provide a basis for rational critique across traditions, since standards of rationality are themselves tradition-constituted.
**回应 / Response**: 麦金泰尔通过"认识论危机"概念回应了这一批评：一个传统可以通过面对他者的挑战而认识到自身的局限，这种认识本身就预设了某种跨传统的理性能力。MacIntyre responds through the concept of "epistemological crisis": a tradition can recognize its own limitations when confronted by the challenge of another, and this recognition itself presupposes a form of cross-traditional rationality.

**批评 / Critique**: 女性主义批评者指出，麦金泰尔对传统的强调可能强化传统中已经存在的权力不平等——特别是性别不平等。Feminist critics argue that MacIntyre's emphasis on tradition may reinforce existing power inequalities within traditions — particularly gender inequality.
**回应 / Response**: 麦金泰尔承认传统内部可能包含压迫性元素，但他认为传统内部的争论机制本身就为批判和改革提供了资源。MacIntyre acknowledges that traditions may contain oppressive elements, but argues that the internal debate mechanism within traditions itself provides resources for critique and reform.

## 跨文化回响 / Cross-cultural Echoes

麦金泰尔的传统构成的合理性与儒家的"道统"（transmission of the Way）概念形成了深刻的对应。在儒家传统中，"道"不是一种超越历史的抽象法则，而是通过圣贤的代际传承而延续和发展的活的智慧。儒家的"道统"——从尧舜禹汤到孔孟——正是一个麦金泰尔意义上的"传统"：一个关于"道"的意义的持续争论。同样，佛教的"法脉传承"（lineage of dharma transmission）概念也体现了类似的传统构成性理解——智慧不是个人独立发现的，而是在师徒传承的活的论证中传递和发展的。

MacIntyre's tradition-constituted rationality finds a profound counterpart in the Confucian concept of *daotong* (transmission of the Way). In the Confucian tradition, the *dao* is not an abstract law transcending history but living wisdom continued and developed through intergenerational transmission by sage-kings. The Confucian *daotong* — from Yao, Shun, Yu, and Tang to Confucius and Mencius — is precisely a "tradition" in MacIntyre's sense: a continuing argument about the meaning of the Way. Similarly, the Buddhist concept of dharma transmission lineage embodies a comparable tradition-constituted understanding — wisdom is not independently discovered by individuals but transmitted and developed within the living argument of teacher-student transmission.
