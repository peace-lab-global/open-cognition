# 善的脆弱性 · Fragility of Goodness

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

"善的脆弱性"（The Fragility of Goodness）是纳斯鲍姆最具原创性的哲学贡献之一，也是她整个思想体系的基石性概念。其核心论题是：人类的善（eudaimonia，即繁荣或美好生活）不是完全处于个人控制之内的——它不可避免地受到运气（tyche）和外在环境的影响。一个真正繁荣的人类生活，恰恰因为其包含了爱、友谊和政治参与等珍贵事物，而必然是脆弱的——因为这些事物都依赖于不可控的外在条件。

"The Fragility of Goodness" is one of Nussbaum's most original philosophical contributions and a foundational concept of her entire intellectual system. Its core thesis is that human goodness (eudaimonia, i.e., flourishing or the good life) is not entirely within individual control — it is inevitably subject to luck (tyche) and external circumstances. A truly flourishing human life, precisely because it includes precious things like love, friendship, and political participation, is necessarily fragile — because these things depend on uncontrollable external conditions.

这一论题构成了对西方伦理学两大传统——斯多亚主义和康德主义——的根本挑战。两者都以不同方式主张善不受运气影响：斯多亚学派认为真正的善只在于内在德性（virtue），外在损失不影响幸福；康德认为道德价值完全取决于善良意志（good will），不受外在条件制约。纳斯鲍姆通过希腊悲剧和亚里士多德伦理学来反驳这种"不受脆弱性影响"的善的观念。

This thesis constitutes a fundamental challenge to two major traditions in Western ethics — Stoicism and Kantianism. Both maintain in different ways that goodness is immune to luck: Stoics hold that true goodness lies only in inner virtue, with external losses not affecting happiness; Kantians hold that moral value depends entirely on the good will, unconstrained by external conditions. Through Greek tragedy and Aristotelian ethics, Nussbaum refutes this conception of goodness "immune to fragility."

## 理论内涵 / Theoretical Content

纳斯鲍姆的论证从三个层面展开：

Nussbaum's argument unfolds on three levels:

**第一层：悲剧的伦理知识。** 通过对欧里庇得斯（Euripides）、索福克勒斯（Sophocles）和埃斯库罗斯（Aeschylus）的悲剧的深入解读，纳斯鲍姆论证了希腊悲剧蕴含着深刻的伦理知识。悲剧中的英雄并非因为道德缺陷而遭受不幸——赫卡柏（Hecuba）失去一切并非因为她的过错，而是因为战争和命运的残酷。悲剧揭示了一个令人不安的真相：即使最有德性的人也可能被运气所摧毁，而正是这种脆弱性使得人类生活具有其独特的深度和价值。

**Level 1: Ethical knowledge of tragedy.** Through close readings of Euripides, Sophocles, and Aeschylus, Nussbaum argues that Greek tragedy contains profound ethical knowledge. Tragic heroes do not suffer misfortune because of moral defects — Hecuba loses everything not through her fault but through the cruelty of war and fate. Tragedy reveals an unsettling truth: even the most virtuous person can be destroyed by luck, and it is precisely this fragility that gives human life its distinctive depth and value.

**第二层：亚里士多德的外在善理论。** 纳斯鲍姆对亚里士多德《尼各马可伦理学》的解读聚焦于"外在善"（external goods）的角色。亚里士多德认为eudaimonia不仅需要德性（virtue），还需要"外在善"——朋友、财富、政治地位、好出身、美貌、好运。没有这些外在善，即使最有德性的人也无法实现完全的繁荣。纳斯鲍姆将这一观点发展为"善的脆弱性"命题：人类的善本质上就是脆弱的，因为它包含了依赖于外在条件的重要成分。

**Level 2: Aristotle's external goods theory.** Nussbaum's reading of Aristotle's Nicomachean Ethics focuses on the role of "external goods." Aristotle holds that eudaimonia requires not only virtue but also "external goods" — friends, wealth, political standing, good birth, beauty, good fortune. Without these external goods, even the most virtuous person cannot achieve complete flourishing. Nussbaum develops this into the "fragility of goodness" thesis: human goodness is inherently fragile because it includes important components dependent on external conditions.

**第三层：对斯多亚"治疗"方案的批评。** 斯多亚学派认识到脆弱性带来的痛苦，因此提出了一种"治疗"方案：通过消除对外在事物的依恋来消除脆弱性。如果你不爱任何可能失去的东西，你就不会因为失去而痛苦。纳斯鲍姆承认这种方案的心理吸引力，但认为它在伦理上是贫乏的——一个为了避免脆弱性而放弃爱、友谊和政治参与的生活，虽然"安全"，却丧失了人类繁荣中最有价值的成分。

**Level 3: Critique of the Stoic "therapy" program.** The Stoics recognized the suffering caused by fragility and proposed a "therapy" program: eliminate fragility by eliminating attachment to external things. If you love nothing that can be lost, you will not suffer from loss. Nussbaum acknowledges this program's psychological appeal but considers it ethically impoverished — a life that avoids fragility by abandoning love, friendship, and political participation, though "safe," loses the most valuable components of human flourishing.

## 思想史脉络 / Intellectual History

善的脆弱性问题在西方伦理学中有着悠久的历史。柏拉图在《理想国》中通过"善的理念"（Form of the Good）试图为善提供一个不受运气影响的形而上学基础。亚里士多德虽然承认外在善的重要性，但在《尼各马可伦理学》第十卷中似乎倾向于将最高的幸福归于沉思生活（theoria），这种生活较少受外在条件影响。斯多亚学派（芝诺、塞内卡、爱比克泰德、马可·奥勒留）将"善不受运气影响"的论题推向极致——只有德性是善，外在事物都是"无关紧要的"（adiaphora）。

The fragility of goodness problem has a long history in Western ethics. Plato in the Republic attempted through the "Form of the Good" to provide a metaphysical foundation for goodness immune to luck. Aristotle, while acknowledging external goods' importance, appears in Nicomachean Ethics Book X to lean toward attributing the highest happiness to contemplative life (theoria), which is less affected by external conditions. The Stoics (Zeno, Seneca, Epictetus, Marcus Aurelius) pushed the "goodness immune to luck" thesis to its extreme — only virtue is good, external things are "indifferent" (adiaphora).

在现代哲学中，康德继承并强化了斯多亚的核心洞见：道德价值只取决于善良意志，不受外在条件制约。伯纳德·威廉姆斯（Bernard Williams）和托马斯·内格尔（Thomas Nagel）关于"道德运气"（moral luck）的讨论在20世纪重新激活了这一问题。纳斯鲍姆的贡献在于她不仅论证了善的脆弱性，还将其发展为政治哲学的出发点——正因为善是脆弱的，正义的制度才需要为所有人提供保护脆弱性的基本条件。

In modern philosophy, Kant inherited and reinforced the Stoic core insight: moral value depends only on the good will, unconstrained by external conditions. Bernard Williams's and Thomas Nagel's discussions of "moral luck" reactivated this issue in the 20th century. Nussbaum's contribution lies not only in demonstrating goodness's fragility but also in developing it as a starting point for political philosophy — precisely because goodness is fragile, just institutions need to provide all people with basic conditions for protecting against vulnerability.

## 关键文本 / Key Passages

> "The ethical concern of the Greeks is not just with being good, but with living well; not just with having a good will, but with having a good life. And a good life requires good things from the world."
> — Nussbaum, *The Fragility of Goodness* (1986), p. 4
>
> "希腊人的伦理关切不仅在于做一个好人，更在于活得好；不仅在于拥有善良的意志，更在于拥有美好的生活。而美好的生活需要来自世界的好事物。"

> "Tragedy shows us that the most valuable elements of a good human life are fragile — they can be destroyed by circumstances beyond the agent's control, and yet they are among the most important constituents of a truly human life."
> — Nussbaum, *The Fragility of Goodness* (1986), p. 2
>
> "悲剧向我们展示，美好人类生活中最有价值的要素是脆弱的——它们可以被超出行动者控制的环境所摧毁，然而它们恰恰是真正人类生活中最重要的组成部分。"

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

善的脆弱性概念对当代多个领域具有深刻意义。在社会政策中，它提醒我们：个人的繁荣不仅取决于个人努力和德性，还取决于社会制度是否为其提供了免受不幸打击的基本保护——这正是福利国家和能力进路的伦理基础。在医学伦理中，它帮助我们理解：疾病和残障不仅损害了身体功能，还威胁了人类生活中最珍贵的关系和能力的脆弱基础。在气候正义中，它揭示了气候变化如何威胁全球最脆弱人群的基本繁荣条件——这些人对气候变化的责任最小，却承受了最大的脆弱性风险。

The fragility of goodness concept has profound significance for multiple contemporary fields. In social policy, it reminds us that individual flourishing depends not only on personal effort and virtue but also on whether social institutions provide basic protection against misfortune — this is the ethical foundation of the welfare state and the capability approach. In medical ethics, it helps us understand that illness and disability damage not only bodily functions but also threaten the fragile foundations of the most precious relationships and capabilities in human life. In climate justice, it reveals how climate change threatens the basic flourishing conditions of the world's most vulnerable populations — those least responsible for climate change bear the greatest vulnerability risks.

## 批评与回应 / Critiques and Responses

**批评一：** 纳斯鲍姆是否低估了斯多亚主义对弱势群体的慰藉价值？ / Does Nussbaum underestimate Stoicism's consolatory value for vulnerable groups?
**回应：** 纳斯鲍姆承认斯多亚主义的心理学价值，但她区分了个人层面的应对策略和制度层面的正义要求。斯多亚主义可以帮助个人应对不幸，但不能作为放弃制度性正义追求的理由。 / Nussbaum acknowledges Stoicism's psychological value but distinguishes personal coping strategies from institutional justice requirements. Stoicism can help individuals cope with misfortune but cannot justify abandoning the pursuit of institutional justice.

**批评二：** 如果善本质上是脆弱的，那是否意味着我们应该接受命运而放弃行动？ / If goodness is inherently fragile, does that mean we should accept fate and abandon action?
**回应：** 恰恰相反——正因为善是脆弱的，我们才更需要通过政治行动来建立保护脆弱性的制度。脆弱性不是放弃的理由，而是行动的呼唤。 / Quite the contrary — precisely because goodness is fragile, we need political action to build institutions that protect against vulnerability. Fragility is not a reason for resignation but a call to action.

## 跨文化回响 / Cross-cultural Echoes

善的脆弱性概念与佛教的"无常"（anitya/impermanence）教义形成了深刻的对话。佛教同样认识到一切有为法（conditioned phenomena）都是无常的、脆弱的，爱别离是人生苦难（dukkha）的核心来源。然而，佛教的回应策略与斯多亚主义类似——通过认识到无常的本质来减少执著，从而减少痛苦。纳斯鲍姆的独特之处在于她拒绝了这种"逃避"策略，主张我们应该在承认脆弱性的同时，通过政治制度来缓冲脆弱性的影响——而不是通过心理修炼来消除对脆弱事物的依恋。在道家传统中，"柔弱胜刚强"（《道德经》第76章）的洞见与善的脆弱性形成了有趣的呼应——但道家对"柔弱"的肯定更接近一种本体论层面的智慧，而非纳斯鲍姆的政治伦理学诉求。

The fragility of goodness concept forms a profound dialogue with Buddhism's "anitya" (impermanence) teaching. Buddhism similarly recognizes that all conditioned phenomena are impermanent and fragile, with separation from loved ones as a core source of suffering (dukkha). However, Buddhism's response strategy parallels Stoicism — reducing attachment through recognizing impermanence's nature, thereby reducing suffering. Nussbaum's distinctiveness lies in rejecting this "escape" strategy, advocating that we acknowledge fragility while buffering its effects through political institutions — rather than eliminating attachment to fragile things through psychological cultivation. In Daoist tradition, the insight that "the soft overcomes the hard" (Daodejing, Ch. 76) interestingly echoes the fragility of goodness — but Daoist affirmation of "softness" approaches an ontological wisdom rather than Nussbaum's political ethics aspiration.
