# 世俗时代 · A Secular Age

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

"世俗时代"（a secular age）是泰勒2007年出版的同名巨著（*A Secular Age*）的核心概念。泰勒追问一个看似简单但极为深刻的问题："为什么在1500年，信仰上帝几乎是不可避免的，而在2000年，许多人却觉得不信上帝更为自然？" 这个问题不是关于宗教是否"正确"，而是关于信仰的条件（conditions of belief）如何发生了根本转变。

"A secular age" is the central concept of Taylor's 2007 magnum opus (*A Secular Age*). Taylor asks a seemingly simple but profoundly deep question: "Why was it virtually impossible not to believe in God in 1500, while in 2000 many find it more natural not to believe?" This question is not about whether religion is "correct" but about how the conditions of belief have fundamentally transformed.

泰勒区分了"世俗性"（secularity）的三种含义：（1）世俗性1——宗教从公共空间的退场（secularization of public spaces）；（2）世俗性2——宗教信仰和实践的衰落（decline of religious belief and practice）；（3）世俗性3——信仰条件的转变，即信仰从"默认的、无可置疑的"变为"诸多选项之一"（a change in the conditions of belief, where faith shifts from "default and unquestioned" to "one option among many"）。泰勒的关注焦点是第三种世俗性。

Taylor distinguishes three meanings of "secularity": (1) Secularity 1 — the retreat of religion from public spaces; (2) Secularity 2 — the decline of religious belief and practice; (3) Secularity 3 — a change in the conditions of belief, where faith shifts from "default and unquestioned" to "one option among many." Taylor's focus is on the third form of secularity.

## 理论内涵 / Theoretical Content

### 减法故事 vs 建构故事 / Subtraction Story vs Construction Story

泰勒对世俗化的主流解释——"减法故事"（subtraction story）——提出了根本性批评。减法故事认为，世俗化就是人类理性逐步剥落宗教迷信和幻觉的过程——当科学和理性足够强大时，宗教自然消亡。泰勒论证，这种叙事本身就是一种意识形态——它预设了"世俗"是"自然的"，而"宗教"是"需要解释的异常"。

Taylor fundamentally criticizes the mainstream explanation of secularization — the "subtraction story." The subtraction story holds that secularization is humanity's reason progressively shedding religious superstition and illusion — when science and reason are strong enough, religion naturally disappears. Taylor argues this narrative is itself ideological — it presupposes that "secular" is "natural" while "religious" is "an anomaly requiring explanation."

泰勒提出的替代方案是"建构故事"（construction story）——世俗性不是宗教迷信被剥落后的"自然残余"，而是一种历史建构的产物，有着自身的谱系和条件。世俗时代有自己的"社会想象"（social imaginary），自己的道德框架，自己的对"完满"（fullness）的理解——它不是宗教的简单否定，而是一种新的存在方式。

Taylor's alternative is a "construction story" — secularity is not the "natural residue" after religious superstition is shed but a historically constructed product with its own genealogy and conditions. The secular age has its own "social imaginary," its own moral framework, its own understanding of "fullness" — it is not the simple negation of religion but a new mode of existence.

### 附魅世界与祛魅世界 / The Enchanted and Disenchanted World

泰勒对比了前现代世界的"附魅"（enchanted）特征与现代世界的"祛魅"（disenchanted）特征。在附魅世界中，自我是"多孔的"（porous）——外部世界的精灵、恶魔、圣物可以直接穿透自我，影响我们的情感、意志和身体。意义不是内在的，而是存在于世界本身——河流、树木、星辰都承载着意义。在祛魅世界中，自我变成了"缓冲的"（buffered）——自我与世界之间有一道屏障，意义被理解为内在的、主观的投射，世界本身被视为无意义的物质。

Taylor contrasts the pre-modern world's "enchanted" character with the modern world's "disenchanted" character. In the enchanted world, the self is "porous" — spirits, demons, and sacred objects from the external world can directly penetrate the self, affecting our emotions, will, and body. Meaning is not internal but exists in the world itself — rivers, trees, and stars all carry meaning. In the disenchanted world, the self becomes "buffered" — a barrier exists between self and world, meaning is understood as internal, subjective projection, and the world itself is seen as meaningless matter.

### 内在框架 / The Immanent Frame

"内在框架"（immanent frame）是泰勒最核心的概念之一——它指的是世俗时代中人类经验被封闭在一个纯粹自然的、无需超越的秩序中的认知和社会想象框架。在内在框架中，社会生活、经济活动、政治秩序都可以在纯粹世俗的层面被理解和运作，无需诉诸上帝或超越性实在。

The "immanent frame" is one of Taylor's most central concepts — it refers to the cognitive and social imaginary framework in which human experience in the secular age is enclosed in a purely natural order requiring no transcendence. Within the immanent frame, social life, economic activity, and political order can be understood and operated on a purely secular level without appeal to God or transcendent reality.

泰勒论证，内在框架可以是"封闭的"（closed）——当人们将其视为全部现实，拒绝任何超越的可能性；也可以是"开放的"（open）——当人们在内在框架中仍然保持对超越维度的敏感和开放。现代人的困境是：我们生活在内在框架中，但我们对超越的渴望并未消失——这产生了泰勒所说的"交叉压力"（cross-pressure）。

Taylor argues that the immanent frame can be "closed" — when people treat it as all of reality, refusing any possibility of transcendence; or "open" — when people remain sensitive and open to the transcendent dimension within the immanent frame. Modern people's predicament is: we live in the immanent frame, but our longing for transcendence has not disappeared — this produces what Taylor calls "cross-pressure."

### 新星效应 / The Nova Effect

泰勒用"新星效应"（nova effect）来描述世俗时代中信仰和灵性选项的爆炸性增殖。当传统的单一信仰框架瓦解后，不是出现了统一的无神论，而是出现了灵性选项的"超新星"（supernova）——从新纪元运动（New Age）到各种灵性实验，从原教旨主义到不可知论，现代人面临着前所未有的信仰选择。这种增殖不是世俗化的证据，而是世俗时代信仰条件的特征。

Taylor uses the "nova effect" to describe the explosive proliferation of faith and spiritual options in the secular age. When the traditional single faith framework collapsed, what emerged was not uniform atheism but a "supernova" of spiritual options — from New Age movements to various spiritual experiments, from fundamentalism to agnosticism, modern people face unprecedented choices of faith. This proliferation is not evidence of secularization but a characteristic feature of belief conditions in the secular age.

## 思想史脉络 / Intellectual History

《世俗时代》是对经典世俗化理论的全面重写。韦伯（Max Weber）的"世界的祛魅"（disenchantment of the world）、涂尔干（Émile Durkheim）的宗教社会学、伯格（Peter Berger）的"神圣的帷幕"（sacred canopy）——这些经典理论都将世俗化理解为宗教的自然衰落。泰勒的贡献在于展示世俗化不是"自然的"过程，而是有着特定历史条件的建构——它主要发生在西方基督教文明中，不一定适用于其他文明。

*A Secular Age* is a comprehensive rewriting of classical secularization theory. Max Weber's "disenchantment of the world," Émile Durkheim's religious sociology, Peter Berger's "sacred canopy" — these classical theories all understood secularization as religion's natural decline. Taylor's contribution was showing that secularization is not a "natural" process but a construction with specific historical conditions — it primarily occurs in Western Christian civilization and may not apply to other civilizations.

## 关键文本 / Key Passages

> "What does it mean to say that we live in a secular age? ... The shift to secularity consists, among other things, of a move from a society where belief in God is unchallenged and indeed, unproblematic, to one in which it is understood to be one option among others."
> — Taylor, *A Secular Age* (2007), p. 3

> "说我们生活在一个世俗时代意味着什么？……向世俗性的转变包括：从一个信仰上帝无可置疑且不成问题的社会，转向一个将其理解为诸多选项之一的社会。"

> "We all see our lives, and/or the space wherein we live our lives, as having a certain moral/spiritual shape. Somewhere, in some activity, or condition, lies a fullness, a richness."
> — Taylor, *A Secular Age* (2007), p. 5

> "我们都看到我们的生活，和/或我们生活的空间，具有某种道德/精神的形态。在某处，在某种活动或条件中，存在着一种完满、一种丰富。"

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

"世俗时代"概念在当代的应用极为广泛。在宗教社会学中，泰勒的理论帮助学者理解全球宗教复兴——伊斯兰复兴运动、中国民间宗教的回潮、拉丁美洲的五旬节运动——不是"反世俗化"，而是在世俗时代条件下宗教的新形态。在政治神学中，"内在框架"概念为分析世俗国家与宗教之间的紧张关系提供了工具——法国"政教分离"（laïcité）政策与美国"宗教自由"传统之间的差异可以被理解为不同的"内在框架"配置。在文化批评中，"交叉压力"概念帮助解释当代人对灵性（spirituality）而非制度性宗教（institutional religion）的偏好——人们在内在框架内寻求超越性体验。

The "secular age" concept has extremely broad contemporary applications. In religious sociology, Taylor's theory helps scholars understand global religious revival — Islamic revivalism, the return of Chinese folk religion, the Pentecostal movement in Latin America — not as "counter-secularization" but as religion's new forms under secular age conditions. In political theology, the "immanent frame" concept provides tools for analyzing tensions between secular states and religion — the difference between French "laïcité" and American "religious freedom" traditions can be understood as different "immanent frame" configurations. In cultural criticism, the "cross-pressure" concept helps explain contemporary preference for "spirituality" over institutional religion — people seek transcendent experiences within the immanent frame.

## 批评与回应 / Critiques and Responses

**批评一：基督教中心主义 / Critique 1: Christian-centrism**
批评者指出，泰勒的叙事主要基于西方基督教文明的历史——对于从未经历"附魅世界"到"祛魅世界"转变的文明（如中国、印度），泰勒的框架可能不适用。Critics note that Taylor's narrative is primarily based on Western Christian civilization's history — for civilizations that never experienced the transition from "enchanted" to "disenchanted" world (such as China, India), Taylor's framework may not apply.

**回应 / Response:** 泰勒在《世俗时代》的后记中承认了这一局限，并鼓励其他学者发展适用于不同文明的世俗化叙事——这正是"多元世俗性"（multiple secularities）研究项目的目标。Taylor acknowledges this limitation in the afterword of *A Secular Age* and encourages other scholars to develop secularization narratives applicable to different civilizations — which is precisely the goal of the "multiple secularities" research program.

**批评二：过于哲学化 / Critique 2: Overly Philosophical**
社会学家批评泰勒的方法过于哲学化和思想史化，忽视了物质因素（经济、技术、城市化）在世俗化中的作用。Sociologists criticize Taylor's approach as overly philosophical and intellectual-historical, neglecting material factors (economics, technology, urbanization) in secularization.

**回应 / Response:** 泰勒不否认物质因素的作用，但他论证物质因素必须通过"社会想象"来起作用——同样的经济变化在不同的文化想象中会产生不同的世俗化路径。Taylor does not deny material factors' role but argues they must work through "social imaginaries" — the same economic changes produce different secularization paths in different cultural imaginaries.

## 跨文化回响 / Cross-cultural Echoes

泰勒的世俗化理论与中国的"宗教性"传统形成了深刻的对照。中国从未经历西方式的"政教分离"——儒家、佛教、道教的共存模式提供了一种"多元宗教性"（multiple religiosities）的文明图景。在中国传统中，"世俗"和"神圣"之间没有清晰的界限——祭祖既是世俗的家庭活动，也具有宗教维度；科举考试既是世俗的政治制度，也体现了"天命"（Mandate of Heaven）的宇宙秩序。这种"内在超越"（immanent transcendence）的模式——牟宗三所使用的概念——与泰勒的"开放内在框架"（open immanent frame）存在有趣的呼应。

Taylor's secularization theory forms a profound contrast with China's "religiosity" tradition. China never experienced Western-style "separation of church and state" — the coexistence model of Confucianism, Buddhism, and Daoism offers a civilizational picture of "multiple religiosities." In Chinese tradition, there is no clear boundary between "secular" and "sacred" — ancestor worship is both a secular family activity and has religious dimensions; the imperial examination system is both secular political institution and embodies the cosmic order of the "Mandate of Heaven." This model of "immanent transcendence" — a concept used by Mou Zongsan — finds interesting echoes in Taylor's "open immanent frame."
