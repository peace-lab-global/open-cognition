# 文森特·梵高 · Vincent van Gogh

## 基本信息 / Basic Information

文森特·威廉·梵高（Vincent Willem van Gogh, 1853年3月30日—1890年7月29日），荷兰后印象派画家，现代表现主义的先驱。出生于荷兰南部津德尔特（Zundert）的一个新教牧师家庭，祖父和父亲均为牧师。梵高早年的人生充满了失败和漂泊——做过画商学徒（在海牙、伦敦和巴黎的古皮尔画廊工作）、学校教师、书店店员和传教士，每一次都以失败告终。1880年，27岁的梵高在经历了比利时博里纳日煤矿传教的深刻精神体验后，决定成为画家——"我要画出能够触动人心的画"。

Vincent Willem van Gogh (March 30, 1853 – July 29, 1890) was a Dutch Post-Impressionist painter and a forerunner of modern Expressionism. Born in Zundert in the southern Netherlands into a Protestant pastor's family, his early life was marked by a series of failures — art dealer apprentice, schoolteacher, bookstore clerk, lay preacher — each ending in disappointment. In 1880, at the age of 27, after a profound spiritual experience among the coal miners of the Borinage in Belgium, he resolved to become a painter: "I want to make drawings that touch people."

梵高的艺术生涯仅有十年（1880—1890），却留下了约2100件作品——860幅油画和超过1300幅素描、水彩和版画。他生前只卖出过一幅画（《红色葡萄园》），却在死后成为全世界最知名、最受爱戴的艺术家之一。他对现代艺术的最根本贡献在于将色彩从"再现"功能中解放出来，使之成为情感的直接表达工具——这一原则直接启发了20世纪的表现主义、野兽派和抽象表现主义。

Van Gogh's artistic career lasted only a decade (1880–1890), yet he produced approximately 2,100 works — 860 oil paintings and over 1,300 drawings, watercolors, and prints. He sold only one painting during his lifetime (*The Red Vineyard*), yet posthumously became one of the most recognized and beloved artists in the world. His most fundamental contribution to modern art was the liberation of color from its representational function, making it a direct vehicle for emotional expression — a principle that directly inspired twentieth-century Expressionism, Fauvism, and Abstract Expressionism.

## 核心命题 / Core Theses

1. **色彩即情感 / Color as Emotion**: 色彩不是自然的摹本，而是情感的直接表达。梵高通过大胆的色彩选择——互补色的并置（黄与紫、蓝与橙、红与绿）——来传达内心的情感强度。"我不是试图精确复制我面前的东西，而是更任意地使用色彩，以便更有力地表达我自己。" / Color is not a copy of nature but a direct expression of emotion. Van Gogh used bold color choices — juxtapositions of complementary colors (yellow and violet, blue and orange, red and green) — to convey inner emotional intensity. "Rather than trying to reproduce exactly what I see, I use color more arbitrarily in order to express myself more forcefully."

2. **厚涂与物质性 / Impasto and Materiality**: 梵高将颜料以厚重、可见的笔触堆积在画布上，使画面不再是光滑的"窗口"（文艺复兴的理想），而是具有触觉般物质性的"表面"。每一笔都是情感的物理痕迹——观者可以"感受到"画家手腕的力量和速度。 / Van Gogh applied paint in thick, visible strokes, transforming the canvas from a smooth Renaissance "window" into a tactile, material "surface." Each brushstroke is a physical trace of emotion — the viewer can "feel" the force and velocity of the painter's wrist.

3. **自然中的精神性 / Spirituality in Nature**: 梵高的绘画深深扎根于自然——麦田、丝柏、向日葵、星空——但他不是再现自然的外表，而是揭示自然内在的精神力量。"我想画出这样的星空——让人看了会觉得自己太渺小。" / Van Gogh's painting is deeply rooted in nature — wheat fields, cypresses, sunflowers, starry skies — but he sought not to reproduce nature's appearance but to reveal its inner spiritual force. "I want to paint a starry night that makes people feel how small they are."

4. **劳动与尊严 / Labor and Dignity**: 梵高始终关注劳动者——农民、矿工、邮递员、纺织工——他以庄严和同情来描绘他们的生活。从早期的《吃土豆的人》到晚期的麦田系列，体现了对"人的尊严"的深切信仰。 / Van Gogh consistently focused on laborers — peasants, miners, postmen, weavers — depicting their lives with solemnity and compassion, from the early *Potato Eaters* to the late wheat field series, embodying a deep faith in human dignity.

5. **日本浮世绘的转化 / Transformation of Ukiyo-e**: 梵高在巴黎期间大量收集和研究日本浮世绘版画，深受其平坦色块、大胆轮廓线和鸟瞰构图的影响。他不是简单地模仿日本艺术，而是将其原则——简化、平面化、色彩纯粹性——转化为自己的视觉语言。 / During his Paris period, Van Gogh collected and studied Japanese ukiyo-e prints extensively, deeply influenced by their flat color areas, bold outlines, and bird's-eye compositions. He did not merely imitate Japanese art but transformed its principles — simplification, flattening, color purity — into his own visual language.

6. **书信作为思想文献 / Letters as Intellectual Documents**: 梵高的书信（尤其是与弟弟提奥的650余封通信）不仅是个人情感的记录，更是一部关于艺术创作的深刻反思——涉及色彩理论、技法探索、艺术使命和精神状态。这些书信是理解梵高艺术思想的最重要第一手文献。 / Van Gogh's letters (especially the 650+ letters to his brother Theo) are not merely personal records but a profound reflection on artistic creation — covering color theory, technical exploration, artistic mission, and mental states. These letters constitute the most important first-person document for understanding Van Gogh's artistic thought.

## 思想脉络 / Intellectual Development

### 早期 / Early Period (1880–1886): 荷兰时期与暗色写实

梵高的艺术起步深受荷兰黄金时代和法国现实主义的影响。早年的人生充满了漂泊和失败——画商学徒、教师、传教士，每一次都以挫折告终。在比利时博里纳日煤矿区的传教经历成为他人生的转折点：面对矿工的极端贫困和苦难，梵高经历了一种深刻的精神觉醒，决定用绘画来表达他对底层人民的同情。1885年的《吃土豆的人》（*The Potato Eaters*）是他的第一幅重要作品——以阴暗的色调和粗犷的笔触描绘荷兰农民围坐在灯下吃土豆的场景。梵高认为这幅画表达了他对劳动者尊严的敬意——"我想让人觉得这些在灯光下吃土豆的人，是用他们伸向盘子的那同一双手挖掘土地的。"这一时期的作品受米勒的农民画和荷兰黄金时代暗色调的深刻影响，但已经展现了梵高特有的粗犷笔触和对劳动者主题的执着关注。

Van Gogh's artistic beginnings were deeply influenced by the Dutch Golden Age and French Realism. His early life was marked by drifting and failure — art dealer apprentice, teacher, preacher — each ending in frustration. His missionary experience among the coal miners of the Borinage in Belgium became a turning point: confronted with extreme poverty and suffering, Van Gogh underwent a profound spiritual awakening and resolved to express his compassion for the underclass through painting. *The Potato Eaters* (1885), his first major work, depicted Dutch peasants gathered around a lamp eating potatoes in dark tones and rough brushwork. The works of this period were deeply influenced by Millet's peasant paintings and the dark palette of the Dutch Golden Age, yet already displayed Van Gogh's characteristic rough brushwork and persistent focus on the dignity of labor.

### 中期 / Middle Period (1886–1889): 巴黎洗礼与阿尔勒的色彩爆发

1886年，梵高来到巴黎与弟弟提奥同住。提奥是古皮尔画廊的经纪人，正在推广印象派画家。梵高由此接触了莫奈、德加、毕沙罗、西斯莱等印象派画家的作品，以及修拉和西涅克的新印象派（点彩法）。更关键的是，他发现了日本浮世绘版画——大量收集和临摹浮世绘作品，深受其平坦色块和大胆构图的影响。巴黎时期的两年间，梵高的调色板从阴暗转向明亮，笔触从粗犷转向碎裂——但他始终保持着与印象派不同的情感强度。1888年2月，梵高离开巴黎前往法国南部小城阿尔勒（Arles），寻求更强烈的阳光和色彩。阿尔勒的15个月是梵高创作生涯的绝对高峰——他在这里完成了约200幅油画和100幅素描，包括最具标志性的作品：《向日葵》系列、《夜间咖啡馆》、《阿尔勒的卧室》、《罗纳河上的星夜》。1888年10月，梵高邀请保罗·高更来阿尔勒共同生活和创作——"南方工作室"的梦想。然而两人的性格和艺术理念剧烈冲突——9周后的高更离去以梵高的精神崩溃和割耳事件告终。

In 1886, Van Gogh moved to Paris to live with his brother Theo, an art dealer promoting Impressionist painters. He encountered works by Monet, Degas, Pissarro, Sisley, as well as the Neo-Impressionism of Seurat and Signac. Crucially, he discovered Japanese ukiyo-e prints, collecting and copying them extensively. During two Paris years, his palette shifted from dark to bright, his brushwork from rough to broken — yet he always maintained an emotional intensity distinct from Impressionism. In February 1888, he left Paris for Arles in southern France, seeking stronger light and color. The 15 months in Arles were the absolute peak of his creative career — approximately 200 oil paintings and 100 drawings, including the *Sunflowers* series, *The Night Café*, *Bedroom in Arles*, and *Starry Night Over the Rhône*. In October 1888, he invited Paul Gauguin to Arles — the dream of a "Studio of the South." But the two clashed in personality and artistic 哲学 — Gauguin's departure after nine weeks ended in Van Gogh's mental breakdown and the ear-cutting incident.

### 晚期 / Late Period (1889–1890): 圣雷米的旋涡与奥维尔的最后七十天

1889年5月，梵高自愿住进圣雷米-德-普罗旺斯的圣保罗精神病院。在疗养院的13个月间，梵高创作了约150幅油画——包括最著名的《星夜》（*The Starry Night*, 1889），以旋涡状的星空和前景的丝柏表达了一种宇宙性的精神狂喜。圣雷米时期的作品充满了旋涡、波浪和颤动的线条——自然似乎被一种内在的能量所驱动。这一时期的书信也变得更加深刻和内省——梵高反复思考艺术、生命和死亡的意义。1890年1月，评论家阿尔贝·奥里耶（Albert Aurier）在《法兰西信使》杂志发表了一篇热情洋溢的评论文章，称梵高为"一个强健的、真正的艺术家"——这是梵高生前获得的极罕见的重要认可。1890年5月，梵高离开圣雷米前往巴黎北郊的奥维尔（Auvers-sur-Oise），在加歇医生的照料下继续创作。在生命的最后70天里，他以惊人的速度完成了约70幅油画——包括《加歇医生肖像》、《奥维尔教堂》和最后的《麦田群鸦》（*Wheatfield with Crows*）。1890年7月27日，梵高在一片麦田中开枪自杀，两天后（7月29日）在弟弟提奥的怀中去世，享年37岁。他的最后话语据说是："悲伤将永远存在"（La tristesse durera toujours）。

In May 1889, Van Gogh voluntarily entered the Saint-Paul-de-Mausole asylum in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence. During his 13 months there, he produced approximately 150 oil paintings — including the most famous *The Starry Night* (1889), expressing a cosmic spiritual ecstasy through swirling stars and foreground cypresses. Works from the Saint-Rémy period are filled with vortices, waves, and trembling lines — nature seems driven by an inner energy. In January 1890, critic Albert Aurier published an enthusiastic review in *Mercure de France*, calling Van Gogh "a robust, true artist" — one of the very few significant recognitions he received during his lifetime. In May 1890, he left Saint-Rémy for Auvers-sur-Oise, continuing to paint under Dr. Gachet's care. In his final 70 days, he completed roughly 70 oil paintings at an astonishing pace — including *Portrait of Dr. Gachet*, *The Church at Auvers*, and *Wheatfield with Crows*. On July 27, 1890, Van Gogh shot himself in a wheat field; two days later he died in his brother Theo's arms at age 37. His last words were reportedly: "La tristesse durera toujours" — "The sadness will last forever."

## 重要概念 / Key Concepts

1. **后印象主义 / [Post-Impressionism](concepts/post-impressionism.md)**: 梵高所代表的超越印象派的艺术运动——将艺术从"视觉记录"转向"情感表达"，为20世纪现代艺术开辟道路。 / The art movement Van Gogh represents — moving art from "visual recording" to "emotional expression," paving the way for 20th-century modern art.

2. **表现性色彩 / [Expressive Color](concepts/expressive-color.md)**: 将色彩从再现功能中解放出来，使之成为情感的直接载体——梵高对现代艺术最核心的贡献。 / Liberating color from its representational function, making it a direct carrier of emotion — Van Gogh's most central contribution to modern art.

3. **厚涂技法 / [Impasto Technique](concepts/impasto-technique.md)**: 将颜料以厚重的笔触堆积在画布上，使画面具有触觉般的物质性——每一笔都是情感的物理痕迹。 / Applying paint in thick, textured strokes that give the surface a tactile materiality — each brushstroke a physical trace of emotion.

4. **苦难与创造力 / [Suffering and Creativity](concepts/suffering-creativity.md)**: 梵高生命中创造力与精神疾病的复杂交织——探讨苦难如何塑造艺术，以及艺术如何成为对抗苦难的方式。 / The complex interplay between creativity and mental illness in Van Gogh's life — exploring how suffering shaped his art and how art became a means of confronting suffering.

5. **星夜 / [Starry Night](concepts/starry-night.md)**: 梵高最著名的作品及其深层含义——旋涡状星空作为宇宙性精神狂喜和个人痛苦的表达。 / Van Gogh's most famous work and its deeper meanings — the swirling starry sky as an expression of cosmic spiritual ecstasy and personal anguish.

6. **互补色并置 / Complementary Color Juxtaposition**: 系统运用色彩理论中的互补色原则——黄与紫、蓝与橙、红与绿——来创造视觉振动和情感张力。 / Systematic use of complementary color principles — yellow/violet, blue/orange, red/green — to create visual vibration and emotional tension.

7. **旋涡线条 / Swirling Lines**: 晚期作品中标志性的旋涡状、波浪状线条——使天空、丝柏和山丘似乎被一种内在能量所驱动。 / The signature swirling, undulating lines of his late works — making skies, cypresses, and hills appear driven by an inner energy.

8. **日本主义 / Japonisme**: 对日本浮世绘的深入研究——平坦色块、大胆轮廓线、鸟瞰构图——被转化为个人视觉语言。 / Deep study of Japanese ukiyo-e — flat color areas, bold outlines, bird's-eye compositions — transformed into a personal visual language.

9. **黄色象征 / Yellow Symbolism**: 将黄色发展为个人标志色——象征温暖、友谊、希望和精神光辉，但也暗示着过度燃烧的危险。 / Developing yellow as a personal signature color — symbolizing warmth, friendship, hope, and spiritual radiance, but also hinting at the danger of burning too intensely.

10. **书信思想 / Epistolary Thought**: 通过书信建立的一套关于艺术的自我反思体系——其文学价值和思想深度可与达·芬奇的笔记本媲美。 / A self-reflective system on art established through letters — rivaling Leonardo's notebooks in literary value and intellectual depth.

## 思想坐标 / Intellectual Coordinates

- **承接 / Inherits**:
  - 米勒（Jean-François Millet）——农民主题的庄严感和对劳动者的同情 / Solemnity of peasant themes and sympathy for laborers
  - 荷兰黄金时代（伦勃朗、哈尔斯）——光影戏剧性和人物心理深度 / Dramatic light and psychological depth
  - 印象派（莫奈、毕沙罗）——户外写生和光色分析 / Plein air painting and light-color analysis
  - 日本浮世绘（歌川广重、葛饰北斋）——平面化、简化和色彩纯粹性 / Flattening, simplification, and color purity
  - 德拉克洛瓦（Delacroix）——色彩理论和浪漫主义情感表达 / Color theory and Romantic emotional expression

- **批判 / Critiques**:
  - 学院派（Academicism）——反对机械化的学院训练和对"正确"技法的教条追求 / Against mechanical academic training and dogmatic pursuit of "correct" technique
  - 印象派的"客观性"——认为印象派过于追求瞬间光色的客观记录，忽视了情感的深度 / Impressionist "objectivity" — believed Impressionism pursued instantaneous optical recording at the expense of emotional depth
  - 表面的美——拒绝为美而美，坚持艺术必须表达真实的情感和存在的深度 / Superficial beauty — refused art-for-beauty's-sake, insisting art must express genuine emotion and existential depth

- **影响 / Influences**:
  - 表现主义（德国桥社、蓝骑士）——直接继承梵高的情感表达原则 / Expressionism (Die Brücke, Der Blaue Reiter) — directly inherited Van Gogh's emotional expression principle
  - 野兽派（马蒂斯）——将梵高的色彩解放推向极致 / Fauvism (Matisse) — pushed Van Gogh's color liberation to its extreme
  - 抽象表现主义（德库宁、波洛克）——继承了笔触的物质性和身体性 / Abstract Expressionism (de Kooning, Pollock) — inherited the materiality and physicality of brushwork
  - 当代艺术治疗——梵高作为创造力与精神健康关联的经典案例 / Contemporary art therapy — Van Gogh as the classic case of the creativity-mental health nexus

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

1. **表现主义传统 / Expressionist Tradition**: 20世纪几乎所有将情感置于再现之上的艺术运动（德国表现主义、抽象表现主义、新表现主义）都追溯至梵高的色彩和笔触解放。 / Nearly every 20th-century art movement that privileged emotion over representation — German Expressionism, Abstract Expressionism, Neo-Expressionism — traces back to Van Gogh's liberation of color and brushstroke.

2. **艺术治疗与心理健康 / Art Therapy and Mental Health**: 梵高的生涯——创造力与精神疾病的复杂交织——是艺术治疗领域的经典案例。他的作品被用于探讨创造力、情感表达和心理康复之间的关系。 / Van Gogh's life — the complex interplay between creativity and mental illness — is a classic case in art therapy. His works are used to explore the relationship between creativity, emotional expression, and psychological recovery.

3. **文化符号与大众传播 / Cultural Symbol and Mass Communication**: 梵高是当代最具辨识度的艺术家之一——《星夜》是全球印刷量最大的艺术图像之一，唐·麦克莱恩的歌曲《Vincent》（*Starry Starry Night*）将梵高的故事传遍了流行文化。梵高已成为"为艺术殉道"的文化原型。 / Van Gogh is one of the most recognizable artists today — *The Starry Night* is among the most reproduced art images globally, and Don McLean's song *Vincent* (*Starry Starry Night*) spread his story throughout popular culture. He has become the cultural archetype of the "artist-martyr."

4. **色彩教育 / Color Education**: 梵高的互补色运用和色彩情感理论对当代艺术教育和设计教育具有持续的启发价值——他的作品是色彩理论教学中最直观的案例。 / Van Gogh's complementary color usage and color-emotion theory continue to inspire contemporary art and design education — his works are the most intuitive teaching cases in color theory.

5. **博物馆与文化遗产 / Museums and Cultural Heritage**: 阿姆斯特丹梵高博物馆、奥特洛库勒-穆勒博物馆、阿尔勒梵高基金会等机构每年吸引数百万访客。2017年沉浸式展览"梵高：星夜"开创了数字艺术体验的新模式。 / The Van Gogh Museum in Amsterdam, the Kröller-Müller Museum, and the Fondation Van Gogh Arles attract millions of visitors annually. The 2017 immersive exhibition *Van Gogh: The Immersive Experience* pioneered a new model of digital art engagement.

6. **数字艺术与AI / Digital Art and AI**: 梵高的风格是神经风格迁移（Neural Style Transfer）最受欢迎的目标之一——将梵高的旋涡笔触应用于任意照片，成为AI艺术应用的标志性案例。 / Van Gogh's style is one of the most popular targets for Neural Style Transfer — applying his swirling brushstrokes to any photograph has become a landmark case in AI art applications.

7. **创意产业 / Creative Industries**: 梵高的作品和形象广泛运用于电影、文学、音乐、时尚和设计领域——从黑泽明的电影《梦》到各类文创产品，梵高是创意产业最丰富的灵感资源之一。 / Van Gogh's works and image are widely used in film, literature, music, fashion, and design — from Kurosawa's film *Dreams* to myriad cultural products, Van Gogh is one of the richest sources of inspiration in the creative industries.

## 常见误读 / Common Misreadings

1. ❌ **"梵高只是疯子画家" / "Van Gogh was just a mad painter"** → ✅ **梵高是一位深刻的思想家和自觉的艺术家。他的书信展示了对色彩理论、构图原则和艺术使命的系统性思考。精神疾病是他生命中的苦难，但不是他艺术的源泉——他最好的作品是在精神清醒时创作的。 / Van Gogh was a profound thinker and a self-aware artist. His letters reveal systematic thinking on color theory, compositional principles, and artistic mission. Mental illness was a suffering in his life, not the source of his art — his best works were created during periods of mental clarity.**

2. ❌ **"梵高生前无人赏识" / "Van Gogh was unrecognized in his lifetime"** → ✅ **梵高生前确实只卖出过一幅画，但他在巴黎前卫艺术圈中并非完全默默无闻——评论家奥里耶曾撰文赞扬他，高更和贝尔纳都认可他的才华。更准确的说法是：他的艺术超前于时代，而非完全被忽视。 / He sold only one painting during his lifetime, but he was not entirely unknown in the Paris avant-garde — critic Aurier praised him in print, and both Gauguin and Bernard recognized his talent. More precisely: his art was ahead of its time, not entirely overlooked.**

3. ❌ **"梵高只是印象派画家" / "Van Gogh was merely an Impressionist"** → ✅ **梵高虽然受到印象派的深刻影响，但他超越了印象派的"视觉记录"——他不追求瞬间光色的客观分析（如莫奈），而是追求情感的强烈表达。他是"后印象派"（Post-Impressionism）的核心代表。 / Though deeply influenced by Impressionism, he transcended its "visual recording" — he did not pursue objective analysis of instantaneous light (like Monet) but pursued intense emotional expression. He is a core representative of "Post-Impressionism."**

4. ❌ **"割耳是因为疯狂" / "The ear-cutting was an act of madness"** → ✅ **割耳事件的具体原因至今有争议——可能是高更离去后的精神崩溃、可能是癫痫发作期间的自伤行为、也可能是某种精神疾病的症状。将其简单归结为"疯了"忽视了梵高精神状态的复杂性。最新的考证（Bernadette Murphy, 2016）揭示了许多此前未知的细节。 / The specific cause of the ear-cutting incident remains disputed — it may have been a breakdown after Gauguin's departure, self-injury during an epileptic seizure, or a symptom of a psychiatric condition. Reducing it to "madness" ignores the complexity of Van Gogh's mental state. Recent 研究 (Bernadette Murphy, 2016) has revealed many previously unknown details.**

## 东西方对话 / East-West Dialogue

梵高与东方思想传统之间存在深刻的、多层次的对话关系，可以从以下几个维度展开：

Van Gogh's relationship with Eastern thought traditions is profound and multi-layered, unfolding along several dimensions:

**日本浮世绘与"日本梦" / Ukiyo-e and the "Japanese Dream"**: 梵高对日本艺术的热爱不仅仅是形式上的借鉴——他将日本视为一种精神理想。在给提奥的信中，他写道："日本人有如此清晰的艺术……他们的画就像空气一样清新。"梵高的"日本梦"与19世纪欧洲的"日本主义"（Japonisme）潮流相呼应，但他的独特之处在于：他不是简单地异国情调化日本，而是将浮世绘的原则——简化、平面化、色彩纯粹性、与自然的和谐——内化为自己的艺术哲学。这种"转化性吸收"与东方思想中"师其意不师其迹"（学习精神而非模仿形式）的传统不谋而合。 / Van Gogh's love of Japanese art went beyond formal borrowing — he saw Japan as a spiritual ideal. In letters to Theo, he wrote: "The Japanese have such clear art… their paintings are as fresh as air." His "Japanese dream" resonated with 19th-century European Japonisme, but his uniqueness lay in not exoticizing Japan but internalizing ukiyo-e principles — simplification, flattening, color purity, harmony with nature — as his own artistic 哲学. This "transformative absorption" parallels the Eastern tradition of "learning the spirit, not copying the form" (师其意不师其迹).

**禅宗精神与自然观 / Zen Spirit and Nature**: 梵高的自然观——在自然中发现神圣、通过绘画与自然合一——与中国山水画的"天人合一"思想和日本禅宗的"一期一会"精神有深刻的共鸣。他在麦田前、星空下所体验到的那种"消融于自然"的感觉，类似于庄子的"坐忘"或禅宗的"无我"。然而，梵高的自然体验始终带有基督教神秘主义的底色——他将自然视为"上帝的手迹"，而非佛教意义上的"空"。 / Van Gogh's view of nature — finding the sacred in nature, becoming one with it through painting — resonates deeply with the Chinese landscape painting idea of "Heaven and humanity as one" (天人合一) and the Japanese Zen spirit of "one time, one meeting" (一期一会). The feeling of "dissolving into nature" he experienced before wheat fields and under starry skies resembles Zhuangzi's "sitting in forgetfulness" (坐忘) or Zen's "no-self." However, Van Gogh's experience of nature always carried a Christian mystical undertone — he saw nature as "God's handwriting," not "emptiness" (空) in the Buddhist sense.

**苦难观的对话 / Dialogue on Suffering**: 梵高的苦难观——苦难是创造力的代价，艺术家必须承受苦难以达到真实——可以与佛教的"苦"（dukkha）和儒家的"穷而后工"进行对话。但与佛教不同的是，梵高并不寻求超越苦难（涅槃），而是将苦难本身转化为艺术。这与儒家"穷而后工"——困境激发创造力——的传统更为接近。 / Van Gogh's view of suffering — that suffering is the price of creativity, that the artist must endure suffering to achieve authenticity — can be placed in dialogue with Buddhism's "dukkha" (suffering) and the Confucian idea of "poverty breeds artistry" (穷而后工). But unlike Buddhism, Van Gogh did not seek to transcend suffering (nirvana); he transformed suffering itself into art. This aligns more closely with the Confucian tradition that adversity stimulates creative power.
