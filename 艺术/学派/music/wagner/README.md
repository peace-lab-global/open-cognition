# 理查德·瓦格纳 · Richard Wagner

## 基本信息 / Basic Information

威廉·理查德·瓦格纳（Wilhelm Richard Wagner，1813年5月22日生于莱比锡——1883年2月13日逝于威尼斯）是19世纪欧洲最具革命性与争议性的艺术家。他身兼作曲家、剧作家、指挥家、理论家与剧场革新者数重身份，独自构建了一个从哲学理论到舞台实践、从和声语法到建筑设计的完整艺术体系。

Wilhelm Richard Wagner (22 May 1813, Leipzig – 13 February 1883, Venice) was one of the most revolutionary and controversial artists in 19th-century Europe. He was simultaneously a composer, dramatist, conductor, theorist, and theatre reformer, single-handedly constructing a comprehensive artistic system spanning philosophy, stage practice, harmonic grammar, and architectural design.

瓦格纳出生于莱比锡一个中产家庭，自幼接触戏剧与文学。他在莱比锡大学短暂学习音乐后，辗转于维尔茨堡、马格德堡、柯尼斯堡和里加等地歌剧院担任指挥，积累了丰富的舞台实践经验。1839年因债务逃离里加，经风暴中的北海航程抵达巴黎，在此接触了梅耶贝尔的法国大歌剧，并开始形成自己对歌剧革新的理论。1842年《黎恩济》在德累斯顿大获成功后，他被任命为萨克森宫廷乐长。

Born into a middle-class family in Leipzig, Wagner was exposed to theatre and literature from childhood. After briefly studying music at Leipzig University, he served as conductor at opera houses in Würzburg, Magdeburg, Königsberg, and Riga, accumulating extensive practical stage experience. Fleeing Riga in 1839 due to debt, he endured a stormy North Sea voyage to Paris, where he encountered Meyerbeer's grand opera and began formulating his own operatic reforms. After the success of *Rienzi* in Dresden in 1842, he was appointed Kapellmeister to the Saxon court.

瓦格纳的遗产是双重的：一方面，他的"总体艺术"理念深刻影响了此后一个半世纪的艺术实践，从象征主义剧场到包豪斯设计，从电影叙事到沉浸式装置艺术；另一方面，他在《音乐中的犹太性》（1850）中表达的种族偏见与反犹主义在纳粹德国被极端利用，至今仍是瓦格纳研究中不可回避的伦理问题。

Wagner's legacy is dual: on one hand, his Gesamtkunstwerk concept profoundly influenced artistic practice for the following century and a half — from Symbolist theatre to Bauhaus design, from cinematic narrative to immersive installation art. On the other hand, the racial prejudice and antisemitism expressed in *Das Judentum in der Musik* (1850) was exploited to extremes in Nazi Germany and remains an inescapable ethical question in Wagner studies to this day.

## 核心命题 / Core Theses

1. **总体艺术（Gesamtkunstwerk）**：未来的艺术应统合音乐、诗、舞蹈、布景与建筑，消解各门类的分工，恢复古希腊悲剧式的整体经验。艺术不应服务于个人虚荣或商业利益，而应成为民族精神的集体表达。

   **Gesamtkunstwerk (Total Artwork)**: The art of the future should unify music, poetry, dance, scenery, and architecture, dissolving the division of labor among art forms and restoring the holistic experience of ancient Greek tragedy. Art should serve neither personal vanity nor commercial interests, but become the collective expression of the Volk's spirit.

2. **乐剧（Musikdrama）**：音乐应服从戏剧目的，以"无止境旋律"（unendliche Melodie）取代传统的分曲结构（编号咏叹调与宣叙调的交替），让和声与旋律连续推进戏剧张力，消除观众与舞台之间的审美距离。

   **Musikdrama (Music Drama)**: Music should serve dramatic purposes, replacing the traditional number-opera structure (alternating arias and recitatives) with "endless melody" (*unendliche Melodie*), allowing harmony and melody to continuously propel dramatic tension and eliminate the aesthetic distance between audience and stage.

3. **主导动机（Leitmotif）**：以可变重复的短小音乐主题标识人物、物体与抽象观念，构成庞大戏剧的音乐记忆网络。主导动机不是简单的标签，而是随戏剧情境不断变形的有机体，在听众的意识中构建出过去、现在与未来的时间叠层。

   **Leitmotif**: Short, variably recurring musical themes designate characters, objects, and abstract ideas, forming a vast musical memory-network within the drama. Leitmotifs are not mere labels but organic entities that continuously transform with dramatic context, constructing temporal layers of past, present, and future in the listener's consciousness.

4. **半音化革命**：以《特里斯坦与伊索尔德》中的半音和声悬置调性中心，释放了"无限渴望"（unendliche Sehnsucht）的音响化身。"特里斯坦和弦"不是一个孤立的技术发明，而是整个调性体系危机的起点，为勋伯格、韦伯恩等人的无调性音乐开辟了道路。

   **Chromatic Revolution**: The chromatic harmony in *Tristan und Isolde* suspends the tonal center, releasing an acoustic embodiment of "infinite longing" (*unendliche Sehnsucht*). The "Tristan chord" is not an isolated technical invention but the starting point of an entire tonal system's crisis, paving the way for the atonal music of Schoenberg and Webern.

5. **神话即普遍性**：神话不是过时的故事，而是人类最深层经验的编码。通过北欧与日耳曼神话的再创造，瓦格纳试图在工业资本主义时代重建一种超越历史的普遍叙事，让现代观众直面权力、爱与救赎等永恒主题。

   **Myth as Universality**: Myth is not an outdated story but an encoding of humanity's deepest experiences. Through the recreation of Norse and Germanic mythology, Wagner attempted to rebuild a transhistorical universal narrative in the age of industrial capitalism, confronting modern audiences with eternal themes of power, love, and redemption.

6. **救赎通过艺术**：艺术——尤其是音乐戏剧——具有宗教般的救赎力量。从《漂泊的荷兰人》到《帕西法尔》，"通过爱（或同情）获得救赎"是贯穿瓦格纳全部创作的终极信念，将艺术提升至准宗教的地位。

   **Redemption through Art**: Art — especially music drama — possesses religion-like redemptive power. From *The Flying Dutchman* to *Parsifal*, "redemption through love (or compassion)" is the ultimate conviction running through Wagner's entire oeuvre, elevating art to a quasi-religious status.

## 思想脉络 / Intellectual Development

### 早期：德累斯顿与理论形成（1813–1849）/ Early Period: Dresden and Theoretical Formation

瓦格纳早年深受韦伯的德国浪漫歌剧与贝多芬的交响曲影响。他在各地歌剧院的指挥生涯使他深刻认识到当时歌剧实践的弊病：意大利歌剧沦为声乐炫技的载体，法国大歌剧追求表面华丽而忽视戏剧内在逻辑。1840年代初定居德累斯顿后，他接触到费尔巴哈的人本主义哲学与青年德意志派的政治激进主义，开始将艺术革新与社会变革联系起来。

Wagner's youth was deeply influenced by Weber's German Romantic opera and Beethoven's symphonies. His conducting career at various opera houses gave him acute awareness of operatic practice's shortcomings: Italian opera had degenerated into a vehicle for vocal display, while French grand opera pursued surface brilliance at the expense of dramatic logic. After settling in Dresden in the early 1840s, he encountered Feuerbach's humanist philosophy and the political radicalism of Young Germany, beginning to link artistic reform with social revolution.

这一时期的创作——《漂泊的荷兰人》（1843）、《汤豪舍》（1845）、《罗恩格林》（1850）——仍属浪漫歌剧范畴，但已显示出明确的革新倾向：主导动机的系统化使用、和声语汇的扩展、对"救赎通过爱"主题的执着。1849年德累斯顿五月起义中，瓦格纳与无政府主义者巴库宁并肩站在街垒上，起义失败后被迫流亡。在流亡前夕，他发表了《艺术与革命》和《未来的艺术作品》，系统阐述了总体艺术的理念与"为人民而艺术"的纲领。

The works of this period — *The Flying Dutchman* (1843), *Tannhäuser* (1845), *Lohengrin* (1850) — still belong to the Romantic opera tradition but already display clear reformist tendencies: systematized use of leitmotifs, expanded harmonic vocabulary, and an obsession with the theme of "redemption through love." During the Dresden May Uprising of 1849, Wagner stood on the barricades alongside the anarchist Bakunin. After the uprising's failure, he was forced into exile. On the eve of exile, he published *Art and Revolution* and *The Artwork of the Future*, systematically articulating the Gesamtkunstwerk concept and his program of "art for the people."

### 中期：流亡、理论与特里斯坦（1849–1870）/ Middle Period: Exile, Theory, and Tristan

流亡苏黎世的十二年是瓦格纳思想最密集、创作最丰产的时期。1851年发表的《歌剧与戏剧》是他最重要的理论著作，系统论述了乐剧的原则：诗（文本）与音乐的关系应是"男性与女性"的互补，音乐是"海洋"，诗是"大地"；传统歌剧的错误在于"手段（音乐）成了目的，而目的（戏剧）成了手段"。他详细阐述了主导动机的戏剧功能——不是装饰性的重复，而是"回忆的动机"（Erinnerungsmotiv）和"预感的动机"（Ahnungsmotiv），在时间的纵深中编织戏剧意义的网络。

The twelve years of Zurich exile were Wagner's most intellectually dense and creatively prolific period. *Opera and Drama* (1851), his most important theoretical work, systematically expounded the principles of music drama: the relationship between poetry (text) and music should be a "masculine-feminine" complementarity; music is the "ocean," poetry the "earth"; traditional opera's error was that "the means (music) became the end, and the end (drama) became the means." He elaborated on the dramatic function of leitmotifs — not decorative repetition, but "motives of recollection" (*Erinnerungsmotiv*) and "motives of premonition" (*Ahnungsmotiv*), weaving the web of dramatic meaning across the depth of time.

1854年接触叔本华哲学是瓦格纳思想的转折点。叔本华将音乐视为"意志本身的直接写照"——这一命题为瓦格纳提供了将音乐置于所有艺术之上的形而上学根据，修正了他早期将音乐视为"服务于戏剧的手段"的立场。与此同时，与赞助人之妻玛蒂尔德·维森东克的热烈而无望的爱情催生了《特里斯坦与伊索尔德》——一部将不可能的渴望、延迟的解决与最终的死亡升华融为一体的音乐戏剧。1864年巴伐利亚国王路德维希二世成为其赞助人，1865年《特里斯坦》在慕尼黑首演，彻底改变了西方音乐的和声语法。

Encountering Schopenhauer's philosophy in 1854 was a turning point in Wagner's thought. Schopenhauer's elevation of music as the "direct copy of the will itself" provided Wagner with a metaphysical justification for placing music above all other arts, modifying his earlier position of treating music as "a means serving drama." Meanwhile, his passionate and hopeless love for Mathilde Wesendonck, his patron's wife, gave birth to *Tristan und Isolde* — a music drama fusing impossible longing, deferred resolution, and final transfiguration through death. In 1864, King Ludwig II of Bavaria became his patron, and in 1865 *Tristan* premiered in Munich, permanently altering the harmonic grammar of Western music.

### 晚期：拜罗伊特与帕西法尔（1870–1883）/ Late Period: Bayreuth and Parsifal

1870年代，瓦格纳实现了毕生的梦想：在巴伐利亚小镇拜罗伊特建造一座完全按照他艺术理念设计的专属剧场——拜罗伊特节日剧院（Bayreuther Festspielhaus）。这座剧场的革命性在于：下沉式乐池使乐队对观众不可见，消除了"第四堵墙"；扇形排列的座位确保每位观众拥有相同视角，体现了民主化的观演理念；专门为此剧场创作的《尼伯龙根的指环》于1876年首演，四联剧连续演出四个夜晚、共约十六小时，是西方艺术史上规模最宏大的单一作品。

In the 1870s, Wagner realized his lifelong dream: constructing a dedicated theatre in the Bavarian town of Bayreuth, designed entirely according to his artistic vision — the Bayreuth Festival Theatre (*Bayreuther Festspielhaus*). The theatre's revolutionary features included: a sunken orchestra pit rendering the musicians invisible to the audience, eliminating the "fourth wall"; fan-shaped seating ensuring every spectator had an equal view, embodying a democratized conception of spectatorship; and the *Ring of the Nibelung* cycle, composed specifically for this venue, premiered in 1876 — four operas performed over four consecutive evenings totaling approximately sixteen hours, the most monumental single work in Western art history.

晚年的瓦格纳深受叔本华的悲观主义与佛教思想影响。他的最后一部作品《帕西法尔》（1882）被他称为"舞台祭献节日剧"（Bühnenweihfestspiel），将基督教的圣杯传说与佛教的慈悲观念融合，探索通过"纯粹的同情"（Mitleid）获得救赎的可能。1883年2月13日，瓦格纳在威尼斯因心脏病发作去世。

In his later years, Wagner was deeply influenced by Schopenhauer's pessimism and Buddhist thought. His final work, *Parsifal* (1882), which he called a "stage-consecrating festival play" (*Bühnenweihfestspiel*), fused the Christian Holy Grail legend with Buddhist concepts of compassion, exploring the possibility of redemption through "pure compassion" (*Mitleid*). On 13 February 1883, Wagner died of a heart attack in Venice.

## 重要概念 / Key Concepts

- **[总体艺术 Gesamtkunstwerk](concepts/gesamtkunstwerk.md)**：统合所有艺术门类以服务于单一戏剧理念的整体艺术形式。瓦格纳认为各门艺术的独立发展导致了艺术的碎片化，唯有在"总体艺术"中，音乐、诗歌、舞蹈、建筑、绘画与雕塑才能恢复其原始的有机统一。→ 详见 [gesamtkunstwerk.md](concepts/gesamtkunstwerk.md)

- **[主导动机 Leitmotif](concepts/leitmotif.md)**：随戏剧情境反复变形的短小音乐主题，是庞大音乐叙事的索引与记忆装置。瓦格纳将其发展为一种复杂的"音乐记忆术"，使长达数小时的乐剧具有内在的有机统一性。→ 详见 [leitmotif.md](concepts/leitmotif.md)

- **[乐剧 Musikdrama](concepts/music-drama.md)**：音乐与戏剧不可分的连续整体，取代传统分曲歌剧。瓦格纳刻意避免使用"歌剧"一词，以"乐剧"强调戏剧与音乐的同等地位。→ 详见 [music-drama.md](concepts/music-drama.md)

- **[指环四联剧 Ring Cycle](concepts/ring-cycle.md)**：《尼伯龙根的指环》作为总体艺术的终极实践，以北欧神话为框架展开对权力、资本、爱与命运的四夜叙事。→ 详见 [ring-cycle.md](concepts/ring-cycle.md)

- **[半音化和声 Chromatic Harmony](concepts/chromatic-harmony.md)**：以《特里斯坦》为标志的半音和声体系，悬置调性中心，释放"无限渴望"的音响维度，是西方音乐从调性走向无调性的关键转折。→ 详见 [chromatic-harmony.md](concepts/chromatic-harmony.md)

- **无止境旋律（Unendliche Melodie / Endless Melody）**：以不间断的和声推进取代分曲的收束，制造永恒渴望的流动感。这一概念既是对传统歌剧中"终止式-新段落"交替模式的否定，也是对叔本华式"意志的永恒涌动"的音乐实现。

- **特里斯坦和弦（Tristan-Akkord / Tristan Chord）**：一个半音化、功能悬置的和弦（F-B-D#-G#），标志调性体系的危机与开放。它在《特里斯坦》前奏曲开篇出现，其和声功能至今仍有争议，成为音乐分析史上被讨论最多的单一和弦。

- **救赎动机（Erlösungsmotiv）**：贯穿瓦格纳全部作品的核心戏剧主题——通过爱、同情或死亡获得救赎。从《漂泊的荷兰人》的森塔到《帕西法尔》的帕西法尔，"救赎者需要被救赎"的悖论构成了瓦格纳戏剧的深层结构。

## 思想坐标 / Intellectual Coordinates

- **承接 / Inherits**:
  - [贝多芬 Beethoven](../../beethoven/) — 第九交响曲中人声与器乐的融合是总体艺术的先声；贝多芬是瓦格纳心目中的"音乐基督"
  - [韦伯 Weber] — 德国浪漫歌剧传统，尤其是《魔弹射手》中对自然与超自然的融合
  - 德国浪漫主义文学（霍夫曼、诺瓦利斯）— 对无限性的渴望与艺术的宗教化
  - 古希腊悲剧 — 瓦格纳视埃斯库罗斯为总体艺术的原型

- **批判 / Critiques**:
  - 意大利歌剧的分曲传统（"编号歌剧"） — 视其为音乐对戏剧的篡夺
  - 法国大歌剧（梅耶贝尔） — 批判其追求表面效果而牺牲内在戏剧逻辑
  - 资本主义文化工业 — 将商业化艺术视为"虚荣"与"金钱"的产物

- **影响 / Influences**:
  - 后浪漫主义音乐（布鲁克纳、马勒、R.施特劳斯）
  - 现代主义音乐（勋伯格、贝尔格、韦伯恩 — 从半音化到无调性）
  - 电影配乐语法（约翰·威廉姆斯、霍华德·肖的主导动机体系）
  - 沉浸式与跨媒介艺术（装置艺术、歌剧电影、沉浸式剧场）
  - 节日制度（拜罗伊特模式成为全球专项艺术节的范本）
  - 哲学与美学（尼采、阿多诺、本雅明、巴迪欧）

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

1. **电影配乐 / Film Scoring**：约翰·威廉姆斯在《星球大战》系列、霍华德·肖在《指环王》三部曲中的主导动机体系直接继承瓦格纳，通过音乐主题的变形与交织构建跨影片的音乐叙事网络。John Williams' *Star Wars* saga and Howard Shore's *Lord of the Rings* trilogy directly inherit Wagner's leitmotif system, constructing cross-film musical narrative networks through thematic transformation and interweaving.

2. **调性理论与分析 / Tonal Theory and Analysis**：申克分析（Schenkerian Analysis）与新里曼理论（Neo-Riemannian Theory）均以《特里斯坦》为调性解体或调性扩展的关键案例。"特里斯坦和弦"至今仍是音乐理论中最具争议的和声现象之一。Schenkerian Analysis and Neo-Riemannian Theory both treat *Tristan* as a crucial case of tonal dissolution or tonal expansion.

3. **沉浸式与跨媒介艺术 / Immersive and Cross-media Art**：总体艺术理念在当代装置艺术、沉浸式剧场（如罗伯特·威尔逊的剧场作品）、歌剧电影（如Patrice Chéreau的《指环》百年制作）以及虚拟现实体验中持续回响。The Gesamtkunstwerk concept continues to resonate in contemporary installation art, immersive theatre (e.g., Robert Wilson's stage works), opera films, and virtual reality experiences.

4. **节日制度 / Festival Model**：拜罗伊特音乐节（Bayreuther Festspiele）的模式——专属场地、专属作品、观众 pilgrimage 式的参与——成为全球专项艺术节（如萨尔茨堡、格林德伯恩、阿维尼翁）的制度原型。The Bayreuth Festival model — dedicated venue, dedicated repertoire, audience pilgrimage — became the institutional prototype for specialized arts festivals worldwide.

5. **叙事学 / Narratology**：瓦格纳的主导动机体系与叙事学中的"主题再现"（thematic recurrence）和"跨媒介叙事"（transmedia storytelling）具有深层同构性，为叙事理论提供了音乐维度的案例。Wagner's leitmotif system shares deep structural isomorphism with "thematic recurrence" and "transmedia storytelling" in narratology.

6. **政治美学 / Political Aesthetics**：瓦格纳案例（艺术的政治化与政治的艺术化）持续激发关于艺术与意识形态关系的讨论——从阿多诺的"文化工业"批判到本雅明的"艺术政治化"论述，再到巴迪欧的《瓦格纳五讲》。The Wagner case (politicization of art and aestheticization of politics) continues to stimulate discussions on the relationship between art and ideology.

7. **认知科学与音乐心理学 / Cognitive Science and Music Psychology**：主导动机的认知加工——听众如何在长时间跨度的音乐中识别、记忆与关联主题——成为音乐认知研究的经典案例。The cognitive processing of leitmotifs — how listeners recognize, remember, and associate themes across long temporal spans — has become a classic case in music cognition research.

## 常见误读 / Common Misreadings

1. ❌ **"瓦格纳只是写宏大音乐的作曲家。"** → ✅ 瓦格纳亲自撰写全部剧本、全部理论著作，并设计了拜罗伊特剧场的建筑。他是完整的艺术—思想—剧场体系的设计者，其文字著述（十六卷全集）的分量与音乐创作同等重要。

   ❌ *"Wagner was just a composer of grand music."* → ✅ Wagner wrote all his own libretti and theoretical treatises, and designed the Bayreuth theatre's architecture. He was the architect of a complete art-thought-theatre system; his written works (16-volume complete edition) are as substantial as his musical output.

2. ❌ **"《指环》是纯粹的神话故事，无关政治。"** → ✅ 《指环》中尼伯龙根的指环象征资本/权力的异化力量，阿尔贝里希对黄金的诅咒与齐格弗里德的"自由英雄"形象都承载着瓦格纳对资本主义与权力的批判——这有明确的费尔巴哈与青年黑格尔派思想背景。

   ❌ *"The Ring is a purely mythological story, unrelated to politics."* → ✅ The Ring in the cycle symbolizes the alienating force of capital/power; Alberich's curse on the gold and Siegfried's "free hero" image both carry Wagner's critique of capitalism and power, grounded in Feuerbachian and Young Hegelian thought.

3. ❌ **"瓦格纳的反犹主义与其音乐无关。"** → ✅ 二者深度纠缠：瓦格纳在《音乐中的犹太性》中将反犹论直接写入美学理论，认为"犹太性"是真正艺术创造的障碍。这一思想暗面在纳粹德国被极端利用，其后果不容回避或美化。

   ❌ *"Wagner's antisemitism is unrelated to his music."* → ✅ The two are deeply entangled: in *Das Judentum in der Musik*, Wagner wrote antisemitism directly into aesthetic theory, claiming "Jewishness" was an obstacle to genuine artistic creation. This dark dimension was exploited to extremes in Nazi Germany, and its consequences cannot be evaded or aestheticized.

4. ❌ **"半音化和声只是纯粹的技术发明。"** → ✅ 半音化和声是叔本华式"无限渴望"（unendliche Sehnsucht）的音响化身。瓦格纳自己在书信中多次将和声创新与哲学体验联系——技术革新与形而上学追求是同构的。

   ❌ *"Chromatic harmony is a purely technical invention."* → ✅ Chromatic harmony is the acoustic embodiment of Schopenhauerian "infinite longing" (*unendliche Sehnsucht*). Wagner himself repeatedly linked harmonic innovation to philosophical experience in his correspondence — technical revolution and metaphysical pursuit are structurally isomorphic.

## 东西方对话 / East-West Dialogue

瓦格纳的"总体艺术"理念与中国传统戏曲的"唱念做打"综合表演体系存在有趣的平行关系。京剧将歌唱、念白、身段、武打与脸谱化妆统合于一个舞台空间，虽然其美学原则（程式化、虚拟性）与瓦格纳（自然主义、沉浸式）截然不同，但二者都追求一种超越单一艺术门类的综合戏剧经验。

Wagner's Gesamtkunstwerk concept has interesting parallels with the "singing, recitation, acting, and martial arts" (*chang-nian-zuo-da*) comprehensive performance system of Chinese traditional opera. Beijing Opera unifies singing, spoken dialogue, stylized movement, martial arts, and facial makeup within a single stage space. Although its aesthetic principles (stylization, virtuality) differ fundamentally from Wagner's (naturalism, immersion), both pursue a comprehensive theatrical experience that transcends single art forms.

瓦格纳晚年对佛教思想的吸收——尤其是《帕西法尔》中"通过慈悲获得救赎"的主题——使他的作品与东方精神传统产生了深层关联。叔本华的意志哲学本身深受印度吠檀多与佛教的影响，瓦格纳通过叔本华间接接触了这些东方思想，并将其转化为音乐戏剧的语言。

Wagner's late engagement with Buddhist thought — especially the theme of "redemption through compassion" in *Parsifal* — creates a deep connection between his work and Eastern spiritual traditions. Schopenhauer's philosophy of the will was itself deeply influenced by Indian Vedanta and Buddhism; Wagner encountered these Eastern ideas indirectly through Schopenhauer and transformed them into the language of music drama.

在当代语境中，瓦格纳的总体艺术理念与日本的"媒体混合"（media mix）文化策略、韩国K-pop的视听一体化制作模式，以及中国当代艺术家（如蔡国强的烟火装置）的跨媒介实践之间，存在着值得探讨的对话空间。

In contemporary contexts, there is a dialogue worth exploring between Wagner's Gesamtkunstwerk concept and Japan's "media mix" cultural strategy, K-pop's audio-visual integrated production model, and the cross-media practices of contemporary Chinese artists (such as Cai Guo-Qiang's fireworks installations).
