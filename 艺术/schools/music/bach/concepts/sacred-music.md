# 宗教音乐 · Sacred Music

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

宗教音乐是巴赫创作的核心领域，也是其音乐哲学的精神根基。巴赫是虔诚的路德宗信徒，他在一本圣经的空白页上写道："在虔诚的音乐中，上帝永远以其恩典临在"（"Bey einer andächtigen Musique ist allezeit Gott mit seiner Gnaden Gegenwart"）。对他而言，音乐不是独立于信仰的艺术活动，而是信仰的直接表达与实践——"一切音乐的最终目的都是上帝的荣耀与灵魂的更新，若非如此，便不是真正的音乐，不过是魔鬼般的喧嚣与嘈杂。"

Sacred music was the central domain of Bach's creative output and the spiritual foundation of his musical philosophy. Bach was a devout Lutheran who wrote on a blank page of his Bible: "Where there is devotional music, God is always present with His grace." For him, music was not an artistic activity separate from faith but a direct expression and practice of faith — "The ultimate purpose of all music is the glory of God and the refreshment of the soul; where this is not the case, there is no true music, but only devilish noise and clamor."

巴赫的宗教音乐创作涵盖了路德宗礼拜所需的全部体裁：宗教康塔塔（cantata）约200首现存，为每个礼拜日和宗教节日而作；受难曲（passion）——《约翰受难曲》和《马太受难曲》——以音乐叙事再现基督受难；弥撒曲——《b小调弥撒》——跨越教派界限的宏大总结；以及大量管风琴众赞歌前奏曲（chorale prelude），以器乐形式冥想路德赞美诗。巴赫将路德宗神学的核心关切——因信称义、恩典、受难与救赎——转化为可听的音乐体验，使宗教音乐成为信仰的直接载体而非仅仅的礼仪装饰。

Bach's sacred output encompassed every genre required for Lutheran worship: approximately 200 surviving sacred cantatas, composed for every Sunday and feast day; passions — the *St John Passion* and *St Matthew Passion* — musical narratives of Christ's suffering; masses — the *Mass in B minor* — a grand cross-confessional summation; and numerous organ chorale preludes, instrumental meditations on Lutheran hymns. Bach translated the core concerns of Lutheran theology — justification by faith, grace, suffering, and redemption — into audible musical experience, making sacred music a direct vehicle of faith rather than mere liturgical decoration.

## 理论内涵 / Theoretical Content

巴赫宗教音乐的核心机制是**众赞歌**（chorale）——路德宗的会众赞美诗旋律。众赞歌在巴赫的宗教作品中具有三重功能：（1）**旋律素材**：众赞歌旋律作为主题材料，被编织进康塔塔的合唱、咏叹调和宣叙调中；（2）**结构框架**：众赞歌的旋律和歌词为作品提供了神学锚点和结构支柱；（3）**会众参与**：众赞歌是可被会众一同歌唱的旋律，使宗教音乐不仅是专业演出的艺术，更是全体信众的共同礼拜行为。

The central mechanism of Bach's sacred music is the **chorale** — the Lutheran congregational hymn melody. The chorale serves three functions in Bach's sacred works: (1) **Melodic material**: chorale melodies serve as thematic material woven into the choruses, arias, and recitatives of cantatas; (2) **Structural framework**: the chorale's melody and text provide theological anchoring and structural pillars; (3) **Congregational participation**: the chorale is a melody that the congregation can sing together, making sacred music not only a professionally performed art but a communal act of worship.

巴赫的宗教康塔塔遵循一个精心设计的结构范式：通常以**众赞歌幻想曲**（chorale fantasia）开场——合唱团在管弦乐队伴奏下演唱众赞歌旋律；中间由若干**宣叙调**（recitatives）和**咏叹调**（arias）交替，以独唱形式冥想当日的经文和众赞歌歌词；以简朴的四声部**众赞歌和声**（chorale harmonization）结束，象征着会众对信息的集体回应。这一结构不仅是音乐形式的安排，更是神学意义的展开过程——从集体宣告，到个人沉思，再到共同体确认。

Bach's sacred cantatas follow a carefully designed structural paradigm: typically opening with a **chorale fantasia** — the choir singing the chorale melody with orchestral accompaniment; the middle sections alternating **recitatives** and **arias** in solo voices, meditating on the day's scripture and chorale text; and concluding with a simple four-part **chorale harmonization**, symbolizing the congregation's collective response to the message. This structure is not merely a musical formal arrangement but an unfolding of theological meaning — from collective proclamation, through individual contemplation, to communal affirmation.

《马太受难曲》是巴赫宗教音乐的最高成就。它以双合唱团和双管弦乐队的宏大编制，将圣经马太福音中的受难叙事与自由诗歌（由诗人皮坎德 Picander 撰写）和传统众赞歌三层文本交织在一起。当基督在十字架上呼喊"我的神，我的神，为什么离弃我"时，音乐以刺耳的不协和音和断裂的节奏表达了这一神学危机；而在"Erbarme dich"（怜悯我）咏叹调中，独奏小提琴与人声的缠绕对话则表达了最深切的人类哀痛与对神圣恩典的渴望。整部作品是一部以声音构建的神学论文——探讨受难、牺牲、罪恶与救赎的意义。

The *St Matthew Passion* is Bach's supreme achievement in sacred music. With its monumental double-choir and double-orchestra forces, it interweaves three textual layers: the passion narrative from the Gospel of Matthew, free poetry (by the poet Picander), and traditional chorales. When Christ cries from the cross "My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?", the music expresses this theological crisis with searing dissonances and fractured rhythms; in the "Erbarme dich" (Have mercy) aria, the intertwining dialogue of solo violin and voice expresses the deepest human grief and longing for divine grace. The entire work is a theological treatise constructed in sound — an exploration of suffering, sacrifice, sin, and redemption.

## 思想史脉络 / Intellectual History

巴赫的宗教音乐直接根植于马丁·路德的音乐神学。路德将音乐视为"上帝赐予的礼物"（*donum Dei*），认为音乐与神学同等重要——"音乐是上帝赐予的，不是人类发明的"。路德开创了众赞歌传统，使会众歌唱成为新教礼拜的核心组成部分，这一遗产构成了巴赫宗教音乐创作的基本语境。

Bach's sacred music is directly rooted in Martin Luther's theology of music. Luther regarded music as a "gift of God" (*donum Dei*), considering it as important as theology itself — "Music is a gift of God, not a human invention." Luther inaugurated the chorale tradition, making congregational singing a central component of Protestant worship, and this heritage forms the essential context for Bach's sacred music output.

在巴赫之前，海因里希·许茨（Heinrich Schütz）和约翰·赫尔曼·沙因（Johann Hermann Schein）等作曲家已经建立了德语宗教音乐的传统。巴赫将这一传统与意大利歌剧的戏剧性表达和法国音乐的精致形式相结合，创造了前所未有的宗教音乐综合。在巴赫之后，宗教音乐在启蒙运动中逐渐世俗化，但门德尔松1829年对《马太受难曲》的复兴演出重新激发了对巴赫宗教音乐的兴趣，并开启了延续至今的"巴赫复兴"运动。

Before Bach, composers such as Heinrich Schütz and Johann Hermann Schein had already established the tradition of German sacred music. Bach combined this tradition with the dramatic expressiveness of Italian opera and the refined forms of French music to create an unprecedented sacred music synthesis. After Bach, sacred music was gradually secularized during the Enlightenment, but Mendelssohn's 1829 revival of the *St Matthew Passion* reignited interest in Bach's sacred music and initiated the "Bach revival" movement that continues to this day.

## 关键文本 / Key Passages

**《马太受难曲》，开场合唱"Kommt, ihr Töchter"（来吧，你们的女儿们）**: 双合唱团在12/8拍的摇曳节奏中相互呼唤，如同两支哀悼队伍缓缓走向各各他。一个合唱团唱出悲痛的邀请，另一个以回问应答——"Wen?"（谁？）"Wie?"（如何？）——童声合唱团在高处唱出众赞歌旋律，如同天使从天上俯瞰。

**St Matthew Passion, Opening Chorus "Kommt, ihr Töchter"**: Two choirs call to each other in swaying 12/8 rhythm, like two mourning processions slowly approaching Golgotha. One choir sings the sorrowful invitation, the other responds with questioning — "Whom?" "How?" — a boys' choir sings the chorale melody from above, like angels looking down from heaven.

**《b小调弥撒》，"Crucifixus"**: 半音下行的固定低音反复十三次（十三象征不完美与背叛），每一次重复都加深了十字架受难的沉重感。当唱到"et resurrexit"（复活）时，音乐突然从阴暗的e小调跃入灿烂的D大调——复活的音乐瞬间。

**Mass in B minor, "Crucifixus"**: A chromatically descending ground bass repeats thirteen times (thirteen symbolizing imperfection and betrayal), each repetition deepening the gravity of the crucifixion. When the text reaches "et resurrexit" (and he rose again), the music suddenly leaps from dark E minor to brilliant D major — the musical moment of resurrection.

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

巴赫的宗教音乐在当代已超越其原始的礼拜语境，成为全球性的文化遗产。日本——一个基督教徒极少的国家——拥有世界上最活跃的巴赫合唱团运动之一。韩国的巴赫学会是亚洲最活跃的音乐学术组织之一。在中国的古典音乐教育中，巴赫康塔塔已成为声乐和合唱教学的重要素材。巴赫宗教音乐的这种跨文化接受现象，证明了其表达的人类普遍情感——对超越的渴望、对苦难的回应、对秩序与美的追求——超越了特定宗教信仰的界限。

Bach's sacred music has transcended its original liturgical context in the contemporary world, becoming a global cultural heritage. Japan — a country with very few Christians — has one of the world's most active Bach choir movements. Korea's Bach Society is among the most active musicological organizations in Asia. In China's classical music education, Bach cantatas have become important material for vocal and choral pedagogy. This cross-cultural reception of Bach's sacred music demonstrates that the universal human emotions it expresses — longing for transcendence, response to suffering, pursuit of order and beauty — transcend the boundaries of specific religious beliefs.

## 批评与回应 / Critiques and Responses

**批评**: 一些批评者认为巴赫的宗教音乐过于复杂和华丽，偏离了路德宗强调简洁和可理解性的初衷。另一些批评者质疑非信徒是否能够真正理解和演绎巴赫的宗教音乐。

**Critique**: Some critics have argued that Bach's sacred music is too complex and ornate, departing from the Lutheran emphasis on simplicity and comprehensibility. Others question whether non-believers can truly understand and perform Bach's sacred music.

**回应**: 巴赫的宗教音乐在复杂性与可理解性之间达成了独特的平衡——康塔塔始终以简朴的众赞歌和声收束，确保会众的参与感。至于非信徒的演绎问题，巴赫音乐的力量恰恰在于其情感表达的深度足以跨越信仰界限——铃木雅明（Masaaki Suzuki）领导的日本巴赫合唱团以其卓越的宗教音乐诠释证明了这一点。

**Response**: Bach's sacred music achieves a unique balance between complexity and comprehensibility — cantatas always conclude with simple chorale harmonizations, ensuring congregational participation. Regarding performance by non-believers, the power of Bach's music lies precisely in the fact that its emotional depth transcends faith boundaries — as demonstrated by Masaaki Suzuki's Japanese Bach Collegium, whose outstanding sacred music interpretations have proven this point.

## 跨文化回响 / Cross-cultural Echoes

巴赫宗教音乐的跨文化接受与佛教**梵呗**（chanting）传统有深刻的对话可能。两者都将声音视为精神修行的媒介，都将重复和仪式化的音乐行为视为通向超越的途径。巴赫的众赞歌前奏曲——以器乐形式沉思一个赞美诗旋律——与佛教法事中反复吟诵经文以达到冥想状态的修行方式具有结构上的相似性。此外，苏菲派的**齐克尔**（dhikr，反复念诵神名）与巴赫音乐中的动机反复也有精神上的共鸣。

The cross-cultural reception of Bach's sacred music has profound dialogue potential with the Buddhist **chanting** tradition. Both treat sound as a medium for spiritual practice and regard repetitive, ritualized musical behavior as a path to transcendence. Bach's chorale preludes — instrumental meditation on a hymn melody — bear structural similarity to the Buddhist practice of repeatedly chanting sutras to achieve meditative states. Additionally, the Sufi **dhikr** (repeated invocation of divine names) resonates spiritually with the motivic repetitions in Bach's music.
