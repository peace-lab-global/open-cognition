# 晚期弦乐四重奏 · Late String Quartets

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

"晚期弦乐四重奏"指贝多芬在1824年至1826年间（生命最后三年）创作的六首弦乐四重奏：Op.127（降E大调）、Op.130（降B大调）、Op.131（升c小调）、Op.132（a小调）、Op.133（大赋格/Grosse Fuge）和Op.135（F大调）。这些作品是贝多芬完全失聪后的创作，呈现出与中期英雄风格截然不同的品质——内省、碎片化、超越调性、融合古老对位法与大胆和声实验。它们被广泛视为西方音乐中最深邃的精神表达之一，也是室内乐的终极巅峰。

The "Late String Quartets" refers to six string quartets Beethoven composed between 1824 and 1826 (the last three years of his life): Op.127 (E-flat major), Op.130 (B-flat major), Op.131 (C-sharp minor), Op.132 (A minor), Op.133 (Grosse Fuge), and Op.135 (F major). These works, created in total deafness, present qualities radically different from the middle-period heroic style—introspective, fragmented, transcending tonality, fusing archaic counterpoint with bold harmonic experimentation. They are widely regarded as among the most profound spiritual expressions in Western music, and the ultimate summit of chamber music.

这些作品在首演时遭到了广泛的困惑和批评——同时代的听众和演奏者都感到无法理解其"超前"的音乐语言。小提琴家路易斯·施波尔称它们"不可理解、毫无条理"。然而，二十世纪以来，晚期四重奏被视为贝多芬最深刻的成就——斯特拉文斯基称《大赋格》为"永远当代的音乐"，阿多诺从中发展出了影响深远的"晚期风格"理论。

These works were met with widespread confusion and criticism at their premieres—contemporary audiences and performers found their "avant-garde" musical language incomprehensible. Violinist Louis Spohr called them "incomprehensible and incoherent." Yet since the twentieth century, the late quartets have been recognized as Beethoven's most profound achievement—Stravinsky called the Grosse Fuge "music that will be forever contemporary," and Adorno developed his influential "late style" theory from them.

## 理论内涵 / Theoretical Content

**一、形式的碎片化与重组 / Fragmentation and Reorganization of Form**：晚期四重奏最显著的特征是对传统四乐章结构的瓦解。Op.131包含七个不间断演奏的乐章（包括仅二十余小节的极短段落），Op.130包含六个乐章，Op.132的五个乐章以非传统方式排列。这种"碎片化"不是混乱，而是一种更高层次的组织方式——各段落之间的对比、呼应和内在联系构成了一种超越古典连续性的新统一性。

The most striking feature of the late quartets is the dissolution of the traditional four-movement structure. Op.131 contains seven continuously played movements (including passages of merely twenty-some measures), Op.130 has six movements, and Op.132's five movements are arranged in non-traditional order. This "fragmentation" is not chaos but a higher-order organizational principle—the contrasts, echoes, and internal connections between sections constitute a new unity transcending Classical continuity.

**二、古老与未来的融合 / Fusion of the Archaic and the Futuristic**：晚期四重奏中，贝多芬同时向两个方向伸展——一方面回归古老的复调形式（赋格、卡农、利底亚调式），另一方面大胆探索接近无调性的和声领域。Op.132第三乐章使用了古老的利底亚调式（Lydian mode），创造出一种"非尘世"的音响品质；而《大赋格》（Op.133）中极端的半音化和不协和音则预示了二十世纪的表现主义音乐。这种"古老-未来"的双重取向赋予晚期四重奏以独特的时间感——它们似乎同时属于过去和未来，而不属于任何确定的当下。

In the late quartets, Beethoven simultaneously reaches in two directions—returning to archaic polyphonic forms (fugue, canon, Lydian mode) while boldly exploring harmonic territories approaching atonality. The third movement of Op.132 uses the ancient Lydian mode, creating an "otherworldly" sonic quality; while the extreme chromaticism and dissonance of the Grosse Fuge (Op.133) foreshadowed twentieth-century Expressionist music. This dual orientation of "archaic-futuristic" gives the late quartets a unique temporal quality—they seem to belong simultaneously to past and future, but to no determinate present.

**三、"内在性"的表达 / Expression of "Inwardness"**：晚期四重奏的音乐语言呈现出一种前所未有的"内在性"——音乐不再是向外部世界"说话"，而是在与自己"对话"。Op.131的第一乐章以一段深沉的赋格开始，仿佛是作曲家内心世界的入口；Op.130的第五乐章"Cavatina"（短歌）被贝多芬自己称为"让他流泪"的乐章——这段简短的慢板以极致的简洁表达了最深沉的哀伤。这种"内在性"使晚期四重奏成为了一种"私密"的艺术——它们不是为了音乐厅的观众而写，而是为了灵魂本身。

The musical language of the late quartets displays an unprecedented "inwardness"—the music no longer "speaks" to the external world but "converses" with itself. The first movement of Op.131 opens with a profound fugue, as if an entrance to the composer's inner world; the fifth movement "Cavatina" of Op.130 was described by Beethoven himself as the movement that "made him weep"—this brief slow movement expresses the deepest sorrow with ultimate simplicity. This "inwardness" makes the late quartets a "private" art—they were written not for concert hall audiences but for the soul itself.

## 思想史脉络 / Intellectual History

晚期四重奏的诞生标志着贝多芬从"英雄风格"向"晚期风格"的根本转变。斯科特·伯恩汉姆指出，如果说英雄风格是关于"行动"的——意志在外部世界中的斗争与凯旋——那么晚期风格是关于"存在"的——对生命、死亡和永恒的冥想。

The birth of the late quartets marks Beethoven's fundamental shift from the "Heroic Style" to the "Late Style." Scott Burnham notes that if the Heroic Style is about "action"—the will's struggle and triumph in the external world—then the Late Style is about "being"—meditation on life, death, and eternity.

西奥多·阿多诺在1937年的论文《贝多芬的晚期风格》中提出了影响深远的"晚期风格"理论：伟大艺术家的晚期作品不呈现和解和圆满，而是呈现裂痕和未完成性——"晚期作品是灾难性的"。阿多诺将这种"不和解"视为艺术的最高真理——艺术不应该粉饰现实的矛盾，而应该将矛盾本身呈现为形式。这一理论后来被爱德华·萨义德发展为更广泛的"晚期风格"概念，应用于文学、电影和文化批评。

Theodor Adorno's 1937 essay "Late Style in Beethoven" proposed the influential "Late Style" theory: great artists' late works do not present reconciliation and fulfillment but rather rifts and incompleteness—"late works are catastrophes." Adorno regarded this "non-reconciliation" as art's highest truth—art should not gloss over reality's contradictions but present the contradictions themselves as form. This theory was later developed by Edward Said into a broader "late style" concept applied to literature, film, and cultural criticism.

## 关键文本 / Key Passages

> "Op.132第三乐章标题：'一个康复者对神的感恩之歌，以利底亚调式写成'（Heiliger Dankgesang eines Genesenen an die Gottheit, in der lydischen Tonart）"
> "Op.132 third movement title: 'A Convalescent's Holy Song of Thanksgiving to the Godhead, in the Lydian mode'"

这一标题是贝多芬留下的最直接的宗教情感表达之一。利底亚调式的使用——一种古老的中世纪教会调式——赋予音乐以超越时间的品质，仿佛连接了古代的虔敬与现代的痛苦。

This title is one of Beethoven's most direct expressions of religious sentiment. The use of the Lydian mode—an ancient medieval church mode—gives the music a quality transcending time, as if connecting ancient devotion with modern suffering.

> Op.135 终乐章题词："Muss es sein? — Es muss sein!"（"必须如此吗？——必须如此！"）
> Op.135 finale inscription: "Muss es sein? — Es muss sein!" ("Must it be? — It must be!")

这段简短的对话——"疑问"与"肯定"——被米兰·昆德拉用作小说《生命中不能承受之轻》的标题来源之一。它浓缩了晚期四重奏的核心精神：面对命运的终极追问，以坚定的肯定作为回答。

This brief dialogue—"question" and "affirmation"—was used by Milan Kundera as one of the sources for the title of *The Unbearable Lightness of Being*. It distills the core spirit of the late quartets: facing fate's ultimate question with a resolute affirmation as answer.

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

晚期四重奏在当代文化中的影响远超音乐领域。在**文学**中，T.S.艾略特的《四个四重奏》在结构和精神上直接受到贝多芬晚期四重奏的启发；米兰·昆德拉的多部小说引用了晚期四重奏的主题。在**电影**中，晚期四重奏被用于《晚期四重奏》(A Late Quartet, 2012)等影片，探讨衰老、疾病和创造力的主题。在**哲学**中，阿多诺和萨义德的"晚期风格"理论已成为文化批评的重要概念工具。在**心理学**中，Op.132的"感恩之歌"被用于正念冥想和创伤后恢复治疗。

The late quartets' influence in contemporary culture extends far beyond music. In **literature**, T.S. Eliot's *Four Quartets* was directly inspired by Beethoven's late quartets in structure and spirit; Milan Kundera's novels reference late quartet themes. In **film**, late quartets feature in films such as *A Late Quartet* (2012), exploring themes of aging, illness, and creativity. In **philosophy**, Adorno and Said's "late style" theory has become an important conceptual tool in cultural criticism. In **psychology**, the Op.132 "Thanksgiving Song" has been applied in mindfulness meditation and post-trauma recovery therapy.

## 批评与回应 / Critiques and Responses

**批评**：一些演奏家和听众认为晚期四重奏"过于内省"、"难以接近"，在音乐会上不如中期作品受欢迎。约瑟夫·克尔曼承认："它们需要一种不同于听其他音乐的聆听方式。"
**Critique**: Some performers and listeners find the late quartets "too introspective" and "difficult to approach," less popular in concert than middle-period works. Joseph Kerman acknowledged: "They require a different kind of listening than other music."

**回应**：晚期四重奏的"难接近"恰恰是其价值所在——它们拒绝提供即时满足，要求听众投入深度聆听。这种"反娱乐"的品质使它们成为对抗当代文化浅薄化的重要资源。
**Response**: The late quartets' "inaccessibility" is precisely their value—they refuse to offer instant gratification, demanding deep listening from the audience. This "anti-entertainment" quality makes them an important resource against the superficialization of contemporary culture.

## 跨文化回响 / Cross-cultural Echoes

晚期四重奏的"内在性"与中国文人音乐（如古琴音乐）中的"内省"传统形成了深层对话。古琴美学强调"琴者，心也"——音乐是内心的直接表达，不需要外在的华丽。贝多芬晚期四重奏中的极简段落（如Op.130的"Cavatina"）与古琴音乐中的"留白"技法——以沉默和简约表达不可言说之物——形成了跨文化的共鸣。日本作曲家武满彻在其弦乐四重奏中将贝多芬的晚期精神与日本传统音乐的"间"（ma, 间隔/沉默）美学相融合。

The "inwardness" of the late quartets creates a deep dialogue with the "introspective" tradition of Chinese literati music (such as guqin music). Guqin aesthetics emphasize "琴者，心也" (the qin is the heart)—music as direct expression of the inner self, without external ornamentation. The minimalist passages in Beethoven's late quartets (such as the "Cavatina" of Op.130) create cross-cultural resonance with the guqin music technique of "留白" (leaving blank)—expressing the unspeakable through silence and simplicity. Japanese composer Toru Takemitsu fused Beethoven's late spirit with the Japanese traditional music aesthetic of "間" (ma, interval/silence) in his string quartets.
