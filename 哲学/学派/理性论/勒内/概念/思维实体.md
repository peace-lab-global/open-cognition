# 思维实体 · Res Cogitans

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

"思维实体"（Res Cogitans）是笛卡尔形而上学的核心概念，指以思维（cogitatio）为本质属性的实体。笛卡尔在《第一哲学沉思集》第二沉思中，通过"我思故我在"发现了"我"的存在之后，进一步追问："这个'我'究竟是什么？"他的回答是：我是一个思维的东西（res cogitans），我的全部本质或本性就在于思维。

"Res Cogitans" (thinking substance/thing) is the core concept of Cartesian metaphysics, referring to a substance whose essential attribute is thought (cogitatio). After discovering the existence of "I" through the cogito in the Second Meditation, Descartes further asks: "What exactly is this 'I'?" His answer: I am a thinking thing (res cogitans), whose entire essence or nature consists in thinking.

## "我是什么？"的追问 / The Question "What Am I?"

在第二沉思中，笛卡尔发现"我存在"之后，立即追问"我"的本质。他逐一排除了以下可能的回答：

In the Second Meditation, after establishing "I exist," Descartes immediately inquires into the nature of "I." He systematically eliminates possible answers:

1. **我不是一个身体**：因为在方法论怀疑中，身体的存在仍然可疑（它可能是梦境或恶魔欺骗的产物）
2. **我不是一个"灵魂"（传统意义上的）**：经院哲学将灵魂视为身体的"形式"，与身体不可分离；笛卡尔拒绝了这种亚里士多德式的灵魂观
3. **我是一个思维的东西**：我唯一能确定归属于"我"的属性是思维——怀疑、理解、肯定、否定、意愿、不愿、想象、感觉

1. **I am not a body**: Under methodological doubt, the body's existence remains questionable (it could be a dream or demonic deception)
2. **I am not a "soul" (in the traditional sense)**: Scholastic philosophy viewed the soul as the "form" of the body, inseparable from it; Descartes rejects this Aristotelian view
3. **I am a thinking thing**: The only attribute I can certainly ascribe to "me" is thinking — doubting, understanding, affirming, denying, willing, not willing, imagining, sensing

## "思维"的广义定义 / The Broad Definition of "Thought"

笛卡尔对"思维"（cogitatio）的定义极为宽泛，远远超出了我们今天通常所说的"思维"。他明确指出："思维"包括一切意识活动——

Descartes's definition of "thought" (cogitatio) is extraordinarily broad, far exceeding what we today typically call "thinking." He explicitly states that "thought" includes all conscious activity:

- **怀疑**（dubitatio）：对事物真假的悬搁判断
- **理解**（intellectio）：对观念的把握
- **肯定**（affirmatio）：对命题的同意
- **否定**（negatio）：对命题的拒绝
- **意愿**（volitio）：意志活动
- **不愿**（nolitio）：意志的拒绝
- **想象**（imaginatio）：对不在场事物的形象化
- **感觉**（sensus）：感官经验（作为意识内容而非物理过程）

- **Doubting** (dubitatio): Suspension of judgment on truth/falsity
- **Understanding** (intellectio): Grasping of ideas
- **Affirming** (affirmatio): Assenting to propositions
- **Denying** (negatio): Rejecting propositions
- **Willing** (volitio): Acts of will
- **Not willing** (nolitio): Refusal of will
- **Imagining** (imaginatio): Forming images of absent things
- **Sensing** (sensus): Sensory experience (as conscious content, not physical process)

因此，笛卡尔的"思维实体"实际上是**意识实体**——一切有意识的活动都是这个实体的状态或样态。

Thus, Descartes's "thinking substance" is effectively a **consciousness substance** — all conscious activities are states or modes of this substance.

## 天赋观念 / Innate Ideas

作为思维实体，心灵中先天包含某些观念——"天赋观念"（ideae innatae）。这些观念不是从感官经验中获得的，也不是由想象力虚构的，而是心灵本身的固有结构。笛卡尔认为的天赋观念包括：

As a thinking substance, the mind innately contains certain ideas — "innate ideas" (ideae innatae). These are neither acquired from sensory experience nor fabricated by imagination, but are inherent structures of the mind itself. Descartes's innate ideas include:

- **上帝观念**：一个完满的、无限的、全知全能的存在者的观念
- **数学公理**：如"二加三等于五"、"全体大于部分"
- **逻辑原则**：如矛盾律、同一律
- **实体观念**：对"实体"本身的一般概念
- **思维的基本范畴**：如因果性、完满性

- **The idea of God**: An idea of a perfect, infinite, omniscient, omnipotent being
- **Mathematical axioms**: Such as "two plus three equals five," "the whole is greater than the part"
- **Logical principles**: Such as the law of contradiction, the law of identity
- **The idea of substance**: The general concept of substance itself
- **Fundamental categories of thought**: Such as causality, perfection

## 对"思维实体"的批评 / Criticisms of "Thinking Substance"

### 休谟的束论

休谟否认存在一个恒常不变的思维实体。他在内省时只发现一系列流动的知觉，无法发现一个独立于这些知觉的"自我"。所谓"自我"不过是这些知觉的集合或"束"（bundle）。

Hume denies the existence of a constant thinking substance. In introspection, he finds only a flowing series of perceptions, never a "self" independent of them. The so-called "self" is merely a collection or "bundle" of these perceptions.

### 康德的先验统觉

康德区分了"经验性的自我"（内省中发现的流动的意识状态）和"先验统觉"（使一切经验成为可能的先验条件）。他同意笛卡尔的"我思"必须伴随着我的一切表象，但否认这个"我思"能够告诉我们任何关于"我"作为一个实体的知识。"我思"只是一个逻辑主体，不是一个实在的思维实体。

Kant distinguished the "empirical self" (flowing conscious states found in introspection) from "transcendental apperception" (the a priori condition making all experience possible). He agreed that the Cartesian "I think" must accompany all my representations, but denied that this "I think" can tell us anything about "me" as a substance. The "I think" is merely a logical subject, not a real thinking substance.

### 萨特的"自我的超越性"

萨特在《自我的超越性》中主张，意识在前反思的层面是"无我的"——不存在一个内在的"自我"作为意识的中心。意识总是"对某物的意识"（意向性），它本身是空的、透明的。"自我"不是意识的内容，而是意识的对象——它是通过反思被建构出来的，而非先天存在于意识中。

Sartre argued in *The Transcendence of the Ego* that consciousness at the pre-reflective level is "egoless" — there is no inner "self" at consciousness's center. Consciousness is always "consciousness of something" (intentionality); it is itself empty, transparent. The "ego" is not consciousness's content but its object — constructed through reflection, not innately present in consciousness.

## 东西方对话 / East-West Dialogue

在东方传统中，佛教的"无我"（anātman）学说是对"思维实体"最直接的反驳。佛教认为，执着于一个恒常的"我"恰恰是痛苦的根源（十二因缘中的"无明"）。瑜伽行派的"八识"体系虽然对意识进行了精细的分析，但其目的恰恰是揭示"我"的虚幻性，而非确立一个实在的思维主体。

In Eastern traditions, the Buddhist anātman (no-self) doctrine is the most direct refutation of "thinking substance." Buddhism holds that clinging to a permanent "I" is precisely the root of suffering (ignorance in the twelve-fold chain of dependent origination). The Yogācāra eight-consciousness system analyzes consciousness in detail precisely to reveal the "self"'s illusory nature, not to establish a real thinking subject.
