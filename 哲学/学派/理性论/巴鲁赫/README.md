# 巴鲁赫·斯宾诺莎 · Baruch Spinoza

## 基本信息 / Biographical Note

巴鲁赫·斯宾诺莎（1632年11月24日–1677年2月21日），荷兰犹太裔哲学家，近代早期理性主义三大代表之一（与笛卡尔、莱布尼茨并称），也是西方哲学史上最激进、最具争议性的思想家之一。他以严格的几何学方法建构了一个包罗万象的形而上学-伦理学体系，以"上帝即自然"（Deus sive Natura）的一元论取代了笛卡尔的身心二元论，并以对情感、自由和幸福的深刻分析影响了从黑格尔到德勒兹的整个哲学传统。

Baruch Spinoza (24 November 1632 – 21 February 1677) was a Dutch-Jewish philosopher and one of the three great early-modern rationalists alongside Descartes and Leibniz. He was also one of the most radical and controversial thinkers in Western 哲学. Using a rigorous geometrical method, he constructed an all-encompassing metaphysical-ethical system, replacing Cartesian mind-body dualism with the monism of "God or Nature" (Deus sive Natura), and profoundly influencing the entire philosophical tradition from Hegel to Deleuze through his deep analysis of affects, freedom, and happiness.

斯宾诺莎出生于阿姆斯特丹的一个塞法迪犹太移民家庭——他的家族在宗教迫害下从葡萄牙逃往荷兰。他在阿姆斯特丹的犹太社区中接受了传统的犹太教育，学习了《塔纳赫》（希伯来圣经）、《塔木德》和中世纪犹太哲学（尤其是迈蒙尼德的著作）。然而，他早年就开始接触笛卡尔哲学和近代科学思想，这些新的思想资源使他逐渐发展出与正统犹太教义格格不入的哲学观点。

Spinoza was born in Amsterdam to a Sephardic Jewish family — his ancestors fled Portugal for the Netherlands under religious persecution. He received traditional Jewish education in Amsterdam's Jewish community, studying the Tanakh (Hebrew Bible), Talmud, and medieval Jewish 哲学 (especially Maimonides). However, he encountered Descartes's 哲学 and modern scientific thought early on, and these new intellectual resources gradually led him to develop philosophical views incompatible with orthodox Judaism.

1656年7月27日，阿姆斯特丹的犹太社区对斯宾诺莎颁布了最严厉的革除教门令（cherem）——"以天使和圣徒的名义……将他逐出教会、诅咒他、开除他、驱除他"。这份革除教门令的措辞之严厉在犹太历史上几乎是无与伦比的，而且从未被撤销。被逐出社区后，斯宾诺莎将名字从"巴鲁赫"（Baruch，希伯来语，意为"受祝福的"）改为"本尼迪克特"（Benedictus，拉丁语，同义）。

On 27 July 1656, Amsterdam's Jewish community issued the harshest possible decree of excommunication (cherem) against Spinoza — "by decree of the angels and by command of the saints … we excommunicate, curse, ban, and expel him." The severity of this decree was virtually unparalleled in Jewish history and was never revoked. After his expulsion, Spinoza Latinized his name from "Baruch" (Hebrew, "blessed") to "Benedictus" (Latin, same meaning).

被逐出社区后，斯宾诺莎以磨镜片为生——这一职业后来因他死于肺部疾病（可能是硅肺病，由磨玻璃粉尘引起）而具有悲剧性的象征意义。他先后居住在莱茵斯堡、伏尔堡和海牙，过着极为简朴的生活。他拒绝了海德堡大学的教职邀请（1673年），理由是这会限制他的思想和写作自由。他还拒绝了法国国王路易十四的年金，因为这意味着他必须将一部著作献给国王。

After his expulsion, Spinoza earned his living grinding lenses — a profession that later acquired tragic symbolism when he died of lung disease (likely silicosis from glass dust). He lived successively in Rijnsburg, Voorburg, and The Hague in extreme simplicity. He declined a professorship at Heidelberg (1673) because it would restrict his freedom of thought and writing. He also declined a pension from King Louis XIV of France because it would require dedicating a work to the king.

斯宾诺莎在海牙度过了生命的最后岁月，住在斯皮克（Spui）运河旁的一座房子里（现在是斯宾诺莎博物馆）。1677年2月21日，他因肺部疾病去世，年仅44岁。他的遗物极为简朴——几件衣服、一些书籍、一架显微镜、几封信件。他的朋友和学生在同年出版了他的遗著《伦理学》（*Ethica*），这部以几何学方式论证的哲学巨著成为整个西方哲学史上最重要的文本之一。

Spinoza spent his final years in The Hague, in a house by the Spui canal (now the Spinoza Museum). He died of lung disease on 21 February 1677, aged only 44. His possessions were meager — a few clothes, some books, a microscope, some letters. His friends and students published his posthumous *Ethics* (*Ethica*) the same year, a philosophical masterpiece demonstrated in geometrical order that became one of the most important texts in Western 哲学.

## 核心命题 / Core Theses

### 1. 实体一元论 · Substance Monism

只有一个实体（Substance）存在，这个实体就是"上帝"或"自然"（Deus sive Natura）。万物都是这一唯一实体的"样式"（modes）——即它在特定条件下的特殊状态或表现。笛卡尔的"思维实体"和"广延实体"实际上只是同一实体的两种"属性"（attributes）。这一学说彻底取消了笛卡尔的身心二元论——身心问题在斯宾诺莎的体系中是一个伪问题，因为身和心不过是同一实在的两种表达方式。

Only one Substance exists, and this substance is "God" or "Nature" (Deus sive Natura). All things are "modes" of this unique substance — its particular states or expressions under specific conditions. Descartes's "thinking substance" and "extended substance" are merely two "attributes" of the same substance. This doctrine completely dissolves Cartesian mind-body dualism — the mind-body problem is a pseudo-problem in Spinoza's system, since body and mind are merely two ways of expressing the same reality.

### 2. 身心平行论 · Mind-Body Parallelism

思想（Thought）和广延（Extension）是同一实体的两种属性，它们之间不存在因果互动。"观念的秩序和联系与事物的秩序和联系是同一的"（Ordo et connexio idearum idem est ac ordo et connexio rerum）——心理事件和物理事件之间没有因果关系，而是同一因果序列的两种表达。这不是二元论的平行论（像莱布尼茨那样），而是一元论的平行论——身和心不是两个实体，而是同一实体的两个方面。

Thought and Extension are two attributes of the same substance; there is no causal interaction between them. "The order and connection of ideas is the same as the order and connection of things" (Ordo et connexio idearum idem est ac ordo et connexio rerum) — mental and physical events are not causally related but are two expressions of the same causal sequence. This is not dualistic parallelism (like Leibniz's) but monistic parallelism — body and mind are not two substances but two aspects of one substance.

### 3. 情感理论 · Affect Theory

情感（affects / affectus）不是心灵的"缺陷"或"疾病"，而是身体行动能力（potentia agendi）的增减变化及其在心灵中的对应观念。斯宾诺莎区分了三种基本情感：欲望（cupiditas，保持存在的努力）、快乐（laetitia，行动能力的增加）和悲伤（tristitia，行动能力的减少）。理解情感的成因——从"不充分观念"（被动情感）走向"充分观念"（主动情感）——就是获得自由和幸福的途径。

Affects (affectus) are not the mind's "defects" or "diseases" but increases and decreases in the body's power of acting (potentia agendi) and their corresponding ideas in the mind. Spinoza distinguishes three basic affects: desire (cupiditas, the striving to persevere), joy (laetitia, increase in power), and sadness (tristitia, decrease in power). Understanding the causes of affects — moving from "inadequate ideas" (passive affects) to "adequate ideas" (active affects) — is the path to freedom and happiness.

### 4. 理智对上帝的爱 · Intellectual Love of God (Amor Dei Intellectualis)

最高的幸福和德性在于通过理性认识上帝/自然的必然性。当我们以"永恒的相"（sub specie aeternitatis）来理解事物——即理解事物作为上帝/自然之必然表达的本质——我们就获得了"理智对上帝的爱"。这种爱不是宗教性的虔诚，而是对宇宙必然性的理性欣赏和接受。它是"第三类知识"（scientia intuitiva，直观知识）的最高果实。

Supreme blessedness and virtue consist in rational knowledge of God/Nature's necessity. When we understand things "under the aspect of eternity" (sub specie aeternitatis) — grasping their essence as necessary expressions of God/Nature — we attain the "intellectual love of God." This love is not religious piety but rational appreciation and acceptance of cosmic necessity. It is the highest fruit of the "third kind of knowledge" (scientia intuitiva, intuitive knowledge).

### 5. 自由即必然性的认识 · Freedom as Understanding of Necessity

斯宾诺莎是严格的决定论者——宇宙中没有偶然，一切事物都必然地由上帝/自然的本质推导出来。但在这个决定论的框架中，自由不是"意志的自由"（liberum arbitrium），而是"自由必然性"（libera necessitas）——自由就是按照自身本性的必然性行动。人通过理性理解必然性，从被外部原因被动决定的"奴隶"状态，转化为由内在理性主动引导的"自由人"状态。

Spinoza is a strict determinist — nothing in the universe is contingent; everything necessarily follows from God/Nature's essence. But within this deterministic framework, freedom is not "free will" (liberum arbitrium) but "free necessity" (libera necessitas) — freedom is acting according to the necessity of one's own nature. Through rational understanding of necessity, humans transform from the "slave" state of being passively determined by external causes to the "free person" state actively guided by internal reason.

### 6. 政教分离与思想自由 · Separation of Religion and State & Freedom of Thought

斯宾诺莎在《神学政治论》（1670）中系统论证了政教分离和思想自由的原则。他认为，国家的目的是保障公民的自由和安全，而非拯救灵魂；宗教权威不应干预政治和哲学探究；圣经应当作为历史文献来解读，而非作为科学和哲学的权威。这些观点在17世纪是极为激进的，直接影响了后来的启蒙运动和自由主义政治哲学。

In the *Theological-Political Treatise* (1670), Spinoza systematically argued for separation of 宗教 and state and freedom of thought. He held that the state's purpose is to secure citizens' freedom and safety, not to save souls; religious authority should not interfere with politics and philosophical inquiry; the Bible should be read as a historical document, not as an authority in science and 哲学. These views were extremely radical in the 17th century, directly influencing the later Enlightenment and liberal political 哲学.

## 思想脉络 / Intellectual Development

### 早期：从犹太传统到理性主义 · Early Period: From Jewish Tradition to Rationalism (1632–1660)

斯宾诺莎的思想发展深深扎根于两个传统：犹太哲学传统（迈蒙尼德、克雷斯卡斯、伊本·以斯拉）和近代理性主义传统（笛卡尔、培根、伽利略）。他在犹太社区中接受了扎实的希伯来文和圣经训练，同时通过弗朗西斯·范·登·恩德（Franciscus van den Enden）等导师接触了笛卡尔哲学和新科学。这种独特的双重背景使他能够以全新的视角审视传统的上帝、自然和人的关系。

Spinoza's intellectual development was deeply rooted in two traditions: Jewish 哲学 (Maimonides, Crescas, Ibn Ezra) and modern rationalism (Descartes, Bacon, Galileo). He received solid training in Hebrew and biblical studies in the Jewish community while encountering Cartesian 哲学 and the new science through teachers like Franciscus van den Enden. This unique dual background enabled him to examine the traditional relationships between God, nature, and humanity from a completely fresh perspective.

### 中期：体系的建构 · Middle Period: Building the System (1660–1670)

被逐出社区后，斯宾诺莎全身心投入哲学研究。他的早期著作《简论上帝、人及其幸福》（约1660年，生前未出版）已经包含了一元论和决定论的核心思想。《知性改进论》（约1662年，未完成）探讨了获得真理和最高幸福的方法论。1663年出版的《笛卡尔哲学原理》以几何学方式阐述了笛卡尔的哲学，同时隐含了对笛卡尔二元论的批判。1670年，《神学政治论》匿名出版——这是斯宾诺莎生前唯一出版的重要著作，引起了巨大的争议，被犹太教和基督教两方谴责。

After his expulsion, Spinoza devoted himself entirely to 哲学. His early work *Short Treatise on God, Man, and His Well-Being* (c. 1660, unpublished in his lifetime) already contained the core ideas of monism and determinism. The *Treatise on the Improvement of the Understanding* (c. 1662, unfinished) explored methodology for attaining truth and supreme happiness. *Principles of Cartesian Philosophy* (1663) presented Descartes's 哲学 in geometrical order while implicitly critiquing Cartesian dualism. The *Theological-Political Treatise* (1670), published anonymously, was Spinoza's only major work published in his lifetime — it provoked enormous controversy, condemned by both Jewish and Christian authorities.

### 晚期：《伦理学》的完成 · Late Period: Completing the Ethics (1670–1677)

斯宾诺莎的最后七年几乎全部用于完成他的巨著《以几何学方式证明的伦理学》（*Ethica, ordine geometrico demonstrata*）。这部著作分为五个部分，以定义、公理、命题、证明和附释的严格几何学形式，建构了从形而上学到伦理学的完整哲学体系。由于《神学政治论》引起的争议，斯宾诺莎不敢在生前出版《伦理学》——他将手稿锁在一个上锁的抽屉里，嘱咐友人在他死后出版。1677年，《伦理学》在他的遗著集（*Opera Posthuma*）中首次面世，从此改变了西方哲学的面貌。

Spinoza's final seven years were devoted almost entirely to completing his magnum opus, *Ethics, Demonstrated in Geometrical Order* (*Ethica, ordine geometrico demonstrata*). Divided into five parts, in the strict geometrical format of definitions, axioms, propositions, demonstrations, and scholia, it constructs a complete philosophical system from metaphysics to ethics. Due to the controversy generated by the *Theological-Political Treatise*, Spinoza dared not publish the *Ethics* in his lifetime — he locked the manuscript in a desk drawer, instructing friends to publish it after his death. In 1677, the *Ethics* appeared for the first time in his *Opera Posthuma*, forever changing the face of Western 哲学.

## 思想坐标 / Position in Intellectual History

斯宾诺莎在世时声名狼藉，被称为"无神论者"和"犹太教叛徒"。他的著作被荷兰当局禁止，被天主教会列入《禁书目录》，被犹太社区视为异端。但在18世纪末和19世纪初，他经历了一场惊人的"平反"运动。

Spinoza was notorious in his lifetime, labeled an "atheist" and "Jewish apostate." His works were banned by Dutch authorities, placed on the Catholic Index of Prohibited Books, and considered heretical by the Jewish community. But in the late 18th and early 19th centuries, he underwent a remarkable "rehabilitation."

德国诗人和哲学家赫尔德（Herder）称他为"高贵的斯宾诺莎"（der edle Spinoza）。歌德宣称斯宾诺莎的哲学是他的"精神家园"。黑格尔甚至断言："要成为哲学家，首先必须成为斯宾诺莎主义者"（Um Philosoph zu sein, muss man erst ein Spinozist gewesen sein）。爱因斯坦被问及是否信仰上帝时回答："我信仰斯宾诺莎的上帝——那个在一切存在物的和谐中显现自身的上帝。"

German poet and philosopher Herder called him "the noble Spinoza" (der edle Spinoza). Goethe declared Spinoza's 哲学 his "spiritual home." Hegel even asserted: "To be a philosopher, one must first have been a Spinozist." When asked if he believed in God, Einstein replied: "I believe in Spinoza's God — who reveals himself in the harmony of all that exists."

在当代哲学中，斯宾诺莎通过德勒兹（Gilles Deleuze）的创造性诠释——《斯宾诺莎与表达问题》（1968）和《斯宾诺莎：实践哲学》（1981）——重新成为哲学焦点。德勒兹将斯宾诺莎解读为"哲学家的基督"（the Christ of philosophers），将他的伦理学解读为一种"快乐的科学"（gay science），一种肯定生命、增强行动能力的实践哲学。内格里（Antonio Negri）则将斯宾诺莎的政治哲学发展为"诸众"（multitude）理论，成为当代激进政治思想的重要资源。

In contemporary philosophy, Spinoza was revived through Gilles Deleuze's creative interpretation — *Spinoza and the Problem of Expression* (1968) and *Spinoza: Practical Philosophy* (1981). Deleuze read Spinoza as "the Christ of philosophers," interpreting his ethics as a "gay science" — an affirmative, power-enhancing practical 哲学. Antonio Negri developed Spinoza's political 哲学 into the "multitude" theory, a key resource for contemporary radical political thought.

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

1. **神经科学**：安东尼奥·达马西奥（Antonio Damasio）在《寻找斯宾诺莎》（2003）中论证，现代神经科学的发现证实了斯宾诺莎的身心平行论——情感不仅仅是心理事件，也是身体状态的变化。达马西奥的"躯体标记假说"（Somatic Marker Hypothesis）被视为斯宾诺莎情感理论的神经科学版本。
2. **情感哲学与文化研究**：斯宾诺莎的情动理论（affect theory）在当代文化研究中经历了重大复兴——马苏米（Brian Massumi）和格雷格（Melissa Gregg）等学者将"情动"（affect）作为分析当代文化和政治的核心范畴。
3. **政治哲学**：内格里的"诸众"理论直接发展了斯宾诺莎的民主思想。斯宾诺莎关于"权力即权利"（jus sive potestas）的观点——权利不是来自法律或道德，而是来自实际的力量——影响了从福柯到哈特的整个权力分析传统。
4. **生态哲学**：Deus sive Natura（上帝即自然）提供了一种不同于人类中心主义的形而上学基础——自然是神圣的，但不是在超验的意义上，而是在内在的意义上。这为深层生态学（Deep Ecology）和生态伦理学提供了哲学支撑。
5. **AI伦理**：斯宾诺莎的决定论与当代关于AI决策和算法偏见的讨论产生了新的对话——如果一切决策都是必然的因果链条的结果，那么"责任"意味着什么？
6. **心理治疗**：斯宾诺莎的"理性理解情感以获得自由"的方法在当代认知行为疗法（CBT）中找到了共鸣——通过理解思维和情感的因果机制来改变它们。
7. **犹太哲学与身份**：斯宾诺莎作为"被逐出社区的犹太思想家"的身份，使他成为犹太现代性和世俗犹太身份的标志性人物。
8. **政治神学**：《神学政治论》中的政教分离论证和圣经批判方法，是当代世俗主义和自由主义政治哲学的先驱。

1. **Neuroscience**: Antonio Damasio's *Looking for Spinoza* (2003) argues modern neuroscience confirms Spinoza's mind-body parallelism — affects are not merely mental events but also bodily state changes. His "Somatic Marker Hypothesis" is a neuroscientific version of Spinozist affect theory.
2. **Affect Theory & Cultural Studies**: Spinozist affect theory has undergone major revival in cultural studies — scholars like Brian Massumi and Melissa Gregg use "affect" as a core analytical category.
3. **Political Philosophy**: Negri's "multitude" theory directly develops Spinoza's democratic thought. Spinoza's "right is power" (jus sive potestas) influenced the entire power-analysis tradition from Foucault to Hardt.
4. **Ecological Philosophy**: Deus sive Natura provides a non-anthropocentric metaphysical foundation — nature is sacred in an immanent, not transcendent, sense, supporting Deep Ecology and environmental ethics.
5. **AI Ethics**: Spinoza's determinism dialogues anew with discussions of AI decision-making and algorithmic bias — if all decisions result from necessary causal chains, what does "responsibility" mean?
6. **Psychotherapy**: Spinoza's method of "rationally understanding affects to gain freedom" resonates in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) — changing thoughts and affects by understanding their causal mechanisms.
7. **Jewish Philosophy & Identity**: Spinoza as the "expelled Jewish thinker" makes him an iconic figure for Jewish modernity and secular Jewish identity.
8. **Political Theology**: The *Theological-Political Treatise*'s arguments for separation of 宗教 and state and biblical criticism are precursors of contemporary secularism and liberal political 哲学.

## 常见误读 / Common Misunderstandings

1. **❌ "斯宾诺莎是无神论者"** → ✅ 他是泛神论者（或更准确地说，万有在神论者/panentheist）——他以上帝/自然为核心，只是他的"上帝"不是人格化的、有意志的上帝，而是等同于自然整体及其必然法则。爱因斯坦对他的信仰做了最精确的描述。
2. **❌ "斯宾诺莎否认自由意志，所以是宿命论者"** → ✅ 斯宾诺莎确实否认"自由意志"（liberum arbitrium），但他的"自由"概念不是宿命论的。自由不是"可以选择不这样做"，而是"按照自身本性的必然性行动"——一棵自由生长的树比被风吹弯的树更自由。
3. **❌ "身心平行论就是二元论"** → ✅ 平行论建立在一元论基础上。笛卡尔的二元论认为身和心是两个不同的实体，斯宾诺莎的平行论认为身和心是同一实体的两个属性——它们的平行不是巧合（如莱布尼茨的前定和谐），而是因为它们本质上就是同一个东西的两种表达。
4. **❌ "《伦理学》是道德教条"** → ✅ 《伦理学》不是"你应该这样/不应该那样"的道德教条，而是一部关于人如何通过理性理解自身和世界的必然性来获得真正幸福和自由的哲学体系。它更接近于一种"生存的艺术"（ars vivendi）而非道德规范。

## 东西方对话 / East-West Dialogue

**与道家思想的对话**：斯宾诺莎的"上帝即自然"（Deus sive Natura）与道家的"道"之间存在深刻的共鸣。两者都拒绝超验的神——斯宾诺莎的上帝不在自然之外，正如道不在万物之外。两者都强调必然性和自发性（"自然"——在道家是"自然而然"，在斯宾诺莎是"natura naturans"，"能生的自然"）的统一。但道家更强调"无为"——顺应自然的无为而治，而斯宾诺莎更强调"理性的积极行动"——通过理解必然性来增强行动能力。

**Dialogue with Daoism**: Spinoza's Deus sive Natura resonates profoundly with the Daoist "Dao." Both reject a transcendent God — Spinoza's God is not outside nature, just as the Dao is not outside all things. Both emphasize the unity of necessity and spontaneity ("nature" — in Daoism, "self-so"; in Spinoza, "natura naturans," "nature naturing"). But Daoism emphasizes "wu wei" — effortless action in accordance with nature; Spinoza emphasizes "rational active engagement" — enhancing power of action through understanding necessity.

**与佛教的对话**：斯宾诺莎的决定论与佛教的缘起论（pratītya-samutpāda）之间存在有趣的平行——两者都否认无因的事件，否认偶然性。但斯宾诺莎的一元论将一切追溯到唯一的实体（上帝/自然），而佛教的缘起论否认有一个终极的"第一因"或"根本实体"。斯宾诺莎的"充分观念"（adequate idea）与佛教的"正见"（samyak-dṛṣṭi）也有可比性——两者都将正确的认知视为解脱/自由的途径。

**Dialogue with Buddhism**: Spinoza's determinism parallels Buddhist dependent origination (pratītya-samutpāda) — both deny uncaused events and contingency. But Spinoza's monism traces everything to one substance (God/Nature), while Buddhist dependent origination denies an ultimate "first cause" or "fundamental substance." Spinoza's "adequate idea" is comparable to Buddhist "right view" (samyak-dṛṣṭi) — both treat correct cognition as the path to liberation/freedom.

**与印度吠檀多的对话**：斯宾诺莎的实体一元论与吠檀多不二论（Advaita Vedānta）的"梵我一如"之间存在显著的结构相似性——两者都主张只有一个终极实在，万物都是这一实在的表现。但吠檀多的"梵"是纯粹意识（cit），而斯宾诺莎的"上帝/自然"同时具有思维和广延（以及无限多的其他属性）。此外，吠檀多的"解脱"（mokṣa）是通过否定现象世界来实现的，而斯宾诺莎的"幸福"（beatitudo）是通过肯定世界的必然性来实现的。

**Dialogue with Advaita Vedānta**: Spinoza's substance monism shares significant structural similarity with Advaita Vedānta's Brahman-Ātman identity — both hold there is only one ultimate reality, all things being its expressions. But Vedānta's Brahman is pure consciousness (cit), while Spinoza's God/Nature has both thought and extension (and infinitely many other attributes). Furthermore, Vedānta's "liberation" (mokṣa) is achieved through negating the phenomenal world, while Spinoza's "blessedness" (beatitudo) is achieved through affirming the world's necessity.

**与儒家的对话**：斯宾诺莎的情感理论——通过理性理解情感来从"被动情感"转化为"主动情感"——与儒家的"克己复礼"存在有趣的对比。两者都强调通过修养/理性来转化情感，但儒家的修养目标是道德人格的完善（"仁"），而斯宾诺莎的目标是理性自由的获得（"至善"summum bonum）。

**Dialogue with Confucianism**: Spinoza's affect theory — transforming passive affects into active affects through rational understanding — invites comparison with Confucian "self-cultivation and ritual propriety" (ke ji fu li). Both emphasize transforming affects through cultivation/reason, but the Confucian goal is moral personality perfection (ren), while Spinoza's goal is attaining rational freedom (summum bonum).
