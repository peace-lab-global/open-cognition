# 先验自然主义 · Transcendental Naturalism

## 概念定位 / Conceptual Position

先验自然主义（Transcendental Naturalism）是McGinn哲学体系中最具原创性的本体论立场。它试图在一个看似矛盾的组合中统一两个通常被认为互不相容的哲学承诺：**自然主义**（naturalism）和**不可知论**（agnosticism/unknowability）。McGinn主张，意识是一种纯粹自然的现象——它的存在和性质完全由自然规律决定，不需要任何超自然的解释——但这个自然主义解释在原则上超出了人类的认知能力。

Transcendental Naturalism is the most original ontological position in McGinn's philosophical system. It attempts to unify two philosophical commitments usually considered incompatible within a seemingly paradoxical combination: **naturalism** and **agnosticism/unknowability**. McGinn argues consciousness is a purely natural phenomenon — its existence and properties are entirely determined by natural laws, requiring no supernatural explanation — but this naturalistic explanation is in principle beyond human cognitive capacity.

"先验"（transcendental）这个术语借鉴了康德哲学。在康德那里，"先验"指的是使经验成为可能的条件——那些在经验之前就存在的认知结构。McGinn的"先验"则有双重含义：（1）意识的自然主义解释"超越"（transcends）了人类的认知能力；（2）对人类认知界限的考察本身就是一种先验哲学——类似于康德对理性界限的考察。

The term "transcendental" borrows from Kantian philosophy. In Kant, "transcendental" refers to conditions that make experience possible — cognitive structures that precede experience. McGinn's "transcendental" has a dual meaning: (1) consciousness's naturalistic explanation "transcends" human cognitive capacity; (2) the investigation of human cognitive limits is itself a kind of transcendental philosophy — analogous to Kant's investigation of reason's limits.

## 核心论述 / Core Argument

McGinn的先验自然主义包含三个关键命题：

McGinn's Transcendental Naturalism contains three key theses:

**命题一：自然主义承诺 / Thesis 1: Naturalistic Commitment**

意识是大脑的产物，完全遵循自然规律。不存在灵魂、精神实体或超自然力量。McGinn在这个意义上与Dennett和Churchland等物理主义者完全一致——他是一位毫不妥协的自然主义者。

Consciousness is a product of the brain, entirely governed by natural laws. No souls, spiritual substances, or supernatural forces exist. In this respect McGinn is fully aligned with physicalists like Dennett and Churchland — he is an uncompromising naturalist.

**命题二：不可知性 / Thesis 2: Unknowability**

尽管意识有自然主义的解释，但人类无法获取这个解释。存在某种（些）性质P，使得大脑具有性质P就产生了意识，但人类的概念系统无法把握P是什么。McGinn将这个隐藏的性质称为意识的"连结属性"（linking property）——它连接了大脑状态和意识状态，但对我们来说是不可见的。

Although consciousness has a naturalistic explanation, humans cannot access this explanation. There exists some property (or properties) P such that when the brain has property P, consciousness arises, but human conceptual systems cannot grasp what P is. McGinn calls this hidden property consciousness's "linking property" — it bridges brain states and conscious states but is invisible to us.

**命题三：区分存在与可知性 / Thesis 3: Distinguishing Existence from Knowability**

McGinn的核心洞见是：**某物存在且是自然的，不等于某物对人类可理解**。这是他的先验自然主义与传统立场的关键区别：

McGinn's core insight is: **that something exists and is natural does not entail it is comprehensible to humans**. This is the key distinction between his Transcendental Naturalism and traditional positions:

## 哲学意义 / Philosophical Significance

**重新定义自然主义的边界 / Redefining Naturalism's Boundaries:** 传统上，自然主义被认为意味着一切都可以通过自然科学来理解。McGinn展示了自然主义并不承诺可知性——我们可以是自然主义者，同时承认某些自然现象超出我们的理解。

Traditionally, naturalism has been assumed to entail everything can be understood through natural science. McGinn demonstrates naturalism does not commit to knowability — we can be naturalists while acknowledging some natural phenomena exceed our understanding.

**对二元论论据的消解 / Dissolving Arguments for Dualism:** 二元论者常常以"意识不可解释"为由论证意识必须是非物质的。McGinn的回应是：不可解释性并不蕴含非物质性。意识可以是物质的但不可解释的——就像狗不能理解的微积分仍然是纯数学事实一样。

Dualists often argue from "consciousness is inexplicable" to "consciousness must be non-material." McGinn's response: inexplicability does not entail non-materiality. Consciousness can be material yet inexplicable — just as calculus that dogs cannot understand remains pure mathematical fact.

**与康德的深层联系 / Deep Connection to Kant:** McGinn的先验自然主义与康德的"先验唯心论"（transcendental idealism）形成了有趣的对照。康德认为，现象界（phenomena）可知但物自体（noumena）不可知。McGinn的立场可以被视为这一思路的延伸：自然世界的大部分可知，但意识-大脑关系属于不可知的领域。

McGinn's Transcendental Naturalism forms an interesting counterpart to Kant's transcendental idealism. Kant held phenomena are knowable but noumena are unknowable. McGinn's position can be seen as extending this approach: most of the natural world is knowable, but the consciousness-brain relation belongs to the unknowable domain.

## 东西方对话 / East-West Dialogue

**与华严宗"理事无碍"的对话 / Dialogue with Huayan Buddhism's "Non-Obstruction of Principle and Phenomena":** 华严宗区分了"理"（终极原理，principle）和"事"（具体现象，phenomena），并主张两者"无碍"（mutually non-obstructing）。McGinn的先验自然主义也区分了意识的自然主义解释（"理"）和意识的主观经验（"事"），但主张两者之间存在不可逾越的认知鸿沟。华严宗认为修行者通过觉悟可以同时把握理和事；McGinn认为人类永远无法把握连接理与事的那个关键性质。

Huayan Buddhism distinguishes "principle" (li) and "phenomena" (shi), claiming they are mutually non-obstructing. McGinn's Transcendental Naturalism also distinguishes consciousness's naturalistic explanation ("principle") and consciousness's subjective experience ("phenomena"), but claims an insurmountable cognitive gap exists between them. Huayan holds practitioners can grasp both principle and phenomena through awakening; McGinn holds humans can never grasp the crucial property connecting principle and phenomena.

**与荀子"天行有常"的对话 / Dialogue with Xunzi's "Nature Has Constant Laws":** 荀子主张"天行有常，不为尧存，不为桀亡"——自然有其客观规律，不因人而变。这与McGinn的自然主义承诺高度一致。但荀子同时认为人可以认识和利用自然规律；McGinn则划出了一条界限：有些自然规律（意识的产生）是人类无法认识的。

Xunzi argues "Nature has constant laws; it does not exist because of Yao, nor perish because of Jie" — nature has objective regularities that do not change for anyone. This aligns with McGinn's naturalistic commitment. But Xunzi also holds humans can know and utilize natural laws; McGinn draws a boundary: some natural laws (consciousness's production) are unknowable to humans.

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

**科学实在论与反实在论之争 / Scientific Realism vs. Anti-Realism Debate:** 先验自然主义在这场争论中占据独特位置。McGinn同意科学实在论者——理论实体（如电子、基因）真实存在。但他也同意反实在论者的某些直觉——我们的理论可能永远无法触及实在的某些层面。

Transcendental Naturalism occupies a unique position in this debate. McGinn agrees with scientific realists — theoretical entities (electrons, genes) really exist. But he also agrees with certain anti-realist intuitions — our theories may never reach some layers of reality.

**自然化认识论 / Naturalized Epistemology:** McGinn的立场为自然化认识论提供了重要的修正。如果人类的认知能力有固有边界，那么认识论就不能假设人类可以认识一切自然现象——认识论必须认真对待认知的生物性限制。

McGinn's position provides an important correction to naturalized epistemology. If human cognitive capacity has inherent boundaries, epistemology cannot assume humans can know all natural phenomena — epistemology must take seriously the biological limits of cognition.
