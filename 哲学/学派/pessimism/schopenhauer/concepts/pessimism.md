# 悲观主义 / Pessimism

> "人生如钟摆，在痛苦与无聊之间来回摆荡。"
> "Life swings like a pendulum to and fro between pain and boredom."
> — Schopenhauer, *Die Welt als Wille und Vorstellung*, Book IV, §57

---

## 概述 / Overview

叔本华的悲观主义不是情绪性的哀叹，而是从其意志形而上学中严格推导出的哲学结论。既然世界的本质是盲目的、永不满足的意志（Wille），而意志的本质是无尽的欲求，那么一切有意识的存在都必然以痛苦为基调。悲观主义因此不是叔本华哲学的附加特征，而是其体系的内在结论。

Schopenhauer's pessimism is not an emotional lament but a rigorous philosophical conclusion derived from his Will metaphysics. Since the world's essence is blind, insatiable Will, and Will's essence is endless striving, all conscious existence must have suffering as its keynote. Pessimism is therefore not an incidental feature of Schopenhauer's philosophy but an intrinsic conclusion of his system.

---

## 痛苦作为积极、快乐作为消极 / Suffering as Positive, Pleasure as Negative

叔本华悲观主义的核心论题是对痛苦与快乐的**不对称分析**：

The central thesis of Schopenhauer's pessimism is an **asymmetric analysis** of suffering and pleasure:

**痛苦是积极的（positive）**——它是直接的、实在的感受，是欲求受阻时的直接体验。饥饿、疼痛、焦虑、失落——这些都是实实在在的感受，不需要任何参照就能被感知。

**Suffering is positive** — it is a direct, real experience, immediately felt when desire is thwarted. Hunger, pain, anxiety, loss — these are tangible sensations perceivable without any reference point.

**快乐是消极的（negative）**——它不是独立的、积极的状态，而仅仅是痛苦的暂时消除或欲望的暂时满足。饱食的快乐以饥饿为前提，休息的快乐以疲劳为前提。快乐本质上是一种"解脱"——从痛苦中的暂时释放。

**Pleasure is negative** — it is not an independent, positive state but merely the temporary removal of suffering or the temporary satisfaction of desire. The pleasure of satiation presupposes hunger; the pleasure of rest presupposes fatigue. Pleasure is essentially a "relief" — a temporary release from suffering.

叔本华用了一个精妙的类比来论证这一点：正如身体健康时我们感觉不到身体（只有生病时才注意到某个器官），意志满足时我们也感觉不到满足（只有欲求受阻时才感到痛苦）。快乐是"无"——是匮乏的缺席——而痛苦是"有"——是实在的、积极的存在。

Schopenhauer employs a subtle analogy: just as in good health we are unaware of our body (noticing a particular organ only when it is diseased), so when the will is satisfied we are unaware of satisfaction (feeling suffering only when desire is thwarted). Pleasure is "nothing" — the absence of deprivation — while suffering is "something" — a real, positive presence.

## 痛苦与无聊的钟摆 / The Pendulum of Pain and Boredom

如果欲求的受阻产生痛苦，那么欲求的满足则产生**无聊**（Langeweile）。当一个欲望被满足后，在新的欲望产生之前，意识陷入一种空洞的、无所事事的状态——无聊。叔本华认为，无聊不是微不足道的感受，而是一种深刻的存在性痛苦：它揭示了生命本身的空洞和无意义。

If the thwarting of desire produces suffering, then the satisfaction of desire produces **boredom** (Langeweile). When one desire is satisfied, before a new one arises, consciousness falls into an empty, idle state — boredom. Schopenhauer holds that boredom is not a trivial sensation but a profound existential suffering: it reveals the emptiness and meaninglessness of life itself.

因此，人生被捕获在一个无法逃脱的钟摆运动中：

Life is thus caught in an inescapable pendulum motion:

```
欲求 → 痛苦（欲求受阻） → 满足 → 无聊（欲求空缺） → 新欲求 → 痛苦 → ...
Desire → Pain (desire thwarted) → Satisfaction → Boredom (desire absent) → New desire → Pain → ...
```

大多数人通过不断地追求新的欲望来逃避无聊——新的恋爱、新的财产、新的娱乐。但这只是将钟摆从无聊这一极推向痛苦那一极，而非真正逃脱钟摆运动本身。

Most people escape boredom by ceaselessly pursuing new desires — new romances, new possessions, new amusements. But this merely swings the pendulum from the pole of boredom to the pole of pain, without ever escaping the pendulum itself.

## 痛苦的普遍性与必然性 / The Universality and Necessity of Suffering

叔本华论证，痛苦不是生命的偶然事故，而是生命意志的必然特征。他从多个层面论证这一点：

Schopenhauer argues that suffering is not an accident of life but an essential feature of the will-to-live. He argues this on multiple levels:

**生物学层面**：一切有机体都处于永恒的匮乏状态——需要不断摄取食物、避免危险、寻找配偶。生命本身就是持续的匮乏和斗争。

**Biological level**: All organisms exist in a permanent state of deprivation — needing to constantly ingest food, avoid danger, find mates. Life itself is continuous deprivation and struggle.

**心理学层面**：人类意识使痛苦更加尖锐。我们不仅能感受当下的痛苦，还能回忆过去的痛苦和预见未来的痛苦。希望和恐惧——作为时间意识的产物——增加了痛苦的维度。

**Psychological level**: Human consciousness makes suffering more acute. We can feel not only present pain but also recall past suffering and anticipate future suffering. Hope and fear — products of temporal consciousness — add dimensions to suffering.

**社会学层面**：人与人之间的竞争、嫉妒、虚荣和仇恨使社会生活成为痛苦的另一个来源。霍布斯式的"一切人对一切人的战争"在叔本华看来不是自然状态的描述，而是生命意志的常态表现。

**Social level**: Competition, envy, vanity, and hatred between people make social life another source of suffering. The Hobbesian "war of all against all" is, for Schopenhauer, not a description of a state of nature but the normal expression of the will-to-live.

## 对乐观主义的批判 / Critique of Optimism

叔本华对乐观主义（包括莱布尼茨的"最好可能世界"论和黑格尔的历史进步论）进行了猛烈批判。他认为乐观主义在哲学上是荒谬的——因为它无视痛苦的实在性——在伦理上是有害的——因为它暗示受苦者"应该"满足，从而加剧了他们的痛苦。

Schopenhauer fiercely attacks optimism (including Leibniz's "best of all possible worlds" and Hegel's historical progress). He considers optimism philosophically absurd — because it ignores the reality of suffering — and ethically harmful — because it implies that sufferers "ought" to be content, thereby compounding their suffering.

他提出了一个思想实验来论证悲观主义的合理性：让一个人在充分了解了世界的全部痛苦之后，决定是否愿意再次经历同样的一生。叔本华确信，任何理性的存在者都会选择"不再存在"（non-existence）——这证明了存在本身不具有内在价值。

He proposes a thought experiment to demonstrate pessimism's rationality: let a person, having fully understood all the world's suffering, decide whether to live the same life again. Schopenhauer is certain that any rational being would choose non-existence — proving that existence itself lacks intrinsic value.

## 悲观主义的伦理功能 / The Ethical Function of Pessimism

值得注意的是，叔本华的悲观主义不是导向绝望或虚无主义，而是具有积极的伦理功能。认识到生命的根本痛苦是同情（Mitleid）的前提：当我们认识到他者与我们一样受苦时，同情自然产生。悲观主义也是意志否定的前提：只有认识到意志的欲求本质上导致痛苦，才有可能产生否定意志的动机。

Notably, Schopenhauer's pessimism leads neither to despair nor nihilism but has a positive ethical function. Recognizing life's fundamental suffering is the precondition for compassion (Mitleid): when we recognize that others suffer as we do, compassion arises naturally. Pessimism is also the precondition for the denial of Will: only by recognizing that Will's striving essentially produces suffering can the motivation to deny Will emerge.

因此，悲观主义在叔本华的体系中扮演了一个辩证的角色：它是从认识（世界是痛苦的）走向解脱（否定造成痛苦的意志）的桥梁。

Pessimism thus plays a dialectical role in Schopenhauer's system: it is the bridge from recognition (the world is suffering) to liberation (denying the Will that causes suffering).

## 后续影响 / Subsequent Influence

叔本华的悲观主义深刻影响了后世哲学和文学：尼采在承认悲观主义洞见的同时试图以"悲剧性肯定"超越它；弗洛伊德的"死亡本能"（Thanatos）概念呼应了叔本华对痛苦和死亡的重视；存在主义传统（尤其是叔本华→尼采→海德格尔→萨特的线索）继承了关于人生荒谬性和焦虑的基本问题意识。在文学中，托马斯·曼、普鲁斯特、托尔斯泰、哈代、博尔赫斯都深受叔本华悲观主义的影响。

Schopenhauer's pessimism profoundly influenced subsequent philosophy and literature: Nietzsche acknowledged the pessimistic insight while attempting to transcend it with "tragic affirmation"; Freud's concept of the death drive (Thanatos) echoes Schopenhauer's emphasis on suffering and death; the existentialist tradition (especially the Schopenhauer → Nietzsche → Heidegger → Sartre lineage) inherits the basic problematic of life's absurdity and anxiety. In literature, Thomas Mann, Proust, Tolstoy, Hardy, and Borges were all deeply influenced by Schopenhauerian pessimism.
