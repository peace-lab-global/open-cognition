# 叔本华 · Arthur Schopenhauer

> "世界是我的表象"——这是第一个也是最根本的真理。
> "The world is my representation" — this is the first and most fundamental truth.
> — *Die Welt als Wille und Vorstellung*, §1

---

## 基本信息 / Biographical Note

亚瑟·叔本华（Arthur Schopenhauer, 1788年2月22日–1860年9月21日），德国哲学家，西方哲学史上最伟大的悲观主义者，也是第一位严肃对待东方哲学传统的西方主要思想家。他出生于但泽（Danzig，今波兰格但斯克）一个富裕的波罗的海德意志商人家庭。父亲海因里希·弗洛里斯·叔本华是一位世界主义的商人，在叔本华16岁时疑似自杀身亡——这一事件深刻地塑造了叔本华对人生苦难本质的认知。母亲约翰娜·叔本华是当时知名的小说家，与歌德（Goethe）交好，正是在歌德的色彩理论讨论中，年轻的叔本华获得了关于感知理论的早期启发。

Arthur Schopenhauer (22 February 1788 – 21 September 1860) was a German philosopher, the greatest pessimist in the history of Western philosophy, and the first major Western thinker to engage seriously with Eastern philosophical traditions. Born into a wealthy Baltic German merchant family in Danzig (now Gdańsk, Poland), his father Heinrich Floris Schopenhauer — a cosmopolitan merchant — died when Arthur was sixteen, likely by suicide, an event that profoundly shaped his understanding of life's suffering. His mother Johanna Schopenhauer was a celebrated novelist friendly with Goethe, in whose discussions on colour theory the young Arthur found early inspiration for his theory of perception.

叔本华先后在哥廷根大学和柏林大学学习哲学，师从费希特（Fichte）。1813年，他以博士论文《充足理由律的四重根》（*Über die vierfache Wurzel des Satzes vom zureichenden Grunde*）获得耶拿大学博士学位，为其认识论体系奠定了基础。1818年，年仅三十岁的叔本华完成了《作为意志和表象的世界》（*Die Welt als Wille und Vorstellung*），但这部杰作在此后二十余年间几乎无人问津——据传首版印刷的500册中有大部分被当作废纸处理。

He studied 哲学 at Göttingen and Berlin under Fichte. In 1813 he earned his doctorate from Jena with *On the Fourfold Root of the Principle of Sufficient Reason*, laying his epistemological foundations. In 1818, at just thirty, he completed *The World as Will and Representation* — yet this masterwork went almost entirely unnoticed for over two decades. Of the original print run of 500 copies, most were reportedly pulped.

在柏林大学担任讲师期间，叔本华刻意将自己的课程安排在与如日中天的黑格尔同一时间——结果只有寥寥数名学生前来听课。这种与黑格尔理性主义的根本对立，贯穿了叔本华整个思想生涯。直到1851年《附录与补遗》（*Parerga und Paralipomena*）出版后，他才终于在大众中获得了迟到的声誉。晚年在法兰克福与一只名为"Atma"（世界灵魂）的卷毛狗为伴，过着规律而孤独的生活，直至1860年因肺炎去世。

During his tenure as a lecturer at Berlin, he deliberately scheduled his courses opposite Hegel's — and attracted almost no students. This fundamental opposition to Hegelian rationalism ran through Schopenhauer's entire career. Only with the publication of *Parerga and Paralipomena* in 1851 did he finally achieve popular recognition. He spent his later years in Frankfurt with a poodle named "Atma" (World Soul), living a regular and solitary life until his death from pneumonia in 1860.

## 一句话定位 / One-Line Positioning

**西方最伟大的悲观主义哲学家，将世界之本质揭示为盲目的"意志"，并在审美沉思与同情伦理中寻求解脱之道——同时也是东西方哲学对话的开创者。**

**The foremost Western pessimist, who revealed the world's essence as blind "Will" and sought liberation through aesthetic contemplation and compassion ethics — and the pioneer of East-West philosophical dialogue.**

## 六大核心命题 / Six Core Theses

### 1. 意志作为物自体 / Will as Thing-in-Itself

→ 详见 [概念/意志作为物自体.md](概念/意志作为物自体.md)

世界的本质不是理性、理念或精神（黑格尔），而是盲目的、无目的的、永不满足的"意志"（Wille）。自然界的一切力量——引力、磁力、生长本能——都是意志在不同层级的客体化（Objektivation）。我们通过内在经验——即对自身身体作为意志行为的直接体验——得以认识这一物自体。意志不是个体的心理状态，而是先于一切认识的形而上学本体。

The essence of the world is not reason, idea, or spirit (Hegel) but blind, purposeless, insatiable "Will" (Wille). All forces of nature — gravity, magnetism, the instinct to grow — are different grades of the Will's objectification. We access this thing-in-itself through inner experience: the immediate experience of our own body as an act of will. Will is not an individual psychological state but a metaphysical reality prior to all cognition.

### 2. 表象世界 / World as Representation

我们所经验的世界是"表象"（Vorstellung）——意志通过主体的认识形式（时间、空间、因果性）所呈现的面貌。这一立场继承了康德的现象/物自体区分，同时以叔本华对充足理由律四重根的独特分析加以改造：表象世界是受充足理由律完全支配的领域，而意志本身则在此律之外。

The world as we experience it is "representation" (Vorstellung) — the Will as it appears through the subject's forms of cognition (space, time, causality). This inherits Kant's phenomenon/thing-in-itself distinction while transforming it through Schopenhauer's unique analysis of the fourfold root of sufficient reason: the world of representation is the domain fully governed by the principle of sufficient reason, while Will itself lies outside this law.

### 3. 悲观主义：痛苦与无聊的钟摆 / Pessimism: The Pendulum of Pain and Boredom

→ 详见 [概念/悲观主义.md](概念/悲观主义.md)

意志的本质是无尽的欲求。每一个欲望的满足都只是暂时的，新的欲求立即填补空缺。因此，生命的基调是痛苦（Leiden），而满足之后则是无聊（Langeweile）。"人生如钟摆，在痛苦与无聊之间来回摆荡。"快乐只是痛苦的暂时消除（消极的），而痛苦才是积极的、根本性的存在。

The Will's essence is endless desire. Each satisfaction is merely temporary; new desires immediately fill the void. Life's keynote is therefore suffering (Leiden), and after satisfaction comes boredom (Langeweile). "Life swings like a pendulum between pain and boredom." Pleasure is merely the temporary removal of pain (negative), while suffering is positive and fundamental.

### 4. 审美沉思作为解脱 / Aesthetic Contemplation as Escape

→ 详见 [概念/审美沉思.md](概念/审美沉思.md)

在审美沉思中，主体摆脱了意志的驱使和个体化原理的束缚，成为"纯粹、无意志的认识主体"（reines, willensfreies Subjekt des Erkennens）。艺术的对象不是个别事物，而是柏拉图式的"理念"（Ideen）——意志的直接客体化层级。在所有艺术中，音乐占有最崇高的地位：它不是理念的摹本，而是意志本身的直接写照。

In aesthetic contemplation, the subject escapes the Will's compulsion and the bonds of the principium individuationis, becoming a "pure, will-less knowing subject." Art's object is not individual things but Platonic Ideas — grades of the Will's direct objectification. Among all arts, music holds the most exalted position: it is not a copy of Ideas but a direct copy of the Will itself.

### 5. 同情伦理 / Compassion Ethics

→ 详见 [概念/同情伦理.md](概念/同情伦理.md)

道德的基础不是康德的绝对命令（kategorischer Imperativ）或功利主义的计算，而是对他者痛苦的直接同情（Mitleid）。同情的形而上学基础在于：通过穿透"个体化原理"（principium individuationis）——即时空所造成的个体分隔——主体认识到他者的内在本质与自己同一，都是同一个意志。印度教格言"汝即彼"（tat tvam asi）表达了这一洞见。

Morality's foundation is neither Kant's categorical imperative nor utilitarian calculation, but direct compassion (Mitleid) for others' suffering. Compassion's metaphysical basis lies in penetrating the principium individuationis — the separation between individuals produced by space and time — so that the subject recognizes the inner essence of others as identical with his own: the same Will. The Hindu formula "tat tvam asi" (thou art that) expresses this insight.

### 6. 否定意志 / Denial of the Will

→ 详见 [概念/意志的否定.md](概念/意志的否定.md)

最终的、彻底的解脱不在于审美沉思（它只是暂时的），而在于意志本身的自我否定（Verneinung des Willens zum Leben）。通过禁欲主义（Askese）和智识洞察，意志认识到自身的虚空和无意义而自行熄灭。这一状态与佛教的涅槃（Nirvana）形成深刻的呼应。叔本华严格区分了否定意志与自杀：自杀恰恰是意志的肯定——因无法忍受痛苦而以暴力对抗生命意志。

Ultimate, thorough liberation lies not in aesthetic contemplation (which is merely temporary) but in the Will's self-negation (Verneinung des Willens zum Leben). Through asceticism (Askese) and intellectual insight, the Will recognizes its own emptiness and meaninglessness, and extinguishes itself. This state deeply resonates with Buddhist Nirvana. Schopenhauer strictly distinguishes the denial of Will from suicide: suicide is precisely an affirmation of Will — a violent protest against the will-to-live due to unbearable suffering.

## 思想脉络 / Intellectual Trajectory

### 早期（1788–1818）：康德与柏拉图的综合 / Early Period: Kant-Plato Synthesis

叔本华的哲学出发点是对康德（Kant）的批判性继承。他接受了现象/物自体的区分，但认为康德将物自体留为不可知是一个错误。叔本华主张，通过一种特殊的内在认识——即我们对自己身体的意志行为的直接体验——我们能够认识物自体，它就是意志。这一洞见与柏拉图的理念论相结合，形成了叔本华独特的美学理论：理念是意志的直接客体化，处于因果链条（充足理由律）之外，因而成为审美沉思的对象。

Schopenhauer's philosophical point of departure is his critical inheritance from Kant. He accepted the phenomenon/thing-in-itself distinction but held that Kant erred in leaving the thing-in-itself unknowable. Through a special kind of inner knowledge — our direct experience of our own body's acts of will — we can know the thing-in-itself: it is Will. This insight, combined with Plato's theory of Ideas, produced Schopenhauer's unique aesthetics: Ideas are the Will's direct objectification, standing outside the causal chain (the principle of sufficient reason), and thus becoming objects of aesthetic contemplation.

### 中期（1818–1844）：《作为意志和表象的世界》体系建构 / Middle Period: System-Building in WWR

《作为意志和表象的世界》（第一卷1818，第二卷1844）是叔本华体系的完整阐述。四卷各有分工：第一卷处理认识论——世界作为受充足理由律支配的表象；第二卷处理形而上学——世界作为意志，揭示一切自然现象背后的本体实在；第三卷处理美学——理念作为意志的直接客体化，艺术作为从意志中的暂时解脱；第四卷处理伦理学——同情、正义、禁欲主义与意志的最终否定。1844年的第二卷大幅扩充了对自然科学、生命意志、性欲的形而上学分析以及个体化原理的深入讨论。

*The World as Will and Representation* (Volume I, 1818; Volume II, 1844) is the complete exposition of Schopenhauer's system. The four books each have their domain: Book I, epistemology — the world as representation governed by the principle of sufficient reason; Book II, metaphysics — the world as Will, revealing the noumenal reality behind all natural phenomena; Book III, 美学 — Ideas as the Will's direct objectification, art as temporary liberation from Will; Book IV, ethics — compassion, justice, asceticism, and the ultimate denial of Will. The expanded 1844 second volume greatly deepened the analysis of natural science, the metaphysics of the will-to-live, sexual love, and the principium individuationis.

### 晚期（1844–1860）：声誉确立与东方哲学对话 / Late Period: Reputation and Eastern Dialogue

1851年的《附录与补遗》（*Parerga und Paralipomena*）终于为叔本华赢得了迟到的声誉。这部两卷本文集以优美的散文探讨了哲学、宗教、文学和人生智慧，其中《人生的智慧》（*Aphorismen zur Lebensweisheit*）部分至今仍是畅销读物。更重要的是，叔本华在晚期著作中进一步发展了与佛教和印度哲学（尤其是吠檀多 Vedānta）的对话，使他成为第一位将东方哲学纳入严肃西方哲学讨论的主要思想家。

*Parerga and Paralipomena* (1851) finally brought Schopenhauer belated fame. This two-volume collection of essays explored philosophy, religion, literature, and worldly wisdom in elegant prose; the section "Aphorisms on the Wisdom of Life" remains a bestseller to this day. More importantly, Schopenhauer's later works further developed his dialogue with Buddhism and Indian 哲学 (especially Vedānta), making him the first major Western philosopher to bring Eastern thought into serious philosophical discussion.

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

### 心理分析的先驱 / Precursor to Psychoanalysis

叔本华的"意志"概念——作为非理性的、无意识的驱力，先于并支配理性认识——被广泛视为弗洛伊德（Freud）"本我"（Id）概念的哲学先驱。弗洛伊德本人承认了这一关联。意志-表象的二元结构在精神分析中转化为无意识-意识的动力学模型。

Schopenhauer's "Will" — as an irrational, unconscious drive preceding and governing rational cognition — is widely regarded as the philosophical precursor to Freud's concept of the Id. Freud himself acknowledged this connection. The Will-representation duality transforms in psychoanalysis into the unconscious-conscious dynamic model.

### 神经科学 / Neuroscience

安东尼奥·达马西奥（Antonio Damasio）的情感理论——理性和决策深深依赖于躯体标记和情绪过程——与叔本华对理性作为意志/情感之仆从的分析形成惊人的呼应。现代神经科学对"理性自主性"的质疑，在叔本华的体系中早有先声。

Antonio Damasio's somatic marker theory — that reason and decision-making deeply depend on emotional and bodily processes — remarkably echoes Schopenhauer's analysis of reason as servant to Will/emotion. Modern neuroscience's questioning of "rational autonomy" finds early expression in Schopenhauer's system.

### 动物伦理 / Animal Ethics

叔本华的同情伦理天然地扩展到动物：既然动物也拥有意志，它们的痛苦与人类的痛苦在本体论上是同一的。这一立场使他成为现代动物权利运动（如彼得·辛格 Peter Singer 的工作）的哲学先驱之一。

Schopenhauer's compassion ethics naturally extends to animals: since animals also possess Will, their suffering is ontologically identical with human suffering. This makes him one of the philosophical precursors of the modern animal rights movement (e.g., Peter Singer's work).

### 正念与冥想 / Mindfulness and Meditation

叔本华的审美沉思概念与冥想实践（尤其是佛教的奢摩他 śamatha 和毗婆舍那 vipaśyanā）之间存在结构性类比：两者都涉及从意志的驱使——即欲望和执着的强迫性循环——中获得暂时或持久的解脱。正念减压疗法（MBSR）等当代心理干预方法在哲学层面呼应了叔本华的洞见。

A structural analogy exists between Schopenhauer's aesthetic contemplation and meditative practice (especially Buddhist śamatha and vipaśyanā): both involve obtaining temporary or lasting freedom from the Will's compulsion — the obsessive cycle of desire and attachment. Contemporary psychological interventions like Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) philosophically echo Schopenhauer's insights.

### 生态伦理 / Ecological Ethics

万物意志同一性的形而上学——即一切存在者在最深层面都是同一个意志的不同显现——为深层生态学（deep ecology）提供了一种非人类中心主义的伦理基础。如果自然界的万物与我们在本体论上同一，那么对自然的破坏就是对自身意志的伤害。

The metaphysics of the unity of all Will — that all beings are at the deepest level different manifestations of one and the same Will — provides deep ecology with a non-anthropocentric ethical foundation. If all of nature is ontologically identical with us, then the destruction of nature is a harm to our own Will.

### 柏格森与生命哲学 / Bergson and the Philosophy of Life

柏格森（Henri Bergson）的"生命冲动"（élan vital）概念明显继承了叔本华的意志形而上学，尽管柏格森赋予生命冲动以更积极的创造性含义——从否定性的生存意志转向肯定性的创造力量。

Henri Bergson's concept of élan vital clearly inherits Schopenhauer's Will metaphysics, though Bergson gives the vital impulse a more positive, creative meaning — shifting from the negative will-to-live to an affirmative creative force.

## 常见误读 / Common Misunderstandings

### 误读一：叔本华主张自杀 / "Schopenhauer advocates suicide"

**纠正**：悲观主义的结论恰恰不是自杀，而是否定意志。叔本华严格论证：自杀是意志的肯定而非否定——自杀者因无法忍受痛苦而以暴力消灭身体，这恰恰是对生命意志的强烈肯定（以一种欲望对抗另一种欲望）。真正的否定意志是通过智识洞察和禁欲使意志自行熄灭。

**Correction**: The conclusion of pessimism is precisely not suicide but the denial of Will. Schopenhauer rigorously argues: suicide affirms rather than denies the Will — the suicide violently destroys the body because of unbearable suffering, which is precisely a powerful affirmation of the will-to-live (opposing one desire with another). True denial of Will comes through intellectual insight and asceticism, causing the Will to extinguish itself.

### 误读二：叔本华只是"悲观的"，没有积极贡献 / "Schopenhauer is merely 'pessimistic' with no positive contributions"

**纠正**：叔本华的审美理论对后世美学产生了深远影响（瓦格纳、普鲁斯特、托马斯·曼）。他的同情伦理学提供了独立于康德义务论和功利主义的第三条道德路径。他对印度哲学和佛教的引介开创了比较哲学的先河。他对无意识和非理性力量的揭示预示了整个深度心理学传统。

**Correction**: Schopenhauer's aesthetic theory profoundly influenced subsequent 美学 (Wagner, Proust, Thomas Mann). His compassion ethics offers a third moral path independent of Kantian deontology and utilitarianism. His introduction of Indian 哲学 and Buddhism pioneered comparative 哲学. His revelation of the unconscious and irrational forces anticipated the entire depth-psychology tradition.

### 误读三：叔本华与尼采基本一致 / "Schopenhauer and Nietzsche are fundamentally the same"

**纠正**：尼采早期确实是叔本华的虔诚追随者（《悲剧的诞生》明显受其影响），但成熟期的尼采以"权力意志"（Wille zur Macht）取代了叔本华的"生存意志"（Wille zum Leben），这是对叔本华的根本批判和颠倒：前者肯定生命和创造，后者否定生命和欲求。尼采将叔本华的"否定"视为颓废（décadence）的症状。

**Correction**: The young Nietzsche was indeed a devoted follower of Schopenhauer (*The Birth of Tragedy* is clearly influenced), but the mature Nietzsche replaced Schopenhauer's "will-to-live" (Wille zum Leben) with "will to power" (Wille zur Macht) — a fundamental critique and inversion: the former affirms life and creation, the latter negates life and desire. Nietzsche regarded Schopenhauer's "negation" as a symptom of décadence.

### 误读四：叔本华的"意志"是心理学概念 / "Schopenhauer's 'Will' is a psychological concept"

**纠正**：叔本华的"意志"是形而上学的本体论概念，而非心理学或经验概念。意志先于一切心理状态和个别意识，是自然界一切力量（包括物理力、化学力、生物力）的内在本质。将"意志"仅仅理解为人类欲望或心理冲动，是将本体论概念还原为经验心理学概念，这恰恰违背了叔本华的核心主张。

**Correction**: Schopenhauer's "Will" is a metaphysical, ontological concept, not a psychological or empirical one. Will precedes all psychological states and individual consciousness; it is the inner essence of all forces in nature (including physical, chemical, and biological forces). Reducing "Will" to human desire or psychological impulse is reducing an ontological concept to empirical 心理学 — precisely contrary to Schopenhauer's central claim.

## 东西方对话：叔本华 × 佛教 / East-West Dialogue: Schopenhauer × Buddhism

叔本华是西方哲学史上第一位与东方传统进行深度对话的思想家。他在1813年左右接触到拉丁文翻译的《奥义书》（Upanishads），称之为"我一生的慰藉"。他对佛教的理解虽受限于当时欧洲的文献条件，但在结构层面揭示了惊人的平行关系。

Schopenhauer was the first thinker in the history of Western 哲学 to engage in deep dialogue with Eastern traditions. He encountered the Upanishads in Latin translation around 1813 and called them "the consolation of my life." Though his understanding of Buddhism was limited by the textual resources available in Europe at the time, his system reveals astonishing structural parallels.

### 四圣谛与意志-悲观-否定结构 / The Four Noble Truths and the Will-Pessimism-Denial Structure

| 佛教四圣谛 | 叔本华对应概念 |
|------------|---------------|
| 苦谛（duḥkha）：一切皆苦 | 悲观主义：意志的本质是痛苦 |
| 集谛（samudāya）：苦因是渴爱（taṇhā） | 意志作为物自体：无尽的欲求是苦的根源 |
| 灭谛（nirodha）：灭苦即涅槃 | 否定意志：通过意志的自我否定获得解脱 |
| 道谛（mārga）：八正道 | 禁欲主义与审美沉思作为解脱之道 |

| Buddhism's Four Noble Truths | Schopenhauer's Corresponding Concept |
|------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Duḥkha: all is suffering | Pessimism: Will's essence is suffering |
| Samudāya: suffering's cause is craving (taṇhā) | Will as thing-in-itself: endless desire is the root of suffering |
| Nirodha: cessation of suffering is Nirvana | Denial of Will: liberation through Will's self-negation |
| Mārga: the Eightfold Path | Asceticism and aesthetic contemplation as the path to liberation |

### 摩耶之幕与个体化原理 / The Veil of Maya and the Principium Individuationis

叔本华将吠檀多（Vedānta）哲学中的"摩耶之幕"（veil of māyā）——即使人将虚幻的现象世界误认为实在的无知之幕——等同于他的"个体化原理"（principium individuationis）。在叔本华看来，时空和因果性使同一个意志显现为无数彼此分离的个体；穿透这层面纱——认识一切个体在本体论上的同一性——是伦理行为和最终解脱的前提。

Schopenhauer equated the Vedānta concept of the "veil of māyā" — the veil of ignorance that causes one to mistake the illusory phenomenal world for reality — with his principium individuationis. For Schopenhauer, space, time, and causality cause one and the same Will to appear as countless separate individuals; penetrating this veil — recognizing the ontological identity of all individuals — is the precondition for ethical action and ultimate liberation.

### 慈悲（karuṇā）与同情（Mitleid） / Karuṇā and Compassion

佛教的慈悲（karuṇā）——对一切众生苦难的感同身受——与叔本华的同情（Mitleid）概念在结构和功能上高度一致：两者都要求超越自我与他者的界限，两者都将此视为道德行为的根源，两者都指向一种本体论层面的洞见——他者的苦即自己的苦。

Buddhist karuṇā — empathetic identification with the suffering of all beings — aligns closely in structure and function with Schopenhauer's Mitleid: both require transcending the boundary between self and other, both identify this as the source of moral action, and both point to an ontological insight — the suffering of others is one's own suffering.

### 涅槃与意志的否定 / Nirvana and the Denial of Will

叔本华的"意志的否定"——通过智识洞察使生命意志自行熄灭——与佛教的涅槃（Nirvāṇa，字面义为"熄灭"）之间存在深刻的呼应。两者都不是虚无主义的消灭，而是对渴爱和执着的超越。叔本华特别指出，他的否定意志与佛教涅槃一样，不是"无"（nothingness），而是从意志的束缚中获得自由之后的状态——一种超越语言和概念所能把握的境地。

Schopenhauer's "denial of Will" — the extinction of the will-to-live through intellectual insight — deeply resonates with Buddhist Nirvāṇa (literally "extinguishing"). Neither is a nihilistic annihilation; both transcend craving and attachment. Schopenhauer specifically notes that, like Buddhist Nirvana, his denial of Will is not "nothingness" but a state of freedom from the Will's bondage — a condition beyond what language and concepts can grasp.

### 唯识学（Yogācāra）与表象论 / Yogācāra Vijñaptimātra and Representation

佛教唯识学派的"唯识无境"（vijñaptimātra）——即一切外境皆是心识的显现，不存在独立于认识的外在对象——与叔本华的"世界是我的表象"（Die Welt ist meine Vorstellung）之间存在有趣的平行关系。两者都否定了一个独立于主体认识的客观实在，尽管叔本华在表象背后设定了意志作为本体，而唯识学则走向了更彻底的空性（śūnyatā）分析。

The Yogācāra school's doctrine of vijñaptimātra ("representation-only") — that all external objects are merely manifestations of consciousness, with no objects existing independently of cognition — bears interesting parallels with Schopenhauer's "the world is my representation." Both deny an objective reality independent of the subject's cognition, though Schopenhauer posits Will as the noumenon behind representation, while Yogācāra moves toward a more thoroughgoing analysis of emptiness (śūnyatā).
