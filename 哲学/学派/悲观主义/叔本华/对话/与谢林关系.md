# 与谢林的关系 / Relation to Schelling

> "谢林的哲学是一堆空话……没有任何实在的内容。"
> "Schelling's philosophy is a heap of empty words… without any real content."
> — Schopenhauer, *Die Welt als Wille und Vorstellung*, Preface to the first edition

---

## 概述 / Overview

叔本华与谢林（Friedrich Wilhelm Joseph Schelling, 1775-1854）的关系是德国哲学史上最尖锐的个人对立之一。两人同在慕尼黑大学时期（1820-1830年代）发生直接冲突——叔本华故意将自己的课程安排在谢林授课的同时，但几乎没有学生来听他的课。这种个人敌意转化为深层的哲学分歧。

The relationship between Schopenhauer and Schelling (1775-1854) is one of the sharpest personal antagonisms in German philosophy. The two clashed directly during their overlapping time at the University of Munich (1820-1830s) — Schopenhauer deliberately scheduled his lectures at the same time as Schelling's, but almost no students came. This personal hostility translated into deep philosophical disagreement.

---

## 共同根源 / Common Roots

尽管后来激烈对立，叔本华和谢林共享某些哲学根源：

Despite later intense opposition, Schopenhauer and Schelling shared certain philosophical roots:

1. **康德的遗产**：两人都从康德出发，试图超越现象/物自体的区分
2. **对费希特的批判**：两人都拒绝费希特（Fichte）的"绝对自我"概念
3. **对黑格尔的敌意**：两人都强烈反对黑格尔的理性主义体系（虽然程度和原因不同）
4. **自然哲学的兴趣**：两人都试图将自然纳入哲学的核心

1. **Kant's legacy**: Both started from Kant, attempting to transcend the appearance/thing-in-itself distinction
2. **Critique of Fichte**: Both rejected Fichte's "absolute ego" concept
3. **Hostility to Hegel**: Both strongly opposed Hegel's rationalist system (though for different reasons and to different degrees)
4. **Interest in natural philosophy**: Both sought to place nature at philosophy's centre

## 核心分歧 / Core Disagreements

### 一、本体论：意志 vs 绝对 / Ontology: Will vs Absolute

| 维度 | 谢林 | 叔本华 |
|------|------|--------|
| 本体 | 绝对（das Absolute）：超越主体/客体对立的无差别同一 | 意志（Wille）：前理性的、盲目的欲求力量 |
| 自然 | 自然是绝对的精神化表达 | 自然是意志的客体化 |
| 理性 | 理性是绝对的自我展开 | 理性是意志的工具 |

| Dimension | Schelling | Schopenhauer |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Noumenon | The Absolute: indifferent identity transcending subject/object opposition | Will: pre-rational, blind striving force |
| Nature | Nature is the Absolute's spiritual expression | Nature is Will's objectification |
| Reason | Reason is the Absolute's self-unfolding | Reason is Will's instrument |

### 二、方法论：智识直观 vs 身体经验 / Method: Intellectual Intuition vs Bodily Experience

谢林认为，把握绝对需要**智识直观**（intellektuelle Anschauung）——一种超越感性和理性的直接认识能力。叔本华嘲笑这一概念为"哲学的魔术"——一种声称能直接看到超感性真理的神秘主义。

Schelling held that grasping the Absolute requires **intellectual intuition** (intellektuelle Anschauung) — a direct cognitive capacity transcending both sensibility and reason. Schopenhauer ridiculed this concept as "philosophical magic" — a mysticism claiming to directly see suprasensory truths.

叔本华提出的替代方案是：通过**身体的直接经验**来通达物自体——这是具体的、可验证的路径，而非谢林的"智识直观"那样的神秘宣称。

Schopenhauer's alternative: accessing the thing-in-itself through **direct bodily experience** — a concrete, verifiable path, not the mystical claim of Schelling's "intellectual intuition."

### 三、自由与恶 / Freedom and Evil

谢林在《论人类自由的本质》（1809）中提出了一个深刻的自由理论：自由是善与恶的选择能力，恶有其实在性。叔本华的自由理论完全不同：意志在本体层面是自由的，但在经验层面完全决定——恶不是自由选择的结果，而是性格的必然表达。

Schelling's *Philosophical Investigations into the Essence of Human Freedom* (1809) proposed a profound theory of freedom: freedom is the capacity to choose between good and evil, and evil has its own reality. Schopenhauer's freedom theory is entirely different: Will is free at the noumenal level but completely determined empirically — evil is not the result of free choice but the necessary expression of character.

## 互相评价 / Mutual Evaluation

### 叔本华对谢林 / Schopenhauer on Schelling

叔本华对谢林的评价极其苛刻：他认为谢林的哲学是"空洞的、浮夸的、故弄玄虚的"，是黑格尔主义的另一种变体。他特别厌恶谢林的"智识直观"概念和晚期的"实证哲学"（positive Philosophie）。

Schopenhauer's evaluation of Schelling was extremely harsh: he considered Schelling's philosophy "empty, pompous, and obscurantist" — another variant of Hegelianism. He particularly despised Schelling's "intellectual intuition" concept and his later "positive philosophy" (positive Philosophie).

### 谢林对叔本华 / Schelling on Schopenhauer

谢林对叔本华的评价相对沉默——他几乎从未公开评论过叔本华的哲学。这可能是因为：（1）叔本华在谢林生前还没有获得广泛的知名度；（2）谢林认为叔本华的哲学不值得认真回应。

Schelling's evaluation of Schopenhauer was relatively silent — he almost never publicly commented on Schopenhauer's philosophy. This may be because: (1) Schopenhauer had not yet achieved wide fame during Schelling's lifetime; (2) Schelling considered Schopenhauer's philosophy unworthy of serious response.

## 后世哲学史的评价 / Later Historiographical Evaluation

20世纪后期以来，哲学史家开始重新评价谢林与叔本华的关系：

Since the late 20th century, historians of philosophy have begun to reassess the Schelling-Schopenhauer relationship:

1. **结构性相似**：两人的哲学在结构上比表面上更相似——都试图超越康德的二元论，都强调非理性力量的重要性
2. **互补性**：谢林的自然哲学和叔本华的意志哲学可以被理解为对同一问题的不同回答
3. **被遮蔽的对话**：叔本华对谢林的个人敌意可能遮蔽了两者之间可能存在的有价值的哲学对话

1. **Structural similarity**: Their philosophies are structurally more similar than they appear — both attempt to transcend Kant's dualism, both emphasise the importance of non-rational forces
2. **Complementarity**: Schelling's natural philosophy and Schopenhauer's Will philosophy can be understood as different answers to the same questions
3. **Obscured dialogue**: Schopenhauer's personal hostility toward Schelling may have obscured valuable philosophical dialogue that could have existed between them

## 对MOCICI冥想的启示 / Implications for MOCICI Meditation

1. **智识直观 vs 直接认知**：谢林的"智识直观"声称超越感性/理性的直接认识——这与MOCICI的直接认知概念有表面相似性。但MOCICI强调直接认知是可训练的、可验证的，而非神秘的"哲学直觉"。
2. **自然哲学的冥想维度**：谢林的自然哲学——将自然视为精神的表达——可以为MOCICI提供一种"自然冥想"的哲学框架：在自然中直接感知意识的更深层结构。

1. **Intellectual intuition vs direct cognition**: Schelling's "intellectual intuition" claims a direct cognition transcending sensibility/reason — superficially similar to MOCICI's direct cognition concept. But MOCICI emphasises that direct cognition is trainable and verifiable, not a mystical "philosophical intuition."
2. **Meditative dimension of natural philosophy**: Schelling's natural philosophy — viewing nature as spirit's expression — can provide MOCICI with a philosophical framework for "natural meditation": directly perceiving deeper structures of consciousness in nature.
