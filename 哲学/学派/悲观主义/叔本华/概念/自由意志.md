# 自由意志 / Free Will

> "一个人可以做他所意愿的，但不能意愿他所意愿的。"
> "A person can do what he wills, but he cannot will what he wills."
> — Schopenhauer, *Preisschrift über die Freiheit des Willens* (1839)

---

## 概述 / Overview

叔本华对自由意志问题的处理是他哲学中最精妙的部分之一。他在1839年挪威皇家科学院的征文《论意志的自由》中系统阐述了这一立场：**经验层面完全决定论，本体层面完全自由**——这一看似矛盾的立场是叔本华意志形而上学的核心推论。

Schopenhauer's treatment of the free will problem is one of the most subtle parts of his philosophy. In his 1839 prize essay for the Royal Norwegian Society of Sciences, *On the Freedom of the Will*, he systematically articulated this position: **complete determinism at the empirical level, complete freedom at the noumenal level** — this apparently paradoxical position is a core implication of Schopenhauer's Will metaphysics.

---

## 对意志自由的论证 / Argument for Freedom of Will

叔本华区分了两个层面的自由问题：

Schopenhauer distinguished two levels of the freedom question:

### 一、意志本身的自由 / Freedom of Will Itself

**意志作为物自体是自由的**——因为它不受充足理由律的约束。充足理由律只适用于表象世界（现象界），而意志作为现象界的本体基础，站在充足理由律之外。因此，意志在其本体层面是绝对自由的。

**Will as thing-in-itself is free** — because it is not constrained by the principle of sufficient reason. The principle of sufficient reason applies only to the world of representation (phenomenal realm), while Will as the noumenal ground of the phenomenal realm stands outside the principle of sufficient reason. Therefore, Will at its noumenal level is absolutely free.

### 二、行为的自由 / Freedom of Action

**经验层面的行为是完全决定的**——给定一个人的性格和当前的动机，他的行为是严格必然的。没有人在相同处境下可以做出不同的行为——因为行为是性格（不可改变的）和动机（由外部决定的）的必然结果。

**Behaviour at the empirical level is completely determined** — given a person's character and present motives, his action is strictly necessary. No one in the same circumstances could have acted differently — because behaviour is the necessary result of character (unalterable) and motives (externally determined).

---

## "能做"与"能意愿" / "Can Do" and "Can Will"

叔本华的核心洞见是区分了两个层面的自由：

Schopenhauer's central insight is distinguishing two levels of freedom:

| 层面 | 自由？ | 原因 |
|------|--------|------|
| 行为层面："能做所愿" | 是 | 身体服从意志的命令（在物理条件允许范围内） |
| 意志层面："能愿所愿" | 否 | 意志本身由性格和动机决定，不受意志自身控制 |

| Level | Free? | Reason |
|-------|-------|--------|
| Action level: "can do what one wills" | Yes | Body obeys Will's commands (within physical constraints) |
| Will level: "can will what one wills" | No | Will itself is determined by character and motives, not under Will's own control |

这一区分解决了"自由意志"问题的表面矛盾：当人们问"我是否自由"时，他们实际上混淆了两个问题——行为的自由（是）和意志的自由（在本体层面是，在经验层面否）。

This distinction resolves the apparent contradiction in the "free will" problem: when people ask "am I free?", they are actually conflating two questions — freedom of action (yes) and freedom of will (yes at the noumenal level, no at the empirical level).

## 道德责任的根基 / Foundation of Moral Responsibility

如果经验层面的行为是完全决定的，道德责任如何可能？叔本华的回答是：

If behaviour at the empirical level is completely determined, how is moral responsibility possible? Schopenhauer's answer:

1. **道德责任不针对行为，而针对性格**——我们不是因为"做了某件事"而被赞扬或谴责，而是因为"是某种人"。
2. **性格是意志的本体层面的选择**——在超时间的层面（ausser der Zeit），每个个体"选择"了自己的性格（即意志的特定客体化形式）。
3. **良心（Gewissen）证实了这一点**——我们内心深处的道德意识不是在评价我们的行为，而是在评价我们的存在（Sein）本身。

1. **Moral responsibility targets not actions but character** — we are praised or blamed not for "doing something" but for "being a certain kind of person."
2. **Character is a noumenal-level "choice" of Will** — at a supratemporal level (ausser der Zeit), each individual "chooses" his character (i.e., the specific form of Will's objectification).
3. **Conscience (Gewissen) confirms this** — our deep moral consciousness evaluates not our actions but our very being (Sein).

## 与康德的对比 / Comparison with Kant

叔本华的自由意志理论是对康德立场的改造：

Schopenhauer's free will theory is an adaptation of Kant's position:

| 维度 | 康德 | 叔本华 |
|------|------|--------|
| 自由的主体 | 理性主体（纯粹实践理性） | 意志（作为物自体） |
| 自由的表现 | 遵循道德法则（自律） | 超越充足理由律 |
| 经验决定论 | 现象界受因果律支配 | 表象世界受充足理由律支配 |
| 道德法则来源 | 理性的先验立法 | 同情的直观认识 |

| Dimension | Kant | Schopenhauer |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| Subject of freedom | Rational subject (pure practical reason) | Will (as thing-in-itself) |
| Expression of freedom | Following moral law (autonomy) | Transcending principle of sufficient reason |
| Empirical determinism | Phenomenal realm governed by causal law | Representation governed by principle of sufficient reason |
| Source of moral law | Reason's a priori legislation | Compassion's intuitive knowledge |

## 与唯识学的对话 / Dialogue with Yogācāra

### 业力与自由 / Karma and Freedom

叔本华的自由意志理论与佛教的**业力**（karma）学说形成有趣的对话：

Schopenhauer's free will theory enters an interesting dialogue with Buddhist **karma** doctrine:

- 两者都承认经验层面的决定论（业力决定/性格决定）
- 两者都保留了某种超越层面的自由（涅槃可能性/本体意志自由）
- 关键差异：佛教的自由是通过修行**获得的**（有为法），叔本华的自由是本体论上**既有的**（无为的）

- Both acknowledge determinism at the empirical level (karmic determination / character determination)
- Both preserve some form of transcendental freedom (possibility of Nirvana / noumenal Will's freedom)
- Key difference: Buddhist freedom is **acquired** through practice (saṃskṛta-dharma), Schopenhauer's freedom is ontologically **given** (asaṃskṛta)

### 无我与意志自由 / No-Self and Will's Freedom

更深层的张力在于：叔本华保留了"意志"作为自由的主体，而佛教的无我（anātman）学说否认有任何固定的主体。在唯识学中，"谁"获得自由这个问题本身就是错误的——只有识的流转（vijñāna-santāna），没有一个固定的"意志"可以被解放。

A deeper tension lies in: Schopenhauer retains "Will" as the subject of freedom, while Buddhist no-self (anātman) doctrine denies any fixed subject. In Yogācāra, the question "who" attains freedom is itself misguided — there is only the stream of consciousness (vijñāna-santāna), no fixed "Will" that can be liberated.

## 对MOCICI冥想的启示 / Implications for MOCICI Meditation

1. **"能做"与"能意愿"的区分**：在冥想实践中，这一区分转化为："我能选择做什么"（表面自由）vs "我能选择想要什么"（深层不自由）。MOCICI的直接认知训练帮助练习者觉察这种深层的不自由——看到意志如何在意识层面之下运作。
2. **自由作为一种认知成就**：叔本华的本体自由是"既有的"（ontological given），但MOCICI将其重新理解为一种**认知成就**——通过直接认知的训练，逐步觉察和转化深层的欲求模式。
3. **从"意志自由"到"从意志中自由"**：叔本华最终的立场是，真正的自由不是"自由地意愿"，而是"从意愿中解脱"——这与MOCICI的冥想目标高度一致。

1. **"Can do" vs "can will" distinction**: In meditation practice, this translates to: "I can choose what to do" (surface freedom) vs "I can choose what to want" (deep unfreedom). MOCICI's direct cognition training helps practitioners notice this deep unfreedom — seeing how Will operates below the level of consciousness.
2. **Freedom as cognitive achievement**: Schopenhauer's noumenal freedom is an "ontological given," but MOCICI reconceptualises it as a **cognitive achievement** — through direct cognition training, gradually noticing and transforming deep desire patterns.
3. **From "freedom of will" to "freedom from will"**: Schopenhauer's ultimate position is that true freedom is not "freely willing" but "liberation from willing" — highly aligned with MOCICI's meditation goals.
