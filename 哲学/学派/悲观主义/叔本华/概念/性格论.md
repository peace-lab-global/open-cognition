# 性格论 / Theory of Character

> "性格是不可改变的；一个人在一生中只能是他所是的那个人。"
> "Character is unalterable; a person can only be who he is throughout his whole life."
> — Schopenhauer, *Die Welt als Wille und Vorstellung*, Book IV, §55

---

## 概述 / Overview

叔本华的性格论是其意志形而上学在伦理学和心理学层面的重要应用。他认为，每个人的性格（Charakter）是**先天给定的、不可改变的**——它是意志在个体中的特定客体化形式。这一学说调和了表面的行为多样性与深层的性格统一性，同时为叔本华的道德评价体系提供了基础。

Schopenhauer's theory of character is an important application of his Will metaphysics to ethics and psychology. He holds that each person's character (Charakter) is **innately given and unalterable** — it is the specific form of the Will's objectification in the individual. This doctrine reconciles surface behavioural diversity with deep character unity, while providing the foundation for Schopenhauer's moral evaluation system.

---

## 三种性格 / Three Kinds of Character

### 悟知性格（intelligibler Charakter）/ Intelligible Character

悟知性格是意志在个体中的**本体层面的规定**——它是个体意志的原始方向和强度。悟知性格不在时空之中，不受因果律支配，因此是**不可改变的**。它是个体一切行为的终极来源。

The intelligible character is the **noumenal determination** of the Will in the individual — the original direction and intensity of the individual's Will. The intelligible character is not in space and time, not subject to causal law, and therefore **unalterable**. It is the ultimate source of all the individual's actions.

叔本华论证：一个人的悟知性格在他出生之前就已经被决定了（作为意志的本体层面的一部分），他的一生只是这个性格在时间和空间中的展开。

Schopenhauer argues: a person's intelligible character was determined before birth (as part of Will's noumenal dimension), and his entire life is merely the unfolding of this character in time and space.

### 验知性格（empirischer Charakter）/ Empirical Character

验知性格是悟知性格在经验世界中的**显现**——即我们在日常生活中观察到的行为模式、习惯和倾向。验知性格受因果律支配，因此在原则上是可预测的：如果我们完全了解一个人的性格和全部处境，我们就能准确预测他的行为。

The empirical character is the **manifestation** of the intelligible character in the empirical world — the behavioural patterns, habits, and tendencies we observe in everyday life. The empirical character is subject to causal law and therefore in principle predictable: if we fully knew a person's character and complete circumstances, we could accurately predict his behaviour.

### 获得的性格（erworbener Charakter）/ Acquired Character

获得的性格不是改变先天性格，而是**认识自己的性格**并据此安排生活的能力。一个拥有"获得的性格"的人清楚地知道自己能做什么、不能做什么，并据此选择适合自己性格的生活方式和职业。

The acquired character is not about changing one's innate character but about **knowing one's character** and arranging one's life accordingly. A person with "acquired character" clearly knows what he can and cannot do, and chooses a way of life and vocation suited to his character.

叔本华认为这是人生智慧的重要组成部分：大多数人之所以不幸，是因为他们试图成为自己不是的人——试图改变不可改变的性格。

Schopenhauer considers this an important component of the wisdom of life: most people are unhappy because they try to become what they are not — attempting to change an unchangeable character.

## 性格与行为的关系 / Character and Behaviour

叔本华用一个精妙的公式来描述性格与行为的关系：

Schopenhauer uses an elegant formula to describe the relationship between character and behaviour:

```
行为 = f（性格 × 动机）
Behaviour = f(Character × Motive)
```

同一个人面对不同的动机会做出不同的行为，但他的**性格**在所有行为中保持不变。性格不变性意味着：一个人在相同处境和相同动机下，必然做出相同的行为——这就是叔本华所说的"行为的严格必然性"。

The same person will behave differently when facing different motives, but his **character** remains unchanged across all actions. Character's immutability means: a person in the same circumstances with the same motives will necessarily perform the same action — this is what Schopenhauer calls the "strict necessity of action."

| 层面 | 变化的 | 不变的 |
|------|--------|--------|
| 行为 | 随动机变化 | 受性格约束 |
| 性格 | ——（不可改变） | 意志的特定客体化 |
| 意志 | ——（本体层面） | 一切行为的终极来源 |

| Level | Variable | Constant |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Behaviour | Changes with motives | Constrained by character |
| Character | — (unalterable) | Specific objectification of Will |
| Will | — (noumenal level) | Ultimate source of all actions |

## 性格的道德评价 / Moral Evaluation of Character

叔本华的道德评价不是针对个别行为，而是针对**性格本身**——即意志在个体中的本体层面的品质。

Schopenhauer's moral evaluation targets not individual actions but **character itself** — the quality of the Will's noumenal dimension in the individual.

### 善的性格 / Good Character

善的性格表现为**较弱的个体化意识**——即更多地穿透个体化原理，对他者的痛苦有更强的同情能力。善的人在直觉中认识到他者与自我在本体上的同一性。

Good character manifests as **weaker individuation consciousness** — greater penetration of the principium individuationis, with stronger capacity for compassion toward others' suffering. The good person intuitively recognises the ontological identity of other and self.

### 恶的性格 / Evil Character

恶的性格表现为**极强的个体化意识**——即完全被个体化原理所束缚，将他者视为与自我根本不同的存在。恶意（Bosheit）甚至追求他者的痛苦——这是个体化意识极端化的表现。

Evil character manifests as **extremely strong individuation consciousness** — completely bound by the principium individuationis, regarding others as fundamentally different from self. Malice (Bosheit) even pursues others' suffering — an extreme manifestation of individuation consciousness.

## 与唯识学的对话 / Dialogue with Yogācāra

### 性格与种子（bīja）/ Character and Seeds

叔本华的悟知性格概念与唯识学的**种子**（bīja）理论存在有趣的平行：两者都指向一种先于经验的、决定行为倾向的深层结构。但关键差异在于：

An interesting parallel exists between Schopenhauer's intelligible character and Yogācāra's **seed** (bīja) theory: both point to a deep structure prior to experience that determines behavioural tendencies. But the key difference lies in:

- 悟知性格是**不可改变的**（once and for all given）
- 种子是**可熏习的**（通过新的经验不断改变——"种子生现行，现行熏种子"）

- Intelligible character is **unalterable** (once and for all given)
- Seeds are **perfumable** (continuously modified through new experience — "seeds produce manifest dharmas, manifest dharmas perfume seeds")

这一差异反映了两种截然不同的解脱观：叔本华认为解脱只能来自意志的自发否定（性格不变），唯识学认为通过系统的修行（转依 āśraya-parāvṛtti）可以逐步转化深层结构。

This difference reflects two fundamentally different views of liberation: Schopenhauer holds that liberation can only come from the Will's spontaneous self-negation (character is unchangeable), while Yogācāra holds that through systematic practice (transformation of the basis, āśraya-parāvṛtti) one can gradually transform the deep structure.

## 批评 / Criticisms

1. **与道德责任的张力**：如果性格不可改变，个体如何对自己的行为承担道德责任？叔本华的回应是：道德责任不是针对个别行为，而是针对"成为这种人"的本体层面的意志行为。
2. **经验反例**：发展心理学和神经科学表明，性格在生命周期中确实会发生变化——这与叔本华的不可改变论断相矛盾。
3. **获得的性格的模糊性**：如果性格不可改变，"获得的性格"概念如何可能？叔本华似乎在这里引入了一个不协调的元素。

1. **Tension with moral responsibility**: If character is unalterable, how can individuals bear moral responsibility for their actions? Schopenhauer's response: moral responsibility targets not individual actions but the noumenal-level act of Will in "becoming this kind of person."
2. **Empirical counterexamples**: Developmental psychology and neuroscience show that character does change across the lifespan — contradicting Schopenhauer's immutability thesis.
3. **Ambiguity of acquired character**: If character is unalterable, how is the concept of "acquired character" possible? Schopenhauer seems to introduce an inconsistent element here.

## 对MOCICI冥想的启示 / Implications for MOCICI Meditation

1. **性格认知与自我认识**：叔本华"获得的性格"概念提示冥想实践的一个重要维度——不是改变性格，而是**认识性格**。MOCICI的直接认知训练可以帮助练习者更清晰地"看到"自己的性格模式。
2. **种子可熏 vs 性格不可变**：唯识学的种子可熏理论为MOCICI提供了一个更乐观的框架——通过系统的冥想训练，深层的认知结构是可以被转化的。
3. **同情能力的训练**：叔本华认为善的性格基于同情能力，但同情能力的强弱是先天的。MOCICI可以通过直接认知的训练来增强同情的直观能力。

1. **Character knowledge and self-knowledge**: Schopenhauer's "acquired character" concept highlights an important dimension of meditation practice — not changing character but **knowing character**. MOCICI's direct cognition training can help practitioners more clearly "see" their character patterns.
2. **Perfuming seeds vs unchangeable character**: Yogācāra's perfumable seeds theory provides MOCICI with a more optimistic framework — deep cognitive structures can be transformed through systematic meditation training.
3. **Training compassion capacity**: Schopenhauer holds that good character is based on compassion capacity, but its strength is innate. MOCICI can enhance the intuitive capacity for compassion through direct cognition training.
