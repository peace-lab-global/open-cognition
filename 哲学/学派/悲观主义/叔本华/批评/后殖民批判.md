# 后殖民批判 / Postcolonial Critique

> "在《奥义书》中，我们找到了几乎每一页都有最深刻智慧的书……它是世界上最有益也最崇高的阅读。"
> "In the Upanishads we find a book that is almost on every page of the deepest wisdom… it is the most beneficial and elevating reading in the world."
> — Schopenhauer, *Die Welt als Wille und Vorstellung*, Vol. II, Ch. 1

---

## 概述 / Overview

叔本华是19世纪欧洲最早认真对待印度哲学的西方思想家之一。他公开宣称印度经典（尤其是《奥义书》和《薄伽梵歌》）对他哲学的影响，并将其与自己的意志形而上学进行比较。后殖民批评对这种跨文化借用的**方式**、**准确性**和**权力关系**提出了质疑。

Schopenhauer was one of the earliest 19th-century European thinkers to seriously engage with Indian philosophy. He openly acknowledged the influence of Indian classics (especially the Upanishads and the Bhagavad Gītā) on his philosophy, comparing them with his own Will metaphysics. Postcolonial critique questions the **manner**, **accuracy**, and **power relations** of this cross-cultural appropriation.

---

## 叔本华与印度哲学 / Schopenhauer and Indian Philosophy

### 接触史 / History of Engagement

- **1813-14年**：通过弗里德里希·迈耶尔（Friedrich Majer）首次接触印度思想
- **1814年**：阅读安奎蒂尔-杜佩龙（Anquetil-Duperron）的拉丁文译本 *Oupnek'hat*（波斯语→拉丁语的《奥义书》译本）
- **持续一生**：《奥义书》成为叔本华的枕边书，他每天阅读

- **1813-14**: First exposure to Indian thought through Friedrich Majer
- **1814**: Read Anquetil-Duperron's Latin translation *Oupnek'hat* (Upanishads translated from Persian to Latin)
- **Throughout life**: The Upanishads became Schopenhauer's bedside book, read daily

### 叔本华的挪用方式 / Schopenhauer's Mode of Appropriation

叔本华引用印度文本的方式有几个显著特征：

Schopenhauer's manner of citing Indian texts has several notable features:

1. **确认性引用**：叔本华不是在"学习"印度哲学，而是在用印度文本**确认**自己的哲学——他已经独立地得出了这些结论，印度文本只是"印证"。
2. **选择性阅读**：叔本华只关注与自己的哲学相吻合的印度概念（摩耶、涅槃、梵我一如），忽略了不符合的部分（如印度哲学中的行动哲学、社会伦理）。
3. **去语境化**：叔本华将印度概念从其原有的哲学和宗教语境中抽离，重新整合进自己的意志形而上学体系。

1. **Confirmatory citation**: Schopenhauer was not "learning" from Indian philosophy but using Indian texts to **confirm** his own philosophy — he had independently reached these conclusions, and Indian texts merely "corroborated" them.
2. **Selective reading**: Schopenhauer attended only to Indian concepts consistent with his philosophy (māyā, nirvana, Brahman-Ātman identity), ignoring inconsistent parts (such as Indian action philosophy, social ethics).
3. **Decontextualisation**: Schopenhauer extracted Indian concepts from their original philosophical and religious contexts, re-integrating them into his own Will metaphysics.

## 后殖民批评的核心论点 / Core Arguments of Postcolonial Critique

### 一、东方主义（Orientalism）/ Orientalism

爱德华·赛义德（Edward Said）的东方主义批评框架适用于叔本华：他将"东方"建构为一个**静态的、神秘的、否定世界的**他者——这与19世纪殖民话语中对印度的浪漫化想象一致。叔本华的"东方"是一个哲学想象，而非对印度实际思想传统的准确反映。

Edward Said's Orientalism critique framework applies to Schopenhauer: he constructs the "East" as a **static, mystical, world-negating** Other — consistent with 19th-century colonial discourse's romanticisation of India. Schopenhauer's "East" is a philosophical imagination, not an accurate reflection of India's actual intellectual traditions.

### 二、哲学挪用（Philosophical Appropriation）/ Philosophical Appropriation

叔本华对印度哲学的挪用可以被视为一种**知识殖民**的形式：

Schopenhauer's appropriation of Indian philosophy can be seen as a form of **epistemic colonialism**:

- 将印度思想简化为符合西方哲学框架的版本
- 剥夺了印度哲学的**多元性**（印度哲学远不止吠檀多一元论）
- 将印度哲学定位为西方哲学的"原始前驱"——暗示西方哲学（叔本华自己的体系）是印度思想的"成熟版本"

- Simplifying Indian thought into versions compatible with Western philosophical frameworks
- Stripping Indian philosophy of its **diversity** (Indian philosophy extends far beyond Vedānta monism)
- Positioning Indian philosophy as a "primitive precursor" to Western philosophy — implying that Western philosophy (Schopenhauer's own system) is the "mature version" of Indian thought

### 三、翻译的政治 / Politics of Translation

叔本华阅读的印度文本经过了**双重翻译**（梵文→波斯语→拉丁语），每一次翻译都涉及文化过滤和概念转换。后殖民批评指出，叔本华所接触的"印度哲学"已经是经过殖民知识体系过滤的产物。

The Indian texts Schopenhauer read underwent **double translation** (Sanskrit → Persian → Latin), each involving cultural filtering and conceptual transformation. Postcolonial critique notes that the "Indian philosophy" Schopenhauer encountered was already a product filtered through colonial knowledge systems.

## 辩护与回应 / Defences and Responses

### 一、跨文化哲学的先驱 / Pioneer of Cross-Cultural Philosophy

支持者论证，叔本华在19世纪欧洲的哲学语境中是**最开放**对待非西方思想的哲学家——他的态度远比同时代的黑格尔（将非洲和亚洲排除在"哲学"之外）进步。

Supporters argue that Schopenhauer was **the most open** to non-Western thought among 19th-century European philosophers — his attitude was far more progressive than his contemporary Hegel (who excluded Africa and Asia from "philosophy").

### 二、真实的哲学共鸣 / Genuine Philosophical Resonance

叔本华与印度哲学之间的相似性可能不是"挪用"的结果，而是**独立的哲学发现**——两种不同的思想传统各自独立地发现了某些关于人类存在的深层真理。

The similarities between Schopenhauer and Indian philosophy may not be the result of "appropriation" but **independent philosophical discoveries** — two different intellectual traditions each independently discovering deep truths about human existence.

## 对MOCICI冥想的启示 / Implications for MOCICI Meditation

1. **尊重性跨文化对话**：MOCICI在与佛教和印度哲学对话时，必须避免叔本华式的"确认性挪用"——即只选择符合自己框架的元素。应当以开放的态度面对差异和不符合之处。
2. **去语境化的风险**：将冥想技术从其宗教和文化语境中抽离（如将正念从佛教八正道中分离）可能复制叔本华的挪用模式。MOCICI需要自觉反思这一问题。
3. **多元性的承认**：MOCICI应当承认佛教和印度哲学内部的**多元性**——不存在单一的"东方智慧"，而是多种不同的、有时相互矛盾的思想传统。

1. **Respectful cross-cultural dialogue**: MOCICI must avoid Schopenhauer-style "confirmatory appropriation" when dialoguing with Buddhist and Indian philosophy — selecting only elements that fit one's framework. One should approach differences and inconsistencies with openness.
2. **Risk of decontextualisation**: Extracting meditation techniques from their religious and cultural contexts (such as separating mindfulness from the Buddhist Eightfold Path) may replicate Schopenhauer's appropriation pattern. MOCICI needs to self-consciously reflect on this issue.
3. **Acknowledging diversity**: MOCICI should acknowledge the **diversity** within Buddhist and Indian philosophy — there is no single "Eastern wisdom," but multiple different, sometimes contradictory, intellectual traditions.
