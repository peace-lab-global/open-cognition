# 尼采对叔本华的批判 / Nietzsche's Critique of Schopenhauer

> "叔本华是伟大的，但他的否定意志是一种颓废。"
> "Schopenhauer is great, but his denial of the Will is a form of décadence."
> — Nietzsche (paraphrase), *Zur Genealogie der Moral*, III

---

## 概述 / Overview

尼采（Friedrich Nietzsche, 1844–1900）与叔本华的关系是西方哲学史上最具戏剧性的思想传承之一——从虔诚的追随者到最深刻的批判者。尼采早期（1868–1876）是叔本华的热烈拥护者，《悲剧的诞生》（1872）和《作为教育家的叔本华》（1874）直接受其影响。但成熟期的尼采（1878–1888）对叔本华进行了系统的、毁灭性的批判，以**权力意志**（Wille zur Macht）取代了叔本华的**生存意志**（Wille zum Leben），以**生命肯定**（Lebensbejahung）取代了**意志否定**（Verneinung des Willens）。

The relationship between Nietzsche (1844–1900) and Schopenhauer is one of the most dramatic intellectual transmissions in the history of Western philosophy — from devoted follower to most profound critic. The young Nietzsche (1868–1876) was an ardent admirer of Schopenhauer; *The Birth of Tragedy* (1872) and *Schopenhauer as Educator* (1874) were directly influenced. But the mature Nietzsche (1878–1888) launched a systematic and devastating critique, replacing Schopenhauer's **will-to-live** (Wille zum Leben) with **will to power** (Wille zur Macht), and **life-affirmation** (Lebensbejahung) with **denial of Will** (Verneinung des Willens).

---

## 早期：叔本华的追随者 / Early Period: A Follower of Schopenhauer

### 1865年的"发现" / The "Discovery" of 1865

1865年，21岁的尼采在莱比锡一家旧书店偶然发现了《作为意志和表象的世界》。据他自述，他翻开第一页就感到"一种不可名状的力量"在向他说话。此后数年，叔本华成为他最重要的哲学导师。

In 1865, the 21-year-old Nietzsche stumbled upon *The World as Will and Representation* in a second-hand bookshop in Leipzig. By his own account, from the first page he felt "an indescribable force" speaking to him. For several years thereafter, Schopenhauer was his most important philosophical mentor.

### 《悲剧的诞生》中的叔本华影响 / Schopenhauer's Influence in *The Birth of Tragedy*

《悲剧的诞生》（1872）中的核心概念直接源自叔本华：

The core concepts in *The Birth of Tragedy* (1872) derive directly from Schopenhauer:

| 尼采概念 | 叔本华来源 |
|----------|-----------|
| 酒神精神（Dionysian） | 意志——非理性的、原始的、痛苦的生命力量 |
| 日神精神（Apollonian） | 表象——美的、有序的、个体化的幻象 |
| 悲剧的本质 | 通过审美沉思暂时从意志的痛苦中解脱 |
| 音乐的地位 | 音乐作为"意志本身的直接写照" |

| Nietzsche's Concept | Schopenhauer's Source |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| Dionysian | Will — irrational, primordial, painful life force |
| Apollonian | Representation — beautiful, ordered, individuated illusion |
| Essence of tragedy | Temporary liberation from Will's suffering through aesthetic contemplation |
| Status of music | Music as "direct copy of the Will itself" |

### 《作为教育家的叔本华》/ *Schopenhauer as Educator* (1874)

在这篇《不合时宜的沉思》第三篇中，尼采将叔本华描绘为**哲学家的典范**——一个敢于直面生命痛苦、不被学院哲学和大众文化所收买的真正思想家。尼采认为叔本华的真诚和勇气使他成为"文化医生"（Arzt der Cultur）。

In the third *Untimely Meditation*, Nietzsche portrays Schopenhauer as the **exemplar of the philosopher** — a true thinker who dares to confront life's suffering without being co-opted by academic philosophy or mass culture. Nietzsche considers Schopenhauer's honesty and courage make him a "physician of culture" (Arzt der Cultur).

## 断裂：从追随到批判 / The Break: From Following to Critiquing

### 1876年拜罗伊特音乐节的幻灭 / The Disillusionment at Bayreuth (1876)

1876年，尼采参加了首届拜罗伊特音乐节（Bayreuth Festival），见证了瓦格纳《尼伯龙根的指环》的首演。这次经历成为尼采思想转向的催化剂：他看到瓦格纳从叔本华式的悲观主义转向了基督教式的救赎叙事，感到深深的失望。

In 1876, Nietzsche attended the inaugural Bayreuth Festival and witnessed the premiere of Wagner's *Ring Cycle*. This experience catalysed his intellectual turn: he saw Wagner shifting from Schopenhauerian pessimism to a Christian narrative of redemption, and felt profound disappointment.

### 《人性的，太人性的》（1878）/ *Human, All Too Human* (1878)

这本书标志着尼采与叔本华-瓦格纳传统的正式决裂。尼采开始用**心理学分析**取代叔本华的**形而上学解释**——不再问"意志的本质是什么"，而是问"是什么心理驱力使哲学家建构出'意志'这个概念？"

This book marks Nietzsche's formal break with the Schopenhauer-Wagner tradition. Nietzsche begins replacing Schopenhauer's **metaphysical explanations** with **psychological analysis** — no longer asking "what is the essence of Will?" but "what psychological drives lead a philosopher to construct the concept of 'Will'?"

## 成熟期的系统批判 / The Mature Systematic Critique

### 批判一：从"生存意志"到"权力意志" / Critique 1: From "Will-to-Live" to "Will to Power"

这是尼采对叔本华最根本的批判。叔本华将意志定义为"生存意志"（Wille zum Leben）——盲目的、无目的的、追求生存的欲求。尼采将其替换为**权力意志**（Wille zur Macht）——追求力量增长、自我超越、创造的欲求。

This is Nietzsche's most fundamental critique of Schopenhauer. Schopenhauer defines Will as "will-to-live" (Wille zum Leben) — blind, purposeless striving for survival. Nietzsche replaces it with **will to power** (Wille zur Macht) — striving for increase of power, self-overcoming, and creation.

| 维度 | 叔本华：生存意志 | 尼采：权力意志 |
|------|-----------------|---------------|
| 本质 | 求生存（survival） | 求权力（power） |
| 方向 | 保守的、重复的 | 扩张的、创造的 |
| 价值评价 | 消极的（应被否定） | 积极的（应被肯定） |
| 对痛苦的态度 | 痛苦应被消除 | 痛苦是力量增长的条件 |
| 对生命的态度 | 否定（解脱） | 肯定（永恒轮回） |

| Dimension | Schopenhauer: Will-to-Live | Nietzsche: Will to Power |
|-----------|---------------------------|--------------------------|
| Essence | Striving for survival | Striving for power |
| Direction | Conservative, repetitive | Expansive, creative |
| Value evaluation | Negative (should be denied) | Positive (should be affirmed) |
| Attitude to suffering | Suffering should be eliminated | Suffering is the condition for growth of power |
| Attitude to life | Denial (liberation) | Affirmation (eternal recurrence) |

尼采论证：叔本华的"生存意志"是对生命力量的一种**弱化描述**——真实的生命不是追求"活着"，而是追求"更强"。一棵树不只是想活着（生存），它想长得更高、更强、占据更多阳光（权力）。

Nietzsche argues: Schopenhauer's "will-to-live" is a **weakened description** of life force — real life does not strive to "live" but to "be stronger." A tree does not merely want to survive; it wants to grow taller, stronger, to occupy more sunlight (power).

### 批判二：悲观主义作为颓废 / Critique 2: Pessimism as Décadence

尼采将叔本华的悲观主义诊断为**颓废**（décadence）的症状——一种生命力衰退的标志。

Nietzsche diagnoses Schopenhauer's pessimism as a symptom of **décadence** — a sign of declining life force.

尼采的论证：

Nietzsche's argument:

1. **悲观主义是虚弱的标志**：只有虚弱的生命才会将痛苦视为"根本性的"——强大的生命将痛苦视为挑战和成长的机会
2. **悲观主义是怨恨的表达**：叔本华的悲观主义隐含了对生命的**怨恨**（ressentiment）——因为无法承受生命的痛苦，所以否定生命本身
3. **悲观主义是虚无主义的前奏**：否定意志→否定生命→否定一切价值→虚无主义（Nihilismus）

1. **Pessimism is a sign of weakness**: Only weak life considers suffering "fundamental" — strong life considers suffering a challenge and an opportunity for growth
2. **Pessimism is an expression of resentment**: Schopenhauer's pessimism contains an implicit **resentment** (ressentiment) against life — because he cannot endure life's suffering, he denies life itself
3. **Pessimism is a prelude to nihilism**: Denial of Will → denial of life → denial of all values → nihilism (Nihilismus)

### 批判三：禁欲理想的意义 / Critique 3: The Meaning of Ascetic Ideals

在《道德的谱系》（1887）第三篇"禁欲理想意味着什么？"中，尼采对叔本华的禁欲主义进行了最系统的分析。

In the third essay of *On the Genealogy of Morals* (1887), "What do Ascetic Ideals Mean?", Nietzsche offers his most systematic analysis of Schopenhauer's asceticism.

尼采论证：禁欲理想不是"解脱"，而是**意志的另一种表达方式**——一种虚弱的意志通过否定自身来获得权力感的方式。

Nietzsche argues: ascetic ideals are not "liberation" but **another mode of the Will's expression** — a way for weak Will to gain a sense of power by denying itself.

```
叔本华的叙事：  痛苦 → 洞察 → 意志否定 → 解脱（积极）
尼采的诊断：    虚弱 → 怨恨 → 禁欲理想 → 自我欺骗（消极）

Schopenhauer's narrative: Suffering → Insight → Denial of Will → Liberation (positive)
Nietzsche's diagnosis:    Weakness → Resentment → Ascetic ideal → Self-deception (negative)
```

### 批判四：同情伦理的批判 / Critique 4: Critique of Compassion Ethics

尼采对叔本华的同情伦理（Mitleid）进行了激烈的批判：

Nietzsche launches a fierce critique of Schopenhauer's compassion ethics (Mitleid):

1. **同情是虚弱的表达**：同情不是道德的成就，而是无力区分自我与他者的标志
2. **同情阻碍伟大**：同情使我们将注意力集中在受苦者身上，而非创造者和超越者身上
3. **同情是群体道德**：同情伦理本质上是"奴隶道德"（Sklavenmoral）——弱者们联合起来反对强者的工具

1. **Compassion is an expression of weakness**: compassion is not a moral achievement but a sign of inability to distinguish self from other
2. **Compassion obstructs greatness**: compassion directs our attention toward sufferers rather than toward creators and overcomers
3. **Compassion is herd morality**: compassion ethics is essentially "slave morality" (Sklavenmoral) — the weak uniting against the strong

### 批判五：东方哲学的影响 / Critique 5: The Influence of Eastern Philosophy

尼采批评叔本华对东方哲学（尤其是佛教和吠檀多）的接纳是一种**欧洲精神的退化**——一种对基督教禁欲理想的世俗化延续。在尼采看来，叔本华用印度的"涅槃"取代了基督教的"天堂"，但本质上仍然是同一种否定生命的理想。

Nietzsche criticises Schopenhauer's embrace of Eastern philosophy (especially Buddhism and Vedānta) as a **regression of the European spirit** — a secularised continuation of Christian ascetic ideals. In Nietzsche's view, Schopenhauer replaced the Christian "heaven" with the Indian "Nirvana," but essentially maintained the same life-denying ideal.

## 尼采的正面替代方案 / Nietzsche's Positive Alternative

### 永恒轮回（Ewige Wiederkehr）/ Eternal Recurrence

与叔本华的"痛苦与无聊的钟摆"相对，尼采提出了**永恒轮回**的思想实验：

Against Schopenhauer's "pendulum of pain and boredom," Nietzsche proposes the thought experiment of **eternal recurrence**:

> "如果有人告诉你，你将无限次地重复你现在的生活——同样的痛苦、同样的快乐、同样的无聊——你会感到绝望，还是会感到狂喜？"
> "If someone told you that you would live this life again and again, infinitely — the same pain, the same pleasure, the same boredom — would you despair or rejoice?"

尼采的理想是：**肯定生命的一切**——包括痛苦——以至于愿意无限次地重复它。这是叔本华悲观主义的彻底颠倒。

Nietzsche's ideal: **affirm everything in life** — including suffering — to the point of being willing to repeat it infinitely. This is the thorough inversion of Schopenhauer's pessimism.

### 超人（Übermensch）/ The Overman

与叔本华的"圣人"（否定意志者）相对，尼采提出了**超人**——一个完全肯定生命、创造新价值、超越一切既定道德的存在者。

Against Schopenhauer's "saint" (one who denies Will), Nietzsche proposes the **overman** — a being who fully affirms life, creates new values, and transcends all established morality.

```
叔本华的理想：圣人 → 否定意志 → 宁静
尼采的理想：  超人 → 肯定生命 → 创造

Schopenhauer's ideal: Saint → Deny Will → Tranquillity
Nietzsche's ideal:    Overman → Affirm Life → Creation
```

## 对批判的评价 / Evaluation of the Critique

### 尼采批判的力量 / Strengths of Nietzsche's Critique

1. **对悲观主义心理学根源的揭示**：尼采正确地指出悲观主义可能有其心理学的（而非纯粹理性的）根源
2. **对生命肯定的辩护**：尼采对生命力量的肯定是叔本华体系中缺乏的维度
3. **对禁欲理想的解构**：尼采对禁欲理想的谱系学分析至今仍是经典

### 尼采批判的局限 / Limitations of Nietzsche's Critique

1. **对痛苦的浪漫化**：尼采将痛苦浪漫化为"力量增长的条件"——这忽视了某些痛苦（如极端贫困、疾病、战争创伤）并不导致成长
2. **对同情的低估**：尼采对同情的批评忽视了同情在人类进化和道德发展中的积极作用
3. **对虚无主义的不充分回应**：尼采的"永恒轮回"和"超人"概念在哲学上的说服力不如他对叔本华的批判

## 与 MOCICI 冥想的对话 / Dialogue with MOCICI Meditation

尼采对叔本华的批判对 MOCICI 冥想提供了一个重要的**辩证视角**：

Nietzsche's critique of Schopenhauer offers an important **dialectical perspective** for MOCICI meditation:

1. **警惕否定性偏见**：叔本华的解脱论可能被误读为"否定一切"——冥想不是否定生命，而是**转化认知**。唯识学的"转识成智"比叔本华的"意志否定"更接近尼采的"生命肯定"——不是消灭，而是转化

2. **痛苦的辩证法**：冥想不追求消除痛苦（叔本华），也不浪漫化痛苦（尼采），而是**直接认知痛苦的本质**——在现量（pratyakṣa）中，痛苦既不是"应被消除的恶"，也不是"应被肯定的力"，而是缘起的、无常的现象

3. **同情的再定位**：尼采批评同情是"虚弱"——但佛教的慈悲（karuṇā）不是被动的共感，而是**积极的力量**。在冥想中培养的慈悲是一种认知成就（穿透无我），而非情感软弱
