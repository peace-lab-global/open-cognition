# 萨特与梅洛-庞蒂的关系 / Sartre and Merleau-Ponty: Fellowship and Fracture

> "萨特是我最亲近的思想对手——我们之间的分歧恰恰证明了我们之间对话的深度。"
> — 梅洛-庞蒂对萨特的一般评价（概述）

---

## 概述 / Overview

莫里斯·梅洛-庞蒂（Maurice Merleau-Ponty, 1908–1961）与萨特的关系是法国现象学运动内部最重要的思想对话。两人年龄相近（相差三岁），同在1930年代接触胡塞尔和海德格尔的现象学，同在被占领的巴黎教授哲学，同在1945年创办《现代》杂志。在最初的十余年中，他们是亲密的思想盟友——但从1950年代初开始，哲学和政治上的分歧逐渐加深，最终导致了不可弥合的疏远。1961年梅洛-庞蒂的突然去世（53岁，心脏病发作）使这段对话永远失去了完成的可能。

Maurice Merleau-Ponty (1908–1961) and Sartre's relationship is the most important intellectual dialogue within the French phenomenological movement. Close in age (three years apart), both encountered Husserl and Heidegger's phenomenology in the 1930s, both taught philosophy in occupied Paris, both co-founded *Les Temps Modernes* in 1945. For the first decade they were close intellectual allies — but from the early 1950s, philosophical and political divergences deepened into an irreparable estrangement. Merleau-Ponty's sudden death in 1961 (aged 53, heart attack) ended the possibility of completing this dialogue.

## 共鸣期（1940s）/ Period of Resonance (1940s)

### 共同的现象学革命 / The Shared Phenomenological Revolution

1940年代，萨特与梅洛-庞蒂共同推动了法国现象学运动的发展。两人的共同立场包括：

In the 1940s, Sartre and Merleau-Ponty jointly advanced the French phenomenological movement. Their shared positions included:

- **反对笛卡尔的二元论**：两人都拒绝将意识与身体截然分开（尽管方式不同）
- **强调具体经验**：两人都主张哲学必须从"活生生的经验"（le vécu）出发，而非从抽象概念出发
- **反法西斯政治立场**：两人都在占领期间以不同方式参与抵抗
- **《现代》杂志的共同编辑**：这份杂志成为战后法国知识界的核心阵地

### 互补的哲学方向 / Complementary Philosophical Directions

萨特的《存在与虚无》（1943）与梅洛-庞蒂的《知觉现象学》（1945）最初被视为互补的姊妹篇：前者从意识出发分析自由和虚无，后者从身体出发分析知觉和世界。萨特对梅洛-庞蒂的身体分析表示赞赏，梅洛-庞蒂对萨特的自由分析表示敬意。

Sartre's *Being and Nothingness* (1943) and Merleau-Ponty's *Phenomenology of Perception* (1945) were initially seen as complementary sister works: the former analyzing freedom and nothingness from consciousness, the latter analyzing perception and the world from the body. Sartre expressed admiration for Merleau-Ponty's analysis of the body; Merleau-Ponty expressed respect for Sartre's analysis of freedom.

## 分歧期（1950s）/ Period of Divergence (1950s)

### 身体 vs 意识的优先性 / The Priority of Body vs Consciousness

核心哲学分歧在于：**意识是否具有优先性，还是身体-知觉具有优先性？** 萨特的存在论以意识（自为存在）为出发点——一切意义都是意识的投射。梅洛-庞蒂则认为知觉先于意识——在意识"思考"世界之前，身体已经"居住"在世界中了。

The core philosophical divergence: **does consciousness have priority, or does body-perception have priority?** Sartre's ontology takes consciousness (being-for-itself) as its starting point — all meaning is consciousness's projection. Merleau-Ponty holds that perception precedes consciousness — before consciousness "thinks" the world, the body already "inhabits" the world.

### 政治的分歧：冷战中的立场 / Political Divergence: Cold War Positions

1950年代初，两人对苏联和共产主义的态度分歧加深。萨特在1952–1956年间采取了"同路人"（compagnon de route）立场——对苏联表示同情，拒绝公开批评。梅洛-庞蒂在1950–1952年间也曾倾向马克思主义，但在1955年的《辩证的历险》中对萨特的政治立场进行了公开批评——指责萨特的"超布尔什维克主义"忽视了苏联体制的根本缺陷。

In the early 1950s, their attitudes toward the Soviet Union and communism diverged. Sartre adopted a "fellow traveler" (compagnon de route) stance from 1952–1956 — sympathizing with the USSR, refusing public criticism. Merleau-Ponty had also leaned Marxist around 1950–1952, but in *Adventures of the Dialectic* (1955) publicly criticized Sartre's political stance — accusing Sartre's "ultrabolshevism" of overlooking fundamental flaws in the Soviet system.

### 萨特的悼词 / Sartre's Eulogy

1961年5月3日，梅洛-庞蒂突然去世。萨特在《现代》杂志上发表了深情的悼词《梅洛-庞蒂》（*Merleau-Ponty vivant*），回顾了两人三十年的友谊与分歧。萨特写道："我们之间有过争论——但这些争论恰恰是我们之间关系的证明。与梅洛-庞蒂争论，比与大多数人达成一致更有价值。"

On 3 May 1961, Merleau-Ponty died suddenly. Sartre published a moving eulogy in *Les Temps Modernes*, "Merleau-Ponty vivant," recalling thirty years of friendship and divergence. Sartre wrote: "We had our disagreements — but these disagreements were precisely proof of our relationship. Arguing with Merleau-Ponty was more valuable than agreeing with most people."

## 遗产 / Legacy

萨特与梅洛-庞蒂的对话代表了法国现象学内部的两种可能路径：**意识的路径（萨特）和身体的路径（梅洛-庞蒂）**。后来的法国哲学——从结构主义到后结构主义——更多地继承了梅洛-庞蒂的遗产（身体、知觉、交织），而非萨特的意识哲学。但在当代具身认知科学和现象学心理学的复兴中，两者的对话获得了新的生命力。

The Sartre-Merleau-Ponty dialogue represents two possible paths within French phenomenology: **the path of consciousness (Sartre) and the path of the body (Merleau-Ponty)**. Later French philosophy — from structuralism to post-structuralism — inherited more from Merleau-Ponty (body, perception, chiasm) than from Sartre's consciousness philosophy. But in the contemporary revival of embodied cognitive science and phenomenological psychology, their dialogue gains new vitality.
