# 加缪著作总览 / Camus's Major Works

---

## 荒诞周期 / The Absurd Cycle

### 《反与正》/ *L'Envers et l'Endroit* (1937)

**法语原名**：*L'Envers et l'Endroit*

**内容概要**：加缪出版的第一部作品，收录五篇散文随笔，写于22–24岁之间。这些短小的散文已经展示了加缪思想的核心张力：世界的冷漠（"反"面）与美（"正"面）不可分割地并存。《讽刺》描写阿尔及尔贫民区的日常生活，《在两者之间》记录了母亲的沉默和贫困，《灵魂中的死亡》探索了老年与死亡的临近，《爱生活》表达了在地中海阳光下对当下的肯定，《反与正》以标题篇收束全书，提出"世界的两面"（l'envers et l'endroit）这一基本对立。

**Overview**: Camus's first published work, a collection of five prose essays written between ages 22 and 24. These short pieces already display the central tension of Camus's thought: the world's indifference (the "wrong side") and beauty (the "right side") are inseparably co-present. "Irony" depicts daily life in the poor quarters of Algiers, "Between Yes and No" records his mother's silence and poverty, "Death in the Soul" explores aging and approaching death, "Love of Life" affirms the present under Mediterranean sunlight, and the title essay closes the book, proposing the basic opposition of "the two sides of the world."

**意义**：加缪自己终生视此书为他最好的作品之一。1958年重新出版时他写道："如果说我写了更好的书，那并非因为我的天分增长了。"

**Significance**: Camus himself considered this book one of his finest throughout his life. When republishing it in 1958, he wrote: "If I have written better books, it is not because my talent has grown."

---

### 《婚礼集》/ *Noces* (1939)

**法语原名**：*Noces*

**内容概要**：四篇地中海颂歌式的散文，将阿尔及利亚的自然风景——提帕萨（Tipasa）的罗马废墟、杰米拉（Djemila）的风、阿尔及尔的海滩——升华为一种生活哲学。"在提帕萨"的名句"我来到这个世界是为了使太阳燃烧"浓缩了加缪对当下、身体和感官经验的绝对肯定。《婚礼集》表达了一种"荒诞的幸福"——在世界无意义的前提下，当下的感官体验本身就是唯一值得拥有的东西。

**Overview**: Four Mediterranean hymns in prose that elevate Algeria's natural landscapes — the Roman ruins of Tipasa, the wind of Djemila, the beaches of Algiers — into a philosophy of life. The famous line from "At Tipasa" — "I came into this world to make the sun burn" — condenses Camus's absolute affirmation of the present, the body, and sensory experience. *Nuptials* expresses an "absurd happiness" — on the premise of the world's meaninglessness, the present sensory experience is itself the only thing worth having.

---

### 《局外人》/ *L'Étranger* (1942)

**法语原名**：*L'Étranger*

**内容概要**：加缪最知名的小说，也是二十世纪最重要的法语小说之一。主人公默尔索（Meursault）是一个对世界和社会规范表现出奇特冷漠的阿尔及尔小职员。他在母亲葬礼上没有流泪，后来在海滩上"因为太阳"而杀了一个阿拉伯人。审判的焦点不是杀人行为本身，而是默尔索在母亲葬礼上的"不当"表现——社会要求他扮演一个悲伤的儿子，而他的拒绝（或无力）扮演这一角色使他成为"局外人"。

**Overview**: Camus's most famous novel and one of the most important French novels of the twentieth century. The protagonist Meursault is an Algiers clerk who displays a peculiar indifference to the world and social norms. He does not cry at his mother's funeral and later kills an Arab on the beach "because of the sun." The trial's focus is not on the killing itself but on Meursault's "improper" behavior at the funeral — society requires him to play the role of a grieving son, and his refusal (or inability) to play this role makes him a "stranger."

**哲学意涵**：默尔索不是"无感情的人"，而是"拒绝说谎的人"——他拒绝为生活提供它不具有的意义。加缪在1955年的序言中将默尔索定义为"一个拒绝参与社会游戏的人"（un homme qui refuse de jouer le jeu）。小说的第二部分——审判和最终在牢房中对牧师的爆发——展示了荒诞意识在面对社会的意义强制时的对抗姿态。

**Philosophical significance**: Meursault is not "an emotionless person" but "one who refuses to lie" — he refuses to provide life with a meaning it does not have. In his 1955 preface, Camus defines Meursault as "a man who refuses to play the game." The novel's second part — the trial and the final outburst against the chaplain in his cell — displays absurd consciousness in its stance of opposition to society's coercion of meaning.

### 《西西弗神话》/ *Le Mythe de Sisyphe* (1942)

**法语原名**：*Le Mythe de Sisyphe*

**内容概要**：加缪荒诞哲学的核心著作。全书从"唯一真正严肃的哲学问题是自杀"这一惊人开篇出发，系统论证了荒诞的本质、哲学性自杀的各种形式（宗教的、哲学的），以及荒诞人的生活方式。全书以西西弗的神话作为结论——这个被诸神惩罚永远推石上山的人，在加缪的重新诠释中成为"荒诞英雄"的原型：他在清醒认识自己命运的无意义后，仍然选择推石上山，而这种选择本身就是对诸神的反抗。

**Overview**: The central work of Camus's absurd philosophy. Starting from the startling opening — "There is but one truly serious philosophical problem, and that is suicide" — the book systematically argues the nature of the absurd, the various forms of philosophical suicide (religious, philosophical), and the absurd man's way of life. The book concludes with the myth of Sisyphus — this man condemned by the gods to push a boulder uphill for eternity becomes, in Camus's reinterpretation, the prototype of the "absurd hero": after lucidly recognizing the meaninglessness of his fate, he still chooses to push the boulder uphill, and this choice itself is a revolt against the gods.

**意义**：这本书是加缪哲学最系统的表达，也是理解其后续思想（尤其是"反抗"概念）的必要前提。

**Significance**: This book is Camus's most systematic philosophical expression and the necessary prerequisite for understanding his subsequent thought (especially the concept of "revolt").

## 反抗周期 / The Revolt Cycle

### 《鼠疫》/ *La Peste* (1947)

**法语原名**：*La Peste*

**内容概要**：加缪最重要的长篇小说，以阿尔及利亚奥兰市（Oran）爆发鼠疫为背景，讲述了里厄医生（Dr. Rieux）和其他市民在瘟疫中的抗争。小说是对纳粹占领法国的寓言式回应——鼠疫象征着法西斯主义，也象征着一切不可被消灭的恶。里厄医生的态度不是英雄主义的壮举，而是"做好本职工作"（faire son métier）——在清醒认识鼠疫永远不会被彻底消灭的前提下，仍然坚持抗争。

**Overview**: Camus's most important novel, set in the Algerian city of Oran during a plague outbreak, narrating Dr. Rieux's and other citizens' struggle against the epidemic. The novel is an allegorical response to the Nazi occupation of France — the plague symbolizes fascism, but also all forms of evil that cannot be definitively eradicated. Dr. Rieux's attitude is not one of heroic grand gestures but of "doing one's job well" (faire son métier) — continuing to resist while lucidly aware that the plague will never be completely eradicated.

**哲学意涵**：从《局外人》的个人荒诞意识到《鼠疫》的集体反抗，加缪的思想经历了从"我"到"我们"的转变。"每个人身上都带着鼠疫"这一命题揭示了恶的内在性和反抗的永恒性。

**Philosophical significance**: From the individual absurd consciousness of *The Stranger* to the collective revolt of *The Plague*, Camus's thought undergoes a shift from "I" to "we." The proposition that "each of us has the plague within" reveals the internality of evil and the permanence of revolt.

### 《反抗者》/ *L'Homme révolté* (1951)

**法语原名**：*L'Homme révolté*

**内容概要**：加缪最具争议性的哲学-政治著作，系统论证了"反抗"（la révolte）的形而上学基础、历史发展和必要限度。全书分为三大部分：第一，"形而上学的反抗"——从萨德侯爵（Sade）到尼采、从浪漫主义到超现实主义，考察反抗如何从对神的否定走向对人的否定；第二，"历史的反抗"——从法国大革命到俄国革命，考察反抗如何从理想走向恐怖；第三，"反抗与限度"——提出"我反抗，因此我们存在"（Je me révolte, donc nous sommes）的核心命题，论证反抗必须有"限度"（la mesure）。

**Overview**: Camus's most controversial philosophical-political work, systematically arguing the metaphysical foundations, historical development, and necessary limits of "revolt" (la révolte). The book has three parts: first, "Metaphysical Rebellion" — from the Marquis de Sade to Nietzsche, from Romanticism to Surrealism, examining how revolt moves from negation of God to negation of man; second, "Historical Rebellion" — from the French Revolution to the Russian Revolution, examining how revolt moves from ideal to terror; third, "Rebellion and Limits" — advancing the core thesis "I revolt, therefore we are" (Je me révolte, donc nous sommes), arguing that revolt must have "measure" (la mesure).

**意义**：这本书直接导致了与萨特的决裂，也使加缪在法国左翼知识分子圈中遭到孤立。但冷战结束后，其"限度"思想获得了重新评价。

**Significance**: This book directly caused the break with Sartre and isolated Camus in the French left-intellectual milieu. But after the Cold War, his thought on "limits" received renewed appreciation.

---

### 《堕落》/ *La Chute* (1956)

**法语原名**：*La Chute*

**内容概要**：加缪生前出版的最后一部小说，以阿姆斯特丹一间酒吧为场景，由前律师让-巴蒂斯特·克莱芒斯（Jean-Baptiste Clamence）进行一场持续数天的独白。克莱芒斯曾是巴黎最受尊敬的律师、"正义的捍卫者"，但在一次深夜听到一名女子的呼救声却选择不救之后，他的自我形象彻底崩塌。这部小说是加缪对"审判者-先知"姿态的最深刻反思——每一个以正义之名审判他人的人，首先必须面对自己的罪责。

**Overview**: Camus's last published novel, set in a bar in Amsterdam, consisting of a multi-day monologue by former lawyer Jean-Baptiste Clamence. Clamence was once Paris's most respected lawyer, "defender of justice," but after hearing a woman's cry for help one night and choosing not to intervene, his self-image collapses entirely. This novel is Camus's most profound reflection on the "judge-prophet" posture — everyone who judges others in the name of justice must first confront their own guilt.

---

### 《流放与王国》/ *L'Exil et le Royaume* (1957)

**法语原名**：*L'Exil et le Royaume*

**内容概要**：六篇短篇小说集，探讨"流放"（l'exil）的多种形态——地理的流放、精神的流放、社会身份的流放——以及"王国"（le royaume）作为每个人内心深处对归属感和意义的渴望。《不忠的女人》《叛逆者》《无声的愤怒》《来客》《约拿》《生长的石头》——每篇都从不同角度探索个体在荒诞世界中的位置和选择。

**Overview**: A collection of six short stories exploring the multiple forms of "exile" (l'exil) — geographical exile, spiritual exile, exile from social identity — and "the kingdom" (le royaume) as each person's innermost longing for belonging and meaning. "The Adulterous Woman," "The Renegade," "The Silent Men," "The Guest," "Jonas," and "The Growing Stone" — each explores from a different angle the individual's position and choices in an absurd world.

## 遗稿 / Posthumous Works

### 《第一人》/ *Le Premier Homme* (1994)

**法语原名**：*Le Premier Homme*

**内容概要**：加缪未完成、未出版的自传体小说，手稿在他去世时随身携带。1994年由女儿卡特琳娜·加缪（Catherine Camus）整理出版。小说以主人公雅克·科梅里（Jacques Cormery）回到阿尔及利亚寻找父亲的故事为线索，实际上是加缪对自身生命的文学性回顾。书中对母亲的描写——"她没有历史，没有过去，她甚至不会读报"——是加缪文学中最感人的段落之一。这部未完成的作品展示了加缪晚期思想的走向：回归身体、回归沉默、回归最初的爱。

**Overview**: Camus's unfinished, unpublished autobiographical novel, the manuscript of which he was carrying when he died. Published in 1994 by his daughter Catherine Camus. The novel follows protagonist Jacques Cormery's return to Algeria to search for his father's story — essentially Camus's literary retrospective on his own life. The portrait of his mother — "She had no history, no past, she could not even read the newspapers" — is among the most moving passages in Camus's writing. This unfinished work reveals the direction of Camus's late thought: a return to the body, to silence, to first love.
