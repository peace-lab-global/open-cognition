# 局外人 / L'Étranger

> "妈妈今天死了。也许是昨天，我不确定。"
> "Aujourd'hui, maman est morte. Ou peut-être hier, je ne sais pas."
> — *L'Étranger*, 开篇

---

## 概述 / Overview

《局外人》（*L'Étranger*, 1942）是加缪的第一部小说，也是他荒诞哲学的文学化身。主人公默尔索（Meursault）是一个阿尔及尔的小职员，他在母亲葬礼上没有流泪，后来在海滩上"因为太阳"而杀了一个阿拉伯人。在审判中，他被定罪的依据不是杀人行为本身，而是他在母亲葬礼上的"不当"表现——他不为社会期待的情感游戏提供配合。默尔索因此成为**拒绝说谎的人**——不是没有感情，而是拒绝为生活提供它不具有的意义。

*The Stranger* (*L'Étranger*, 1942) is Camus's first novel and the literary embodiment of his absurd philosophy. The protagonist Meursault is a clerk in Algiers who does not cry at his mother's funeral and later kills an Arab on the beach "because of the sun." At his trial, he is condemned not for the killing itself but for his "improper" behaviour at the funeral — he refuses to cooperate with the emotional game society expects. Meursault is thus **one who refuses to lie** — not without feelings, but refusing to provide life with a meaning it does not have.

## 核心要义 / Core Arguments

### 默尔索的荒诞意识 / Meursault's Absurd Consciousness

默尔索的特征不是"冷漠"（indifférence）——加缪在1955年序言中强调这一点——而是**拒绝参与社会的意义游戏**（refuser de jouer le jeu）。具体表现：

Meursault's characteristic is not "indifference" — Camus stresses this in his 1955 preface — but **refusal to participate in society's meaning-game** (refuser de jouer le jeu). Specifically:

1. **拒绝情感表演**：在母亲葬礼上不哭不是因为不爱母亲，而是因为他不按照社会期望表演悲伤
2. **拒绝宗教安慰**：在牢房中对牧师的爆发——"你的确定性抵不上女人的一缕头发"——是对一切超越性安慰的拒绝
3. **拒绝法律叙事**：审判中，法庭要求他为自己的行为提供"动机"和"意义"，而他的回答只是"因为太阳"
4. **接受死亡**：在牢房中最终接受"世界温柔的冷漠"（la tendre indifférence du monde），感到幸福

### 审判作为意义强制 / The Trial as Meaning-Coercion

小说第二部分的审判是加缪对社会机制的深刻批判。法庭的真正罪行不是杀人——法庭每天处理杀人案——而是默尔索**拒绝配合社会的意义体系**。检察官论证：一个在母亲葬礼上不哭的人"灵魂中有犯罪的倾向"。社会的恐惧不是杀人犯，而是**不提供意义的人**。

The trial in Part Two is Camus's profound critique of social mechanisms. The court's real crime is not murder — courts deal with murder daily — but Meursault's **refusal to cooperate with society's meaning-system**. The prosecutor argues: one who does not cry at his mother's funeral has "a criminal tendency in his soul." Society's fear is not of murderers but of **those who do not provide meaning**.

### 牢房中的爆发 / The Outburst in the Cell

小说的高潮是默尔索对牧师的爆发——这是荒诞意识对信仰的最强烈拒绝：

The novel's climax is Meursault's outburst against the chaplain — absurd consciousness's strongest rejection of faith:

> "在我所度过的整个荒诞的生活中……在我所度过的整个荒诞的生活中，一种深息的气息从我的未来深处，穿越那些尚未到来的年月，向我袭来……没有什么，没有什么重要的，我很清楚为什么。"
> "Throughout the absurd life I had lived... a dark breath had been surging toward me from the depths of my future... and nothing, nothing had importance, and I quite well knew why."

这一刻，默尔索不再是被动的"局外人"——他**主动选择**了自己的荒诞清醒。他"向着世界温柔的冷漠敞开了心扉"，感到幸福。

In this moment, Meursault is no longer a passive "outsider" — he **actively chooses** his absurd lucidity. He "opened himself to the gentle indifference of the world" and felt happy.

## 应用场景 / Applications

### 法律哲学

默尔索的审判揭示了法律系统对"叙事"的依赖——法庭需要的不仅是事实，还有一个连贯的"故事"。不提供这个故事的人（如默尔索）在法律面前处于结构性的劣势。这与当代法律叙事学（legal narratology）的研究相呼应。

Meursault's trial reveals the legal system's dependence on "narrative" — the court needs not just facts but a coherent "story." Those who do not provide this story (like Meursault) are structurally disadvantaged before the law. This resonates with contemporary legal narratology research.

### 后殖民批评

默尔索杀害的"阿拉伯人"在整部小说中没有名字——这个匿名性本身揭示了殖民地社会的种族结构。当代后殖民批评（尤其是萨义德的分析）将这一点视为加缪小说中的殖民主义盲点，也有学者为加缪辩护，认为这种匿名性恰恰是对殖民结构的暴露。

The "Arab" Meursault kills remains unnamed throughout the novel — this anonymity itself reveals the racial structure of colonial society. Contemporary postcolonial criticism (especially Said's analysis) treats this as a colonial blind spot in Camus's fiction, while other scholars defend Camus, arguing the anonymity is precisely an exposure of colonial structures.

## 常见误读 / Common Misunderstandings

### 误读一：默尔索是"反社会人格" / "Meursault is a 'sociopath'"

**纠正**：加缪在1955年序言中明确说：默尔索"远非一个没有感情的人"（loin d'être un homme sans sentiments）。他的"冷漠"不是心理疾病，而是哲学立场——他拒绝为生活附加它不具有的意义。将默尔索病理化，恰恰是社会用来取消荒诞问题的一种方式。

**Correction**: Camus explicitly states in his 1955 preface: Meursault is "far from being a man without feelings" (loin d'être un homme sans sentiments). His "indifference" is not a psychological disorder but a philosophical stance — he refuses to attach to life a meaning it does not have. Pathologizing Meursault is precisely one way society uses to cancel the absurd question.
