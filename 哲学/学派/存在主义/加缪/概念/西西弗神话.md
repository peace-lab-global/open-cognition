# 西西弗神话 / Le Mythe de Sisyphe

> "应该想象西西弗是幸福的。"
> "Il faut imaginer Sisyphe heureux."
> — Camus, *Le Mythe de Sisyphe*, 最终句

---

## 概述 / Overview

西西弗（Sisyphe / Sisyphus）是希腊神话中的人物，因触怒诸神而被罚永远推一块巨石上山，每当石头接近山顶时便滚落回山脚，一切从头开始。加缪在《西西弗神话》（1942）的结尾，将这位古代受罚者重新诠释为**荒诞英雄的原型**——一个在清醒认识自身命运的无意义之后，仍然选择推石上山的人。这个选择本身，就是对诸神（即荒诞命运的施加者）的最深刻反抗。

Sisyphus is a figure from Greek mythology condemned by the gods to push a boulder up a mountain for eternity; each time the stone nears the summit it rolls back down, and he must begin again. In the closing pages of *The Myth of Sisyphus* (1942), Camus reinterprets this ancient punished figure as the **prototype of the absurd hero** — one who, after lucidly recognizing the meaninglessness of his fate, still chooses to push the boulder uphill. This choice itself is the most profound revolt against the gods (the imposers of the absurd fate).

## 核心要义 / Core Arguments

### 西西弗的处境 / Sisyphus's Condition

诸神认为"没有比无用的劳动和无望的工作更可怕的惩罚了"（Il n'est pas de punition plus terrible que le travail inutile et sans espoir）。西西弗的惩罚的恐怖不在于劳动本身，而在于**劳动的无用性**——每一次努力都注定被取消。

The gods reasoned that "there is no more dreadful punishment than futile and hopeless labour." The horror of Sisyphus's punishment lies not in the labour itself but in the **futility of the labour** — every effort is destined to be cancelled.

### 下山时刻 / The Moment of Descent

加缪最关注的不是西西弗推石上山的过程，而是他**下山走回山脚的那个时刻**——这是西西弗的**意识**时刻，是他清醒认识自己命运的时刻。加缪写道：

Camus is most interested not in the ascent but in **the moment Sisyphus walks back down to the foot of the mountain** — this is Sisyphus's moment of **consciousness**, the moment he lucidly recognizes his fate. Camus writes:

> "在那微妙的时刻，他回到石头那里，在那停顿中……那种清醒的意识同时构成了他的折磨和他的胜利。"
> "In that subtle moment when man glances backward over his life... that lucid consciousness constitutes at once his torment and his victory."

### 反抗通过清醒 / Revolt Through Lucidity

西西弗的胜利不在于改变命运——他无法让石头留在山顶。他的胜利在于**在清醒中接受命运**，而这种接受本身就是对命运的主人的蔑视。诸神以为他们在惩罚他，但他的清醒意识使他**高于**他的命运：

Sisyphus's victory lies not in changing his fate — he cannot keep the boulder at the summit. His victory lies in **accepting his fate in lucidity**, and this acceptance itself is contempt for the masters of his fate. The gods thought they were punishing him, but his lucid consciousness makes him **superior** to his fate:

> "没有轻蔑克服不了的命运。"
> "There is no fate that cannot be surmounted by scorn."

### 幸福与荒诞 / Happiness and the Absurd

"应该想象西西弗是幸福的"——这不是讽刺，而是加缪荒诞哲学的**最高命题**。幸福不是来自意义的发现（世界不提供意义），而是来自**清醒地活在没有意义的世界中**这一行为本身。推石上山的行为——作为清醒的、自主的、蔑视命运的行为——本身就是充实的。

"One must imagine Sisyphus happy" — this is not irony but the **supreme thesis** of Camus's absurd philosophy. Happiness does not come from discovering meaning (the world provides none) but from the act itself of **living lucidly in a meaningless world**. The act of pushing the boulder — as a lucid, autonomous, fate-scorn­ing act — is itself fulfilling.

## 应用场景 / Applications

### 劳动哲学

西西弗的神话为当代劳动哲学提供了一个深刻的隐喻：在异化劳动（alienated labour）中，工人的处境与西西弗类似——劳动产品不属于自己，劳动过程不受自己控制。但加缪的解读指向的不是马克思式的结构性变革，而是**态度上的转变**——在清醒意识中，即使是异化的劳动也可以成为一种反抗。

Sisyphus's myth provides a profound metaphor for contemporary labour philosophy: in alienated labour, the worker's condition parallels Sisyphus's — the products of labour do not belong to oneself, the labour process is not under one's control. But Camus's reading points not to Marxian structural transformation but to an **attitudinal shift** — in lucid consciousness, even alienated labour can become a form of revolt.

### 心理韧性

西西弗的"清醒接受"为面对不可改变的逆境（慢性疾病、丧失、结构性不公正）提供了心理韧性的哲学基础：不是否认困境（乐观主义的自我欺骗），不是屈服于困境（绝望），而是在清醒认识困境后仍然选择行动。

Sisyphus's "lucid acceptance" provides a philosophical foundation for psychological resilience in the face of unchangeable adversity (chronic illness, loss, structural injustice): neither denying the predicament (optimism's self-deception) nor surrendering to it (despair), but choosing to act after lucidly recognizing the predicament.

## 常见误读 / Common Misunderstandings

### 误读一：西西弗是"接受命运"的保守主义者 / "Sisyphus is a conservative who 'accepts his fate'"

**纠正**：西西弗的态度不是消极的接受（résignation），而是积极的蔑视（mépris）。他接受的是**处境**（他必须推石上山），但他不接受**命运的主人**（诸神）。他的清醒意识就是对诸神的反抗——这不是保守主义，而是一种在无能为力的处境中保持精神自由的姿态。

**Correction**: Sisyphus's attitude is not passive resignation but active scorn (mépris). He accepts the **situation** (he must push the boulder) but not the **masters of fate** (the gods). His lucid consciousness is itself revolt against the gods — this is not conservatism but a stance of maintaining spiritual freedom in a powerless situation.

### 误读二："应该想象西西弗是幸福的"是讽刺 / "One must imagine Sisyphus happy" is ironic

**纠正**：这是加缪哲学中最常被误解的一句话。加缪是认真的——但这种"幸福"不是传统的幸福（满足、安宁、意义），而是荒诞人的特有幸福：清醒的、充实的、蔑视命运的意识本身就是幸福。正如加缪在前文中说："没有明天的一日，就是对永恒的蔑视。这已经足够了。"

**Correction**: This is the most frequently misunderstood sentence in Camus's philosophy. Camus is serious — but this "happiness" is not traditional happiness (satisfaction, peace, meaning). It is the absurd man's peculiar happiness: lucid, fulfilling, fate-scorn­ing consciousness is itself happiness. As Camus says earlier: "A face that toils so close to stones is already stone itself... One day of life without a tomorrow is a scorn for eternity. That is enough."
