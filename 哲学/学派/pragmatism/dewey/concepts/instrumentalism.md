# 工具主义 · Instrumentalism

> "Ideas are operational in that they instigate and direct further operations."
>
> "思想是操作性的——它们激发并指导进一步的操作。"
>
> — John Dewey, *The Quest for Certainty* (1929)

---

## 概述 / Overview

工具主义（Instrumentalism）是杜威认识论的核心立场。它主张：思想、概念和理论不是对现实的静态"镜像"，而是解决具体问题的工具。一个观念的价值不在于它是否"符合"某个独立的实在，而在于它是否有效地指导行动、将不确定的情境转化为确定的情境。这一立场从根本上挑战了西方哲学自柏拉图以来的"旁观者知识论"（spectator theory of knowledge）。

Instrumentalism is the core epistemological stance of Dewey's philosophy. It holds that ideas, concepts, and theories are not static "mirrors" of reality but tools for solving concrete problems. The value of an idea lies not in whether it "corresponds" to some independent reality, but in whether it effectively guides action—transforming indeterminate situations into determinate ones. This stance fundamentally challenges the spectator theory of knowledge that has dominated Western philosophy since Plato.

## 思想作为工具 / Ideas as Tools

### 从"表象"到"操作" / From Representation to Operation

传统认识论——无论是理性主义还是经验主义——都预设了知识是心灵对外部世界的"表象"（representation）。真理就是表象与对象的"符合"。杜威拒绝了这一框架。在达尔文进化论的启发下，他将认知视为有机体适应环境的主动行为：思想不是对世界的被动反映，而是有机体在与环境交互中发展出来的操作性能力。

Traditional epistemology—whether rationalist or empiricist—presupposes that knowledge is the mind's "representation" of the external world. Truth is the correspondence of representation to object. Dewey rejected this framework. Inspired by Darwinian evolution, he conceived cognition as the organism's active behavior in adapting to its environment: thought is not passive reflection of the world but an operational capacity developed through organism-environment interaction.

这意味着一个概念就像一把锤子——我们不会问锤子是否"真实地反映了"钉子的本质，而是问它是否好用。同样，牛顿力学不需要"真实地反映"宇宙的终极结构，只需要它在特定问题域中能有效运作。

This means a concept is like a hammer—we do not ask whether a hammer "truly reflects" the essence of a nail, but whether it works well. Similarly, Newtonian mechanics need not "truly reflect" the ultimate structure of the cosmos; it only needs to function effectively within a specific problem domain.

### 概念的功能分类 / Functional Classification of Concepts

杜威将概念按其在探究中的功能分为不同层次：

- **通用概念**（generic concepts）：如"金属"——帮助我们预判和归类，但不声称揭示了事物的本质。
- **操作性概念**（operational concepts）：如"力"或"能量"——直接指导实验操作。
- **终极概念**（ultimate concepts）：如"存在"或"实在"——杜威认为这些在传统形而上学中往往被过度实体化。

Dewey classified concepts by their function in inquiry:

- **Generic concepts**: such as "metal"—they help us anticipate and classify but do not claim to reveal the essence of things.
- **Operational concepts**: such as "force" or "energy"—they directly guide experimental operations.
- **Ultimate concepts**: such as "being" or "reality"—these, in traditional metaphysics, are often over-substantialized.

## 有理由的断定性 / Warranted Assertibility

### 取代"真理" / Replacing "Truth"

杜威最具争议的认识论主张之一，是他用"有理由的断定性"（warranted assertibility）取代了传统的"真理"概念。在《逻辑：探究理论》（1938）中，他论证说：当一个命题经过严格探究而被证成时，它就获得了"有理由的断定性"。这个概念的优点在于：

One of Dewey's most controversial epistemological claims is his replacement of the traditional concept of "truth" with "warranted assertibility." In *Logic: The Theory of Inquiry* (1938), he argued that when a proposition has been warranted through rigorous inquiry, it achieves "warranted assertibility." The advantages of this concept include:

1. **动态性 / Dynamism**：一个命题今天被证成了，不意味着它在未来所有情境中仍然有效。知识是可修正的。
2. **过程性 / Processuality**：强调知识产生于探究过程，而非静态的命题-事实对应关系。
3. **实践性 / Practicality**：将认识论的焦点从"知识是什么"转向"知识如何产生和使用"。

1. **Dynamism**: A proposition warranted today does not mean it will remain valid in all future situations. Knowledge is fallible and revisable.
2. **Processuality**: Emphasizes that knowledge arises from the inquiry process, not from a static proposition-fact correspondence.
3. **Practicality**: Shifts the epistemological focus from "what knowledge is" to "how knowledge is produced and used."

### 与符合论的区别 / Difference from Correspondence Theory

符合论认为真理是命题与事实的对应。杜威认为这种"对应"在实践中无法独立验证——我们总是在探究中确认对应关系，而探究本身就预设了操作和检验的过程。因此，"符合"不是独立于探究的标准，而是探究成功的副产品。

Correspondence theory holds that truth is the correspondence of a proposition to a fact. Dewey argued that such "correspondence" cannot be independently verified in practice—we always confirm correspondence within inquiry, and inquiry itself presupposes operations and testing. Therefore, "correspondence" is not a standard independent of inquiry but a byproduct of successful inquiry.

## 实用主义知识论 / Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge

### 知识的实践根源 / Practical Roots of Knowledge

杜威在《确定性的追求》（1929）中论证说，西方哲学传统中理论与实践的二元对立——从亚里士多德的theoria（沉思）vs praxis（行动），到笛卡尔的心灵vs身体——根源于古代社会中劳动者与思想者的阶级分工。哲学家贬低实践，抬高沉思，不是因为沉思更接近"真理"，而是因为哲学家属于不劳动的特权阶层。

In *The Quest for Certainty* (1929), Dewey argued that the theory-practice dichotomy in Western philosophy—from Aristotle's theoria vs praxis to Descartes's mind vs body—is rooted in the class division between laborers and thinkers in ancient society. Philosophers devalued practice and elevated contemplation not because contemplation is closer to "truth," but because philosophers belonged to a privileged class that did not labor.

杜威的实用主义知识论打破了这一传统等级：知识不是高于行动的沉思状态，而是行动本身的一个环节——探究是行动的最高形式，因为它能够有意识地指导后续行动。

Dewey's pragmatic epistemology dismantles this traditional hierarchy: knowledge is not a contemplative state superior to action but a phase of action itself—inquiry is the highest form of action because it can consciously guide subsequent action.

### 知识的可修正性 / Fallibilism of Knowledge

与皮尔士的可修正主义（fallibilism）一脉相承，杜威认为没有任何知识是绝对确定的。所有的"真理"都是暂时的——它们在特定探究中获得了证成，但随时可能在新的问题情境中被修正或推翻。这不是相对主义：杜威区分了"有理由的断定"和"未经检验的意见"。前者经过了严格的方法论程序，后者没有。

In line with Peirce's fallibilism, Dewey held that no knowledge is absolutely certain. All "truths" are provisional—they have been warranted within specific inquiries but may be revised or overturned in new problematic situations. This is not relativism: Dewey distinguished "warranted assertions" from "untested opinions." The former have undergone rigorous methodological procedures; the latter have not.

## 与其他思想家的关系 / Relations to Other Thinkers

- **皮尔士 / Peirce**：杜威继承了皮尔士"信念作为行动习惯"的观点，但更强调知识的社会和实践维度。/ Dewey inherited Peirce's view of "belief as habit of action" but emphasized knowledge's social and practical dimensions more.
- **詹姆斯 / James**：詹姆斯的"真理是有用的"被批评为过于主观；杜威的"有理由的断定性"是一个更严谨的替代方案。/ James's "truth is useful" was criticized as too subjective; Dewey's "warranted assertibility" is a more rigorous alternative.
- **罗素 / Russell**：罗素批评杜威放弃了真理的客观标准；杜威回应说，他放弃的是不可操作的形而上学标准，而非客观性本身。/ Russell criticized Dewey for abandoning objective standards of truth; Dewey responded that he abandoned inoperable metaphysical standards, not objectivity itself.
- **波普尔 / Popper**：两者都强调知识的可修正性，但波普尔聚焦于"证伪"，杜威则关注更广泛的探究过程。/ Both emphasized fallibility, but Popper focused on "falsification" while Dewey focused on the broader inquiry process.

## 当代意义 / Contemporary Significance

工具主义在当代科学哲学、技术哲学和设计理论中持续发挥影响：

Instrumentalism continues to exert influence in contemporary philosophy of science, philosophy of technology, and design theory:

- **科学模型论 / Scientific Modeling**：科学模型被视为工具而非实在的镜像，这与杜威的工具主义高度一致。/ Scientific models are treated as tools rather than mirrors of reality, aligning with Dewey's instrumentalism.
- **人工智能 / AI**：机器学习中模型的"性能指标"本质上是工具主义的评价方式——不在于模型是否"真实"，而在于它是否有效。/ The "performance metrics" of machine learning models are essentially instrumentalist evaluations—not whether the model is "true" but whether it works.
- **实用主义法学 / Pragmatist Jurisprudence**：法律规则被视为解决社会问题的工具，而非永恒正义的体现。/ Legal rules are viewed as tools for solving social problems rather than embodiments of eternal justice.
