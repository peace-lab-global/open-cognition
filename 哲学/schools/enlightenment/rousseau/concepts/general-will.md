> "公意永远正确，但引导公意的判断并不总是明智的。"
> "The general will is always right, but the judgment which guides it is not always enlightened."
> — 《社会契约论》*The Social Contract*, II.6

# 公意 · General Will (Volonté Générale)

## 定义与核心地位 / Definition and Central Role

公意（volonté générale）是卢梭政治哲学的拱顶概念，也是现代政治理论中最具争议的概念之一。在社会契约建立的共同体中，**公意是主权者（全体公民）以公共利益为对象的集体意志**。它不是个人意志的简单加总，而是经过公共审议形成的、指向共同善的理性判断。

The general will (volonté générale) is the keystone concept of Rousseau's political philosophy and one of the most contested concepts in modern political theory. In the community established by the social contract, **the general will is the collective will of the sovereign (all citizens) directed at the common good**. It is not a simple aggregate of individual wills but a rational judgment formed through public deliberation, aimed at the common good.

## 公意与众意的区分 / General Will vs. Will of All

卢梭做出了一个至关重要的区分：

Rousseau made a crucial distinction:

| | 公意 (Volonté Générale) | 众意 (Volonté de Tous) |
|---|---|---|
| 对象 | 公共利益 | 私人利益的总和 |
| 性质 | 质的统一体 | 量的加总 |
| 形成方式 | 公共审议与理性判断 | 个别意志的聚合 |
| 投票含义 | "我认为这对共同体最好" | "这对我的利益最好" |
| 结果 | 法律（loi） | 命令（décret） |

| | General Will | Will of All |
|---|---|---|
| Object | Common good | Sum of private interests |
| Nature | Qualitative unity | Quantitative aggregate |
| Formation | Public deliberation and rational judgment | Aggregation of particular wills |
| Voting meaning | "I judge this best for the community" | "This serves my interest best" |
| Result | Law (loi) | Decree (décret) |

卢梭写道："众意与公意之间往往有很大的差别：公意只着眼于公共的利益，而众意则着眼于私人的利益，众意只是个别意志的总和。"（II.3）

Rousseau wrote: "There is often a great deal of difference between the will of all and the general will: the latter considers only the common interest, while the former considers private interest, and is only a sum of particular wills." (II.3)

### 数学隐喻 / A Mathematical Metaphor

卢梭提供了一个独特的说明：如果从众意中"去掉"互相抵消的个别意志的"正负差异"，剩余的差额就是公意。这意味着公意不是所有人想要的东西的交集，而是当每个人抛开私人利益、以公民身份思考时共同指向的东西。

Rousseau offered a distinctive illustration: if one "removes" from the will of all the "pluses and minuses" of particular wills that cancel each other out, the remaining sum is the general will. This means the general will is not the intersection of what everyone wants, but what emerges when each person thinks as a citizen, setting aside private interest.

## 公意的四个属性 / Four Properties of the General Will

### 1. 不可转让（Inaliénable）

主权者不能将自己的意志转让给代表。"权力可以转移，但意志不可以。"这意味着主权必须直接行使，不能被代表——这是卢梭反对代议制民主的核心论据。

Sovereignty cannot transfer its will to representatives. "Power can be transferred, but not will." This means sovereignty must be exercised directly and cannot be represented — the core argument behind Rousseau's opposition to representative democracy.

### 2. 不可分割（Indivisible）

公意作为整体意志，不能被分解为不同的部分或权力分支。卢梭嘲笑政治理论家将主权分为立法权、行政权、司法权的做法——他认为这就像把人分成几个身体一样荒谬。行政、司法等只是主权的应用，不是主权的组成部分。

The general will, as a whole will, cannot be divided into parts or branches of power. Rousseau mocked political theorists who divide sovereignty into legislative, executive, and judicial powers — he deemed this as absurd as dividing a person into several bodies. Administration and justice are applications of sovereignty, not components of it.

### 3. 永远正确（Toujours Droite）

"公意永远是正确的（droite），人民永远不会被腐蚀。"但这不意味着人民的判断永远明智。卢梭区分了公意本身的正确性和人民对公意的识别能力：公意作为理性指向公共善的意志，其正确性是结构性的；但人民可能被"特殊意志"所蒙蔽，误把众意当作公意。

"The general will is always right (droite), and the people is never corrupted." But this does not mean the people's judgment is always enlightened. Rousseau distinguished the correctness of the general will itself from the people's ability to identify it: the general will's correctness as a rational orientation toward the common good is structural; but the people may be deceived by "particular wills," mistaking the will of all for the general will.

### 4. 普遍性（Généralité）

公意必须在两个方面是普遍的：**它来源于全体成员**，且**它适用于全体成员**。这意味着法律（公意的表达）必须是抽象的、一般的，不能针对特定个人或群体。"当我说法律的对象是普遍的，我的意思是法律考虑的是共同体的整体以及行为的抽象方面，而不是任何个人或个别行为。"

The general will must be general in two respects: **it emanates from all members** and **it applies to all members**. This means laws (expressions of the general will) must be abstract and general, never targeting specific individuals or groups. "When I say the object of the laws is always general, I mean that law considers subjects en masse and actions in the abstract, and never a particular person or action."

## 公意的形成条件 / Conditions for the Formation of the General Will

卢梭认为公意的正确表达需要几个条件：

Rousseau believed the general will's correct expression requires several conditions:

1. **充分的信息与审议**：公民需要对公共事务有充分了解，并进行理性审议。
2. **没有派系（factions）**：卢梭警告派系和利益集团会扭曲公意——它们使个别意志以集体的面目出现，压制真正的公意。"为了很好地表达公意，最重要的是国家之内不能有派系，每个公民只能表达自己的意见。"
3. **财产的相对平等**：极端的不平等使公意不可能——富人的意志必然压倒穷人的意志。
4. **小国规模**：卢梭认为公意只有在公民彼此认识、能够集会的共同体中才能有效运作。

1. **Adequate information and deliberation**: Citizens need sufficient knowledge of public affairs and must deliberate rationally.
2. **No factions**: Rousseau warned that factions and interest groups distort the general will — they make particular wills appear collective, suppressing the true general will. "For the general will to be well expressed, it is therefore important that there should be no sectional associations in the State, and that each citizen should express only his own opinion."
3. **Relative equality of property**: Extreme inequality makes the general will impossible — the rich will inevitably dominate the poor.
4. **Small-state scale**: Rousseau believed the general will functions effectively only in communities where citizens know each other and can assemble.

## "强迫自由"的悖论 / The Paradox of "Forced to Be Free"

卢梭最著名的（也是最受争议的）命题之一是：**"任何人拒不服从公意的，全体就要迫使他服从公意。这就是说人们要强迫他自由。"**（on le forcera d'être libre）

One of Rousseau's most famous (and most contested) propositions: **"Whoever refuses to obey the general will shall be compelled to do so by the whole body. This means nothing less than he will be forced to be free."** (on le forcera d'être libre)

### 卢梭的辩护 / Rousseau's Defense

卢梭认为这不是暴政而是自由的保障：既然公意表达了每个公民作为立法者的真实意志（而非其一时的偏好），那么违反法律就是违反自己的深层意志。强制服从法律实际上是在帮助人们实现他们真正想要的东西——公共善。这类似于家长强迫孩子服药：孩子在表面意志上反抗，但在深层意志上想要健康。

Rousseau argued this is not tyranny but freedom's guarantee: since the general will expresses each citizen's true will as legislator (not momentary preference), violating the law means violating one's own deep will. Coercion to obey the law actually helps people achieve what they truly want — the common good. This is analogous to parents forcing medicine on children: the child resists at the surface level but wants health at the deeper level.

### 批评者的解读 / Critics' Readings

- **伯林（Berlin）**：这是"积极自由"走向极权主义的经典表述——用一个抽象的"真实自我"取代个体实际表达的意志，为压迫提供了哲学辩护。
- **塔尔蒙（Talmon）**：这一命题是雅各宾恐怖统治的理论蓝图——罗伯斯庇尔正是以"人民真正想要的"之名实施恐怖。
- **罗尔斯（Rawls）**的温和解读：卢梭所说的"强迫自由"是指法律对所有人的平等约束——没有人可以凌驾于法律之上，这种普遍服从恰恰是自由的条件。

- **Berlin**: This is the classic expression of "positive liberty" turning totalitarian — substituting an abstract "true self" for individuals' actually expressed wills, philosophically justifying oppression.
- **Talmon**: This proposition is the theoretical blueprint for Jacobin terror — Robespierre invoked "what the people truly want" to implement terror.
- **Rawls** (moderate reading): "Forced to be free" means equal constraint of law on all — no one stands above the law, and this universal subjection is precisely the condition of freedom.

## 康德对公意的转化 / Kant's Transformation of the General Will

康德将卢梭的政治公意转化为道德领域的"定言命令"（kategorischer Imperativ）：道德法则就是理性存在者的普遍意志的自我立法。康德自己承认，正是卢梭教会了他"尊重人"——将人视为目的而非手段。公意的普遍性要求直接启发了康德的第一公式："只按照你同时能够意愿它成为一条普遍法则的那个准则去行动。"

Kant transformed Rousseau's political general will into the moral domain as the "categorical imperative" (kategorischer Imperativ): moral law is the self-legislation of rational beings' universal will. Kant himself acknowledged that Rousseau taught him to "respect man" — treating persons as ends rather than means. The general will's demand for universality directly inspired Kant's first formula: "Act only according to that maxim whereby you can at the same time will that it should become a universal law."

## 黑格尔对公意的发展 / Hegel's Development of the General Will

黑格尔认为卢梭的公意概念过于抽象——它只是"绝对自由的否定性"，缺乏制度化的实体。黑格尔将其发展为**伦理生活**（Sittlichkeit）的概念：公意必须在家庭、公民社会和国家等具体制度中得到实现。在黑格尔看来，卢梭的公意停留在"应当"的层面，而真正的自由需要在客观的制度世界中实现。

Hegel found Rousseau's general will too abstract — merely "the negativity of absolute freedom" lacking institutional substance. He developed it into the concept of **ethical life** (Sittlichkeit): the general will must be realized in concrete institutions — family, civil society, and the state. For Hegel, Rousseau's general will remains at the level of "ought," while true freedom requires realization in the objective institutional world.
