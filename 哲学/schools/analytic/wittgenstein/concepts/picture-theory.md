# 图像论 · Picture Theory of Meaning (Bildtheorie)

## 概念定位 / Conceptual Position

图像论是前期维特根斯坦在《逻辑哲学论》（Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus, 1921）中提出的核心理论，主张命题（proposition）是事实（fact）的逻辑图像（logical picture）。语言之所以能够表征世界，是因为命题与事实共享相同的逻辑形式（logical form）。这一理论深刻影响了20世纪的逻辑实证主义、语言哲学和形式语义学。

The picture theory is the central thesis of early Wittgenstein's *Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus* (1921), holding that a proposition is a logical picture (logisches Bild) of a fact. Language can represent the world because propositions and facts share the same logical form. This theory profoundly influenced 20th-century logical positivism, philosophy of language, and formal semantics.

## 核心论述 / Core Argument

### 世界、事实与对象 / World, Facts, and Objects

《逻辑哲学论》开篇宣示："世界是事实的总和，而非事物的总和"（1.1）。世界的结构是：

The *Tractatus* opens: "The world is the totality of facts, not of things" (1.1). The world's structure:

- **世界（World）** = 所有事实的总和 / The totality of all facts
- **事实（Fact）** = 事态（state of affairs）的存在 / The existence of states of affairs
- **事态（State of Affairs）** = 对象（objects）的结合 / Combinations of objects
- **对象（Object）** = 简单的、不可再分的实体 / Simple, indivisible entities

### 命题作为图像 / Propositions as Pictures

命题之所以能"说"某事，是因为它与它所描绘的事实共享逻辑结构。如同一幅画通过颜料在画布上的空间排列来描绘一个场景，命题通过名称在命题中的逻辑排列来描绘一个事实。关键是：名称对应对象，名称的排列方式对应对象的结合方式。

A proposition can "say" something because it shares logical structure with the fact it depicts. Just as a painting depicts a scene through the spatial arrangement of pigments on canvas, a proposition depicts a fact through the logical arrangement of names. The key: names correspond to objects, and the arrangement of names corresponds to the combination of objects.

### 逻辑形式 / Logical Form

图像论的核心洞见是：命题和事实必须共享某种东西，才能使表征成为可能——这个东西就是"逻辑形式"。逻辑形式不能被命题言说（say），只能被显示（show）。这就是《逻辑哲学论》中"可说"与"可显示"之间著名区分的来源。

The picture theory's core insight: propositions and facts must share something for representation to be possible — this "something" is logical form. Logical form cannot be said by propositions; it can only be shown. This is the source of the famous sayable/showable distinction in the *Tractatus*.

### 可说与不可说 / The Sayable and the Unsayable

图像论划定了语言的界限：只有描绘事实的命题（即自然科学命题）才是有意义的。伦理学、美学、宗教和形而上学的命题不描绘任何事实，因此在严格意义上是"无意义的"（unsinnig）——但这不意味着它们不重要。维特根斯坦说：正是这些不可言说的东西是最重要的——它们以神秘的方式"显示"自身。

The picture theory draws language's limits: only propositions depicting facts (i.e., propositions of natural science) are meaningful. Propositions of ethics, aesthetics, religion, and metaphysics depict no facts and are therefore strictly "nonsensical" (unsinnig) — but this does not mean they are unimportant. Wittgenstein says: precisely these unsayable things are the most important — they "show" themselves in a mystical way.

### 梯子比喻 / The Ladder Metaphor

维特根斯坦以自我指涉的方式结束《逻辑哲学论》（6.54）：理解他的人必须把书中的命题当作梯子——攀上去之后就要踢掉。命题本身最终也被揭示为"无意义的"——因为它们试图说出只能被显示的东西。但正是这种"无意义"使它们成为有用的哲学工具。

Wittgenstein ends the *Tractatus* self-referentially (6.54): anyone who understands him must treat the propositions as a ladder — climb up and then kick it away. The propositions themselves are ultimately revealed as "nonsensical" — because they attempt to say what can only be shown. But it is precisely this "nonsense" that makes them useful philosophical tools.

## 哲学意义 / Philosophical Significance

1. **逻辑实证主义的基础 / Foundation of Logical Positivism**: 维也纳学派将图像论发展为"可证实性原则"
2. **形式语义学 / Formal Semantics**: 命题与事实的同构思想影响了模型论（model theory）
3. **语言界限 / Limits of Language**: 为"什么是可以说的问题"提供了精确的形式化标准
4. **后期自我批判 / Later Self-Critique**: 维特根斯坦后期认为图像论是一种过度简化的语言模型

## 与东方思想的对话 / Dialogue with Eastern Thought

### 与唯识学"能所" / With Yogacara's "Subject-Object" Structure
唯识学分析认知结构时区分"能"（主观面，grāhaka）和"所"（客观面，grāhya），认为二者的对应关系是认知建构的产物而非实在的结构。这与图像论形成深层对话：维特根斯坦认为命题与事实的同构是逻辑的必然，唯识学则质疑这种"能所对应"本身是分别心的投射。

Yogacara analyzes cognitive structure by distinguishing "subject-side" (grāhaka) and "object-side" (grāhya), holding that their correspondence is a product of cognitive construction, not a real structure. This creates deep dialogue with the picture theory: Wittgenstein holds proposition-fact isomorphism is a logical necessity, while Yogacara questions whether this "subject-object correspondence" itself is a projection of discriminating mind.

### 与"指月"传统 / With the "Finger Pointing at the Moon" Tradition
佛教经典中反复使用"指月"比喻——语言如同指向月亮的手指，不应将手指当作月亮。这与维特根斯坦的梯子比喻惊人地相似：命题是指向真理的工具，使用后应被超越。不同之处在于，维特根斯坦的"月亮"（不可说者）是逻辑界限之外的东西，佛教的"月亮"是可以通过直觉体验到的觉悟。

Buddhist texts repeatedly use the "finger pointing at the moon" metaphor — language is like a finger pointing at the moon; one should not mistake the finger for the moon. This is strikingly similar to Wittgenstein's ladder metaphor: propositions are tools pointing toward truth, to be transcended after use. The difference: Wittgenstein's "moon" (the unsayable) is what lies beyond the limits of logic; Buddhism's "moon" is the awakening accessible through direct experience.

## 后期批判 / The Later Critique

后期维特根斯坦认为图像论犯了"过度一般化"的错误：它把语言的一种功能（描述事实）当作唯一功能，忽视了语言的多样性。语言游戏概念取代了图像论——语言不只是"描画"世界，还在无数种活动中被使用。

Later Wittgenstein held that the picture theory committed the error of "over-generalization": it treated one function of language (describing facts) as the only function, ignoring language's diversity. The language-game concept replaced the picture theory — language does not merely "picture" the world but is used in countless kinds of activities.
