# 吸引力（杂耍蒙太奇）· Montage of Attractions

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

"吸引力"（attraction / аттракцион）是爱森斯坦最早提出的理论概念之一，首次系统阐述于1923年的论文《杂耍蒙太奇》（*Montazh attraktsionov*）。在这篇论文中，爱森斯坦将戏剧（后来扩展到电影）的基本构成单元定义为"吸引力"——即任何能对观众产生可计算的心理冲击（shock）的独立瞬间或场景单元。一个"吸引力"不必是"美的"或"真实的"，它的唯一标准是是否有效地对观众的心理产生了预定的效果。

The "attraction" (аттракцион) is one of Eisenstein's earliest theoretical concepts, first systematically articulated in the 1923 essay "Montage of Attractions." In this essay, Eisenstein defined the basic constitutive unit of theater (later extended to cinema) as the "attraction" — any independent moment or scene-unit capable of producing a calculable psychological shock on the spectator. An "attraction" need not be "beautiful" or "true"; its sole criterion is whether it effectively produces the intended effect on the spectator's psyche.

这一概念标志着爱森斯坦对自然主义戏剧的根本决裂。他反对斯坦尼斯拉夫斯基式的"体验派"表演和"第四面墙"的幻觉——在自然主义戏剧中，观众被邀请"沉浸"于剧情；而在杂耍蒙太奇中，观众被"冲击"出被动状态，被迫进行积极的意义建构。爱森斯坦将这种从"沉浸"到"冲击"的转变视为戏剧和电影从"模仿"到"工程"的根本升级。

This concept marks Eisenstein's fundamental break with naturalist theater. He opposed Stanislavskian "experiential" acting and the "fourth wall" illusion — in naturalist theater, the spectator is invited to "immerse" in the drama; in montage of attractions, the spectator is "shocked" out of passivity, forced into active meaning-construction. Eisenstein viewed this shift from "immersion" to "shock" as a fundamental upgrade from "imitation" to "engineering" in theater and cinema.

## 理论内涵 / Theoretical Content

爱森斯坦将"吸引力"定义为："任何进攻性的（aggressive）要素——也就是说，任何能使观众经受某种感性或心理作用的要素。"（*Montage of Attractions*, 1923）关键在于，吸引力不是叙事的有机组成部分——它可以从叙事链条中"抽取"出来，然后按照蒙太奇的原则被重新"组装"。这种"组装"不是按照因果逻辑（A导致B），而是按照效果逻辑（A冲击观众，B冲击观众，A+B产生概念C）进行。

Eisenstein defined the "attraction" as: "any aggressive element — that is, any element that subjects the spectator to a certain sensory or psychological effect." (*Montage of Attractions*, 1923) Crucially, the attraction is not an organic part of narrative — it can be "extracted" from the narrative chain and then "assembled" according to montage principles. This "assembly" proceeds not by causal logic (A causes B) but by effect logic (A shocks the spectator, B shocks the spectator, A+B produces concept C).

杂耍蒙太奇的核心操作是"抽取"（extraction）和"并置"（juxtaposition）。以1924年的电影《罢工》为例：影片中工人被军队屠杀的画面与屠宰场宰牛的镜头被交叉剪辑在一起。宰牛的镜头不是叙事的有机部分（故事中没有屠宰场），它是被"抽取"出来、按照蒙太奇原则"并置"到屠杀场景中的——其目的是制造一个视觉隐喻："工人像牲口一样被屠杀。"这种操作完全脱离了叙事因果性，而是遵循冲击—效果的逻辑。

The core operations of montage of attractions are "extraction" and "juxtaposition." In the 1924 film *Strike*, images of workers being massacred by troops are cross-cut with footage of cattle slaughter in an abattoir. The slaughter footage is not an organic part of the narrative (there is no abattoir in the story); it is "extracted" and "juxtaposed" into the massacre scene according to montage principles — its purpose is to create a visual metaphor: "workers are slaughtered like cattle." This operation completely departs from narrative causality, following instead the shock-effect logic.

爱森斯坦后来将吸引力的概念从戏剧扩展到电影，并进一步发展为更系统的冲突蒙太奇理论。但"吸引力"的核心洞见——艺术的基本单元是冲击而非叙事——贯穿了他的整个思想生涯。他晚年关于迪斯尼动画的研究中，仍然在使用"吸引力"的框架来分析卡通中的"可塑性"（plasmaticness）——即动画形象脱离物理法则、自由变形的能力如何冲击观众的认知预期。

Eisenstein later extended the concept of attractions from theater to cinema, further developing it into the more systematic theory of conflict montage. But the core insight of "attractions" — that art's basic unit is shock rather than narrative — runs through his entire intellectual career. In his late studies of Disney animation, he was still using the "attractions" framework to analyze "plasmaticness" in cartoons — how the animated image's capacity to escape physical laws and freely transform shocks the spectator's cognitive expectations.

## 思想史脉络 / Intellectual History

吸引力概念的提出有着深刻的历史语境。1920年代初的苏联正处于内战后的重建期，无产阶级文化运动（Proletkult）试图创造一种全新的、革命的艺术形式。爱森斯坦在Proletkult剧团工作期间，深受梅耶霍德的"生物力学"戏剧和构成主义的"生产艺术"（productivism）影响。梅耶霍德将演员的身体视为可以精确操控的"机器"，构成主义将艺术家视为"工程师"——这些思想为吸引力概念提供了实践基础。

The emergence of the attractions concept had a profound historical context. The early 1920s Soviet Union was in post-civil war reconstruction, and the Proletkult movement sought to create an entirely new, revolutionary art form. During his time in the Proletkult theater troupe, Eisenstein was deeply influenced by Meyerhold's "biomechanical" theater and Constructivism's "productivism." Meyerhold treated the actor's body as a precisely controllable "machine," and Constructivism treated the artist as an "engineer" — these ideas provided the practical foundation for the attractions concept.

在更广阔的思想史中，吸引力概念与同时代的德国剧作家布莱希特（Bertolt Brecht）的"间离效果"（Verfremdungseffekt）形成了引人注目的平行。两者都反对自然主义的"沉浸"，都主张通过"冲击"使观众从被动消费中觉醒，都试图将艺术转化为政治启蒙的工具。然而，爱森斯坦更强调感官冲击的直接性，布莱希特更强调理性距离的反思性。

In broader intellectual history, the attractions concept forms a striking parallel with the "alienation effect" (Verfremdungseffekt) of the contemporaneous German playwright Bertolt Brecht. Both opposed naturalist "immersion," both advocated "shocking" the spectator out of passive consumption, and both sought to transform art into a tool of political enlightenment. However, Eisenstein emphasized the immediacy of sensory shock, while Brecht emphasized the reflectivity of rational distance.

## 关键文本 / Key Passages

> "吸引力是戏剧中一切有效要素的基本单元，它独立于叙事之外，其唯一的主人是观众的感知。"
> "The attraction is the basic unit of all effective elements in theater, independent of narrative, whose only master is the spectator's perception."
> — Eisenstein, "Montage of Attractions" (1923)

> "杂耍蒙太奇就是吸引力的自由组合——从叙事中抽取出来的独立要素，按照效果的需要被重新组装。"
> "Montage of attractions is the free assembly of attractions — independent elements extracted from narrative, reassembled according to the requirements of effect."
> — Eisenstein, "Montage of Attractions" (1923)

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

吸引力概念在当代文化中最明显的体现是短视频平台（TikTok、抖音、Instagram Reels）的内容逻辑。每一个短视频都是一个精心设计的"吸引力"单元——它不追求完整的叙事，而追求在几秒钟内对观众产生最大的心理冲击。"转场"技巧（transition）就是数字时代的杂耍蒙太奇：通过突然的画面切换来制造冲击效果。

The most evident manifestation of the attractions concept in contemporary culture is the content logic of short-form video platforms (TikTok, Instagram Reels). Every short video is a carefully designed "attraction" unit — it pursues not complete narrative but maximum psychological impact on the spectator within seconds. "Transition" techniques are the montage of attractions in the digital age: creating shock effects through sudden image switches.

在体验设计和主题乐园领域，迪士尼乐园的游乐项目就是一种现代"吸引力"——每个项目都被设计为一个独立的感官冲击单元，游客在不同项目之间的移动构成了一种空间化的杂耍蒙太奇。

In experience design and theme parks, Disneyland rides are a modern "attraction" — each ride is designed as an independent sensory-impact unit, and the visitor's movement between rides constitutes a spatialized montage of attractions.

## 批评与回应 / Critiques and Responses

**人文主义批评 / Humanist Critique:** 许多批评者认为吸引力理论将观众物化为被操控的"材料"，忽视了观众的主体性和自由意志。这种批评在爱森斯坦1920年代的文本中确有依据——他曾明确将观众比作"待加工的原料"。
Many critics argue that attraction theory reduces the spectator to manipulated "material," ignoring spectator subjectivity and free will. This critique finds textual support in Eisenstein's 1920s writings — he explicitly compared spectators to "raw material to be processed."

**回应 / Response:** 爱森斯坦晚年修改了这一立场，开始强调观众的积极参与——蒙太奇需要观众主动"完成"意义的建构，而非被动接受预设的效果。
Eisenstein revised this position in later years, beginning to emphasize the spectator's active participation — montage requires the spectator to actively "complete" meaning-construction rather than passively receiving preset effects.

## 跨文化回响 / Cross-cultural Echoes

吸引力概念与中国戏曲的"绝活"传统有着有趣的对应。在京剧、川剧等中国传统戏曲中，演员会在剧情中插入高难度的技艺展示（如变脸、喷火、翻跟头），这些"绝活"本质上是独立的"吸引力"单元——它们的冲击力来自技艺本身而非叙事逻辑。爱森斯坦的"吸引力"理论与这种"绝活"美学在结构上高度相似：两者都将表演分解为独立的冲击单元，然后按照效果逻辑进行组装。

The attractions concept has an interesting correspondence with the "special skill" (juehuo) tradition in Chinese opera. In Beijing opera, Sichuan opera, and other traditional Chinese theatrical forms, actors insert highly difficult technical displays (face-changing, fire-breathing, somersaults) into the plot — these "special skills" are essentially independent "attraction" units, whose impact derives from the skill itself rather than narrative logic. Eisenstein's "attractions" theory and this "special skill" aesthetics are structurally highly similar: both decompose performance into independent shock units, then assemble them according to effect logic.
