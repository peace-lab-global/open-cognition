# 米开朗基罗 · Michelangelo Buonarroti

## 基本信息 / Basic Information

米开朗基罗·迪·洛多维科·博那罗蒂·西蒙尼（Michelangelo di Lodovico Buonarroti Simoni，1475年3月6日—1564年2月18日）是意大利文艺复兴时期最崇高的艺术家，被同时代人尊称为"神圣的"（Il Divino）。他出生于佛罗伦萨共和国的卡普雷塞（Caprese），成长于佛罗伦萨，一生主要活跃于佛罗伦萨与罗马两座城市。米开朗基罗以雕塑家自居，却在绘画、建筑和诗歌领域同样达到了巅峰——他是西方艺术史上极少数在多种媒介中均实现最高成就的创造者。

Michelangelo di Lodovico Buonarroti Simoni (March 6, 1475 – February 18, 1564) was the most sublime artist of the Italian Renaissance, called "Il Divino" (The Divine One) by his contemporaries. Born in Caprese in the Florentine Republic and raised in Florence, he worked primarily in Florence and Rome. Michelangelo considered himself above all a sculptor, yet achieved the highest accomplishments in painting, architecture, and poetry — one of the very few creators in Western art history to reach the summit across multiple media.

他的艺术生涯横跨近七十年，从1488年进入基尔兰达约（Ghirlandaio）工作室学画，到1564年以八十八岁高龄在罗马去世时仍在雕刻《隆达尼尼圣母怜子》。他的一生见证了盛期文艺复兴的兴起与鼎盛、宗教改革的震荡、以及矫饰主义（Mannerism）的萌发。他的艺术哲学——将雕塑视为从物质中解放精神的过程——深刻影响了此后五百年的西方艺术观念。

His artistic career spanned nearly seventy years, from his apprenticeship in Ghirlandaio's workshop in 1488 to his death at age 88 in Rome while still carving the Rondanini Pieta. His lifetime witnessed the rise and zenith of the High Renaissance, the upheaval of the Reformation, and the emergence of Mannerism. His artistic philosophy — viewing sculpture as the process of liberating spirit from matter — profoundly shaped Western conceptions of art for five centuries thereafter.

## 核心命题 / Core Theses

1. **雕塑即解放 / Sculpture as Liberation**：形象已完整存在于石块之中，雕塑家只需把多余的部分去掉——艺术是去除遮蔽的哲学，是从物质中释放精神的形而上过程。The figure already exists complete within the stone; the sculptor need only remove the excess — art is a philosophy of subtraction, a metaphysical process of releasing spirit from matter.

2. **崇高（Terribilità） / Terribilita (Awe-Inspiring Sublimity)**：伟大艺术应令人敬畏而非仅仅愉悦，以超人的体量与力量感将观者推向崇高——这是一种压倒性的、令人震颤的美学体验。Great art should inspire awe rather than mere pleasure, elevating the viewer toward the sublime through superhuman scale and force — an overwhelming, tremor-inducing aesthetic experience.

3. **身体即精神 / The Body as Spirit**：理想化的人体是灵魂与神圣理念的可见化身，肉身承载形而上的张力——每一块肌肉的扭动都是精神挣扎的外化。The idealized human body is the visible incarnation of soul and divine idea; flesh carries metaphysical tension — every torsion of muscle is the exteriorization of spiritual struggle.

4. **未完成即真理 / Non-finito as Truth**：诸多"未完成"作品恰恰暴露了从物质中挣扎显现的过程本身，揭示了艺术创作中理念与材料之间永恒的张力。Many "unfinished" works expose the very process of struggling emergence from matter, revealing the eternal tension between idea and material in artistic creation.

5. **disegno（设计/素描）/ Disegno (Design/Drawing)**：disegno既是构图技艺也是内在观念（idea）的呈现，是一切视觉艺术的智性基础——它使艺术从手艺上升为自由学科。Disegno is both compositional technique and the presentation of inner idea — the intellectual foundation of all visual arts, elevating art from craft to liberal discipline.

6. **跨媒介统一 / Cross-Media Unity**：雕塑、绘画、建筑、诗歌是同一disegno的不同呈现——艺术家不应被单一媒介束缚，真正的创造力超越材料界限。Sculpture, painting, architecture, and poetry are different manifestations of the same disegno — the true artist should not be confined to a single medium; genuine creativity transcends material boundaries.

## 思想脉络 / Intellectual Development

### 早期：佛罗伦萨与均衡理想（1488–1505） / Early Period: Florence and the Ideal of Harmony

米开朗基罗的艺术形成深受三个因素的塑造：石匠家庭的体力经验、美第奇宫廷的新柏拉图主义智识氛围、以及对古典雕塑的直接研习。幼年丧母后寄养于塞蒂尼亚诺（Settignano）的石匠之家，他日后自嘲"与石匠的奶水一起吸入了雕刻的技艺"。1488年入基尔兰达约工作室学画，仅一年后便转入洛伦佐·德·美第奇（Lorenzo de' Medici）资助的"美第奇花园"——一个集古典雕塑收藏、复制和创作于一体的艺术学园。在这里，他直接受到马尔西利奥·费奇诺（Marsilio Ficino）和皮科·德拉·米兰多拉（Pico della Mirandola）等新柏拉图主义哲学家的影响，形成了"理念先于物质"的核心信念。

Michelangelo's artistic formation was shaped by three forces: the physical experience of a stonecutter's household, the Neoplatonic intellectual atmosphere of the Medici court, and direct study of classical sculpture. Orphaned of his mother early, he was fostered with a stonecutter's family in Settignano — he later quipped that he "suckled in the craft of sculpture with his nurse's milk." In 1488 he entered Ghirlandaio's workshop, but after only a year transferred to the "Medici Garden," an art academy combining classical sculpture collection, copying, and creation, sponsored by Lorenzo de' Medici. Here he absorbed the influence of Neoplatonic philosophers Marsilio Ficino and Pico della Mirandola, forming his core conviction that "idea precedes matter."

1496年首次赴罗马，1498–1499年创作了《圣母怜子》（Pietà），以二十三岁的年纪实现了盛期文艺复兴的均衡理想——圣母的安详面容与基督身体的柔和曲线达到了古典式的完美统一。1501年返回佛罗伦萨后，接受了一块被弃置四十余年的巨型大理石，于1504年完成了《大卫》（David）。这座5.17米的巨像将城市共和国的自由理想与完美人体合一，成为文艺复兴公民精神的最高象征。

His first trip to Rome in 1496 led to the Pieta (1498–1499), achieving the High Renaissance ideal of harmony at age twenty-three — the Virgin's serene face and Christ's softly curved body reaching classical perfection. Returning to Florence in 1501, he accepted a massive marble block abandoned for forty years and completed David in 1504. This 5.17-meter colossus united republican civic ideals with the perfect human form, becoming the supreme symbol of Renaissance civic spirit.

### 中期：西斯廷天顶与陵墓（1505–1534） / Middle Period: The Sistine Ceiling and the Tomb

1505年，教皇朱利叶斯二世（Julius II）召米开朗基罗赴罗马，委托修建其陵墓——这将成为一个纠缠他四十年的"陵墓悲剧"。原设计是一座三层巨型陵墓，包含四十余座雕塑，但因经费和政治原因一再缩减。1508年，教皇又迫使他接受西斯廷礼拜堂天顶的湿壁画工程。米开朗基罗视绘画为"女人的艺术"，对这一任务充满抗拒，却在1508–1512年间以惊人的意志力独自完成了约五百平方米的天顶画——以《创世纪》为核心，辅以先知、女巫和基督祖先的形象，创造了一部神创宇宙的视觉史诗。

In 1505, Pope Julius II summoned Michelangelo to Rome to build his tomb — a "tragedy of the tomb" that would haunt him for forty years. The original design was a three-tiered colossus with over forty statues, repeatedly reduced by finances and politics. In 1508 the Pope forced him to accept the Sistine Chapel ceiling fresco commission. Michelangelo considered painting "women's art" and resisted fiercely, yet between 1508 and 1512 he single-handedly completed approximately 500 square meters of ceiling frescoes — centered on Genesis, flanked by prophets, sibyls, and ancestors of Christ — creating a visual epic of divine creation.

西斯廷天顶的革命性在于：以雕塑家的眼光处理绘画——人物具有雕塑般的体量感和解剖学精确性，画面构图以人体的扭转（contrapposto）和张力取代了传统叙事的线性逻辑。与此同时，陵墓项目中的《摩西》（Moses, 1513–1515）和"奴隶"系列（Dying Slave, Rebellious Slave等）深化了身体与束缚的母题——被捆绑的人体挣扎着要从物质中解放，成为人类精神困境的寓言。

The ceiling's revolution lay in treating painting with a sculptor's eye — figures possess sculptural mass and anatomical precision, composition replacing linear narrative logic with bodily torsion (contrapposto) and tension. Meanwhile, the tomb project's Moses (1513–1515) and the Slave series (Dying Slave, Rebellious Slave, etc.) deepened the motif of body and bondage — bound figures struggling for liberation from matter, allegories of the human spiritual predicament.

### 晚期：审判、建筑与诗歌（1534–1564） / Late Period: Judgment, Architecture, and Poetry

1534年，米开朗基罗永久定居罗马。1536–1541年间，受教皇保罗三世委托，他在西斯廷礼拜堂的祭坛墙上绘制了《最后的审判》（The Last Judgment）。与天顶画的秩序与和谐不同，《最后的审判》呈现出一个漩涡般的末日场景——赤裸的人体在基督审判者的威严手势下翻涌升沉，没有天堂的安宁，只有救赎与堕落的剧烈张力。这幅作品深刻反映了宗教改革时代的精神危机，也标志着米开朗基罗从盛期文艺复兴的均衡走向一种更内省、更不安的美学。

In 1534, Michelangelo settled permanently in Rome. Between 1536 and 1541, commissioned by Pope Paul III, he painted The Last Judgment on the altar wall of the Sistine Chapel. Unlike the ceiling's order and harmony, The Last Judgment presents a vortex-like apocalyptic scene — naked bodies churning beneath Christ the Judge's commanding gesture, no heavenly peace, only the violent tension between salvation and damnation. This work profoundly reflected the spiritual crisis of the Reformation era, marking Michelangelo's departure from High Renaissance equilibrium toward a more introspective, unsettled aesthetic.

晚年的米开朗基罗日益转向建筑与诗歌。1546年被任命为圣彼得大教堂首席建筑师，设计了其标志性的穹顶——以佛罗伦萨圣母百花大教堂的穹顶为灵感，却以更强烈的垂直动势和肋拱结构创造了罗马天际线的永恒标志。他的诗歌创作在此时达到高峰，约三百首十四行诗和牧歌表达了对神圣之爱的渴望、对肉体衰老的悲叹、以及对艺术虚荣的深刻反省。"十字架诗"组中，他写道："绘画和雕塑已无法平息我的灵魂，它已转向那神圣的爱。"

The aging Michelangelo turned increasingly to architecture and poetry. Appointed chief architect of St. Peter's Basilica in 1546, he designed its iconic dome — inspired by Florence's Santa Maria del Fiore but with a more forceful vertical dynamism and ribbed structure, creating an eternal landmark on Rome's skyline. His poetry reached its peak in this period, with approximately 300 sonnets and madrigals expressing longing for divine love, lamentation over bodily decay, and deep reflection on artistic vanity. In the "Crucifixion poems," he wrote: "Neither painting nor sculpture can now calm my soul, turned toward that divine love."

## 重要概念 / Key Concepts

- **崇高 / Terribilità**：令人敬畏的、近乎压倒性的力量感，区别于温润的"美"——详见 [terribilita](concepts/terribilita.md)。An awe-inspiring, nearly overwhelming sense of power, distinct from gentle "beauty."

- **雕塑至上 / Sculpture Primacy**：雕塑是最高的艺术形式，因为它是从物质中直接解放精神的过程——详见 [sculpture-primacy](concepts/sculpture-primacy.md)。Sculpture as the highest art form, being the direct liberation of spirit from matter.

- **西斯廷天顶 / Sistine Ceiling**：以雕塑思维改造绘画的里程碑——详见 [sistine-ceiling](concepts/sistine-ceiling.md)。The landmark of transforming painting through sculptural thinking.

- **新柏拉图主义艺术 / Neoplatonism in Art**：理念先于物质，艺术是理念的感性显现——详见 [neoplatonism-art](concepts/neoplatonism-art.md)。Idea precedes matter; art is the sensuous manifestation of idea.

- **人体与神圣 / Human Form Divine**：理想人体是神圣精神的可见载体——详见 [human-form-divine](concepts/human-form-divine.md)。The ideal human body as visible vessel of divine spirit.

- **未完成（Non-finito）**：刻意或命运性的未完成状态，使形象与物质处于持续的显现之中。A state of deliberate or fated incompletion, keeping figure and matter in perpetual emergence.

- **disegno（设计/素描）**：既是构图技艺，也是内在观念（idea）的呈现——文艺复兴艺术的核心智性概念。Both compositional technique and presentation of inner idea — the core intellectual concept of Renaissance art.

- **湿壁画（Fresco）**：在新鲜灰泥上作画使颜料与墙面化学合一，文艺复兴大型绘画的标准工艺。Painting on fresh plaster so pigment chemically bonds with wall — the standard technique for Renaissance monumental painting.

- **contrapposto（对立式平衡）**：人体重心置于一足，造成臀肩反向倾斜的动态平衡——古典雕塑的核心姿态法则。Weight on one foot, creating dynamic equilibrium through counter-tilt of hips and shoulders — the core postural principle of classical sculpture.

- **陵墓悲剧 / Tragedy of the Tomb**：朱利叶斯二世陵墓项目四十年间的反复缩减，成为艺术理想与现实妥协的寓言。The forty-year repeated reduction of Julius II's tomb project, an allegory of artistic ideal versus pragmatic compromise.

## 思想坐标 / Intellectual Coordinates

- **承接 / Inherits**: 多纳泰罗（Donatello）的雕塑传统；吉贝尔蒂（Ghiberti）的浮雕叙事；费奇诺（Ficino）的新柏拉图主义哲学圈；古典希腊罗马雕塑（尤以《拉奥孔》和《观景楼躯干》为代表）
- **批判 / Critiques**: 佩鲁吉诺（Perugino）等前辈的"甜腻"风格；达·芬奇（Leonardo）将绘画置于雕塑之上的立场；北方哥特艺术的"缺乏理念"；教廷将艺术工具化的政治倾向
- **影响 / Influences**: 矫饰主义（Mannerism）——蓬托尔莫（Pontormo）、罗索（Rosso Fiorentino）；巴洛克雕塑——贝尼尼（Bernini）直接继承其崇高美学；浪漫主义对天才与崇高的崇拜；现代纪念碑观念——从罗丹（Rodin）到当代公共雕塑

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

- **公共纪念碑美学 / Public Monument Aesthetics**: 《大卫》和圣彼得穹顶仍是城市纪念性与公民身份建构的范本——当代城市广场、纪念碑和公共艺术的设计逻辑仍可追溯至米开朗基罗的尺度与姿态法则。David and St. Peter's dome remain paradigms for civic monumentality — contemporary public art design logic traces back to Michelangelo's rules of scale and gesture.

- **解剖学与数字角色建模 / Anatomy and Digital Character Modeling**: 其对人体动态（torso的扭转、肌肉在运动中的变化）的精确把握影响了现代角色建模、CG动画和解剖教学——好莱坞和游戏工业的角色设计师仍以米开朗基罗的人体比例和动态为参照。His precise grasp of bodily dynamics influenced modern character modeling, CG animation, and anatomical teaching — Hollywood and game industry character designers still reference Michelangelo's proportions and dynamics.

- **艺术心理学 / Psychology of Art**: "未完成"（non-finito）被用于研究创作焦虑、完美主义、以及过程与结果的关系——现代心理治疗中的"过程取向"与米开朗基罗的未完成美学形成了跨世纪的共鸣。Non-finito is used to study creative anxiety, perfectionism, and the process-versus-product relationship — modern process-oriented psychotherapy resonates across centuries with Michelangelo's aesthetics of incompletion.

- **跨媒介创作范式 / Cross-Media Creative Paradigm**: 雕塑—绘画—建筑—诗歌的统一范式持续启发跨学科艺术家——当代"总体艺术"（Gesamtkunstwerk）的理念可追溯至米开朗基罗的disegno统一论。The unified paradigm of sculpture-painting-architecture-poetry continues to inspire interdisciplinary artists — the contemporary Gesamtkunstwerk concept traces back to Michelangelo's disegno unity theory.

- **建筑空间与城市设计 / Architectural Space and Urban Design**: 卡比托利欧广场（Campidoglio）的椭圆形地面图案和梯形空间透视是城市空间设计的经典案例——其"反透视"手法（以梯形平面制造空间纵深感）至今影响着广场与公共空间的设计。The Campidoglio's oval ground pattern and trapezoidal perspective remains a classic case in urban spatial design — its "counter-perspective" technique continues to influence plaza and public space design.

- **艺术修复理论 / Art Restoration Theory**: 1980–1990年代西斯廷天顶的大规模修复引发了关于修复伦理的深刻辩论——"应恢复到什么状态"的问题至今是文物保护领域的核心议题。The 1980s–1990s Sistine Ceiling restoration sparked profound debates on restoration ethics — the question "to what state should we restore?" remains central to cultural heritage conservation.

- **身体哲学与女性主义批评 / Body Philosophy and Feminist Critique**: 米开朗基罗对人体的极端理想化引发了关于性别、力量和身体政治的当代讨论——其女性形象常被批评为"男性化的"，这一争议丰富了对文艺复兴身体观念的反思。Michelangelo's extreme idealization of the body has sparked contemporary discussions on gender, power, and body politics — his female figures are often criticized as "masculinized," enriching reflection on Renaissance body concepts.

## 常见误读 / Common Misreadings

- ❌ "米开朗基罗讨厌绘画、只爱雕塑。" / "Michelangelo hated painting and only loved sculpture."
  → ✅ 他视雕塑为至上，却以湿壁画达到了绘画史的高峰——二者是同一disegno的两种呈现。他的书信中对绘画的贬低往往出于对被迫接受绘画委托的愤怒，而非对绘画本身的美学否定。He regarded sculpture as supreme yet achieved painting's pinnacle through fresco — both are manifestations of the same disegno. His letters disparaging painting often expressed anger at being forced into painting commissions, not aesthetic denial of painting itself.

- ❌ "他是孤独的天才、与现代世界无关。" / "He was a lonely genius, irrelevant to the modern world."
  → ✅ 他深度卷入教皇政治、家族忠诚与共和理想，其崇高美学深刻塑造了现代纪念碑观念。他的书信揭示了一个深度社会化的艺术家——管理家族财产、与教皇博弈、指导年轻艺术家。He was deeply involved in papal politics, family loyalty, and republican ideals; his sublime aesthetics profoundly shaped modern monumentality. His letters reveal a deeply social artist — managing family estates, negotiating with popes, mentoring younger artists.

- ❌ "西斯廷天顶是仰躺画的。" / "The Sistine ceiling was painted lying on his back."
  → ✅ 他站在自制的脚手架上仰头作画，姿态虽痛苦却非传说中那种平躺。他自己在诗中抱怨："我的胡子朝向天，我的脑袋弯向肩……画笔滴下颜料，在我脸上绘出丰富的马赛克。" He stood on scaffolding he designed himself, head craned upward — painful but not the legendary supine position. He complained in verse: "My beard turns up to heaven, my head bends toward my shoulder... the brush drips paint, making a rich mosaic of my face."

- ❌ "晚年作品比早期更完美。" / "Late works are more perfect than early ones."
  → ✅ 晚期反而更粗粝、未完成、内省——完美并非其终点。《隆达尼尼圣母怜子》几乎剥离了一切"美"的外壳，只剩下精神挣扎的赤裸痕迹。这一转变并非技术的衰退，而是价值观的根本转向——从追求外在完美转向表达内在真实。Late works are rougher, more unfinished, more introspective — perfection was not his destination. The Rondanini Pieta strips away nearly all "beauty," leaving only naked traces of spiritual struggle. This shift represents not technical decline but a fundamental transformation of values — from pursuing external perfection to expressing inner truth.

## 东西方对话 / East-West Dialogue

米开朗基罗的艺术哲学与东方美学传统形成了深刻的对照与互补。在"形神"关系上，中国传统画论追求"以形写神"（顾恺之）乃至"遗形取神"，而米开朗基罗的路径恰恰相反——他通过极度精确和理想化的"形"（人体解剖）来呈现"神"（灵魂与神圣理念）。二者殊途同归，均指向超越物质表象的精神真实。

Michelangelo's artistic philosophy forms profound contrast and complementarity with East Asian aesthetic traditions. On the "form-spirit" (形神) relationship, Chinese painting theory pursues "using form to express spirit" (顾恺之) or even "abandoning form to capture spirit," while Michelangelo's path is precisely opposite — through extremely precise and idealized "form" (human anatomy) he presents "spirit" (soul and divine idea). Both paths converge, pointing toward spiritual truth beyond material appearance.

在"留白"与"未完成"之间也存在有趣的呼应。中国水墨画的"留白"是有意为之的空间哲学——虚处即实在；米开朗基罗的non-finito虽然成因不同（常因外部约束而非自觉选择），却在效果上达到了类似的哲学深度——未雕凿的大理石与已完成的人物之间的张力，恰如留白与墨迹之间的对话。

An interesting resonance exists between "blank space" (留白) and non-finito. Chinese ink painting's blank space is deliberate spatial philosophy — emptiness as substance. Michelangelo's non-finito, though arising from different causes (often external constraints rather than conscious choice), achieves comparable philosophical depth — the tension between uncarved marble and finished figures mirrors the dialogue between blank space and ink marks.

日本美学中的"侘寂"（wabi-sabi）——在不完美和无常中发现美——与米开朗基罗晚期的非完美主义形成了跨文化的呼应。《隆达尼尼圣母怜子》的粗粝表面和极端简化，与侘寂美学所珍视的"不完整之美"和"岁月痕迹"产生了意外的共鸣。

Japanese wabi-sabi aesthetics — finding beauty in imperfection and impermanence — forms a cross-cultural echo with Michelangelo's late anti-perfectionism. The rough surfaces and extreme simplification of the Rondanini Pieta unexpectedly resonate with wabi-sabi's treasured "beauty of incompleteness" and "traces of time."

## 跨学科关联 / Interdisciplinary Connections

- [柏拉图/新柏拉图主义 / Plato & Neoplatonism] `[同源 / Shared Source]` 理念先行于物质，艺术是理念的感性显现。Idea precedes matter; art is the sensuous manifestation of idea.
- [崇高美学 / Sublime Aesthetics] `[互补 / Complementary]` terribilità是崇高的身体化范例——伯克（Burke）和康德（Kant）的崇高理论在米开朗基罗的作品中找到了最佳的视觉实证。Terribilita is the embodied exemplar of the sublime — Burke's and Kant's sublime theories find their best visual evidence in Michelangelo's works.
- [康德美学 / Kant's Aesthetics] `[平行 / Parallel]` 崇高与数学/力量的量级体验——《最后的审判》的空间压倒性正是康德"力学的崇高"的视觉等价物。The sublime as magnitude experience — The Last Judgment's spatial overwhelmingness is the visual equivalent of Kant's "dynamic sublime."
- [达·芬奇 / Leonardo da Vinci] `[对立·平行 / Opposition-Parallel]` 同为盛期文艺复兴巨匠，在disegno概念之上分道扬镳——达·芬奇以绘画为科学认知，米开朗基罗以雕塑为精神解放。Both High Renaissance giants diverged beyond disegno — Leonardo pursued painting as scientific cognition, Michelangelo pursued sculpture as spiritual liberation.
- [贝尼尼 / Bernini] `[影响 / Influence]` 巴洛克雕塑的直接继承者——贝尼尼的《圣特蕾莎的狂喜》将米开朗基罗的崇高推向了戏剧化的极致。Direct successor in Baroque sculpture — Bernini's Ecstasy of Saint Teresa pushed Michelangelo's sublime to dramatic extremes.
- [浪漫主义 / Romanticism] `[影响 / Influence]` 德拉克洛瓦（Delacroix）和布莱克（Blake）均深受米开朗基罗人体观的影响——布莱克甚至直接临摹了其作品并发展出"人体即神圣幻象"的哲学。Delacroix and Blake were deeply influenced by Michelangelo's body vision — Blake directly copied his works and developed a philosophy of "the human body as divine vision."

## 进阶阅读 / Further Reading

### 原典 / Primary Sources

- Michelangelo, *Rime* (诗集) — 约三百首十四行诗和牧歌，以意大利文写成，是其精神世界的直接记录
- Giorgio Vasari, *Le Vite de' più eccellenti pittori, scultori e architettori* (《艺苑名人传》, 1550/1568) — 米开朗基罗最早的完整传记
- Ascanio Condivi, *Vita di Michelagnolo Buonarroti* (《米开朗基罗传》, 1553) — 经米开朗基罗本人审定的传记，可视为其自述
- Casa Buonarroti Archive — 佛罗伦萨博那罗蒂故居博物馆所藏大量书信、素描和手稿
- *The Letters of Michelangelo* (E. Sedgwick Wickham编 / Creighton Gilbert编) — 书信集英译本
- 西斯廷天顶修复档案（1980–1994） — 梵蒂冈博物馆出版的修复记录

### 二手研究 / Secondary Literature

- Howard Hibbard, *Michelangelo* (Harper & Row, 1974) — 标准学术传记，平衡而全面
- William E. Wallace, *Michelangelo: The Artist, the Man, and His Times* (Cambridge UP, 2009) — 利用最新书信研究成果的新传记
- Michael Hirst, *Michelangelo and His Drawings* (Yale UP, 1988) — 素描研究的权威著作
- Charles de Tolnay, *Michelangelo* (5 vols, Princeton UP, 1943–1960) — 百科全书式的专题研究
- Ross King, *Michelangelo and the Pope's Ceiling* (Walker, 2003) — 叙事性极强的大众读物
- Leo Steinberg, "The Line of Fate in Michelangelo's Painting" (1980) — 形式分析的经典论文
- Edgar Wind, *Pagan Mysteries in the Renaissance* (1958) — 新柏拉图主义图像学解读
- Paul Barolsky, *Michelangelo's Nose* (Penn State UP, 1991) — 从神话和家族角度重读米开朗基罗

### 中文资源 / Chinese Resources

- 罗曼·罗兰（Romain Rolland），《米开朗基罗传》（傅雷译）— 经典精神传记，文学性极强
- 罗斯·金（Ross King），《米开朗基罗与教皇的天花板》（吴家恒译）— 叙事性通俗史学
- 布克哈特（Jacob Burckhardt），《意大利文艺复兴时期的文化》相关章节 — 文化史背景
- 贡布里希（E.H. Gombrich），《艺术的故事》第13–14章 — 最佳入门导读
- 欧文·潘诺夫斯基（Erwin Panofsky），《图像学研究》相关章节 — 图像学方法论
- 瓦萨里（Vasari），《艺苑名人传》中译本 — 原始传记文献
