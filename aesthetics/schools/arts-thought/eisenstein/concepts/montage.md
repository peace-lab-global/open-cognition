# 蒙太奇 / Montage

## 概念定义 / Definition

蒙太奇（Montage，源自法语"组装"）是爱森斯坦电影理论的核心概念：通过将两个或多个独立的镜头并置，产生单个镜头所包含不了的新意义。爱森斯坦将蒙太奇提升为一种辩证法——正题（镜头A）与反题（镜头B）碰撞产生合题（新概念C），C不存在于A或B之中。

Montage (from French "assembly") is Eisenstein's core concept: juxtaposing two or more independent shots to produce new meaning not contained in any individual shot. Eisenstein elevated montage to dialectics — thesis (shot A) and antithesis (shot B) collide to produce synthesis (new concept C), where C exists in neither A nor B.

## 五种蒙太奇类型 / Five Types of Montage

### 1. 节拍蒙太奇 / Metric Montage
按固定长度剪辑镜头，不考虑内容——纯粹的节拍节奏。最原始的蒙太奇形式。
Editing shots by fixed length regardless of content — pure rhythmic beat. Most primitive montage form.

### 2. 节奏蒙太奇 / Rhythmic Montage
画面内部的运动决定了剪辑的节奏——当运动达到某个"节点"时切换镜头。
Movement within the frame determines editing rhythm — cutting when movement reaches a "node."

### 3. 色调蒙太奇 / Tonal Montage
基于画面的光影、色调进行剪辑——创造情感氛围。
Editing based on light, shadow, and tonal qualities — creating emotional atmosphere.

### 4. 和声蒙太奇 / Overtonal Montage
将节拍、节奏、色调多层次复合——创造复合的情感效果。
Combining metric, rhythmic, and tonal layers — creating composite emotional effects.

### 5. 理性蒙太奇 / Intellectual Montage
通过镜头冲突表达抽象概念——"意义"而非"情感"成为蒙太奇的目标。
Expressing abstract concepts through shot conflict — "meaning" rather than "emotion" becomes montage's goal.

## 与黑格尔辩证法的关系 / Relation to Hegelian Dialectics

爱森斯坦明确将蒙太奇与黑格尔辩证法联系：
- 镜头A = 正题（Thesis）
- 镜头B = 反题（Antithesis）
- 新概念C = 合题（Synthesis）
- C = A + B 的冲突产物，不是A或B的简单加和

Eisenstein explicitly linked montage to Hegelian dialectics: Shot A = Thesis; Shot B = Antithesis; New concept C = Synthesis; C = product of A+B conflict, not simple sum of A or B.

## "敖德萨阶梯"案例 / "Odessa Steps" Case Study

《战舰波将金号》中的"敖德萨阶梯"段落是蒙太奇的经典案例：
- 节拍蒙太奇：士兵整齐的脚步
- 节奏蒙太奇：婴儿车滚下阶梯
- 色调蒙太奇：明暗交替的画面
- 理性蒙太奇：士兵的无情 vs 平民的恐惧

The "Odessa Steps" sequence in *Battleship Potemkin* exemplifies montage: metric (soldiers' marching), rhythmic (baby carriage rolling), tonal (light-dark alternation), intellectual (soldiers' ruthlessness vs. civilians' fear).
