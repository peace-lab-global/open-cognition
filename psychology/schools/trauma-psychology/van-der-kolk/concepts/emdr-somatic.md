# EMDR 与躯体疗法 · EMDR and Somatic Approaches

## 定义 / Definition

**EMDR 与躯体疗法**（EMDR and Somatic Approaches）是范德科尔克整合框架中"自下而上"（bottom-up）创伤治疗方法的集合。这些方法的共同假设是：创伤记忆主要以感觉-运动和自主神经反应的形式储存在大脑的皮层下区域和身体之中，传统"自上而下"（top-down）的认知和谈话方法无法充分触及这些编码层面，因此需要通过特定的身体导向技术来激活和重新整合创伤记忆。范德科尔克推动的核心躯体疗法包括：眼动脱敏再加工（EMDR）、体感体验（Somatic Experiencing）、创伤敏感瑜伽（Trauma-Sensitive Yoga）、神经反馈（Neurofeedback）和基于戏剧/运动的治疗方法。

**EMDR and Somatic Approaches** is the collection of "bottom-up" trauma treatment methods within van der Kolk's integrative framework. Their shared hypothesis is: traumatic memory is stored primarily as sensorimotor and autonomic nervous responses in subcortical brain regions and the body; traditional "top-down" cognitive and verbal methods cannot fully access these encoding levels, so specific body-oriented techniques are needed to activate and reintegrate traumatic memory. The core somatic therapies van der Kolk promoted include: Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR), Somatic Experiencing, Trauma-Sensitive Yoga, Neurofeedback, and theater/movement-based therapies.

## 核心机制 / Core Mechanisms

### 1. EMDR：眼动脱敏再加工 / EMDR: Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing

**EMDR** 由弗朗辛·夏皮罗（Francine Shapiro）在1987年发明，其核心程序包括八个阶段：（1）病史采集与评估；（2）准备阶段（建立安全感和资源）；（3）评估目标记忆（确定图像、负性认知、身体感觉）；（4）脱敏阶段（在双侧刺激下处理创伤记忆）；（5）植入正性认知；（6）身体扫描（检查残余身体紧张）；（7）闭合；（8）再评估。

**EMDR** was developed by Francine Shapiro in 1987. Its core protocol includes eight phases: (1) history-taking and assessment; (2) preparation (building safety and resources); (3) target memory assessment (identifying image, negative cognition, body sensation); (4) desensitization (processing traumatic memory under bilateral stimulation); (5) positive cognition installation; (6) body scan (checking residual body tension); (7) closure; (8) reassessment.

**双侧刺激（Bilateral Stimulation）的作用机制：** EMDR 的核心特征是双侧刺激——通常是通过治疗师手指引导的水平眼动，也可通过双侧触觉敲击或交替声音。范德科尔克认为，双侧刺激可能通过以下机制促进创伤记忆的再加工：

**Mechanism of Bilateral Stimulation**: EMDR's core feature is bilateral stimulation — typically horizontal eye movements guided by the therapist's finger, but also achievable through bilateral tactile tapping or alternating sounds. Van der Kolk proposes bilateral stimulation may facilitate traumatic memory reprocessing through these mechanisms:

- **工作记忆负荷假说**：眼动占用了工作记忆（working memory）的资源，使创伤记忆在同时被激活时变得"不那么鲜明"（less vivid），从而降低了情感负荷，使患者能够在不被淹没的情况下处理创伤材料。

- **Working memory load hypothesis**: Eye movements consume working memory resources, making simultaneously activated traumatic memories "less vivid," thereby reducing emotional load and allowing patients to process traumatic material without being overwhelmed.

- **REM 睡眠模拟假说**：快速眼动（REM）与REM睡眠中的眼动模式相似，而REM睡眠被认为是大脑进行记忆整合和情感处理的关键时期。EMDR 可能模拟了REM睡眠的神经处理过程，促进创伤记忆从"冻结"状态转化为"整合"状态。

- **REM sleep simulation hypothesis**: Rapid eye movements mirror the eye movement patterns of REM sleep, which is considered a critical period for memory consolidation and emotional processing. EMDR may simulate REM sleep neural processing, facilitating transformation of traumatic memory from "frozen" to "integrated" state.

- **双侧半球激活假说**：交替的眼动或触觉刺激可能促进了左右大脑半球之间的协调，有助于将创伤记忆从右半球的感觉-运动编码"桥接"到左半球的言语-叙事编码，从而完成记忆的整合。

- **Bilateral hemisphere activation hypothesis**: Alternating eye movements or tactile stimulation may promote interhemispheric coordination, helping "bridge" traumatic memory from right-hemisphere sensorimotor encoding to left-hemisphere verbal-narrative encoding, completing memory integration.

### 2. 体感体验（Somatic Experiencing, SE） / Somatic Experiencing

体感体验由彼得·莱文（Peter Levine）创立，其核心理论假设是：创伤导致"生存能量"（survival energy）被困在神经系统中——当战斗或逃跑反应被启动但未能完成时（如在冻结状态中），这种能量无法自然释放，导致自主神经系统的慢性失调。SE 的治疗方法是通过缓慢地、滴定地（titrated）引导患者注意身体的感觉——特别是与创伤相关的身体紧张、温度变化、振动感——帮助神经系统逐步"完成"那些被中断的生存反应，释放被困的能量，恢复自主神经系统的自然流动性。

Somatic Experiencing (SE), founded by Peter Levine, rests on the core theoretical assumption that trauma causes "survival energy" to become trapped in the nervous system — when fight or flight responses are initiated but not completed (as in freeze states), this energy cannot naturally discharge, leading to chronic autonomic nervous system dysregulation. SE treatment involves slowly, in a titrated manner, guiding patients to attend to body sensations — particularly trauma-related bodily tension, temperature changes, vibration sensations — helping the nervous system gradually "complete" interrupted survival responses, discharge trapped energy, and restore autonomic nervous system natural fluidity.

范德科尔克将 SE 视为"自下而上"治疗的典范方法，因为它完全从身体的感觉入手，而非从认知或叙事入手。在 SE 治疗中，治疗师不要求患者详细叙述创伤事件的内容，而是引导患者追踪身体内部正在发生什么（"当你想到那件事时，你的身体里正在发生什么？"），帮助患者建立对躯体感觉的安全觉察和逐步释放。

Van der Kolk considers SE a paradigmatic "bottom-up" treatment because it works entirely from body sensation rather than cognition or narrative. In SE therapy, the therapist does not require patients to narrate traumatic event details in detail, but instead guides patients to track what is happening inside the body ("What is happening in your body right now as you think about that?"), helping patients build safe awareness and gradual discharge of somatic sensations.

### 3. 创伤敏感瑜伽 / Trauma-Sensitive Yoga (TSY)

范德科尔克与戴维·爱默生（David Emerson）合作开发并验证了创伤敏感瑜伽（TSY）的临床效果。TSY 与普通瑜伽的关键区别在于：（1）**强调内感受而非外形**——关注"做这个姿势时身体内部的感觉是什么"，而非姿势是否正确或美观；（2）**提供选择而非指令**——用"如果你愿意的话"（if you like）和"你可以尝试"（you might try）的语言替代命令式的指令，帮助创伤幸存者重建能动感（agency）；（3）**避免身体触碰**——不使用调整性触碰（hands-on adjustments），因为未经同意的触碰可能触发创伤反应；（4）**关注当下体验**——通过呼吸觉察和身体扫描帮助患者锚定在当下时刻，而非被创伤记忆拉回过去。

Van der Kolk collaborated with David Emerson to develop and validate Trauma-Sensitive Yoga (TSY). Key distinctions from regular yoga include: (1) **emphasizing interoception over form** — focusing on "what does the body feel like inside this pose" rather than whether the pose is correct or aesthetically pleasing; (2) **offering choices rather than commands** — using "if you like" and "you might try" language instead of directive instructions, helping trauma survivors rebuild agency; (3) **avoiding physical touch** — no hands-on adjustments, as non-consensual touch may trigger trauma responses; (4) **focusing on present-moment experience** — using breath awareness and body scans to help patients anchor in the present moment rather than being pulled into the past by traumatic memory.

范德科尔克的研究团队发表的多项 RCT 表明，TSY 对慢性 PTSD（包括对标准治疗无反应的患者）具有显著疗效，能显著降低 PTSD 症状严重度、改善身体感知和增强情感调节能力。

Multiple RCTs published by van der Kolk's research team demonstrate that TSY has significant efficacy for chronic PTSD (including patients unresponsive to standard treatments), significantly reducing PTSD symptom severity, improving body awareness, and enhancing emotional regulation capacity.

### 4. 神经反馈 / Neurofeedback

神经反馈是一种直接训练大脑活动模式的技术。在神经反馈治疗中，患者通过 EEG（脑电图）或 fMRI 实时监测自己的大脑活动，并通过视觉或听觉反馈学习调节特定的脑波模式。范德科尔克将神经反馈引入创伤治疗，特别关注以下脑波训练目标：（1）**增加 alpha 波（8–12 Hz）**——促进放松和内在平静感；（2）**调节 theta 波（4–8 Hz）**——改善创伤相关的解离症状；（3）**降低高频 beta 波（20–30 Hz）**——减少过度警觉和焦虑；（4）**增强感觉运动节律（SMR, 12–15 Hz）**——改善身体感知和情绪稳定性。

Neurofeedback is a technique for directly training brain activity patterns. In neurofeedback treatment, patients monitor their own brain activity in real-time through EEG or fMRI, learning to regulate specific brainwave patterns through visual or auditory feedback. Van der Kolk introduced neurofeedback into trauma treatment, focusing on these brainwave training targets: (1) **increasing alpha waves (8–12 Hz)** — promoting relaxation and inner calm; (2) **regulating theta waves (4–8 Hz)** — improving trauma-related dissociative symptoms; (3) **reducing high-frequency beta waves (20–30 Hz)** — reducing hypervigilance and anxiety; (4) **enhancing sensorimotor rhythm (SMR, 12–15 Hz)** — improving body awareness and emotional stability.

## 发展脉络 / Developmental Trajectory

范德科尔克对躯体疗法的整合经历了三个关键阶段：

Van der Kolk's integration of somatic therapies progressed through three key stages:

**发现阶段（1990s）**：在发现谈话治疗的局限后，范德科尔克开始探索替代性治疗方法。1990年代初，他接触到 EMDR，并通过临床研究验证了其在 PTSD 治疗中的有效性。同时，他开始关注莱文的体感体验和帕特·奥格登（Pat Ogden）的感觉运动心理治疗（Sensorimotor Psychotherapy）。

**Discovery phase (1990s)**: After discovering talk therapy's limitations, van der Kolk began exploring alternative treatment methods. In the early 1990s, he encountered EMDR and validated its effectiveness in PTSD treatment through clinical research. Simultaneously, he began attending to Levine's Somatic Experiencing and Pat Ogden's Sensorimotor Psychotherapy.

**验证阶段（2000s–2010s）**：范德科尔克通过严格的随机对照试验（RCT）验证了多种躯体疗法的有效性。他发表了关于 EMDR 治疗 PTSD 的多项研究、TSY 对慢性 PTSD 的 RCT、以及神经反馈对复杂性创伤的临床研究，为这些方法建立了循证基础。

**Validation phase (2000s–2010s)**: Van der Kolk validated multiple somatic therapies' effectiveness through rigorous RCTs. He published multiple studies on EMDR for PTSD, TSY RCTs for chronic PTSD, and neurofeedback clinical studies for complex trauma, establishing their evidence base.

**整合阶段（2010s–至今）**：通过《身体从未忘记》和创伤中心培训项目，范德科尔克将多种躯体疗法整合为"多模式创伤治疗"框架，强调治疗师应根据患者的具体需要灵活选择和组合不同的方法——而非固守单一技术。

**Integration phase (2010s–present)**: Through *The Body Keeps the Score* and Trauma Center training programs, van der Kolk integrated multiple somatic therapies into the "multimodal trauma treatment" framework, emphasizing that therapists should flexibly select and combine different methods based on patients' specific needs — rather than adhering rigidly to single techniques.

## 临床应用 / Clinical Applications

### EMDR 的临床适应证 / EMDR Clinical Indications

EMDR 目前被世界卫生组织（WHO）、美国退伍军人事务部（VA）、英国国家健康与临床卓越研究所（NICE）和国际创伤应激研究学会（ISTSS）推荐为 PTSD 的一线治疗方法。其临床适应证包括：（1）单次事件创伤后 PTSD（I 型创伤）；（2）复杂性 PTSD（需要更多的稳定化准备）；（3）惊恐发作和特定恐惧症；（4）创伤相关的躯体症状；（5）悲伤和丧失（complicated grief）。范德科尔克强调，对于复杂性创伤，EMDR 必须在充分的安全与稳定化准备之后才能开始，否则可能导致患者被创伤记忆淹没。

EMDR is currently recommended as a first-line PTSD treatment by the WHO, the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA), the UK National Institute for Health and Clinical Excellence (NICE), and ISTSS. Clinical indications include: (1) PTSD following single-event trauma (Type I); (2) Complex PTSD (requiring more extensive stabilization preparation); (3) panic attacks and specific phobias; (4) trauma-related somatic symptoms; (5) complicated grief. Van der Kolk emphasizes that for complex trauma, EMDR must begin only after adequate safety and stabilization preparation; otherwise patients risk being overwhelmed by traumatic memories.

### 多模式整合的临床实践 / Multimodal Integration in Clinical Practice

范德科尔克倡导的治疗实践不是选择"一种最好的方法"，而是根据患者的当前状态和需要灵活地整合多种方法。例如：在治疗的早期阶段，使用瑜伽和正念练习帮助患者建立身体觉察和情感调节的基础；在中期阶段，使用 EMDR 处理特定的创伤记忆；在整个治疗过程中，穿插使用体感体验技术帮助患者释放身体层面的创伤紧张；在需要时使用神经反馈来稳定过度激活的自主神经系统。

The treatment practice van der Kolk advocates is not about choosing "one best method" but flexibly integrating multiple methods based on the patient's current state and needs. For example: in early treatment phases, using yoga and mindfulness to help patients build foundations of body awareness and emotional regulation; in middle phases, using EMDR to process specific traumatic memories; throughout treatment, interspersing Somatic Experiencing techniques to help patients release somatic trauma tension; and using neurofeedback as needed to stabilize hyperactivated autonomic nervous systems.

## 东西方对话 / East-West Dialogue

### 与气功和太极的对话 / Dialogue with Qigong and Tai Chi

EMDR 和体感体验中强调的"双侧节律性刺激"与中国传统的气功和太极练习存在有趣的对应关系。太极拳强调左右对称的运动、缓慢的重心转移和呼吸的节律性调节——这些元素在功能上类似于 EMDR 的双侧刺激和 SE 的身体追踪。气功的"导引"（通过意念引导气的流动）可以被理解为一种"内感受训练"——练习者学习感知和调节身体内部的能量流动，这与范德科尔克倡导的"恢复内感受能力"的治疗目标不谋而合。将这些传统身体实践纳入创伤治疗的整合框架，是一个值得探索的方向。

The "bilateral rhythmic stimulation" emphasized in EMDR and Somatic Experiencing has interesting correspondences with traditional Chinese qigong and tai chi practice. Tai chi emphasizes symmetrical bilateral movement, slow weight-shifting, and rhythmic breath regulation — elements functionally similar to EMDR's bilateral stimulation and SE's body tracking. Qigong's "daoyin" (guiding qi flow through intention) can be understood as a form of "interoceptive training" — practitioners learn to perceive and regulate internal energy flow, coinciding with van der Kolk's therapeutic goal of "restoring interoceptive capacity." Integrating these traditional body practices into trauma treatment frameworks is a direction worth exploring.

### 与瑜伽传统的对话 / Dialogue with Yoga Traditions

范德科尔克将瑜伽引入创伤治疗，本身就是东方身体传统与西方临床科学的跨文化对话。然而，他特别强调了"创伤敏感"的改编——这涉及到对传统瑜伽实践的批判性反思：传统的瑜伽教学可能包含命令式的语言、身体触碰调整和对"完美姿势"的追求，这些元素可能无意中触发创伤反应。范德科尔克的 TSY 模型代表了东方身体实践在西方创伤临床中的创造性转化——保留了瑜伽的核心疗愈机制（呼吸觉察、身体觉察、当下锚定），同时去除了可能造成伤害的元素。

Van der Kolk's introduction of yoga into trauma treatment is itself a cross-cultural dialogue between Eastern body traditions and Western clinical science. However, he specifically emphasized "trauma-sensitive" adaptation — involving critical reflection on traditional yoga practice: traditional yoga teaching may include directive language, physical touch adjustments, and pursuit of "perfect poses," elements that may inadvertently trigger trauma responses. Van der Kolk's TSY model represents a creative transformation of Eastern body practice within Western trauma clinical settings — preserving yoga's core healing mechanisms (breath awareness, body awareness, present-moment anchoring) while removing potentially harmful elements.
