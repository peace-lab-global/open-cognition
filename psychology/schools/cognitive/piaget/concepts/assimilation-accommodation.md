# 同化与顺应 · Assimilation and Accommodation

## 定义 / Definition

**中文：** 同化（assimilation）与顺应（accommodation）是皮亚杰认知发展理论中的一对核心互补过程，共同构成**适应（adaptation）**的两个面向。**同化**是指主体将新的外部刺激纳入已有的认知图式中的过程——用旧的方式理解新的对象。**顺应**是指主体修改已有的认知图式以适应新刺激的过程——改变旧的方式来适应新的对象。两者之间的动态调节——**平衡化（equilibration）**——是认知发展的根本动力。

皮亚杰借用了生物学中的同化（将食物转化为身体组织）和顺应（有机体改变自身以适应环境）概念，将其提升到认知层面，构建了一套描述认知适应过程的完整理论。

**English:** Assimilation and accommodation are a pair of core complementary processes in Piaget's cognitive development theory, together constituting the two facets of **adaptation**. **Assimilation** refers to the process by which the subject fits new external stimuli into existing cognitive schemas — understanding new objects in old ways. **Accommodation** refers to the process by which the subject modifies existing cognitive schemas to fit new stimuli — changing old ways to adapt to new objects. The dynamic regulation between them — **equilibration** — is the fundamental driver of cognitive development.

Piaget borrowed the biological concepts of assimilation (transforming food into body tissue) and accommodation (organism modifying itself to fit the environment), elevating them to the cognitive level to construct a complete theory describing the process of cognitive adaptation.

## 核心机制 / Core Mechanism

### 同化的运作 / How Assimilation Works

**中文：** 同化是认知的"保守"面向——它维护已有认知结构的稳定性，将新经验整合进已有框架中。同化有三种形式：

1. **再生性同化（Reproductive assimilation）**：重复已有的行动模式。例如婴儿反复吸吮不同的物体——奶嘴、手指、玩具。
2. **识别性同化（Recognitory assimilation）**：识别出刺激属于已有图式的范畴。例如看到一只新品种的狗，仍然能将其归类为"狗"。
3. **概括性同化（Generalizing assimilation）**：将图式应用到新的情境中。例如婴儿学会抓握后，试图抓握任何够得到的物体。

同化的本质是一种"认知节约"——不需要每次都重新建构认知结构，而是尽可能利用已有资源。但过度同化会导致认知的"固化"——用旧框架强行解释新现象，忽视不符合之处。

**English:** Assimilation is the "conservative" face of cognition — it maintains the stability of existing cognitive structures by integrating new experiences into established frameworks. Assimilation takes three forms:

1. **Reproductive assimilation**: Repeating existing action patterns. For example, an infant repeatedly sucks different objects — pacifier, finger, toy.
2. **Recognitory assimilation**: Recognizing that a stimulus belongs to an existing schema category. For example, seeing a new breed of dog and still categorizing it as "dog."
3. **Generalizing assimilation**: Applying a schema to new situations. For example, after learning to grasp, an infant attempts to grasp any reachable object.

The essence of assimilation is a form of "cognitive economy" — there is no need to reconstruct cognitive structures every time; existing resources are utilized as much as possible. However, excessive assimilation leads to cognitive "rigidity" — forcing old frameworks onto new phenomena while ignoring discrepancies.

### 顺应的运作 / How Accommodation Works

**中文：** 顺应是认知的"变革"面向——它修改已有认知结构以适应新经验。当同化无法处理新刺激时，认知失衡产生，顺应过程被触发。

顺应的基本形式包括：
1. **图式分化（Schema differentiation）**：一个笼统的图式分化为更精细的子图式。例如"动物"图式分化为"会叫的动物"和"不会叫的动物"。
2. **图式重组（Schema reorganization）**：重新组织图式之间的关系。例如儿童发现"鲸鱼不是鱼"后，需要重新组织关于动物的分类结构。
3. **新图式建构（New schema construction）**：创造全新的认知结构。例如儿童第一次理解"可能性"概念时，建构了一种全新的思维维度。

**English:** Accommodation is the "revolutionary" face of cognition — it modifies existing cognitive structures to fit new experiences. When assimilation cannot handle new stimuli, cognitive disequilibrium arises, triggering the accommodation process.

Basic forms of accommodation include:
1. **Schema differentiation**: A general schema differentiates into more refined sub-schemas. For example, the "animal" schema differentiates into "animals that bark" and "animals that don't bark."
2. **Schema reorganization**: Reorganizing relationships between schemas. For example, when a child discovers "whales are not fish," they must reorganize their classification structure for animals.
3. **New schema construction**: Creating entirely new cognitive structures. For example, when a child first understands the concept of "possibility," they construct an entirely new dimension of thinking.

### 平衡化：发展的引擎 / Equilibration: The Engine of Development

**中文：** 平衡化（equilibration）不是简单的"平衡状态"，而是一个**动态调节过程**——它在同化和顺应之间寻找最优配比。

平衡化运作的典型循环：
1. **平衡状态**：现有图式能有效处理环境中的刺激。
2. **认知冲突**：新的刺激无法被同化，产生认知失衡（disequilibrium）。
3. **顺应过程**：修改或建构新的图式以适应新刺激。
4. **新的平衡**：新的认知结构形成，能处理更广泛的刺激。

这个循环不是简单的重复，而是**螺旋式上升**——每一次平衡-失衡-再平衡的循环都带来认知结构的质变。平衡化因此是认知发展的**根本动力**。

**English:** Equilibration is not simply a "state of balance" but a **dynamic regulatory process** — it seeks the optimal ratio between assimilation and accommodation.

The typical cycle of equilibration:
1. **State of equilibrium**: Existing schemas effectively handle stimuli in the environment.
2. **Cognitive conflict**: New stimuli cannot be assimilated, producing cognitive disequilibrium.
3. **Accommodation process**: Modifying or constructing new schemas to fit new stimuli.
4. **New equilibrium**: New cognitive structures form, able to handle a broader range of stimuli.

This cycle is not simple repetition but a **spiral ascent** — each equilibrium-disequilibrium-re-equilibration cycle brings qualitative changes in cognitive structures. Equilibration is therefore the **fundamental driving force** of cognitive development.

## 发展脉络 / Development

**中文：** 同化与顺应概念在皮亚杰思想中的演变：

- **生物学阶段**：皮亚杰最初在软体动物研究中接触生物学意义上的同化与顺应——生物有机体如何通过这两个过程与环境达成适应。
- **心理学转换**：在比奈实验室工作后，皮亚杰将这一对概念从生物学层面转移到认知层面——认知主体如何通过认知层面的同化与顺应与认知环境达成平衡。
- **理论核心化**：到 1930–40 年代，同化与顺应成为皮亚杰理论的核心机制，贯穿其全部著作。
- **晚期深化**：在《平衡化》（1975）中，皮亚杰进一步细化了平衡化机制，区分了"α平衡"（行为层面的调节）和"β平衡"（反思层面的调节），以及更高级的"γ平衡"（理解自身认知运作方式）。

**English:** The evolution of assimilation and accommodation concepts in Piaget's thought:

- **Biological stage**: Piaget first encountered assimilation and accommodation in their biological sense through mollusk research — how biological organisms achieve adaptation with their environment through these two processes.
- **Psychological transition**: After working at the Binet laboratory, Piaget transferred this pair of concepts from the biological level to the cognitive level — how cognitive subjects achieve balance with the cognitive environment through cognitive-level assimilation and accommodation.
- **Theoretical centralization**: By the 1930s–40s, assimilation and accommodation became the central mechanism of Piaget's theory, running through all his works.
- **Late-period deepening**: In *Equilibration* (1975), Piaget further refined the equilibration mechanism, distinguishing "alpha equilibration" (behavioral-level regulation), "beta equilibration" (reflective-level regulation), and the more advanced "gamma equilibration" (understanding one's own cognitive operations).

## 临床应用 / Clinical Applications

### 认知行为疗法（CBT）中的同化与顺应 / Assimilation and Accommodation in CBT

**中文：** 在 CBT 中，"认知重构"可以被理解为一种**有意识的顺应过程**。当来访者发现其核心信念（图式）无法解释现实经验时（同化失败），治疗师引导来访者修改或替换旧图式（顺应），建构更适应性的认知框架。

例如，一个持有"我一无是处"核心信念的来访者，在治疗中被引导注意到自己在工作中的成就——这些正面证据无法被同化到"一无是处"的图式中，认知冲突产生。通过顺应过程，来访者修改图式为"我虽然有不足，但也有能力"。

**English:** In CBT, "cognitive restructuring" can be understood as a **conscious accommodation process**. When clients discover that their core beliefs (schemas) cannot explain real experiences (assimilation failure), the therapist guides clients to modify or replace old schemas (accommodation), constructing more adaptive cognitive frameworks.

For example, a client holding the core belief "I am worthless" is guided in therapy to notice achievements at work — these positive pieces of evidence cannot be assimilated into the "worthless" schema, producing cognitive conflict. Through accommodation, the client revises the schema to "I have weaknesses but also capabilities."

### 教育中的认知冲突策略 / Cognitive Conflict Strategies in Education

**中文：** 建构主义教学法的核心策略之一就是**有意制造认知冲突**。教师设计的"认知冲突情境"（如预测-观察-解释范式，POE）旨在触发学生的顺应过程。例如，在物理课上，让学生先预测"重的球和轻的球谁先落地"，然后观察实际实验结果——当观察到两者同时落地时，认知冲突产生，推动学生修改关于重力和下落的旧图式。

**English:** One of the core strategies of constructivist pedagogy is **deliberately creating cognitive conflict**. The "cognitive conflict situations" designed by teachers (such as the Predict-Observe-Explain paradigm, POE) aim to trigger students' accommodation process. For example, in a physics class, students first predict "which ball falls first, a heavy one or a light one," then observe the actual experimental result — when they observe both landing simultaneously, cognitive conflict arises, driving students to revise their old schemas about gravity and falling.

### 组织学习与变革管理 / Organizational Learning and Change Management

**中文：** 在组织变革中，"范式转换"可以被理解为组织层面的顺应过程。当旧的组织模式（图式）无法应对新的市场环境时，组织需要经历从失衡到顺应到新平衡的转变过程。理解这一机制有助于设计更有效的变革策略——不是强行替换旧模式，而是创造足够的认知冲突，让组织成员自主发现旧模式的不足，主动顺应新模式。

**English:** In organizational change, "paradigm shifts" can be understood as organization-level accommodation processes. When old organizational models (schemas) cannot cope with new market environments, organizations need to undergo the transition from disequilibrium through accommodation to new equilibrium. Understanding this mechanism helps design more effective change strategies — not forcibly replacing old models, but creating sufficient cognitive conflict so that organizational members autonomously discover the inadequacy of old models and actively accommodate new ones.

## 与其他概念的关联 / Related Concepts

### 内部关联 / Internal Connections

- **图式（Schema）**：同化和顺应操作的对象——图式是被同化或顺应的认知结构。
- **平衡化（Equilibration）**：同化与顺应之间的动态调节过程。
- **认知发展阶段（Stages）**：阶段转换是大规模顺应过程的结果。
- **认知发展理论（Cognitive Development）**：同化与顺应是其核心机制。

### 外部关联 / External Connections

- **认知失调（Cognitive Dissonance, Festinger）**：认知冲突产生的不适感类似于费斯廷格的认知失调——不一致的信息驱动态度改变。
- **贝克的认知图式（Beck's Cognitive Schemas）**：CBT 中的"核心信念修改"就是认知层面的顺应过程。
- **库恩的范式转换（Kuhn's Paradigm Shift）**：科学革命中的范式转换是知识社会学层面的大规模顺应过程。
- **佛教的"破执"**：佛教修行中"破除执着"可以被理解为一种极致的顺应——不断放下旧图式以适应更深层的实相。

## 东西方对话 / East-West Dialogue

### 与老子"无为"的对话 / Dialogue with Laozi's Wu Wei

**中文：** 老子的"无为"（wú wéi）概念与皮亚杰的同化-顺应辩证法形成了有趣的对话。"无为"不是"不作为"，而是"不强为"——不过度地用自己的框架（图式）去控制事物（过度同化），也不被动地随波逐流（无目的的顺应），而是"顺应自然"——在与环境的动态互动中找到恰如其分的行动方式。

这与皮亚杰的平衡化理想状态相呼应——最有效的认知发展发生在同化和顺应之间的恰当配比中，而非任何一方的极端。

**English:** Laozi's concept of "wu wei" (non-forcing action) creates an interesting dialogue with Piaget's assimilation-accommodation dialectic. "Wu wei" is not "non-action" but "non-forcing action" — neither excessively imposing one's own framework (schemas) to control things (excessive assimilation), nor passively drifting (aimless accommodation), but "following the natural way" — finding the appropriate mode of action within dynamic interaction with the environment.

This echoes Piaget's ideal state of equilibration — the most effective cognitive development occurs at the optimal ratio between assimilation and accommodation, not at either extreme.

### 与唯识学"转识成智"的对话 / Dialogue with Yogācāra's "Transforming Consciousness into Wisdom"

**中文：** 唯识学的核心修行路径是"转识成智"——将虚妄的分别识转化为无分别的智慧。"转"（parāvṛtti）这个过程可以被理解为一种极致的顺应——彻底放下所有旧有的认知图式（包括分别心、执着心），以接纳"如如"的实相。

但唯识学与皮亚杰的关键分歧在于：皮亚杰认为顺应产生新的图式（认知永远在建构中），而唯识学的最终目标是超越一切图式（"无分别智"不是新的图式，而是对一切图式的超越）。

**English:** Yogācāra's core soteriological path is "transforming consciousness into wisdom" (转识成智, zhuǎn shí chéng zhì) — transforming deluded discriminative consciousness into non-discriminative wisdom. The process of "transformation" (parāvṛtti) can be understood as an ultimate form of accommodation — completely releasing all old cognitive schemas (including discriminative mind, clinging mind) to receive reality as-it-is (如如, rú rú).

However, the key divergence between Yogācāra and Piaget is: Piaget holds that accommodation produces new schemas (cognition is perpetually under construction), while Yogācāra's ultimate goal is to transcend all schemas ("non-discriminative wisdom" is not a new schema but a transcendence of all schemas).
