# 正义 Justice

## 定义 / Definition

**正义**（Justice）在柯尔伯格的理论中占据核心地位——它既是道德发展的核心取向（orienting principle），也是最高阶段的组织原则。柯尔伯格认为，道德发展的本质是"正义感"（sense of justice）的发展：个体从以自身利益为中心的前习俗正义观，发展到以社会规范为基础的习俗正义观，最终达到以可普遍化的公正原则（如平等、人的尊严、互惠）为基础的后习俗正义观。正义不是外部强加的规则，而是道德认知结构发展的内在方向。

**Justice** occupies the central position in Kohlberg's theory — it is both the core orientation of moral development and the organizing principle of the highest stage. Kohlberg held that the essence of moral development is the development of a "sense of justice": the individual's progression from a pre-conventional justice view centered on self-interest, through a conventional justice view based on social norms, ultimately reaching a post-conventional justice view grounded in universalizable principles of justice (such as equality, human dignity, reciprocity). Justice is not an externally imposed rule but the intrinsic direction of moral cognitive structural development.

## 核心机制 / Core Mechanisms

### 1. 正义作为道德的形式原则 / Justice as the Formal Principle of Morality

柯尔伯格继承了康德伦理学的核心洞见：道德的本质不在于具体的道德内容（哪些行为是对的），而在于道德判断的形式特征（如何判断行为的对错）。正义作为形式原则，要求道德判断具有"可普遍化"（universalizability）和"可逆性"（reversibility）——一个道德判断只有在"所有人都处于相同情况时都应该做出相同判断"且"站在任何当事人的立场都会做出相同判断"时才具有道德正当性。

Kohlberg inherited Kantian ethics' core insight: the essence of morality lies not in specific moral content (which behaviors are right) but in the formal characteristics of moral judgment (how to judge right and wrong). Justice as a formal principle requires that moral judgments possess "universalizability" and "reversibility" — a moral judgment has moral legitimacy only when "all people in the same situation should make the same judgment" and "one would make the same judgment standing in the position of any party involved."

### 2. 正义感的发展阶段 / Developmental Stages of the Sense of Justice

正义感在每个道德阶段具有不同的结构特征：

The sense of justice has different structural characteristics at each moral stage:

- **前习俗水平**：正义等同于"力量即权利"（阶段1）或"等价交换"（阶段2）。"公平"被理解为对自己有利的事情 / Justice equated with "might makes right" (Stage 1) or "equivalent exchange" (Stage 2). "Fairness" is understood as what benefits oneself
- **习俗水平**：正义等同于遵守社会规范和法律。"公平"被理解为"黄金法则"（阶段3："己所不欲，勿施于人"）或"社会秩序"（阶段4：每个人都有义务遵守规则）/ Justice equated with conforming to social norms and laws. "Fairness" understood as the "Golden Rule" (Stage 3) or "social order" (Stage 4)
- **后习俗水平**：正义基于超越具体社会规范的可普遍化原则。"公平"被理解为罗尔斯式的"无知之幕"下的公正选择（阶段5）或康德式的"将每个人视为目的而非手段"（阶段6）/ Justice based on universalizable principles transcending specific social norms. "Fairness" understood as Rawlsian fair choices behind a "veil of ignorance" (Stage 5) or Kantian "treating each person as an end, never merely as a means" (Stage 6)

### 3. 与罗尔斯正义论的整合 / Integration with Rawls's Theory of Justice

柯尔伯格将罗尔斯1971年的《正义论》（*A Theory of Justice*）视为其道德发展理论的哲学支柱。罗尔斯的两个正义原则——"自由原则"（每个人享有最广泛的基本自由）和"差异原则"（社会不平等只有在有利于最弱势群体时才具有正当性）——为柯尔伯格的第五和第六阶段提供了哲学论证。罗尔斯的"无知之幕"（veil of ignorance）思想实验被柯尔伯格视为后习俗道德推理的理想化形式：只有当你不知道自己将处于社会的哪个位置时，你才能做出真正公正的判断。

Kohlberg viewed Rawls's 1971 *A Theory of Justice* as the philosophical pillar of his moral development theory. Rawls's two principles of justice — the "liberty principle" (each person enjoys the most extensive basic liberties) and the "difference principle" (social inequalities are legitimate only when they benefit the least advantaged) — provided philosophical justification for Kohlberg's Stages 5 and 6. Rawls's "veil of ignorance" thought experiment was viewed by Kohlberg as the idealized form of post-conventional moral reasoning: only when you don't know what position you'll occupy in society can you make truly just judgments.

### 4. "可逆性"作为正义的操作机制 / "Reversibility" as the Operational Mechanism of Justice

柯尔伯格将"可逆性"（reversibility）——也称作"道德椅子交换"（moral musical chairs）——视为正义推理的操作机制。可逆性要求道德判断者在做出判断时，能够在所有当事人的立场之间自由转换，确保判断在任何立场上都能被接受。这不同于简单的"换位思考"（empathy），而是一种逻辑-结构性的操作：它要求判断者检验自己的道德判断在"角色互换"后是否仍然成立。

Kohlberg viewed "reversibility" — also called "moral musical chairs" — as the operational mechanism of justice reasoning. Reversibility requires that moral judges, when making judgments, be able to freely switch among all parties' positions, ensuring the judgment is acceptable from any position. This differs from simple "perspective-taking" (empathy) — it is a logical-structural operation: it requires the judge to test whether their moral judgment still holds after "role exchange."

## 发展脉络 / Historical Development

### 哲学基础：从康德到罗尔斯 / Philosophical Foundations: From Kant to Rawls

柯尔伯格的正义概念有三个主要哲学来源：(1) 康德的绝对命令——道德判断必须可普遍化，人必须被视为目的而非手段；(2) 罗尔斯的正义论——正义是社会制度的首要德性，"无知之幕"确保公正；(3) 鲍德温的发展心理学——道德发展是自我与社会关系的辩证发展。柯尔伯格将这些哲学概念与认知发展心理学结合，论证了正义不仅是哲学理想，也是心理发展的内在方向。

Kohlberg's justice concept has three main philosophical sources: (1) Kant's categorical imperative — moral judgments must be universalizable, persons must be treated as ends not merely means; (2) Rawls's theory of justice — justice is the first virtue of social institutions, the "veil of ignorance" ensures fairness; (3) Baldwin's developmental psychology — moral development is the dialectical development of self-society relations. Kohlberg combined these philosophical concepts with cognitive developmental psychology, arguing that justice is not only a philosophical ideal but also the intrinsic direction of psychological development.

### 从"公正取向"到"公正+关怀" / From "Justice Orientation" to "Justice + Care"

吉利根1982年的批评揭示了柯尔伯格理论的局限：过度强调"公正"取向可能遮蔽了"关怀"取向的道德价值。柯尔伯格在后期研究中承认，公正和关怀可能代表两种平行但不同的道德取向——公正取向聚焦于权利、义务和原则，关怀取向聚焦于关系、责任和回应需要。柯尔伯格认为两者可以在更高的理论层面整合，但他未能完成这一整合工作。

Gilligan's 1982 critique revealed a limitation in Kohlberg's theory: overemphasis on the "justice" orientation may obscure the moral value of the "care" orientation. In later research, Kohlberg acknowledged that justice and care may represent two parallel but different moral orientations — justice orientation focuses on rights, obligations, and principles, while care orientation focuses on relationships, responsibilities, and responding to needs. Kohlberg believed both could be integrated at a higher theoretical level, but he did not complete this integrative work.

## 临床应用 / Clinical Applications

### 司法伦理教育 / Judicial Ethics Education

柯尔伯格的正义概念被应用于法律教育中。研究表明，法学院教育能有效促进学生的道德推理发展，特别是从习俗水平（"法律就是法律"）向后习俗水平（"法律是否公正？"）的过渡。法律伦理课程中的"苏格拉底式对话"（Socratic dialogue）和案例教学法本质上都是"正义推理训练"。

Kohlberg's justice concept has been applied in legal education. Research shows that law school education effectively promotes students' moral reasoning development, especially the transition from conventional level ("the law is the law") to post-conventional level ("is the law just?"). "Socratic dialogue" and case-based teaching methods in legal ethics courses are essentially "justice reasoning training."

### 组织公正 / Organizational Justice

组织行为学中的"程序公正"（procedural justice）和"分配公正"（distributive justice）概念与柯尔伯格的正义理论有直接关联。柯尔伯格的阶段框架可用于分析组织中的道德决策过程——不同道德推理水平的管理者对"公平"有不同的理解和实践方式。

"Procedural justice" and "distributive justice" concepts in organizational behavior are directly related to Kohlberg's justice theory. Kohlberg's stage framework can be used to analyze moral decision-making processes in organizations — managers at different moral reasoning levels have different understandings and practices of "fairness."

### 冲突解决与调解 / Conflict Resolution and Mediation

柯尔伯格的"可逆性"概念为冲突解决提供了方法论框架：有效的调解要求所有当事人能够在对方立场和自身立场之间自由转换，寻找所有相关方都能接受的解决方案。这与"哈佛谈判项目"（Harvard Negotiation Project）的"原则性谈判"（principled negotiation）方法有深层共鸣。

Kohlberg's "reversibility" concept provides a methodological framework for conflict resolution: effective mediation requires all parties to be able to freely switch between the other's position and their own, seeking solutions acceptable to all stakeholders. This has deep resonance with the Harvard Negotiation Project's "principled negotiation" approach.

## 东西方对话 / East-West Dialogue

### 与墨子"兼爱"的对话 / Dialogue with Mozi's "Universal Love"

墨子的"兼爱"（jian ai）主张无差别地爱所有人，这与柯尔伯格的"可普遍化"原则有结构性平行。墨子认为，社会不义的根源在于人们的"别相恶"——只爱自己人而不爱他人。"兼爱"要求将对自己亲人的关怀同等地扩展到所有人。这与柯尔伯格第六阶段的"将每个人视为目的而非手段"的理想高度呼应。然而，墨子的论证基于功利主义（"交相利"），而柯尔伯格的论证基于义务论（"正义本身就是目的"），两者在哲学基础上的差异值得注意。

Mozi's "universal love" (jian ai) advocates loving all people without discrimination, structurally paralleling Kohlberg's "universalizability" principle. Mozi held that the root of social injustice lies in people's "partial hatred" — loving only one's own people but not others. "Universal love" requires extending the care for one's own kin equally to all people. This resonates closely with Kohlberg's Stage 6 ideal of "treating each person as an end, never merely as a means." However, Mozi's argument is based on utilitarianism ("mutual benefit"), while Kohlberg's is based on deontology ("justice is an end in itself") — the philosophical foundation difference is noteworthy.

### 与佛教"平等"观的对话 / Dialogue with Buddhist "Equanimity"

佛教的"平等"（samatā）概念——对一切有情众生持有平等的慈悲，超越亲疏远近的分别——与柯尔伯格的"可逆性"概念有深层共鸣。佛教修行中的"四无量心"（慈、悲、喜、舍）特别是"舍"（upekkhā，平等心）的修习，可以被视为培养"正义感"的精神实践——通过冥想训练消除对不同众生的差别态度，达到对所有众生的平等关怀。

Buddhism's "equanimity" (samata) concept — holding equal compassion for all sentient beings, transcending distinctions of closeness — has deep resonance with Kohlberg's "reversibility" concept. The Buddhist practice of the "Four Immeasurables" (loving-kindness, compassion, sympathetic joy, equanimity), especially the cultivation of "equanimity" (upekkha), can be viewed as spiritual practice cultivating the "sense of justice" — eliminating differential attitudes toward different beings through meditation training, reaching equal care for all sentient beings.

### 与孟子"义"的概念对话 / Dialogue with Mencius's Concept of "Righteousness"

孟子的"义"（yi）——内在的道德正直感，"羞恶之心，义之端也"——与柯尔伯格的"正义感"有有趣的平行。孟子认为"义"是人固有的道德本性（性善论），需要通过"养浩然之气"来发展。柯尔伯格则认为正义感是认知发展的产物，通过认知冲突和阶段跃迁来发展。两者的对话揭示了道德哲学中"先天论"与"建构论"之间的张力——正义感究竟是人固有的道德直觉还是通过认知发展而建构的能力？

Mencius's "righteousness" (yi) — the inner sense of moral integrity, "the sense of shame and dislike is the beginning of righteousness" — has interesting parallels with Kohlberg's "sense of justice." Mencius held that "righteousness" is humanity's inherent moral nature (theory of innate goodness), requiring development through "cultivating noble qi." Kohlberg viewed the sense of justice as a product of cognitive development, developing through cognitive conflict and stage transitions. The dialogue between the two reveals the tension between "nativism" and "constructivism" in moral philosophy — is the sense of justice an innate moral intuition or an ability constructed through cognitive development?
