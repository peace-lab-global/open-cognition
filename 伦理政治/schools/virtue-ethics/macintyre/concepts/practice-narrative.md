# 实践与叙事 · Practice and Narrative

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

"实践"与"叙事"是麦金泰尔美德伦理学理论架构中的两个核心支柱。"实践"（practice）被定义为"人类活动的任何融贯的、复杂的社会合作形式"——它是美德得以培育和展现的场域。"叙事"（narrative）则是人类行为获得可理解性的基本框架——我们通过将行为和事件嵌入连贯的故事来理解它们的意义。这两个概念共同构成了麦金泰尔对现代道德哲学的根本替代方案：道德生活不是在抽象原则之下进行的选择，而是在具体实践中通过叙事而展开的善的追求。

"Practice" and "narrative" are two central pillars of MacIntyre's virtue ethics theoretical architecture. "Practice" is defined as "any coherent and complex form of socially established cooperative human activity" — it is the arena within which virtues are cultivated and displayed. "Narrative" is the fundamental framework through which human actions become intelligible — we understand the meaning of actions and events by situating them within coherent stories. Together, these concepts constitute MacIntyre's fundamental alternative to modern moral philosophy: moral life is not a matter of choice under abstract principles but a pursuit of the good that unfolds through narrative within concrete practices.

## 理论内涵 / Theoretical Content

### 实践 / Practice

麦金泰尔对"实践"的定义极为精确："实践是指任何融贯的、复杂的社会既定的人类合作活动形式，通过它，与该活动形式相关的内在善在追求该活动的卓越标准的过程中得以实现。"这一定义有几个关键要素：首先，实践必须是社会性的——它不是孤立的个人行为，而是在共同体中进行的合作活动；其次，实践必须具有内在标准——即关于什么是"好的棋手""好的建筑师""好的科学家"的判断标准，这些标准由实践本身而非外部市场来界定；第三，实践必须追求内在善——即只有通过参与实践本身才能获得的卓越品质。

MacIntyre's definition of practice is highly precise: "By a 'practice' I am going to mean any coherent and complex form of socially established cooperative human activity through which goods internal to that form of activity are realized in the course of trying to achieve those standards of excellence which are appropriate to, and partially definitive of, that form of activity." This definition has several key elements: first, practices must be social — not isolated individual behavior but cooperative activity conducted within a community; second, practices must have internal standards — criteria for what constitutes a "good chess player," "good architect," or "good scientist," defined by the practice itself rather than the external market; third, practices must pursue internal goods — excellences attainable only through participation in the practice itself.

麦金泰尔特别强调实践与制度（institution）的区分。制度是实践的外部组织形式——医院是医学实践的制度，大学是教育实践的制度，法庭是法律实践的制度。制度的功能是维持实践所需的外部条件（资金、场地、组织结构），但制度也始终面临着用外在善（利润、效率、声望）压制内在善的危险。这种张力是麦金泰尔分析现代制度腐败的核心框架。

MacIntyre particularly emphasizes the distinction between practice and institution. Institutions are the external organizational forms of practices — hospitals institutionalize medical practice, universities institutionalize educational practice, courts institutionalize legal practice. The function of institutions is to maintain the external conditions practices require (funding, premises, organizational structure), but institutions always face the danger of allowing external goods (profit, efficiency, prestige) to override internal goods. This tension is the core framework of MacIntyre's analysis of modern institutional corruption.

### 叙事 / Narrative

麦金泰尔的叙事概念建立在对人类行为之可理解性条件的分析之上。他反对行为主义和某些分析哲学将人类行为视为离散事件的集合的观点，主张人类行为本质上是叙事性的。一个行为的意义只有在叙事语境中才能被理解——"举起手臂"这个动作，在不同的叙事语境中可以是投票、打招呼、叫出租车或投降。

MacIntyre's concept of narrative is grounded in an analysis of the conditions of intelligibility of human actions. He opposes the behaviorist and certain analytic-philosophical views that treat human actions as collections of discrete events, arguing instead that human action is essentially narrative in character. The meaning of an action can only be understood within a narrative context — "raising an arm" can be voting, greeting, hailing a taxi, or surrendering, depending on the narrative context.

更重要的是，麦金泰尔将叙事概念扩展到人的整体生活。他提出了"叙事统一性"（narrative unity）的概念：一个人的生活不是孤立事件的序列，而是一个具有开端、发展和（预期的）结局的故事。美德是使这个生活叙事趋向统一和完善的品质。这种叙事统一性不是强加于生活之上的文学结构，而是生活本身的内在形式。我们通过叙事来理解自己和他人，这是人类作为"讲故事的动物"（story-telling animals）的根本特征。

More importantly, MacIntyre extends the narrative concept to the whole of human life. He proposes the concept of "narrative unity": a person's life is not a sequence of isolated events but a story with a beginning, development, and (anticipated) ending. A virtue is a quality that enables this life narrative to move toward unity and completion. This narrative unity is not a literary structure imposed upon life but an intrinsic form of life itself. We understand ourselves and others through narrative — this is the fundamental characteristic of human beings as "story-telling animals."

## 思想史脉络 / Intellectual History

麦金泰尔的"实践"概念有其哲学先驱。亚里士多德的"实践智慧"（phronesis）概念为麦金泰尔提供了重要基础——在亚里士多德看来，实践智慧不是理论知识的简单应用，而是通过参与政治和伦理实践而培养的判断力。伽达默尔（Hans-Georg Gadamer）的诠释学传统也对麦金泰尔产生了影响——特别是在理解总是嵌入在特定历史传统之中这一观点上。麦金泰尔的"叙事"概念则与利科（Paul Ricoeur）的叙事认同理论形成了密切的对话关系。两者都反对将个人身份视为某种固定的本质，而将其理解为通过叙事而构成的动态统一体。

MacIntyre's concept of "practice" has philosophical predecessors. Aristotle's concept of *phronesis* (practical wisdom) provided an important foundation — for Aristotle, practical wisdom is not the simple application of theoretical knowledge but a capacity of judgment cultivated through participation in political and ethical practice. Gadamer's hermeneutic tradition also influenced MacIntyre — particularly the idea that understanding is always embedded in a particular historical tradition. MacIntyre's concept of "narrative" entered into close dialogue with Ricoeur's narrative identity theory. Both reject the view of personal identity as a fixed essence, understanding it instead as a dynamic unity constituted through narrative.

## 关键文本 / Key Passages

> "By a 'practice' I am going to mean any coherent and complex form of socially established cooperative human activity through which goods internal to that form of activity are realized in the course of trying to achieve those standards of excellence which are appropriate to, and partially definitive of, that form of activity, with the result that human powers to achieve excellence, and human conceptions of the ends and goods involved, are systematically extended." (*After Virtue*, Ch. 14)

这是麦金泰尔对"实践"的完整定义，包含了实践的社会性、内在善和卓越标准三个核心要素。This is MacIntyre's complete definition of "practice," incorporating the three core elements: sociality, internal goods, and standards of excellence.

> "Man is in his actions and practice, as well as in his fictions, essentially a story-telling animal." (*After Virtue*, Ch. 15)

这是麦金泰尔对人类本性的一个核心刻画：我们不仅在虚构作品中，而且在行动和实践中都是讲故事的动物。This is a central characterization of human nature: we are story-telling animals not only in our fictions but in our actions and practices.

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

实践与叙事概念在当代社会科学中得到了广泛应用。在组织研究中，学者们使用"实践-制度"模型分析企业文化如何支持或损害其核心实践。在护理学中，实践概念帮助重新界定护理工作的意义——超越任务执行而追求卓越照护的内在善。在叙事医学中，叙事概念被用于培训医生理解患者的疾病故事。在教育学中，麦金泰尔的框架被用于重新思考教师培养——教师不仅是知识传授者，更是教育实践中的美德培养者。

The practice-narrative concepts have been widely applied in contemporary social science. In organizational studies, scholars use the "practice-institution" model to analyze how corporate cultures support or undermine their core practices. In nursing, the practice concept helps redefine the meaning of nursing work — beyond task execution toward the internal goods of excellent care. In narrative medicine, the narrative concept is used to train physicians in understanding patients' illness stories. In education, MacIntyre's framework has been used to rethink teacher preparation — teachers not merely as knowledge transmitters but as cultivators of virtue within the practice of education.

## 批评与回应 / Critiques and Responses

**批评 / Critique**: 一些学者质疑麦金泰尔对"实践"的界定过于狭隘，认为许多日常活动（如烹饪、园艺、育儿）也应当被视为实践，即使它们不完全符合他的严格定义。Some scholars question whether MacIntyre's definition of "practice" is too narrow, arguing that many everyday activities (cooking, gardening, parenting) should be regarded as practices even if they do not fully meet his strict definition.
**回应 / Response**: 麦金泰尔在后来的著作中承认，实践的范围比他最初的定义更为广泛，包括了家庭生活中的各种实践形式。MacIntyre acknowledged in later works that the scope of practice is broader than his initial definition, including various forms of practice within family life.

**批评 / Critique**: 后结构主义批评者认为叙事统一性概念压制了身份的多元性和流动性。Poststructuralist critics argue that the concept of narrative unity suppresses the plurality and fluidity of identity.
**回应 / Response**: 麦金泰尔指出，叙事统一性不意味着简单的一致性——一个好的叙事可以包含矛盾、转变和意外，关键是要有使这些元素变得可理解的统一框架。MacIntyre notes that narrative unity does not mean simple consistency — a good narrative can contain contradictions, transformations, and surprises; what matters is a unifying framework that makes these elements intelligible.

## 跨文化回响 / Cross-cultural Echoes

麦金泰尔的"实践"概念与儒家的"礼"（ritual propriety）形成了深层的共鸣。在儒家传统中，"礼"不是外在的行为规范，而是通过持续的社会实践而内化的品质——这与麦金泰尔对实践作为美德培育场域的理解高度一致。同样，儒家的"修身"概念——通过日常实践来培养和完善自我——可以被理解为一种特定形式的麦金泰尔式实践。在叙事方面，中国史学传统中的"纪传体"（biographical narrative）与麦金泰尔的叙事统一性概念相呼应：通过为一个人撰写传记，我们揭示了其生活的叙事结构和道德意义。

MacIntyre's concept of "practice" resonates deeply with the Confucian concept of *li* (ritual propriety). In the Confucian tradition, *li* is not an external behavioral norm but a quality internalized through sustained social practice — closely aligned with MacIntyre's understanding of practice as an arena for virtue cultivation. Similarly, the Confucian concept of *xiushen* (self-cultivation) — perfecting oneself through daily practice — can be understood as a particular form of MacIntyrean practice. Regarding narrative, the Chinese historiographical tradition of *jizhuanti* (biographical narrative) echoes MacIntyre's concept of narrative unity: by writing a biography, we reveal the narrative structure and moral significance of a person's life.
