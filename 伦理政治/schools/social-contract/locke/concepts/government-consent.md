# 基于同意的政府 · Government by Consent

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

洛克的"基于同意的政府"理论是他政治哲学的拱顶石：一切正当的政治权力都必须建立在被治理者的**同意**（consent）之上，政府的唯一正当目的是保护人民的自然权利（生命、自由和财产），而人民仅将执行自然法的权力以**信托**（trust）的形式委托给政府——而非绝对让渡。当政府违背信托、侵犯自然权利时，人民有权**解散**它并建立新政府。这一"革命权"理论是洛克与霍布斯最根本的分歧，也是现代民主政治的哲学基石。

Locke's theory of "government by consent" is the keystone of his political philosophy: all legitimate political power must rest on the **consent** of the governed; government's sole legitimate purpose is to protect natural rights (life, liberty, and property); and the people merely **entrust** the executive power of the law of nature to government — rather than absolutely alienating it. When government breaches trust and violates natural rights, the people have the right to **dissolve** it and establish a new one. This "right of revolution" theory marks the most fundamental divergence from Hobbes, and constitutes the philosophical bedrock of modern democratic politics.

## 理论内涵 / Theoretical Content

### 从自然状态到政治社会 / From State of Nature to Political Society

洛克的自然状态虽然不同于霍布斯的战争状态，但存在三个严重的"不便"（inconveniences），驱使理性的人们寻求建立政治社会：

Locke's state of nature, while distinct from Hobbes's state of war, suffers from three grave "inconveniences" that drive rational people to seek political society:

1. **缺乏确定的、已知的法律**（Want of established, settled, known law）——自然法虽然存在，但人们对它的理解不一，缺乏统一的裁判标准 / Although natural law exists, people interpret it differently, lacking a unified standard of adjudication
2. **缺乏公正的法官**（Want of a known and indifferent judge）——在自然状态中，每个人都是自己案件的法官，偏私在所难免 / In the state of nature, everyone is judge in their own case, and partiality is inevitable
3. **缺乏执行判决的权力**（Want of power to back and support sentences）——即使判决正确，也缺乏强制执行的手段 / Even correct judgments lack means of enforcement

### 信托 vs 让渡：与霍布斯的根本分歧 / Trust vs Alienation: The Fundamental Divergence from Hobbes

洛克与霍布斯的关键区别在于对**社会契约性质**的理解：

The key difference between Locke and Hobbes lies in their understanding of the **nature of the social contract**:

| 要素 Element | 霍布斯 Hobbes（让渡 Alienation） | 洛克 Locke（信托 Trust） |
|---|---|---|
| 权利让渡 Rights transferred | 全部（仅保留自我保存权）All (except self-preservation) | 仅执行自然法的权力 Only executive power of natural law |
| 可撤回性 Revocability | 不可撤回 Irrevocable | 可撤回（违背信托时）Revocable (upon breach of trust) |
| 政府地位 Government's position | 契约之外（第三方受益人）Outside the contract | 信托之内（受托人）Within the trust (trustee) |
| 革命权 Right of revolution | 无 None | **有** Yes |

洛克强调，人们进入政治社会时并没有放弃自己的自然权利——他们只是将**执行**这些权利的权力委托给了公共权威。这种委托是有条件的、可撤回的，如同信托关系中的受益人可以在受托人违反信托义务时解除信托。

Locke emphasized that people entering political society did not surrender their natural rights — they merely delegated the **executive** power to enforce those rights to a public authority. This delegation is conditional and revocable, just as beneficiaries in a trust relationship can dissolve the trust when the trustee breaches fiduciary duty.

### 革命权的论证 / The Argument for the Right of Revolution

洛克是西方思想史上最早系统论证**革命权**的思想家。他的论证结构如下：

Locke was among the earliest thinkers in Western intellectual history to systematically argue for the **right of revolution**. His argumentative structure:

1. 政府是保护自然权利的**工具**，不是绝对的主权者 / Government is an **instrument** for protecting natural rights, not an absolute sovereign
2. 当立法者或行政者**侵犯财产权**或**企图获得绝对权力**时，即进入"与人民的战争状态" / When legislators or executives **violate property rights** or **attempt to gain absolute power**, they enter "a state of war with the people"
3. 在这种情况下，人民有权**诉诸上天**（appeal to Heaven）——即通过革命来裁判争议 / In such cases, the people have the right to **appeal to Heaven** — i.e., to adjudicate the dispute through revolution
4. 革命不是无政府主义——人民革命后应当**重建**政府，而非永久处于无政府状态 / Revolution is not anarchism — after revolution the people should **reconstitute** government, not remain permanently in anarchy

### 分权理论 / Theory of Separation of Powers

洛克提出了权力的分离，虽然不如后来孟德斯鸠的三权分立那样精致：

Locke proposed a separation of powers, though less refined than Montesquieu's later tripartite system:

- **立法权**（Legislative Power）：制定法律的权力——最高权力，但受制于自然法和公共利益 / Power to make laws — supreme, but bound by natural law and public good
- **执行权**（Executive Power）：执行法律的权力——从属于立法权 / Power to execute laws — subordinate to legislative
- **对外权**（Federative Power）：处理战争、和平与外交的权力——通常与执行权由同一人执掌 / Power over war, peace, and diplomacy — typically held by the same person as executive power

## 思想史脉络 / Intellectual History

洛克的同意理论是在英国十七世纪的政治辩论中诞生的。它的直接对手是罗伯特·菲尔默（Robert Filmer）的《家长制》（*Patriarcha*），该书主张国王的权力来源于上帝对亚当的授权，通过世袭传承至今。洛克的《政府论》上篇（First Treatise）逐章反驳了菲尔默的论证，下篇（Second Treatise）则正面阐述了自己的同意理论。

Locke's consent theory was born in the political debates of seventeenth-century England. Its direct adversary was Robert Filmer's *Patriarcha*, which argued that the king's power derived from God's grant to Adam, transmitted through hereditary succession. Locke's *First Treatise* systematically refuted Filmer's argument chapter by chapter, while the *Second Treatise* positively articulated his own consent theory.

洛克的理论对美国革命产生了最直接的影响。杰斐逊在《独立宣言》中的论证结构——列举国王的暴行，由此论证人民有权解散旧政府建立新政府——几乎是洛克革命权理论的实践翻版。法国大革命时期的《人权宣言》同样深受洛克影响。在当代，罗尔斯的"原初状态"（original position）和"假设同意"（hypothetical consent）概念可以被看作洛克同意理论在当代的哲学重构。

Locke's theory had the most direct impact on the American Revolution. Jefferson's argumentative structure in the Declaration of Independence — enumerating the king's abuses to justify the people's right to dissolve old government and establish new — is virtually a practical enactment of Locke's revolution theory. The French Declaration of the Rights of Man was likewise deeply influenced by Locke. In contemporary thought, Rawls's "original position" and "hypothetical consent" can be seen as philosophical reconstructions of Lockean consent theory.

## 关键文本 / Key Passages

> "The legislative cannot transfer the power of making laws to any other hands: for it being but a delegated power from the people, they who have it cannot pass it over to others."
> — *Two Treatises*, Second Treatise, §141

> "立法权不能转让制定法律的权力给任何其他人：因为它只是人民委托的权力，拥有这种权力的人不能把它转让给他人。"

> "Whenever the legislators endeavour to take away, and destroy the property of the people, or to reduce them to slavery under arbitrary power, they put themselves into a state of war with the people, who are thereupon absolved from any farther obedience."
> — *Two Treatises*, Second Treatise, §222

> "当立法者企图夺取和破坏人民的财产，或把他们贬低为奴隶受专制权力支配时，他们就使自己与人民处于战争状态，人民因此免除了进一步服从的义务。"

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

洛克的同意理论在当代政治中仍然具有核心地位。**民主选举**本身就是同意原则的制度化表达——定期的、自由的选举是人民表达对政府同意的机制。**宪法法院的违宪审查**体现了洛克的信托理论——宪法是人民对政府设定的信托条件，法院是判断政府是否违背信托的裁判者。**公民不服从**（civil disobedience）运动——从马丁·路德·金到当代的民主运动——都在不同程度上援引了洛克的革命权理论：当政府系统性地违背信托时，人民有权以非暴力方式表达不服从。

Locke's consent theory retains a central place in contemporary politics. **Democratic elections** are themselves the institutionalized expression of the consent principle — regular, free elections are the mechanism by which the people express consent to government. **Constitutional judicial review** embodies Locke's trust theory — the constitution sets the trust conditions the people impose on government, and courts adjudicate whether government has breached the trust. **Civil disobedience** movements — from Martin Luther King to contemporary democratic movements — invoke Locke's revolution theory in varying degrees: when government systematically breaches trust, the people have the right to express nonviolent disobedience.

## 批评与回应 / Critiques and Responses

**休谟（Hume）的"默示同意"批评**：洛克认为住在一国领土上就构成了对政府的"默示同意"，但休谟指出这是荒谬的——一个贫苦的农民真的能自由地选择离开自己的祖国吗？把"留在"等同于"同意"是对同意概念的滥用。洛克辩护者回应：默示同意确实是弱形式的同意，但洛克同时区分了"明示同意"（express consent）和"默示同意"，前者才产生完整的公民身份。 / Locke held that residing on a country's territory constitutes "tacit consent" to its government, but Hume pointed out this is absurd — can a poor peasant truly choose to leave their homeland? Equating "staying" with "consent" abuses the concept. Lockean defenders respond: tacit consent is indeed a weak form of consent, but Locke distinguished it from "express consent," the latter alone producing full citizenship.

**女性主义批评**：洛克的"同意"预设了同意的主体是独立的个人，但在十七世纪的英国，已婚妇女在法律上并不具有独立的同意能力。洛克的社会契约在事实上是一个**男性的契约**（Carole Pateman: *The Sexual Contract*）。 / Locke's "consent" presupposes that the consenting subject is an independent individual, but in seventeenth-century England, married women lacked independent legal capacity to consent. Locke's social contract was in fact a **sexual contract** (Carole Pateman).

## 跨文化回响 / Cross-cultural Echoes

洛克的同意理论与孟子的"天命"观有着深层的呼应。孟子认为，天子的权力来源于"天命"，而天命的体现就是人民的接受——"天视自我民视，天听自我民听"。当统治者丧失天命（即失去人民的接受），革命就是正当的——"闻诛一夫纣矣，未闻弑君也"。这与洛克的革命权理论在结构上惊人地一致：两者都将统治的正当性建立在被治者的某种形式的接受之上。然而，关键差异在于：洛克的"同意"是**个人主义**的（每个人的同意），孟子的"天命"是**整体性**的（天下之民的集体接受）。

Locke's consent theory resonates deeply with Mencius's doctrine of the "Mandate of Heaven." Mencius held that the emperor's power derives from Heaven's mandate, and that mandate is manifested in the people's acceptance — "Heaven sees as the people see; Heaven hears as the people hear." When a ruler loses the Mandate (i.e., loses the people's acceptance), revolution is legitimate — "I have heard of the execution of the fellow Zhou, but not of regicide." This structurally parallels Locke: both ground political legitimacy in some form of the governed's acceptance. The key difference, however, is that Locke's "consent" is **individualistic** (each person's consent), while Mencius's "Mandate" is **holistic** (the collective acceptance of all under heaven).
