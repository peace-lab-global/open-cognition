# 财产权劳动论 · Labor Theory of Property

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

洛克的财产权劳动论是他政治哲学中最具原创性和争议性的理论。洛克主张：上帝将世界"给予人类共有"（given to mankind in common），但每个人对自己的**人身**（person）拥有财产权，因此对自己的**劳动**（labour）——即人身的活动——也拥有财产权。当一个人将自己的劳动"掺入"（mix with）某种自然物，使之脱离其原始的共有状态时，该物即成为他的私有财产。这一理论试图回答一个根本问题：在上帝将世界给予全人类共有的前提下，私有财产权如何可能？

Locke's labour theory of property is the most original and controversial element of his political philosophy. Locke argues: God gave the world "to mankind in common," but every person has a property in their own **person**, and therefore in the **labour** of their body — the work of their hands. When a person **mixes** their labour with any natural object, removing it from its original common state, that object becomes their private property. This theory attempts to answer a fundamental question: given that God gave the world to all humankind in common, how is private property possible?

财产权劳动论的重要性远超其历史语境。它为知识产权理论、数字财产权、环境正义等当代议题提供了基本的分析框架，同时也引发了关于殖民主义正当性、财富不平等等持久争论。

The labour theory of property's significance extends far beyond its historical context. It provides the basic analytical framework for contemporary debates on intellectual property, digital property rights, and environmental justice, while also generating enduring controversies about the justification of colonialism and wealth inequality.

## 理论内涵 / Theoretical Content

### 自我所有权与劳动掺入 / Self-Ownership and Labour Mixing

洛克的论证建立在一个关键的逻辑链条上：

Locke's argument rests on a crucial logical chain:

1. **自我所有权**（Self-ownership）：每个人对自己的人身拥有财产权——"每人对他自己的人身享有一种财产权，除他以外任何人都没有这种权利" / Every man has a property in his own person — "this nobody has any right to but himself"
2. **劳动所有权**（Ownership of labour）：身体的劳动是身体的延伸，因此人对自己的劳动也拥有财产权 / The labour of the body is an extension of the body, so one owns one's labour
3. **劳动掺入**（Labour mixing）：当劳动被掺入自然物时，该物即被"标记"为劳动者的财产 / When labour is mixed with a natural object, that object is "marked" as the labourer's property
4. **增值论证**（Value-added argument）：劳动是价值的主要来源——"将劳动加入任何东西，使它与共有物区别开来，这就产生了财产权" / Labour is the primary source of value — "labour being the unquestionable property of the labourer, no man but he can have a right to what that is once joined to"

洛克特别强调劳动创造价值的力量："劳动使一切东西具有不同的价值。"他估计，土地产出的十分之九是劳动的结果，仅有十分之一来自自然本身。这一观点后来被亚当·斯密和大卫·李嘉图发展为劳动价值论，又被马克思推向了极致。

Locke especially emphasized labour's value-creating power: "Labour makes the far greatest part of the value of things we enjoy in the world." He estimated that nine-tenths of the products of the earth effective to human life are the products of labour, only one-tenth from nature itself. This view was later developed into the labour theory of value by Adam Smith and David Ricardo, and pushed to its extreme by Marx.

### 两个限制条件 / The Two Provisos

洛克的财产权并非不受限制。他设定了两个著名的约束条件：

Locke's property right is not unconstrained. He set two famous limiting conditions:

**充足条件**（Sufficiency Proviso）：占有的前提是"至少还留有足够的和同样好的东西给其他人所共有"（at least where there is enough, and as good left in common for others）。这一条件确保私有财产不会剥夺他人生存的机会。 / Appropriation is permissible only when "there is enough, and as good left in common for others." This proviso ensures that private property does not deprive others of the means of survival.

**腐败条件**（Spoilage Proviso）：一个人占有的数量不应超过他能够在其腐败之前加以使用的限度。"谁能在一件东西败坏之前尽量用它来供生活所需，谁就可以在那个限度内以他的劳动多少确定他的财产权。" / One should not appropriate more than one can use before it spoils. "As much as anyone can make use of to any advantage of life before it spoils, so much he may by his labour fix a property in."

### 货币的作用 / The Role of Money

洛克认为，货币的发明**根本性地改变**了财产权的格局。货币不会腐败（金银可以无限保存），因此腐败条件事实上失效了。同时，货币使不等价的交换成为可能，导致土地和其他资源的分配日益不均——充足条件也随之松动。洛克认为这是通过人们的**默示同意**（tacit consent）实现的："人们同意使用货币，即同意了不平等的占有。"

Locke held that the invention of money **fundamentally transformed** the landscape of property. Money does not spoil (gold and silver can be stored indefinitely), so the spoilage proviso effectively ceases to operate. Simultaneously, money enables unequal exchanges, leading to increasingly uneven distribution of land and resources — the sufficiency proviso loosens accordingly. Locke held this was accomplished through people's **tacit consent**: "men have agreed to a disproportionate and unequal possession of the earth through the use of money."

## 思想史脉络 / Intellectual History

洛克的财产权理论在思想史上处于一个关键的转折点。在他之前，格劳秀斯和普芬道夫认为财产权来源于人类的约定或同意（convention）。洛克的革命性在于：他认为财产权先于政治社会和人类约定——它直接来源于劳动，是一种**自然权利**而非法律权利。这一立场使他能够为抵抗君主侵犯臣民财产权提供强有力的哲学依据。

Locke's property theory occupies a crucial turning point in intellectual history. Before him, Grotius and Pufendorf held that property arose from human convention or agreement. Locke's revolutionary move was to argue that property precedes both political society and human convention — it arises directly from labour, as a **natural right** rather than a legal right. This position enabled him to provide a powerful philosophical basis for resisting monarchical expropriation of subjects' property.

然而，洛克的财产权理论也被用于为殖民主义辩护。他在《政府论》下篇中论证，未开垦的美洲土地不属于美洲原住民——因为他们没有通过劳动"改良"土地。这一论证被广泛用于为欧洲殖民者剥夺原住民土地提供合法性，至今仍是洛克理论中最受争议的部分。

However, Locke's property theory was also deployed to justify colonialism. In the *Second Treatise*, he argued that uncultivated American land did not belong to indigenous peoples — because they had not "improved" it through labour. This argument was widely used to legitimize European colonizers' dispossession of indigenous peoples, and remains the most controversial aspect of Locke's theory to this day.

## 关键文本 / Key Passages

> "Though the earth, and all inferior creatures, be common to all men, yet every man has a property in his own person: this nobody has any right to but himself. The labour of his body, and the work of his hands, we may say, are properly his. Whatsoever then he removes out of the state that nature hath provided, and left it in, he hath mixed his labour with, and joined to it something that is his own, and thereby makes it his property."
> — *Two Treatises*, Second Treatise, §27

> "虽然土地和一切低等造物为人类共有，但每人对他自己的人身享有一种财产权，除他以外任何人都没有这种权利。他的身体所从事的劳动和他的双手所进行的工作，我们可以说，是正当地属于他的。所以只要他使任何东西脱离自然所提供的和那个东西所处的状态，他就已经掺进他的劳动，在这上面参加他自己所有的某种东西，因而使它成为他的财产。"

这段引文是洛克财产权论证的核心段落。注意其中的论证结构：从自我所有权出发，经由劳动所有权，到劳动掺入自然物，最终得出财产权。这一链条的每一步都受到了后世学者的严格审视。

This passage is the core of Locke's property argument. Note its structure: from self-ownership, through ownership of labour, to mixing labour with natural objects, culminating in property rights. Every step in this chain has been subjected to rigorous scholarly scrutiny.

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

洛克的劳动财产权论在当代有三大前沿应用领域。在**知识产权**方面，创作者将智力劳动"掺入"公共知识领域（如算法、文学作品、发明），从而产生对作品的权利——但"充足条件"提出了重要质疑：专利是否阻碍了他人对相同知识领域的利用？在**数字财产权**方面，用户在社交媒体平台上投入的劳动（创作内容、建立社交网络）是否产生了洛克式的财产权？平台对用户数据的控制是否构成对"劳动产品"的剥夺？在**环境伦理**方面，洛克的"腐败条件"被重新诠释为可持续发展原则——过度开采自然资源违反了洛克式财产权的内在限制。

Locke's labour theory of property has three frontier applications today. In **intellectual property**, creators "mix" intellectual labour with the commons of public knowledge (algorithms, literary works, inventions), generating rights over their works — but the sufficiency proviso raises important questions: do patents prevent others from using the same domain of knowledge? In **digital property**, does user labour invested in social media platforms (creating content, building social networks) generate Lockean property rights? Does platform control over user data constitute expropriation of "the product of labour"? In **environmental ethics**, Locke's spoilage proviso has been reinterpreted as a sustainability principle — over-extraction of natural resources violates the internal limits of Lockean property.

## 批评与回应 / Critiques and Responses

**诺齐克（Nozick）的"番茄汁"反驳**：如果我把自己的一罐番茄汁倒入大海，我是否拥有了大海？劳动"掺入"自然物的比喻不够精确——我们需要更清晰的财产权获取理论。洛克辩护者回应：洛克的条件（充足条件、腐败条件）恰恰是为了防止这种荒谬的结果。 / The "tomato juice" objection: if I pour a can of tomato juice into the ocean, do I own the ocean? The "mixing" metaphor is imprecise — we need a clearer theory of property acquisition. Lockean defenders respond: Locke's provisos (sufficiency, spoilage) are precisely designed to prevent such absurd results.

**马克思的异化批评**：资本主义使劳动者与其劳动产品相分离——工人创造了价值却无法拥有产品。这是对洛克财产权论的最激进的内在批判：如果劳动创造财产权，那么资本家占有剩余价值就是对工人财产权的侵犯。 / Capitalism separates workers from the products of their labour — workers create value but cannot own the product. This is the most radical immanent critique of Locke: if labour creates property, then capitalists' appropriation of surplus value is a violation of workers' property rights.

**殖民主义批评**：洛克的"改良"论证被用于剥夺原住民土地——他们的狩猎采集生活方式不符合洛克的"劳动改良"标准。当代回应：洛克的劳动概念过于狭隘，忽视了非农业的土地利用方式。 / Locke's "improvement" argument was used to dispossess indigenous peoples — their hunter-gatherer lifestyles did not meet Locke's standard of "labour improvement." Contemporary response: Locke's concept of labour is too narrow, neglecting non-agricultural forms of land use.

## 跨文化回响 / Cross-cultural Echoes

洛克的劳动财产权论与中国传统的"耕者有其田"理念有着深层的共鸣。两者都主张：直接从事劳动的人应当拥有劳动的成果。然而，洛克的论证是**个人主义**的——财产权属于个人劳动者；而中国传统更倾向于**家庭/社区**层面的权利——土地权利与家族传承和村落共同体密切相关。在道家传统中，庄子对"有用"与"无用"的区分也质疑了洛克的"改良"标准：一棵"无用"的大树因其不被砍伐而得以保全——洛克式的"改良"是否恰恰是对自然之"用"的狭隘理解？

Locke's labour theory of property resonates deeply with the traditional Chinese principle that "those who till the land should own it." Both hold that those who directly perform labour should own its fruits. However, Locke's argument is **individualistic** — property belongs to the individual labourer; while Chinese tradition tends toward **family/community-level** rights — land rights are closely tied to family lineage and village community. In the Daoist tradition, Zhuangzi's distinction between "useful" and "useless" also challenges Locke's "improvement" standard: a "useless" great tree survives precisely because it is not cut down — is Lockean "improvement" precisely a narrow understanding of nature's "use"?
