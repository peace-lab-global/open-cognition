# 承认的政治 · Politics of Recognition

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

"承认的政治"（politics of recognition）是泰勒在1992年发表的同名论文中系统阐述的核心概念，也是当代认同政治（identity politics）和多元文化主义（multiculturalism）讨论的奠基性文本。泰勒论证，在现代民主社会中，"认同"（identity）与"承认"（recognition）之间存在本质的联系——我们的认同部分地由他者的承认或误认（misrecognition）所构成；如果社会向一个人或群体投射出贬低性的、限制性的形象，那么被投射者就会内化这种形象，遭受真实的、深层的伤害。

The "politics of recognition" is the central concept Taylor systematically elaborated in his 1992 essay of the same name, and it is the foundational text for contemporary identity politics and multiculturalism discussions. Taylor argues that in modern democratic societies, there is an essential link between "identity" and "recognition" — our identity is partly constituted by others' recognition or misrecognition; when society projects a demeaning or constricting image onto a person or group, those targeted internalize this image and suffer real, deep harm.

泰勒的核心贡献在于将"承认"从一个哲学概念（源自黑格尔的主奴辩证法）转化为一个政治理论概念——承认不再只是意识哲学的问题，而是正义理论的问题。

Taylor's core contribution lies in transforming "recognition" from a philosophical concept (derived from Hegel's master-slave dialectic) into a political theory concept — recognition is no longer merely a question of philosophy of consciousness but a question of justice theory.

## 理论内涵 / Theoretical Content

### 认同的对话性构成 / The Dialogical Constitution of Identity

泰勒论证，人类认同不是独白式（monological）形成的，而是对话式（dialogical）构成的。我们通过与"重要他者"（significant others）的互动来形成自我理解——在童年，父母和同伴是我们最重要的"镜子"；在成年，更广泛的社会和文化环境提供了我们理解自身的框架。因此，认同不是"我独自发现的东西"，而是"在与他者的对话中不断构成和重构的东西"。

Taylor argues that human identity is not formed monologically but constituted dialogically. We form self-understanding through interaction with "significant others" — in childhood, parents and peers are our most important "mirrors"; in adulthood, broader social and cultural environments provide the frameworks through which we understand ourselves. Identity is therefore not "something I discover alone" but "something continually constituted and reconstituted in dialogue with others."

### 两种承认政治 / Two Forms of the Politics of Recognition

泰勒区分了两种承认政治的传统：

Taylor distinguishes two traditions of the politics of recognition:

**第一种：平等尊严的政治 / First: The Politics of Equal Dignity**
源于康德的普遍主义传统——所有人作为理性主体都享有平等的尊严。这种政治要求"色盲"（color-blind）的平等对待——法律不应考虑个人的种族、性别、文化差异，而应将所有人视为平等的权利主体。罗尔斯的政治自由主义是这一传统的当代表达。From the Kantian universalist tradition — all persons as rational subjects enjoy equal dignity. This politics demands "color-blind" equal treatment — law should not consider individuals' race, gender, or cultural differences but treat all as equal rights-bearing subjects. Rawls' political liberalism is this tradition's contemporary expression.

**第二种：差异的政治 / Second: The Politics of Difference**
源于赫尔德的特殊主义传统——每个个体和每个文化群体都有其独特的认同，这种独特性值得承认和尊重。这种政治要求承认差异——不是将所有人视为"一样的"，而是承认他们"不同的"认同具有平等的价值。赫尔德的"民族精神"（Volksgeist）概念是这一传统的源头。From the Herderian particularist tradition — each individual and each cultural group has a unique identity that deserves recognition and respect. This politics demands recognition of difference — not treating everyone as "the same" but acknowledging their "different" identities as having equal value. Herder's "Volksgeist" concept is this tradition's origin.

### 两种政治之间的张力 / The Tension Between the Two

泰勒论证，这两种政治之间存在真实的张力。平等尊严的政治可能忽视少数群体的特殊需要——例如，"色盲"政策可能实际上强化了主流群体的文化霸权。差异的政治可能违反平等对待的原则——例如，给予某些群体特殊权利可能被视为对其他群体的歧视。

Taylor argues that there is a real tension between these two forms. The politics of equal dignity may neglect minority groups' special needs — for example, "color-blind" policies may actually reinforce the dominant group's cultural hegemony. The politics of difference may violate the principle of equal treatment — for example, granting certain groups special rights may be seen as discrimination against other groups.

泰勒提出的解决方案是"有保留的承认"（presumption of equal worth）——我们应当假设所有文化都有能力产生对人类有价值的贡献，但这种假设需要通过实际的研究和对话来检验，而不是不加批判地接受。

Taylor's proposed solution is a "presumption of equal worth" — we should presume that all cultures have the potential to produce contributions of value to humanity, but this presumption needs to be tested through actual study and dialogue, not accepted uncritically.

### 误认作为伤害 / Misrecognition as Harm

泰勒最具影响力的论点之一是"误认即伤害"（misrecognition as harm）。他论证，误认不只是"冒犯"（offense）或"不礼貌"（incivility），而是一种深层的、结构性的伤害——它通过投射贬低性的形象来扭曲被误认者的自我理解，限制他们的行动可能性，削弱他们的自尊。这一论点为理解种族主义、性别歧视、文化偏见等结构性不平等的机制提供了重要理论资源。

One of Taylor's most influential arguments is "misrecognition as harm." He argues that misrecognition is not merely offense or incivility but a deep, structural harm — it distorts the misrecognized person's self-understanding by projecting demeaning images, limits their possibilities for action, and undermines their self-respect. This argument provides important theoretical resources for understanding the mechanisms of structural inequality such as racism, sexism, and cultural prejudice.

## 思想史脉络 / Intellectual History

"承认"概念的思想谱系可追溯到黑格尔的"主奴辩证法"（master-slave dialectic）。在《精神现象学》（*Phenomenology of Spirit*, 1807）中，黑格尔论证自我意识需要另一个自我意识的承认——"自我意识只有在另一个自我意识中才能得到满足"。这一思想经过科耶夫（Alexandre Kojève）的法国化解读，影响了萨特（Sartre）、法农（Fanon）和法兰克福学派。

The "recognition" concept's intellectual genealogy traces to Hegel's "master-slave dialectic." In *Phenomenology of Spirit* (1807), Hegel argues that self-consciousness needs another self-consciousness's recognition — "self-consciousness finds satisfaction only in another self-consciousness." This thought, through Alexandre Kojève's French interpretation, influenced Sartre, Fanon, and the Frankfurt School.

泰勒的贡献在于将这一哲学传统引入当代政治理论——他展示了"承认"如何从意识哲学的问题转变为多元文化社会中正义理论的问题。这一转变的直接背景是20世纪末加拿大的政治危机——魁北克分离运动、原住民权利运动和多元文化主义政策的争论。

Taylor's contribution was bringing this philosophical tradition into contemporary political theory — he demonstrated how "recognition" transforms from a philosophy of consciousness question to a justice theory question in multicultural societies. The direct context for this transformation was late 20th-century Canadian political crises — Quebec separatist movements, indigenous rights movements, and multiculturalism policy debates.

## 关键文本 / Key Passages

> "Nonrecognition or misrecognition can inflict harm, can be a form of oppression, imprisoning someone in a false, distorted, and reduced mode of being."
> — Taylor, "The Politics of Recognition" (1992), p. 25

> "不承认或误认可以施加伤害，可以成为一种压制形式，将某人囚禁在虚假的、扭曲的、缩减的存在方式中。"

> "Our identity is partly shaped by recognition or its absence, often by the misrecognition of others, and so a person or group of people can suffer real damage, real distortion, if the people or society around them mirror back to them a confining or demeaning or contemptible picture of themselves."
> — Taylor, "The Politics of Recognition" (1992), p. 25

> "我们的认同部分地由承认或其缺席所塑造，往往由他者的误认所塑造，因此，如果周围的人或社会向他们投射出一种限制性的、贬低性的或可鄙的自我形象，一个人或群体就会遭受真实的伤害、真实的扭曲。"

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

承认政治理论在当代有广泛而深远的应用。在原住民权利运动中，泰勒的理论为"承认原住民独特的文化和政治地位"提供了哲学论证——不仅仅是经济赔偿，更需要文化和政治上的承认。在跨性别权利运动中，"误认即伤害"的框架为理解"拒绝承认跨性别者的性别认同"如何构成结构性伤害提供了理论工具。在国际关系中，承认政治为分析"文明冲突"提供了新视角——某些冲突的根源不是利益冲突，而是文化间的不承认或误认。在全球化语境中，承认政治也涉及如何在全球范围内承认不同文明和传统的价值——亨廷顿的"文明冲突论"恰恰是一种不承认的表达。

The politics of recognition theory has broad and far-reaching contemporary applications. In indigenous rights movements, Taylor's theory provides philosophical justification for "recognizing indigenous peoples' unique cultural and political status" — not merely economic compensation but cultural and political recognition. In transgender rights movements, the "misrecognition as harm" framework provides a theoretical tool for understanding how "refusing to recognize transgender people's gender identity" constitutes structural harm. In international relations, the politics of recognition provides new perspectives for analyzing "clash of civilizations" — some conflicts root not in conflicting interests but in non-recognition or misrecognition between cultures. In globalization contexts, the politics of recognition also involves how to recognize the value of different civilizations and traditions globally — Huntington's "clash of civilizations" thesis is precisely an expression of non-recognition.

## 批评与回应 / Critiques and Responses

**批评一：认同的本质主义 / Critique 1: Essentialism of Identity**
后结构主义批评者（如Judith Butler）认为，泰勒的承认政治预设了相对稳定的群体认同——但认同是流动的、杂交的、不断被解构和重构的。Poststructuralist critics (such as Judith Butler) argue that Taylor's politics of recognition presupposes relatively stable group identities — but identity is fluid, hybrid, and constantly deconstructed and reconstructed.

**回应 / Response:** 泰勒在后续著作中承认了认同的流动性和杂交性，但坚持认为即使流动的认同也需要承认——问题不是认同是否固定，而是承认的政治条件是否允许认同的自由形成。Taylor acknowledges identity's fluidity and hybridity in later works but insists that even fluid identities need recognition — the issue is not whether identity is fixed but whether the political conditions for recognition allow free identity formation.

**批评二：承认与再分配 / Critique 2: Recognition vs Redistribution**
南希·弗雷泽（Nancy Fraser）提出了著名的"承认与再分配"（recognition vs redistribution）两难——承认政治可能转移对经济不平等的关注。Nancy Fraser posed the famous "recognition vs redistribution" dilemma — the politics of recognition may divert attention from economic inequality.

**回应 / Response:** 泰勒承认经济不平等的重要性，但论证了承认和经济正义不是竞争性的零和关系——误认和经济剥夺往往是相互加强的。Taylor acknowledges economic inequality's importance but argues that recognition and economic justice are not competitive zero-sum relationships — misrecognition and economic deprivation are often mutually reinforcing.

## 跨文化回响 / Cross-cultural Echoes

承认政治与儒家的"礼"（li，ritual propriety）概念之间存在有趣的对话空间。儒家的"礼"本质上是一种相互承认的制度化实践——通过仪式和规范来表达对他者身份和地位的承认。然而，儒家的"礼"强调等级性的承认（君臣、父子），而泰勒的承认政治追求平等性的承认。佛教的"无缘大慈、同体大悲"概念则为超越身份差异的普遍承认提供了资源——这与泰勒试图调和普遍主义和特殊主义的努力形成有趣的对照。

The politics of recognition opens interesting dialogue with the Confucian concept of "li" (礼, ritual propriety). Confucian "li" is essentially an institutionalized practice of mutual recognition — expressing recognition of others' identity and status through ritual and norms. However, Confucian "li" emphasizes hierarchical recognition (ruler-minister, father-son), while Taylor's politics of recognition pursues egalitarian recognition. Buddhism's concept of "boundless compassion and shared embodiment" (无缘大慈、同体大悲) provides resources for universal recognition transcending identity differences — forming an interesting contrast with Taylor's attempt to reconcile universalism and particularism.
