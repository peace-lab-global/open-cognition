# 情感的剧变 · Upheavals of Thought

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

《思想的剧变：情感的智性》（Upheavals of Thought: The Intelligence of Emotions, 2001）是纳斯鲍姆最具雄心的哲学著作之一，全书超过700页，系统地重构了情感的哲学理论。其核心论题是：情感并非非理性的冲动或纯粹的生理反应，而是包含"升华性判断"（eudaimonistic judgments）的复杂认知状态——它们体现了主体对其所重视之事物的评价性认识。

Upheavals of Thought: The Intelligence of Emotions (2001) is one of Nussbaum's most ambitious philosophical works, spanning over 700 pages and systematically reconstructing the philosophical theory of emotions. Its core thesis is that emotions are not irrational impulses or mere physiological responses, but complex cognitive states containing "eudaimonistic judgments" — they embody the subject's evaluative recognition of what they value.

这一论题具有深远的哲学意涵。如果情感包含认知，那么情感教育就成为伦理学和政治哲学的核心议题；如果情感是对重要事物的评价性认识，那么文学、音乐、公共纪念等能够塑造情感的文化实践就具有了独特的伦理价值。纳斯鲍姆通过这一理论，将情感从传统伦理学的边缘地带提升到了核心位置。

This thesis has far-reaching philosophical implications. If emotions contain cognition, then emotion education becomes a central concern of ethics and political philosophy. If emotions are evaluative recognition of important things, then cultural practices that shape emotions — literature, music, public commemoration — acquire distinctive ethical value. Through this theory, Nussbaum elevates emotions from the margins of traditional ethics to its center.

## 理论内涵 / Theoretical Content

纳斯鲍姆的情感理论建立在三个关键命题之上：

Nussbaum's emotion theory rests on three key propositions:

**命题一：情感是认知性的。** 每种情感都包含对某事物的判断（judgment），这种判断不是简单的信念，而是一种"升华性判断"——即主体认识到某个对象对其自身的繁荣（eudaimonia）具有重要意义。例如，恐惧不仅是对危险的生理反应，更是对"某事物威胁到我珍视的东西"这一判断的体现。

**Proposition 1: Emotions are cognitive.** Each emotion contains a judgment about something — not a simple belief, but a "eudaimonistic judgment," recognizing that some object is significant for one's own flourishing. Fear, for instance, is not merely a physiological response to danger but embodies the judgment that "something threatens what I cherish."

**命题二：情感具有意向性。** 情感总是关于某事物的——它们有对象（object），而这些对象通常是外在于主体的。爱总是爱某人或某物，恐惧总是恐惧某事。这种意向性结构使情感成为主体与世界之间关系的反映。

**Proposition 2: Emotions have intentionality.** Emotions are always about something — they have objects, which are typically external to the subject. Love is always love of someone or something, fear is always fear of something. This intentional structure makes emotions reflections of the relationship between subject and world.

**命题三：情感揭示了我们对他者的依赖性。** 最强烈的情感——爱、悲、渴望——恰恰揭示了人类繁荣的脆弱性。当我们深爱某人时，我们就将自身的一部分幸福交托给了不可控的外在世界。这种依赖性不是道德上的缺陷，而是人类处境的基本特征。

**Proposition 3: Emotions reveal our dependence on others.** The most intense emotions — love, grief, longing — precisely reveal the fragility of human flourishing. When we deeply love someone, we entrust part of our well-being to the uncontrollable external world. This dependence is not a moral defect but a fundamental feature of the human condition.

纳斯鲍姆特别以"悲恸"（grief）为例论证其理论。当我们为亲人的死亡而悲恸时，这种情感不是非理性的——它包含了一个深刻的判断："这个人的生命对我而言是极其重要的，而现在这种重要关系永远失去了。"悲恸因此成为一种独特的认知形式，它揭示了我们与他人之间关系的深度和重要性。

Nussbaum particularly uses "grief" to illustrate her theory. When we grieve a loved one's death, this emotion is not irrational — it contains a profound judgment: "This person's life was immensely important to me, and now that important relationship is forever lost." Grief thus becomes a distinctive form of cognition, revealing the depth and significance of our relationships with others.

## 思想史脉络 / Intellectual History

纳斯鲍姆的情感理论处于多条思想线索的交汇点。在古代哲学中，她继承了亚里士多德关于情感包含认知成分的观点（《修辞学》第二卷），同时批评了斯多亚学派试图通过消除情感来消除脆弱性的"治疗"方案。在近代哲学中，她与亚当·斯密的"同情"理论形成对话，但超越了斯密将同情视为单一机制的简化倾向。在当代哲学中，她回应了罗伯特·所罗门（Robert Solomon）的情感认知理论，但比所罗门更强调情感的升华性（eudaimonistic）维度——即情感总是与主体对自身繁荣的关切相关联。

Nussbaum's emotion theory sits at the intersection of multiple intellectual lineages. In ancient philosophy, she inherits Aristotle's view that emotions contain cognitive components (Rhetoric, Book II) while critiquing the Stoics' attempt to eliminate emotions as a "therapy" for vulnerability. In modern philosophy, she dialogues with Adam Smith's "sympathy" theory while transcending Smith's tendency to reduce sympathy to a single mechanism. In contemporary philosophy, she responds to Robert Solomon's cognitive theory of emotions while emphasizing more than Solomon the eudaimonistic dimension — that emotions are always linked to the subject's concern for their own flourishing.

本书的写作也与20世纪末"情感转向"（affective turn）的大背景密切相关。在分析哲学传统中，情感长期被视为非理性的、需要被理性控制的"低等"心理状态。纳斯鲍姆通过系统论证情感的认知性和评价性，为情感在哲学中的地位带来了根本性的改变。

The book's composition was closely tied to the late-20th-century "affective turn." In the analytic philosophy tradition, emotions were long viewed as irrational, "lower" mental states requiring rational control. Through systematic argument for the cognitive and evaluative nature of emotions, Nussbaum fundamentally altered emotions' status in philosophy.

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

纳斯鲍姆的情感理论对多个当代领域产生了影响。在道德教育中，它支持将情感培养纳入课程——不是简单地要求学生"控制情绪"，而是帮助他们发展对重要事物的适当情感反应。在心理治疗领域，认知行为疗法（CBT）的核心前提——情绪障碍涉及认知扭曲——与纳斯鲍姆的情感认知理论高度一致。在法律理论中，她的理论为"情感证据"（emotional evidence）的法律地位提供了哲学基础——受害者的恐惧和悲恸本身承载着关于伤害严重性的认知信息。在AI伦理中，关于机器是否能"真正"拥有情感的讨论，可以从纳斯鲍姆的情感认知理论中获得启发——如果情感的核心是评价性判断，那么关键问题不是机器能否模拟生理反应，而是机器能否做出真正的"升华性判断"。

Nussbaum's emotion theory has influenced multiple contemporary fields. In moral education, it supports integrating emotion cultivation into curricula — not simply requiring students to "control emotions" but helping them develop appropriate emotional responses to important matters. In psychotherapy, the core premise of cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) — that emotional disorders involve cognitive distortions — aligns closely with Nussbaum's cognitive emotion theory. In legal theory, her work provides philosophical foundations for the legal status of "emotional evidence" — victims' fear and grief carry cognitive information about the severity of harm. In AI ethics, discussions about whether machines can "truly" have emotions can be illuminated by Nussbaum's cognitive theory — if the core of emotion is evaluative judgment, the key question is not whether machines can simulate physiological responses but whether they can make genuine "eudaimonistic judgments."

## 批评与回应 / Critiques and Responses

**批评一：** 情感的认知理论是否过度理性化了情感？某些情感（如恐惧症中的恐惧）似乎不包含明确的判断。 / Does the cognitive theory over-rationalize emotions? Some emotions (like phobic fear) seem not to contain explicit judgments.
**回应：** 纳斯鲍姆承认情感判断可以是前语言的（pre-linguistic）和非命题性的（non-propositional），动物和婴儿也可以做出这类判断。她区分了情感的"核心"认知结构和其表面的非理性表现。 / Nussbaum acknowledges emotional judgments can be pre-linguistic and non-propositional; animals and infants can make such judgments. She distinguishes between the "core" cognitive structure of emotions and their surface irrational manifestations.

**批评二：** 将情感还原为判断是否忽视了其身体维度？ / Does reducing emotions to judgments ignore their bodily dimension?
**回应：** 纳斯鲍姆不否认情感的身体表现，但她主张身体反应本身是被认知结构所组织的——心跳加速之所以是恐惧的一部分，是因为它服务于主体对威胁的认知性评估。 / Nussbaum does not deny emotions' bodily manifestations but argues bodily responses are themselves organized by cognitive structure — accelerated heartbeat is part of fear because it serves the subject's cognitive appraisal of threat.

## 跨文化回响 / Cross-cultural Echoes

纳斯鲍姆的情感认知理论与中国古典哲学中的"情"概念形成了深层对话。荀子在《性恶》篇中论证"情"（情感/欲望）是"性"（人性）的表现，需要通过"礼"来调节和教育——这与纳斯鲍姆关于情感需要培养的论题高度呼应。更重要的是，儒家"情理"传统的核心洞见——情感与理性不是对立的，而是相互渗透的——与纳斯鲍姆的情感认知理论异曲同工。王阳明的"致良知"学说将道德情感（如恻隐之心）视为包含认知判断的道德直觉，这与纳斯鲍姆将情感理解为评价性判断的观点形成了跨越时空的对话。

Nussbaum's cognitive emotion theory forms a deep dialogue with the Chinese classical concept of "qing" (emotion/feeling). Xunzi argues in the "Xing'e" chapter that "qing" (emotions/desires) are manifestations of "xing" (human nature) requiring regulation and education through "li" (ritual/propriety) — highly resonant with Nussbaum's thesis on emotion cultivation. More significantly, the Confucian "qingli" tradition's core insight — that emotion and reason are not opposed but interpenetrating — parallels Nussbaum's cognitive emotion theory. Wang Yangming's doctrine of "extending innate moral knowledge" treats moral emotions (like compassion) as moral intuitions containing cognitive judgments, forming a cross-temporal dialogue with Nussbaum's understanding of emotions as evaluative judgments.
