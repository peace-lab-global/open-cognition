# 政治情感 · Political Emotions

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

"政治情感"（Political Emotions）是纳斯鲍姆在2013年同名著作中系统阐述的核心概念。其基本论题是：一个正义的自由主义社会不仅需要正确的制度设计，还需要培育能够支撑这些制度的公共情感。民主制度如果缺乏公民之间的情感纽带——爱、同情、相互尊重——将无法长期存续。这一论题是对罗尔斯式政治自由主义的重要补充和修正。

"Political Emotions" is a core concept Nussbaum systematically articulated in her 2013 book of the same name. Its basic thesis is that a just liberal society requires not only correct institutional design but also the cultivation of public emotions capable of sustaining those institutions. Democratic institutions lacking emotional bonds among citizens — love, compassion, mutual respect — cannot endure long-term. This thesis constitutes a significant supplement and correction to Rawlsian political liberalism.

纳斯鲍姆的核心关切是"自由主义的稳定性问题"：一个建立在多元主义和中立性原则之上的社会，如何确保公民有足够的动机去维护正义制度？罗尔斯诉诸"重叠共识"（overlapping consensus）来回答这个问题，但纳斯鲍姆认为纯粹理性的共识是不够的——人们不会为他们不爱的制度而牺牲。

Nussbaum's central concern is the "stability problem of liberalism": how can a society built on pluralism and neutrality principles ensure citizens have sufficient motivation to maintain just institutions? Rawls appeals to "overlapping consensus" to answer this question, but Nussbaum argues purely rational consensus is insufficient — people will not sacrifice for institutions they do not love.

## 理论内涵 / Theoretical Content

纳斯鲍姆的政治情感理论包含三个核心层次：

Nussbaum's political emotions theory encompasses three core levels:

**第一层：公共情感的类型学。** 纳斯鲍姆区分了三种对民主至关重要的公共情感：（a）"同情性想象"（compassionate imagination）——能够站在他者的立场理解其处境；（b）"爱国主义"（patriotism）——对国家共同体的归属感，但这种爱国主义必须是"世界主义的爱国主义"（cosmopolitan patriotism），即对国家理想的热爱而非对族群身份的排他性认同；（c）"批判性情感"（critical emotions）——对社会不正义的愤怒和羞耻，推动公民参与改革。

**Level 1: Typology of public emotions.** Nussbaum distinguishes three types of public emotions crucial to democracy: (a) "compassionate imagination" — the ability to understand others' situations from their standpoint; (b) "patriotism" — a sense of belonging to the national community, but this must be "cosmopolitan patriotism," love of national ideals rather than exclusive identification with ethnic identity; (c) "critical emotions" — anger and shame at social injustice, driving citizen participation in reform.

**第二层：情感培育的制度化路径。** 纳斯鲍姆提出了多种制度化的情感培育机制：公共纪念物和国家节日（如马丁·路德·金纪念日）能够塑造集体记忆和公民情感；公立教育中的文学和人文学科教育培养同情性想象力；公共建筑和艺术空间的设计能够促进公民之间的相遇和交流；国家领导人的修辞实践（如林肯的葛底斯堡演说、尼赫鲁的就职演说）能够激发公共情感。

**Level 2: Institutionalized paths of emotion cultivation.** Nussbaum proposes multiple institutionalized emotion-cultivation mechanisms: public monuments and national holidays (like Martin Luther King Day) shape collective memory and civic emotions; literature and humanities education in public schools cultivates compassionate imagination; public architecture and art spaces promote encounters and exchange among citizens; national leaders' rhetorical practices (like Lincoln's Gettysburg Address, Nehru's inaugural speech) can inspire public emotions.

**第三层：反情感的批判。** 纳斯鲍姆同时批评了那些损害民主的"反情感"（anti-emotions）：厌恶（disgust）和羞耻（shame）如果被政治利用，可能导致对边缘群体的排斥和迫害。她以纳粹德国和当代印度的种姓歧视为例，论证了"厌恶政治"（politics of disgust）的危害。《躲避人性》（Hiding from Humanity, 2004）中的这一主题在《政治情感》中得到了更系统的阐述。

**Level 3: Critique of anti-emotions.** Nussbaum simultaneously critiques "anti-emotions" that damage democracy: disgust and shame, when politically exploited, can lead to exclusion and persecution of marginalized groups. Using Nazi Germany and contemporary Indian caste discrimination as examples, she demonstrates the dangers of the "politics of disgust." This theme from Hiding from Humanity (2004) receives more systematic treatment in Political Emotions.

## 思想史脉络 / Intellectual History

政治情感的思想史根源可追溯到卢梭的"公民宗教"（civil religion）概念——卢梭认为共和国需要一种能够凝聚公民忠诚的公共信仰。纳斯鲍姆拒绝了卢梭方案中的强制性和排他性维度，但继承了其核心洞见：政治制度不能仅仅依靠利益计算来维持。她也从杜威（John Dewey）的教育哲学中汲取资源——杜威论证了民主不仅需要制度，更需要"民主的习惯"（democratic habits），而教育正是培养这些习惯的关键场域。

The intellectual history of political emotions traces back to Rousseau's "civil religion" concept — Rousseau argued republics need a public faith capable of凝聚ing civic loyalty. Nussbaum rejects the coercive and exclusive dimensions of Rousseau's proposal but inherits his core insight: political institutions cannot be sustained by interest calculations alone. She also draws on John Dewey's educational philosophy — Dewey argued democracy requires not only institutions but "democratic habits," with education as the key site for cultivating these habits.

在当代政治哲学中，纳斯鲍姆的工作与尤尔根·哈贝马斯（Jürgen Habermas）的"宪法爱国主义"（constitutional patriotism）形成了互补关系。两者都主张民主的忠诚应指向宪法原则而非族群身份，但纳斯鲍姆比哈贝马斯更重视情感维度——她认为即使是宪法原则也需要被"爱上"（loved），而不仅仅是被"同意"（agreed to）。

In contemporary political philosophy, Nussbaum's work forms a complementary relationship with Jürgen Habermas's "constitutional patriotism." Both argue democratic loyalty should be directed toward constitutional principles rather than ethnic identity, but Nussbaum places greater emphasis on the emotional dimension — she argues even constitutional principles need to be "loved," not merely "agreed to."

## 关键文本 / Key Passages

> "A decent society is not a machine, and its principles cannot operate without emotional support. What holds a decent society together is not just abstract principle but love of the country and its ideals."
> — Nussbaum, *Political Emotions* (2013), p. 3
>
> "一个体面的社会不是一台机器，其原则不可能在缺乏情感支持的情况下运作。将一个体面的社会维系在一起的，不仅仅是抽象的原则，更是对国家及其理想的爱。"

> "All decent societies need to be concerned with the stability of their just institutions, and with the production of a culture that will sustain them over time."
> — Nussbaum, *Political Emotions* (2013), p. 15
>
> "所有体面的社会都需要关注其正义制度的稳定性，关注生产一种能够长期支撑这些制度的文化。"

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

政治情感理论对当代民主危机具有直接的解释力。面对全球范围内的民粹主义浪潮和民主倒退（democratic backsliding），纳斯鲍姆的分析框架帮助理解：当自由主义制度缺乏情感基础时，它们如何变得脆弱；当民粹主义领袖成功地将恐惧和厌恶政治化时，民主如何受到威胁。该理论也为多元文化社会中的公民教育提供了指导——如何培养既尊重差异又维护共同制度的"情感公民"（emotional citizens）。在数字时代，社交媒体的"回音室效应"对同情性想象力构成了新挑战，纳斯鲍姆的理论为数字公民教育提供了哲学基础。

Political emotions theory has direct explanatory power for contemporary democratic crises. Facing global populist waves and democratic backsliding, Nussbaum's analytical framework helps understand how liberal institutions become fragile when lacking emotional foundations, and how democracy is threatened when populist leaders successfully politicize fear and disgust. The theory also guides civic education in multicultural societies — how to cultivate "emotional citizens" who respect difference while maintaining common institutions. In the digital age, social media's "echo chamber effect" poses new challenges to compassionate imagination, and Nussbaum's theory provides philosophical foundations for digital civic education.

## 批评与回应 / Critiques and Responses

**批评一：** 国家主导的情感培育是否接近宣传（propaganda）？ / Does state-directed emotion cultivation approach propaganda?
**回应：** 纳斯鲍姆明确区分了自由主义的情感培育和威权主义的宣传。前者尊重公民的批判性自主权，鼓励对制度的反思性忠诚；后者则要求无条件服从。关键区别在于是否允许和欢迎批评。 / Nussbaum explicitly distinguishes liberal emotion cultivation from authoritarian propaganda. The former respects citizens' critical autonomy, encouraging reflective loyalty; the latter demands unconditional obedience. The key difference lies in whether criticism is permitted and welcomed.

**批评二：** 纳斯鲍姆是否过于乐观地看待了国家塑造情感的能力？ / Is Nussbaum overly optimistic about the state's ability to shape emotions?
**回应：** 纳斯鲍姆承认国家能力有限，但她指出即使在最自由的社会中，国家已经在通过教育、纪念活动和公共建筑塑造情感——问题是做得好还是做得坏。 / Nussbaum acknowledges state capacity is limited, but notes that even in the freest societies, states already shape emotions through education, commemoration, and public architecture — the question is whether this is done well or poorly.

## 跨文化回响 / Cross-cultural Echoes

政治情感理论与中国传统中的"礼乐"政治哲学形成了深层对话。《礼记·乐记》认为"乐"（音乐和艺术）具有"和民声"（harmonizing people's emotions）的政治功能，而"礼"（仪式和制度）则"节民心"（regulating people's hearts）。这一"礼乐并治"的理念与纳斯鲍姆关于公共文化和制度设计共同培育政治情感的构想高度一致。两者都认为政治秩序不仅依赖法律和强制，更需要通过文化实践来培养公民的内在情感认同。此外，孟子的"四端"学说——恻隐之心、羞恶之心、辞让之心、是非之心——可以被理解为一种关于"政治情感"基础的理论，它认为正义的政治秩序需要以这些基本道德情感为根基。

Political emotions theory forms a deep dialogue with the Chinese traditional "liyue" (ritual and music) political philosophy. The "Record of Music" chapter in the Book of Rites argues that "yue" (music and arts) has the political function of "harmonizing people's emotions," while "li" (ritual and institutions) "regulates people's hearts." This "liyue joint governance" ideal closely parallels Nussbaum's vision of public culture and institutional design jointly cultivating political emotions. Both hold that political order relies not only on law and coercion but also on cultivating citizens' inner emotional identification through cultural practices. Moreover, Mencius's doctrine of the "Four Beginnings" — compassion, shame, deference, and sense of right and wrong — can be understood as a theory about the foundations of "political emotions," holding that just political order needs to be rooted in these basic moral emotions.
