# 内在善与外在善 · Internal Goods and External Goods

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

"内在善"与"外在善"的区分是麦金泰尔美德伦理学中最具实践影响力的概念工具之一。内在善（internal goods）是指只有通过参与某种实践本身才能获得的卓越品质——例如下棋中战略思维的精妙、科学研究中对真理的洞见、医学实践中对患者需求的精准回应。这些善是"内在的"，因为它们不能通过实践之外的途径获得：你不能通过购买来获得一个优秀棋手的战略眼光。外在善（external goods）则是指金钱、权力、名誉和地位——它们可以通过多种途径获得，与实践本身的卓越性没有必然联系。麦金泰尔认为，美德实践的本质在于追求内在善，而当外在善压制了内在善时，实践就会被腐蚀。

The distinction between "internal goods" and "external goods" is one of the most practically influential conceptual tools in MacIntyre's virtue ethics. Internal goods are excellences attainable only through participation in a practice itself — for example, the subtlety of strategic thinking in chess, insight into truth in scientific research, or precise responsiveness to patient needs in medical practice. These goods are "internal" because they cannot be acquired through means external to the practice: you cannot buy a good chess player's strategic vision. External goods are money, power, fame, and status — achievable through many avenues and bearing no necessary connection to excellence within the practice. MacIntyre argues that the essence of virtuous practice lies in pursuing internal goods, and when external goods override internal goods, the practice becomes corrupted.

## 理论内涵 / Theoretical Content

麦金泰尔对内在善与外在善的区分有三个层次的含义。第一层是获得途径的区分：内在善只能通过参与实践本身——通过经历学徒期、接受实践标准的约束、追求卓越的持续努力——才能获得。一个初学者不能通过捷径获得一个大师的洞见；一个医学生不能通过死记硬背获得临床判断力。外在善则不同：一个人可以通过继承、投机或欺骗获得财富，而不需要任何特定的实践卓越。

MacIntyre's distinction between internal and external goods operates on three levels. The first concerns the mode of acquisition: internal goods can only be obtained through participation in the practice itself — through apprenticeship, submission to the standards of the practice, and sustained effort toward excellence. A beginner cannot shortcut their way to a master's insight; a medical student cannot acquire clinical judgment through rote memorization. External goods are different: one can acquire wealth through inheritance, speculation, or deception without any particular practical excellence.

第二层是可共享性的区分：内在善的获得往往扩展了整个实践共同体的视野和能力——当一个科学家做出突破性发现时，整个科学共同体都受益。外在善则本质上是竞争性的——一个人获得的财富和权力是以他人的损失为代价的。这一区分对理解市场经济对实践的影响至关重要：当实践被纳入以竞争外在善为目标的制度框架时，实践本身的合作性质就会受到侵蚀。

The second level concerns shareability: the achievement of internal goods typically expands the horizons and capacities of the entire practice community — when a scientist makes a breakthrough discovery, the whole scientific community benefits. External goods are essentially competitive — one person's acquisition of wealth and power comes at the expense of others. This distinction is crucial for understanding the impact of market economies on practices: when practices are incorporated into institutional frameworks aimed at competing for external goods, the cooperative nature of the practice itself is eroded.

第三层是与美德的关系：美德——作为使实践成为可能的品质——本质上指向内在善的追求。一个真正有美德的棋手追求的是下棋的卓越，而非比赛奖金；一个有美德的医生追求的是医术的精湛，而非诊费收入。当然，麦金泰尔并不否认外在善的必要性——实践需要制度支持，从业者需要物质保障——但他坚持认为，当外在善从服务于内在善的手段变为目的本身时，实践就会堕落。

The third level concerns the relationship to virtue: virtues — as the qualities that make practices possible — are essentially oriented toward the pursuit of internal goods. A truly virtuous chess player pursues excellence in chess, not tournament prizes; a virtuous physician pursues mastery of medical art, not fee income. MacIntyre does not deny the necessity of external goods — practices need institutional support, practitioners need material security — but he insists that when external goods shift from means serving internal goods to ends in themselves, the practice degenerates.

## 思想史脉络 / Intellectual History

内在善与外在善的区分有其亚里士多德的根源。亚里士多德区分了"实践智慧"（phronesis）与"技术知识"（techne）：前者指向行动本身的善，后者指向行动之外的产品。托马斯·阿奎那进一步发展了这一区分，强调"行动本身"（actus humanus）与"外部行为"（actus externus）的道德差异。在现代哲学中，汉娜·阿伦特（Hannah Arendt）的"行动"（action）与"劳动"（labor）的区分也影响了麦金泰尔的思考。然而，麦金泰尔的独特贡献在于将这一区分系统地应用于分析当代制度——企业、医院、大学——中的结构性腐败。

The distinction between internal and external goods has Aristotelian roots. Aristotle distinguished *phronesis* (practical wisdom) from *techne* (technical knowledge): the former aims at the good of the action itself, the latter at a product external to the action. Thomas Aquinas further developed this distinction, emphasizing the moral difference between *actus humanus* (human act) and *actus externus* (external act). In modern philosophy, Hannah Arendt's distinction between "action" and "labor" also influenced MacIntyre's thinking. However, MacIntyre's distinctive contribution lies in systematically applying this distinction to analyze structural corruption in contemporary institutions — corporations, hospitals, universities.

## 关键文本 / Key Passages

> "I call those goods external which, when we achieve them, are always some individual's property and possession... the more someone has of them the less there is for other people. This is sometimes necessarily so, as with power and fame, sometimes contingently so, as with money. External goods are therefore characteristically objects of competition in which victory goes to some, defeat to others." (*After Virtue*, Ch. 14)

麦金泰尔在此精确定义了外在善的竞争性质：它们是零和博弈的对象，一个人的获得意味着他人的失去。MacIntyre here precisely defines the competitive nature of external goods: they are objects of zero-sum competition, where one person's gain means another's loss.

> "Internal goods are indeed the outcome of competition to excel, but it is characteristic of them that their achievement is a good for the whole community who participate in the practice." (*After Virtue*, Ch. 14)

内在善的获得虽然也涉及竞争（追求卓越），但其结果惠及整个实践共同体——这与外在善的零和性质形成鲜明对比。The achievement of internal goods also involves competition (to excel), but its outcome benefits the entire practice community — in sharp contrast to the zero-sum nature of external goods.

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

内在善与外在善的区分在当代商业伦理学中被广泛应用。穆尔（Geoff Moore）等学者论证了企业可以同时追求内在善（产品卓越、客户价值）和外在善（利润），但当前者被后者压制时，企业就会丧失其核心实践的完整性。在学术领域，这一区分被用于批判学术商品化——当大学排名和研究经费压倒了追求真理的内在善时，学术实践就会退化。在医疗领域，它为分析医疗市场化对医患关系的侵蚀提供了框架。在体育领域，它被用于讨论竞技体育中的兴奋剂问题和商业赞助对运动精神的腐蚀。

The internal/external goods distinction has been widely applied in contemporary business ethics. Scholars like Geoff Moore have argued that corporations can pursue both internal goods (product excellence, customer value) and external goods (profit), but when the latter overrides the former, the corporation loses the integrity of its core practice. In academia, this distinction has been used to critique academic commodification — when university rankings and research funding override the internal goods of truth-seeking, academic practice degenerates. In healthcare, it provides a framework for analyzing how marketization erodes the doctor-patient relationship. In sports, it has been used to discuss doping and how commercial sponsorship corrupts the spirit of athletic competition.

## 批评与回应 / Critiques and Responses

**批评 / Critique**: 一些批评者认为内在善与外在善的界限并不清晰——在实践中，两者常常交织在一起，难以区分。Some critics argue that the boundary between internal and external goods is unclear — in practice, the two are often intertwined and difficult to separate.
**回应 / Response**: 麦金泰尔承认两者在实践中常常交织，但他坚持认为这种区分在分析上是必要的，而且实践共同体的成员通常能够辨别何时外在善正在压制内在善。MacIntyre acknowledges that the two are often intertwined in practice, but insists the distinction is analytically necessary, and that members of practice communities can typically discern when external goods are overriding internal goods.

**批评 / Critique**: 批评者指出，麦金泰尔低估了外在善在维持实践中的积极作用——没有足够的经济回报，许多人将无法继续从事实践。Critics argue that MacIntyre underestimates the positive role of external goods in sustaining practices — without adequate economic returns, many people cannot continue their practices.
**回应 / Response**: 麦金泰尔在《依赖性的理性动物》中承认了外在善的必要性，但坚持认为它们应当服务于而非取代内在善。MacIntyre acknowledged the necessity of external goods in *Dependent Rational Animals*, but maintained they should serve rather than supplant internal goods.

## 跨文化回响 / Cross-cultural Echoes

内在善与外在善的区分与儒家的"义利之辨"（distinction between righteousness and profit）有着深层的对应。孔子说"君子喻于义，小人喻于利"——君子追求的是道义（类似于内在善），小人追求的是利益（类似于外在善）。孟子进一步论证了"舍生取义"的必要性——当生命（外在善）与道义（内在善）不可兼得时，应当选择道义。这种义利之辨构成了儒家伦理学的核心议题之一，与麦金泰尔的内在善/外在善区分形成了跨文化的哲学对话。

The internal/external goods distinction finds a deep counterpart in the Confucian distinction between *yi* (righteousness) and *li* (profit). Confucius said, "The gentleman understands righteousness; the petty person understands profit" — the gentleman pursues the Way (analogous to internal goods), the petty person pursues gain (analogous to external goods). Mencius further argued for the necessity of "letting go of life to choose righteousness" — when life (an external good) and righteousness (an internal good) cannot both be had, one must choose righteousness. This *yi-li* distinction constitutes one of the core issues of Confucian ethics and forms a cross-cultural philosophical dialogue with MacIntyre's internal/external goods framework.
