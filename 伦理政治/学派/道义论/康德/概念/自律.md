# 自律 · Autonomy

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

自律（Autonomie）是康德道德哲学的核心概念之一，指理性存在者自我立法、自我约束的能力。在康德看来，真正的自由不是"消极自由"（摆脱外在约束），而是"积极自由"——即意志按照自己为自己制定的普遍法则来行动的能力。自律概念是康德对人类自由之谜的解答：如果人仅仅受自然因果律支配，就没有自由可言；但作为理性存在者，人能够超越感性欲望的决定，按照理性的法则自主行动——这种自我立法的能力就是人的尊严的最终根据。

Autonomy (Autonomie) is one of the core concepts of Kantian moral philosophy, referring to the capacity of rational beings to self-legislate and self-govern. For Kant, true freedom is not "negative freedom" (freedom from external constraints) but "positive freedom"—the ability of the will to act according to laws it gives itself. The concept of autonomy is Kant's answer to the puzzle of human freedom: if humans are governed solely by natural causality, there can be no freedom; but as rational beings, humans can transcend the determination of sensible desires and act autonomously according to rational law—this capacity for self-legislation is the ultimate ground of human dignity.

康德将自律与他律（Heteronomie）对立起来。他律意味着意志被某种外在的因素——无论是感性欲望、社会习俗、宗教权威还是功利计算——所决定。只有自律的意志才是真正自由的意志，因为它不依赖于任何外在于理性本身的条件。

Kant opposes autonomy to heteronomy (Heteronomie). Heteronomy means the will is determined by some external factor—whether sensible desires, social customs, religious authority, or utilitarian calculation. Only an autonomous will is truly free, because it depends on no condition external to reason itself.

## 理论内涵 / Theoretical Content

### 自律与道德法则的关系 / Autonomy and the Moral Law

康德在《道德形而上学奠基》第三章中论证，自律的意志必然是服从道德法则的意志。论证的关键在于：一个自律的意志所服从的法则必须是自己制定的；但一个理性存在者制定的法则不能是任意的（否则就不是法则），而必须是能够被一致地普遍化的——这正是定言命令的内容。因此，自律与道德法则互为条件：自由意志就是服从道德法则的意志，道德法则就是自由意志的自我立法。

In Groundwork Chapter III, Kant argues that an autonomous will necessarily submits to the moral law. The crux of the argument is: a law that an autonomous will obeys must be self-given; but a law given by a rational being cannot be arbitrary (otherwise it would not be a law), and must be consistently universalizable—which is precisely the content of the categorical imperative. Thus autonomy and the moral law are mutually conditioning: a free will is a will that obeys the moral law, and the moral law is a free will's self-legislation.

### 积极自由与消极自由 / Positive and Negative Freedom

康德区分了意志的两种自由：消极自由是意志独立于感性冲动的因果性（即不被欲望所决定），积极自由是纯粹理性自身成为实践的能力（即理性自我立法）。消极自由是自律的前提条件，但仅有消极自由是不够的——一个不受欲望驱使但也不服从理性法则的存在者（如果这样的存在者可以设想的话）既不是自由的，也不是不自由的。真正的自由在于积极地按照理性的法则行动。

Kant distinguishes two kinds of freedom of the will: negative freedom is the will's independence from the causality of sensible impulses (not being determined by desires), while positive freedom is pure reason's capacity to be practical in itself (rational self-legislation). Negative freedom is a precondition of autonomy, but negative freedom alone is insufficient—a being not driven by desires yet also not subject to rational law would be neither free nor unfree. True freedom consists in positively acting according to rational law.

### 尊严与自律 / Dignity and Autonomy

康德在人性公式的论证中指出，人的尊严（Würde）最终根源于自律能力。事物具有价格（Preis），可以被替换；而理性存在者具有尊严，不可替代。这种不可替代性之所以成立，是因为理性存在者作为自律的道德主体，是价值（包括价格体系）的赋予者，而非价值的承受者。用当代康德主义者科尔斯戈德的话说，理性存在者是"价值的条件"而非"有条件价值的东西"。

Kant argues in the Humanity Formula that human dignity (Würde) is ultimately rooted in the capacity for autonomy. Things have a price (Preis) and can be replaced; rational beings have dignity and are irreplaceable. This irreplaceability holds because rational beings, as autonomous moral agents, are the conferrers of value (including price systems), not merely the bearers of value. In Korsgaard's terms, rational beings are "the condition of value" rather than "things with conditioned value."

## 思想史脉络 / Intellectual History

自律概念的思想史源头可以追溯到卢梭。卢梭在《社会契约论》中提出，真正的自由在于服从自己参与制定的法律——"服从自己为自己制定的法律才是自由"。康德将这一政治领域的洞见转化为道德哲学的基本原则：道德自律就是意志服从自己为自己制定的理性法则。

The intellectual-historical origin of the concept of autonomy can be traced to Rousseau. In The Social Contract, Rousseau argued that true freedom consists in obeying laws one participates in making—"obedience to a law one prescribes to oneself is freedom." Kant transformed this political insight into a fundamental principle of moral philosophy: moral autonomy is the will's obedience to the rational law it gives itself.

在后世的发展中，自律概念经历了多重转化。费希特将自律推至极致，认为自我设定自身的存在；黑格尔则在"伦理生活"（Sittlichkeit）中重新安置了自律，认为抽象的道德自律必须在具体的社会制度中实现。二十世纪，罗尔斯将自律概念程序化，通过"原初状态"的设计使自律原则成为可操作的正义程序。

In subsequent developments, the concept of autonomy underwent multiple transformations. Fichte pushed autonomy to its extreme, arguing that the self posits its own existence; Hegel relocated autonomy within "ethical life" (Sittlichkeit), arguing that abstract moral autonomy must be realized in concrete social institutions. In the twentieth century, Rawls proceduralized the concept of autonomy, making the principle of autonomy an operable procedure of justice through the design of the "original position."

## 关键文本 / Key Passages

> —《实践理性批判》

> "Autonomy of the will, i.e., the will's property of being a law to itself, is the sole principle of all moral laws."
> —*Critique of Practical Reason*

> "意志不仅仅服从法则，而是以这样一种方式服从法则，即它必须也被视为自我立法的，并且正因为如此才服从法则。"
> —《道德形而上学奠基》，第二章

> "The will is not merely subject to the law, but subject in such a way that it must also be viewed as self-legislating, and only for this reason subject to the law."
> —*Groundwork*, Chapter II

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

自律概念在当代有着广泛的实践应用。在医学伦理中，"患者自主权"原则直接源于康德的自律概念——患者有权在充分了解信息的基础上自主做出医疗决定，医生不得代替患者做决定。在教育哲学中，自律理念支持"批判性思维"教育的目标——培养能够独立思考和判断的主体，而非被动接受权威的学生。在政治哲学中，自律原则要求政治制度的设计必须尊重公民的自主决定权，这是民主制度的道德基础。

The concept of autonomy has wide practical applications today. In medical ethics, the principle of "patient autonomy" derives directly from the Kantian concept of autonomy—patients have the right to make autonomous medical decisions based on adequate information, and physicians must not decide for patients. In educational philosophy, the idea of autonomy supports the goal of "critical thinking" education—cultivating subjects capable of independent thinking and judgment, not students who passively accept authority. In political philosophy, the principle of autonomy requires that political institutions respect citizens' autonomous decision-making, which is the moral foundation of democracy.

## 批评与回应 / Critiques and Responses

**社群主义批评 / Communitarian Critique**: 桑德尔和麦金太尔等社群主义者批评康德的自律概念过于抽象，预设了一个脱离社会关系的"无牵无挂的自我"（unencumbered self）。回应：当代康德主义者如科尔斯戈德指出，康德并不否认人嵌入在社会关系中，自律所要求的是人在反思中能够对自己的社会角色进行批判性审视。

Communitarians like Sandel and MacIntyre criticize Kant's concept of autonomy as too abstract, presupposing an "unencumbered self" detached from social relations. Response: Contemporary Kantians like Korsgaard argue that Kant does not deny that persons are embedded in social relationships; what autonomy requires is that persons be able to critically examine their social roles through reflection.

**女性主义批评 / Feminist Critique**: 一些女性主义哲学家认为康德的自律概念过度强调独立性和自我充足性，忽视了关系性和依赖性在人类生活中的重要性。回应：新康德主义女性主义者主张，真正的自律应当包含对自身依赖关系的自觉承认和理性管理，而非假装独立。

Some feminist philosophers argue that Kant's concept of autonomy overemphasizes independence and self-sufficiency, neglecting the importance of relationality and dependency in human life. Response: Neo-Kantian feminists argue that genuine autonomy should include conscious acknowledgment and rational management of one's dependencies, rather than pretending independence.

## 跨文化回响 / Cross-cultural Echoes

在佛教哲学中，"自觉"（自我觉醒）的概念与康德的自律有深层的共鸣。佛教强调从外在的执着和欲望中解脱（涅槃），依自性（佛性）而行——这与康德从感性他律中解放、依理性自律而行的思想结构类似。然而差异也很明显：佛教的自觉最终指向超越主客对立的觉悟境界，而康德的自律始终在理性主体与感性世界的二元框架中运作。在儒家传统中，"慎独"的概念——在独处时依然保持道德自觉——体现了与康德自律思想相呼应的自我约束精神。

In Buddhist philosophy, the concept of "self-awakening" resonates deeply with Kantian autonomy. Buddhism emphasizes liberation from external attachments and desires (nirvāṇa), acting in accordance with one's true nature (Buddha-nature)—a structural parallel to Kant's liberation from sensible heteronomy and action according to rational autonomy. However, differences are equally clear: Buddhist self-awakening ultimately points to an enlightened state transcending subject-object opposition, while Kantian autonomy always operates within the dualistic framework of rational subject and sensible world. In the Confucian tradition, the concept of "shen du" (慎独, watchfulness over oneself in solitude)—maintaining moral self-awareness even in solitude—embodies a spirit of self-discipline echoing Kantian autonomy.
