# 塔特 · Charles Tart

> "我们都身处共识恍惚之中，而我们甚至不自知。"
> "We are all in a consensus trance, and we don't even know it."

## 基本信息 / Basic Information

**中文：** 查尔斯·塔特（Charles Theodore Tart, 1937–2024），美国心理学家，**变异意识状态（Altered States of Consciousness, ASC）研究**的先驱人物，超个人心理学（Transpersonal Psychology）的核心奠基者之一。他出生于美国新泽西州特伦顿市（Trenton, New Jersey），在杜克大学（Duke University）获得电气工程学士学位后转向心理学，于斯坦福大学（Stanford University）师从催眠研究巨擘欧内斯特·希尔加德（Ernest Hilgard）获得心理学博士学位。他长期担任加利福尼亚大学戴维斯分校（UC Davis）心理学教授，并在斯坦福研究所（Stanford Research Institute, SRI）、加州整合研究学院（CIIS）等机构兼任研究员。

塔特的核心贡献在于：他将变异意识状态——包括冥想、催眠、梦境、药物诱发状态等——从边缘研究领域带入了主流心理学的学术视野，并以严谨的科学方法论为其建立了理论框架。他提出的"共识恍惚"（consensus trance）概念和"状态特异性科学"（state-specific science）方法论，对意识研究、冥想科学、超个人心理学和跨文化心理学产生了深远影响。

**English:** Charles Tart (1937–2024) was an American psychologist and a pioneering figure in the study of **Altered States of Consciousness (ASC)**, as well as one of the foundational architects of Transpersonal Psychology. Born in Trenton, New Jersey, he initially earned a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering from Duke University before pivoting to psychology, completing his PhD at Stanford University under the supervision of Ernest Hilgard, a leading figure in hypnosis 研究. He served as a long-term professor of 心理学 at the University of California, Davis (UC Davis), and held 研究 affiliations with the Stanford Research Institute (SRI) and the California Institute of Integral Studies (CIIS).

Tart's central contribution was bringing altered states of consciousness — including meditation, hypnosis, dreaming, and drug-induced states — from the margins of psychological 研究 into mainstream academic discourse, establishing a rigorous scientific and theoretical framework for their study. His concepts of "consensus trance" and "state-specific science" as methodological innovations have profoundly influenced consciousness studies, contemplative science, transpersonal psychology, and cross-cultural 心理学.

## 核心命题 / Core Propositions

### 1. 意识是离散的多元状态，而非单一连续谱 / Consciousness Consists of Discrete Multiple States, Not a Single Continuum

**中文：** 塔特认为，人类的意识不是单一、连续的"流"，而是由多个**离散的"状态"（discrete states of consciousness, d-SoC）**构成。每种状态——清醒、做梦、深睡、催眠、冥想定境、药物状态——都有其独特的内部结构和组织方式。状态之间的转换不是渐变的，而是在某个临界点上发生质的跃迁。这一理论彻底改变了意识研究的范式：从追问"意识的程度"转向追问"意识的种类"。

**English:** Tart argued that human consciousness is not a single, continuous "stream" but is composed of multiple **discrete states of consciousness (d-SoC)**. Each state — waking, dreaming, deep sleep, hypnosis, meditative absorption, drug-induced states — possesses its own unique internal structure and organizational logic. Transitions between states are not gradual but involve qualitative leaps at critical thresholds. This theory fundamentally shifted the paradigm of consciousness research: from asking "how much consciousness" to asking "what kind of consciousness."

### 2. 日常清醒意识本身就是一种"共识恍惚" / Ordinary Waking Consciousness Is Itself a "Consensus Trance"

**中文：** 塔特最具挑战性的命题之一是：我们通常认为最"正常"、最"真实"的清醒意识，本身就是一种由社会文化条件化塑造的**恍惚状态（trance）**。从出生起，我们就被训练以特定方式感知、思考和回应世界——我们把这种约定俗成的感知模式误认为"唯一的现实"。这与佛教"无明"（avidyā）的概念形成深度共鸣：我们不是清醒地看世界，而是沉睡在集体编织的认知幻象之中。

**English:** One of Tart's most challenging propositions is that our supposedly most "normal" and "real" waking consciousness is itself a **trance state** shaped by sociocultural conditioning. From birth, we are trained to perceive, think, and respond in culturally specific ways — mistaking this conventional perceptual mode for "the only reality." This resonates deeply with the Buddhist concept of "ignorance" (avidyā): we are not awake to the world but asleep within a collectively woven cognitive illusion.

### 3. 某些知识只在特定意识状态中可获得 / Certain Knowledge Is Only Accessible in Specific States of Consciousness

**中文：** "状态特异性"（state-specificity）是塔特方法论的核心。他认为：冥想中获得的"无我"体验，在清醒状态中无法被完整还原；催眠中回忆起的创伤细节，在清醒后可能被遗忘。更激进的是，他提出了**状态特异性科学（State-Specific Science, SSS）**——主张不同意识状态可能需要不同的研究方法论，而非将清醒状态的科学标准强加于所有其他状态之上。

**English:** "State-specificity" is central to Tart's methodology. He argued that the "no-self" experience accessed in meditation cannot be fully reproduced in ordinary waking consciousness; traumatic details recalled during hypnosis may be forgotten upon awakening. More radically, he proposed **State-Specific Science (SSS)** — the idea that different states of consciousness may require different 研究 methodologies, rather than imposing the scientific standards of the waking state upon all other states.

### 4. 超个人现象需要严格的科学方法 / Transpersonal Phenomena Require Rigorous Scientific Methods

**中文：** 塔特始终坚持：对冥想体验、神秘经验、超感知觉等超个人现象的研究，不应因其"非主流"而被排斥，也不应因其"神奇"而被盲目接受。他主张一种**本质主义的科学态度**（essentialist science）——对现象本身保持开放，但对证据标准保持严格。这一立场使他同时受到唯物主义科学家的怀疑（因其研究对象"太玄"）和新纪元运动拥护者的不满（因其方法论"太严"），但他始终拒绝向任何一方妥协。

**English:** Tart consistently maintained that the study of transpersonal phenomena — meditative experiences, mystical states, extrasensory perception — should neither be dismissed for being "non-mainstream" nor uncritically accepted for being "marvelous." He advocated an **essentialist scientific attitude** — remaining open to the phenomena themselves while maintaining rigorous evidential standards. This position left him simultaneously distrusted by materialist scientists (for studying subjects deemed "too fringe") and dissatisfying to New Age enthusiasts (for being "too rigorous" methodologically), yet he refused to compromise with either camp.

### 5. 冥想训练可以培养更精微的科学观察者 / Meditative Training Can Cultivate More Subtle Scientific Observers

**中文：** 塔特认为，科学研究的质量取决于观察者的能力。而传统的科学训练只培养了外在感官的精密度，却忽略了**内在观察者**的训练。通过冥想（特别是佛教的内观传统），科学家可以训练出更稳定、更精微的觉知能力，从而观察到目前被忽略的心理和意识现象。这相当于扩展了科学研究的"仪器"——观察者本身就是最精密的仪器。

**English:** Tart argued that the quality of scientific 研究 depends on the observer's capabilities. Traditional scientific training develops precision in external sensory observation but neglects the training of the **inner observer**. Through meditation (particularly Buddhist insight traditions), scientists can develop more stable and subtle awareness capacities, enabling observation of psychological and consciousness phenomena that are currently overlooked. This is equivalent to upgrading the "instrument" of scientific 研究 — the observer herself is the most precise instrument.

### 6. 从"共识恍惚"中觉醒是人类潜能的核心 / Awakening from the "Consensus Trance" Is Central to Human Potential

**中文：** 塔特的终极关怀不仅在于学术理论，更在于人类的解放潜能。他认为，意识到自己身处共识恍惚之中，是觉醒的第一步。通过冥想、自我探索、心理治疗等途径，人类可以逐步从条件化的知觉模式中解脱出来，实现马斯洛所说的"自我实现"乃至"自我超越"。他将东方智慧传统的修行路径与现代心理学的实证方法进行了系统整合。

**English:** Tart's ultimate concern extended beyond academic theory to human liberation potential. He held that recognizing one's existence within a consensus trance is the first step toward awakening. Through meditation, self-inquiry, and psychotherapy, humans can progressively free themselves from conditioned perceptual modes, achieving what Maslow termed "self-actualization" and even "self-transcendence." He systematically integrated the contemplative paths of Eastern wisdom traditions with the empirical methods of modern 心理学.

## 思想脉络 / Intellectual Trajectory

### 第一阶段：学术训练与催眠研究（1950s–1960s） / Phase 1: Academic Training and Hypnosis Research

**中文：** 塔特的学术之路始于工程学。他在杜克大学学习电气工程期间，对人类意识和技术系统的类比产生了浓厚兴趣。转入心理学后，他在斯坦福大学师从 Ernest Hilgard——20世纪最重要的催眠研究者之一——研究催眠现象。这段训练奠定了他的方法论基础：用严格的实验设计来研究通常被视为"主观"或"不可测量"的意识现象。1963 年，他在斯坦福获得博士学位。在斯坦福研究所期间，他开始研究药物（尤其是大麻和 LSD）诱发的意识状态，积累了大量第一手数据。

**English:** Tart's academic journey began in engineering. While studying electrical engineering at Duke University, he developed a keen interest in analogies between human consciousness and technical systems. After switching to psychology, he studied under Ernest Hilgard — one of the most important hypnosis researchers of the 20th century — at Stanford University. This training laid his methodological foundation: using rigorous experimental designs to study consciousness phenomena typically considered "subjective" or "immeasurable." He received his PhD from Stanford in 1963. During his time at the Stanford Research Institute, he began studying drug-induced states of consciousness (especially cannabis and LSD), accumulating extensive firsthand data.

### 第二阶段：理论建构与学科奠基（1969–1986） / Phase 2: Theoretical Construction and Disciplinary Foundation

**中文：** 1969 年，塔特编辑出版了里程碑式的文集《变异意识状态》（*Altered States of Consciousness*），首次系统整理了来自催眠、冥想、梦境、药物、感觉剥夺等多个领域的 ASC 研究，为该领域建立了统一的学术话语框架。1975 年，他出版了理论巨著《意识状态》（*States of Consciousness*），提出了离散意识状态（d-SoC）的完整理论框架，包括状态的结构分析、状态转换的机制、状态特异性的认识论含义等。这一时期，他参与创立了超个人心理学协会（Association for Transpersonal Psychology），并长期担任《超个人心理学杂志》（*Journal of Transpersonal Psychology*）编委。1986 年，*Waking Up: Overcoming the Obstacles to Human Potential* 出版，整合了东方冥想传统与西方心理学的实践智慧，成为将正念带入主流文化的重要著作。

**English:** In 1969, Tart edited and published the landmark anthology *Altered States of Consciousness*, which for the first time systematically organized ASC 研究 from hypnosis, meditation, dreaming, drugs, sensory deprivation, and other fields, establishing a unified academic discourse framework for the field. In 1975, he published the theoretical magnum opus *States of Consciousness*, proposing a complete theoretical framework for discrete states of consciousness (d-SoC), including structural analysis of states, mechanisms of state transitions, and epistemological implications of state-specificity. During this period, he co-founded the Association for Transpersonal Psychology and served as a long-term editorial board member of the *Journal of Transpersonal Psychology*. In 1986, *Waking Up: Overcoming the Obstacles to Human Potential* was published, integrating Eastern meditative traditions with Western psychological practice wisdom, becoming an important work in bringing mindfulness into mainstream culture.

### 第三阶段：整合、批判与晚年深化（1990s–2024） / Phase 3: Integration, Critique, and Late-Career Deepening

**中文：** 1990 年代以后，塔特的研究兴趣进一步扩展到身心整合、超感知觉（psi phenomena）的科学研究，以及对科学唯物主义的哲学批判。1999 年出版的 *Body Mind Spirit* 提供了身心整合的实践指南。2009 年的《唯物主义的终结》（*The End of Materialism*）是他最具争议性的著作——他在书中论证： psi 现象（如遥视、预知）的实证证据已经足够强，足以挑战唯物主义意识观的基本假设。这一立场使他在主流心理学界面临边缘化压力，但他始终坚持"以证据说话"的科学原则。他晚年担任加州整合研究学院（CIIS）的荣誉教授，持续推动意识研究的跨文化和跨范式对话，直到 2024 年去世。

**English:** From the 1990s onward, Tart's 研究 interests expanded further into mind-body integration, the scientific study of psi phenomena, and philosophical critique of scientific materialism. *Body Mind Spirit* (1999) offered a practical guide to mind-body integration. *The End of Materialism* (2009) was his most controversial work — arguing that empirical evidence for psi phenomena (such as remote viewing and precognition) was strong enough to challenge the fundamental assumptions of the materialist view of consciousness. This position subjected him to marginalization pressures within mainstream psychology, yet he consistently upheld the scientific principle of "following the evidence." In his later years, he served as a professor emeritus at the California Institute of Integral Studies (CIIS), continuing to promote cross-cultural and cross-paradigm dialogue in consciousness 研究 until his death in 2024.

## 重要概念 / Key Concepts

1. **离散意识状态（Discrete States of Consciousness, d-SoC）**：意识在不同状态间发生质的跃迁，而非渐变过渡。每种 d-SoC 有其独特的知觉、认知和情感模式。详见 → [consciousness-states.md](concepts/consciousness-states.md)

2. **变异意识状态（Altered States of Consciousness, ASC）**：与基准清醒意识（baseline waking consciousness）在结构上有质的差异的意识状态。详见 → [altered-states.md](concepts/altered-states.md)

3. **状态特异性科学（State-Specific Science, SSS）**：某些知识和现象只有在特定意识状态中才能被完整观察和验证——不同状态可能需要不同的科学方法论。详见 → [state-specific-science.md](concepts/state-specific-science.md)

4. **共识恍惚（Consensus Trance）**：日常清醒意识本身是由社会文化条件化塑造的"集体催眠"。我们习以为常的感知方式只是众多可能方式之一。

5. **正念与觉知训练（Mindfulness and Awareness Training）**：塔特将佛教正念传统引入西方心理学的科学研究框架，主张冥想训练可以扩展人类的觉知能力。详见 → [正念.md](concepts/正念.md)

6. **超个人心理学（Transpersonal Psychology）**：作为心理学"第四势力"的超个人心理学，将灵性维度纳入科学心理学的研究范围。详见 → [transpersonal.md](concepts/transpersonal.md)

7. **状态特异性记忆（State-Specific Memory）**：某些记忆只在诱发的意识状态中可获取，跨状态时产生"状态依赖性遗忘"（state-dependent forgetting）。

8. **意识状态稳定化（Stabilization of Consciousness States）**：各传统发展出的维持特定意识状态的技术——佛教的止（śamatha）、催眠的暗示、清醒梦的现实检验（reality check）。

9. **观察者的训练（Training of the Observer）**：通过冥想等内观训练，科学家可以成为更精密的观察"仪器"——这是扩展科学研究范畴的方法论路径。

10. **本质主义科学态度（Essentialist Scientific Attitude）**：对非常规现象保持方法论上的开放，同时坚持证据标准上的严格——既不轻信也不武断排斥。

## 思想坐标 / Intellectual Coordinates

### 继承 / Inherited

- **威廉·詹姆斯 William James**：意识流理论与宗教经验研究——詹姆斯的"意识的多元性"直接启发了塔特的离散状态理论。
- **马斯洛 Abraham Maslow**：高峰体验（peak experience）与自我超越（self-transcendence）——为超个人心理学的人本主义基础。
- **希尔加德 Ernest Hilgard**：催眠研究的方法论训练——希尔加德的"新解离理论"（neo-dissociation theory）影响了塔特对意识状态分化的理解。
- **格罗夫 Stanislav Grof**：意识图谱（cartography of consciousness）——LSD 研究中对非普通意识状态的系统描述。

### 批判 / Critiqued

- **还原论的清醒至上主义 Wake-State Chauvinism**：认为只有清醒意识产生的知识才是有效的——塔特称之为"单一状态偏见"（one-state bias）。
- **科学唯物主义 Scientific Materialism**：将意识还原为大脑化学反应的唯物主义立场——塔特认为这忽略了意识的第一人称维度。
- **新纪元的非批判性接受 New Age Uncritical Acceptance**：对灵性体验的盲目信仰——塔特坚持科学标准的不可替代性。

### 影响 / Influenced

- **冥想科学研究 Contemplative Science**：Kabat-Zinn 的 MBSR、Goleman 的情商研究与冥想神经科学。
- **超个人心理学运动 Transpersonal Psychology Movement**：Wilber、Grof、Washburn 等人的理论发展。
- **意识研究 Consciousness Studies**：Baars 的全局工作空间理论、Chalmers 的意识"困难问题"。
- **整合心理学 Integrative Psychology**：身心医学、整合医学的理论基础。

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

1. **冥想神经科学 / Contemplative Neuroscience**：塔特将冥想视为独立意识状态的理论框架，直接影响了当代对冥想状态的神经影像学研究（如 Lutz & Davidson 对长期冥想者的 EEG 研究）。

2. **心理治疗 / Psychotherapy**：催眠、正念和 ASC 被广泛应用于创伤处理（EMDR）、焦虑障碍（MBSR/MBCT）、成瘾治疗（基于正念的复发预防）等临床领域。

3. **意识测量与评估 / Consciousness Assessment & Measurement**：状态理论为意识的多维度测量提供了分类学框架，影响了脑电图（EEG）意识状态分类、麻醉深度监测等技术发展。

4. **跨文化心理学 / Cross-Cultural Psychology**："共识恍惚"概念为理解文化对意识的塑造作用提供了分析工具，推动了文化心理学与意识研究的整合。

5. **教育 / Education**：认识到不同意识状态下的学习可能性——例如，创造性思维可能在放松状态（hypnagogic state）中更为活跃，教育者可以据此设计学习环境。

6. **临终关怀与死亡学 / End-of-Life Care & Thanatology**：塔特对意识状态多样性的理解，为临终体验（near-death experiences, NDE）的研究和临终心理支持提供了理论框架。

7. **致幻剂辅助治疗 / Psychedelic-Assisted Therapy**：随着 MDMA、裸盖菇素（psilocybin）辅助治疗的临床复兴，塔特的 ASC 理论框架为理解治疗机制提供了重要参照。

8. **人工智能意识研究 / AI Consciousness Research**：离散状态理论引发了关于人工智能系统是否可能拥有"类似意识状态"的哲学讨论——特别是在整合信息理论（IIT）和全局工作空间理论（GWT）的框架下。

## 常见误读 / Common Misreadings

**1. ❌ "塔特是非科学的神秘主义者。"**
→ **✅ 塔特始终坚持科学方法论。他主张的是"更开放的科学"，而非"放弃科学标准"。** 他的整个学术生涯都在用实验设计、统计分析和可重复性标准来研究 ASC。他对 psi 现象的探讨也是在严格的实验框架内进行的。

**1. ❌ "Tart was an unscientific mystic."**
→ **✅ Tart consistently upheld scientific methodology. What he advocated was "a more open science," not "abandoning scientific standards."** His entire academic career employed experimental designs, statistical analysis, and replicability criteria to study ASC. His exploration of psi phenomena was conducted within rigorous experimental frameworks.

**2. ❌ "变异意识状态就是'不正常'或'病态'。"**
→ **✅ 塔特论证 ASC 是人类意识的自然能力，每种状态都有其适应性功能。** 做梦有助于记忆整合，冥想可以增强情绪调节，催眠可以用于疼痛管理。ASC 不是"偏离正常"，而是"正常意识的多元表达"。

**2. ❌ "Altered states of consciousness are 'abnormal' or 'pathological'."**
→ **✅ Tart demonstrated that ASC is a natural capacity of human consciousness, with each state serving adaptive functions.** Dreaming aids memory consolidation, meditation enhances emotional regulation, and hypnosis can manage pain. ASC is not "deviation from normal" but "plural expression of normal consciousness."

**3. ❌ "共识恍惚论是阴谋论。"**
→ **✅ 这不是阴谋，而是社会化的自然结果。** 所有文化都有自己的"共识恍惚"——它不是少数人故意设计的骗局，而是语言、教育、媒体和社会互动共同塑造的集体认知模式。意识到共识恍惚，不是要"打破阴谋"，而是要扩展觉知的可能性。

**3. ❌ "The consensus trance theory is a conspiracy theory."**
→ **✅ This is not a conspiracy but a natural outcome of socialization.** Every culture has its own "consensus trance" — it is not a deliberate deception engineered by a few, but a collective cognitive pattern shaped by language, education, media, and social interaction. Recognizing consensus trance is not about "breaking a conspiracy" but expanding the possibilities of awareness.

**4. ❌ "状态特异性科学意味着'怎么都行'的认识论相对主义。"**
→ **✅ 塔特的 SSS 不是认识论的无政府主义，而是在清醒科学方法论基础上的谨慎扩展。** 他要求每个状态领域都建立自己的严格验证标准，而非放弃标准本身。这与费耶阿本德（Feyerabend）的"怎么都行"（anything goes）有本质区别。

**4. ❌ "State-specific science implies epistemological relativism — 'anything goes'."**
→ **✅ Tart's SSS is not epistemological anarchism but a cautious extension built upon the methodological foundations of waking-state science.** He required each state domain to establish its own rigorous verification standards, not to abandon standards altogether. This is fundamentally different from Feyerabend's "anything goes."

## 东西方对话 / East-West Dialogue

### 佛教认识论 / Buddhist Epistemology

**中文：** 塔特的"共识恍惚"与佛教的"无明"（avidyā）形成直接对话。佛教认为，众生的根本困境在于无明——对实相的系统性误认。塔特用现代心理学语言重新表述了这一洞见：我们的日常意识被文化条件所局限，无法直接触及实相的本来面目。他的"从共识恍惚中觉醒"，在结构上对应佛教的"觉悟"（bodhi）。

不同之处在于：佛教的觉悟是一个终极性的转化事件（"见道"），而塔特将其理解为一个渐进的、可度量的心理学过程。佛教强调通过止观（śamatha-vipaśyanā）系统训练来实现觉醒，塔特则尝试整合多种途径（冥想、心理治疗、团体工作）。

**English:** Tart's "consensus trance" dialogues directly with Buddhism's "ignorance" (avidyā). Buddhism holds that the fundamental predicament of sentient beings is ignorance — a systematic misapprehension of reality. Tart restated this insight in modern psychological language: our everyday consciousness is constrained by cultural conditioning, unable to directly apprehend reality as it is. His "awakening from the consensus trance" structurally corresponds to Buddhism's "awakening" (bodhi).

The difference lies in the fact that Buddhist awakening is an ultimate transformative event ("seeing the path"), while Tart understood it as a gradual, measurable psychological process. Buddhism emphasizes systematic training through calm-abiding and insight (śamatha-vipaśyanā) to achieve awakening, while Tart attempted to integrate multiple pathways (meditation, psychotherapy, group work).

### 道家与庄子 / Daoism and Zhuangzi

**中文：** 庄子"齐物论"中的"吾丧我"——超越个体小我的局限——与塔特的"去条件化"（deconditioning）概念形成呼应。庄子的"心斋"（虚其心以容纳万物）和"坐忘"（堕肢体，黜聪明，离形去知）可以被理解为从共识恍惚中觉醒的具体技术。塔特的状态理论也为理解道家修炼提供了现代心理学框架：从"有为"（共识恍惚中的自动化反应）到"无为"（觉醒后的自发行动）。

**English:** Zhuangzi's "losing the self" (吾丧我) — transcending the limitations of the individual ego — in the "Equalization of Things" chapter resonates with Tart's "deconditioning" concept. Zhuangzi's "fasting of the mind" (心斋, emptying the mind to receive all things) and "sitting and forgetting" (坐忘, "letting fall the limbs, dismissing perception, leaving form and abandoning knowledge") can be understood as specific technologies for awakening from consensus trance. Tart's state theory also provides a modern psychological framework for understanding Daoist cultivation: from "youwei" (automatic reactions within consensus trance) to "wuwei" (spontaneous action after awakening).

### 瑜伽与吠檀多 / Yoga and Vedānta

**中文：** 瑜伽传统的意识层次模型——从醒位（jāgrat）到梦位（svapna）到深睡位（suṣupti）到图利亚位（turīya，纯粹意识）——与塔特的离散意识状态理论高度平行。吠檀多的"摩耶"（māyā，幻象）概念在功能上等同于"共识恍惚"——两者都指向日常经验对终极实相的遮蔽。塔特在 *Waking Up* 中明确引用了这些东方传统，将西方心理学的实证方法与东方智慧传统的修行技术进行了创造性的整合。

**English:** The yoga tradition's model of consciousness levels — from waking (jāgrat) to dreaming (svapna) to deep sleep (suṣupti) to turīya (pure consciousness) — closely parallels Tart's discrete states of consciousness theory. Vedānta's concept of "māyā" (illusion) is functionally equivalent to "consensus trance" — both point to the obscuration of ultimate reality by everyday experience. In *Waking Up*, Tart explicitly referenced these Eastern traditions, creatively integrating Western psychology's empirical methods with Eastern wisdom traditions' contemplative technologies.
