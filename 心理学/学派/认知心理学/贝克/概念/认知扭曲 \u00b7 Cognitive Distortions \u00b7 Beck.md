# 认知扭曲 \u00b7 Cognitive Distortions

## 定义 / Definition

**认知扭曲 (Cognitive Distortions)** 是 Aaron T. Beck 识别出的自动思维中普遍存在的**系统性逻辑错误**。这些扭曲不是随机的，而是有规律可循的思维模式\u2014\u2014它们系统性地偏离现实，通常朝着负面方向偏差，使个体对自身、世界和未来形成不准确的认知。

**Cognitive Distortions** are systematic logical errors in automatic thinking identified by Aaron T. Beck. These distortions are not random but follow patterned forms\u2014they systematically deviate from reality, typically in a negative direction, leading individuals to form inaccurate cognitions about themselves, the world, and the future.

认知扭曲的概念后经 David Burns 的普及著作《伯恩斯新情绪疗法》(*Feeling Good*, 1980) 而广为人知，成为 CBT 中最被大众理解和应用的概念之一。

The concept was popularized by David Burns in *Feeling Good* (1980), becoming one of the most widely understood and applied CBT concepts among the general public.

## 核心机制 / Core Mechanisms

### 典型认知扭曲类型 / Types of Cognitive Distortions

贝克及其后继者识别了以下主要的认知扭曲模式：

Beck and successors identified the following major distortion patterns:

#### 1. 全或无思维 / All-or-Nothing Thinking (Dichotomous Reasoning)

以两极化的方式看待事物，没有中间地带。

Viewing things in polarized, black-or-white terms with no middle ground.

> "如果我不是第一名，我就是失败者。"\
> "If I'm not the best, I'm a total failure."

#### 2. 过度概括 / Overgeneralization

基于单一事件得出广泛的负面结论。

Drawing broad negative conclusions from a single event.

> "这次面试失败了，我永远找不到工作。"\
> "I failed this interview\u2014I'll never get a job."

#### 3. 心理过滤 / Mental Filter (Selective Abstraction)

专注于单一负面细节，忽略整体画面。

Focusing exclusively on a single negative detail while ignoring the overall picture.

> 收到十条评论中九条是正面的，但只记住那一条批评。\
> Receiving ten comments\u2014nine positive\u2014but remembering only the one criticism.

#### 4. 去正面化 / Disqualifying the Positive

将正面经验重新解释为"不算数"。

Reinterpreting positive experiences as "not counting."

> "他们夸我只是出于礼貌。"\
> "They only complimented me out of politeness."

#### 5. 妄下结论 / Jumping to Conclusions

- **读心术 (Mind Reading)**：假设自己知道别人的想法。"他们一定觉得我很蠢。"
- **算命术 (Fortune Telling)**：预测事情会变糟。"我知道这个项目会失败。"

- **Mind Reading**: Assuming you know what others think. "They must think I'm stupid."
- **Fortune Telling**: Predicting things will go badly. "I just know this project will fail."

#### 6. 灾难化 / Catastrophizing (Magnification)

将事件的重要性不合理地放大。

Blowing the significance of events out of proportion.

> "如果我在演讲中忘词，我的职业生涯就完了。"\
> "If I forget my words during the presentation, my career is over."

#### 7. 缩小化 / Minimization

将自己的优点或成就的重要性不合理地缩小。

Shrinking the significance of one's own strengths or achievements.

> "那只是因为任务简单。"\
> "That was only because the task was easy."

#### 8. 情绪推理 / Emotional Reasoning

将情绪当作证据。"我感到自己很蠢，所以我一定是蠢的。"

Using feelings as evidence. "I feel stupid, so I must be stupid."

#### 9. 应该陈述 / Should Statements (Musts/Oughts)

用"应该"、"必须"来要求自己或他人。

Using "should," "must," "ought" to demand of self or others.

> "我应该永远不会犯错。" / "我应该让所有人满意。"\
> "I should never make mistakes." / "I must please everyone."

#### 10. 贴标签 / Labeling

给自己或他人贴上全局性的负面标签，而非描述具体行为。

Attaching a global negative label to self or others, rather than describing specific behavior.

> 不说"我犯了一个错误"，而说"我是一个失败者"。\
> Instead of "I made a mistake," saying "I am a failure."

#### 11. 个人化 / Personalization

将外部事件归咎于自己，承担不属于自己的责任。

Attributing external events to oneself, taking responsibility that doesn't belong to you.

> "我的孩子在学校表现不好，一定是我的错。"\
> "My child is doing poorly in school\u2014it must be my fault."

#### 12. 控制谬误 / Control Fallacies

- **外部控制谬误**：认为自己完全是外部力量的受害者，无力改变。"都是环境的错。"
- **内部控制谬误**：认为自己对他人的痛苦负有全部责任。"如果我的朋友不开心，一定是我做了什么。"

- **External control fallacy**: Believing oneself to be entirely a victim of external forces.
- **Internal control fallacy**: Believing oneself responsible for everyone else's pain.

## 发展脉络 / Development History

- **1960s** \u2014 贝克在抑郁症研究中开始识别系统性的思维偏差
- **1967** \u2014 《抑郁症》中讨论"认知错误" (cognitive errors) 的初步类型
- **1976** \u2014 《认知疗法与情绪障碍》中更系统地阐述认知扭曲的分类
- **1980** \u2014 David Burns 在《伯恩斯新情绪疗法》(*Feeling Good*) 中将认知扭曲通俗化为 10 种类型，极大促进了公众理解
- **1990s** \u2014 Judith Beck 在教科书中进一步细化分类，区分了核心信念层面和自动思维层面的扭曲
- **2000s+** \u2014 认知科学研究将认知扭曲与**信息加工偏差** (information processing biases) 的神经机制相关联；进化心理学提出某些"扭曲"可能是适应性的快速启发式判断在现代社会中的不适应表现
- **2010s+** \u2014 数字 CBT 工具将认知扭曲识别游戏化（如 Woebot 的"扭曲类型"匹配练习）

## 临床应用 / Clinical Applications

### 认知扭曲的识别与修正流程

**Step 1: 觉察 (Awareness)** \u2014 通过思维日记记录自动思维
**Step 2: 分类 (Classification)** \u2014 识别属于哪种（或哪几种）认知扭曲
**Step 3: 质疑 (Challenge)** \u2014 用苏格拉底式提问检验：

**Step 1: Awareness** \u2014 Record automatic thoughts via thought records
**Step 2: Classification** \u2014 Identify which distortion(s) are present
**Step 3: Challenge** \u2014 Use Socratic questioning to examine:

- 证据是什么？/ What is the evidence?
- 有其他解释吗？/ Are there alternative explanations?
- 最坏、最好和最可能的结果分别是什么？/ What are the worst, best, and most realistic outcomes?
- 这个想法对我有帮助吗？/ Is this thought helpful to me?

**Step 4: 替代 (Replacement)** \u2014 发展更平衡、更符合现实的替代思维
**Step 5: 检验 (Testing)** \u2014 通过行为实验在真实世界中验证

**Step 4: Replacement** \u2014 Develop more balanced, realistic alternative thoughts
**Step 5: Testing** \u2014 Validate through behavioral experiments in real-world settings

### 认知扭曲的跨文化考量 / Cross-Cultural Considerations

认知扭曲的识别需要考虑文化语境。某些在西方个人主义文化中被视为"扭曲"的思维模式，在集体主义文化中可能是适应性的：

- **"应该陈述"**在强调社会规范和角色责任的东亚文化中，可能具有更合理的社会功能
- **"个人化"**在关系导向的文化中可能反映的是真实的互依关系，而非认知偏差

治疗师需要区分**文化适应性的思维方式**与**导致功能损害的认知偏差**。

Identifying cognitive distortions requires cultural context. Some thought patterns labeled "distortions" in Western individualistic cultures may be adaptive in collectivist cultures: "should statements" may serve reasonable social functions in East Asian cultures emphasizing norms and role responsibility; "personalization" may reflect genuine interdependence in relationship-oriented cultures. Therapists must distinguish **culturally adaptive thinking** from **functionally impairing cognitive biases**.

## 东西方对话 / East-West Dialogue

### 认知扭曲 x 唯识遍计所执 / Cognitive Distortions x Yog\u0101c\u0101ra Parikalpita

贝克的认知扭曲与唯识学的**遍计所执性 (parikalpita-svabh\u0101va)** 在结构上对应：

Beck's cognitive distortions structurally correspond to Yog\u0101c\u0101ra's **imagined nature (parikalpita-svabh\u0101va)**:

- **遍计所执**是在依他起 (paratantra, 因缘和合的现象流) 上投射虚假的实体性\u2014\u2014把无常的当作恒常的，把无自性的当作有自性的。认知扭曲同样是在经验流上投射错误的解释框架\u2014\u2014把暂时的当作永久的（过度概括），把局部的当作全体的（全或无思维）。
- 两者的**区别**：贝克关注的是具体的、可枚举的思维错误模式；唯识关注的是更根本的**认识论错误**\u2014\u2014一切执着于"有自性"本身就是遍计所执。

**Parikalpita** projects false substantiality onto dependent-arising (paratantra)\u2014treating the impermanent as permanent, the selfless as having self-nature. Cognitive distortions similarly project erroneous interpretive frameworks onto the stream of experience\u2014treating the temporary as permanent (overgeneralization), the partial as total (all-or-nothing thinking). **The difference**: Beck focuses on specific, enumerable error patterns; Yog\u0101c\u0101ra focuses on a more fundamental **epistemological error**\u2014all grasping at "inherent existence" is itself parikalpita.

### 认知扭曲 x 庄子"彼是" / Cognitive Distortions x Zhuangzi's "This and That"

庄子在《齐物论》中指出："彼亦一是非，此亦一是非。"\u2014\u2014一切是非判断都是立场性的、受限的。这与认知扭曲的核心洞察相通：人的认知判断往往是偏颇的、受视角限制的。

但庄子提供的解决方案是**超越是非**（"莫若以明"），而非修正具体的认知偏差。贝克的方法是**在是非框架内**工作\u2014\u2014用更准确的判断替换扭曲的判断。庄子走向**认识论的谦虚与超脱**；贝克走向**认识论的精确与实用**。

Zhuangzi in "Discussion on Making All Things Equal" notes: "That is one set of right and wrong; this is another set"\u2014all judgments of right and wrong are perspectival and limited. This resonates with the core insight of cognitive distortions: human cognitive judgments are often biased and perspective-limited. But Zhuangzi's solution is to **transcend right and wrong** ("nothing is better than illumination"), not to correct specific biases. Beck works **within the right/wrong framework**\u2014replacing distorted judgments with more accurate ones. Zhuangzi moves toward **epistemological humility and transcendence**; Beck toward **epistemological precision and pragmatism**.
