# 迈克尔·怀特 · Michael White

## 基本信息 / Basic Information

迈克尔·怀特（Michael White，1948年—2008年4月4日），澳大利亚社会工作者、家庭治疗师，叙事疗法（Narrative Therapy）的联合创始人（与新西兰的大卫·艾普斯顿 David Epston 合作），阿德莱德杜尔维奇中心（Dulwich Centre）创始人。他将后结构主义哲学——尤其是福柯的权力/知识理论——引入心理治疗实践，深刻改变了当代治疗领域对权力关系、身份建构和意义生产的理解。

Michael White (1948 – April 4, 2008) was an Australian social worker and family therapist, co-founder (with David Epston of New Zealand) of Narrative Therapy, and founder of the Dulwich Centre in Adelaide. He introduced post-structuralist 哲学 — particularly Foucault's power/knowledge theory — into psychotherapeutic practice, profoundly transforming contemporary understandings of power relations, identity construction, and meaning-making in the therapeutic field.

- **国籍 / Nationality**：澳大利亚 / Australian
- **教育背景 / Education**：南澳大利亚理工学院社会工作专业 / Social Work, South Australian Institute of Technology
- **专业身份 / Professional Identity**：社会工作者、家庭治疗师、叙事实践先驱 / Social worker, family therapist, pioneer of narrative practice
- **机构归属 / Institutional Affiliations**：杜尔维奇中心（Dulwich Centre, Adelaide）、叙事实践国际网络 / Dulwich Centre and the international narrative practice network
- **主要合作者 / Key Collaborators**：大卫·艾普斯顿（David Epston）、芭芭拉·迈尔霍夫（Barbara Myerhoff）、吉尔·弗里德曼（Jill Freedman）、吉恩·康普斯（Gene Combs）
- **逝世 / Death**：2008年4月4日于圣迭戈，在教学工作坊期间因心脏病突发去世 / April 4, 2008, in San Diego; died of a heart attack while conducting a teaching workshop

## 核心命题 / Core Propositions

### 命题一：问题是问题，人不是问题 / Proposition 1: The Problem Is the Problem — The Person Is Not the Problem

怀特最具革命性的立场宣言是：将"问题"从人的身份中分离出来。在传统治疗中，人被"诊断"为问题本身（"你是一个焦虑的人"），而叙事实践坚持：问题是一种社会文化建构的叙事效果，而非人内在的本质属性。这一立场降低了羞耻感，打开了治疗空间。

White's most revolutionary stance is to separate the "problem" from the person's identity. In traditional therapy, people are "diagnosed" as being the problem itself ("you are an anxious person"), whereas narrative practice insists that the problem is a socioculturally constructed narrative effect, not an inherent attribute of the person. This stance reduces shame and opens therapeutic space.

### 命题二：人通过叙事建构身份 / Proposition 2: Identity Is Constructed Through Narrative

怀特从布鲁纳（Jerome Bruner）的叙事心理学和迈尔霍夫（Barbara Myerhoff）的叙事人类学中汲取灵感，认为人并非被动地"发现"自我，而是通过讲述和组织生活经验的"故事"来主动"建构"身份。主流叙事（dominant narratives）——那些被社会文化权力所支撑的故事线——常将人"锁定"在问题化的、单薄的身份描述中。

Drawing from Jerome Bruner's narrative 心理学 and Barbara Myerhoff's narrative anthropology, White held that people do not passively "discover" the self but actively "construct" identity through the stories they tell and organize around their lived experience. Dominant narratives — those storylines supported by sociocultural power — often "lock" people into problem-saturated, thin identity descriptions.

### 命题三：治疗是政治性的 / Proposition 3: Therapy Is Political

受福柯启发，怀特深刻意识到心理治疗并非价值中立的科学活动，而是参与着权力关系的再生产。诊断标签、正常化话语、专家权威——这些都是福柯所说的"规训权力"在治疗领域的运作。叙事实践因此采取"去中心化但具影响力的"（decentred but influential）治疗师立场，拒绝成为新的规训力量。

Inspired by Foucault, White was deeply aware that psychotherapy is not a value-neutral scientific activity but participates in the reproduction of power relations. Diagnostic labels, normalizing discourses, and expert authority — these are all operations of what Foucault called "disciplinary power" in the therapeutic domain. Narrative practice therefore adopts a "decentred but influential" therapeutic stance, refusing to become yet another disciplinary force.

### 命题四：独特结果是替代故事的入口 / Proposition 4: Unique Outcomes Are Entry Points to Alternative Stories

怀特认为，即使在最"问题化"的生活中，也存在着不符合问题故事的例外时刻——他称之为"独特结果"（unique outcomes）。这些被忽略的、未被讲述的经验片段，是发展替代叙事（alternative stories）的种子。治疗师的任务是敏锐地捕捉这些独特结果，并通过重写对话将其编织为更丰厚的身份叙事。

White believed that even in the most "problem-saturated" lives, there exist moments of exception that do not fit the problem story — what he called "unique outcomes." These overlooked, unstoried fragments of experience are the seeds for developing alternative stories. The therapist's task is to keenly attend to these unique outcomes and weave them, through re-authoring conversations, into richer identity narratives.

### 命题五：身份是社会性的、关系性的 / Proposition 5: Identity Is Social and Relational

怀特反对将身份视为个体内在的、固定的本质。身份是在社会关系中被建构、被维持、被挑战的。定义式仪式（definitional ceremony）正是基于这一洞见：通过"外部见证者"（outsider witnesses）的参与，人的身份转变得以在社群关系中被确认和巩固。这使叙事实践具有了强烈的社群治疗和社会正义取向。

White rejected the notion of identity as an individual, internal, fixed essence. Identity is constructed, maintained, and challenged within social relationships. Definitional ceremony is precisely based on this insight: through the participation of "outsider witnesses," a person's identity transition is confirmed and consolidated within community relationships. This gives narrative practice a strong community-therapy and social-justice orientation.

### 命题六：治疗师的"非专家立场" / Proposition 6: The Therapist's "Not-Knowing" Position

怀特坚持治疗师不应扮演"诊断专家"的角色，而应采取"好奇的探究者"立场——这一立场后来被安德森（Harlene Anderson）和古力申（Harry Goolishian）发展为"不知"（not-knowing）的治疗姿态。治疗师是"协作者"而非"权威"，通过精心设计的提问（而非解释或建议）来引导来访者发现自身的知识和资源。

White insisted that therapists should not assume the role of "diagnostic experts" but adopt the stance of "curious inquirers" — a stance later developed by Harlene Anderson and Harry Goolishian as the "not-knowing" therapeutic position. The therapist is a "collaborator" rather than an "authority," guiding clients to discover their own knowledge and resources through carefully crafted questions rather than interpretations or advice.

## 思想脉络 / Intellectual Trajectory

### 早期：社会工作与家庭治疗时期（1948–1980年代中期）/ Early Period: Social Work and Family Therapy (1948–mid-1980s)

怀特出生于阿德莱德，在南澳大利亚接受社会工作训练。1970年代，他在阿德莱德从事家庭治疗实践，深受系统家庭治疗（systemic family therapy）和米兰学派（Milan School）的影响。然而，他对传统家庭治疗中将家庭"病理化"的倾向感到不满——特别是当治疗师用"功能失调家庭"的标签来定义来访者的身份时。他开始寻找一种更加尊重来访者主体性的治疗方式。这一时期，他与新西兰奥克兰的大卫·艾普斯顿建立了跨塔斯曼海的学术合作关系，这一合作持续了三十余年。

White was born in Adelaide and trained as a social worker in South Australia. During the 1970s, he practiced family therapy in Adelaide, deeply influenced by systemic family therapy and the Milan School. However, he grew dissatisfied with traditional family therapy's tendency to "pathologize" families — particularly when therapists used labels like "dysfunctional family" to define clients' identities. He began searching for a therapeutic approach that more fully respected client agency. During this period, he established a trans-Tasman academic partnership with David Epston in Auckland, New Zealand, a collaboration that lasted over thirty years.

**关键发展 / Key Developments**：
- 从系统家庭治疗转向更具人文关怀的治疗取向 / Shifted from systemic family therapy to a more humanistic therapeutic orientation
- 与艾普斯顿建立长期合作关系 / Established a long-term partnership with Epston
- 开始关注治疗中的权力关系问题 / Began attending to power relations in therapy

### 中期：叙事疗法的创立与发展（1980年代中期–1990年代末）/ Middle Period: Founding and Development of Narrative Therapy (mid-1980s–late 1990s)

1980年代中期是怀特思想的关键转折期。他接触了福柯的权力/知识理论和布鲁纳的叙事心理学，这些后结构主义和建构主义的理论资源使他从根本上重新思考治疗实践的本质。1990年，他与艾普斯顿出版了里程碑式的著作《叙事疗法》（*Narrative Means to Therapeutic Ends*），系统提出了"外化对话"（externalizing conversations）、"重写"（re-authoring）和"独特结果"（unique outcomes）等核心概念。1983年创立的杜尔维奇中心成为叙事实践的国际枢纽，培训了来自世界各地的治疗师和社区工作者。叙事疗法迅速在家庭治疗、社区工作和跨文化治疗领域传播开来。

The mid-1980s marked a pivotal turning point in White's intellectual development. His encounter with Foucault's power/knowledge theory and Bruner's narrative 心理学 — post-structuralist and constructivist theoretical resources — led him to fundamentally rethink the nature of therapeutic practice. In 1990, he and Epston published the landmark work *Narrative Means to Therapeutic Ends*, systematically proposing core concepts such as "externalizing conversations," "re-authoring," and "unique outcomes." The Dulwich Centre, founded in 1983, became the international hub for narrative practice, training therapists and community workers from around the world. Narrative therapy spread rapidly in family therapy, community work, and cross-cultural therapeutic fields.

**关键发展 / Key Developments**：
- 系统提出外化对话技术 / Systematically developed externalizing conversation techniques
- 将福柯的权力分析引入治疗实践 / Introduced Foucault's power analysis into therapeutic practice
- 杜尔维奇中心成为国际叙事实践网络的核心 / The Dulwich Centre became the core of the international narrative practice network
- 在原住民社区、难民群体的治疗实践中广泛应用 / Widely applied in therapeutic practice with Indigenous communities and refugee populations

### 晚期：叙事实践地图的精细化与全球传播（2000年代–2008）/ Late Period: Refinement of Narrative Practice Maps and Global Dissemination (2000s–2008)

进入21世纪，怀特致力于将叙事实践的理论框架精细化和系统化。2007年出版的《叙事实践的地图》（*Maps of Narrative Practice*）是他最具系统性的著作，提出了六大治疗"地图"——外化对话地图、重写对话地图、说知见证地图（re-membering conversations）、定义式仪式地图、悲伤地图（grief map）和重建对话地图（re-negotiation map）。他越来越多地在国际场合——特别是非西方文化语境中——开展工作，探索叙事实践在原住民社区、受创伤影响的社群和跨文化对话中的应用。2008年4月4日，他在美国圣迭戈进行教学工作坊时因心脏病突发去世，享年60岁。他的逝世对整个叙事治疗社区造成了巨大的损失，但他的思想遗产通过杜尔维奇中心和全球叙事实践网络持续影响着世界各地的治疗师和社区工作者。

In the 21st century, White devoted himself to refining and systematizing the theoretical framework of narrative practice. *Maps of Narrative Practice* (2007), his most systematic work, proposed six therapeutic "maps" — the externalizing conversations map, the re-authoring conversations map, the re-membering conversations map, the definitional ceremony map, the grief map, and the re-negotiation map. He increasingly worked in international contexts — particularly in non-Western cultural settings — exploring the application of narrative practice in Indigenous communities, trauma-affected populations, and cross-cultural dialogue. On April 4, 2008, he died of a heart attack while conducting a teaching workshop in San Diego, aged 60. His passing was a tremendous loss to the entire narrative therapy community, but his intellectual legacy continues to influence therapists and community workers worldwide through the Dulwich Centre and the global narrative practice network.

**关键发展 / Key Developments**：
- 出版《叙事实践的地图》，系统化治疗框架 / Published *Maps of Narrative Practice*, systematizing the therapeutic framework
- 推进叙事实践在原住民和受创伤社群中的应用 / Advanced narrative practice applications in Indigenous and trauma-affected communities
- 深化了对治疗伦理和政治维度的思考 / Deepened reflection on the ethical and political dimensions of therapy

## 重要概念 / Key Concepts

1. **叙事疗法 / Narrative Therapy**：以叙事隐喻为核心的后结构主义心理治疗方法，认为人通过故事建构身份，治疗是帮助人重写被问题主导的生活叙事。→ [详见子条目](concepts/narrative-therapy.md) / A post-structuralist psychotherapeutic approach centered on the narrative metaphor, holding that people construct identity through stories, and therapy helps people re-author problem-dominated life narratives.

2. **外化 / Externalization**：将问题从人的身份中分离出来的核心治疗技术，使"问题是问题，人不是问题"成为可能。→ [详见子条目](concepts/externalization.md) / The core therapeutic technique of separating the problem from the person's identity, making possible "the problem is the problem, the person is not the problem."

3. **重写 / Re-authoring**：通过发现"独特结果"并将其编织为替代叙事来丰富来访者身份认同的治疗过程。→ [详见子条目](concepts/re-authoring.md) / The therapeutic process of enriching the client's identity by discovering "unique outcomes" and weaving them into alternative narratives.

4. **后结构主义在叙事实践中的运用 / Post-structuralism in Narrative Practice**：福柯的权力/知识理论如何从根本上塑造了叙事实践对治疗关系、诊断和"正常化"的批判性理解。→ [详见子条目](concepts/post-structuralism.md) / How Foucault's power/knowledge theory fundamentally shaped narrative practice's critical understanding of therapeutic relationships, diagnosis, and "normalization."

5. **定义式仪式 / Definitional Ceremony**：通过外部见证者的参与，仪式性地确认和强化个人身份转变的社群治疗实践。→ [详见子条目](concepts/definitional-ceremony.md) / A community therapeutic practice that ritually confirms and strengthens personal identity transitions through the participation of outsider witnesses.

6. **独特结果 / Unique Outcomes**：不符合问题主导叙事的例外时刻和经验片段，是发展替代故事的入口 / Moments of exception and fragments of experience that do not fit the problem-dominated narrative; entry points for developing alternative stories.

7. **非专家立场 / The "Not-Knowing" Position**：治疗师拒绝扮演诊断权威角色，以好奇的探究者姿态与来访者协作 / The therapist refuses the role of diagnostic authority, adopting a stance of curious inquiry in collaboration with the client.

8. **说知见证 / Re-membering Conversations**：通过重新召唤生命中重要人物（真实的或想象的）的声音来丰富身份认同的对话技术 / A conversational technique that enriches identity by re-invoking the voices of significant figures (real or imagined) in one's life.

9. **主流叙事与替代叙事 / Dominant and Alternative Narratives**：社会文化中占支配地位的故事线（常将人问题化）与个体生活中被忽略的替代性故事线之间的张力 / The tension between the dominant storylines in sociocultural contexts (which often problem-saturate people) and the overlooked alternative storylines in individual lives.

10. **丰厚描述 / Thick Description**：借自人类学家格尔茨（Clifford Geertz）的概念，指通过多层次、多声部的叙事来构建丰富的身份认同，与问题叙事的"单薄描述"（thin description）形成对比 / A concept borrowed from anthropologist Clifford Geertz, referring to the construction of rich identity through multi-layered, multi-voiced narratives, contrasted with the "thin description" of problem narratives.

## 思想坐标 / Intellectual Coordinates

### 承接 / Inherited From

- **福柯的权力/知识理论**：福柯对规训权力、话语建构和"正常化判断"的分析，为叙事实践提供了批判性理论基础 / Foucault's power/knowledge theory: Foucault's analysis of disciplinary power, discursive construction, and "normalizing judgment" provided the critical theoretical foundation for narrative practice
- **布鲁纳的叙事心理学**：布鲁纳关于人通过叙事来组织和理解经验的研究，为叙事隐喻提供了心理学支撑 / Bruner's narrative psychology: Bruner's 研究 on how people organize and understand experience through narrative provided psychological support for the narrative metaphor
- **迈尔霍夫的叙事人类学**：迈尔霍夫对"说知"（re-membering）和定义式仪式的民族志研究，直接启发了怀特的社群治疗实践 / Myerhoff's narrative anthropology: Myerhoff's ethnographic 研究 on "re-membering" and definitional ceremony directly inspired White's community therapeutic practice
- **系统家庭治疗传统**：米兰学派的循环提问和系统思维为怀特早期的家庭治疗实践奠定了基础 / The systemic family therapy tradition: The Milan School's circular questioning and systemic thinking laid the foundation for White's early family therapy practice
- **建构主义与社会建构论**：格尔根（Kenneth Gergen）等人的社会建构论思想为叙事实践的哲学根基提供了支持 / Constructivism and social constructionism: The social constructionist thought of Kenneth Gergen and others supported the philosophical foundations of narrative practice

### 批判 / Critiqued

- **诊断模型的还原主义**：怀特批评DSM等诊断系统将复杂的人类经验简化为标签和类别，剥夺了来访者的主体性 / The reductionism of the diagnostic model: White criticized diagnostic systems such as the DSM for reducing complex human experience to labels and categories, stripping clients of their agency
- **治疗师的专家权威**：他挑战传统治疗中治疗师作为"真相发现者"的角色，认为这种权力不对称会再生产压迫性关系 / Therapist expert authority: He challenged the traditional role of the therapist as "truth-finder," arguing that this power asymmetry reproduces oppressive relationships
- **正常化话语的暴力**：受福柯启发，他揭示了"什么是正常的家庭/行为/情感"这类话语如何对不符合标准的人施加隐性暴力 / The violence of normalizing discourse: Inspired by Foucault, he revealed how discourses about "what constitutes a normal family/behavior/emotion" inflict subtle violence on those who do not conform to standards
- **个体化心理学对社会的忽视**：他批评将心理问题完全归结为个体内在因素的倾向，忽视了社会文化语境的作用 / Individualized psychology's neglect of society: He criticized the tendency to attribute psychological problems entirely to individual internal factors, neglecting the role of sociocultural contexts

### 影响 / Influenced

- **叙事实践国际网络**：杜尔维奇中心培训的治疗师和社区工作者遍布全球，叙事实践已成为当代心理治疗的重要流派 / The international narrative practice network: Therapists and community workers trained by the Dulwich Centre span the globe; narrative practice has become a major school in contemporary psychotherapy
- **社区叙事实践**：恩库比（Ncazelo Ncube）、德纳姆（David Denborough）等人在非洲、澳洲原住民社区推进的集体叙事实践 / Community narrative practice: Collective narrative practice advanced by Ncazelo Ncube, David Denborough, and others in African and Australian Indigenous communities
- **后现代与后结构主义治疗运动**：叙事实践与焦点解决短期治疗、合作对话实践等共同构成了后现代治疗运动的核心 / The postmodern and post-structuralist therapy movement: Narrative practice, along with solution-focused brief therapy and collaborative dialogic practice, forms the core of the postmodern therapy movement
- **社会工作与社区发展**：叙事实践在社会工作、教育、组织咨询等领域得到广泛应用 / Social work and community development: Narrative practice has been widely applied in social work, education, organizational consulting, and other fields

## 当代应用 / Contemporary Applications

1. **原住民与跨文化治疗 / Indigenous and Cross-cultural Therapy**：叙事实践特别适用于非西方文化语境，因为它尊重来访者的文化知识，避免了西方心理学的文化帝国主义倾向。在澳大利亚原住民社区、加拿大第一民族和南非社区中得到了广泛应用 / Narrative practice is particularly suited to non-Western cultural contexts because it respects clients' cultural knowledge and avoids the cultural imperialism tendencies of Western 心理学. It has been widely applied in Australian Indigenous communities, Canadian First Nations, and South African communities.

2. **创伤后治疗 / Post-Trauma Therapy**：叙事实践的外化技术和重写对话为创伤幸存者提供了安全的治疗路径。通过将创伤经验外化为"创伤的影响"而非人的本质，来访者能够逐步重建自我认同 / The externalization techniques and re-authoring conversations of narrative practice provide a safe therapeutic pathway for trauma survivors. By externalizing traumatic experiences as "the effects of trauma" rather than the person's essence, clients can gradually rebuild self-identity.

3. **LGBTQ+群体治疗 / LGBTQ+ Therapy**：叙事实践对"主流叙事"的批判性分析和对"替代叙事"的发展，特别适合帮助LGBTQ+群体挑战异性恋规范的内化压迫 / Narrative practice's critical analysis of "dominant narratives" and development of "alternative narratives" is particularly suited to helping LGBTQ+ populations challenge the internalized oppression of heteronormative standards.

4. **儿童与青少年治疗 / Child and Adolescent Therapy**：外化技术在儿童治疗中尤为有效——通过将"愤怒怪兽"或"忧虑虫"与儿童分开，治疗师能够帮助儿童以一种游戏化的方式与问题互动 / Externalization techniques are particularly effective in child therapy — by separating "the anger monster" or "the worry bug" from the child, therapists can help children engage with problems in a playful manner.

5. **数字时代的叙事身份 / Narrative Identity in the Digital Age**：社交媒体时代，个人叙事被空前地公开化和碎片化。叙事实践为理解数字身份建构提供了理论工具，也为数字心理健康干预提供了框架 / In the age of social media, personal narratives are unprecedentedly publicized and fragmented. Narrative practice provides theoretical tools for understanding digital identity construction and a framework for digital mental health interventions.

6. **组织叙事与变革管理 / Organizational Narrative and Change Management**：叙事实践的原理被应用于组织咨询——帮助组织成员识别限制性叙事、发展替代性组织故事 / The principles of narrative practice have been applied to organizational consulting — helping organizational members identify restrictive narratives and develop alternative organizational stories.

7. **叙事医学 / Narrative Medicine**：丽塔·卡伦（Rita Charon）等人发展的叙事医学运动，与叙事实践共享对患者故事和意义的关注 / The narrative medicine movement developed by Rita Charon and others shares with narrative practice an attention to patients' stories and meanings.

8. **AI与叙事实践 / AI and Narrative Practice**：AI对话系统能否有效运用外化对话技术？叙事实践的"非专家立场"是否特别适合作为AI辅助心理治疗的伦理框架？这些问题正引发新的研究和讨论 / Can AI dialogue systems effectively employ externalizing conversation techniques? Is narrative practice's "not-knowing position" particularly suited as an ethical framework for AI-assisted psychotherapy? These questions are sparking new 研究 and discussion.

## 常见误读 / Common Misreadings

1. **❌ "叙事疗法只是一种讲故事的技巧，没有理论深度。" / "Narrative therapy is just a storytelling technique without theoretical depth."**
   **✅ 正确理解**：叙事实践深深扎根于后结构主义哲学（福柯）、叙事心理学（布鲁纳）、叙事人类学（迈尔霍夫）和社会建构论（格尔根），拥有坚实的理论基础。外化对话和重写技术背后有精密的理论框架支撑。/ **Correct understanding**: Narrative practice is deeply rooted in post-structuralist 哲学 (Foucault), narrative 心理学 (Bruner), narrative anthropology (Myerhoff), and social constructionism (Gergen), possessing a solid theoretical foundation. Externalizing conversations and re-authoring techniques are supported by a sophisticated theoretical framework.

2. **❌ "叙事疗法不关心现实，认为一切都是叙事建构。" / "Narrative therapy doesn't care about reality, believing everything is narrative construction."**
   **✅ 正确理解**：怀特并非否认客观现实的存在，而是强调我们对现实的"理解"和"意义"是通过叙事框架来组织的。创伤、虐待和不公正是真实的，但人们对这些经验的理解和身份认同可以通过不同的叙事来重新建构。/ **Correct understanding**: White did not deny the existence of objective reality but emphasized that our "understanding" and "meaning" of reality are organized through narrative frameworks. Trauma, abuse, and injustice are real, but people's understanding of and identity around these experiences can be re-constructed through different narratives.

3. **❌ "叙事疗法的治疗师完全不介入，只是被动地听故事。" / "Narrative therapists are completely non-interventionist, just passively listening to stories."**
   **✅ 正确理解**：怀特的"非专家立场"并非不介入，而是以不同的方式介入——治疗师通过精心设计的提问、外化对话和重写技术来积极地引导治疗过程，只是拒绝扮演"全知权威"的角色。这是一种"去中心化但具影响力的"立场。/ **Correct understanding**: White's "not-knowing position" is not non-interventionist but intervenes differently — the therapist actively guides the therapeutic process through carefully designed questions, externalizing conversations, and re-authoring techniques, simply refusing the role of "omniscient authority." This is a "decentred but influential" stance.

4. **❌ "叙事疗法只适用于家庭治疗，对其他心理问题无效。" / "Narrative therapy only works for family therapy and is ineffective for other psychological problems."**
   **✅ 正确理解**：虽然叙事实践起源于家庭治疗，但它已被成功应用于个体治疗、团体治疗、社区工作、创伤治疗、进食障碍、愤怒管理、哀伤辅导等众多领域，其适用范围远超家庭治疗的原始语境。/ **Correct understanding**: Although narrative practice originated in family therapy, it has been successfully applied in individual therapy, group therapy, community work, trauma therapy, eating disorders, anger management, grief counseling, and many other areas. Its scope of application extends far beyond the original family therapy context.

## 东西方对话 / East-West Dialogue

### 与佛教"无我"观的对话 / Dialogue with the Buddhist Concept of Anattā (Non-Self)

怀特对"固定身份"的批判与佛教的"无我"（anattā / anātman）教义形成深刻对话。佛教认为不存在一个恒常不变的"自我"实体，一切所谓"我"只是五蕴（色、受、想、行、识）的暂时聚合。怀特同样反对将身份本质化，认为身份是通过叙事不断建构和重构的。但两者的路径不同：佛教通过禅修直接体验"无我"来解脱执着，而怀特通过"重写叙事"来建构更丰厚的、符合来访者价值取向的身份认同。可以说，怀特提供了一种"世俗化"的无我实践——不是消融自我，而是解放自我于限制性叙事之外。

White's critique of "fixed identity" enters deep dialogue with the Buddhist teaching of anattā (non-self). Buddhism holds that no permanent, unchanging "self" entity exists; all so-called "self" is merely the temporary aggregation of the five aggregates (form, feeling, perception, mental formations, consciousness). White similarly opposed essentializing identity, believing it is continuously constructed and reconstructed through narrative. But their paths differ: Buddhism liberates attachment through meditative direct experience of "non-self," while White constructs richer, more value-congruent identity through "re-authoring narratives." White may be said to offer a "secularized" practice of non-self — not dissolving the self, but liberating the self from restrictive narratives.

### 与儒家"正名"思想的对话 / Dialogue with the Confucian "Rectification of Names"

怀特对"命名"（naming）力量的敏感与儒家的"正名"（正名）思想有结构性呼应。孔子曰："名不正则言不顺，言不顺则事不成。"怀特同样深刻地意识到话语和命名如何塑造现实和身份——"焦虑症患者"这一命名如何限制了人的可能性。但两者的价值取向不同：儒家的正名旨在恢复社会秩序的伦理规范，而怀特的叙事实践旨在解构限制性命名、赋予来访者重新命名的权力。

White's sensitivity to the power of "naming" structurally resonates with the Confucian concept of "rectification of names" (zhèng míng). Confucius said: "When names are not correct, language does not accord with truth; when language does not accord with truth, matters cannot be accomplished." White was equally aware of how discourse and naming shape reality and identity — how the name "anxiety patient" constrains human possibility. But their value orientations differ: Confucian rectification of names aims to restore ethical norms of social order, while White's narrative practice aims to deconstruct restrictive naming and empower clients to re-name themselves.

### 与庄子"齐物"思想的对话 / Dialogue with Zhuangzi's "Equalization of Things"

庄子《齐物论》中对一切固定判断和区分的解构——"彼亦一是非，此亦一是非"——与怀特对"主流叙事"的解构精神相通。两者都警惕单一叙事对多元经验的压制。但庄子走向"逍遥"——超越一切是非对立的精神自由，而怀特则走向"重写"——在具体的社会关系中建构替代性的、更丰厚的身份认同。

Zhuangzi's *Qi Wu Lun* ("Equalization of Things") deconstructs all fixed judgments and distinctions — "that too has its right and wrong, this too has its right and wrong" — which resonates with White's deconstructive spirit toward "dominant narratives." Both are wary of the suppression of plural experience by singular narratives. But Zhuangzi moves toward "carefree wandering" — spiritual freedom beyond all oppositions — while White moves toward "re-authoring" — constructing alternative, richer identities within concrete social relationships.
