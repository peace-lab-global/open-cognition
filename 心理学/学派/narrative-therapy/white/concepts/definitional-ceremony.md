# 定义式仪式 · Definitional Ceremony

## 定义 · Definition

定义式仪式（Definitional Ceremony）是叙事疗法中一种通过社群见证来确认和强化个人身份转变的治疗实践，由迈克尔·怀特从人类学家芭芭拉·迈尔霍夫（Barbara Myerhoff）的民族志研究中借鉴并发展而来。其核心理念是：身份认同不是个体独自建构的，而是在社会关系中被"定义"和"确认"的。当一个人经历了重要的身份转变——从"被问题控制的人"到"与问题抗争的人"——这种转变需要被他人"见证"（witnessed）才能被真正巩固。定义式仪式正是创造这种社群见证空间的治疗实践。

Definitional Ceremony is a therapeutic practice in narrative therapy that confirms and strengthens personal identity transitions through community witnessing, developed by Michael White from the ethnographic research of anthropologist Barbara Myerhoff. Its core premise is that identity is not constructed by the individual alone but is "defined" and "confirmed" within social relationships. When a person undergoes a significant identity transition — from "a person controlled by the problem" to "a person standing up to the problem" — this transition needs to be "witnessed" by others to be truly consolidated. Definitional ceremony is precisely the therapeutic practice that creates this community witnessing space.

## 核心机制 · Core Mechanisms

### 迈尔霍夫的灵感 · Myerhoff's Inspiration

定义式仪式的直接灵感来自迈尔霍夫对洛杉矶威尼斯区老年犹太社区的研究。她观察到，这些老人在日常生活中通过"说知"（re-membering）实践——即召唤过去的关系和记忆中的"见证者"——来维持和丰富自己的身份认同。迈尔霍夫将这种实践称为"定义式仪式"：一种人们通过相互见证来"定义"自己是谁的仪式性过程。

The direct inspiration for definitional ceremony came from Myerhoff's study of an elderly Jewish community in Venice, Los Angeles. She observed that these elders maintained and enriched their identities through everyday "re-membering" practices — invoking past relationships and "witnesses" from memory. Myerhoff called this practice "definitional ceremony": a ritual process through which people "define" who they are through mutual witnessing.

怀特将这一人类学观察创造性地转化为治疗实践：在治疗中引入"外部见证者"（outsider witnesses），让他们聆听来访者的替代叙事，并以特定的方式回应。

White creatively transformed this anthropological observation into therapeutic practice: introducing "outsider witnesses" into therapy to listen to the client's alternative narrative and respond in specific ways.

### 外部见证的四步回应结构 · The Four-Step Response Structure of Outsider Witnessing

怀特在《叙事实践的地图》中提出了外部见证者的四步回应结构，这是定义式仪式的核心操作框架：

White proposed a four-step response structure for outsider witnesses in *Maps of Narrative Practice*, which is the core operational framework of definitional ceremony:

**1. 表达兴趣（Identifying the Expression）/ Identifying the Expression**

见证者首先指出在聆听过程中最吸引他们注意力的具体内容——一个词、一个画面、一个故事片段。这不是泛泛的赞美，而是对具体细节的关注。

Witnesses first identify what specifically caught their attention during listening — a word, an image, a story fragment. This is not general praise but attention to specific details.

**2. 描述意象（Describing the Image）/ Describing the Image**

见证者描述这个具体内容在他们心中唤起的意象、画面或联想——这些内容如何触动了自己的想象。

Witnesses describe the images, pictures, or associations this specific content evoked in them — how the content touched their imagination.

**3. 共鸣的个人经验（Identifying Personal Resonance）/ Identifying Personal Resonance**

见证者反思为什么这些内容触动了他们——它与见证者自身的生活经验有什么关联？这一步将见证者与来访者的叙事建立了"人性联结"。

Witnesses reflect on why this content touched them — what connection does it have to the witness's own life experience? This step establishes a "human connection" between witness and client.

**4. 思想的触动（Identifying Transport）/ Identifying Transport**

见证者反思：通过聆听这些故事，他们自己的思想、理解或行为可能发生什么改变？他们被"带向"了什么新的方向？

Witnesses reflect: through listening to these stories, what changes might occur in their own thinking, understanding, or behavior? Where have they been "transported" to?

### 治疗性效果 · Therapeutic Effects

定义式仪式产生多重治疗效果：

Definitional ceremony produces multiple therapeutic effects:

- **身份的社群确认**：替代叙事不再只是来访者和治疗师之间的"私密"建构，而是在更广泛的社群中被见证和确认 / **Community confirmation of identity**: The alternative narrative is no longer just a "private" construction between client and therapist but is witnessed and confirmed in a broader community
- **丰厚化身份描述**：见证者的回应为来访者的叙事增添了新的层次和意义维度 / **Enrichment of identity description**: Witnesses' responses add new layers and dimensions of meaning to the client's narrative
- **建立"共鸣共同体"**：见证过程在见证者和来访者之间建立了深层的人性联结，对抗了问题叙事常伴随的"孤立" / **Building a "resonance community"**: The witnessing process establishes deep human connections between witnesses and clients, countering the "isolation" often accompanying problem narratives
- **双向转化**：见证者自身也在聆听和回应过程中被触动和转化 / **Mutual transformation**: Witnesses themselves are touched and transformed through the process of listening and responding

## 发展脉络 · Development

### 从民族志到治疗实践（1980年代–1990年代）/ From Ethnography to Therapeutic Practice (1980s–1990s)

迈尔霍夫1978年出版的《数算我们的日子》（*Number Our Days*）引起了怀特的极大兴趣。他在1980年代后期开始将"定义式仪式"的概念引入治疗实践——最初在治疗团体中邀请其他成员作为见证者，后来发展为更加结构化的外部见证程序。

Myerhoff's *Number Our Days* (1978) captured White's deep interest. In the late 1980s, he began introducing the concept of "definitional ceremony" into therapeutic practice — initially inviting other group members as witnesses in therapy groups, later developing more structured outsider witness procedures.

### 结构化与传播（2000年代至今）/ Structuring and Dissemination (2000s–Present)

在2007年的《叙事实践的地图》中，怀特将定义式仪式精细化为包含四步回应结构的完整治疗框架。他去世后，杜尔维奇中心继续推进定义式仪式在各种社群情境中的应用——从创伤后社区到难民营、从原住民社区到学校。

In *Maps of Narrative Practice* (2007), White refined definitional ceremony into a complete therapeutic framework including the four-step response structure. After his passing, the Dulwich Centre continued advancing the application of definitional ceremony in various community contexts — from post-trauma communities to refugee camps, from Indigenous communities to schools.

## 临床应用 · Clinical Applications

### 个体治疗中的定义式仪式 / Definitional Ceremony in Individual Therapy

即使在个体治疗中，也可以引入见证者：

Even in individual therapy, witnesses can be introduced:

- **治疗师作为见证者**：治疗师以来自迈尔霍夫传统的"外部见证者"身份回应来访者的替代叙事 / **Therapist as witness**: The therapist responds to the client's alternative narrative from the position of an "outsider witness" in the Myerhoff tradition
- **书信见证**：治疗师在会谈后写信给来访者，记录和见证来访者在会谈中展现的力量和价值 / **Letter witnessing**: The therapist writes letters to the client after sessions, recording and witnessing the strengths and values the client demonstrated
- **"俱乐部"成员**：邀请来访者生活中已经认识或可能认识的"同路人"参与见证 / **"Club" members**: Inviting "fellow travelers" from the client's life — known or potential — to participate in witnessing

### 团体与社区中的应用 / Applications in Groups and Communities

定义式仪式在团体和社区治疗中发挥最大威力：

Definitional ceremony exerts its greatest power in group and community therapy:

- **治疗团体**：团体成员轮流讲述和见证彼此的替代叙事 / **Therapy groups**: Group members take turns telling and witnessing each other's alternative narratives
- **社区叙事项目**：在受集体创伤影响的社区（如自然灾害、战争、种族迫害）中组织大规模的叙事见证活动 / **Community narrative projects**: Organizing large-scale narrative witnessing events in communities affected by collective trauma (e.g., natural disasters, war, ethnic persecution)
- **"生命之树"项目**：德纳姆等人发展的"生命之树"（Tree of Life）方法，将定义式仪式融入儿童和青少年的团体叙事工作 / **"Tree of Life" projects**: The "Tree of Life" method developed by Denborough and others integrates definitional ceremony into group narrative work with children and adolescents

### 见证的伦理 / Ethics of Witnessing

怀特特别强调外部见证的伦理规范：

White particularly emphasized the ethical norms of outsider witnessing:

- **禁止评判和建议**：见证者不应评判来访者的叙事或提供建议 / **No judgment or advice**: Witnesses should not judge the client's narrative or offer advice
- **禁止"拯救"冲动**：见证者应克制"帮助"或"拯救"来访者的冲动 / **No "rescue" impulse**: Witnesses should resist the impulse to "help" or "rescue" the client
- **聚焦于共鸣而非差异**：见证者应关注与自身经验的共鸣，而非强调"你的情况比我好/差" / **Focus on resonance, not difference**: Witnesses should attend to resonance with their own experience, not emphasize "your situation is better/worse than mine"
- **尊重来访者的主权**：见证者是来"聆听和回应"的，不是来"治疗"或"修复"的 / **Respect client sovereignty**: Witnesses are there to "listen and respond," not to "treat" or "fix"

## 东西方对话 · East-West Dialogue

定义式仪式与中国传统文化中的"宗族祠堂"和"乡约"制度有深层呼应。在传统的宗族文化中，个人的身份和成就需要在宗族祭祀和社区聚会中被"见证"和"确认"——一个人的"功名"不仅是个人的事，更是整个宗族的事。这种"身份的社群确认"传统与定义式仪式的逻辑相通。

Definitional ceremony has deep resonance with the traditional Chinese institutions of "ancestral halls" (zōngzú cítáng) and "community compacts" (xiāngyuē). In traditional clan culture, personal identity and achievements needed to be "witnessed" and "confirmed" in clan rituals and community gatherings — a person's "honors" were not merely individual matters but concerned the entire clan. This tradition of "community confirmation of identity" shares logic with definitional ceremony.

此外，佛教的"回向"（parināmana）实践——将修行的功德"回向"给一切众生——与定义式仪式的"双向转化"精神相通：见证者在见证他人的生命故事时，自身也被触动和转化，形成一个相互滋养的意义共同体。

Additionally, the Buddhist practice of "parināmana" (dedication of merit) — dedicating the merit of practice to all beings — resonates with the "mutual transformation" spirit of definitional ceremony: witnesses, in witnessing others' life stories, are themselves touched and transformed, forming a mutually nourishing community of meaning.
