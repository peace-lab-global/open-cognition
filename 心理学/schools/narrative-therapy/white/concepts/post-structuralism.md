# 后结构主义与叙事实践 · Post-structuralism and Narrative Practice

## 定义 · Definition

后结构主义（Post-structuralism）是迈克尔·怀特叙事实践的理论基石。怀特主要从福柯（Michel Foucault）的权力/知识理论中汲取灵感，将其系统性地引入心理治疗实践，从根本上重新审视了治疗师的权威角色、诊断标签的权力效应、以及"正常化"话语对人的规训作用。叙事疗法因此不仅是一种临床技术，更是一种具有深刻政治批判意识的实践哲学。

Post-structuralism is the theoretical cornerstone of Michael White's narrative practice. White drew primarily from Michel Foucault's power/knowledge theory, systematically introducing it into psychotherapeutic practice, fundamentally re-examining the therapist's authoritative role, the power effects of diagnostic labels, and the disciplinary function of "normalizing" discourses. Narrative therapy is therefore not merely a clinical technique but a practice philosophy with profound political-critical consciousness.

## 核心机制 · Core Mechanisms

### 福柯的权力/知识理论 · Foucault's Power/Knowledge Theory

怀特的叙事实践建立在福柯的几个核心洞见之上：

White's narrative practice is built on several core Foucauldian insights:

**1. 权力不是"拥有"的，而是"运作"的 / Power is not "possessed" but "exercised"**

传统观点将权力理解为某人"拥有"的资源（如医生对患者的权力）。福柯认为权力更像是一种弥散性的网络关系，通过话语、制度和日常实践来运作。在治疗关系中，权力不仅存在于治疗师的"权威地位"中，更嵌入在诊断标签、评估工具和"什么是正常"的话语框架中。

Traditional views understand power as a resource someone "possesses" (e.g., the doctor's power over patients). Foucault argued that power is more like a diffuse network of relations, operating through discourses, institutions, and everyday practices. In the therapeutic relationship, power resides not only in the therapist's "authoritative position" but is embedded in diagnostic labels, assessment tools, and discursive frameworks of "what is normal."

**2. 知识与权力的共生关系 / The Symbiotic Relationship of Knowledge and Power**

福柯提出"权力/知识"（pouvoir/savoir）这一复合概念，强调知识的生产从来不脱离权力关系，而权力的运作也依赖于知识的建构。心理学"知识"（如DSM分类系统）并非价值中立的科学发现，而是在特定历史和社会语境中产生的话语建构，它们反过来又参与着对人的"规训"和"正常化"。

Foucault proposed the compound concept of "power/knowledge" (pouvoir/savoir), emphasizing that knowledge production is never detached from power relations, and the operation of power relies on the construction of knowledge. Psychological "knowledge" (such as the DSM classification system) is not a value-neutral scientific discovery but a discursive construction produced within specific historical and social contexts, which in turn participates in the "disciplining" and "normalizing" of persons.

**3. 规训权力与正常化判断 / Disciplinary Power and Normalizing Judgment**

福柯分析了现代社会中"规训权力"（disciplinary power）的运作方式——它不通过暴力来统治，而是通过"正常化判断"来运作：定义什么是"正常的"家庭、"健康的"心理、"适当的"情感表达，然后对不符合标准的人施加隐性压力。怀特深刻意识到，心理治疗正是这种规训权力的重要运作场域。

Foucault analyzed the operation of "disciplinary power" in modern society — it does not dominate through violence but operates through "normalizing judgment": defining what constitutes a "normal" family, "healthy" psychology, "appropriate" emotional expression, then exerting subtle pressure on those who do not meet standards. White was deeply aware that psychotherapy is a significant site for the operation of this disciplinary power.

### 叙事实践对福柯的创造性运用 · Narrative Practice's Creative Application of Foucault

怀特并非简单地将福柯的理论"应用"到治疗中，而是创造性地将其转化为具体的治疗立场和技术：

White did not simply "apply" Foucault's theory to therapy but creatively transformed it into specific therapeutic stances and techniques:

**去中心化但有影响力的治疗师 / The Decentred but Influential Therapist**

怀特拒绝治疗师作为"真相发现者"的传统角色，但并非放弃治疗师的所有影响力。他提出"去中心化但有影响力的"立场：治疗师不宣称拥有对来访者生活的"专业真相"，但通过精心设计的提问来创造反思的空间。治疗师的影响力不在于"权威"，而在于"提问的艺术"。

White rejected the traditional role of the therapist as "truth-finder" but did not abandon all therapeutic influence. He proposed a "decentred but influential" stance: the therapist does not claim to possess "professional truth" about the client's life but creates spaces for reflection through carefully designed questions. The therapist's influence lies not in "authority" but in "the art of questioning."

**解构实践 / Deconstructive Practice**

叙事治疗包含对"主流话语"的系统性解构——帮助来访者审视那些被视为"理所当然"的信念和假设：

Narrative therapy includes systematic deconstruction of "dominant discourses" — helping clients examine beliefs and assumptions taken for granted:

- "是谁告诉你'一个好母亲'应该是什么样的？" / "Who told you what 'a good mother' should be like?"
- "这个关于'成功'的定义——它是你自己的，还是社会强加给你的？" / "This definition of 'success' — is it yours, or one imposed by society?"
- "当你说自己'不正常'时，你用的是谁的标准？" / "When you say you're 'not normal,' whose standards are you using?"

**对诊断的批判性立场 / A Critical Stance Toward Diagnosis**

怀特对DSM等诊断系统持批判性立场。他并非否认诊断在某些情境中的实用性，而是警惕诊断标签的权力效应：一个"抑郁症"的诊断可能帮助来访者获得治疗资源，但也可能将一个多维的人简化为"一个抑郁症患者"，限制其自我认同的可能性。

White held a critical stance toward diagnostic systems such as the DSM. He did not deny the utility of diagnosis in certain contexts but was wary of the power effects of diagnostic labels: a "depression" diagnosis may help clients access treatment resources, but it may also reduce a multi-dimensional person to "a depression patient," constraining the possibilities of their self-identity.

## 发展脉络 · Development

### 福柯理论的引入（1980年代末）/ Introduction of Foucauldian Theory (late 1980s)

怀特在1980年代末开始系统阅读福柯的著作，特别是《规训与惩罚》（*Discipline and Punish*）和《性史》（*The History of Sexuality*）。他发现福柯对"权力如何建构主体"的分析，精确地描述了治疗领域中来访者如何被诊断标签和正常化话语所"困住"。这一理论洞见成为叙事实践区别于其他后现代治疗流派的关键标志。

White began systematically reading Foucault's works in the late 1980s, particularly *Discipline and Punish* and *The History of Sexuality*. He found that Foucault's analysis of "how power constructs subjects" precisely described how clients in the therapeutic field are "trapped" by diagnostic labels and normalizing discourses. This theoretical insight became the key distinguishing mark of narrative practice from other postmodern therapy schools.

### 理论的系统化（1990年代–2000年代）/ Systematization of Theory (1990s–2000s)

在《叙事疗法》（1990）和《叙事实践反思》（2000）中，怀特逐步将福柯的理论整合为叙事实践的完整哲学框架。他特别发展了"治疗作为政治实践"的理念——治疗不仅关乎个体心理，更关乎权力关系、社会正义和文化多元性。

In *Narrative Means to Therapeutic Ends* (1990) and *Reflections on Narrative Practice* (2000), White progressively integrated Foucault's theory into a comprehensive philosophical framework for narrative practice. He particularly developed the concept of "therapy as political practice" — therapy concerns not only individual psychology but also power relations, social justice, and cultural diversity.

### 批评与回应（2000年代）/ Criticisms and Responses (2000s)

叙事实践的后结构主义立场引发了不少争议。批评者认为：（1）过度强调权力可能导致"治疗虚无主义"；（2）福柯的理论过于抽象，难以指导具体临床实践；（3）对诊断的批判可能妨碍来访者获得必要的医疗资源。怀特在2000年的《叙事实践反思》中系统回应了这些批评，强调叙事实践并非"反科学"，而是对科学在治疗领域中的权力运作保持批判性警觉。

The post-structuralist stance of narrative practice has generated considerable controversy. Critics argue: (1) overemphasizing power may lead to "therapeutic nihilism"; (2) Foucault's theory is too abstract to guide specific clinical practice; (3) criticism of diagnosis may hinder clients' access to necessary medical resources. White systematically responded to these criticisms in *Reflections on Narrative Practice* (2000), emphasizing that narrative practice is not "anti-science" but maintains critical vigilance toward the operation of power in the application of science to the therapeutic field.

## 临床应用 · Clinical Applications

### 权力地图的探索 / Exploring Power Maps

叙事治疗师帮助来访者绘制"权力地图"——审视哪些社会话语和权力关系在塑造他们的问题叙事：

Narrative therapists help clients map "power maps" — examining which social discourses and power relations are shaping their problem narratives:

- **性别话语**："做一个'好女人'的社会期望如何影响了你对自己愤怒的感受？" / **Gender discourse**: "How have social expectations of being a 'good woman' affected your feelings about your own anger?"
- **阶级话语**："'成功人士'的定义如何塑造了你对自己职业困境的理解？" / **Class discourse**: "How has the definition of a 'successful person' shaped your understanding of your career difficulties?"
- **文化话语**："作为一个移民，'融入'的压力如何影响了你对自身文化的态度？" / **Cultural discourse**: "As an immigrant, how has the pressure to 'fit in' affected your attitude toward your own culture?"

### 叙事实践的去殖民化维度 / The Decolonizing Dimension of Narrative Practice

怀特特别关注叙事实践在殖民和后殖民语境中的应用。在澳大利亚原住民社区和南非等地区的实践中，他强调治疗师必须审视西方心理学的文化霸权，尊重在地文化知识和治疗传统。这一"去殖民化"立场使叙事实践成为全球南方（Global South）特别受欢迎的治疗方法。

White paid particular attention to the application of narrative practice in colonial and post-colonial contexts. In practice in Australian Indigenous communities and regions such as South Africa, he emphasized that therapists must examine the cultural hegemony of Western psychology and respect local cultural knowledge and therapeutic traditions. This "decolonizing" stance has made narrative practice a particularly welcomed therapeutic approach in the Global South.

## 东西方对话 · East-West Dialogue

福柯对"规训权力"和"正常化"的批判与道家对"名教"的批判有深层呼应。庄子对儒家礼教的解构——"彼亦一是非，此亦一是非"——揭示了一切"正常化"标准的相对性和权力性。老子"绝圣弃智"的主张同样指向对知识/权力共生的批判。不同的是，道家走向"无为"——回归自然的超越性解决方案；怀特则走向"重写"——在具体的社会关系中建构替代性叙事的实践性方案。

Foucault's critique of "disciplinary power" and "normalization" has deep resonance with the Daoist critique of "míngjiào" (the teaching of names/propriety). Zhuangzi's deconstruction of Confucian ritual propriety — "that too has its right and wrong, this too has its right and wrong" — reveals the relativity and power-laden nature of all "normalizing" standards. Laozi's call to "abandon sagacity and discard cleverness" similarly points to a critique of the knowledge/power symbiosis. The difference is that Daoism moves toward "wúwéi" — a transcendent solution of returning to nature; White moves toward "re-authoring" — a practical solution of constructing alternative narratives within concrete social relationships.
