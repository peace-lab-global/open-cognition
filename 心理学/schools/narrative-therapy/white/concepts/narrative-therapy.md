# 叙事疗法 · Narrative Therapy

## 定义 · Definition

叙事疗法（Narrative Therapy）是一种以后结构主义哲学和叙事隐喻为核心的心理治疗方法，由迈克尔·怀特（Michael White）和大卫·艾普斯顿（David Epston）于1980年代在澳大利亚和新西兰共同发展。其基本前提是：人通过"故事"来组织经验、建构意义和形成身份认同；心理困扰源于被"问题主导叙事"（problem-dominated narrative）所"锁定"的单薄身份描述；治疗的目的是帮助来访者"重写"（re-author）自己的生活叙事，发展更丰厚、更符合其价值取向的替代性身份认同。

Narrative Therapy is a psychotherapeutic approach centered on post-structuralist philosophy and the narrative metaphor, co-developed by Michael White and David Epston in Australia and New Zealand during the 1980s. Its fundamental premise is that people organize experience, construct meaning, and form identity through "stories"; psychological distress stems from thin identity descriptions in which people are "locked" by problem-dominated narratives; and the purpose of therapy is to help clients "re-author" their life narratives, developing richer, more value-congruent alternative identities.

叙事疗法不是一种"技术手册"式的操作方法，而是一整套理解人、理解问题和理解治疗关系的哲学立场和实践框架。

Narrative Therapy is not a "technique manual" approach but an entire philosophical stance and practice framework for understanding people, problems, and therapeutic relationships.

## 核心机制 · Core Mechanisms

### 叙事隐喻 · The Narrative Metaphor

怀特从布鲁纳（Jerome Bruner）的叙事心理学中汲取核心洞见：人类是"讲故事的动物"（storytelling animals），我们通过叙事来组织和赋予生活经验以意义。叙事不是对现实的被动反映，而是主动地"建构"了我们所经验到的现实和身份。

White drew a core insight from Bruner's narrative psychology: humans are "storytelling animals" who organize and give meaning to life experience through narrative. Narratives do not passively reflect reality but actively "construct" the reality and identity we experience.

**叙事的关键特征 / Key Features of Narrative**：
- **选择性**：叙事选择性地包含和排除经验——某些事件被赋予意义（"情节化"），另一些被忽略 / **Selectivity**: Narratives selectively include and exclude experience — certain events are given meaning ("emplotted"), while others are overlooked
- **时间性**：叙事将分散的事件组织为具有时间连续性的故事线 / **Temporality**: Narratives organize discrete events into storylines with temporal continuity
- **社会性**：叙事在社会文化语境中被建构，受权力关系和主流话语的影响 / **Sociality**: Narratives are constructed within sociocultural contexts, shaped by power relations and dominant discourses
- **建构性**：叙事不是"发现"身份，而是"建构"身份——我们通过讲述自己的故事来"成为"自己 / **Constructiveness**: Narratives do not "discover" identity but "construct" it — we "become" ourselves by telling our stories

### 主流叙事与单薄描述 · Dominant Narratives and Thin Descriptions

在社会文化语境中，某些故事线比其他故事线更具"话语权"——怀特称之为"主流叙事"（dominant narratives）。例如，"成功学叙事"、"正常家庭叙事"、"异性恋规范叙事"等。当来访者的生活不符合主流叙事时，他们往往被"问题化"——被赋予"抑郁症患者"、"问题少年"、"功能失调家庭"等标签。

Within sociocultural contexts, certain storylines carry more "discursive power" than others — what White called "dominant narratives." Examples include "success narratives," "normal family narratives," and "heteronormative narratives." When clients' lives do not conform to dominant narratives, they are often "problem-saturated" — labeled as "depression patient," "problem youth," or "dysfunctional family."

这些标签构成怀特所说的"单薄描述"（thin descriptions）——它们将复杂的、多维的人简化为单一的、问题化的身份。单薄描述具有自我验证的循环效应：一旦被标签化，人会不自觉地按照标签行事，进一步巩固问题叙事。

These labels constitute what White called "thin descriptions" — they reduce complex, multi-dimensional persons to single, problem-saturated identities. Thin descriptions have a self-validating circular effect: once labeled, people unconsciously behave in accordance with the label, further consolidating the problem narrative.

### 叙事治疗的基本框架 · The Basic Framework of Narrative Therapy

叙事治疗遵循以下基本过程（但并非线性的"步骤"，而是灵活的"地图"）：

Narrative therapy follows a basic process (not as linear "steps" but as flexible "maps"):

1. **外化对话**：将问题与人分离——"问题是问题，人不是问题" / **Externalizing conversations**: Separating the problem from the person — "the problem is the problem, the person is not the problem"
2. **解构主流叙事**：探索问题叙事背后的社会文化假设和权力关系 / **Deconstructing dominant narratives**: Exploring the sociocultural assumptions and power relations behind the problem narrative
3. **发现独特结果**：寻找不符合问题故事的例外时刻 / **Discovering unique outcomes**: Finding moments of exception that don't fit the problem story
4. **重写对话**：将独特结果编织为替代叙事 / **Re-authoring conversations**: Weaving unique outcomes into alternative narratives
5. **巩固新叙事**：通过见证者、文件和仪式来强化替代身份 / **Consolidating new narratives**: Strengthening alternative identity through witnesses, documents, and ceremonies

## 发展脉络 · Development

### 创立期（1980年代）/ Founding Period (1980s)

怀特和艾普斯顿在1980年代初期开始合作，将系统家庭治疗与后结构主义理论相结合。1988年怀特发表"The Externalizing Conversation"一文，首次系统阐述外化对话技术。1989年两人合著 *Literate Means to Therapeutic Ends*，探讨书写在治疗中的运用。

White and Epston began collaborating in the early 1980s, combining systemic family therapy with post-structuralist theory. In 1988, White published "The Externalizing Conversation," the first systematic exposition of externalizing conversation techniques. In 1989, they co-authored *Literate Means to Therapeutic Ends*, exploring the use of writing in therapy.

### 系统化期（1990年代）/ Systematization Period (1990s)

1990年的《叙事疗法》（*Narrative Means to Therapeutic Ends*）标志着叙事疗法作为一个独立治疗流派的正式确立。弗里德曼和康普斯（Freedman & Combs）1996年的《叙事疗法：偏好的现实的社会建构》进一步拓展了叙事实践的理论基础和临床应用范围。

*Narrative Means to Therapeutic Ends* (1990) marked the formal establishment of narrative therapy as an independent therapeutic school. Freedman and Combs' *Narrative Therapy: The Social Construction of Preferred Realities* (1996) further expanded the theoretical foundations and clinical application scope of narrative practice.

### 全球化与精细化（2000年代至今）/ Globalization and Refinement (2000s–Present)

怀特2007年的《叙事实践的地图》提供了最系统化的治疗框架。在他去世后，杜尔维奇中心继续在德纳姆（David Denborough）等人领导下推进叙事实践的全球传播。叙事实践已应用于50多个国家的临床治疗、社区发展、教育和组织咨询领域。

White's *Maps of Narrative Practice* (2007) provided the most systematized therapeutic framework. After his passing, the Dulwich Centre continued to advance the global dissemination of narrative practice under the leadership of David Denborough and others. Narrative practice has been applied in clinical therapy, community development, education, and organizational consulting in over 50 countries.

## 临床应用 · Clinical Applications

### 进食障碍治疗 / Eating Disorder Treatment

叙事疗法在进食障碍治疗中取得显著效果。通过外化"厌食症"为一个独立的"声音"或"暴君"，治疗师帮助来访者认识到：不是她们"想要"节食，而是"厌食症"在欺骗和控制她们。这一外化使来访者能够与"厌食症"建立对抗关系，重新找回对生活的自主权。

Narrative therapy has achieved significant results in eating disorder treatment. By externalizing "anorexia" as an independent "voice" or "tyrant," therapists help clients recognize that it is not that they "want" to diet but that "anorexia" is deceiving and controlling them. This externalization enables clients to establish an adversarial relationship with "anorexia" and reclaim autonomy over their lives.

### 儿童行为问题 / Childhood Behavioral Issues

对儿童使用外化技术尤为有效。治疗师可能会问："'捣蛋鬼'什么时候来找你？它来的时候你在做什么？你和爸爸妈妈一起做过什么来对抗'捣蛋鬼'？"这种游戏化的外化使儿童能够以安全的方式审视自己的行为模式。

Externalization techniques are particularly effective with children. A therapist might ask: "When does the 'troublemaker' come to visit you? What are you doing when it comes? What have you and your parents done together to stand up to the 'troublemaker'?" This playful externalization enables children to examine their behavioral patterns in a safe manner.

### 创伤后治疗 / Post-Trauma Therapy

叙事实践为创伤幸存者提供了一条既不回避创伤经验也不被创伤经验所淹没的治疗路径。通过将创伤的影响外化（"创伤教给了你什么关于世界的'规则'？"），来访者能够审视创伤对身份认同的影响，同时发现那些创伤未能摧毁的力量和价值。

Narrative practice offers trauma survivors a therapeutic pathway that neither avoids traumatic experience nor is overwhelmed by it. By externalizing the effects of trauma ("What 'rules' about the world did the trauma teach you?"), clients can examine trauma's impact on identity while discovering strengths and values that trauma could not destroy.

## 东西方对话 · East-West Dialogue

叙事疗法与中国传统文化中的"说书"（storytelling）传统和"修史"（historiographical）传统有深层共鸣。中国传统的"说书人"不仅是故事的讲述者，更是意义的建构者——通过讲述和重述来塑造社群记忆和集体身份。同样，司马迁的《史记》通过"列传"形式为边缘人物赋予叙事存在，这与叙事疗法为被"主流叙事"忽略的人发展"替代叙事"的实践精神相通。

Narrative Therapy has deep resonance with the Chinese cultural traditions of "shuōshū" (storytelling) and "xiūshǐ" (historiography). Traditional Chinese storytellers were not merely narrators of tales but constructors of meaning — shaping communal memory and collective identity through telling and retelling. Similarly, Sima Qian's *Records of the Grand Historian* gave narrative existence to marginalized figures through its "biographical" form, resonating with narrative therapy's practice of developing "alternative narratives" for those overlooked by "dominant narratives."
