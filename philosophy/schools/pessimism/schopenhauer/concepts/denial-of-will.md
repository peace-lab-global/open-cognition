# 意志的否定 / Denial of the Will

> "意志自行转向否定的那一刻，一切痛苦都结束了。"
> "The moment the Will turns of itself to denial, all suffering comes to an end."
> — Schopenhauer, *Die Welt als Wille und Vorstellung*, Book IV, §68

---

## 概述 / Overview

意志的否定（Verneinung des Willens zum Leben）是叔本华哲学体系中的终极解脱概念。如果说审美沉思提供了从意志中的**暂时**解脱，那么意志的否定则提供了**永久**的解脱——通过意志本身的自我否定和自我消灭。这是叔本华哲学中最具宗教色彩的维度，也是与佛教涅槃概念形成最深刻呼应的地方。

The denial of the Will (Verneinung des Willens zum Leben) is the concept of ultimate liberation in Schopenhauer's philosophical system. If aesthetic contemplation provides **temporary** liberation from the Will, the denial of Will provides **permanent** liberation — through the Will's own self-negation and self-extinction. This is the most religiously coloured dimension of Schopenhauer's philosophy, and the point of deepest resonance with the Buddhist concept of Nirvana.

---

## 从肯定到否定 / From Affirmation to Denial

叔本华区分了意志的两种基本朝向：

Schopenhauer distinguishes two fundamental orientations of the Will:

**意志的肯定（Bejahung des Willens）**：这是绝大多数人的常态——意志盲目地肯定自身，不断追求欲求的满足，将生命视为值得追求的。即使在痛苦中，生命意志也驱使我们继续生存、繁衍和竞争。生命意志的肯定是整个表象世界——即充满痛苦的现象世界——得以维持的根本力量。

**Affirmation of the Will (Bejahung des Willens)**: This is the default state of most people — the Will blindly affirms itself, ceaselessly pursuing the satisfaction of desires, regarding life as worth pursuing. Even in suffering, the will-to-live drives us to continue surviving, reproducing, and competing. The affirmation of the will-to-live is the fundamental force that maintains the entire world of representation — the phenomenal world full of suffering.

**意志的否定（Verneinung des Willens）**：在某些极端条件下——通过深刻的苦难体验或对世界本质的智识洞察——意志可能"转向"自身，认识到欲求的无意义和痛苦的不可避免性，从而自发地否定自身。这种否定不是外在力量施加于意志的，而是意志的**自我否定**——意志"看到"了自身的本质而自行熄灭。

**Denial of the Will (Verneinung des Willens)**: Under certain extreme conditions — through profound suffering or intellectual insight into the world's essence — the Will may "turn" against itself, recognizing the meaninglessness of striving and the inevitability of suffering, and thereby spontaneously negate itself. This denial is not an external force imposed on the Will but the Will's **self-negation** — the Will "sees" its own essence and extinguishes itself.

## 禁欲主义（Askese）/ Asceticism

意志否定的实际表现是**禁欲主义**——自愿放弃一切感官享受、世俗追求和个人欲望。叔本华描述了禁欲者的三个阶段：

The practical expression of the Will's denial is **asceticism** — the voluntary renunciation of all sensory enjoyment, worldly pursuits, and personal desires. Schopenhauer describes three stages of the ascetic:

### 第一阶段：自愿贫穷 / First Stage: Voluntary Poverty

放弃一切财产和物质享受——不是因为被迫，而是出于对财富之虚幻性的认识。财产是意志客体化的外在形式，放弃财产就是削弱意志的外在支撑。

Renouncing all property and material comfort — not from compulsion but from recognition of wealth's illusoriness. Property is the external form of the Will's objectification; renouncing property weakens the Will's external supports.

### 第二阶段：自我克制与斋戒 / Second Stage: Self-Denial and Fasting

通过斋戒、贞洁和自我苦行来直接抑制身体中的生命意志。这不是对身体的仇恨（masochism），而是对意志的战略性削弱——通过减少对意志的"喂养"来使意志逐渐枯萎。

Through fasting, chastity, and self-mortification, directly suppressing the will-to-live in the body. This is not hatred of the body (masochism) but a strategic weakening of the Will — by reducing the "feeding" of the Will, causing it to gradually wither.

### 第三阶段：完全的内在弃绝 / Third Stage: Complete Inner Renunciation

最终的状态不是外在的苦行，而是内在的完全弃绝（Gelassenheit，借用埃克哈特大师 Meister Eckhart 的术语）——一种对一切事物完全无动于衷的内在宁静。在此状态中，意志已经完全熄灭，主体进入了一种超越痛苦也超越快乐的境地。

The final state is not external mortification but complete inner renunciation (Gelassenheit, borrowing Meister Eckhart's term) — an inner tranquility of complete indifference to all things. In this state, the Will is fully extinguished, and the subject enters a condition beyond both suffering and pleasure.

## 与佛教涅槃的类比 / Analogy with Buddhist Nirvana

叔本华的意志否定与佛教的**涅槃**（Nirvāṇa）之间存在深刻的结构平行：

A deep structural parallel exists between Schopenhauer's denial of Will and Buddhist **Nirvāṇa**:

| 叔本华概念 | 佛教对应概念 |
|-----------|-------------|
| 意志的否定 | 涅槃（字面义："熄灭"） |
| 意志的自我熄灭 | 贪嗔痴的断除 |
| 禁欲主义 | 戒定慧三学 |
| 穿透个体化原理 | 无我（anātman）的智慧 |
| 意志否定的圣者 | 阿罗汉（arhat） |
| 解脱后的"空" | 空性（śūnyatā） |

| Schopenhauer's Concept | Buddhist Correspondent |
|------------------------|----------------------|
| Denial of Will | Nirvāṇa (lit. "extinguishing") |
| Self-extinction of Will | Extinction of greed, hatred, delusion |
| Asceticism | Threefold training (śīla, samādhi, prajñā) |
| Penetration of principium individuationis | Wisdom of no-self (anātman) |
| The saint who denies Will | The arhat |
| The "void" after liberation | Emptiness (śūnyatā) |

叔本华本人清楚地意识到了这一平行，他明确引用了佛教文献来支持自己的论证。但他也指出一个关键差异：佛教的涅槃是通过系统的修行道路（八正道）可以**主动**达到的状态，而叔本华的意志否定更像是一种**恩赐**（Gnade）——它不是通过理性努力可以获得的，而是意志本身的自发性转向。

Schopenhauer himself was clearly aware of this parallel and explicitly cited Buddhist texts to support his argument. But he also noted a key difference: Buddhist Nirvana is a state that can be **actively** attained through a systematic path of practice (the Eightfold Path), while Schopenhauer's denial of Will is more like a **grace** (Gnade) — not attainable through rational effort but a spontaneous turning of the Will itself.

## 圣人与自杀者 / The Saint and the Suicide

叔本华严格区分了**圣人**（否定意志者）和**自杀者**，这一区分是其哲学中最重要也最常被误解的部分：

Schopenhauer strictly distinguishes the **saint** (one who denies the Will) from the **suicide** — one of the most important and most frequently misunderstood parts of his philosophy:

**圣人**（Heilige / Saint）：圣人通过智识洞察和禁欲实践使意志**自行熄灭**。圣人不是"不想活"，而是"不再欲求"——他的生命意志已经枯萎，但身体仍然存活着。圣人面对痛苦时的态度是宁静的接受，而非暴力的反抗。圣人的状态是从生命意志中**解脱**（Erlösung），而非逃避。

**The Saint** (Heilige): The saint causes the Will to **extinguish itself** through intellectual insight and ascetic practice. The saint does not "not want to live" but "no longer wills" — the will-to-live has withered, though the body still lives. The saint's attitude toward suffering is tranquil acceptance, not violent resistance. The saint's state is **redemption** (Erlösung) from the will-to-live, not escape from it.

**自杀者**（Selbstmörder / Suicide）：自杀者并非否定了生命意志，恰恰相反，自杀者是**肯定**了生命意志——他之所以自杀，是因为他强烈地欲求生命的美好但无法忍受当前的痛苦。自杀是对生命意志的一种暴力表达：通过消灭身体来"解决"痛苦的问题。但身体只是意志的客体化形式，消灭身体并不等于否定意志——意志本身仍然完好无损。

**The Suicide** (Selbstmörder): The suicide has not denied the will-to-live; on the contrary, the suicide **affirms** the will-to-live — he kills himself precisely because he intensely desires life's goodness but cannot endure present suffering. Suicide is a violent expression of the will-to-live: attempting to "solve" the problem of suffering by destroying the body. But the body is merely the Will's objectified form; destroying the body does not equal denying the Will — the Will itself remains intact.

```
圣人：   痛苦 → 洞察 → 意志自行熄灭 → 宁静地活着
自杀者：  痛苦 → 愤怒 → 以暴力消灭身体 → 意志仍然完好

Saint:   Suffering → Insight → Will extinguishes itself → Lives in tranquility
Suicide: Suffering → Anger → Violently destroys body → Will remains intact
```

## 意志否定后的状态 / The State After the Denial of Will

叔本华对意志否定后的状态描述是暧昧的。他拒绝将其称为"虚无"（Nichts），因为"虚无"是一个相对于"存在"的概念，而意志否定后的状态超越了存在/虚无的二元对立。他用了一系列否定性的描述：

Schopenhauer's description of the state after the denial of Will is deliberately ambiguous. He refuses to call it "nothingness" (Nichts), because "nothingness" is a concept relative to "being," while the post-denial state transcends the being/nothingness dichotomy. He employs a series of negative descriptions:

- 不是"无"，而是从意志的束缚中获得的自由 / Not "nothing" but freedom from the Will's bondage
- 没有主体、没有客体、没有表象、没有意志 / No subject, no object, no representation, no will
- 对于那些意志仍然活跃的人来说，它看起来像"无" / To those whose Will is still active, it appears as "nothing"
- 但对于已经否定意志的人来说，"我们的这个如此充满的世界……就是——无" / But for those who have denied the Will, "this our world, so very real... is — nothing"

这一描述与佛教中观学派（Mādhyamaka）对涅槃的描述——涅槃不是某个"地方"或"状态"，而是对一切执着的彻底超越——形成了深刻的呼应。

This description deeply resonates with the Mādhyamaka school's description of Nirvana — Nirvana is not some "place" or "state" but the thorough transcendence of all attachment.

## 意志否定的可能性问题 / The Problem of the Possibility of Will's Denial

叔本华的意志否定概念面临一个深刻的哲学难题：如果意志是万物的本体实在，那么**谁来否定意志**？否定意志的主体似乎必须站在意志之外——但如果一切都是意志，这似乎是不可能的。

Schopenhauer's concept of the denial of Will faces a deep philosophical difficulty: if Will is the noumenal reality of all things, then **who denies the Will?** The subject that denies Will seems to have to stand outside Will — but if everything is Will, this seems impossible.

叔本华的回应是：否定意志的是**意志本身**——意志通过其在人类意识中的最高客体化获得了自我认识的能力，而这种自我认识可能导致意志的自发否定。换言之，意志的否定不是"某物否定意志"，而是"意志否定意志"——这是一种自我指涉的、辩证的自我超越。

Schopenhauer's response is: it is **the Will itself** that denies the Will — the Will, through its highest objectification in human consciousness, acquires the capacity for self-knowledge, and this self-knowledge may lead to the Will's spontaneous self-negation. In other words, the denial of Will is not "something denying Will" but "Will denying Will" — a self-referential, dialectical self-transcendence.
