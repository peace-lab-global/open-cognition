# 我思故我在 · Cogito Ergo Sum

## 概念概述 / Concept Overview

"我思故我在"（拉丁文：Cogito, ergo sum；法文：Je pense, donc je suis）是笛卡尔哲学的第一原理，也是整个近代哲学的出发点。这一命题出现在《谈谈方法》（1637）第四部分和《第一哲学沉思集》（1641）第二沉思中。它表达了这样一个洞见：在彻底怀疑一切之后，唯一不可怀疑的事实是怀疑活动本身的存在——即思维活动的存在。

"Cogito, ergo sum" (Latin; French: "Je pense, donc je suis") is the first principle of Cartesian philosophy and the starting point of all modern philosophy. This proposition appears in Part IV of the *Discourse on the Method* (1637) and the Second Meditation of *Meditations on First Philosophy* (1641). It expresses the insight that after radical doubt of everything, the only indubitable fact is the existence of doubting itself — that is, the existence of thinking activity.

## 论证结构 / Argumentative Structure

### 第一沉思：怀疑的清扫

在《第一哲学沉思集》中，笛卡尔首先执行了系统的怀疑程序。他依次怀疑了三类信念：

In the *Meditations*, Descartes first executes a systematic doubt procedure, doubting three classes of beliefs in sequence:

1. **感官信念**：感官时常欺骗我们（远处的塔看起来是圆的，实际是方的），因此感官知识不可靠。
2. **梦境论证**：我们在梦中也能有逼真的体验，没有任何确定的标记可以区分梦境与清醒状态，因此我们无法确定当前的经验不是梦境。
3. **恶魔假说**：假设存在一个全能的、狡猾的恶魔（genius malignus），它系统地欺骗我，使我在一切认知上都出错——包括数学真理（2+3=5也可能是恶魔的欺骗）。

1. **Sensory beliefs**: The senses sometimes deceive (a distant tower appears round but is square), so sensory knowledge is unreliable.
2. **Dream argument**: Vivid experiences occur in dreams with no certain mark to distinguish dreaming from waking; we cannot be certain current experience is not a dream.
3. **Evil genius hypothesis**: Suppose an omnipotent, cunning deceiver (genius malignus) systematically deceives me about everything — including mathematical truths (2+3=5 could be demonic deception).

### 第二沉思："我思"的发现

在经过这一极端怀疑之后，笛卡尔发现了一个不可动摇的确定性：

After this extreme doubt, Descartes discovers an unshakable certainty:

> "但是有一个我不知道什么的全能者，他在用全力来欺骗我；那么，如果他在欺骗我，而我在思维，那么我毫无疑问是存在的；而且，随他怎么欺骗我吧，只要我想到我是某个东西，他就绝不能使我什么都不是。所以，在非常仔细地考察了一切之后，我必须最后得出这样的结论：'我在，我存在'（Ego sum, ego existo）这个命题，每当它由我说出或在心里被想到时，都必然是真的。"

> "But there is some omnipotent deceiver who is doing his utmost to deceive me; yet if he deceives me, I undoubtedly exist, so long as I think that I am something; and let him deceive me as much as he will, he can never bring it about that I am nothing, so long as I think that I am something. So that, having thoroughly weighed all considerations, I must finally conclude that the proposition 'I am, I exist' (Ego sum, ego existo) is necessarily true whenever it is put forward by me or conceived in my mind."

### "我思"不是三段论

笛卡尔明确否认"我思故我在"是一个三段论推理（syllogism）。如果它是一个三段论，就需要一个隐藏的大前提"凡思维者皆存在"（Omne quod cogitat existit），然后以"我在思维"为小前提，推出"我存在"。但笛卡尔认为，这恰恰颠倒了认识的顺序——我们不是先知道"凡思维者皆存在"，再推出"我存在"；而是在"我思"的当下体验中直接认识到"我存在"。

Descartes explicitly denies that the cogito is a syllogism. If it were, it would require a hidden major premise "everything that thinks exists" (Omne quod cogitat existit), then the minor premise "I think," yielding "I exist." But Descartes argues this inverts the order of knowledge — we do not first know the universal premise and then deduce "I exist"; rather, we directly recognize "I exist" in the immediate experience of "I think."

"我思故我在"因此是一种**直接直观**（intuitus）——在反思的瞬间，思维活动的存在与思维主体的存在同时呈现。这是一种"自反性的确定性"：思维在反思自身时发现自身的不可取消的存在。

The cogito is therefore a **direct intuition** (intuitus) — in the instant of reflection, the existence of thinking activity and the existence of the thinking subject present themselves simultaneously. This is a "reflexive certainty": thinking, in reflecting on itself, discovers its own irrevocable existence.

## "我思"确立了什么？/ What Does the Cogito Establish?

"我思"确立的最小事实是：**有思维活动在发生**（there is thinking occurring）。但笛卡尔在此基础上做出了进一步的推论：

The minimal fact established by the cogito is: **there is thinking occurring**. But Descartes makes further inferences:

1. **有一个"我"**：思维需要一个主体——"我"作为思维活动的承担者而存在。
2. **我是一个思维实体（res cogitans）**：我能够确定地认识到的关于"我"的唯一属性是思维，因此我的本质是思维。
3. **心灵比身体更容易认识**：我不需要身体来认识我的存在（因为身体可能是梦境或欺骗的产物），但我必然通过思维来认识我的存在。

1. **There is an "I"**: Thinking requires a subject — "I" exists as the bearer of thinking activity.
2. **I am a thinking substance (res cogitans)**: The only attribute I can certainly know about "me" is thinking; therefore my essence is thought.
3. **Mind is better known than body**: I do not need a body to know my existence (since the body could be a dream product or deception), but I necessarily know my existence through thinking.

## 批评与争论 / Criticisms and Debates

### 休谟的批评

大卫·休谟质疑从"有思维在发生"到"有一个持续存在的思维主体"的推论。休谟认为，我们在内省时只能发现一个个具体的知觉（快乐、痛苦、思考），而无法发现一个恒常不变的"自我"。所谓"自我"不过是"一束知觉"（a bundle of perceptions）的虚构。

David Hume challenged the inference from "there is thinking" to "there is a persisting thinking subject." Hume argued that introspection reveals only individual perceptions (pleasure, pain, thought) but never a constant "self." The so-called "self" is a fiction — merely "a bundle of perceptions."

### 尼采的批评

尼采进一步指出，"我思"的语法结构暗含了未经检验的形而上学假设。"我"这个主语不过是语法习惯的产物——语言要求每个动词都有一个主语，但这不意味着现实中有一个叫做"我"的实体。更准确的表达应该是"它思"（es denkt），如同"它下雨"（es regnat）一样。

Nietzsche further argued that the grammatical structure of "I think" smuggles in unexamined metaphysical assumptions. The subject "I" is merely a product of grammatical habit — language requires every verb to have a subject, but this does not mean there exists an entity called "I." A more accurate expression would be "it thinks" (es denkt), analogous to "it rains."

### 佛教的回应

佛教唯识学会同意休谟和尼采的诊断：不存在恒常不变的"自我"。但唯识学进一步指出，"我思"的体验本身就是一种"末那识"（manas）的执著——第七识将阿赖耶识的流转误认为一个恒常的自我。从这个角度看，笛卡尔的"我思"恰恰是佛教要破除的根本无明（avidyā）。

Buddhist Yogācāra would agree with Hume and Nietzsche: there is no permanent "self." But Yogācāra further argues that the experience of "I think" is itself a fixation of manas (the seventh consciousness) — which misidentifies the flow of ālaya-vijñāna as a permanent self. From this perspective, the Cartesian cogito is precisely the fundamental ignorance (avidyā) that Buddhism seeks to dissolve.

### 当代哲学中的地位

在当代心智哲学中，"我思"的核心洞见——思维具有某种自反性的确定性——仍然受到重视，但其形而上学推论（即存在一个非物质的思维实体）则被大多数哲学家所拒斥。功能主义者认为思维可以在任何适当的物理基底上实现；物理主义者认为思维最终可以还原为神经活动；而泛心论者则认为意识是物质的基本属性。

In contemporary philosophy of mind, the cogito's core insight — that thinking possesses a reflexive certainty — is still valued, while its metaphysical inference (that an immaterial thinking substance exists) is rejected by most philosophers. Functionalists argue thinking can be realized on any appropriate physical substrate; physicalists argue it ultimately reduces to neural activity; panpsychists argue consciousness is a fundamental property of matter.

## 跨文化回响 / Cross-Cultural Echoes

"我思"的回响远不限于西方哲学传统。在日本，西田几多郎的"纯粹经验"概念试图超越主客二分的"我思"模式。在印度，吠檀多的"我知"（aham brahmāsmi，"我即梵"）将自我意识提升到宇宙本体论的高度。在中国，王阳明的"致良知"则将认知的确定性锚定在道德直觉而非理论反思上。

The cogito's echoes extend far beyond Western philosophy. In Japan, Nishida Kitarō's "pure experience" sought to transcend the subject-object dichotomy of the cogito. In India, Vedānta's "I know" (aham brahmāsmi, "I am Brahman") elevates self-consciousness to cosmic ontology. In China, Wang Yangming's "extending innate knowledge" (zhiliangzhi) anchors certainty in moral intuition rather than theoretical reflection.
