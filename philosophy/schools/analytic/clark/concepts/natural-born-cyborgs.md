# 天生赛博格 · Natural-Born Cyborgs

## 概念定位 / Conceptual Position
人类天生倾向于将认知卸载到外部工具和符号系统中。技术不是外在辅助，而是心智的构成部分。

Humans are naturally inclined to offload cognition onto external tools and symbol systems. Technology is not external aid but a constitutive part of mind.

## 核心论述 / Core Argument

### 从石器到智能手机 / From Stone Tools to Smartphones
人类与工具的共演化已有数百万年历史。语言本身就是第一种"认知技术"——它改变了我们的思维方式，而非仅仅表达已有的思想。

Human-tool co-evolution spans millions of years. Language itself is the first "cognitive technology" — it changed how we think, not just how we express pre-existing thoughts.

### "赛博格"不是科幻 / "Cyborg" Is Not Sci-Fi
Clark论证：我们已经一直是赛博格——使用眼镜、助听器、笔记本、计算器。未来的脑机接口和AR只是这一趋势的延续。

We have always been cyborgs — using glasses, hearing aids, notebooks, calculators. Future brain-computer interfaces and AR are just continuations of this trend.

## 哲学意义 / Philosophical Significance
改变了我们对"人类本质"的理解——人类不是"使用工具的动物"，而是"与工具共演化的存在"。

## 东西方对话 / East-West Dialogue
### 与庄子"机心" / With Zhuangzi's "Mechanical Mind"
庄子在《天地》篇中通过"抱瓮老人"的故事质疑技术——"有机事者必有机心"。Clark则论证技术与心智是一体的。这构成了关于技术与人性的深刻对话。

Zhuangzi's story of the old man carrying a pot questions technology — "who has mechanical affairs will have a mechanical mind." Clark argues technology and mind are one. This forms a deep dialogue about technology and humanity.
